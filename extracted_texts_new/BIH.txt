**LAW**
**ON WATERS**

**I. BASIC PROVISIONS**

**Article 1.**
**Subject of the Law**

(1) This Law shall regulate the manner of water management within the territory of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina (hereinafter: the Federation).

(2) Water management shall comprise water protection, water use, protection from the adverse effects of water, and the regulation of watercourses and other waters.

(3) This Law shall regulate the water estate and public water estate, water facilities, legal persons and other institutions competent for specific issues of water management, and other matters concerning waters in the Federation.

**Article 2.**
**Purpose of the Law**

(1) The purpose of this Law is to ensure water management with the aim of:

1. reducing water pollution, achieving good water status, and preventing water degradation,
2. achieving sustainable use of waters,
3. ensuring equitable access to waters,
4. promoting social and economic development,
---
*(Page 2)*

5. protecting ecosystems,
6. reducing the risk of floods and other negative impacts of water,
7. ensuring public participation in the adoption of decisions related to waters,
8. preventing and resolving conflicts regarding the protection and use of waters,
9. fulfilling obligations from international treaties that are binding for Bosnia and Herzegovina.

**II. FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES AND DEFINITIONS**

**Article 3.**
**General Principles**

(1) Waters are a common good and as such are under the special protection of Bosnia and Herzegovina, the Federation, cantons, cities, and municipalities.

(2) Water management shall be based on the principles of:

1. non-commerciality, according to which water is fundamentally not a commercial product, but a heritage that must be preserved, protected, and treated accordingly,
2. integrity, which takes into account the natural processes and dynamics of waters and the mutual connection and interdependence of water and water-related ecosystems,
3. long-term protection of the quality and rational use of available quantities of water,
4. ensuring protection from the adverse effects of water, which arises from the need to protect the population and their property, taking into account the effects of natural processes,
5. economic valuation of waters, which includes the costs of burdening, protection, and regulation of waters, and protection from the adverse effects of water,
6. public participation in the adoption of water management plans,
7. taking into account the best available technologies and new scientific achievements on natural laws and best environmental practices.

**Article 4.**
**Definitions**

The terms used in this Law shall have the following meanings:

1. "surface waters" shall mean all inland waters, except groundwater; transitional waters and coastal waters, except for marine waters belonging to territorial waters;
2. "inland waters" shall mean all still or flowing waters on the surface of the land and all groundwater on the landward side of the line from which the breadth of territorial waters is measured;
3. "groundwater" shall mean all water which is below the surface of the ground in the saturation zone and in direct contact with the ground or subsoil;
4. "river" shall mean a body of water flowing for the most part on the surface of the land but which may, for a part of its course, flow underground;
---
*(Page 3)*

5. "coastal waters" shall mean surface waters on the landward side of a line every point of which is at a distance of one nautical mile on the seaward side from the nearest point of the baseline from which the breadth of territorial waters is measured, extending, where appropriate, up to the outer limit of transitional waters;
6. "transitional waters" shall mean bodies of surface water in the vicinity of river mouths which are partly saline in character as a result of their proximity to coastal waters but which are substantially influenced by freshwater flows;
7. "watercourse" shall mean the bed of a flowing body of water together with its banks and the water that flows through it permanently or intermittently;
8. "bed" shall mean the terrain depression through which water flows permanently or intermittently;
9. "water regime" shall mean a set of hydrological, hydro-morphological, hydraulic, chemical, and biological properties of surface and groundwaters in a certain area and in a certain time;
10. "change of water regime" shall mean all changes in the water regime, resulting from natural forces or human activities;
11. "ecosystems" shall mean aquatic, semi-aquatic, and terrestrial ecosystems that directly or indirectly depend on waters;
12. "river basin" shall mean the area of land from which all surface run-off flows through a sequence of streams, rivers and, possibly, lakes into the sea at a single river mouth, estuary or delta;
13. "sub-basin" shall mean the area of land from which all surface run-off flows through a series of streams, rivers and, possibly, lakes to a particular point in a watercourse (normally a lake or a confluence of rivers);
14. "water area (district)" shall mean the area of land and sea, made up of one or more neighbouring river basins together with their associated groundwaters and coastal waters, which is defined as the main unit for water management;
15. "transboundary waters" shall mean waters that form or cross the border of Bosnia and Herzegovina;
16. "body of surface water" shall mean a discrete and significant element of surface water such as a lake, a stream, river or canal, part of a stream, river or canal, transitional waters or a stretch of coastal water;
17. "status of surface waters" shall mean the status of a body of surface water, determined by the poorer of its ecological and chemical status;
18. "ecological status" shall mean the quality of the structure and functioning of aquatic ecosystems associated with surface waters;
19. "chemical status" shall mean the degree of chemical pollution of waters;
20. "artificial body of water" shall mean a body of surface water created by human activity;
21. "heavily modified body of water" shall mean a body of surface water which as a result of physical alterations by human activity is substantially changed in character;
22. "aquifer" shall mean a subsurface layer or layers of rock or other geological strata of sufficient porosity and permeability to allow either a significant flow of groundwater or the abstraction of significant quantities of groundwater;
23. "body of groundwater" shall mean a distinct volume of groundwater within an aquifer or aquifers;
24. "status of groundwater" shall mean the status of a body of groundwater, determined by the poorer of its quantitative and chemical status;
---
*(Page 4)*

25. "quantitative status of groundwater" shall mean the degree to which a body of groundwater is affected by direct and indirect abstractions;
26. "drinking water" shall mean water intended for human consumption which, in terms of quality, complies with special regulations issued by the ministry competent for health matters;
27. "public water supply" shall mean the activities of abstraction of ground or surface waters for the purpose of supplying the population with water, their purification to the level of health safety, and delivery to the point of consumption and distribution to water users, if the total abstracted quantity is greater than 10 m³ per day;
28. "mineral waters" shall mean groundwaters which by their characteristics correspond to special standards for mineral waters, and are abstracted from wells or springs;
29. "thermal waters" shall mean groundwaters which by their characteristics correspond to special standards for thermal waters, and are abstracted from wells or springs;
30. "thermo-mineral waters" shall mean thermal waters with the properties of mineral waters;
31. "direct discharge to groundwater" shall mean the discharge of pollutants into groundwater without percolation through the ground or subsoil;
32. "material in watercourses" shall mean gravel, sand, silt, plants, and other similar natural or artificial materials that are removed from a watercourse or from the water estate for the purpose of their maintenance or regulation;
33. "hazardous substances" shall mean substances or groups of substances that are toxic, persistent and liable to bio-accumulate, and other substances or groups of substances which give rise to an equivalent level of concern;
34. "priority substances" shall mean substances defined in Article 2, item 30 of Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council;
35. "polluting substance" shall mean any substance liable to cause pollution, in particular the substances listed in Annex VIII of Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council;
36. "wastewater" shall mean water which has had its natural physical, chemical, or biological properties altered as a result of human activities;
37. "person" shall mean a natural or legal person;
38. "operator" shall mean a person who operates or controls an installation or activity, or a person to whom such power has been delegated in accordance with the law;
39. "activity" shall mean any activity that has or may have an impact on the quality or quantity of waters, ecosystems, the morphology of a watercourse, or its use;
40. "interested party" shall mean a person whose right, interest, or obligation may be affected by the activity of another person;
41. "regulated flood plain" shall mean the land between the riverbed and the outer (water) edge of the defensive embankment;
42. "urban area" shall mean one or more settlements that represent a spatial urban unit or a spatially-functionally interconnected unit, established by the Spatial Plan of the Federation, canton, city, and municipality, in accordance with regulations on spatial planning.

**Article 6.**
**Division of surface waters**

(1) Surface waters include running waters and standing waters.

(2) Running waters are natural watercourses such as torrents, streams, and rivers, regardless of whether they have a permanent or intermittent flow.

(3) Running waters also include regulated watercourses, watercourses created by the displacement of a natural watercourse, or by the partial abstraction of water from a natural watercourse.

(4) Standing waters are coastal sea waters, natural lakes, including those that occasionally dry up, marshes, wetlands, and other natural water accumulations that have a permanent or intermittent inflow or outflow of surface or groundwater.

(5) Standing waters also include waters and water accumulations created by damming running waters or by other interventions in the area.

**Article 7.**
**Water estate**

(1) The water estate, according to this Law, is a set of land parcels that includes:

1. land on which surface water is permanently or temporarily present, and as a result, special hydrological, geomorphological, and biological relationships are formed, which determine the water and water-dependent ecosystems;
2. the main bed of running waters, including islands and banks up to a distinct geomorphological change;
3. a riparian belt 15 meters wide from the bank line (distinct morphological change) for Category I surface waters, or a riparian belt 5 meters wide from the bank line (distinct morphological change) for Category II surface waters;
4. land submerged by standing waters, including the bank up to the highest recorded water level;
5. abandoned riverbeds which are occasionally flooded by water, wetlands, and land submerged by water due to interventions in the area;
---
*(Page 7)*

6. the regulated flood plain;
7. land under and adjacent to water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law.

(2) Regardless of the provisions of paragraph 1, item 3 of this Article, the Government of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina (hereinafter: the Government of the Federation) may determine a different width of the riparian belt if it is necessary for: water protection, protection from the adverse effects of water, enabling the general use of waters, and the operation of competent institutions to which public authorities have been assigned under this Law.

(3) The water estate may be owned by the Federation, a canton, a city, a municipality, or a legal or natural person.

(4) In the case of the sale of a land parcel owned by a legal or natural person which belongs to the water estate, the Federation, canton, city, or municipality shall have the right of first refusal.

**Article 8.**
**Delimitation of the water estate boundary**

(1) The Federal Minister of Agriculture, Water Management and Forestry (hereinafter: the Federal Minister) shall issue a regulation on the manner of determining the boundary of the water estate from Article 7, paragraph 1 of this Law and on the procedure for determining the affiliation of a land parcel to the public water estate from Article 9, paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Law.

(2) The boundary of an individual water estate shall be determined, on the basis of the regulation from paragraph 1 of this Article, by the Federal Ministry of Agriculture, Water Management and Forestry (hereinafter: the Federal Ministry) for Category I surface waters, and by the cantonal ministry competent for waters for Category II surface waters.

(3) The boundary of the water estate shall be recorded in the land cadastre.

**Article 9.**
**Public water estate**

(1) The public water estate consists of the land parcels from Article 7, paragraph 1 of this Law, which until the date of entry into force of this Law were registered in the land registers as public good, state property, or property of a city or municipality, or which by virtue of law became state property.

(2) The public water estate shall be deemed to include, until proven otherwise, those land parcels from Article 7, paragraph 1 of this Law which, until the date of entry into force of this Law, were not registered in the land registers, or which are registered in the land registers, but no one is listed as their owner.

(3) The public water estate defined in paragraph 1 of this Article is a good of general interest and:

1. is owned by the Federation for all Category I surface waters within the meaning of this Law,
2. is owned by the city and municipalities for all Category II surface waters within the meaning of this Law, unless otherwise regulated by a special cantonal regulation.

(4) The powers of managing the public water estate shall be exercised by government bodies, legal persons, and other institutions, to the extent and in the manner prescribed by this Law or other acts of the competent bodies to which the right of managing the public water estate has been transferred.

**Article 10.**

(1) A decision of the Federal Minister shall determine the affiliation of the water estate to the public water estate for Category I surface waters, in accordance with the regulation from Article 8, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(2) A decision of the cantonal minister competent for waters shall determine the affiliation of the water estate to the public water estate for Category II surface waters, in accordance with the regulation from Article 8, paragraph 1 of this Law.
---
*(Page 8)*

(3) The public water estate may not be the subject of commerce, but may, under special conditions determined by this Law or by a by-law based on the law, be the subject of limited rights of use in the form of a lease.

(4) The Federal Minister shall issue a regulation on the manner and conditions of the limited rights of use of the public water estate from paragraph 3 of this Article.

**Article 11.**

(1) A land parcel shall cease to be part of the public water estate under the following conditions:

1. if a decision of the competent minister from Article 10, paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Law determines that this parcel no longer meets the conditions of the water estate from Article 7, paragraph 1 of this Law;
2. by the adoption of a decision by the Government of the Federation, which determines in another way, for special general needs and purposes, the use of that public water estate.

(2) The status of the public water estate shall cease on the date of entry of the change of status in the land registers, on the basis of the adopted decision or ruling from paragraph 1 of this Article.

**Article 12.**
**Interventions on the water estate**

(1) On the water estate, it is not permitted to carry out interventions, except for:

1. the construction of public infrastructure facilities (roads, railways, bridges, pipelines, etc.);
2. the construction of protective water facilities;
3. interventions related to the improvement of the hydro-morphological and biological properties of surface waters;
4. interventions related to nature protection;
5. the construction of facilities necessary for the use of waters (facilities for the abstraction and accumulation of water) and facilities for ensuring navigation and protection from drowning at natural swimming areas;
6. the construction of facilities intended for the protection of waters from pollution;
7. the construction of facilities necessary for the work of state bodies, for the protection and rescue of people, animals, or property from natural and other disasters, and for carrying out police duties.

(2) The interventions from paragraph 1 of this Article are subject to the conditions prescribed by this Law and by regulations on construction.

**Article 13.**
**Water facilities**

Water facilities are facilities or sets of facilities which, together with their associated devices, form a technical or technological unit and serve for: protection from the adverse effects of water, abstraction of water for their intended use, and protection of waters from pollution.

**Article 14.**
**Types of facilities according to purpose**

(1) Water facilities, according to their purpose, are:
---
*(Page 9)*

1. protective facilities - embankments, bank revetments, regulated watercourse beds, drainage channels, perimeter (lateral) channels for protection from external waters, drainage tunnels, dams with accumulations, weirs, retentions, pumping stations for flood defense and other appurtenant facilities, as well as facilities for protection against erosion and torrents;
2. drainage facilities - main and detailed drainage channel network, pumping stations for drainage, and other appurtenant facilities;
3. facilities for the exploitation of waters for:
    - water supply (except for general water use) - dams and accumulations, water intakes, wells, spring catchments with corresponding equipment, water purification plants for drinking, reservoirs and pipelines, and other appurtenant facilities,
    - water supply for economic entities - dams and accumulations, water intakes, wells, spring catchments with corresponding equipment and pipelines, and other appurtenant facilities,
    - irrigation - dams and accumulations, supply channels and tunnels, water intake structures, pumping stations, weirs, supply and distribution networks, and other appurtenant facilities,
    - use of water power - dams and accumulations, water intakes, supply and discharge facilities, and other appurtenant facilities and equipment,
    - navigation - navigable ways, locks and weirs, and other appurtenant facilities and equipment,
    - other purposes - facilities for fish farming, recreational pools, lakes, etc.;
4. facilities for the protection of waters from pollution (hereinafter: water protection facilities) - collectors for the reception and transport of wastewater, wastewater treatment plants, outfalls into the recipient, and other appurtenant facilities and equipment.

(2) Water facilities from paragraph 1 of this Article may have multiple purposes simultaneously - multi-purpose water facilities.

**Article 15.**
**Ownership of facilities**

(1) Protective water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law on watercourses belonging to Category I surface waters are the property of the Federation, with the exception of regulated beds in urban areas.

(2) The Federation shall transfer the right of management and use of the water facilities from paragraph 1 of this Article to the competent water agencies from Article 152 of this Law, to the extent and in the manner prescribed by this Law or other acts of the Federation.

(3) Protective water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law on watercourses belonging to Category II surface waters and regulated beds in urban areas on Category I surface waters are the property of the city and municipality, unless otherwise determined by cantonal regulation.

(4) The owners of water facilities from paragraph 3 of this Article may transfer the right of management and use to the competent government bodies, legal persons, or other institutions, to the extent and in the manner prescribed by this Law or other acts of the city and municipality.

**Article 16.**

(1) Water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 2 and item 3, sub-items 2, 3, and 6 of this Law which were built with state funds are the property of the canton in whose territory they are located.

(2) The owners of water facilities from paragraph 1 of this Article may transfer the right of management and use to legal persons.

(3) Water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 2 and item 3, sub-items 2, 3, and 6 of this Law which were built by legal or natural persons for their own needs are their property, and they are responsible for the management of these facilities.
---
*(Page 10)*

**Article 17.**

(1) Water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 3, sub-item 1 and item 4 of this Law are the property of the municipality or city, unless otherwise determined by cantonal regulation.

(2) The owners of water facilities from paragraph 1 of this Article may transfer the right of management and use to legal persons established in accordance with regulations on communal activities.

(3) Water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 3, sub-item 1 and item 4 of this Law which were built by legal or natural persons for their own needs are their property, and they are responsible for the management of these facilities.

**Article 18.**

Water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 3, sub-item 4 of this Law are the property of the legal or natural persons who built these facilities, and they are responsible for the management of these facilities.

**Article 19.**

(1) Water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 3, sub-item 5 of this Law are the property of the Federation.

(2) The owner of the water facility from paragraph 1 of this Article may transfer the right of management and use to a legal person or another institution, to the extent and in the manner prescribed by law.

**Article 20.**

(1) The owners or users of water facilities from Articles 15, 16, 17, 18, and 19 of this Law are obliged to maintain the facilities in a functional state and use them in accordance with their nature and purpose.

(2) The owner or user of the water facility from paragraph 1 of this Article is obliged, in the case of cessation of use of that facility, to act in accordance with the water act, that is, to maintain the facility in a state that will not cause damage to the water regime and third parties.

(3) If the owner or user does not maintain the water facility in a functional state and does not use it in the manner specified in paragraph 1 of this Article, the federal water inspection from Article 183, paragraph 1 of this Law, or the cantonal water inspection from Article 198 of this Law shall order the necessary measures by a decision.

(4) The owner or user of the water facility is obliged to maintain and use the water facility in accordance with the general act on the maintenance, use, and observation of the water facility and the procedure in case of failure or accident at the facility.

(5) The owner or user of the water facility is obliged to ensure the protection of the water facilities and the devices on them from accidental or intentional damage or destruction.

(6) The Federal Minister shall issue a regulation on the content of the general act from paragraph 4 of this Article.

(7) The user of water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law is not liable for damages to third parties caused by the non-construction or insufficient capacity of the water facilities.

**IV. WATER MANAGEMENT**

**Article 21.**
**Water management**

Bosnia and Herzegovina, the Federation, the canton, the city, and the municipality are competent for water management, in the manner established by this Law.
---
*(Page 11)*

**Article 22.**
**Objectives of water management**

The objectives of water management are to achieve a good status, or good ecological potential of surface and groundwaters, or water and water-related ecosystems, to reduce damages caused by various adverse effects of water, to ensure the necessary quantities of water of appropriate quality for various purposes, and to promote the sustainable use of waters, taking into account the long-term protection of available sources and their quality.

**1. Territorial basis of water management**

**Article 23.**
**Water areas**

(1) The basic territorial unit for water management is the water area.

(2) For the purpose of water management in the territory of Bosnia and Herzegovina, or the Federation, the following water areas are established:

1. The Sava Water Area and
2. The Adriatic Sea Water Area.

(3) The Sava Water Area includes part of the international Danube river basin (part of the international Sava sub-basin) in the territory of Bosnia and Herzegovina, or the Federation.

(4) The Adriatic Sea Water Area includes parts of the international river basins of the Neretva with the Trebišnjica, the Cetina, and the Krka in the territory of Bosnia and Herzegovina, or the Federation.

(5) The boundaries of the river basins and water areas in the territory of the Federation shall be determined by the Government of the Federation, upon the proposal of the Federal Ministry.

**2. Strategy and plans for water management**

**Article 24.**
**Water management strategy**

(1) Water management policy is determined by the water management strategy.

(2) The Federal Ministry shall prepare the draft Water Management Strategy with the consent of the federal ministry competent for the environment.

(3) The Water Management Strategy shall contain in particular:

1. an assessment of the state in the area of water management,
2. the objectives and directions of water protection, protection from the adverse effects of water, and sustainable water use,
3. priorities for achieving the objectives of water management,
4. an assessment of the necessary funds for the implementation of the program and the deadlines for achieving the objectives,
5. the necessary activities for the implementation of obligations from international treaties that relate to water management.

(4) The draft strategy from paragraph 2 of this Article shall be submitted to the federal ministry competent for the environment, to the cantonal ministries competent for waters, and to the advisory councils of the water areas from Article 164 of this Law, for the purpose of giving comments and suggestions.
---
*(Page 12)*

(5) Comments and suggestions on the draft water management strategy shall be submitted within three months from the date of receipt of the draft.

(6) The Federal Ministry has the obligation to establish and ensure the coordination of all activities with the ministry of Republika Srpska competent for waters, in order to ensure the adoption of a unified water management strategy for the whole of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

(7) The Water Management Strategy shall be adopted, upon the proposal of the Government of the Federation, by the Parliament of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina (hereinafter: the Parliament of the Federation), or the Parliamentary Assembly of Bosnia and Herzegovina (hereinafter: the Parliamentary Assembly of BiH), for a period of up to 12 years.

(8) The first water management strategy shall be adopted no later than 2009.

(9) The Water Management Strategy is part of the Environmental Protection Strategy.

**Article 25.**
**Water management plans**

(1) For the implementation of the Strategy from Article 24, paragraph 7 of this Law, water management plans shall be adopted for the Sava Water Area and the Adriatic Sea Water Area.

(2) The water management plan from paragraph 1 of this Article shall mandatorily contain:

1. A general description of the characteristics of the water area, namely:
a) For surface waters:
    - maps of the location and boundaries of water bodies,
    - maps of ecoregions and types of surface waters in the water area,
    - identification of reference conditions for the types of surface waters;
b) For groundwaters:
    - maps of the location and boundaries of groundwater bodies;
2. A summary of significant pressures and impacts of human activity on the status of surface and groundwaters, including:
    - an estimate of pollution from point sources,
    - an estimate of pollution from diffuse sources, including a summary of land use,
    - an estimate of pressures on the quantitative status of waters, including abstractions,
    - an analysis of other impacts of human activities on the status of waters;
3. Identification and maps of protected areas;
4. A map of the monitoring network and a presentation of the results of the monitoring program which monitors the status of:
    - surface waters (ecological and chemical),
    - groundwaters (chemical and quantitative),
    - protected areas;
5. Water management objectives, in particular:
    - objectives for achieving good status and ecological potential of water bodies,
    - objectives regarding the regulation of waters and protection from the adverse effects of water,
---
*(Page 13)*

    - objectives for sustainable water use,
    - deadlines for achieving the stated objectives;
6. A review of the economic analysis of water use;
7. A presentation of the program of measures, including the ways to achieve the objectives from item 5, paragraph 2 of this Article, namely:
    - a summary of measures for water protection,
    - a report on the practical steps and measures taken to apply the principle of recovery of the costs of water use,
    - a review of measures taken to establish monitoring of all sources that are used or are planned to be used for public water supply with a yield greater than 100 m³/day,
    - a summary of controls on the abstraction and impoundment of water, including a review of registers and an indication of cases where exemptions have been made,
    - a summary of controls carried out for point source discharges and other activities that affect the status of waters,
    - an indication of cases where direct discharge into groundwater is permitted,
    - a review of measures taken to prevent the pollution of waters in connection with priority substances,
    - a review of measures taken to prevent or reduce the impact of accidental pollution,
    - a review of measures taken for water bodies for which it is questionable whether the objectives from item 5, paragraph 2 of this Article can be achieved,
    - a detailed presentation of supplementary measures for which a need has been identified in order to achieve the set objectives from item 5, paragraph 2 of this Article,
    - a detailed presentation of measures to prevent marine pollution,
    - an estimate of the necessary funds for the implementation of the program of measures and the identification of ways to secure these funds;
8. A review of all more detailed programs and plans for water management relating to certain river basins, sub-basins, sectoral plans, problems, or types of water, together with a summary of their contents;
9. A report which includes a description of the activities and results of public participation in the process of preparing the plan;
10. A list of institutions and the method of obtaining the documents on the basis of which the plan was prepared;
11. A review of the international obligations undertaken by Bosnia and Herzegovina, which relate to water management and the manner of their implementation.

(3) The amendment and supplement to the water management plan, in addition to the content from paragraph 2 of this Article, shall also contain:

1. a review of all changes or additions to the plan since its entry into force,
2. an assessment of the degree of achievement of the objectives,
3. a review of measures that were foreseen in the previous plan but were not realized, with a statement of the reasons for non-realization,
4. a review of measures that were not foreseen in the previous plan but were realized in order to achieve the objectives.

(4) The detailed content and manner of adopting the plan from paragraph 1 of this Article shall be prescribed by a special act which, upon the proposal of the Federal Ministry, is adopted by the Government of the Federation.

(5) The act from paragraph 4 of this Article shall be harmonized at the level of Bosnia and Herzegovina in relation to issues for which Bosnia and Herzegovina is competent.

**Article 26.**
**Programme of measures**
---
*(Page 14)*

(1) The program of measures shall contain the basic measures necessary to achieve the objectives regarding water protection, water regulation, and protection from the adverse effects of water and water use.

(2) The basic measures from paragraph 1 of this Article are:

1. measures related to water protection:
    - determined by this Law and by-laws adopted on the basis of this Law,
    - determined by regulations on environmental protection and nature protection, which relate to water and water-dependent ecosystems,
    - which ensure the appropriate quality of water for the supply of drinking water;
2. measures related to the regulation of waters and protection from the adverse effects of water, namely on:
    - preservation and balancing of water quantities,
    - protection from the adverse effects of water,
    - determining the scope of construction of water facilities;
3. measures related to the use of waters, which:
    - are applied in the procedures for issuing permits for water use,
    - relate to the recovery of costs for water use and protection,
    - promote sustainable water use.

(3) The program of measures may also contain supplementary measures, if they are necessary to achieve a good status of waters.

(4) The program of measures shall also determine the priorities for the implementation of individual measures from paragraph 2, item 2 of this Article.

(5) If, on the basis of monitoring or other data, it is determined that the objectives from the strategy and water management plans for a particular water body will not be achieved, the Federal Ministry shall determine the reasons, review and verify the issued permits and concessions, review and adapt the monitoring programs, and propose that the Government of the Federation adopt additional measures, including the determination of stricter limit values for water pollution.

(6) The program of measures is part of the water management plan.

(7) The Government of the Federation shall report to the Parliament of the Federation every two years on the implementation of the Program of Measures from this Article.

**Article 27.**
**Deadlines for preparation and revision of the water management plan**

(1) The first water management plans shall be adopted no later than 2012.

(2) The water management plans shall be revised and supplemented every six years, in accordance with the procedure for the preparation and adoption of the water management plan.

**Article 28.**
**Preparation of the first water management plan**

The work plan for the preparation of the water management plans shall be communicated to the public from Article 38, paragraph 1 of this Law at least three years before the adoption of the plan.

**Article 29.**
**Special duties of the water agency in water management**

The competent water agency from Article 152 of this Law shall:
---
*(Page 15)*

1. prepare an analysis of the characteristics of the water area,
2. prepare a review of the impact of human activities on the status of surface and groundwaters,
3. prepare an economic analysis of water use,
4. establish a register of protected areas from Article 65 of this Law, as well as areas with special protection determined by a decision of the Government of the Federation,
5. establish a register of water bodies that are used or are planned to be used for the abstraction of water for human consumption,
6. prepare a classification of the ecological, chemical, and quantitative status of waters,
7. prepare a program and organize the monitoring of the status of waters,
8. prepare a water management plan and a program of measures.

**Article 30.**
**Environmental protection objectives in water management plans**

(1) The implementation of the program of measures established by the water management plans shall ensure:

1. the prevention of the deterioration of the status of bodies of surface and groundwater and the achievement of at least their good status;
2. the achievement of good ecological potential and good chemical status of artificial or heavily modified water bodies;
3. the protection, enhancement, and restoration of bodies of surface water in order to achieve the status from items 1 and 2, paragraph 1 of this Article, no later than six years from the adoption of the first water management plan;
4. the protection, enhancement, and restoration of bodies of groundwater in order to achieve the status from item 1 of this paragraph, no later than six years from the adoption of the first water management plan;
5. the implementation of progressive reduction of pollution of bodies of surface water by priority substances;
6. the gradual reduction of pollution of bodies of groundwater caused by human activity;
7. the harmonization of standards and objectives of water protection in protected zones, by which certain protected areas from item 4, paragraph 1 of this Article have been established, with international standards for such protection, no later than six years from the adoption of the first water management plan.

(2) The temporary deterioration of the status of water bodies shall not be considered a violation of the objectives of water management if it is the result of circumstances arising from natural changes or force majeure, or if this deterioration could not have been realistically foreseen, provided that all practical steps have been taken to prevent further deterioration of the status.

**Article 31.**
**Determination of characteristics of types of water bodies**

(1) In order to achieve and maintain good status or good ecological potential, the characteristics of types of water bodies shall be determined.

(2) The bodies of surface water in the water area shall be classified into one of the following types: rivers, lakes, transitional waters, and coastal waters, or as an artificial or heavily modified water body.
---
*(Page 16)*

(3) In each category of surface waters from Article 5 of this Law, surface waters in the water area shall be classified into types, in accordance with the methodology established by the by-law from Article 43, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law.

(4) For artificial or heavily modified water bodies, the same methodology shall be applied as for the types of surface waters that most closely correspond to the description of that artificial or heavily modified water body.

(5) The determination of the characteristics of bodies of surface and groundwater shall be carried out in accordance with the methodology established by the by-law from Article 43, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law. For the initial determination of characteristics, water bodies may be grouped.

**Article 32.**
**Classification of water status**

(1) The classification of the status of bodies of surface and groundwater shall be determined on the basis of the intensity of changes caused by human activities.

(2) The status of a body of surface water shall be determined by its ecological and chemical status, whichever is the worse.

(3) The ecological status of a body of surface water is classified as high, good, moderate, poor, and bad. The classification is carried out according to the reference conditions which are determined by the by-law from Article 43, paragraph 1, item 2 of this Law.

(4) The chemical status of a body of surface water is classified as good and bad. The classification is carried out according to the reference conditions which are determined by the by-law from Article 43, paragraph 1, item 2 of this Law.

(5) The status of a body of groundwater is determined by its quantitative and chemical status, whichever is the worse. The classification of the quantitative and chemical status of groundwater is carried out according to the by-law from Article 43, paragraph 1, item 3 of this Law.

(6) For artificial and heavily modified bodies of surface water, the criteria for determining the ecological status of bodies of surface water from paragraph 3 of this Article shall be applied, taking the one that is most similar to them. In the classification of ecological status, the characteristics for high ecological status will be determined as the conditions for maximum ecological potential.

**Article 33.**
**Artificial or heavily modified water bodies**

(1) In the water management plan, a body of surface water may be designated as artificial or heavily modified when:

1. the changes to its hydro-morphological characteristics, which are necessary for achieving good ecological status, have significant adverse effects on:
    - the wider environment,
    - navigation, including port facilities or recreation,
    - activities for which water is stored such as water supply, power generation, or irrigation,
    - water regulation, flood protection, drainage,
    - other equally important development activities;
2. the beneficial objectives which can be achieved by the use of artificial or heavily modified water bodies cannot, for reasons of technical feasibility or disproportionately high costs, be achieved by other means which are considered a better environmental option.

(2) The decision to designate a body of surface water as artificial or heavily modified, as well as the reasons for it, shall be specifically stated in the water management plans and revised every six years.
---
*(Page 17)*

**Article 34.**
**Phased achievement of environmental protection objectives**

(1) The deadlines from Article 30 of this Law may be extended in the water management plan for the purpose of phased achievement of the objectives, provided that no further deterioration of the status of the water body occurs, if:

1. achieving the improvements within the given deadline would be technically infeasible or disproportionately expensive, or
2. natural conditions do not allow for the timely improvement of the status of the water body.

(2) The extension of deadlines and the reasons for the extension are specifically elaborated and explained in the water management plan.

(3) The extension is limited to a maximum of two further revisions of the management plan, except when natural conditions do not allow for the achievement of the objectives in that period.

**Article 35.**
**Modification of environmental objectives**

(1) For certain water bodies that are more severely threatened by human activities or whose natural condition is such that achieving the environmental protection objectives would be difficult to achieve or disproportionately expensive, less stringent objectives from Article 30 of this Law may be established, if the following conditions are cumulatively met:

1. if, due to human activity, socio-economic needs, and the need for environmental protection, they cannot be achieved in another way, although it would be a significantly better environmental option, but it is irrationally expensive;
2. if the highest possible ecological and chemical status has been achieved for surface waters, with impacts that could not have been objectively avoided due to the nature of the human activity or pollution;
3. if the smallest possible changes to the good status of groundwater have been achieved, with impacts that could not have been objectively avoided due to the nature of the human activity or pollution;
4. if no further deterioration of the status of that water body occurs;
5. if the reasons for establishing less stringent environmental protection objectives are explained in the water management plan.

(2) The less stringent environmental protection objectives established in the water management plans shall be revised every six years.

**Article 36.**
**Exemptions from environmental objectives**

(1) Exemptions from the environmental objectives from Article 30 of this Law may be established in the water management plan when:

1. due to changes in the characteristics of the water body, it is impossible to achieve good status of groundwater, good ecological status of surface waters or, where relevant, good ecological potential of an artificial water body and where it is impossible to prevent deterioration of the status of water bodies;
2. due to human activities in the context of sustainable development, it is impossible to prevent the deterioration of a water body from high to good status.

(2) Exemptions from environmental objectives may be established under the following conditions:

1. if all practical steps have been taken to mitigate the negative impact on the status of the water body;
2. if the reasons for the modification and change are stated and explained in the water management plan;
---
*(Page 18)*

3. if the benefits for human health, safety, and sustainable development are greater than those that would be achieved by fulfilling the environmental objectives from Article 30 of this Law;
4. if the benefits, which are obtained by these modifications or changes, cannot be achieved in another way due to technical infeasibility or unrealistically high costs.

**Article 37.**

The application of Articles 33, 34, 35, and 36 of this Law to certain water bodies must not permanently exclude or compromise the achievement of the objectives from Article 30 of this Law in other water bodies and must guarantee a unified level of protection within the water area.

**Article 38.**
**Public consultation**

(1) At the beginning of the preparation of the water management plan, the agency for the water area from Article 152 of this Law shall inform in writing the Advisory Council for Waters from Article 164 of this Law, the canton, city, and municipality, and through public information media, the legal and natural persons who have their seat or residence in the territory of the water area for which the plan is being prepared, at least three years before the beginning of the period to which the plan relates.

(2) The written notice from paragraph 1 of this Article shall contain a call for cooperation, a draft content of the plan, and the deadlines for the preparation and adoption of the plan.

(3) Legal and natural persons from paragraph 1 of this Article, within one year from the date of the notice, shall submit to the agency for the water area written proposals and opinions on all issues related to the water management plan.

(4) The agency for the water area, at least two years before the beginning of the period to which the plan relates, shall present to the legal and natural persons from paragraph 1 of this Article a periodic report on the progress of the plan preparation, which particularly contains a description of the most significant issues of water management.

(5) The agency for the water area shall publicly announce the draft plan, at least one year before the beginning of the period to which the plan relates. At the request of legal and natural persons from paragraph 1 of this Article, the agency for the water area is obliged to allow insight into the documents on the basis of which the draft plan was made.

(6) Legal and natural persons from paragraph 1 of this Article may submit to the agency for the water area written comments on the draft plan, within six months of its publication.

(7) The agency for the water area, within three months of receiving the comments from paragraph 6 of this Article, shall prepare a report containing the adopted or rejected comments on the draft plan from paragraph 5 of this Article, with an explanation. The report is an integral part of the plan.

(8) The procedures and requirements related to public consultation in planning in the field of water management may be regulated by the regulations of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

(9) Public consultation in international river basins may be further regulated by international agreements concluded by Bosnia and Herzegovina.

(10) After the conclusion of the procedures from this Article, the agency for the water area shall prepare the draft plan.

The decision on the adopted plan is published in the "Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH".

(4) The agency for the water area shall present the draft plan from paragraph 1 of this Article to the persons from paragraph 3 of this Article, who may, within 60 days of the presentation, submit to the agency written proposals, opinions, and initiatives.

(5) The agency for the water area, before adopting the plan from paragraph 1 of this Article, shall inform the persons from paragraph 3 of this Article to what extent and in what way it has taken into account their proposals, opinions, and initiatives.

(6) The plans from paragraph 1 of this Article are adopted by the Government of the Federation upon the proposal of the Federal Minister, in accordance with the regulation from Article 25, paragraph 4 of this Law. The decision on the adopted plan is published in the "Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH".

**V. WATER USE**

**Article 44.**
**Water use**

Water use, according to this Law, includes:
---
*(Page 21)*

1. abstraction, pumping, and use of surface and groundwaters for various purposes (supply of drinking water, sanitary and technological needs, irrigation, etc.),
2. use of water power for the production of electrical energy and other propulsion purposes,
3. use of waters for fish farming,
4. use of waters for navigation,
5. use of waters for sports, swimming, recreation, and other similar purposes.

**Article 45.**

(1) Everyone is permitted to use water under the conditions determined by this Law.

(2) Water must be used rationally and economically. Every water user is obliged to use water in a manner and to an extent that preserves the water from waste and adverse changes to its properties (quality) and allows the legal right of water use to other persons.

**Article 46.**
**General use of water**

(1) Everyone is permitted to use water in an ordinary manner that does not require special devices and does not exclude others from equal water use (general use of water).

(2) General use of water particularly includes:

1. abstraction of water without special devices from watercourses and lakes for the basic needs of a household,
2. abstraction of groundwater (a well on one's own land) or water from a spring on one's own land, which is used for the basic needs of a household,
3. collection and use of rainwater for the basic needs of a household, which is collected on one's own land,
4. recreation on waters.

(3) The basic needs of a household from paragraph 2 of this Article are considered to be the use of water for drinking, maintaining cleanliness, and similar needs in the household. Such use is not considered to be the use of water for irrigation, nor the use of water in a technological process when performing economic activities.

**Article 47.**

(1) For any water use that exceeds the scope of general use from Article 46 of this Law, a water permit is required, in accordance with this Law.

(2) The use of water for supplying the population with drinking water, for sanitary needs, and for fire protection needs has priority over the use of water for other purposes from Article 44 of this Law.

(3) The water management plan may, due to specific conditions and needs, determine the right of priority for the use of water for other purposes, with the mandatory giving of priority to the purposes from paragraph 2 of this Article.

**Article 48.**
**Quality of drinking water**
---
*(Page 22)*

(1) The quality of water used for drinking (public water supply) must meet the conditions established by the regulation on the quality of drinking water, which is issued by the federal minister competent for health.

(2) A legal person performing public water supply activities is obliged to ensure the constant and systematic monitoring of water and to undertake measures to ensure the health safety of drinking water and the technical correctness of the devices, in accordance with the regulation from paragraph 1 of this Article.

(3) The control of water for the needs of water supply for drinking may only be performed by an authorized laboratory.

(4) The conditions that an authorized laboratory must meet, the content and manner of obtaining authorization are prescribed by the federal minister competent for health.

**Article 49.**
**Use of water from springs and groundwater**

(1) The use of water from springs and groundwater, except for the general use of water, may be approved only if water exploration works have been previously carried out.

(2) Water exploration works are considered to be works and tests for the purpose of determining the existence, extent, quantity, and quality of groundwater in a specific area.

**Article 50.**
**Obligation to keep records**

(1) Legal and natural persons who abstract and draw water, except for the general use of water, are obliged to keep records of the quantities of abstracted water and to submit data on this to the competent agency for the water area.

(2) The regulation on the content and manner of keeping records and submitting data from paragraph 1 of this Article is issued by the Federal Minister.

**Article 51.**
**Melioration systems**

(1) For the purpose of building and/or using a melioration system for irrigation and/or drainage of interest to multiple owners or users of land, a special legal person (cooperative, association, etc.) may be established.

(2) The legal person from paragraph 1 of this Article is obliged to obtain the right to use water.

(3) The legal person from paragraph 1 of this Article is obliged to provide financial resources for the maintenance and operation of the melioration system.

(4) A legal person from paragraph 1 of this Article, to whom the right to manage and use water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, items 2 and 3, sub-item 3 of this Law has been transferred, in connection with Article 16, paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Law, is obliged to provide financial resources for the maintenance and operation of the melioration system.

(5) The legal person from paragraph 1 of this Article may entrust the tasks of technical maintenance of the melioration system to a legal person registered for performing such activities.

**Article 52.**
**Obligation to report**

(1) A legal and natural person who, during mining works, tunnel excavation, other excavations, and soil drilling, encounters groundwater is obliged, no later than 48 hours from the discovery of the water, to report this to the water inspector, or the agency for the water area or the nearest police administration, which is obliged to immediately inform the competent agency for the water area about it.
---
*(Page 23)*

(2) The legal or natural person from paragraph 1 of this Article is obliged to allow the water inspector or an authorized person of the agency for the water area to take data and carry out the necessary tests to determine the location, quantity, and quality of the water, and to undertake the necessary safety measures, which are ordered by the water inspector or the authorized person.

**VI. WATER PROTECTION**

**1. Prohibitions and restrictions**

**Article 53.**
**General water protection**

(1) The discharge of wastewater into surface waters is permitted only in the manner and under the conditions determined by this Law and the by-law from Article 55, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(2) It is forbidden to discharge wastewater into a natural lake, fishpond, wetland, and other natural water accumulation that has a permanent or intermittent inflow or outflow of surface or groundwater, as well as into a water accumulation that was created due to the extraction or exploitation of mineral raw materials or other similar intervention, and which is in contact with groundwater.

(3) In a natural lake, fishpond, wetland, and other natural water accumulation that has a permanent or intermittent inflow or outflow of surface or groundwater, it is forbidden to use water in a way that could endanger its ecological or chemical status.

(4) The direct discharge of wastewater into groundwater is forbidden.

(5) The indirect discharge of wastewater into groundwater is permitted only in the manner and under the conditions determined by this Law and the by-law from Article 55, paragraph 1 of this Law.

**Article 54.**
**General obligation for drainage and treatment of wastewater**

(1) A natural and legal person is obliged to discharge wastewater into a public sewerage system or in another way, in accordance with the decision on the drainage of wastewater.

(2) The decision from paragraph 1 of this Article shall contain in particular: provisions on the manner of draining wastewater, the obligation to connect to the public sewerage system, the conditions and manner of discharging wastewater in areas where such a system is not built, the obligation of special disposal and removal of hazardous and other substances, and the obligation to maintain public sewerage systems.

(3) The decision on the drainage of wastewater in the area of a city or municipality is adopted by the city or municipal council. In the case that the same public drainage system drains water from the area of several municipalities, the decision is adopted by the body determined by the regulation of the canton.

**Article 55.**
**Limit values for wastewater discharge**

(1) The Government of the Federation shall adopt a regulation on the limit values of polluting substances in wastewater and other requirements for the discharge of wastewater into surface waters and the indirect discharge of wastewater into groundwater, as well as a regulation on hazardous and priority substances, upon the proposal of the federal minister competent for the environment.

(2) The Government of the Federation shall adopt the regulation from paragraph 1 of this Article only if the Council of Ministers of Bosnia and Herzegovina has not adopted such a regulation.

(3) Before adopting the regulation from paragraph 1 of this Article, the Government of the Federation must ensure, in consultation with the Government of Republika Srpska, its full harmonization with the corresponding regulation of Republika Srpska.
---
*(Page 24)*

(4) The government of the canton may prescribe additional (stricter) conditions than the conditions specified in the regulation from paragraph 1 of this Article, for waters that are under the competence of the canton.

**Article 56.**
**Prohibition of fertilisation and use of plant protection products**

(1) The Federal Minister, in agreement with the federal minister competent for the environment, shall issue a regulation on the rules of good agricultural practice that apply in areas where water is polluted with nitrates and plant protection products or in areas where there is a risk of such pollution.

(2) The Federal Minister, in cooperation with the federal minister competent for the environment, shall issue a regulation on the methodology for determining the areas from paragraph 1 of this Article.

(3) The Federal Minister may by his regulation restrict or prohibit the use of artificial fertilizers, manure, or plant protection products in the areas from paragraph 1 of this Article.

(4) The use of fertilizers or the use of plant protection products on the water estate is prohibited.

**Article 57.**
**Navigation restrictions**

(1) The Government of the Federation, upon the proposal of the Federal Minister, shall determine individual surface waters or parts of surface waters on which navigation by vessels that use petroleum derivatives for propulsion is prohibited.

(2) The prohibition from paragraph 1 of this Article does not apply to vessels that are in the function of rescuing people, property, and goods, nor to vessels that are in the function of carrying out police and defense tasks.

(3) The protection of coastal sea waters from pollution caused by vessels is regulated in accordance with special regulations that regulate the area of maritime transport.

(4) The discharge of wastewater generated on a vessel directly from the vessel is prohibited, except for unpolluted cooling water.

**Article 58.**
**Prohibition of vehicle washing**

It is forbidden to wash a vehicle (car, truck, and other machinery and equipment) in surface water and on land belonging to the water estate.

**Article 59.**
**Disposal and deposition of hazardous materials or waste**

(1) It is forbidden to produce, handle, store, and dispose of hazardous materials and waste on waters and the water estate.

(2) Waste, within the meaning of this Law, shall be understood as materials defined in the Law on Waste Management ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 33/03).

(3) As an exception to paragraph 1 of this Article, the federal minister competent for the environment may prescribe special conditions for the production, handling, and storage of hazardous materials and waste for the activities of ports, defense, and similar purposes.

**Article 60.**
**Transport of hazardous materials**

In the regulations relating to the transport and transshipment of hazardous materials on inland waters and at sea, the conditions for the transport and transshipment of these materials shall be regulated in such a way that the possibility of water pollution is excluded or reduced to the minimum possible extent.
---
*(Page 25)*

**Article 61.**
**Accidents/Incidents**

(1) If, as a result of a sudden event, failure, or for other reasons, a danger of water pollution has occurred, the legal and natural person in connection with whose activity or omission such a danger has occurred must immediately take all necessary measures to prevent or mitigate the effects caused by the incident and prevent the recurrence of the incident, and then, without delay, inform the nearest police administration, water inspection, the federal inspection competent for environmental protection, the agency for the water area, or the operational center of civil protection.

(2) Any natural person who notices that a situation from paragraph 1 of this Article has occurred is obliged to inform the nearest police administration, water inspection, environmental inspection, the agency for the water area, or the operational center of civil protection.

(3) The police administration and the operational center of civil protection, upon learning of the occurred pollution or danger of water pollution, shall inform the nearest water inspector, environmental inspector, or the locally competent agency for the water area.

(4) The competent agency for the water area or another legal person determined by the water management plan, immediately upon learning of water pollution or the occurrence of danger of pollution, shall take measures to eliminate or prevent the pollution, or take all measures to reduce the harmful consequences for the environment to the minimum possible extent.

(5) The agency for the water area may, for the purpose of carrying out the works from paragraph 4 of this Article, engage the nearest specialized and authorized legal person.

(6) The Federal Minister shall prescribe the conditions that the legal person from paragraph 5 of this Article must meet (personnel, equipment, etc.) and the manner of granting authorization.

(7) The authorization from paragraph 6 of this Article is given by the Federal Ministry.

(8) The costs of the undertaken measures (elimination of consequences) shall be borne by the legal and natural person whose action or omission caused the pollution or danger of pollution.

(9) The federal minister competent for the environment, in agreement with the Federal Minister, shall prescribe the procedure and measures to be taken in case of an accident.

**Article 62.**
**Environmentally acceptable flow**

(1) The environmentally acceptable flow represents the minimum flow that ensures the preservation of the natural balance of water-related ecosystems.

(2) The environmentally acceptable flow is determined on the basis of conducted investigative works and in accordance with the methodology for its determination, established by the regulation from paragraph 4 of this Article.

(3) Until the adoption of the regulation from paragraph 4 of this Article, the environmentally acceptable flow is determined on the basis of the hydrological characteristics of the water body for characteristic seasons, as the minimum average monthly flow with a 95% probability of occurrence.

(4) The Federal Minister, in agreement with the federal minister competent for the environment, shall issue a regulation on the manner of determining the environmentally acceptable flow. This regulation shall particularly contain: the methodology and necessary research, taking into account the specifics of the local ecosystem and seasonal flow variations, and the procedures for determining this flow.

(5) The costs of the necessary research shall be borne by the investor or user.
---
*(Page 26)*

**Article 63.**
**Extraction of material**

The extraction of material from a watercourse is permitted to the extent and in a manner that does not significantly change the natural processes, does not disturb the natural balance of the ecosystem, or does not promote the adverse effects of water.

**Article 64.**
**Reference laboratory and authorised laboratories**

(1) The reference laboratory is competent for monitoring the status of waters, verifying the results of the work of an authorised laboratory, and for carrying out tasks under the competence of the Federal Ministry, the federal ministry competent for the environment, the cantonal ministries competent for waters and the environment, and the agencies for the water area.

(2) Until the establishment of a reference laboratory at the state level, the tasks and duties of the reference laboratory may be assigned to one of the authorised laboratories.

(3) The monitoring of the quality of wastewater and effluent from wastewater treatment plants may only be performed by an authorised laboratory.

(4) Supervision of the implementation of the provisions on water protection established by this Law, the undertaking of measures, and the imposition of penalties for their violation may be carried out only on the basis of the results of the analysis of the reference or authorised laboratory.

(5) The conditions that a reference or authorised laboratory must meet, the content and manner of granting authorization are determined by the Federal Minister by a special regulation.

(6) The authorization for the work of the laboratory from paragraph 5 of this Article is given by the Federal Ministry.

**2. Protected areas**

**Article 65.**
**Types of protected areas**

(1) For the purpose of establishing special protection of surface and groundwaters, habitats of plant and animal species, or aquatic species, areas and water bodies may be declared protected areas.

(2) Protected areas are:

1. areas intended for the abstraction of drinking water,
2. areas intended for the protection of economically important aquatic species,
3. surface water bodies intended for recreation, including areas designated for bathing,
4. areas susceptible to eutrophication and areas sensitive to nitrates,
5. areas intended for the protection of habitats of plant and animal species or aquatic species, where the maintenance or improvement of the water status is an important condition for their survival and reproduction.

**Article 66.**
**Protection areas for drinking water sources**

(1) The area where a water source is located, which in terms of quantity and quality can be used or is used for public water supply, must be protected from pollution and other adverse impacts on the health safety of the water or the yield of the source.
---
*(Page 27)*

(2) In the area from paragraph 1 of this Article, the protection of the source is carried out by establishing sanitary protection zones, the size, boundaries, sanitary regime, protection measures, and other conditions of which are determined according to the regulation from paragraph 3 of this Article.

(3) The Federal Minister, in cooperation with the federal ministers competent for health and the environment, shall issue a regulation on the manner of establishing the conditions for determining sanitary protection zones and protection measures for the source from paragraph 1 of this Article.

(4) On the basis of the regulation from paragraph 3 of this Article and investigative works, a decision on the implementation of source protection is adopted. The decision shall particularly contain: the boundaries of the sanitary protection zones, sanitary and other conditions in individual zones and other protection measures, prohibitions and restrictions, sources and manner of financing for the implementation of protective measures, the names of the bodies and legal persons that will implement the decision, and penalties for the violation of the provisions of that decision.

**Article 67.**
**Sanitary protection zones in water management plans**

Sanitary protection zones established by the decision on source protection, as well as the area reserved for the establishment of sanitary protection zones for sources that are in use for which a decision has not been adopted, are entered into the water management plan.

**Article 68.**
**Decision on source protection**

(1) Sanitary protection zones and protective measures are determined by the municipal administrative body competent for waters in whose territory the source is located.

(2) The decision on the protection of a source whose sanitary protection zones extend over the territory of a single city or municipality is adopted by the competent city or municipal council.

(3) The decision on the protection of a source whose sanitary protection zones extend over the territory of several cities or municipalities within the same canton is adopted by the body determined by the regulation of the canton.

(4) The decision on the protection of a source whose sanitary protection zones extend over the territory of two or more cantons is adopted by the Government of the Federation, upon the proposal of the Federal Ministry.

(5) The decision on the protection of a source whose sanitary protection zones extend over the territory of the Federation and Republika Srpska, or the Brčko District, is adopted by mutual agreement of the governments of the Federation and Republika Srpska, or the Brčko District.

(6) The decision on the protection of a source whose sanitary protection zones extend over the territory of Bosnia and Herzegovina and a neighboring state must be harmonized with the international agreement signed by Bosnia and Herzegovina.

**Article 69.**
**Protected areas of inland water reserves**

(1) In addition to the protected areas from Article 66 of this Law, the agency for the water area may also determine protected areas of inland water reserves, regardless of their future purpose.

(2) The areas from paragraph 1 of this Article are determined on the basis of established reserves of inland waters which, with regard to their chemical, physico-chemical, and microbiological characteristics, are classified in the highest order.

(3) Upon the proposal of the agency for the water area, the Federal Minister, in cooperation with the federal ministers competent for health and spatial planning, shall issue a regulation for determining restrictions and prohibitions on the burdening of the space and activities that may threaten the qualitative or quantitative status of inland waters in the areas from paragraph 2 of this Article.

**Article 70.**
**Temporary protection**
---
*(Page 28)*

(1) For a water source of importance for the future supply of drinking water, the use of sources of mineral, thermal, thermo-mineral, or other groundwater, the agency for the water area shall propose the adoption of a decision on the temporary protection of the area where the source is located.

(2) The decision on temporary protection from paragraph 1 of this Article shall determine:

1. the boundary of the protected area,
2. the temporary regime of water protection in the protected area and in the individual protected belt,
3. the manner of financing the protection and maintenance of the area,
4. supervision of the implementation of the prescribed regime.

(3) The decision from paragraph 2 of this Article shall apply until the entry into force of the decision on protection from Article 68 of this Law.

(4) The competent body from Article 68 of this Law, upon the proposal of the agency for the water area, for the areas from paragraph 1 of this Article, shall implement protection measures in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 71.**
**Areas intended for the protection of economically important aquatic species**

(1) The protection of areas where economically important aquatic species are farmed is carried out by establishing special protection from pollution and other adverse impacts on individual aquatic species.

(2) The Federal Minister, in cooperation with the federal minister competent for veterinary medicine and the federal minister competent for spatial planning, shall issue a regulation on the manner of determining the areas from paragraph 1 of this Article and the protection measures in those areas.

(3) The proposal for a decision on the protection of the area from paragraph 1 of this Article is prepared by the legal or natural person who carries out the activity of farming aquatic species.

(4) Depending on the extent of the protected area from paragraph 1 of this Article, the decision on protection is adopted by the municipal administrative body competent for waters, or the body from Article 68, paragraphs 3, 4, 5, and 6 of this Law.

**Article 72.**
**Water bodies intended for recreation**

(1) A bathing area is an area for public recreation on water (swimming, etc.) and an area where bathing is not prohibited, which is traditionally used for that purpose.

(2) A bathing area is declared by the municipal administrative body competent for waters in accordance with the regulation from paragraph 6 of this Article.

(3) In the area of a bathing area, it is not permitted to erect permanent or temporary facilities or other obstacles that would prevent the free flow of water, the free passage over the water estate, or that would prevent free access to the banks and bed of a watercourse, a natural and artificial lake, as well as to the coastal sea water.

(4) The area from paragraph 1 of this Article must be protected from pollution and from other forms of use or phenomena that may affect the quality of water for bathing. Activities that endanger or could endanger the health or life of bathers must be prohibited.

(5) The bathing areas from paragraph 1 of this Article are entered into the water management plan and spatial plans.

(6) The Federal Minister, in cooperation with the federal minister competent for health, shall prescribe the criteria for the areas from paragraph 1 of this Article.
---
*(Page 29)*

**Article 73.**
**Areas susceptible to eutrophication and sensitive to nitrates**

(1) For the purpose of water protection, areas susceptible to eutrophication and areas sensitive to nitrates are classified into sensitive and less sensitive areas.

(2) The federal minister competent for the environment, in agreement with the Federal Minister, shall issue a regulation for determining sensitive and less sensitive areas, as well as protection measures, prohibitions, and restrictions in the sensitive area.

**Article 74.**
**Declaration of sensitive and less sensitive areas**

(1) The federal minister competent for the environment shall declare sensitive areas in the territory of the Federation.

(2) A sensitive area that extends over the territory of the Federation and Republika Srpska is declared by the ministry of Bosnia and Herzegovina competent for waters, upon the proposal of the ministers of the Federation and Republika Srpska competent for waters and the environment.

(3) A sensitive area that extends over the territory of Bosnia and Herzegovina and a neighboring state is declared by the body of Bosnia and Herzegovina determined by a regulation of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

**Article 75.**
**Obligations and restrictions of activities in sensitive areas**

(1) In a sensitive area, activities that may threaten the quantitative or qualitative status of water are restricted or prohibited, in accordance with the regulation from Article 73, paragraph 2 of this Law.

(2) The owner or possessor of land in a sensitive area is obliged to implement measures to protect the quantity and quality of water.

(3) Prohibitions and restrictions are periodically revised depending on the results of monitoring.

(4) The application of good agricultural practice is mandatory in sensitive areas. The Government of the Federation may adopt a decision on the application of this practice for the entire territory of the Federation.

**Article 76.**
**Monitoring in sensitive areas**

(1) Monitoring in a sensitive area is mandatory and includes the monitoring of water and the monitoring of activities.

(2) The federal minister competent for the environment, in agreement with the Federal Minister, shall issue a regulation on the manner of monitoring from paragraph 1 of this Article.

**Article 77.**
**Areas intended for the protection of habitats or species**

An area intended for the protection of habitats of plant and animal species or aquatic species, where the maintenance or improvement of the water status is an important condition for their survival or reproduction, is determined in accordance with the regulations on environmental protection and nature protection.

**Article 78.**
**Renaturalization of surface waters**

(1) For the purpose of improving the disturbed natural balance of surface water ecosystems, the Federal Minister, in cooperation with the federal minister competent for the environment, shall give the initiative for the implementation of their renaturalization.
---
*(Page 30)*

(2) The measures and methods of renaturalization are determined by the water management plan, and are implemented by the Federal Ministry and the federal ministry competent for the environment.

(3) Renaturalization may be urgent, necessary, or recommended, which is decided by the Federal Minister, in agreement with the federal minister competent for the environment.

**Article 79.**
**Restrictions for the protection of aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms**

(1) For the protection of aquatic and semi-aquatic species and their habitats when burdening water or the water estate, account must be taken of:

1. maintaining the conditions for their reproduction,
2. maintaining appropriate conditions in their winter shelters,
3. maintaining other conditions related to their preservation and development, including their replacement and enrichment,
4. preserving the achieved water quality objectives from Article 30 of this Law.

(2) The obligations from paragraph 1 of this Article are determined in the water consent.

(3) The federal minister competent for the environment may, in accordance with the rules on nature conservation, prescribe methods and conditions of protection for individual cases of burdening water, banks, or the water estate.

(4) The regulation from paragraph 3 of this Article may contain obligations to ensure passage for aquatic and semi-aquatic species.

**VII. REGULATION OF WATERCOURSES AND OTHER WATERS AND PROTECTION FROM THE ADVERSE EFFECTS OF WATER**

**Article 80.**
**Regulation of watercourses and other waters**

According to this Law, the regulation of watercourses and other waters is the maintenance of watercourses and the water estate, as well as other works which enable a controlled and harmless flow of water.

**Article 81.**
**Maintenance of watercourses, water estate, and water facilities**

The maintenance of watercourses, the water estate, water facilities, and systems includes in particular:

1. work on the maintenance of natural and artificial watercourses such as lining the bed and banks, cleaning, removing sediment and partial deepening of the bed bottom, mitigating bends without significantly changing the course of the bed, etc.,
2. carrying out earth and similar works on the regulation and maintenance of banks, earthworks in the flood plain, clearing and mowing vegetation, renewing and maintaining culverts and crossings,
3. maintenance of regulation and protective water facilities such as repair, reinforcement, and renewal of embankments, clearing, mowing, and work on the vegetative protection of the facility, replacement of damaged parts of the structure, etc.,
4. maintenance of water facilities for melioration drainage such as cleaning, technical and vegetative maintenance of the facilities and the belt along the facilities, earthworks on minor additions to the channel network, mitigation of bends and slopes, drainage works, construction of standard culverts and similar facilities that are an integral part of the channel network, their renewal, replacement, etc.
---
*(Page 31)*

**Article 82.**
**Improvement of the hydro-morphological status of surface waters**

(1) For the purpose of improving the disturbed natural balance of water and riparian ecosystems, or due to the poor status of waters, especially in an artificial and heavily modified water body, measures are implemented to improve the dynamics of the natural processes of waters.

(2) Measures to improve the hydro-morphological status include the renewal and re-establishment of the structure and shape of the water body, which affect the improvement of its chemical and ecological status.

(3) The measures from paragraph 1 of this Article are determined by the program of measures from Article 26, paragraph 3 of this Law.

**Article 83.**
**Planning and execution of regulation**

Interventions for the purpose of water regulation are planned and executed in a way that does not affect the deterioration of the characteristics of the water regime and does not significantly disturb the natural balance of water and riparian ecosystems.

**Article 84.**
**Obligations of competent levels of government**

(1) The Federation, canton, city, and municipality shall ensure the regulation of watercourses and other waters in accordance with Articles 5 and 9 of this Law, each within its competence.

(2) The Federation, canton, city, municipality, and a legal person shall ensure the construction, reconstruction, and maintenance of water facilities from Articles 15 to 19 of this Law, each within its competence, in the manner prescribed by this Law.

**Article 85.**
**Protection from the adverse effects of water**

(1) Protection from the adverse effects of water shall be understood as the implementation of activities and measures aimed at reducing or preventing the endangerment of people and material goods from the adverse effects of water and eliminating the consequences of their effects.

(2) Protection from the adverse effects of water relates to the defense against floods and ice on watercourses and protection from erosion and torrents.

(3) In addition to the protection from paragraph 2 of this Article, protection from the adverse effects of water also includes measures to protect and eliminate the consequences caused by extraordinary water pollution.

**Article 86.**
**Endangered areas**

(1) For the purpose of reducing the degree of endangerment of people and material goods and for the implementation of measures and activities for protection from the adverse effects of water, an area that is endangered is determined:

1. by floods (hereinafter: flood-prone area),
2. by erosion of surface waters (hereinafter: erosion-prone area).

(2) The areas from paragraph 1 of this Article (hereinafter: endangered areas) are determined by the Government of the Federation upon the proposal of the Federal Minister, taking into account the natural possibilities and conditions for the occurrence of the adverse effects of water, the number of potentially endangered inhabitants, and the magnitude of possible damage to land, facilities, and other goods.

(3) For the purpose of protection from the adverse effects of water, with regard to the degree of endangerment, the land in the endangered area may be divided into categories.
---
*(Page 32)*

(4) The Federal Minister shall issue a regulation on the manner of determining the endangered area and classifying the land into categories of endangerment.

**Article 87.**
**Flood-prone area**

A flood-prone area is an area along a watercourse that may be flooded during a flood event as a result of water spilling from the bed, regardless of whether the area is protected by protective facilities.

**Article 88.**
**Erosion-prone area**

An erosion-prone area is land that is permanently or intermittently under the influence of surface, deep, or lateral water erosion, and which is:

1. a source of sediment (erosion focus),
2. under the influence of torrents,
3. composed of soil susceptible to washing away,
4. under the influence of sea waves.

**Article 89.**
**Obligations of competent levels of government**

(1) For the purpose of protection from the adverse effects of water, the Federation, cantons, cities, and municipalities in the endangered area shall ensure, each within its competence according to the provisions of this Law, the planning of protection measures, the construction and management of protective water facilities, and in particular the construction of embankments, dams, barriers, facilities for the stabilization of the bed and banks, facilities for the diversion of internal waters, etc.

(2) The measures and activities from paragraph 1 of this Article are also implemented outside the endangered area, up to the level of the river basin, if they increase the degree of protection.

(3) The objectives of protection from the adverse effects of water must also be contained in other sectoral plans in accordance with the provisions of Article 42 of this Law.

(4) The measures and activities for flood protection are divided into the following phases: the planning and preparatory phase, the phase of active flood defense, and the phase of remediation of the consequences of the adverse effects of water.

**Article 90.**
**Scope of protection from the adverse effects of water**

(1) The scope of protection from the adverse effects of water and the necessary measures are determined by special plans: special plans for protection against floods and ice, plans for protection against erosion and torrents, and plans for protection against extraordinary water pollution.

(2) When determining the scope of protection from paragraph 1 of this Article, particular account shall be taken of:

1. the significance of the endangered area, the size of the settlement, the value of the infrastructure or land,
2. the natural and social conditions of the population in the endangered area,
3. the necessary measures for protection, including an assessment of their feasibility, the amount of costs, and the consequences for the natural balance,
---
*(Page 33)*

4. the sensitivity of the water wave to the possibility of sudden pollution.

(3) The Government of the Federation, upon the proposal of the Federal Minister, shall issue a regulation on the types and content of the plans from paragraph 1 of this Article.

**Article 91.**
**Observation, forecast, and early warning**

(1) The Federation shall ensure directly, in accordance with the regulations of the water sector and the sector for protection against natural and other disasters, the performance of activities of monitoring natural and other phenomena that are related to protection from the adverse effects of water, and in particular:

1. monitoring and forecasting extraordinary hydrological conditions and other natural phenomena such as floods, the occurrence of torrents and erosion, and ice on watercourses,
2. monitoring the condition of high barriers (dams) on the basis of data from the operational monitoring of water facilities or devices from Article 15, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(2) The agency for the water area is obliged to establish a system for monitoring and forecasting extraordinary hydrological conditions in the corresponding water area and to ensure the timely notification of the population in the endangered areas.

(3) The Federal Meteorological Institute is obliged to establish a system for monitoring and forecasting extraordinary meteorological conditions and to timely submit forecasts of such conditions to the water information system from Article 98 of this Law.

(4) The owner or operator of a facility of importance for protection from the adverse effects of water is obliged to carry out monitoring and to submit data on this to the water information system from Article 98 of this Law, in the manner prescribed by the water act.

(5) The monitoring data from paragraphs 1, 2, 3, and 4 of this Article are part of the system for monitoring, notification, and alerting, in accordance with the regulations on protection against natural and other disasters.

**Article 92.**
**Phase of active flood defense**

(1) During the immediate danger of the occurrence of high (flood) waters, the Federation, canton, city, and municipality shall ensure, each within its competence according to the provisions of this Law, the implementation of active flood defense measures.

(2) The measures from paragraph 1 of this Article are in particular:

1. measures in endangered areas and on protective water facilities which prevent the occurrence of more severe consequences of the adverse effects of water,
2. a continuous on-duty service of the legal person from Article 157 of this Law, during the period of danger and for the duration of high waters,
3. increased supervision of protective facilities and in endangered areas,
4. removal of debris to increase the flow capacity of the riverbed,
5. implementation of temporary measures such as the erection of a defensive embankment, backfilling, remediation of breaches, etc.
---
*(Page 34)*

(3) When implementing the active phase of flood defense, it is necessary to undertake the greatest possible measures to protect the protective water facilities and devices, as well as the greatest possible measures to preserve the characteristics of the ecosystems and natural values.

(4) The Federal Minister, on the basis of the regulation from Article 90, paragraph 3 of this Law, shall adopt a plan on measures and the manner of their implementation, for areas along Category I surface waters.

(5) The cantonal minister competent for waters, on the basis of the regulation from Article 90, paragraph 3 of this Law, shall adopt a plan on measures and the manner of their implementation, for areas along Category II surface waters in the territory of the canton.

**Article 93.**
**Manner of implementing measures**

When implementing measures of active flood defense or in case of extraordinary pollution, the competent government bodies, legal and natural persons must act in such a way as to endanger the quality of water as little as possible and not to damage the water estate and riparian land, water facilities, and other infrastructure, and to preserve the hydro-morphological characteristics of the watercourse to the greatest extent possible.

Article 94.
Remediation of the Consequences of the Adverse Effects of Water

(1) Remediation of the consequences of the adverse effects of water shall be carried out on the basis of a remediation program.

(2) If the consequences of the adverse effects of water have occurred on the water estate, riparian or other land, or on water infrastructure or another water facility and device owned by the Federation, the funds for their remediation shall be provided by the Federation.

(3) If the consequences of the adverse effects of water have occurred on a water facility or device intended for the special use of water and the water estate which is not owned by the Federation, its owner or operator shall be obliged to ensure its remediation.

(4) If the person from paragraph 3 of this Article fails to ensure remediation, and this is significant for the functioning of the system for protection from the adverse effects of water, the Federal Ministry shall ensure remediation at their expense.

(5) Regardless of the provisions of paragraphs 2 to 4 of this Article, the costs of remediating the consequences of extraordinary water pollution shall be borne by the legal and/or natural person whose action or omission caused the pollution.

(6) The remediation program from paragraph 1 of this Article shall be proposed by the Federal Ministry and adopted by the Government of the Federation within six months of the date of damage assessment and the preparation of the draft remediation program. The damage assessment and the preparation of the draft remediation program shall be carried out by a special commission for damage assessment, in accordance with this Law and regulations on protection against natural and other disasters.

Article 95.
General Prohibitions

On the water estate and riparian land, activities and interventions in the space are prohibited which may:

endanger the stability of the water and riparian land,

reduce the degree of protection from the adverse effects of water,

prevent the normal flow of water, sediment, and silt,

prevent the survival and reproduction of aquatic and water-related organisms.

(Page 35)

Article 96.
Prohibitions in the Flood-Prone Area

(1) In the flood-prone area, in addition to the prohibitions from Article 12 of this Law, all activities that may have a harmful effect on waters, the water estate and riparian land, and protective facilities during floods are explicitly prohibited, and in particular:

digging soil, planting trees and shrubs, driving stakes and other objects, and setting up ramps on a defensive embankment or perimeter channel and in the flood-prone area, unless otherwise determined by this Law;

constructing facilities on an embankment that do not have the character of a protective facility;

extracting materials (gravel, sand, stone, and clay) in the flood-prone area or in the defended area at a distance of less than 100 meters from the outer (water) toe of the embankment;

constructing wells at a distance of less than 50 meters from the outer toe of the embankment, digging (drilling) pits or parallel channels at a distance of less than 20 meters from the inner toe, or 10 meters from the outer toe of the embankment;

erecting fences and hedges and planting trees at a distance of less than 20 meters from the inner side and 10 meters from the outer side of the embankment toe, as well as constructing facilities (residential, commercial, and other) on the inner side of the embankment at a distance of less than 20 meters from the embankment toe and in the flood-prone area;

cultivating land at a distance of less than 10 meters from the embankment toe;

erecting barriers in the flood-prone area;

allowing livestock to graze on the embankment, perimeter, and drainage channel or moving livestock outside the designated crossing point;

damaging or destroying devices on the embankment (ramps, signs, water gauges, etc.) or devices for registering changes in waters or other signs;

unloading or leaving any material on the embankment and its protective belt and in the flood-prone area, except for material for flood defense (deposits of stone and earth);

damaging or operating without authorization accumulations and their equipment, pumping stations, weirs, channels, tunnels, gate towers and their appurtenant facilities, power lines, transformer stations, and communication systems;

using access roads to protective facilities without authorization;

changing the course of a watercourse and channel without water consent and a water permit or contrary to the conditions specified in the water consent and water permit;

introducing into watercourses, lakes, the sea, accumulations, and retentions, or depositing on the banks of watercourses, lakes, the sea, accumulations, and retentions: stone, earth, tailings, and other solid and liquid materials and substances;

planting trees at a distance of less than 10 meters from the line of high water reach of a watercourse and the sea, or 10 meters from the edge line of an accumulation or retention, a perimeter or artificial channel, except for protective forests;

erecting buildings and other facilities that do not serve for flood defense and that prevent access to the watercourse at a distance of less than 10 meters from the line of high water reach of one-hundred-year floods for all surface waters, the highest level of the coastal sea, except where the owner or user is conditioned to construct the facility by the prior undertaking of protective measures that prevent or reduce the harmful consequences of water;

(Page 36)

performing actions that may damage the bed and banks of a watercourse, lake, or the bed of a channel, the bed and banks of an accumulation or retention or a tunnel, or obstruct the free flow of water;

carrying out works in the vicinity of a watercourse, lake, channel, tunnel, accumulation, and retention, which could endanger the stability of protective water facilities or their use (digging gravel, sand, stone, etc.).

(2) It is prohibited, without the written consent of the competent water authority:

to plant trees and shrubs, cut trees and clear vegetation in the flood-prone area;

to cut protective forests or other trees and perform other works that may lead to erosive processes in the flood-prone area;

to moor a vessel to the banks of a watercourse, lake, or the sea coast for loading or unloading cargo and to deposit or unload materials or substances intended for loading onto the vessel on the bank of the watercourse, lake, or on the sea coast;

to operate vehicles on defensive embankments.


Article 97.
Prohibitions in the Erosion-Prone Area

(1) In the erosion-prone area, it is prohibited:

to carry out construction and other interventions in the space in a manner that promotes erosion and the formation of torrents,

to denude surfaces,

to clear forest areas that prevent landslides and snow avalanches, regulate flows, or otherwise protect downstream areas from the harmful effects of erosion,

to fill in springs,

to collect or divert collected waters over erosive or sliding land without supervision,

to restrict the flow of torrential waters, increase the erosive force of water, and weaken balanced relationships,

to extract, dispose of, or store wood and other materials,

to backfill with excavated or waste material,

to collect sediment from the bottom and bed, except to ensure the flow capacity of the torrent bed.

(2) The conditions for intervention in the space in an endangered area are specified in detail in the water acts, in accordance with the provision of Article 109, paragraph 2, item 3 of this Law.

VIII. WATER INFORMATION SYSTEM

Article 98.
Objectives of Establishing the Water Information System

(1) The general objectives of establishing the Water Information System (hereinafter: WIS) are:

the rationalization, integration, and optimization of decision-making in the water sector, through the process of using information and communication technology,

the exchange and consolidation of information internally - within the water sector, and externally - with external and international institutions.

(2) The specific objectives of establishing the WIS are:

improving the accuracy and reliability of information from the water management system,

shortening the time of information availability to the water management system,

establishing a basis for the continuous monitoring of changes in the water management system,

rational use and protection of water resources,

increasing the quality of developmental decision-making,

increasing intellectual capital,

promoting a positive image of water management, based on professional and responsible work.

Article 99.
Content of the WIS

(1) The WIS, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, contains two basic groups of data:

data under the competence of institutions in the water sector,

data under the competence of external institutions (civil protection, firefighters, police, etc.), which are of importance for water management.

(2) The basic group of data from paragraph 1, item 1 of this Article consists of:

the water cadastre,

the water book,

a record of issued concessions on waters and the water estate,

a record of inspection supervisions,

data on intellectual capital,

other documentation.

(3) The basic group of data from paragraph 1, item 2 of this Article consists of:

geographical data and resources,

demographic, sociological, and economic data.

Article 100.
Water Cadastre

(1) The water cadastre consists of the cadastres of:

waters,

(Page 38)

the water estate and water facilities,

regulation and protection from the adverse effects of water,

water use,

water protection,

other water documentation.

(2) The water cadastre contains data on surface, ground, and transitional waters, and coastal sea waters.

(3) Water cadastres contain temporally unchangeable and temporally changeable data.

Article 101.
Water Book

(1) All data on issued water acts and orders are recorded in the water book.

(2) The water book consists of a register, a collection of documents, and technical documentation.

(3) The water book is kept by the body from Article 139 of this Law, competent for issuing water acts.

(4) The cantonal, city, or municipal body competent for waters is obliged to submit a copy of the issued water act to the locally competent agency for waters.

Article 102.
Record of Concessions on Waters and the Water Estate

(1) The record of issued concessions on waters and the water estate contains data on decisions and contracts on concessions on waters and the water estate.

(2) The body that grants the concession from paragraph 1 of this Article is obliged to submit a copy of the documentation on the granting of the concession to the locally competent agency for waters.

Article 103.
Record of Inspection Supervisons

The water inspection is obliged to submit a copy of the file on the inspection supervision to the locally competent agency for the water area.

Article 104.
Organization and Management of the WIS

(1) For the purpose of optimal collection, use, and linking of the basic groups of data that form the basis for water management at the river basin level, the WIS is established at the level of water areas, for:

The Sava Water Area,

The Adriatic Sea Water Area.

(2) The locally competent agency for the water area organizes, establishes, and manages the WIS.

(3) The Federal Minister shall issue a regulation on the establishment and management of the WIS.

(Page 39)

Article 105.
Obligation to Provide Data for the WIS

Federal, cantonal, city, and municipal bodies and other institutions competent for waters, as well as bodies, institutions, and other natural and legal persons who possess data significant for water management, have an obligation to provide this data to the locally competent agency for the water area, free of charge.

Article 106.
Access to WIS Information

(1) The agency for the water area, at the request of the federal, cantonal, city, and municipal administrative bodies competent for waters and the agencies for the water area from the same water area of Republika Srpska and the competent bodies for waters of the Brčko District, shall provide the requested information from the WIS, free of charge.

(2) The agency for the water area, at the request of a legal or natural person, shall issue the requested information from the WIS for a fee, in accordance with the Law on Freedom of Access to Information in the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 32/01).

IX. WATER ACTS

Article 107.
Types of Water Acts

(1) For the purpose of ensuring water management and ensuring equitable access to water for all legal and natural persons, this Law prescribes the manner of exercising the right to water by issuing water acts. Water acts within the meaning of this Law are:

preliminary water consent,

water consent, and

water permit.

(2) Water acts are administrative acts and are issued in the form of a decision, unless otherwise prescribed by this Law.

(3) The procedure for issuing water acts is conducted according to the rules established by this Law, with the subsidiary application of the Law on Administrative Procedure ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", Nos. 2/98 and 48/99).

(4) The Federal Minister shall issue a regulation on the content, form, conditions, manner of issuance, keeping, and other issues of importance for the water acts from paragraph 1 of this Article.

Article 108.
Content of a Water Act

A water act shall contain in particular:

the name or title of the natural or legal person and contact information for the applicant and the holder of the right from the act, as well as a brief designation of the subject of the procedure,

a description of the place, type, scope, and purpose of the right from the act,

the period of validity of the act,

the deadline for the commencement of the activity from the act that is issued, which may be a maximum of three years,

the deadline for the completion of the activity from the act that is issued, which may be a maximum of five years,

(Page 40)

conditions for preventing or mitigating negative impacts,

the amount of the procedure costs,

information on the legal remedy.

Article 109.
Activities for Which Water Acts are Always Required

(1) A preliminary water consent, water consent, and water permit must be obtained, regardless of their impacts, for the following activities:

water abstraction in all economic branches and activities, and in particular for:

industry and energy,

agriculture,

water supply,

service activities that use water in the technological process,

tourist activities;

discharge of wastewater into surface waters;

indirect discharge of wastewater into groundwaters;

artificial recharge of groundwaters;

extraction of material from watercourses;

construction of facilities for the exploitation of hydro-energy;

permanent raising of the water level and, as a result, the flooding of normally dry land;

construction of flood protection facilities;

construction of transport routes (road and rail), including forest roads;

construction of bridges or other facilities over or in a watercourse;

construction or arrangement of waste disposal sites;

initiation of the procedure for issuing concessions on waters and the water estate;

for the transport of hazardous materials and products from these materials which, after use, end up in waters;

regulation of watercourses.

(2) In addition to the activities from paragraph 1 of this Article, a preliminary water consent, water consent, and water permit are also issued for activities that may:

temporarily or permanently degrade the quality of waters, or hinder the improvement of their existing quality;

adversely affect aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems;

increase the risk of flood or erosion;

significantly reduce the quantity of water, change the morphology of the watercourse, interfere with the use of surface waters for recreation, etc.

(3) The activities from paragraph 2 of this Article are defined in more detail by the regulation from Article 107, paragraph 4 of this Law.

Article 110.
Facilities and Activities for Which Water Acts are Not Required

(1) Water acts are not required for the use of water that does not exceed the scope of general water use from Article 46 of this Law.

(2) Water acts are not required for fire extinguishing and for undertaking urgent sanitary and other measures in case of general danger.

(3) Water acts are not required for facilities and activities that are not subject to the obligation of obtaining an environmental permit within the meaning of the provisions of the Law on Environmental Protection ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 33/03), and which secure water for performing economic or trade activities by connecting to the public water supply system and discharge wastewater into the public sewerage system, with the approval of the operator of these systems.

(4) Water acts are not required to be obtained for the construction of new and reconstruction of existing residential buildings that are supplied with water by connection to the public water supply system and discharge wastewater into the public sewerage system.

(5) The provisions of paragraphs 3 and 4 of this Article do not apply to facilities that, by their size and method of foundation, affect the groundwater regime.

(6) The operator of the water supply or sewerage system may not issue an approval for connection to the water supply system or the sewerage system to natural and legal persons from paragraphs 3 and 4 of this Article if the operator itself does not possess a water permit for that system.

(7) The content of the approval from paragraph 3 of this Article is determined by the regulation from Article 107, paragraph 4 of this Law.

Article 111.
Preliminary Water Consent

(1) A preliminary water consent decides on the existence of conditions for the realization of the right to water of the applicant, as well as the manner of exercising that right, and the conditions to be met by the documentation for the construction of new, reconstruction, or removal of existing facilities and other activities that are not considered construction, and which may permanently, occasionally, or temporarily affect a change in the water regime.

(2) The preliminary water consent from paragraph 1 of this Article is obtained in the procedure for issuing an environmental permit, an urban planning consent for facilities and activities for which an environmental permit is not required, and in the procedure for issuing a concession on waters and the water estate.

(3) For facilities or works for which a preliminary water consent from paragraph 1 of this Article has been obtained, and for which an application for the issuance of a water consent has not been submitted, the preliminary water consent shall cease to be valid upon the expiration of a period of three years.

Article 112.
Preliminary Water Consent and Environmental Permit

(1) An environmental permit for plants and installations for which it is necessary to obtain an environmental permit in accordance with the Law on Environmental Protection is issued on the basis of a preliminary water consent, for facilities for which this Law prescribes the obligation to obtain a preliminary water consent.

(2) The application for the issuance of a preliminary water consent from paragraph 1 of this Article is submitted by the body competent for issuing the environmental permit.

(Page 42)

(3) For plants, installations, and activities that, before obtaining an environmental permit, must undergo an environmental impact assessment procedure, the body competent for issuing water acts participates in the environmental impact assessment procedure, at the request of the body conducting the environmental impact assessment procedure.

(4) For plants, installations, or activities that must undergo an environmental impact assessment procedure, an environmental impact study shall be submitted with the application from Article 120, paragraph 1 of this Law, in accordance with the provisions of the Law on Environmental Protection.

Article 113.
Preliminary Water Consent and Concessions

(1) The body competent for issuing a concession for the use of water and the water estate is obliged, before initiating the procedure for granting a concession, to obtain a preliminary water consent from the competent body in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

(2) The preliminary water consent from paragraph 1 of this Article, after the procedure for granting the concession has been carried out, is transferred to the legal person who has been granted the concession.

(3) The legal person from paragraph 2 of this Article is obliged to obtain other water acts, in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Article 114.
Water Consent

(1) A water consent determines that the documentation attached to the application for the issuance of the water consent is in accordance with the preliminary water consent, regulations on waters, and planning documents.

(2) The approval for the construction of new or the reconstruction of existing facilities within the meaning of regulations on spatial planning is issued after the issuance of a water consent for the facilities for which this Law prescribes the obligation to obtain a water consent.

(3) The water consent is issued in accordance with the provisions of Article 120 of this Law, with the verification of the documentation for construction, which confirms that this documentation was prepared in accordance with the preliminary water consent. The verification is performed by entering the number and placing the stamp of the body competent for issuing the water act on that documentation.

(4) A water consent will not be issued if the documentation for construction or other activities is not prepared in accordance with the preliminary water consent.

(5) For exceptional cases, for which a water permit is not required according to the provisions of Article 115, paragraph 1 of this Law, an integral part of the water consent are the conditions for the arrangement of the locality and the subject watercourse section.

(6) The water consent shall cease to be valid upon the expiration of a period of two years from the date of its issuance, if by that date the construction permit has not been obtained and the works have not commenced.

Article 115.
Water Permit

(1) A water permit is required for all facilities and activities for which this Law prescribes the issuance of a water consent, with the exception of the extraction of material from a watercourse from Article 63 and the regulation of a watercourse from Article 80 of this Law.

(2) If, due to a change in technology or for other reasons, the scope and conditions of water use or the discharge of wastewater into surface waters are subsequently changed, a new water permit is required.

Article 116.

(Page 43)

(1) A water permit determines the purpose, manner, and conditions of water exploitation, the operating regime of facilities and plants, the manner and conditions of wastewater discharge, the manner and conditions of solid and liquid waste disposal, and other conditions.

(2) A water permit determines that the conditions specified by the water consent have been met and that a general act from Article 20, paragraph 6 of this Law, which is adopted before the issuance of the water permit, has been attached to the application for the issuance of the water permit.

Article 117.

(1) A water permit is issued for a specified period of time, and for a maximum of 15 years.

(2) The right to use or utilize water or to discharge wastewater, acquired on the basis of a water permit, may not be transferred to another legal and natural person.

(3) The issuance of a water permit precedes the issuance of a use permit within the meaning of regulations on construction.

Article 118.

(1) A water permit shall cease to be valid:

upon the expiration of the validity period of the water permit,

upon the user's renunciation of the right from the water permit,

by not using the right from the water permit for more than two years without justified reasons.

(2) The decision on determining the cessation of the right within the meaning of the provision from paragraph 1 of this Article is issued by the body that issued the water permit.

(3) In the case from paragraph 1, item 1 of this Article, the water permit may be extended at the request of the user.

Article 119.
Obligations of the Future Holder of the Right to Water Use

(1) A water act may determine that the future holder of the right to water use must, when planning, constructing, and operating the facility, enable the use of that facility for protection from the adverse effects of water.

(2) A water act may determine that the future holder of the right to water use must enable the use of the water facility for the general use of water or for the supply of drinking water, if this does not significantly worsen the conditions for the realization of his right.

Article 120.
Application for the Issuance of a Water Act

(1) A water act is issued upon the written application of the investor or user, the body competent for issuing urban planning consent according to regulations on spatial planning, the body competent for issuing an environmental permit, or the body competent for initiating the procedure for granting concessions on waters and the water estate according to regulations on concessions.

(2) With the application for the issuance of a water act, the necessary data on the locality, type, and scope of the activity intended to be carried out and the technical documentation that elaborates the specific activity are submitted. The type and content of the data and documentation are determined by the regulation from Article 107, paragraph 4 of this Law.

(3) Water acts are issued by the competent body from Article 139 of this Law.

(Page 44)

(4) Water acts regulating the use of transboundary waters or the discharge of wastewater into transboundary waters are issued in accordance with the procedures established by international agreements or conventions.

(5) The documentation submitted with the application for the issuance of a water act is prepared by a legal person registered for that activity.

(6) The Federal Minister shall issue a regulation prescribing the conditions and criteria that the legal person from paragraph 5 of this Article must meet.

Article 121.
Additional Requirements for the Applicant

(1) Depending on the nature of the activity and its expected impacts, the body competent for issuing water acts may require additional data and supplementary documentation.

(2) The body from paragraph 1 of this Article is obliged to request additional data or supplementary documentation in written form, stating the documentation and data being requested, and warning the applicant of the consequences that will occur if the request is not fulfilled. The requested documentation and data are to be submitted within the deadline set by the body conducting the procedure.

(3) If the investor, user, or competent body from Article 120, paragraph 1 of this Law does not submit the documentation within the deadline set by the body conducting the procedure, the application for the issuance of the water act will be rejected by a conclusion against which a special appeal is permitted.

Article 122.
Expert Opinion of the Water Agency

(1) The cantonal, city, or municipal administrative body competent for issuing water acts, in the procedure for issuing a water act from its competence, may request an expert opinion from the competent agency for the water area.

(2) The regulation from Article 107, paragraph 4 of this Law regulates the procedure for issuing the expert opinion from paragraph 1 of this Article.

Article 123.
Cooperation of Bodies in the Procedure for Issuing Water Acts

(1) If, in the procedure for issuing a water act, especially a preliminary water consent, the existence of reasons is established which indicate that a facility, installation, or activity that is planned or located in the territory of the Federation may have negative impacts on waters in the territory of Republika Srpska, the competent body of the Federation shall submit data and documentation from the application to the competent body of Republika Srpska and request the issuance of a preliminary consent.

(2) If the reasons from paragraph 1 of this Article relate to negative impacts on the waters of another state, the competent body from Article 139 of this Law is obliged, through the institution of Bosnia and Herzegovina competent for waters, to forward the data from the documentation to the competent body of the respective state for its statement.

(3) In carrying out the activities from paragraph 2 of this Article, the competent institution shall act in accordance with the conventions and international agreements ratified or signed by Bosnia and Herzegovina.

Article 124.
Notification of Interested Parties and the Public

(1) To ensure public participation in the work and the resolution of possible conflicts of interest, the competent body from Article 139 of this Law, before issuing a preliminary water consent, is obliged to inform the interested parties and the public in the river basin area about the application, through a notice on the notice board of the body competent for issuing water acts, by advertising in the local public information medium, and in the case of inter-entity impact, in at least two public information media that are available to the public and in the territory of Republika Srpska.

(Page 45)

(2) The public notice from paragraph 1 of this Article contains information prescribed by the by-law from Article 107, paragraph 4 of this Law.

(3) The competent body from Article 139 of this Law is obliged to set a deadline for interested parties and the public for a written statement on the proposed activities from the application, which may not be longer than 30 days from the date of the public notification, or from the date of delivery of the notice on the conduct of the procedure.

(4) The competent body from Article 139 of this Law may, according to its free assessment, organize a public debate on the issue from paragraph 1 of this Article.

(5) At the invitation of the competent body that issues the environmental permit to participate in the public debate, the body that issued the preliminary water consent shall participate in the public debate.

Article 125.
Statement of the Applicant

(1) The applicant has the right to make a written statement on the opinion of the competent bodies and on the comments and opinions submitted in the procedure of public consultation, interested parties, and public debate.

(2) The applicant shall submit the statement from paragraph 1 of this Article within the deadline set by the body conducting the procedure. Failure to provide a statement within the given deadline does not delay the conduct of the procedure.

Article 126.
Delivery of the Decision and Notification

(1) The decision of the issued water act must be available to interested parties and the public, at their request.

(2) The competent body from Article 139 of this Law shall deliver the issued water act to the applicant.

(3) The applicant and the interested party have the right to appeal against the issued water act.

(4) The competent body from Article 139 of this Law shall publish the issued water act in the manner determined by the by-law from Article 107, paragraph 4 of this Law.

Article 127.
Costs of the Procedure

(1) The costs of the procedure for issuing a water act shall be borne by the applicant.

(2) The competent body from Article 139 of this Law shall decide on the costs of the procedure from paragraph 1 of this Article by a special conclusion.

Article 128.
Additional Conditions in the Issued Water Act

The competent body from Article 139 of this Law may, in the issued water act, prescribe special conditions for the prevention or mitigation of negative impacts caused by the activity that is the subject of the act, for the obligation to protect the facility that is the subject of the water act from the adverse effects of water, and the like.

Article 129.
Period of Validity of the Water Act

(1) Water acts are issued for a specific period determined by the provisions of this Law. Depending on the technological complexity of the facility, a water permit may be issued for a shorter period than the time determined in Article 117, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(Page 46)

(2) If a water permit is issued for a period longer than five years, the conditions from the permit must be reviewed every five years. The body that issued the permit shall, ex officio, initiate the procedure for reviewing the conditions from the permit, with a request to the user to submit the necessary documentation within a specified period.

(3) If the user does not act upon the request of the body, the competent body from Article 139 of this Law shall revoke the issued water permit.

Article 130.
Amendments to Water Acts

(1) The competent body from Article 139 of this Law may amend a previously issued water act if:

the amendment is necessary to ensure the supply of drinking water from Article 47, paragraph 2 of this Law,

the impacts of the activity that is the subject of the water act are significantly different from those that were assessed during the issuance of the water act,

due to the introduction of more advanced technology, the impacts of the activity could be significantly reduced, without excessive costs for the user,

the amendment is necessary for the implementation of international agreements concluded by Bosnia and Herzegovina.

(2) The amendments from paragraph 1 of this Article may not be made by the competent body if they would prevent or significantly hinder the continuation of the activity.

(3) The application for the amendment of a water act may be submitted by the inspection body, the holder of the right from the water act, or an interested party. The body that issues the water act shall initiate the amendment of the act ex officio.

(4) The application for the amendment of a water act is resolved in the same procedure as the application for its issuance.

Article 131.
Temporary Restriction and Adjustment of the Right to Use Water

(1) To ensure the necessary quantities and quality of water and to preserve the natural balance of aquatic and water-dependent ecosystems, in the event of a natural phenomenon that causes a temporary shortage of water or the danger of the adverse effects of water occurs, the right holder to use water may be temporarily restricted by a special decision, or suspended, or an obligation may be established to adapt the exercise of the right to use water in a specific area by technical or other measures for a certain period.

(2) In addition to the restriction from paragraph 1 of this Article, the exercise of the right to use water may be temporarily restricted or temporarily suspended in a specific area if an ecological disaster occurs, greater damage to water facilities, or other events that cause a shortage of water or the danger of the adverse effects of water occurs.

(3) In case of restriction or interruption of water use from paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, priority is given to ensuring water for drinking and water for fire protection.

(4) If the holder of the right to use water does not respect the restrictions from paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, the right to use water is partially or wholly withdrawn from him.

(5) Due to the temporary restriction or temporary interruption of the exercise of the right to use water from paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, the right holder has no right to compensation for damages.

(6) Regardless of the provisions of paragraph 5 of this Article, the holder of the right to use water may claim compensation for damages directly from the person who caused the ecological disaster, damage to the water facility, or other event from paragraph 2 of this Article.

(Page 47)

(7) The provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article do not apply to the holder of the right to use water for supplying the population with drinking water.

(8) The body that issued the water act shall decide on the withdrawal of the right from paragraph 4 of this Article.

Article 132.
Order

(1) If, within the validity period of a water act, a circumstance from Article 131, paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Law occurs, the body competent for issuing the water act may, in order to harmonize the actions with the newly arisen conditions, issue an order.

(2) The order from paragraph 1 of this Article orders the user to perform a certain action, make an investment, or refrain from a certain action within a specified period in order to eliminate the danger of a threatening or already occurred disturbance of the water regime.

(3) The provisions on the decision in an administrative procedure shall apply accordingly to the form and manner of issuing an order, unless otherwise determined by this Law.

Article 133.
Issuance of the Order

(1) An order is issued exclusively by the body that issued the water act.

(2) A copy of the order is delivered to the federal or cantonal water inspection.

Article 134.
Objection to the Order

(1) An objection may be filed against the order within seven days from the date of receipt of the decision, with the body that issued the order.

(2) The objection does not delay the execution of the issued order.

(3) The deadline for adopting a decision on the objection to the issued order is 15 days from the date of filing the objection.

Article 135.
Revocation of the Water Act

(1) A water act may be permanently or temporarily revoked from a legal person.

(2) The decision on the permanent or temporary revocation of the water act is issued by the body that issued the water act.

Article 136.
Temporary Revocation

(1) A water act will be temporarily revoked when the user does not perform the action, does not make the investment, or does not refrain from the action ordered in the order within the specified deadline, and if, due to the non-execution of the order, an immediate danger to human life and health or a disturbance in the economy occurs.

(2) A water act may be temporarily revoked for a period of up to six months, during which the user's right acquired on the basis of the water act is suspended and during which the user is obliged to perform the action for which the water act was temporarily revoked.

Article 137.
Permanent Revocation

(Page 48)

A water act is permanently revoked if:

within the specified period, the user does not perform the action, does not make the investment, or does not refrain from the action ordered in the order, for which the water act was temporarily revoked,

non-execution of the order could lead to or has led to a serious and immediate danger to the life or health of people, or if a disturbance in the economy has occurred,

the applicant provided incorrect data that misled the competent body regarding the facts decisive for the issuance of the water act,

the conditions from the water act are significantly violated, despite a written warning from the inspection body,

the activity is not carried out for a period of two consecutive years,

the activity causes impacts that are prohibited by this Law, and these impacts cannot be prevented or mitigated by amending the water act in accordance with the provisions of Article 130, paragraph 1 of this Law.

Article 138.
Procedure for Revoking a Water Act

(1) The application for the revocation of a water act is submitted by the inspection body or the competent body from Article 139 of this Law, ex officio.

(2) The competent body from paragraph 1 of this Article shall immediately notify the holder of the right from the water act of the submitted application for the revocation of the water act. The holder of the right from the water act is obliged to respond to the notification.

(3) The holder of the right from the water act whose water act has been revoked may request the issuance of a new water act if they meet the necessary conditions established by the regulation from Article 107, paragraph 4 of this Law.

(4) The decision on the permanent revocation of the water act is delivered to the federal, cantonal, city, and municipal body competent for spatial planning and environmental protection.

Article 139.
Division of Competence for Issuing Water Acts

(1) The agency for the water area is competent for issuing water acts for:

abstraction of water in a quantity greater than 10 liters per second;

discharge of wastewater for settlements with more than 2,000 inhabitants;

discharge of technological wastewater into surface waters;

indirect discharge of wastewater into groundwaters;

artificial recharge of groundwaters;

extraction of material from Category I surface waters;

construction of a hydroelectric power plant for the production of electrical energy, when:

the plant is located on Category I surface waters,

the plant is located on Category II surface waters and has a capacity greater than 5 MW,

(Page 49)

two or more plants are located on Category II surface waters, with a capacity greater than 2 MW each and are located at a distance of less than 2 kilometers from each other;

formation of an accumulation which is:

located on Category I surface waters,

located in the territory of two or more cantons;

construction of facilities for flood protection on Category I surface waters, as well as the construction of facilities for flood protection on Category II surface waters that may affect:

Category I surface waters,

the water estate located along Category I surface waters;

activities from Article 109, paragraph 2, items 1 to 4 of this Law.

(2) The cantonal ministry competent for waters is competent for issuing water acts for:

abstraction of water in a quantity of up to 10 liters per second;

discharge of wastewater for settlements with up to 2,000 inhabitants;

extraction of material from Category II surface waters;

construction of a hydroelectric power plant for the production of electrical energy, when the plant is located on Category II surface waters, with a capacity of up to 5 MW;

formation of an accumulation which is located on Category II surface waters and is located in the territory of the canton;

construction of facilities for flood protection on Category II surface waters, if these activities do not affect Category I surface waters.

(3) The cantonal regulation determines the transfer of a part of the cantonal competence for issuing water acts to the city and municipality.

X. RESTRICTIONS ON THE RIGHTS OF OWNERS AND USERS OF LAND

Article 140.
Easements

For the establishment of the right of easement for the implementation of this Law, the provisions of the Law on Ownership-Legal Relations ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", Nos. 6/98 and 29/03), and the provisions on easements from the regulations on construction and expropriation shall apply.

Article 141.
Right of Passage over Land

The owner or user of the land is obliged to allow passage over the land to persons authorized to research, survey, record, or mark the water estate or waters, as well as to persons who perform works on the construction, reconstruction, or maintenance of water facilities or plants from Article 14 of this Law.

Article 142.
Decision on Temporary Occupation of Land

(Page 50)

(1) If the owner or user of the land does not act in accordance with the provision of Article 141 of this Law, a decision on the temporary use of the land shall be issued by the municipal administrative body competent for property-legal affairs, no later than eight days from the date of submission of the request.

(2) An appeal against the decision from paragraph 1 of this Article does not delay the execution of the decision.

Article 143.
Temporary Use of Land for Water Protection

(1) The owner or user of the land in the endangered area from Article 86, paragraph 1 of this Law is obliged to permit the temporary use of that land for the implementation of protection measures and activities from Articles 85 and 92 of this Law.

(2) The contractor of the works is obliged, when implementing the measures and activities from paragraph 1 of this Article, to affect the condition of the land as little as possible, and after the cessation of the danger from the adverse effects of water, to remedy the damage caused.

Article 144.

(1) If the owner or user of the land from Article 143, paragraph 1 of this Law does not permit the temporary use of that land, the municipal administrative body competent for property-legal affairs shall order them by a decision to act in accordance with the provisions of Article 143, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(2) An appeal against the decision from paragraph 1 of this Article does not delay the execution of the decision.

Article 145.
Use of Material for Flood Defense

(1) The contractor of the works, when implementing measures and activities of flood defense, has the right to use gravel, sand, earth, or other material from the land of the owner or user of the land on which the measures and activities from Article 92 of this Law are carried out.

(2) The contractor of the works is obliged, when using the land from paragraph 1 of this Article, to affect the condition of the land as little as possible, and after the cessation of the danger of floods, to remedy the damage caused.

Article 146.

(1) The owner or user of the land from Article 143, paragraph 1 of this Law is obliged to permit the use of gravel, sand, earth, or other material from that land for the implementation of measures and activities from Article 92 of this Law.

(2) If the owner or user does not permit the use of the land in accordance with the provision of paragraph 1 of this Article, the municipal administrative body competent for property-legal affairs shall issue a decision on the use of that land.

(3) An appeal against the decision from paragraph 2 of this Article does not delay the execution of the decision.

Article 147.
Restrictions on Ownership Rights and Compensation

(1) The owner or user of the land has the right to compensation for damage caused by the temporary use of the land from Article 141 of this Law, or compensation in case of the impossibility of complete remediation of the damage caused by the implementation of measures and activities from Article 143, paragraph 1 and Article 145, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(2) The owner or user of the land which is the subject of the restriction from Articles 141, 143, and 146 of this Law shall determine the compensation from paragraph 1 of this Article by agreement with the agency for the water area, the cantonal ministry competent for waters, or the administrative body of the city or municipality competent for waters. If an agreement is not reached, the competent court shall decide on the amount of compensation.

(Page 51)

Article 148.

(1) If, in the implementation of protective measures from Article 68 of this Law, damage occurs to the owner or user of a facility and other real estate, the investor or the owner or user of the water supply system is obliged to determine the method of compensation in accordance with general regulations for compensation for damage, as well as the sources of funds for compensation.

(2) If the investor or the owner or user of the water supply system does not reach an agreement on compensation from paragraph 1 of this Article, the compensation shall be paid in accordance with the regulations on expropriation.

Article 149.

The owner or user of the land located in the endangered area from Article 86 of this Law has no right to compensation for damages for the restrictions or prohibitions from Articles 95 to 97 of this Law.

Article 150.
Drainage of Stormwater and Drainage Water

(1) If significant damage or nuisance is not caused to a natural or legal person and if the drainage of water cannot reasonably be carried out in another way, the owner or possessor of the land for which there is a need for the drainage of stormwater or drainage water may:

build a channel or any other facility on land owned by a natural or legal person, with their consent;

drain stormwater or drainage water into a channel or other corresponding facility owned by a natural or legal person, with their consent.

(2) The natural or legal person who undertakes the activities from paragraph 1, item 1 of this Article is obliged to maintain the facility in a condition that does not cause damage to the land of another natural or legal person. The natural or legal person who undertakes the activities from paragraph 1, item 2 of this Article shall bear the costs for the necessary changes to the said facilities, as well as its corresponding part of the future costs of maintaining the facilities.

(3) In the case that the parties from paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article do not reach an agreement, the competent administrative body or court shall decide on the disputed issue.

Article 151.
Passage and Use of Land for Water Monitoring

(1) The owner or user of the land is obliged to allow passage over the land and the use of the land to the body competent for waters, the agency for the water area, the operator, or other persons engaged for water monitoring.

(2) In the case that the monitoring from paragraph 1 of this Article is prevented, the municipal administrative body competent for property-legal affairs shall decide on the disputed issue.

(3) The owner or user of the land from paragraph 1 of this Article has the right to compensation for damages. The amount of compensation is determined by agreement. If an agreement is not reached, the competent court shall decide on the amount of compensation.

XI. ORGANIZATION OF WATER MANAGEMENT

1. Water Agencies

Article 152.
Establishment of Water Agencies

(Page 52)

(1) This Law establishes agencies for water areas (hereinafter: water agencies) for the purpose of carrying out water management tasks which are placed under their competence by this Law and by regulations adopted on the basis of this Law.

(2) The water agencies from paragraph 1 of this Article are:

"Agency for the Sava River Water Area" and

"Agency for the Adriatic Sea Water Area".

(3) The abbreviated names of the water agencies are:

"AVP Sava" and

"AVP Adriatic Sea".

(4) The headquarters of "AVP Sava" is in Sarajevo, and the headquarters of "AVP Adriatic Sea" is in Mostar.

(5) The local competence of "AVP Sava" and "AVP Adriatic Sea" covers the corresponding water area, determined by the act from Article 23, paragraph 5 of this Law.

Article 153.

(1) The water agencies are federal public institutions that operate in accordance with the regulations governing the work of institutions, unless otherwise determined by this Law.

(2) The water agencies are legal persons with the rights, obligations, and responsibilities determined by this Law, other laws, and the statute of the agency.

(3) The water agencies are non-profit legal persons that operate through their own transaction account for carrying out their activities.

(4) The water agencies are registered in the court register.

(5) The work of the water agencies is public. The agency for waters informs the public about its work in a timely and truthful manner in the way determined by the statute of the agency.

Article 154.
Regional Offices of the Agency

(1) For the purpose of ensuring the efficient execution of tasks under the competence of the water agency and promoting the principle of proximity to the user, regional offices of "AVP Sava" are established for: the sub-basin of the Una River in Bihać, the sub-basin of the Vrbas River in Jajce, the sub-basin of the Bosna River in Zenica, and the sub-basin of the Drina River in Goražde, and a regional office of "AVP Adriatic Sea" for the basins of the Cetina and Krka in Livno and a regional office in Konjic for the upper course of the Neretva River with the Rakitnica and the middle lake course of the Neretva River.

(2) For areas with specific problems in the field of waters, the water agencies may establish other regional offices.

(3) The decision on the establishment of regional offices from paragraph 2 of this Article is made by the Federal Minister upon the proposal of the management board of the water agency.

(4) The regional offices from paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article are organizational units of the water agency. The tasks, duties, and scope of work of the regional offices are regulated by the statute of the water agency.

(Page 53)

Article 155.
Tasks of the Water Agency

(1) The tasks of the water agency are the permanent and uninterrupted performance of activities that achieve water management to the extent determined by the plans from Article 160, paragraph 1 and 2, item 2 of this Law and in accordance with the funds that are provided for these purposes on the basis of this Law.

(2) For the performance of tasks of public interest, in accordance with this Law, the water agency has prescribed powers, within which it issues administrative and other acts, and decides on issues significant for water management.

(3) Within the powers from paragraph 2 of this Article, the water agency exercises the powers and obligations of the Federation towards the protective water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law which are owned by the Federation.

Article 156.

(1) In addition to the tasks from Articles 29 and 155 of this Law, the water agency for the area for which it is competent shall perform the following tasks:

organizes, collects, manages, and distributes data on water resources in accordance with the provisions of this Law, including the establishment and maintenance of the WIS;

organizes hydrological monitoring and monitoring of water quality, monitoring of the ecological status of surface waters, and monitoring of groundwaters, prepares a report on the status of waters and proposes the necessary measures;

prepares a water management plan for the corresponding water area, organizes the preparation of technical documentation for specific issues of water management, and performs other tasks related to water management, in accordance with this Law;

prepares plans for the prevention and reduction of harmful impacts caused by floods, droughts, erosion of the banks of the water body, and organizes the implementation of these plans;

manages the public water estate from Article 9, paragraph 3, item 1 of this Law in the manner determined by this Law;

undertakes urgent measures to prevent or reduce the harmful impacts caused by incidental pollution and prepares plans for such measures;

issues water acts in accordance with this Law, for which a special organizational unit is established within the water agency;

provides expert opinions on requests for the issuance of water acts from the competence of the cantonal ministry competent for waters;

provides expert opinions from the standpoint of waters on documents from the competence of other federal and cantonal ministries that are requested by those bodies;

participates in the preparation of water sector policy and legislation related to waters;

promotes research work in the field of waters and sustainable water management;

organizes public awareness raising regarding the sustainable use of waters, water protection, and the protection of aquatic ecosystems;

participates in the coordination of activities on the preparation and implementation of water management plans with the competent organizations from Republika Srpska at the level of Bosnia and Herzegovina, or with the competent bodies for the area of the international sub-basin of the Sava River and the international river basins of the Neretva and Cetina;

(Page 54)

according to the decision of the Federal Ministry, carries out activities related to the implementation of projects financed by international institutions, or which are financed from the budget of the Federation;

conducts regular consultations with the agency for the water area in Republika Srpska, in the same water area, which relate to the tasks from paragraph 1, items 1 to 14 of this Article;

undertakes activities regarding the collection of water fees and reporting to the competent institutions on the degree of settlement of obligations by the payers of water fees, etc.;

performs other tasks prescribed by this Law and the acts of the water agency.

(2) The water agencies are obliged to harmonize the methodology and manner of executing the tasks from Articles 29 and 155 of this Law and paragraph 1 of this Article for the territory of the Federation, and to prepare the tasks from paragraph 1, item 10 of this Article jointly for the entire territory of the Federation.

Article 157.
Entrusting Professional-Technical Tasks to Third Parties

(1) The performance of professional-technical tasks from its competence, the water agency may entrust to authorized legal persons.

(2) The Federal Minister shall prescribe the conditions and criteria that the authorized legal persons from paragraph 1 of this Article must meet and the manner of issuing the authorization.

Article 158.
Statute of the Water Agency

(1) The water agency has a statute and other general acts.

(2) The statute of the water agency shall regulate in particular:

the scope of work,

management, direction, and supervision of the work of the water agency,

the internal organization of the water agency,

the powers and responsibilities of the director of the water agency,

restrictions regarding the acquisition, encumbrance, and alienation of real estate and other property of the water agency,

the purpose and manner of distributing, or using the funds of the water agency,

the public nature of the work, cooperation with state bodies and other institutions in the realization of the activities of the water agency,

the performance of professional, administrative, and other tasks of the water agency,

issues for which it is prescribed by law to be regulated by the statute, and

other issues of importance for the performance of the activities and business of the water agency.

Article 159.
Management Board of the Water Agency

(1) The water agency is managed by a management board.

(Page 55)

(2) The management board has five members. The president and members of the management board are appointed and dismissed by the Government of the Federation upon the proposal of the Federal Ministry, and on the basis of a conducted public competition, in accordance with the law.

(3) The members of the management board are elected from among experts in the fields of water management, public finance, and other areas, provided that one member of the management board is appointed from among the employees of the water agency and one member from the Federal Ministry.

(4) The management board is appointed for a period of four years. One person may be appointed as a member of the management board for a maximum of two terms.

(5) The president of the management board may not be from the same constituent people as the director of the Water Agency.

Article 160.
Competence of the Management Board

(1) The management board, with the consent of the Government of the Federation, shall adopt a framework plan and financial plan document for the water agency for a period of at least three years.

(2) The management board, with the consent of the Federal Ministry:

adopts the statute of the water agency,

adopts the annual plan and financial plan of the water agency,

adopts the report on the business of the water agency for the previous year,

appoints and dismisses the director of the water agency.

(3) The management board independently:

adopts the annual financial statement of the water agency,

adopts reports on business and statements on financial operations of the water agency during the year,

adopts general acts of the water agency,

decides on other issues determined by law and the statute of the water agency.

(4) The management board of the water agency is particularly responsible for the implementation of the established water management policy and the orders of the Federal Ministry.

(5) The manner of work and decision-making of the management board is regulated by the statute of the water agency.

Article 161.
Supervisory Board

(1) The work of the water agency is supervised by a supervisory board.

(2) The supervisory board has three members. The president and members of the supervisory board are appointed and dismissed by the Government of the Federation upon the proposal of the Federal Ministry.

(3) The supervisory board is appointed for a period of four years.

Article 162.

(Page 56)

(1) The supervisory board shall perform the following tasks:

analyzes the report on the business of the water agency,

reviews the annual report on business and the annual financial statement of the water agency,

reviews and checks the orderliness and legality of keeping the business books,

reports to the Federal Ministry, the Management Board, and the director of the water agency on the results of the supervision.

(2) The manner of work and decision-making of the supervisory board is regulated by the statute of the water agency.

Article 163.
Director of the Agency

(1) The water agency is managed by a director. The director represents and acts on behalf of the water agency.

(2) The director of the water agency is appointed and dismissed by the management board, with the consent of the Federal Ministry. The term of office of the director is four years.

(3) The director is elected on the basis of a public competition which must be published in at least two daily newspapers distributed throughout the territory of the Federation, which is carried out in accordance with the law and the statute of the water agency.

(4) A person with a university degree is appointed as the director of the water agency. More detailed provisions on the conditions that a person appointed as director must meet and the procedure for appointing the director are determined by the statute of the water agency.

(5) The director of the water agency is responsible to the management board for the work of the agency. More detailed provisions on the scope, powers, and responsibilities of the director are determined by the statute of the water agency.

2. Advisory Council of the Water Area

Article 164.
Establishment of the Advisory Council

(1) For the consideration of systemic issues of water management of importance for the water area, the consideration of different needs and interests, and the proposal of measures for the development and improvement of water management in the water area, the Government of the Federation establishes:

The Advisory Council of the Sava River Water Area and

The Advisory Council of the Adriatic Sea Water Area.

(2) The composition and number of members of the council of the water area (hereinafter: the council) are determined by an act on the establishment of the council, taking into account the characteristics of the water area, the number of cantons in the water area, the significance of different types of water use, and other specifics of the water area, in accordance with the following criteria:

the Government of the Federation is represented by at least five members, according to the competences and interests for water issues;

each canton from the water area is represented by at least one member. The total number of representatives of the cantons is determined in accordance with the area and the number of inhabitants of the canton, provided that the total number of representatives of the cantons in the council cannot be less than the total number of representatives of the Government of the Federation;

representatives of water users make up at least one third of all members of the council;

(Page 57)

non-governmental organizations from the water area are represented in the council by at least three members;

scientific institutions with headquarters in the water area that deal with the problems of waters, the environment, and other similar areas are represented in the council by at least three members.

(3) The act on the establishment of the council and its composition, in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of this Article, is adopted by the Government of the Federation upon the proposal of the Federal Ministry.

(4) The costs of the work of the council are financed from the Budget of the Federation.

(5) The Government of the Federation, upon the proposal of the Federal Ministry, shall prescribe the type and amount of costs from paragraph 4 of this Article.

Article 165.
Role and Tasks of the Council

(1) The council from Article 164, paragraph 1 of this Law:

participates in the preparation of the draft water management plan and the preparation of the water management strategy;

considers and provides an opinion on strategic documents from the competence of other federal and cantonal ministries, which relate to water management and vice versa;

considers and provides an opinion on issues of interest for the river basin or sub-basin within the water area, which relate to inter-entity cooperation;

considers and provides an opinion on issues of interest for both water areas, which relate to the use or protection of waters;

considers and provides an opinion on water management issues that are of importance for inter-entity cooperation, for Bosnia and Herzegovina as a whole, and the international obligations of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

(2) The council provides an opinion on all issues related to waters, which are requested by the Government of the Federation or the governments of the cantons.

(3) The council may propose to the Government of the Federation and the water agency study or research works that relate to water management issues in the water area.

(4) When performing tasks from its competence, the water agency takes into account the opinion and recommendations of the council.

Article 166.
Manner of Work of the Council

(1) The council adopts rules of procedure on its work.

(2) The council meets at least three times a year.

(3) The meetings of the council are attended by the director of the water agency.

(4) The council is obliged to ensure the possibility of attendance at the meetings of the council by representatives of the water agency from the same water area of Republika Srpska.

XII. FINANCING OF WATER MANAGEMENT

Article 168.
Sources of Funds

Funds for the performance of tasks and duties determined by this Law are provided from:

general water fee;

special water fees;

revenues from the lease of the public water estate;

the Budget of the Federation, the budgets of cantons, the budgets of cities and municipalities;

credit funds;

funds provided by a special law;

donations and other funds, in accordance with the law.

Article 169.
General Water Fee

(1) The payers of the general water fee are natural and legal persons registered for performing activities.

(2) The payers from paragraph 1 of this Article are obliged to pay the general water fee in the amount of 0.5% of the base which consists of the net salary of the employee in an employment relationship for an indefinite and definite period and the fee paid on the basis of a work contract.

(3) The fee from paragraph 2 of this Article is calculated and paid simultaneously with the payment of salaries or the determined fee under the contract.

Article 170.
Special Water Fees

(1) Special water fees are:

Fee for the use of surface and groundwaters which includes the abstraction of:

water for public water supply,

water and mineral water used for bottling water,

water for irrigation,

water for fish farming in fishponds,

water for industrial processes, including thermal power plants,

water for other purposes.

The fee from item 1, paragraph 1 of this Article is calculated on the basis of the quantity of abstracted water expressed in m³. The amount of this fee may be different, depending on the purpose and quality of the water.

(Page 59)

Fee for the use of water for the production of electrical energy obtained by using hydro-energy. This fee is calculated on the basis of the produced electrical energy expressed in kWh;

Fee for water protection:

which is paid by the owners of transport means that use oil or oil derivatives for propulsion. This fee is calculated on the basis of the amount of pollution expressed through the equivalent number of inhabitants (hereinafter: PE);

for the discharge of wastewater. This fee is calculated on the basis of the amount of pollution expressed through PE;

for fish farming. This fee is calculated per 1 kg of produced fish;

for the use of artificial fertilizers and chemicals for plant protection. This fee is calculated per 1 kg of produced or imported artificial fertilizer or chemical for plant protection;

Fee for the extraction of material from a watercourse. This fee is calculated per 1 m³ of extracted material;

Fee for flood protection:

of agricultural, forest, or construction land that is protected by flood protection facilities. The definitions of agricultural, forest, or construction land adopted in the tax regulations are applied. This fee is calculated per 1 ha of protected land. The amount of this fee may be different, depending on the type of defended land;

of residential, commercial, and other facilities that are protected by flood protection facilities. This fee is calculated per 1 m² of usable area of the facility.

(2) The payer of special water fees from paragraph 1, items 1, 2, and item 3, sub-items 2 and 3, and item 4 of this Article is a natural and legal person who is obliged to obtain a water permit or water consent for the corresponding activity within the meaning of the provisions of this Law. A natural and legal person from Article 110, paragraph 3 of this Law is not subject to this obligation, because in that case the payer of the fee is the operator of the public water supply or sewerage system.

(3) The payer of the special water fee from paragraph 1, item 3, sub-item 1 of this Article is a natural and legal person in whose name the transport means is registered, or the owner of a construction machine for which registration is not required.

(4) The payer of the special water fee from paragraph 1, item 3, sub-item 4 of this Article is a natural and legal person who produces or imports artificial fertilizers or chemicals for plant protection.

(5) The payer of the special water fee from paragraph 1, item 5, sub-items 1 and 2 of this Article is the owner of the land or facility.

Article 171.
Amount of the Special Water Fee

The amount of the special water fee from Article 170 of this Law is prescribed by the Government of the Federation, on the joint proposal of the Federal Ministry and the federal ministry competent for the environment, with the prior consent of the federal ministry competent for finance.

Article 172.
Exemption from Payment of Special Water Fees

The special water fee for flood protection from Article 170, paragraph 1, item 5, sub-items 1 and 2 of this Law is not paid in the following cases:

for real estate that directly serves for air, rail, and road traffic, ensuring health and social protection, education, culture, cemeteries, or religious purposes;

(Page 60)

for agricultural and forest land, if the owner of the land is a parent, spouse, or minor child of a deceased veteran, as well as a natural person in a state of social need, provided that the land is not leased to another natural or legal person, which is decided by the federal body competent for determining tax obligations;

for protective water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law, water facilities for drainage from Article 14, paragraph 1, item 2 of this Law, and the public water estate from Article 9 of this Law.

Article 173.
Temporary Exemption from Payment of Special Water Fee

(1) The payer of the special water fee from Article 170, paragraph 1, item 3, sub-item 2 of this Law may, in special cases, be fully or partially exempted from paying the special water fee.

(2) The payer from paragraph 1 of this Article is fully exempted from paying the special water fee if:

they independently treat wastewater in a way that meets the conditions on the limit values of polluting substances from Article 55 of this Law and if the sludge from the treatment plant is disposed of in the prescribed manner,

they are able to prove, with an appropriate technical study, that they ensure a reduction in the emission of polluting substances below the value determined by the regulation from Article 55 of this Law.

(3) The payer from paragraph 1 of this Article is partially exempted from paying the special water fee if they use these funds to invest in works to reduce the emission of polluting substances.

(4) The exemption from payment of the fee from paragraph 3 of this Article is granted up to a maximum of 40% of the amount of the annual special water fee, when the payer is able to prove that they have started works to reduce the emission of polluting substances.

(5) In case of non-fulfillment of the undertaken obligations from paragraph 4 of this Article, the payer is obliged to pay the full amount of the special water fee within six months.

(6) The payer from paragraph 5 of this Article has no right to submit an application for exemption from payment of the special water fee in the next two years.

(7) The Government of the Federation, upon the proposal of the Federal Minister and the federal minister competent for the environment, shall issue a regulation on the conditions for exemption from payment of the special water fee, which shall regulate in detail the conditions, procedure, deadlines, and measures in cases of non-fulfillment of obligations to reduce the emission of polluting substances.

(8) The Federal Ministry shall decide on the exemption from payment of the special water fee, in accordance with the regulation from paragraph 7 of this Article.

Article 174.
Collection of Water Fees

(1) The manner of calculation, the procedure and deadlines for calculation and payment, as well as the control of the settlement of obligations on the basis of the general water fee and special water fees are prescribed by the Federal Minister in cooperation with the federal minister of finance.

(2) The funds from leasing the public water estate are realized on the basis of a lease agreement and are paid to the payment account opened in accordance with a special regulation for the payment of public revenues.

Article 175.
Interest on Late Payments

(Page 61)

Interest on late payments on the basis of the general water fee, special water fees, and revenues from leasing the public water estate are calculated and paid in accordance with the Law on the Amount of the Default Interest Rate on Public Revenues ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", Nos. 48/01, 52/01 and 42/06).

Article 176.
Payment Order

The collection of water fees from Articles 169, 170, and 175 of this Law and revenues from the lease of the public water estate is carried out by a payment order for the payment of public revenues, to the payment accounts opened in accordance with the special regulation for the payment of public revenues issued by the Federal Ministry of Finance.

Article 177.
Distribution of Water Fees

(1) The water fees from Articles 169 and 170 of this Law and the revenues collected on the basis of the lease of the public water estate on Category I surface waters are distributed in the following manner:

to the competent water agency 40%,

for the benefit of the cantonal budget 45% and

for the benefit of the Fund for Environmental Protection of the Federation 15%.

(2) The revenues from the lease of the public water estate on Category II surface waters belong entirely to the budget of the canton.

(3) For the use of the funds from paragraph 1, item 2 and paragraph 2 of this Article, the cantons are obliged to establish an institution by the regulation from Article 167, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(4) The distribution of the funds from paragraph 1 of this Article may be changed. The Government of the Federation shall decide on the need to change the distribution every two years, on the harmonized proposal of the Federal Ministry, the federal ministry competent for the environment, and the cantonal ministries competent for waters.

Article 178.
Use of Revenues

(1) The revenues from Article 177, paragraph 1, item 1 of this Law are used for:

tasks and duties from Articles 29 and 156 of this Law,

maintenance of protective facilities owned by the Federation,

other tasks and activities entrusted to the water agency by this Law, and

financing the work of the water agency.

(2) The revenues from Article 177, paragraph 1, item 2 of this Law are used for co-financing the construction and maintenance of water facilities from Article 14, paragraph 1 of this Law, except for the facilities from item 3, sub-items 2, 4, 5, and 6 of that Article, as well as other activities related to water management tasks (preparation of technical documentation, basis for issuing concessions, etc.), in accordance with the annual plan and program of the cantonal ministry competent for waters.

(3) The revenues from Article 177, paragraph 1, item 3 of this Law are used exclusively for carrying out tasks that are given by this Law under the competence of the federal ministry competent for the environment and for co-financing the infrastructure for water protection of importance for the Federation.

XIII. SUPERVISION OF THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THIS LAW

(Page 62)

1. Supervision by the Federal Ministry

Article 179.

The Federal Ministry shall carry out administrative supervision over the implementation of this Law and the regulations adopted on the basis of this Law, as well as administrative supervision over the work of the water agencies in performing the tasks that are given by this Law under the competence of the water agencies.

Article 180.

(1) In carrying out supervision over the execution of tasks that are given by this Law under the competence of the water agencies, the Federal Ministry shall have direct insight and control into the actions of the water agencies, review the acts and documentation issued by the agencies and the records they keep, issue orders and instructions for the execution of tasks, and request data and information on the execution of obligations from this Law and other regulations adopted on the basis of this Law and reports on the execution of the tasks of the agencies prescribed by this Law.

(2) If the Federal Ministry determines that a water agency is not fulfilling the obligations from this Law and the regulations adopted on the basis of this Law that are under its competence or the general acts for which it is authorized to execute, or finds irregularities in the work of the agency, it shall set a deadline within which the obligations from this Law, regulation, or general act must be fulfilled, or the irregularities must be eliminated.

(3) If the water agency does not fulfill the obligation imposed on it in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of this Article within the deadline, the Federal Ministry shall inform the Government of the Federation of the identified omission or irregularity.

(4) In the case from paragraph 3 of this Article, the Government of the Federation shall issue guidelines to the water agency or take measures to ensure the performance of tasks in the manner determined by this Law.

Article 181.

(1) The Federal Ministry shall supervise the legality of the water acts issued by the water agencies, as well as the cantonal, city, and municipal administrative bodies competent for waters, resolving in administrative matters the rights and obligations of natural and legal persons which are prescribed by this Law as the competence of the water agencies, or the competence of the cantonal, city, and municipal administrative body competent for waters.

(2) An appeal against the decision of the water agency on the issued water act from Article 107 of this Law, in connection with Article 139, paragraph 1 of this Law, adopted in the first-instance procedure, shall be filed with the Federal Ministry.

(3) An appeal against the decision of the cantonal, city, or municipal administrative body competent for waters on the issued water act from Article 107 of this Law, in connection with Article 139, paragraph 2 of this Law, adopted in the first-instance procedure, shall be filed with the Federal Ministry.

2. Organization of Inspection Supervision

Article 182.
Joint Provision

Inspection supervision over the implementation of this Law and the regulations adopted on the basis of this Law is carried out in accordance with the provisions of this Law and the Law on the Organization of Administrative Bodies in the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 35/05) which relate to inspection supervision.

a) Federal Water Inspection

Article 183.

(Page 63)

(1) Inspection supervision over the implementation of this Law and the regulations adopted on the basis of this Law, which are placed under the competence of the Federation by this Law, is carried out by the Federal Ministry through the federal water inspection, which is part of the Federal Ministry, in accordance with Article 216 of this Law.

(2) Inspection supervision over the implementation of the regulations adopted on the basis of this Law, which are issued by the federal minister competent for the environment, is carried out by the federal ministry competent for the environment, through the federal inspection competent for environmental protection.

Article 184.
Organization of the Federal Water Inspection

(1) For the purpose of ensuring the efficient performance of inspection tasks, the federal water inspection is organized for:

the immediate basin of the Sava,

the sub-basin of the Una with the Glina and Korana,

the sub-basin of the Vrbas,

the sub-basin of the Bosna,

the sub-basin of the Drina,

the basin of the Neretva,

the basins of the Krka and Cetina, and

the coastal sea.

(2) The number and territorial distribution of federal water inspectors are determined by the Rulebook on the internal organization of the Federal Ministry.

Article 185.
Chief and Federal Water Inspectors

(1) The chief federal water inspector and federal water inspectors are appointed in accordance with the Law on the Civil Service in the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", Nos. 29/03, 23/04, 39/04, 54/04, 67/05 and 8/06).

(2) The special conditions that a person appointed as the chief federal water inspector and federal water inspector must meet are determined by the rulebook from Article 184, paragraph 2 of this Law.

Article 186.
Subject of Inspection Supervision

Inspection supervision includes:

undertaking inspection actions to determine the state of implementation of regulations by legal and natural persons who, in the performance of their activities and work, are obliged to act in accordance with the provisions of this Law and the regulations adopted on the basis of this Law,

ordering administrative measures to prevent and eliminate illegalities in the implementation of the regulations from item 1 of this Article,

undertaking other measures determined by this Law and other regulations.

(Page 64)

Article 187.
Powers of the Inspector

(1) In addition to the powers and duties from Article 182 of this Law, the federal water inspector is, under this Law, authorized and obliged to:

prevent the unauthorized use of water, within the meaning of the provisions of this Law,

prevent or restrict the exercise of the right to use water if it is not used in accordance with the water permit,

halt the construction or execution of an intervention in the space if such construction and intervention are carried out without a water consent or in contravention of it,

order the temporary cessation of activities if the provisions of the permit from item 2, paragraph 1 of this Article are violated,

prohibit the use of a facility and device if it is used without a water consent or in contravention of it,

prohibit or restrict the discharge of wastewater into surface waters or the indirect discharge of wastewater into groundwaters if this discharge is carried out in contravention of the provisions of Article 55 of this Law and order the restoration of the original state,

prohibit the extraction of material from surface waters if the extraction of this material is carried out without a water act or in contravention of the water act,

order the obligation to determine the damage and compensate for the damage caused by the actions from item 7, paragraph 1 of this Article and the obligation to return to the original state,

order other measures in accordance with this Law or on the basis of regulations and water acts adopted on the basis of this Law.

(2) If it is necessary to eliminate the sources and consequences of excessive water pollution, the federal water inspector may order remediation measures or propose to the Federal Ministry the preparation and implementation of a remediation program.

Article 188.
Administrative Actions

(1) The federal water inspector shall draw up a record of the established facts in the performance of inspection supervision tasks.

(2) The federal water inspector shall issue a written decision and immediately order its execution if:

there is an imminent danger of floods, water spillage, or deterioration of the situation during a flood,

there is an imminent danger of water scarcity or difficulties in water supply,

there is a danger of water pollution or pollution has occurred to an extent that represents an immediate danger to the life and health of people or to the animal or plant world,

there is an immediate danger of pollution of a water source intended for drinking water supply,

there is an immediate danger of pollution of surface and groundwaters,

a drought occurs, or if for other reasons a shortage of water or difficulties in the supply of drinking water could occur,

(Page 65)

there is an immediate danger of a landslide, rockfall, or other forms of erosion,

it is necessary to ensure the implementation of the general use of water from Article 46 of this Law or to ensure a real easement from Articles 140 to 151 of this Law, if the owner or other user of the water estate from Articles 7 and 8 of this Law or other riparian land opposes the passage over their land or the performance of works for the implementation of this Law,

it is necessary to ensure the execution of works on water investigation, in accordance with the provisions of this Law,

other urgent situations determined by law so require.

(3) Exceptionally, the federal water inspector for the cases from items 1, 3, 4, 7, and 10 of paragraph 2 of this Article may issue an oral decision and order its execution without delay, provided that they are obliged to issue a written decision no later than eight days from the date of the oral decision. The content of the written decision must correspond to the content of the oral decision.

(4) If the legal or natural person responsible for the dangers and consequences from items 1, 3, 4, 7, and 10 of paragraph 2 of this Article does not act immediately upon the order of the water inspector, or if the person responsible for the occurrence of the danger and consequences cannot be identified, the water inspection shall engage an authorized institution from Article 189, paragraph 1 of this Law to implement measures to eliminate the danger or mitigate the consequences.

(5) The costs incurred by the implementation of the measures from paragraph 4 of this Article shall be borne by the competent water agency, and these costs shall be subsequently collected from the responsible person whose action or inaction led to the occurrence of the danger or undesirable consequences.

Article 189.
Professional Tasks by Order of the Inspection

(1) The performance of certain professional tasks for the needs of inspection supervision (works to prevent the spread of pollution in case of an accident and cleaning of pollution from water and the water estate, testing, expert analysis, etc.), which require special technical equipment and specialized experts or the application of scientific methods and procedures, may be entrusted to authorized and qualified institutions (institutes, companies, laboratories, associations) that perform such tasks.

(2) The institutions from paragraph 1 of this Article are engaged on the basis of an order from the federal water inspector, through the locally competent water agency.

Article 190.
Inspection Procedures

(1) When it is determined that this Law, a regulation, or another act adopted on the basis of this Law has been violated, the federal water inspector shall state the irregularity or deficiency in a record and, by a decision, determine measures and a deadline for their elimination.

(2) In addition to the powers and duties determined by law, in the case from paragraph 1 of this Article, the federal water inspector is authorized and obliged to:

order the cessation of works if they are carried out contrary to a water act or without a water act, when the obligation to obtain one is prescribed,

in cooperation with the construction-urban planning inspection, order the removal of a constructed facility or plant built without a water act, if a water act is not obtained within 60 days,

order the temporary suspension of the work or activity of a legal person or its parts if it is determined that the wastewater contains hazardous substances in a quantity greater than permitted or if quantities of water greater than permitted are being abstracted,

(Page 66)

prohibit the undertaking of actions that are not permitted by this Law.

(3) The federal water inspector shall cooperate with the water inspection body of neighboring and other states on issues of unauthorized changes in the water regime on the control profiles of transboundary watercourses, in accordance with an international treaty, agreement, or convention concluded by Bosnia and Herzegovina.

Article 191.
Initiation of Procedures

(1) The federal water inspector is always obliged, when it is determined that an act has been committed which has the characteristics of a misdemeanor prescribed by this Law, to submit a request to the competent body for the initiation of a misdemeanor procedure.

(2) The federal water inspector is always obliged, when it is determined that an act has been committed which has the characteristics of a criminal offense, the characteristics of which are prescribed by criminal law, to file a written report to the competent prosecutor's office.

Article 192.
Appeals Against the Inspector's Decision

(1) An appeal against the decision of the federal water inspector shall be filed with the Federal Ministry, through the federal water inspection.

(2) An appeal against the decision of the cantonal, city, or municipal water inspector, which is adopted on the basis of this Law and regulations adopted on the basis of this Law, shall be filed with the federal water inspection, through the cantonal, city, or municipal water inspection.

(3) An appeal against the decision of the water inspector shall suspend the execution of the decision, except in the cases from Article 188, paragraphs 2 and 3 of this Law.

Article 193.
Accidents

(1) In the event of an accident or other extraordinary event, immediately upon learning of its occurrence, the water inspector is obliged, in the area for which they are competent, to undertake the actions and measures provided for by this Law and regulations adopted on the basis of this Law, regardless of the actual competence (federal or cantonal), and to inform the federal inspection competent for environmental protection about it.

(2) In carrying out the activities from paragraph 1 of this Article, the water inspectors are obliged to cooperate and exchange information on the measures and activities that have been undertaken or are necessary to be undertaken.

Article 194.
Cooperation of Water Inspectors

(1) Federal water inspectors are obliged to cooperate with each other and to inform each other about phenomena, problems, and the manner of resolving individual cases. The initiative for cooperation can be launched by any of the water inspectors.

(2) The cooperation from paragraph 1 of this Article is also realized with cantonal, city, and municipal water inspectors.

(3) The chief federal water inspector is obliged to initiate and organize a joint meeting of all water inspectors from paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article at least twice a year, about which they shall keep a special record to be submitted to the Federal Minister, the water agencies, and the cantonal ministries competent for waters.

(4) In their work, the federal water inspection shall cooperate with other inspections and bodies in the exercise of their powers.

Article 195.
Relations Between Federal and Cantonal Water Inspections

(Page 67)

(1) If the federal water inspector determines that the cantonal water inspector is not implementing the provisions of this Law related to supervision from their competence, they shall, in writing, request the cantonal administrative body competent for water inspection to undertake the measures and activities established by law without delay.

(2) If, despite the activity undertaken by the federal water inspector from paragraph 1 of this Article, the cantonal water inspector fails to undertake the measures established by law, the federal water inspector is obliged to implement these measures at the expense of the canton's budget and to inform the cantonal minister competent for waters about it.

Article 196.
Special Powers of the Federal Water Inspector

(1) Exceptionally, at the request of the cantonal ministry competent for waters, the Federal Minister may, by a decision, determine that the supervision over the implementation of this Law from the competence of the canton in the territory of the canton shall be carried out by the federal water inspector for a specified period.

(2) No special appeal is allowed against the decision from paragraph 1 of this Article.

Article 197.
Obligations of Water Agencies Towards the Water Inspection

For the purpose of performing inspection supervision tasks and undertaking inspection measures prescribed by this Law and regulations adopted on the basis of this Law, the water agencies are obliged, at the request of the competent federal or cantonal water or environmental inspector, to:

provide the necessary data, acts, or documentation,

provide an appropriate expert opinion,

carry out testing, or organize the performance of additional necessary water quality tests,

carry out other necessary actions from the competence of the agency.

b) Cantonal Water Inspection

Article 198.

(1) Inspection supervision over the implementation of this Law and regulations adopted on the basis of this Law, which are placed under the competence of the canton by this Law, is carried out by the cantonal administrative body competent for waters through the cantonal water inspection.

(2) A city or municipality may establish a city or municipal water inspection for the performance of inspection supervision tasks that are placed under the competence of the canton by this Law, which is determined by a regulation of the canton.

(3) In performing the tasks from paragraph 1 of this Article, the cantonal water inspection shall act in accordance with the provisions of Articles 186 to 198 of this Law.

c) Inter-entity Cooperation of Water Inspection Services

Article 199.

(1) The federal water inspection is obliged to cooperate and mutually inform the water inspection of Republika Srpska about phenomena, problems, and the manner of resolving individual cases of common interest.

(2) The proposal for holding joint meetings of the inspections from paragraph 1 of this Article is given by the chief federal water inspector or the chief water inspector of Republika Srpska.

(Page 68)

Article 200.

The water inspectors of the Federation have the right to jointly carry out an inspection with the water inspection of Republika Srpska in the following cases:

if an incident of pollution has occurred on waters and the water estate, the consequences of which are evident in the territory of the Federation and Republika Srpska,

if it is determined that a facility, installation, or activity located or taking place in the territory of Republika Srpska has negative impacts on waters in the territory of the Federation,

if it is determined that a facility, installation, or activity located or taking place in the territory of the Federation has negative impacts on waters in the territory of Republika Srpska,

when an agreement on this is reached by the chief water inspectors of the Federation and Republika Srpska.

Article 201.

(1) In the case of a joint inspection by water inspectors from the Federation and Republika Srpska, the inspector from the Federation shall draw up a record and undertake appropriate administrative measures if the incident occurred in the Federation or if the facility or activity that is the subject of the inspection is located in the territory of the Federation, and likewise for Republika Srpska if the incident occurred in Republika Srpska or if the facility or activity that is the subject of the inspection is located in the territory of Republika Srpska.

(2) In the case of an inspection from paragraph 1 of this Article, the water inspector from the Federation or Republika Srpska in whose territory the impacts are evident may draw up an official note.

(3) The water inspectors, in the cases from paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article, are obliged to exchange copies of the prepared record, note, and other relevant documents.

Article 202.

(1) A joint inspection by the water inspectors from the Federation and Republika Srpska is agreed upon through the chief water inspectors.

(2) The initiative for a joint inspection may be launched by any of the water inspectors through an oral or written request addressed to the chief inspector or the competent minister from the Federation or Republika Srpska.

(3) The chief water inspectors are obliged to inform their competent minister of the agreement from paragraph 1 of this Article.

3. Water Ranger Service

Article 203.

(1) For the purpose of preventing water pollution and damage to water facilities, the Federal Ministry may organize a water ranger service.

(2) The Federal Minister shall issue a regulation on the powers and tasks of the water ranger service from paragraph 1 of this Article.

(3) The Federal Minister, in agreement with the federal minister of justice, shall issue a regulation on the official badge, identification card, and uniform of the water ranger.

XIV. PENAL PROVISIONS

(Page 69)

Article 204.
Misdemeanors

(1) A legal person shall be fined in the amount of 5,000.00 KM to 50,000.00 KM for a misdemeanor if:

it acts contrary to the provisions of Article 12 of this Law,

the water facility from Article 14 of this Law is not maintained in a functional state and is not used in accordance with its nature and purpose (Article 20, paragraph 1),

after the cessation of use of the water facility from Article 14 of this Law, it does not act in accordance with the provisions of Article 20, paragraph 2 of this Law,

it does not safeguard the water facility from Article 14 of this Law and the devices on it in accordance with the provisions of Article 20, paragraph 5 of this Law,

it does not ensure constant and systematic monitoring of water and does not take measures to ensure the health safety of drinking water and the technical correctness of the devices (Article 48, paragraph 2),

it carries out actions contrary to the provisions of Article 53, paragraphs 2, 3, and 4 of this Law,

it discharges wastewater contrary to the provision of Article 54, paragraph 1 of this Law,

it produces, handles, stores, or disposes of hazardous materials and waste on waters and the water estate (Article 59, paragraph 1),

it fails to take measures to prevent or mitigate the impacts caused by an incident and fails to timely inform the police administration, the water inspection, or the water agency about it (Article 61, paragraph 1),

it does not implement the decision on the protection of a source (Article 66, paragraph 4),

it does not take measures to protect a bathing area from Article 72, paragraph 1 of this Law (Article 72, paragraph 4),

it carries out works and actions prohibited by Article 96, paragraph 1 and Article 97 of this Law,

it uses water, discharges wastewater into surface waters and the water estate, or carries out other activities and interventions without obtaining a water act, for which the obligation to obtain a water act is prescribed by this Law (Article 109),

it uses water, discharges wastewater into surface waters and the water estate, or carries out other activities and interventions contrary to the provisions of the water act,

upon the expiration of the validity period of the water consent for the extraction of material, it does not restore the bed and banks of the watercourse to the state provided for by the water act (Article 114, paragraph 5),

it does not act upon the decision of the water inspector from Article 190, paragraph 1 of this Law.

(2) For a misdemeanor from paragraph 1 of this Article, the responsible person in the legal person shall also be fined in the amount of 500.00 KM to 5,000.00 KM.

Article 205.

(1) A legal person shall be fined in the amount of 2,000.00 KM to 20,000.00 KM for a misdemeanor if:

it does not keep records of the quantities of abstracted water and does not submit data thereon to the competent agency for waters (Article 50, paragraph 1),

(Page 70)

during mining works, tunnel excavation, and other excavations and drilling of the ground, it encounters groundwater and fails to notify the competent body thereof (Article 52, paragraph 1),

it does not allow an authorized person to take data and carry out the necessary tests of the discovered water deposit (Article 52, paragraph 2),

it uses fertilizer or means for plant protection on the water estate (Article 56, paragraph 4),

it navigates a vessel that uses petroleum derivatives for propulsion on surface waters where it is prohibited (Article 57, paragraph 1),

it discharges wastewater directly from a vessel (Article 57, paragraph 4),

it washes a vehicle or other machinery and equipment in surface water (Article 58),

it notices an incident from Article 61, paragraph 1 of this Law and fails to notify the competent body thereof (Article 61, paragraph 2),

it does not comply with the provisions of the decision from Article 70, paragraph 2 of this Law,

it does not carry out the protection of economically important aquatic species from Article 71, paragraph 1 of this Law,

it erects permanent or temporary facilities or other obstacles or if it prevents free passage over the water estate and access to the banks in a bathing area (Article 72, paragraph 3),

it does not implement measures to protect the quantity and quality of water in a sensitive area (Article 75, paragraph 2),

it does not carry out monitoring of importance for protection from the adverse effects of water and does not submit data thereon to the WIS (Article 91, paragraph 4),

it carries out works and actions prohibited by Article 96, paragraph 2 of this Law,

it does not provide the locally competent agency for the water area with the data it possesses, which are of importance for water management (Article 105).

(2) For a misdemeanor from paragraph 1 of this Article, the responsible person in the legal person shall also be fined in the amount of 300.00 KM to 3,000.00 KM.

Article 206.

A natural person shall be fined in the amount of 100.00 KM to 2,000.00 KM for a misdemeanor if:

they act contrary to the provisions of Article 12 of this Law,

the water facility from Article 16, paragraph 3, Article 17, paragraph 3, and Article 18 of this Law is not maintained in a functional state and is not used in accordance with its nature and purpose (Article 20, paragraph 1),

after the cessation of use of the water facility from Article 16, paragraph 3, Article 17, paragraph 3, and Article 18 of this Law, they do not act in accordance with the provisions of Article 20, paragraph 2 of this Law,

they do not safeguard the water facility from Article 16, paragraph 3, Article 17, paragraph 3, and Article 18 of this Law and the devices on it in accordance with the provisions of Article 20, paragraph 5 of this Law,

they do not allow an authorized person to take data and carry out the necessary tests of the discovered water deposit (Article 52, paragraph 2),

(Page 71)

they carry out actions contrary to the provisions of Article 53, paragraphs 1, 2, 3, and 4 of this Law, or indirectly discharge wastewater into groundwaters contrary to the manner and conditions established by this Law and beyond the limit values established by the by-law from Article 55, paragraph 1 of this Law,

they discharge wastewater contrary to the provision of Article 54, paragraph 1 of this Law,

they use fertilizer or means for plant protection on the water estate (Article 56, paragraph 4),

they navigate a vessel that uses petroleum derivatives for propulsion on surface waters where it is prohibited (Article 57, paragraph 1),

they discharge wastewater directly from a vessel (Article 57, paragraph 4),

they wash a vehicle or other machinery and equipment in surface water (Article 58),

they produce, handle, store, or dispose of hazardous materials and waste on waters and the water estate (Article 59, paragraph 1),

they do not take the necessary measures to prevent or mitigate the impacts caused by an incident and do not timely inform the police administration, the water inspection, or the water agency about it (Article 61, paragraph 1),

they fail to notify the competent body about an incident from Article 61, paragraph 1 of this Law (Article 61, paragraph 2),

they do not comply with the prohibitions from the decision on the protection of a source from Article 66, paragraph 4 of this Law,

they do not comply with the prohibitions from the decision from Article 70, paragraph 2 of this Law,

they do not comply with the prohibitions from the decision on the protection of economically important aquatic species from Article 71, paragraph 1 of this Law,

they erect permanent or temporary facilities or other obstacles or if they prevent free passage over the water estate and access to the banks in a bathing area (Article 72, paragraph 3),

they do not implement measures to protect the quantity and quality of water in a sensitive area (Article 75, paragraph 2),

they carry out works and actions prohibited by Articles 96 and 97 of this Law,

they do not provide the locally competent agency for the water area with the data they possess, which are of importance for water management (Article 105),

they use water, discharge wastewater into surface waters and the water estate, or carry out other activities and interventions without obtaining a water act, for which the obligation to obtain a water act is prescribed by this Law (Article 109),

they use water, discharge wastewater into surface waters and the water estate, or carry out other activities and interventions contrary to the provisions of the water act,

upon the expiration of the validity period of the water consent for the extraction of material, they do not restore the bed and banks of the watercourse to the state provided for by the water act (Article 114, paragraph 5),

they do not act upon the decision of the water inspector from Article 190, paragraph 1 of this Law.

XV. TRANSITIONAL AND FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 207.
Commencement of Work of the Water Agencies

(Page 72)

(1) The "Agency for the Sava River Water Area" and the "Agency for the Adriatic Sea Water Area" shall be organized, judicially registered, and commence their work in accordance with this Law within six months of the date of entry into force of this Law.

(2) The founding act for the entry into the court register of the water agencies from paragraph 1 of this Article is this Law.

(3) The date of commencement of the work of the agencies from paragraph 1 of this Article is the date of entry into the court register.

(4) Until the commencement of the work of the water agencies, the Public Enterprise for the "Sava River Basin Water Area" Sarajevo and the Public Enterprise for the "Adriatic Sea Basin Water Area" Mostar shall perform the tasks and duties from their competence according to the Law on Waters ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 18/98) and the regulations adopted on the basis of that law.

Article 208.

Until the date of entry into the court register of the agencies from Article 207, paragraph 1 of this Law, the funds of special water fees shall be paid in accordance with the Rulebook on the manner of payment of public revenues of the budget and extra-budgetary funds in the territory of the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 6/05).

Article 209.

On the date of entry into the court register of the agencies from Article 207, paragraph 1 of this Law:

the Public Enterprise for the "Sava River Basin Water Area" Sarajevo and the Public Enterprise for the "Adriatic Sea Basin Water Area" Mostar shall cease to operate,

the assets, rights, and obligations of the Public Enterprise for the "Sava River Basin Water Area" Sarajevo shall become the assets, rights, and obligations of the "Agency for the Sava River Water Area" Sarajevo,

the assets, rights, and obligations of the Public Enterprise for the "Adriatic Sea Basin Water Area" Mostar shall become the assets, rights, and obligations of the "Agency for the Adriatic Sea Water Area" Mostar.

Article 210.
Status of Employees of the Public Enterprises for Water Areas

(1) On the date of entry into the court register of the "Agency for the Sava River Water Area," the employees of the Public Enterprise for the "Sava River Basin Water Area" Sarajevo shall become employees of the "Agency for the Sava River Water Area".

(2) On the date of entry into the court register of the "Agency for the Adriatic Sea Water Area," the employees of the Public Enterprise for the "Adriatic Sea Basin Water Area" Mostar shall become employees of the "Agency for the Adriatic Sea Water Area".

Article 211.
Management Boards

(1) The Government of the Federation shall appoint the president and members of the Management Board of the "Agency for the Sava River Water Area" and the "Agency for the Adriatic Sea Water Area" within three months of the date of entry into force of this Law.

(2) On the date of entry into the court register of the agencies from Article 207, paragraph 1 of this Law, the Management Board of the Public Enterprise for the "Sava River Basin Water Area" Sarajevo and the Management Board of the Public Enterprise for the "Adriatic Sea Basin Water Area" Mostar shall cease to operate.

(3) The member of the management board from among the employees of the water agency from Article 207, paragraph 1 of this Law, for the first management board, shall be elected and appointed from among the employees of the Public Enterprise for the "Sava River Basin Water Area" Sarajevo and the Public Enterprise for the "Adriatic Sea Basin Water Area" Mostar.

(Page 73)

Article 212.
Establishment of the Advisory Council of the Water Area

The advisory councils of the water areas from Article 164, paragraph 1 of this Law shall be established within one year of the date of entry into the court register of the agencies from Article 207, paragraph 1 of this Law.

Article 213.
Planning Documents

Until the adoption of the water management strategy from Article 24 of this Law, the relevant development plans and programs for water management and individual areas of water management (protection from the adverse effects of water, water protection, and water use), adopted before the date of entry into force of this Law, shall be used as planning documents for water management in the Federation.

Article 214.
Previously Issued Water Management Acts

(1) Water management permits issued before the date of commencement of the work of the water agencies from Article 152 of this Law shall be harmonized with the provisions of this Law within a period of up to five years from the date of entry into force of this Law.

(2) Water management conditions issued before the date of commencement of the work of the water agencies from Article 152 of this Law shall be harmonized with the provisions of this Law upon the issuance of the water consent.

(3) Water management consents issued before the date of commencement of the work of the water agencies from Article 152 of this Law shall be harmonized with the provisions of this Law upon the issuance of the water permits.

(4) The holders of the right from the water management act from paragraph 1 of this Law are obliged to submit an application for the review of the water permit within two years of the date of entry into force of this Law.

(5) In the case that the application from paragraph 4 of this Law is not submitted within the period from paragraph 4 of this Law, the water management act from paragraph 1 of this Article shall cease to be valid.

(6) The provisions of paragraph 1 of this Article do not apply to water management permits that are valid for five years or less from the date of entry into force of this Law.

Article 215.
Unresolved Applications for the Issuance of Water Management Acts

Applications for the issuance of water management acts that were submitted to the competent body before the date of commencement of the work of the agencies from Article 152 of this Law shall be resolved in accordance with the Law on Waters ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 18/98) and the regulations adopted on the basis of that law.

Article 216.
Inspection Supervision

(1) Until the date of commencement of the work of the agencies from Article 152 of this Law, inspection supervision shall be carried out in accordance with the Law on Waters ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 18/98) and the regulations adopted on the basis of that law.

(2) The provisions of Article 183, paragraph 1, Article 184, and Article 198, paragraph 1 of this Law shall apply until the organization of the water inspection by a special federal law.

Article 217.
Procedures on Appeals

Procedures on appeals that are not resolved by the date of commencement of the work of the agencies from Article 152 of this Law shall be resolved in accordance with the Law on Waters ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 18/98) and the regulations adopted on the basis of that law.

(Page 74)

Article 218.
Existing Facilities and Activities for Which a Water Management Act has Not Been Issued

(1) The owners or users of existing facilities and plants that use water or discharge wastewater and other hazardous and harmful substances into waters, for which a water management permit was not obtained by the date of entry into force of this Law, are obliged to submit an application for the issuance of a water permit within six months of the date of commencement of the work of the agencies from Article 152 of this Law.

(2) In the case that the application from paragraph 1 of this Article is not submitted within the prescribed period, the competent body of the water inspection shall, by a decision, prohibit the use of the facility or plant from paragraph 1 of this Article.

Article 219.
Deadlines for the Adoption of Regulations by the Government of the Federation

(1) The Government of the Federation shall adopt the regulations from Article 43, paragraph 1 and the regulation from Article 25, paragraph 4 of this Law within two years of the date of entry into force of this Law.

(2) The Government of the Federation shall adopt other regulations for the implementation of this Law within:

six months of the date of entry into force of this Law, the regulations from Articles 23, 164, paragraphs 3 and 5, and 171 of this Law,

one year of the date of entry into force of this Law, the regulation from Article 173, paragraph 7 of this Law,

two years of the date of entry into force of this Law, the regulations from Articles 55 and 57 of this Law.

Article 220.
Deadlines for the Adoption of Regulations by the Federal Ministers

(1) The federal minister shall adopt the regulations for the implementation of this Law within:

six months of the date of entry into force of this Law, the regulation from Article 107, paragraph 4, Article 120, paragraph 6, and Article 174, paragraph 1 of this Law,

one year of the date of entry into force of this Law, the regulations from Articles 10, 50, and 104 of this Law,

two years of the date of entry into force of this Law, the regulations from Articles 64, 66, 86, and 90 of this Law.

(2) The federal minister competent for the environment shall adopt the regulations from Articles 59, 61, 73, 74, and 76 of this Law within one year of the date of entry into force of this Law.

Article 221.
Application of Existing By-laws

Until the adoption of the regulations from Article 219, paragraphs 1 and 2 and Article 220, paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Law, the by-laws adopted on the basis of the Law on Waters ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 18/98), as well as the by-laws that were applied until the date of entry into force of this Law, and which are not in conflict with this Law, shall apply.

Article 222.
Cantonal Regulations

(1) The cantons are obliged to harmonize the provisions of the cantonal law on waters with the provisions of this Law within six months of the date of entry into force of this Law.

(2) The laws of the cantons shall regulate the issues of organization and the manner of performing the tasks that are placed under the competence of the canton by this Law.

(Page 75)

Article 223.
Cessation of Application of Laws

On the day of commencement of the application of this Law, the following shall cease to be valid:

Law on Waters ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", No. 18/98),

Law on Water Protection ("Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", Nos. 33/03 and 54/04).

Article 224.
Entry into Force and Commencement of Application

This Law shall enter into force on the eighth day following its publication in the "Official Gazette of the Federation of BiH", and its application shall commence on the date of the commencement of the work of the water agencies, in accordance with Article 207, paragraph 3 of this Law.
