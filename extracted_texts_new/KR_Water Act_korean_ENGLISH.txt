**CHAPTER 1 GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**FRAMEWORK ACT ON WATER MANAGEMENT**
**[Enforced Jul. 6, 2021] [Act No. 17841, Jan. 5, 2021, Partially Amended]**

**FRAMEWORK ACT ON WATER MANAGEMENT**
Ministry of Environment (Water Management Policy Division) 044-201-7625

**Article 1 (Purpose)**
The purpose of this Act is to establish a sustainable water cycle system and contribute to improving the quality of life for the public by presenting the basic principles and policy directions for water management and stipulating fundamental matters necessary for such management, thereby ensuring a stable supply of water, conserving and managing the water environment, and preventing disasters arising from droughts, floods, and other events.

**Article 2 (Basic Principles)**
The basic principles of this Act are that water, as a public resource obtained through the Earth's water cycle system, shall be used rationally by all people, animals, plants, and other living organisms; that in managing water, its utility shall be maximized while preventing its misuse or waste; and that it shall be continuously used and conserved in harmony with the natural environment and socio-economic life, thereby carrying its value forward to the future. <Amended May 26, 2020>

**Article 3 (Definitions)**
The terms used in this Act are defined as follows:
1.  "Water cycle" means the continuous flow of precipitation becoming surface water and groundwater, which then flows to or is stored in rivers, lakes, wetlands, seas, etc., before evaporating to become precipitation again.
2.  "Water management" means the conservation and economic use of water by all people and living organisms as a component of the natural environment and a necessary element and resource for socio-economic activities, and the reduction or prevention of disasters caused by droughts and floods.
3.  "Water resources" means water as a resource that can be used for human life, economic activities, maintenance of the natural environment, and other purposes.
4.  "Basin" means a specific area, bordered by a watershed divide, where rivers and other water bodies converge.

**Article 4 (Rights and Duties concerning Water Use)**
① Everyone has the right to be stably supplied with and use water of a quality suitable for its intended purpose, to be safely protected from disasters such as droughts and floods, and to live in a healthy and pleasant water environment.
② Everyone shall endeavor to establish a sustainable water cycle system and shall cooperate with the water management policies of the State and local governments.

**Article 5 (Responsibilities of the State and Local Governments)**
① The State has the responsibility to establish a sustainable water management system in accordance with the basic principles of water management and to formulate and implement comprehensive plans necessary to improve the quality of life for the public.
② Local governments have the responsibility to formulate and implement water management plans that are consistent with the State's water management policies and tailored to the regional characteristics of their jurisdictions.

**Article 6 (Responsibilities of Business Operators)**
① Business operators shall use water appropriately throughout all processes of their business activities and shall endeavor not to adversely affect a healthy water environment and the normal water cycle.
② Business operators shall actively participate and cooperate in the water management policies pursued by the State or local governments.

**Article 7 (Relationship with Other Acts)**
① When enacting or amending other Acts concerning water management, they shall be made to conform with this Act.
② Except as otherwise specifically provided for in other Acts concerning water management, the provisions of this Act shall apply.

**CHAPTER 2 BASIC PRINCIPLES OF WATER MANAGEMENT**

**Article 8 (Public Nature of Water)**
Water shall be used in a manner that does not infringe upon the public interest, does not obstruct the State's water management policies, and minimizes its impact on the water environment.

**Article 9 (Sound Water Cycle)**
The State and local governments, considering that water sustains life on Earth through its cycle and plays a crucial role in public life and industrial activities, shall ensure that the functions of water for maintaining ecosystems and supporting human activities can be properly sustained.

**Article 10 (Conservation of Aquatic Ecosystems)**
When formulating and implementing policies for water management, the State and local governments shall consider the function and value of water as a habitat for living organisms and shall endeavor to conserve sustainable aquatic ecosystems, including by improving and restoring them when the health of such ecosystems is compromised.

**Article 11 (Basin-Based Management)**
Water shall, in principle, be managed on a basin-unit basis to promote its sustainable development, use, and conservation and to prevent disasters arising from droughts, floods, and other events; however, inter-basin water management shall achieve harmony and balance.

**Article 12 (Integrated Water Management)**
① The State and local governments shall manage all forms of water in the water cycle, including surface water and groundwater, in a mutually balanced manner.
② When the State and local governments formulate and implement water-related policies, they shall consider the entire cycle of water circulation.
③ When managing water, the State and local governments shall comprehensively consider factors such as securing water quantity, conserving water quality, preventing disasters arising from droughts and floods, and the impacts on the natural environment, including climate, land, resources, environment, and vegetation, as well as on the economy and society. <Amended May 26, 2020>

**Article 13 (Cooperative and Linked Management)**
The State and local governments shall consider the entire basin when implementing water management policies and shall build inter-basin and inter-regional solidarity by ensuring that changes in water management conditions in one area do not adversely affect the soundness of the water cycle in other areas.

**Article 14 (Water Allocation)**
The State and local governments shall allocate water rationally and equitably so that its benefits may be enjoyed evenly, and in such cases, shall also consider the allocation of water for securing the health of ecosystems, including animals and plants.

**Article 15 (Water Demand Management, etc.)**
① When the State and local governments intend to formulate plans for the development and supply of water resources, they shall, prior to formulating such plans, consider the necessity of properly managing water demand through efforts to conserve water and reduce water loss.
② To prepare for disasters from water resource shortages, droughts, or floods, the State and local governments shall actively encourage the development of alternative water resources, such as the management and use of precipitation, reuse of wastewater, and desalination of saltwater, as well as the development of technologies for disaster prevention.

**Article 16 (Permits for Water Use, etc.)**
Any person who intends to use water shall obtain a permit or other authorization in accordance with relevant Acts.

**Article 17 (Cost Bearing)**
① The costs incurred for water management shall, in principle, be borne, in whole or in part, by the persons who use the water: Provided, that this shall not apply where special circumstances exist as prescribed by this Act or other Acts.
② Where a person has caused an impediment to water management, the costs incurred for water management, such as the prevention and restoration of such impediment, shall, in principle, be borne, in whole or in part, by the person who caused it.
③ The bearing and management of costs under paragraphs (1) and (2) shall be governed by the provisions of relevant Acts, and the revenue received from such costs shall be used for water management.

**Article 18 (Response to Climate Change)**
The State and local governments shall minimize vulnerabilities in water management due to climate change and shall prepare water management measures to actively respond to climate change, including through the restoration of the water cycle.

**Article 19 (Participation in Water Management Policymaking)**
Decisions on water management policies shall be made through the broad participation of stakeholders, including relevant public officials of the State and local governments, water users, local residents, and related experts, and through the collection of diverse opinions.

**Article 20 (Establishment of National and Basin Water Management Committees, etc.)**
① A National Water Management Committee shall be established under the President to deliberate and resolve on important matters concerning water management, and Basin Water Management Committees shall be established for each basin under the National Water Management Committee.
② The names, locations, and jurisdictions of the Basin Water Management Committees shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Article 21 (Composition of the National Water Management Committee, etc.)**
① The National Water Management Committee shall be composed of not less than 30 and not more than 50 members, including two chairpersons.
② The chairpersons of the National Water Management Committee shall be the Prime Minister and a person appointed by the President from among the members falling under paragraph (3) 3 (a) or (b).
③ The members of the National Water Management Committee shall be the following persons. In such cases, members who are not public officials shall constitute a majority of the total members:
1.  The Minister of Economy and Finance, the Minister of the Interior and Safety, the Minister of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs, the Minister of Trade, Industry and Energy, the Minister of Environment, the Minister of Land, Infrastructure and Transport, the Minister of Oceans and Fisheries, the Minister for Government Policy Coordination, the chairperson of each Basin Water Management Committee, and other public officials prescribed by Presidential Decree;
2.  The heads of public institutions prescribed by Presidential Decree among public institutions under the Act on the Management of Public Institutions;
3.  Persons commissioned by the President from among the following:
    a.  A person who has served for ten years or more as an associate professor or higher, or in an equivalent position, in the field of water management at a university or a recognized research institution;
    b.  A person who has been engaged for ten years or more in a water-related organization or institution;
    c.  A person who has served for ten years or more as a judge, prosecutor, or attorney-at-law;
    d.  Other persons of high social standing with extensive knowledge and experience in the field of water management.
④ If both chairpersons are unable to perform their duties for unavoidable reasons, a member pre-designated by the chairperson who is the Prime Minister shall act on their behalf.
⑤ Except as provided for in this Act, matters necessary for the composition of the National Water Management Committee shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Article 22 (Functions of the National Water Management Committee)**
The National Water Management Committee shall deliberate and resolve on the following matters:
1.  The establishment and amendment of the National Master Plan for Water Management under Article 27;
2.  The conformity of the Basin Comprehensive Plans for Water Management under Article 29 with the National Master Plan for Water Management;
3.  The designation of the scope of river system basins;
4.  Inter-basin water transfers for the proper allocation of water;
5.  The conformity of water management-related plans submitted by relevant central administrative agencies with the National Master Plan for Water Management;
6.  The mediation of the following water disputes under Article 32:
    a.  Water disputes to which a central administrative agency or a metropolitan local government is a party;
    b.  Water disputes that span two or more basins;
    c.  Other water disputes prescribed by Presidential Decree;
7.  The evaluation of the implementation status of the National Master Plan for Water Management and of overall water management;
8.  Matters related to water management referred to a meeting by the chairperson of the National Water Management Committee;
9.  Other matters to be deliberated and resolved on by the National Water Management Committee pursuant to this Act or other Acts.

**Article 23 (Composition of Basin Water Management Committees)**
① A Basin Water Management Committee shall be composed of not less than 30 and not more than 50 members, including two chairpersons.
② The chairpersons of a Basin Water Management Committee shall be the Minister of Environment and a person appointed by the President upon the joint recommendation of the chairpersons of the National Water Management Committee from among the members falling under paragraph (3) 3 (a) or (b).
③ The members of a Basin Water Management Committee shall be the following persons. In such cases, members who are not public officials shall constitute a majority of the total members:

**Article 24 (Functions of Basin Water Management Committees)**
A Basin Water Management Committee shall deliberate and resolve on the following matters:
1.  The establishment and amendment of the Basin Comprehensive Plan for Water Management under Article 28;
2.  The conformity of water management-related plans within the basin, submitted by the heads of local governments under Article 30, with the relevant basin plan;
3.  Intra-basin water transfers for the proper allocation of water;
4.  The mediation of water disputes arising within the basin under Article 32 (excluding matters falling under any of the subparagraphs of Article 22, item 6);
5.  Matters related to water management within the basin referred to a meeting by the chairperson of the Basin Water Management Committee;
6.  Other matters to be deliberated and resolved on by the Basin Water Management Committee pursuant to this Act or other Acts.

**Article 25 (Term of Office of Members, etc.)**
① The term of office for members of the National Water Management Committee and the Basin Water Management Committees (hereinafter referred to as "Water Management Committees") who fall under Articles 21 (3) 3 and 23 (3) 3 shall be three years, and they may be reappointed for one additional term only.
② Each Water Management Committee shall have one secretary, who shall be designated by the chairpersons through consultation from among the members falling under Articles 21 (3) 3 and 23 (3) 3.

**Article 26 (Meetings of Water Management Committees, etc.)**
① The chairpersons of a Water Management Committee shall convene and preside over the meetings of the Committee.
② Meetings of a Committee shall be classified as regular meetings and special meetings. A special meeting shall be convened by the chairpersons when they deem it necessary or upon the request of at least one-fourth of the incumbent members.
③ A meeting of a Committee shall be opened with the attendance of a majority of its members and shall make decisions by a majority vote of the members present. However, matters prescribed by Presidential Decree may be deliberated and resolved on in writing.
④ Subcommittees may be established for the efficient operation of a Water Management Committee.
⑤ A secretariat shall be established for the National Water Management Committee.
⑥ Matters necessary for the operation of the Water Management Committees, the composition and operation of subcommittees, and the organization of the secretariat shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**CHAPTER 4 NATIONAL MASTER PLAN FOR WATER MANAGEMENT, ETC.**

**Article 27 (Establishment of the National Master Plan for Water Management, etc.)**
① The Minister of Environment shall, every ten years, establish a National Master Plan for Water Management (hereinafter referred to as the "National Plan") that includes the matters in the following subparagraphs, after consulting with the heads of relevant central administrative agencies and the chairpersons of the Basin Water Management Committees and undergoing deliberation by the National Water Management Committee:
1.  Basic objectives and implementation directions of the national water management policy;
2.  Performance evaluation of the national water management policy, and changes and outlooks in water management conditions;
3.  Matters concerning the conservation, management, and restoration of the water environment;
4.  The supply, use, and allocation of water, and the development and conservation of water resources, along with mid- to long-term supply and demand forecasts;

5.  Matters concerning the mitigation and prevention of disasters arising from droughts, floods, and other events;
6.  Measures to address vulnerabilities in water management due to climate change;
7.  Principles and standards for mediating water disputes and for the rational cost-sharing of water resource use;
8.  Matters concerning the mid- to long-term investment direction of the water management budget;
9.  The fostering of the water industry and enhancement of its competitiveness;
10. Basic guidelines for the Basin Comprehensive Plans for Water Management;
11. Other matters prescribed by Presidential Decree for sustainable water management.

In such cases, the head of the relevant central administrative agency shall comply with the request unless there is a special reason not to do so.
⑤ The procedures for establishing the National Plan under paragraph (1) and other related matters shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Article 28 (Establishment of Basin Comprehensive Plans for Water Management)**
① The chairperson of a Basin Water Management Committee shall, every ten years, establish a Basin Comprehensive Plan for Water Management (hereinafter referred to as a "Basin Plan") that includes the matters in the following subparagraphs, based on the National Plan under Article 27 (1), after consulting with the heads of relevant central administrative agencies and local governments and undergoing deliberation by the National Water Management Committee and the Basin Water Management Committee:
1.  Changes and outlooks in water-related conditions of the basin;
2.  The development, conservation, and diversification of the basin's water resources, and the supply, use, and allocation of water;
3.  Matters concerning the mitigation and prevention of disasters arising from droughts, floods, and other events in the basin;
4.  Matters concerning the conservation, management, and restoration of the basin's water environment;
5.  Measures to address vulnerabilities in the basin's water management due to climate change;
6.  Estimation of the basin's water management costs and plans for securing financial resources;
7.  Participation of stakeholders, including local residents, and the creation of a water culture;
8.  Other matters prescribed by Presidential Decree for the sustainable water management of the basin.
② The chairperson of a Basin Water Management Committee shall review the feasibility of the Basin Plan every five years from the date of its establishment and shall reflect the results in the Basin Plan.
③ The procedures for establishing a Basin Plan under paragraph (1) and other related matters shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Article 29 (Deliberation and Coordination of Basin Plans)**
① The National Water Management Committee may, after deliberating on the conformity of a Basin Plan submitted by the chairperson of a Basin Water Management Committee with the National Plan, request an adjustment to the plan if necessary.
② A chairperson of a Basin Water Management Committee who has been requested to adjust a Basin Plan pursuant to paragraph (1) shall reflect the adjustment unless there is a special reason not to do so.

**Article 30 (Application of Basin Plans)**
① The heads of local governments shall establish or amend water management-related plans prescribed by Presidential Decree to align with the relevant Basin Plan. When intending to establish or amend such plans, they must undergo deliberation by the Basin Water Management Committee regarding the plan's conformity with the Basin Plan.
② A Basin Water Management Committee may, after deliberating on the conformity of a water management-related plan submitted by the head of a local government with the relevant Basin Plan, request an adjustment to the plan if necessary. In such cases, the head of the local government shall comply with the request unless there is a special reason not to do so.

**CHAPTER 5 MEDIATION OF WATER DISPUTES, ETC.**

**Article 32 (Mediation of Water Disputes)**
① If a dispute arises from differing opinions concerning the development, use, and management of water resources (hereinafter referred to as a "water dispute"), any interested party (hereinafter referred to as a "party") may, notwithstanding the provisions of other Acts, apply for mediation of the water dispute to a Water Management Committee as prescribed by Presidential Decree. <Amended May 26, 2020>
② When a party applies for mediation of a water dispute, the application shall be made to the National Water Management Committee for matters falling under Article 22, item 6, and to the competent Basin Water Management Committee for matters falling under Article 24, item 4. <Amended May 26, 2020>
③ A Water Management Committee may, as prescribed by Presidential Decree, commence mediation procedures ex officio for a water dispute that causes serious harm to the public interest, even if there is no application from a party. <Amended May 26, 2020>
④ A Water Management Committee that receives an application for mediation pursuant to paragraph (1) shall notify the other party of the details of the application without delay.
⑤ If a Water Management Committee mediates a water dispute pursuant to paragraph (3), it shall notify the parties of its intention in advance.
⑥ A Water Management Committee may choose not to mediate if it deems that the nature of the water dispute makes it inappropriate for the Committee to mediate, or if it recognizes that the application for mediation was made for an unjust purpose. In such cases, it shall notify the parties of the reasons for not mediating.
⑦ A Water Management Committee that receives an application for mediation of a water dispute under paragraph (1) or commences mediation procedures ex officio under paragraph (3) may present the details to the parties and recommend a settlement before mediation.

**Article 33 (Processing of Mediation)**
① A Water Management Committee shall examine the case and prepare a mediation proposal within 90 days from the date the application is received (or the date the parties are notified, in the case of Article 32 (5)), and shall present it to the parties without delay. However, if there are unavoidable circumstances, the period may be extended within a scope of 60 days by a resolution of the relevant Water Management Committee, and if the period is extended, the parties shall be notified of the reason.
② If one of the parties files a lawsuit, the Water Management Committee shall suspend the mediation process and notify the parties of this fact.
③ A party who has been presented with a mediation proposal pursuant to paragraph (1) shall notify the relevant Water Management Committee of its acceptance or rejection within 30 days from the date of presentation. In such cases, if no indication of intent is given within 30 days, the party shall be deemed to have accepted it.
④ When the parties accept the mediation proposal, the Water Management Committee shall immediately draft a mediation agreement, and the chairperson of the Water Management Committee and each party shall sign or affix their seal to the mediation agreement. However, where acceptance is deemed to have occurred pursuant to the latter part of paragraph (3), the signatures or seals of the parties may be omitted.
⑤ Other matters necessary for the method and procedures of water dispute mediation, the processing of mediation duties, and the exclusion, challenge, or recusal of mediation members shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Article 34 (Request for Data, etc.)**
① A Water Management Committee that receives an application for mediation under Article 32 (1) or commences mediation procedures ex officio under Article 32 (3) may request necessary data for the mediation of the relevant water dispute from the parties. In such cases, the parties shall comply with the request unless there is a justifiable reason not to do so.

**CHAPTER 6 FOSTERING OF WATER CULTURE, INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION, ETC.**

**Article 35 (Fostering of Water Culture, etc.)**
① The State and local governments shall devise necessary measures to foster a water culture, including promoting school and social education, dissemination, and development, to enhance public understanding and knowledge of water, including the water cycle and water management.
② The State and local governments may, to raise domestic and international interest in water, hold events for Water Day and Water Week and related projects befitting their purpose, or support the activities of organizations.

**Article 36 (Promotion of International Cooperation in Water Management)**
The State and local governments shall formulate and implement policies to promote technical cooperation, information exchange, joint surveys and research, and other activities with foreign countries and international organizations for efficient water management.

**Article 36-2 (Establishment of the UNESCO International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management, etc.)**
① The UNESCO International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management (hereinafter referred to as the "International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management") shall be established to encourage the implementation of the "Agreement between the Government of the Republic of Korea and UNESCO regarding the Establishment of the International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management (Category 2) under the Auspices of UNESCO" and to promote global water security and sustainable water management.
② The International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management shall be a legal entity.
③ The International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management shall carry out the following projects:
1.  Research and content development projects on global water security and sustainable water management;
2.  Production and dissemination of research and educational materials and other publications on global water security and sustainable water management;
3.  Education, training, and capacity-building projects to promote global water security and sustainable water management;
4.  Domestic and international exchange and cooperation projects to promote global water security and sustainable water management;
5.  Other projects necessary to promote global water security and sustainable water management.
④ The International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management shall have executive officers and necessary staff as provided for in its articles of association.
⑤ With regard to the International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management, the provisions of the Civil Act concerning incorporated foundations shall apply mutatis mutandis, except as otherwise provided for in this Act.
⑥ The State and local governments may, within their respective budgets, provide support for the expenses necessary for the operation and project implementation of the International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management.

**Article 37 (Inter-Korean Cooperation in Water Management)**
The State and local governments shall, recognizing that water shared between South and North Korea is a common asset of the Korean people, endeavor to promote mutual exchange and cooperation in the water management sector, including the joint management of shared inter-Korean rivers.

**Article 38 (Water Management Agreements)**
① Central administrative agencies and local governments with a legitimate interest in water management, or local governments among themselves, may conclude water management agreements for the purpose of water use and allocation, conservation and management of the water environment, and prevention of disasters from droughts and floods. In such cases, local community groups, business operators, and organizations related to the water management subject to the agreement may be included as parties to the water management agreement.
② Matters necessary for the method, content, procedures, and implementation of water management agreements under paragraph (1) shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Article 39 (Support for Research and Technological Development, etc.)**
The State and local governments may carry out projects necessary to support surveys, research, and technological development concerning water and to train professional human resources in water management.

**Article 40 (Invigoration of Private-Sector Participation)**
The State and local governments shall devise necessary measures to invigorate and support activities for water environment conservation and for maintaining a healthy water cycle voluntarily pursued by water users, local residents, or private organizations.

**Article 41 (Computerization of Water Management Data, etc.)**
① The State and local governments shall endeavor to computerize water management data so that various data and information necessary for water management may be efficiently utilized.
② The State and local governments shall systematically integrate and manage water management data and information and shall provide them transparently so that anyone may use them efficiently.

**Article 42 (Delegation and Entrustment of Duties)**
① The Minister of Environment or the chairperson of a Basin Water Management Committee may, if deemed necessary, delegate or entrust some of their duties under Articles 27 and 28 to a person prescribed by Presidential Decree.
② The heads of central administrative agencies and the heads of local governments may delegate or entrust some of their duties under Articles 35 through 41 to a person prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Article 43 (Establishment of Organizations)**
① Persons engaged in research and duties related to water may, as prescribed by Presidential Decree, establish an organization with the authorization of the head of the relevant central administrative agency for the purposes of surveys, research, technological development, education, and public relations concerning water, and international cooperation in the water sector.
② An organization under paragraph (1) shall be a legal entity.
③ Matters necessary for the articles of association, operation, and supervision of an organization under paragraph (1) shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.
④ With regard to an organization under paragraph (1), the provisions of the Civil Act concerning incorporated associations shall apply mutatis mutandis, except as otherwise provided for in this Act.

**Article 44 (Financial Support)**
The State and local governments may subsidize all or part of the expenses for projects carried out by a person performing any of the following duties:
1.  Fostering of water culture;
2.  International cooperation in water management;
3.  Surveys and research on North Korea's water resources;
4.  Surveys, research, and technological development concerning water management;
5.  Training of professional human resources in water management;
6.  Standardization and computerization of water management data;
7.  Education and public relations concerning water management;
8.  Export of water-related technologies.

**Article 45 (Deeming Members Who Are Not Public Officials to Be Public Officials in Application of Penalty Provisions)**
Members of a Water Management Committee who are not public officials shall be deemed to be public officials when applying penalty provisions under the Criminal Act or other Acts.

④ In the case of paragraph (1), the executive officers and employees of the Foundation Centre at the time this Act enters into force shall be deemed to have been appointed or employed as the executive officers and employees of the International Centre for Water Security and Sustainable Management. In such cases, the term of office for executive officers shall be the remaining period of their term under the articles of association of the Foundation Centre.
