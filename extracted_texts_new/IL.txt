The Water Law, 5719-1959

Chapter One: Introduction
1. The water sources in the State are public property, subject to the control of the State, and are designated for the needs of its inhabitants and for the development of the country.	Water Sources and their Purpose
2. For the purpose of this Law, water sources are springs, streams, rivers, lakes, and other currents and bodies of water, whether surface or subterranean, natural, regulated or installed, whether the water flows or is contained in them continuously or intermittently, including drainage water and wastewater.	What Water Sources Are
3. Every person is entitled to receive water and to use it, subject to the provisions of this Law.	The Individual's Right to Water
4. A person's right in land does not grant him a right in a water source situated on that land or passing through it or on its boundary; however, this provision shall not derogate from a person's right under section 3.	The Connection Between Land and Water
5. A person's right to receive water from a water source shall subsist as long as its receipt does not cause the salination or depletion of the source.	Water Source Not to be Depleted
6. Every right to water is attached to one of the purposes of water enumerated below; when the purpose ceases, the right to water expires; and these are the purposes of water—	Attachment of the Right to the Purpose

Chapter Two: Regulation of the Use of Water

Section A: Conservation of Water

8. In this chapter – "depletion of a water source" – including the lowering of the water table, whether surface or subterranean, and the worsening of the possibility of raising water to the surface or of conveying it from place to place.	Definitions (Amendment of 5732)
9. A person shall – (1) handle the water at his disposal efficiently and sparingly; (2) keep the water installations in his possession in proper condition in order to prevent waste of water; (3) refrain from causing the obstruction and depletion of any water source; (4) prevent the obstruction and depletion of the water source from which he produces water.	Rules for Conservation of Water (Amendment of 5732)
10. (Repealed).	(Amendment of 5732)
11. If the Water Commissioner appointed under section 138 (hereinafter: "the Water Commissioner") finds that a provision of section 9 has not been complied with, he may— (1) order the person responsible for complying with the provision to rectify the defect in accordance with the order; and if it is not rectified within a reasonable time, to do what is necessary to rectify it, and also to order the cessation of production or supply or	Powers of the Water Commissioner in Conserving Water

the consumption of water, or its reduction, as may be required by the circumstances, until the defect is rectified;
(2) take measures to prevent immediate serious damage to a water source if it cannot be prevented in any other way.

12. The Water Commissioner may, by order, impose upon a person who was responsible for complying with the provisions of section 9 the expenses he incurred for an action under section 11, and upon his doing so, the expenses shall be collected as if they were a tax to which the Taxes (Collection) Ordinance, except for section 12 thereof, applies.	Charging of Expenses
13. A person who considers himself aggrieved by an order under section 11 or by the imposition of expenses under section 12 may appeal against it to the Tribunal established under section 140 (hereinafter: "the Tribunal"). An appeal shall not stay the execution of the order unless the Tribunal orders it to be stayed, but expenses under section 12 shall not be collected until after the Tribunal has given its decision on the appeal.	Appeal
14. The Minister of Agriculture may, after consultation with the Water Council appointed under section 125 (hereinafter: "the Water Council"), prescribe rules concerning the width and area of protective strips, and if he does so, the Water Commissioner shall not determine a protective strip otherwise than within the framework of such rules and not more than is necessary to achieve the purpose for which the protective strip was determined.	Dimensions of Protective Strips
15. If the Water Commissioner sees a need to do so for the conservation of water, a water source, a water undertaking, or an installation for the production, storage, or conveyance of water, he may, by order, designate a protective strip around or alongside that source or installation, to which entry and passage shall be forbidden except under a permit from the Water Commissioner and in accordance with the conditions of the permit.	Designation of Protective Strips
16. A person who considers himself aggrieved by the designation of a protective strip, by the refusal of the Water Commissioner to grant a permit under section 15, or by the conditions of the permit, may appeal against it to the Tribunal.	Appeal
17. The Water Commissioner, and any person authorized by him in writing to do so, may enter any place, after prior written notice to the holder of that place, and carry out	Right of Entry, Inspection, etc.

any act necessary for the supervision of a water source and the conservation of water; - and he may also act to uncover water sources, to measure their output and properties, and to test soil, vegetation, and other local conditions for the purpose of determining water consumption.

18. A person to whom damage is caused by the designation of a protective strip or by an action under section 17 is entitled to compensation from the State Treasury; in the absence of agreement between the claimant and the Water Commissioner concerning the compensation, its amount, and the conditions of its payment, the Tribunal shall decide.	Compensation
19. (a) If the Water Commissioner finds that a water source is being depleted to an extent that its output under regular production is insufficient to maintain the regular supply of water from it, the Water Commissioner may, with the approval of the Minister of Agriculture, order the producer of the water to reduce the production from that source, or order the regulation of production or the adoption of other emergency measures to ensure the supply of water, as he may see fit in the circumstances of the case. (b) The approval of the Minister of Agriculture for an order under subsection (a) shall be given after consultation with the Water Council, unless in the Minister's opinion, immediate action is required; in such a case, the matter shall be brought to the knowledge of the Water Council within a reasonable time after the order is given. (c) If the order is not complied with within a reasonable time specified therein, the Water Commissioner may, after written warning, do what is required on his part, and upon his doing so, collect the expenses involved from the person to whom the order was directed.	Depleted Water Source
20. If the Water Commissioner finds that the water source of a supplier or producer has become depleted or that the use of the source or the installations in his possession has been impaired to an extent that its output is insufficient to maintain the supply of the regular quantity of water from them, he may require the owner of a pipeline, channel, or other installation for the conveyance of water that is not being utilized by him, to convey water for the supplier or producer who has been affected as aforesaid or for the consumers of the supplier; in the absence of agreement between the parties on the quantity of water, the conditions of its conveyance, and the payments involved, these shall be determined by the Water Commissioner. Section A1: Prevention of Water Pollution

20A. In this section – "water pollution" – a change in the properties of water in a water source in a physical, chemical, organoleptic, biological, bacteriological, radioactive, or other respect, or a change which makes the water dangerous to public health, or liable to harm animal or plant life, or less suitable for the purpose for which it is used or was intended to be used; "water source" – as defined in section 2, including open or closed water conduits, reservoirs, and drainage canals; "polluting factor" – an industrial or agricultural undertaking, a building as defined in the Planning and Building Law, 5725-1965, an installation, including a sewage installation, a machine or a means of transport, whose location, establishment, operation, maintenance, or use causes or is liable to cause water pollution; "section A1" – including regulations made and orders issued thereunder.	Definitions (Amendment of 5751)
20B. (a) A person shall refrain from any act that pollutes water or is liable to cause water pollution, directly or indirectly, immediately or after a time; and it shall be immaterial whether the water source was polluted before that act or not. (b) A person shall not throw or discharge into or near a water source any liquid, solid, or gaseous substances, nor shall he place them in or near it.	Prohibition of Pollution
20C. A person who possesses an installation for the production, supply, conveyance, storage, or injection of water into the subsoil, shall take all reasonable measures to prevent the installation or its operation from causing water pollution.	Prevention of Water Pollution in Water Installations
20D. (a) To prevent water pollution and to protect water sources from pollution, the Minister of Environmental Protection may, after consultation with the Water Council, make regulations prescribing, inter alia, restrictions, prohibitions, conditions, and other directives concerning— (1) the location and establishment of specified polluting factors; such regulations shall require the approval of the Economics Committee of the Knesset; (2) the use of certain materials or certain methods in the production processes of a polluting factor, its operation, and use, including agricultural processing methods, and also fertilization and spraying; such regulations shall be made in consultation with the Minister of Health; (3) the production, import, distribution, and marketing of certain materials and products;	Regulations for the Prevention of Water Pollution (Amendment of 5751)

such regulations shall be made in consultation with the Minister of Commerce and Industry and shall be brought to the prior knowledge of the Economics Committee of the Knesset;
(4) the regulation of the movement and stay of means of transport and their use, on or near water sources; such regulations shall be made with the consent of the Minister of Transport.
(b) Regulations under this section shall not derogate from the obligations set forth in sections 20B and 20C.

20E. (a) A person who possesses a polluting factor whose operation or use requires the disposal of sewage therefrom, shall, by order of the Water Commissioner, submit for his approval a plan detailing the manner of disposal of the sewage, its nature and quantity, its chemical, physical, and biological composition, and any other detail that the Water Commissioner shall require for this purpose; the Water Commissioner may refuse to approve the plan, or may amend it, or may make it conditional on such terms as he sees fit. (b) If a person has been ordered to submit a plan as aforesaid in subsection (a), the sewage shall not be disposed of as long as the plan has not been approved, but the Water Commissioner may order a temporary manner of sewage disposal until the plan is approved. (c) If a plan for the disposal of sewage has been approved, the sewage shall not be disposed of otherwise than in accordance with the approved plan. (d) If a person who was ordered to submit a plan as aforesaid in subsection (a) has not done so by the date prescribed for it in the order, or if his plan has not been approved, or if he has not carried out the amendments in the plan required of him, or if he has not complied with the conditions on which the plan was made conditional, the Water Commissioner may prepare a plan for the disposal of sewage on his behalf, and upon his doing so, the person shall be liable for the expenses of preparing the plan; the Taxes (Collection) Ordinance, except for section 12 thereof, shall apply to the collection of these expenses. (e) The Water Commissioner shall not use the power vested in him by subsection (d) except at the end of one month from the date prescribed for submitting the plan, carrying out the amendments, or complying with the conditions, as the case may be. (f) A person on whose behalf the Water Commissioner has prepared a plan as aforesaid in subsection (d) shall not dispose of sewage from the polluting factor otherwise than in accordance with the plan so prepared. (g) In all matters concerning the exercise of his powers under this section, the Water Commissioner shall hold prior consultation with a person authorized for that purpose by the Minister of Health.	Disposal of Sewage from a Polluting Factor
20F. The Minister of Environmental Protection or the Water Commissioner, as the case may be, may make conditional any approvals, licenses, and permits granted under this Law or under the Drainage and Flood Control Law, 5718-1957, on conditions concerning the prevention of water pollution.	Imposition of Conditions for the Prevention of Water Pollution (Amendment of 5751)
20G. (a) If the Water Commissioner finds that water pollution has been caused, he may order the person who caused that pollution to do everything necessary to stop the water pollution, to restore the situation to its previous state before the pollution was caused, and to prevent the recurrence of the water pollution, all as shall be detailed in the order. (b) If the provisions of an order under subsection (a) are not fulfilled within a reasonable time prescribed in the order, the Water Commissioner may do everything detailed in the order, and upon his doing so, the person who was ordered and did not comply with the provisions of the order shall be liable for the expenses involved; the Taxes (Collection) Ordinance, except for section 12 thereof, shall apply to the collection of these expenses.	Rectification of Defect
20H. (a) The Water Commissioner may order the cessation of the production, supply, or consumption of water, or their reduction, or may refrain from allocating it (hereinafter: "a cessation order"), to a person who has caused water pollution, or has not heeded an instruction given to him under the provisions of this section, or has violated a provision of this section or a regulation or order thereunder, after being warned thereof, provided that drinking water is not thereby denied to him. (b) A cessation order shall remain in force as long as the pollution has not ceased, the situation has not been restored to its state before the pollution was caused, and as long as steps have not been taken to prevent the recurrence of the water pollution; however, the Water Commissioner may revoke the order, with or without conditions, if it has been proven to him that the person to whom the order was directed is carrying out all that is necessary to stop the water pollution, to restore the situation to its previous state, and to prevent the recurrence of the water pollution, or if a guarantee to his satisfaction has been given by the person to whom the order was directed, for the performance of the said actions within a reasonable time. (c) If a cessation order would affect the consumers of a supplier to whom the order was directed, the order shall not be given until after the Water Commissioner has arranged a supply of proper water for those consumers, as long as the order is in force and on the conditions he has prescribed.	Cessation Order
20I. If the Water Commissioner has found that water pollution was caused in circumstances over which a person has no control, or that there is a foreseeable danger of such pollution, and the matter requires, inter alia, the issuance of a cessation order, a cessation order shall not be given until after the Water Commissioner has arranged, as far as possible and on the conditions he has prescribed, a supply of proper water, as long as the order is in force, to all those whose water was stopped or reduced as a result of the cessation order.	Cessation Order in Special Cases
20J. If the Water Commissioner is convinced that serious water pollution has been caused or that there is a danger of serious water pollution, and the matter requires, inter alia, the immediate cessation or reduction of the production, supply, or consumption of water from a certain water source, he may take all measures that seem to him appropriate in the circumstances of the case, in order to stop or prevent the water pollution or its consequences, and for this purpose, he may use force to the extent necessary.	Emergency Powers
20K. (a) If the Water Commissioner is convinced, after consultation with a person authorized for that purpose by the Minister of Health, of one of the following: (1) that a certain action is intended to improve water quality, enhance its quality, disinfect it, dilute it, prevent danger to the public, and the like, or to convey materials in water for a purpose pre-approved by the Water Commissioner; (2) that the circumstances of the case leave no choice but to permit the disposal of sewage into a certain water source for a specified limited period; the action or the disposal of sewage shall not be deemed water pollution within its meaning in this section, if they are done in accordance with an authorization order given for this purpose by the Water Commissioner. (b) In an authorization order, the Water Commissioner may prescribe conditions, restrictions, and limitations, whether at the time of its issuance or at a later date, and upon his doing so, the person to whom the authorization order was given shall be obligated to act in accordance with the conditions of the order, its restrictions, and its limitations. (c) An authorization order for the matter of subsection (a)(2) shall be a personal and reasoned order and shall be in force for one year, but the Water Commissioner may extend it from time to time, for reasons to be detailed. (d) The Water Commissioner, after consultation with a person authorized for that purpose by the Minister of Health, may revoke the authorization order, or change its conditions, restrictions, and limitations, if the circumstances of the case have changed, or if he has found that the public good requires it, or if he finds that the order or its conditions, restrictions, or limitations have been violated. (e) The Water Commissioner shall submit to the Economics Committee of the Knesset a report on the authorization orders he has given, at times to be determined by the Economics Committee, and at least once a year. (f) The list of authorization orders given by the Water Commissioner shall be open for public inspection without payment.	Authorization Order
20L. (a) The Minister of Environmental Protection or the Water Commissioner, as the case may be, may grant powers under this section or any part thereof, except the power to make regulations having legislative effect and the power to issue cessation orders or authorization orders, to a water authority, a drainage authority, a local authority, or a union of towns or to a corporation as defined in the Water and Sewage Corporations Law, 5761-2001 (hereinafter in this section - an authority), in all matters relating to the prevention of water pollution within their respective jurisdictions. (b) Authorities as aforesaid in subsection (a) having a common interest in preventing water pollution within their jurisdictions, may incorporate as a corporation for the purpose of being granted the powers to prevent water pollution within the jurisdictions of the said authorities. (c) The grant of powers as aforesaid in subsections (a) or (b) shall be made with the consent of the authority to which the powers are granted, and in the case of granting powers to a local authority, to a union of towns, to a corporation as defined in the Water and Sewage Corporations Law, 5761-2001, or to a corporation of authorities as aforesaid in subsection (b) which includes a local authority or a union of towns - also with the consent of the Minister of the Interior. (d) Upon granting powers as aforesaid in subsections (a) or (b), the Minister of Environmental Protection or the Water Commissioner, as the case may be, shall determine by order the powers granted as aforesaid. (e) If power has been granted to an authority as aforesaid in subsection (a), or to a corporation of authorities as aforesaid in subsection (b), the authority or the corporation shall be authorized to act for the purpose of exercising that power, notwithstanding any restriction existing by law or otherwise.	Granting of Powers (Amendments: 5751, 5762)
20M. (a) The Minister of Environmental Protection may, after consultation with the Water Council, prescribe by regulations provisions regarding the quality of water for various purposes, including floodwater and sewage, but excluding the sanitary quality of drinking water, as defined in Part E1 of the Public Health Ordinance, 1940. (b) Regulations as aforesaid in subsection (a), insofar as they relate to public health, shall be made after consultation with the Minister of Health. (c) If regulations as aforesaid in subsection (a) have been made, the Water Commissioner shall not permit the production, supply, and consumption of water, for the various purposes and uses, unless in accordance with the said regulations, and the Water Commissioner may prohibit the production, supply, or consumption of water that is not in accordance with the said regulations, or change the purpose of the water, provided that it is suitable for that purpose.	Provisions Concerning Water Quality (Amendment of 5751)

20O. An order under this section may be general or for a specific person, for a class of persons, for a specific polluting factor, for a class of polluting factors, or for part of a polluting factor, unless otherwise stated in this section.	General and Specific Orders
20P. Regulations and orders under this section may apply to the entire territory of the State or to a part thereof, or to a specific water source, all as detailed in the regulations or orders; an order applied to a part of the State shall be notified in advance to the Economics Committee of the Knesset.	Area of Application

(b) The filing of an appeal under this section shall not stay the execution of the action being appealed unless the Tribunal has ordered it to be stayed, but expenses shall not be collected, where their collection is permitted under the provisions of this section, until after the Tribunal has given its decision on the appeal and according to the results of its decision. (c) The provisions of subsection (b) shall not derogate from the provisions of section 153.	Right of Appeal (Amendment of 5751)

(b) The period of time mentioned in subsection (a) shall not exceed six months from the day this section comes into force.	Transitional Period (Amendment of 5751)

Liability of Officers of a Body of Persons (Amendment of 5751)

Section B: Planning of Water Undertakings

Section C: Special Payment

124A. The Water Commissioner may impose a special payment on a producer or supplier who has done one of the following: (1) produced or supplied water in a quantity exceeding the quantity permitted for production or supply in accordance with the conditions of the license he received or the regulations made under this Law; (2) supplied to a consumer water in a quantity exceeding the quantity permitted to be supplied to that consumer in accordance with the conditions of the license he received or the regulations made under this Law; (3) supplied water to a person whose name was not included in the list of consumers in the license, if there was such a list, except if the consumer's name was replaced as a result of the transfer of ownership or possession of the land or property for which the supply is intended.	Imposition of Special Payment (Amendment of 5721)

Passing the Payment on to the Consumer (Amendment of 5721)

Section D: Linkage and Interest Differentials

124H. In this section – "excess payment" – a payment under this Law that a person paid without being liable for it or an excess payment that a person paid over a sum he was liable for under this Law; "linkage differentials" – an addition according to the rate of increase of the index, as it is calculated, as the case may be— (1) in a payment under this Law – from the index last published before the due date until the index last published before the day of actual payment; (2) in an excess payment – from the index last published before the due date until the index last published before the day of its actual refund; "index" – the consumer price index published by the Central Bureau of Statistics; "due date" – the date for making a payment under this Law as specified in the notice regarding it;	Definitions (Amendment of 5753)
"interest" – interest linked to the index at a rate of 4% per year— (1) on a payment under this Law – from the due date until the date of actual payment; (2) on an excess payment – from the day of its payment until the day of its actual refund.	
124I. (a) A payment under this Law that was not paid on its due date shall be paid with the addition of linkage and interest differentials. (b) The law of linkage and interest differentials, for the purpose of their collection, shall be as the law of the payment for which they are collected.	Arrears Payments (Amendment of 5753)
124J. If a person made an excess payment and it was not refunded to him within thirty days from the day of its payment, the Water Commissioner shall refund it, with the addition of linkage and interest differentials.	Refund of Excess Payments (Amendments: 5753, 5759)
Chapter Five: Organization

Section A: The Water Council and Other Bodies

125. The Government shall appoint a national council to advise the Minister of Agriculture on questions of water policy and to fulfill the functions assigned to it under this Law (in this Law - the Water Council); a notice of the appointment of the Council, of its composition, and of any change therein shall be published in Reshumot.	The Water Council
126. (a) The Water Council shall have not less than 27 and not more than 39 members, including the Minister of Agriculture who shall be the chairman of the Council, the Water Commissioner who shall be the deputy chairman of the Council, public representatives, government representatives, and a representative of the World Zionist Organization and its institutions.	Composition of the Water Council (Amendment of 5725)
(b) The number of public representatives on the Water Council shall be not less than two-thirds of the members of the Council, and the number of government representatives with the representative of the World Zionist Organization shall not exceed one-third of the members of the Council. (c) Among the public representatives shall be consumer representatives who shall be appointed taking into account the extent of consumption for the various water purposes, and supplier representatives, provided that the number of consumer representatives shall not be less than half of all the members of the Council.	
127. (a) A member of the Water Council, other than the Minister of Agriculture and the Water Commissioner, shall be appointed for a period of three years and shall hold office until a member is appointed in his stead, and may be reappointed. (b) An act of the Water Council or of one of its committees shall not be invalidated for the sole reason that at the time of the act, the place of a member of its members was vacant for any reason whatsoever.	Term of Office of Members
128. (a) The Minister of Agriculture shall convene the Water Council as needed or at the request of one-third of its members, but at least once a month, and in the first year, twelve meetings, at least. (b) The Minister of Agriculture shall determine the agenda of the meetings of the Council, except for meetings he convenes at the request of its members.	Convening of the Council
129. The Water Council shall itself determine its rules of procedure and those of its committees, including the legal quorum at its meetings, to the extent that this is not prescribed in regulations; regulations under this section shall be made after consultation with the Water Council.	Rules of Procedure
130. (a) The Water Council may choose from among its members committees and delegate to them its powers; the Council shall not delegate its powers in these matters: (1) questions of water policy under section 125; (2) prescribing norms and rules for the use of water under section 21; (3) declaration of rationing areas under section 36; (4) prescribing rules and rates of compensation payable in a rationing area under section 44; (5) approval of undertaking plans under section 67; (6) approval of existing undertakings as water undertakings under section 74;	Committees (Amendment of 5759)
(7) prescribing rules for the calculation of compensation due from water authorities under section 93; (8) prescribing rules for the calculation of water charges under section 111; (9) prescribing tariffs for water charges under section 112; (10)-(12) (Repealed); (13) prescribing areas for the purpose of this Law under section 155; (14) drawing up a list of public representatives in the Tribunal under section 141. (b) A notice of the election of a committee of the Water Council and of the powers delegated to it shall be published in Reshumot.	
131. The Water Council may, with the consent of the Minister of Agriculture, choose regional committees whose members shall be members of the Council and non-members of the Council, provided that the number of the former is greater than that of the latter. The Water Council may not delegate its powers to a regional committee except in matters relating to that region.	Regional Committees
132. The Water Council shall appoint, with the consent of the Minister of Agriculture, and taking into account the various factors in agriculture, a committee for the supply of water for agriculture to deal with matters related to the supply of water for agriculture, and for the use of water for this purpose, and also a supply committee for other water purposes, to deal with the supply and use of water for the other purposes. A member of a supply committee may be a person who is not a member of the Council.	Supply Committees
133. (a) The Minister of Agriculture shall appoint a committee of not more than 11 members to be the Planning Committee for the examination of undertaking plans under the provisions of this Law or other large-scale water undertaking plans designated for that purpose by the Minister of Agriculture or the Water Commissioner; a notice of the appointment of the Committee, of its composition, and of any change therein shall be published in Reshumot. (b) The Planning Committee shall itself determine its rules of procedure, including the legal quorum at its meetings, to the extent that this is not prescribed in regulations.	Planning Committee (Amendment of 5725)
(c) An act of the Planning Committee shall not be invalidated for the sole reason that at the time of the act, the place of a member of its members was vacant for any reason whatsoever.	
134. (a) The Minister of Agriculture may, by order in Reshumot, establish, next to a regional water authority, a council whose function shall be to represent the consumers of the water authority before it and before the Water Council (hereinafter - a consumers' representation). (b) A consumers' representation shall be entitled to present arguments and proposals before the water authority in all matters relating to the development and expansion of the water undertaking and in all matters relating to the supply of water from it, and the water authority must give the consumers' representation an opportunity to voice arguments and proposals before it before any decision on the development of the undertaking and on a change in the conditions of the supply of water from it.	Consumers' Representation Before a Water Authority

If a regional committee of the Water Council has been established for a certain area in accordance with section 131, the Minister of Agriculture may, by order in Reshumot, establish a consumers' representation in that area whose function shall be to represent the consumers of the area before the said committee and before the Water Council.	Consumers' Representation Before a Regional Committee

Section C: The Tribunal for Water Affairs

140. (a) The Minister of Justice shall establish, by order, a tribunal or tribunals for water affairs and shall determine their seat and area of jurisdiction. (b) The Tribunal shall be competent to hear, in addition to the matters assigned to it under this Law, all matters assigned to the jurisdiction and decision of a tribunal of appeal under the Drainage and Flood Control Law, 5718-1957.	Establishment of the Tribunal, its Jurisdiction and Powers
141. (a) A tribunal for water affairs (in this law - the Tribunal) shall sit in a panel of three; a judge appointed by the Minister of Justice and two public representatives from a list drawn up by the Minister of Agriculture, after consultation with the Water Council and with organizations of local authorities. (b) The public representatives who will sit on a particular case shall be determined by the president of the district court in whose jurisdiction the seat of the Tribunal is located. (c) The judge is the presiding judge of the Tribunal.	Composition of the Tribunal
142. (a) With the consent of the parties, the presiding judge of the Tribunal may, sitting alone, hear them and give the force of a judgment to a compromise agreement between them. (b) The presiding judge of the Tribunal may, sitting alone, grant interim orders and stay orders.	Powers of the Presiding Judge of the Tribunal
143. Within its jurisdiction, the force of the Tribunal shall be as the force of a district court in a civil matter.	Powers of the Tribunal
144. The Tribunal may, for reasons it shall detail in its decision, accept evidence even if it would not be admissible in a court.	Evidence
145. The Tribunal hearing an appeal may confirm the decision or action being appealed, with or without amendments, or set it aside, and may also grant interim orders.	Decisions of the Tribunal
146. A judgment and another decision of the Tribunal are appealable and enforceable as a judgment and another decision of a district court in a civil matter.	Appeal and Execution
147. The Minister of Justice may make regulations regarding the rules of procedure before the Tribunal, including the time limits for appeals, to the extent that they are not prescribed in this Law, the holding of sessions of the Tribunal outside its seat, appeals on its decisions, and the fees payable in the Tribunal, and he may also prescribe the remuneration to be paid to the public representatives in the Tribunal.	Regulations of the Tribunal
