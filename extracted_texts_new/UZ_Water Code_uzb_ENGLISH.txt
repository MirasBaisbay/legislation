29-30 05 2025 TASHKENT LAW SPRING IV INTERNATIONAL JURIDICAL FORUM

**LAW OF THE REPUBLIC OF UZBEKISTAN**

**ON WATER AND WATER USE**

**CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS**

(See previous version.)

**Article 1. Purpose and Main Tasks of this Law**

The purpose of this Law is to regulate water relations.

The main tasks of this Law are to ensure the rational use of water for the needs of the population and sectors of the economy, to protect waters from pollution, contamination, and depletion, to prevent and eliminate the adverse effects of water, to improve the condition of water bodies, and also to protect the rights and legitimate interests of enterprises, institutions, organizations, farmer and dekhkan farms, and citizens in the sphere of water relations.
(Article 1 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 2. Water Legislation**
(Title of Article 2 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

(See previous version.)

In the Republic of Uzbekistan, water relations are regulated by this Law and other legislative acts on water issued in accordance with it.
(Part one of Article 2 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

(See previous version.)

In the Republic of Karakalpakstan, water relations are also regulated by the legislation of the Republic of Karakalpakstan.
(Part two of Article 2 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

(See previous version.)

**Article 2¹. Basic Concepts**

The following basic concepts are used in this Law:

**groundwater** — waters located below the earth's surface in the rock strata of the earth's crust;

**surface water** — waters located on the surface of the earth's crust;

**melioration facilities** — water management facilities that facilitate the collection of collector-drainage and surface runoff water and its discharge outside of irrigated lands, and include collectors and collector-drainage networks, vertical drainage wells, melioration pumping stations (units), and a monitoring network;

**waters** — the aggregate of all waters accumulated in water bodies;

**water consumption** — the use of water resources by legal entities and natural persons to satisfy their needs by withdrawing them from a water body in the established manner;

**water consumers' association** — a non-governmental non-profit organization established on a voluntary basis by water consumers that are legal entities to coordinate their activities in the sphere of water relations, as well as to represent and protect their common interests;

(See previous version.)

**water consumer** — a legal entity or natural person who withdraws water resources from a water body or a water management facility in the established manner to satisfy their needs;
(Paragraph eight of Article 2¹ as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

**water body** — natural (streams, brooks, rivers, etc.) and artificial (open and closed canals, as well as collector-drainage networks) watercourses where waters are permanently or temporarily accumulated and which have specific forms and signs of a water regime, natural (lakes, seas, groundwater aquifers) and artificial (reservoirs, mudflow collection sites, ponds, etc.) water basins, as well as springs and other objects;

**protection of water bodies** — a system of measures aimed at preserving and restoring water bodies;

**water regime** — changes over time in the level, velocity, flow rate, and volume of water in water bodies and soil-ground;

**water resources** — the waters of water bodies that are used or are to be used;

**water management** — the branch of the economy that covers the study, accounting, management, use, and protection of water resources and water bodies, as well as combating the adverse effects of water;

**water management facility** — a water body where water management activities are carried out for the purpose of collecting, managing, delivering, using, consuming, allocating, and protecting water resources;

(See previous version.)

**water management operating organizations** — organizations that operate water management facilities ("Water Supply Service" state institutions, irrigation system departments, main canal (system) departments, pumping station and energy departments, melioration expeditions, reservoir and large hydraulic structure operation departments, the Ministry of Water Resources of the Republic of Karakalpakstan, basin irrigation system departments under the Ministry of Water Resources of the Republic of Uzbekistan);
(Paragraph fifteen of Article 2¹ as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-981 of October 22, 2024 — National Database of Legislation, 24.10.2024, No. 03/24/981/0841)

**water use** — the use of water resources by legal entities and natural persons for their needs without withdrawing them from a water body;

**water user** — a legal entity or natural person who uses water resources for their needs without withdrawing them from a water body;

**adverse effects of water** — erosion, destruction, silting, waterlogging, salinization, and other negative phenomena of certain natural and economic facilities and territories as a result of flooding, inundation, waterlogging, and other effects of water;

(See previous version.)

**payment for water supply and other water management services** — making payments on a contractual basis for water supply services provided by water supply organizations (water management operating organizations, water consumers' associations, etc.) (except for water supply services for agricultural needs provided by water management operating organizations) and other water management services (repair of water management facilities, introduction of water-saving irrigation technologies, assistance in the use of water management and accounting tools);
(Paragraph nineteen of Article 2¹ as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-981 of October 22, 2024 — National Database of Legislation, 24.10.2024, No. 03/24/981/0841)

**transboundary water bodies** — water bodies that cross or are located on the borders of two or more states;

**transboundary waters** — any surface or groundwater that crosses or is located on the borders of two or more states;
(Article 2¹ introduced by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

**Article 3. State Ownership of Water**

Water is the state property of the Republic of Uzbekistan, a national asset, which must be used rationally and is protected by the state.

**Article 4. Unified State Water Fund**

(See previous version.)

The Unified State Water Fund consists of:
the waters of streams, brooks, rivers, reservoirs, lakes, seas, canals, collector-drainage networks, springs, ponds, and other surface waters;
groundwater, snow reserves, and glaciers.

The right to use the waters of transboundary water bodies (the Amu Darya, Syr Darya, Zarafshan rivers, the Aral Sea, and other transboundary water bodies) is determined by the international treaties of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
(Text of Article 4 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**CHAPTER III. STATE MANAGEMENT AND CONTROL IN THE SPHERE OF WATER USE AND PROTECTION**

**Article 8. State Management in the Sphere of Water Use**

(See previous version.)

State management in the sphere of water use is carried out by the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan, local state authorities, as well as specially authorized state administration bodies that regulate water use directly or through basin (territorial) departments, and other state bodies.
(Part one of Article 8 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

The Ministry of Water Resources of the Republic of Uzbekistan (for surface waters) and the State Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan for Geology and Mineral Resources (for groundwater) are the specially authorized state administration bodies in the sphere of regulating water use within their powers.
(Part two of Article 8 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

**Article 9. State Control over Water Use and Protection**

(See previous version.)

The task of state control over water use and protection is to ensure compliance by all ministries, agencies, enterprises, institutions, organizations, farmer and dekhkan farms, and citizens with the established procedure for water use, the fulfillment of duties for water protection, prevention and elimination of the adverse effects of water, the rules for water accounting, as well as other rules established by water legislation.
(Part one of Article 9 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-880 of November 28, 2023 — National Database of Legislation, 29.11.2023, No. 03/23/880/0905)

(See previous version.)

State control over water use and protection is carried out by local state authorities, the State Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan for Ecology and Environmental Protection, the State Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan for Geology and Mineral Resources, the Ministry of Health of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the Ministry of Water Resources of the Republic of Uzbekistan, and the Inspectorate for Control over the Use of Drinking Water under the Ministry of Housing and Communal Services of the Republic of Uzbekistan in the manner prescribed by legislation.
(Part two of Article 9 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-898 of January 17, 2024 — National Database of Legislation, 18.01.2024, No. 03/24/898/0046)

(See previous version.)

**Article 10. Participation of Water Consumers' Associations, Other Non-Governmental Non-Profit Organizations, and Citizens in the Implementation of Measures for the Rational Use and Protection of Waters and Water Bodies**

Water consumers' associations, other non-governmental non-profit organizations in accordance with their charters, and citizens assist state bodies in implementing measures for the rational use and protection of waters and water bodies. State bodies may take into account the proposals of water consumers' associations, other non-governmental non-profit organizations, and citizens when carrying out these measures.
(Article 10 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

**CHAPTER IV. SITING, DESIGN, CONSTRUCTION, RECONSTRUCTION, REPAIR, RESTORATION, AND COMMISSIONING OF ENTERPRISES, STRUCTURES, AND OTHER FACILITIES AFFECTING THE CONDITION OF WATERS AND WATER BODIES**
(Title of Chapter IV as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 11. Conditions for Siting, Design, Construction, Reconstruction, Repair, Restoration, and Commissioning of Enterprises, Structures, and Other Facilities Affecting the Condition of Waters and Water Bodies**

(See previous version.)

When siting, designing, constructing, reconstructing, repairing, restoring, and commissioning enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the condition of waters and water bodies, and when introducing new technological processes, rational water use must be ensured, observing the requirements for safeguarding public health and primarily satisfying drinking and domestic water needs, as well as a convenient regime for the discharge of collector-drainage and wastewater. In doing so, measures shall be envisaged to ensure the accounting of water withdrawn from and returned to water bodies, the protection of waters from pollution, contamination, and depletion, the prevention of the adverse effects of water, the minimization of land flooding, the protection of lands from salinization, waterlogging, or desiccation, as well as the preservation of favorable natural conditions and landscapes.
(Part one of Article 11 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

Projects for the construction and reconstruction of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the condition of waters and water bodies must be developed taking into account the possibilities of using water bodies for recreation and sports.
(Article 11 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 12. Conditions for Siting, Design, Construction, Reconstruction, and Commissioning of Enterprises, Structures, and Other Facilities in Fishery Water Basins**

When siting, designing, constructing, reconstructing, and commissioning new and repaired enterprises, structures, and other facilities in fishery water basins, in addition to complying with the requirements of Article 15 of this Law, measures must be timely implemented to compensate for the damage caused to fish stocks, to protect other aquatic animals and plants, and to ensure their preservation, restoration, and reproduction.
(Article 12 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 13. Determining the Location for Construction, Reconstruction, Repair, and Restoration of Enterprises, Structures, and Other Facilities Affecting the Condition of Waters and Water Bodies**

(See previous version.)

The location for the construction, reconstruction, repair, and restoration of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the condition of waters and water bodies shall be determined in agreement with the organizations using these water bodies, as well as other interested organizations, in accordance with legislation.
(Text of Article 13 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

**Article 14. Approval and State Expert Review of Projects for the Construction, Reconstruction, Repair, and Restoration of Enterprises, Structures, and Other Facilities Affecting the Condition of Waters and Water Bodies**

(Article 14 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 15. Prohibition on Commissioning Enterprises, Structures, and Other Facilities Affecting the Condition of Waters and Water Bodies**

It is prohibited to commission:
new and reconstructed enterprises, workshops, units, communal, and other facilities that are not equipped with devices preventing the pollution and contamination of waters, or that have a negative impact on the condition of waters and water bodies;
irrigation and water supply systems, reservoirs, and canals until the measures provided for in the projects for preventing land flooding, waterlogging, swamping, salinization, and soil erosion have been implemented;
drainage networks without the completion of water intake and other structures in accordance with approved projects;
water intake structures without fish protection devices in accordance with approved projects;
hydraulic structures where devices for passing floodwaters and fish are not ready, in accordance with approved projects;
a group of water intake facilities for which groundwater reserves have not been approved;
drilled wells for water abstraction without being equipped with water-regulating devices and, in relevant cases, without the establishment of sanitary protection zones.

It is prohibited to fill reservoirs with water until the measures for preparing their beds as specified in the project have been implemented.
(Article 15 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 16. Approval of Projects for the Construction, Reconstruction, Repair, and Restoration of Bridges, Crossings, and Other Transport Communications Across Water Bodies**

(See previous version.)

Projects for the construction and reconstruction of bridges, crossings, and other transport communications across water bodies must be agreed upon with water management, ecology and environmental protection, and water management facility safety control bodies, the Ministry of Emergency Situations of the Republic of Uzbekistan, and, where necessary, with energy, architecture and construction bodies, and the Ministry of Transport of the Republic of Uzbekistan (regarding river transport), and must also provide for measures to ensure the passage of floodwaters, the water body use regime, the prevention of pollution, contamination, and depletion of waters, and the prevention of their adverse effects.
(Part one of Article 16 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

Projects for the repair and restoration of bridges, crossings, and other transport communications across water bodies that affect the condition of waters and water bodies must be agreed upon with water management bodies, as well as with other bodies in accordance with legislation, and must provide for measures to ensure the passage of floodwaters, the water body use regime, and the prevention of water pollution and contamination.
(Part two of Article 16 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

**CHAPTER VI. WATER USERS, WATER CONSUMERS, AND THE FACILITIES THEY USE**

**Article 18. Water Users**

Water management operating organizations, water consumers' associations, communal-domestic sector enterprises, hydropower enterprises, other enterprises, institutions, organizations, citizens of the Republic of Uzbekistan, citizens of other states, and stateless persons may be water users.
(Article 18 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 18¹. Water Consumers**

Enterprises, institutions, organizations, farmer and dekhkan farms, as well as citizens of the Republic of Uzbekistan, citizens of other states, and stateless persons may be water consumers.

**Article 18². Water Consumers' Associations**

Water consumers' associations are primarily established based on the hydrographic principle or other conditions that ensure the rational management and use of water resources.

The founders of water consumers' associations may be farmer farms, dekhkan farms that have established a legal entity, as well as other water consumers that are legal entities.

Members of water consumers' associations may be farmer and dekhkan farms, citizens' self-governing bodies, as well as other water consumers.

(See previous version.)

Water relations between a water consumers' association and its members located in its service area, as well as with other water management operating organizations and other legal entities and natural persons, are regulated on a contractual basis.
(Part four of Article 18² as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

The financing of the activities of water consumers' associations is carried out at the expense of the annual membership fees of their members, payments for water supply and other water management services on a contractual basis, as well as other sources not prohibited by legislation.

In the event of violation of water use rules, non-purposeful use of water, as well as withdrawal of water in excess of the limit, and untimely payment of membership fees, payments for water supply and other water management services, water consumers' associations have the right to suspend water supply to water consumers until the identified violations of the law and the terms of the contract are eliminated.
(Article 18² supplemented with parts five and six in accordance with the Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

**Article 19. Water Use Facilities**

(See previous version.)

Water bodies or parts thereof may be water use facilities and may be granted for use on the principles of public-private partnership and other terms in the manner prescribed by legislation, with the exception of reservoirs, mudflow-reservoirs, as well as large and particularly important water management facilities.
(Text of Article 19 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

**Article 19¹. Water Consumption Sources**

(See previous version.)

Water bodies or parts thereof may be water consumption sources and may be provided for water consumption in the manner prescribed by legislation.
(Text of Article 19¹ as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

**Article 20. Partial or Complete Prohibition on the Use of Water Bodies**

(See previous version.)

The use of water bodies of special state significance or of special scientific or cultural value may be partially or completely prohibited in the manner prescribed by legislation.
(Text of Article 20 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

**CHAPTER VII. TYPES OF WATER USE AND WATER CONSUMPTION**
(Title of Chapter VII as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

**Article 21. Types of Water Use**

Depending on the method of water withdrawal from a water body and the impact on the condition of waters and water bodies, water use is divided into general and special water use.

Water use by natural persons without the use of special structures and devices that affect the condition of waters and water bodies for the purpose of satisfying their own and other needs is general water use.

Water use carried out by legal entities and natural persons with the use of special structures and devices that affect the condition of waters and water bodies is special water use.

In certain cases, special water use may also include water use without the application of special structures and devices, but which affects the condition of waters and water bodies.

(See previous version.)

Lists of types of general and special water use are determined by the Ministry of Water Resources of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the Ministry of Health of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the State Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan for Ecology and Environmental Protection, and the State Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan for Geology and Mineral Resources in accordance with legislation.
(Part four of Article 21 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

Water bodies may be in sole or joint use.

(See previous version.)

A water body granted entirely or partially for use to a legal entity or natural person in the manner prescribed by legislation is a water body in sole use.

**Article 21¹. Types of Water Consumption**

According to its intended purpose, water consumption is divided into drinking, communal-domestic, therapeutic, resort, recreational, fishery, industrial, energy, agricultural water consumption, and other types.

Water consumption by natural persons without the use of special structures and devices that affect the condition of waters and water bodies for the purpose of satisfying their personal drinking, domestic, recreational, and therapeutic needs, watering animals, and satisfying other needs is general water consumption.

Water consumption carried out by legal entities and natural persons with the use of special structures and devices that affect the condition of waters and water bodies is special water consumption.

**Article 36. Grounds for Terminating the Right of Water Use or Water Consumption**

The right of water use or water consumption is terminated when:
there is no longer a need for water use or water consumption, or upon their relinquishment;
the term of water use or water consumption expires;
an enterprise, institution, organization, or farmer farm is liquidated, or a dekhkan farm is liquidated (its activity is terminated);

a need arises to reclaim water bodies in sole use.

**CHAPTER XXIV. PROTECTION OF WATERS AND WATER BODIES**
(Title of Chapter XXIV as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

**Article 97. Tasks of Water Protection**

All waters (water bodies) must be protected from cases that may cause harm to public health, as well as a decrease in fish stocks, a deterioration of water supply conditions, and a decline in the physical, chemical, and biological properties of water, a decrease in the natural self-purification capacity of water, a disturbance of the hydrological and hydrogeological regime of water, leading to other adverse phenomena.

(See previous version.)

**Article 98. Implementation of Measures Ensuring the Protection of Waters and Water Bodies, as well as the Improvement of the Water Regime and Water Condition**

(See previous version.)

Enterprises, organizations, and institutions whose activities affect the condition of waters and water bodies are obliged to carry out technological, forest-melioration and agrotechnical, hydraulic, and sanitary-technical measures in agreement with ecology and environmental protection, water management, and sanitary control bodies, as well as other interested agencies.
(Text of Article 98 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-638 of September 28, 2020 — National Database of Legislative Acts, 28.09.2020, No. 03/20/638/1333)

(See previous version.)

**Article 99. Protection of Waters and Water Bodies from Contamination and Pollution by Waste and Refuse**
(Title of Article 99 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

Enterprises, organizations, institutions, and citizens are prohibited from:
dumping industrial, domestic, and other types of waste and refuse into water bodies;

(See previous version.)

polluting and contaminating waters and water bodies as a result of spills of oils, timber, chemical and petroleum products, and other products;
(Paragraph three of part one of Article 99 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

polluting and contaminating the surface of water basins, the surface of ice and glaciers covering water basins, with industrial waste, domestic refuse, and other discarded materials, as well as with petroleum and chemical products that could worsen the quality of surface and groundwater;

(See previous version.)

polluting waters with fertilizers, toxic chemicals, and other harmful substances;
(Paragraph five of part one of Article 99 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

(See previous version.)

violating the regime of water protection and sanitary-protection zones of water bodies.
(Part one of Article 99 supplemented with paragraph six in accordance with the Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

The discharge of wastewater into water bodies is permitted only in compliance with the requirements specified in Articles 74 and 75 of this Law.
(Part two of Article 99 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

**Article 100. Water Protection Zones, Riparian Strips, and Sanitary Protection Zones of Water Bodies**

(See previous version.)

For the purpose of preventing and eliminating the pollution, contamination, depletion, and silting of water bodies with soil erosion products, as well as maintaining a favorable water regime and creating normal conditions for operation and repair-restoration works, water protection zones and riparian strips of water bodies are established in accordance with legislation.

For the purpose of protecting waters used for the drinking, domestic, and therapeutic-health-improvement needs of the population, sanitary protection zones of water bodies are established in accordance with legislation.
(Parts one and two of Article 100 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

Depending on the function, location, and technical parameters of water bodies, a special regime of economic activity is established in the water protection zones, riparian strips, and sanitary protection zones of water bodies.

The procedure and conditions for the protection and use of water protection zones, riparian strips, and sanitary protection zones of water bodies are established by the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
(Article 100 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

**Article 101. Protection of Groundwater**

Agencies engaged in the extraction and use of groundwater are obliged to monitor compliance with the groundwater regimes in the extraction area and adjacent territories, as well as to keep records of the quantity and quality of the water being used.

(See previous version.)

If groundwater-bearing strata are discovered during drilling and other mining-prospecting works related to the exploration, study, and exploitation of mineral deposits, the geology and mineral resources bodies must be informed, and measures aimed at protecting groundwater must be taken in the established manner.
(Part two of Article 101 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

In all cases where wells drilled for the discharge of industrial wastewater may become a source of contamination of water-bearing strata, the drilling of such wells is prohibited.
(Part three of Article 101 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

The drilling of wells into groundwater is carried out on the basis of a permit issued by the State Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan for Geology and Mineral Resources in the manner established by the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
(Article 101 supplemented with part four in accordance with the Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

Wells that spontaneously discharge water and whose water is unsuitable for use must be equipped with water control equipment, and they must be suspended or liquidated in the manner prescribed by legislation.
(Part four of Article 101 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

In the vicinity of sources where high-quality groundwater accumulates, the accumulation of solid and liquid waste, the creation of dumps, and the construction of industrial, agricultural, and other facilities that could potentially become a source of groundwater pollution are not permitted.

Measures for the protection of groundwater, including the creation of a network of observation wells, are carried out by enterprises whose activities affect the condition of groundwater.

**Article 102. Protection of Small Rivers**

(See previous version.)

Enterprises, institutions, organizations, agricultural cooperatives, farmer and dekhkan farms whose economic activities have a negative impact on the condition and regime of small rivers must, together with water management and ecology and environmental protection bodies, take measures to preserve the water, its purity, and quality.
(Part one of Article 102 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-995 of November 8, 2024 — National Database of Legislation, 11.11.2024, No. 03/24/995/0908 — Date of entry into force — February 12, 2025)

**CHAPTER XXVI. STATE ACCOUNTING AND PLANNING OF WATER USE**

**Article 107. Tasks of State Accounting of Water and Its Use**

(See previous version.)

The task of state accounting of water and its use is to determine the quantity and quality of waters constituting the unified water fund, and data on water use for the needs of the population and sectors of the economy.
(Text of Article 107 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-240 of December 25, 2009 — Collection of Legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2009, No. 52, Art. 555)

**Article 108. Planning of Water Use and Its Protection**

The planning of water use must ensure, first and foremost, the satisfaction of the drinking and domestic needs of the population, the scientifically-based distribution of water among water users, the protection of water, and the prevention of the adverse effects of water.

When planning water use, data from the state water cadastre, water management balances, and schemes for the integrated use and protection of water are taken into account.

**Article 109. State Water Cadastre**

The state water cadastre includes data on the accounting of water by quantitative and qualitative indicators, and the registration and accounting of water use.

**Article 110. Water Management Balances**

(See previous version.)

To assess the available quantity of water and the level of its use, water management balances are drawn up for river basins, basin irrigation systems, and economic regions.
(Text of Article 110 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. 568-II of December 12, 2003 — Bulletin of the Oliy Majlis of the Republic of Uzbekistan, 2004, No. 1-2, Art. 18)

(See previous version.)

Water management balances are drawn up by the specially authorized state administration bodies in the sphere of regulating water use, as provided for in part two of Article 8 of this Law, within the scope of their powers, and are consolidated by the Ministry of Water Resources of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
(Part two of Article 110 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

**Article 111. Schemes for the Integrated Use and Protection of Water**

(See previous version.)

General and basin (territorial) schemes for the integrated use and protection of water, including waters discharged for ecosystem needs, determine the main water management and other measures that must be implemented to satisfy the future water needs of the population and sectors of the economy, as well as for water protection and the prevention of the adverse effects of water.
(Text of Article 111 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

**Article 112. Procedure for State Accounting of Water and Its Use, Maintaining the State Water Cadastre, Drawing up Water Management Balances, and Developing Schemes for the Integrated Use and Protection of Water**

The work of state accounting of water and its use, maintaining the state water cadastre, drawing up water management balances, and developing schemes for the integrated use and protection of water is carried out at the expense of the budget in the manner determined by the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan.

(See previous version.)

**Article 113. Procedure for Maintaining Water Accounting, Reporting, and Monitoring in Water Use and Water Consumption**

Special water users and special water consumers maintain records and reports on water use in the established manner.

Water resources monitoring is carried out by authorized bodies for the purpose of identifying and assessing changes in water resources, including groundwater, preventing and eliminating negative processes, and developing forecasts of the state of water resources.

The procedure for accounting, reporting, and monitoring of water resources during special water use and special water consumption is established by the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
(Article 113 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-733 of November 30, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 01.12.2021, No. 03/21/733/1112 — shall enter into force on March 2, 2022)

(See previous version.)

**CHAPTER XXVII. LIABILITY FOR VIOLATION OF WATER LEGISLATION**
(Title of Chapter XXVII as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

**Article 114. Invalidity of Transactions Violating the State's Right of Ownership to Water**

The transfer of the right of water use and other transactions that overtly or covertly violate the state's right of ownership to water are deemed invalid.

(See previous version.)

**Article 115. Liability for Violation of Water Legislation**

Persons guilty of violating water legislation shall be held liable in the established manner.
(Article 115 as amended by Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. ZRU-683 of April 21, 2021 — National Database of Legislation, 21.04.2021, No. 03/21/683/0375)

**Article 116. Return of Arbitrarily Seized Water Bodies**

Arbitrarily seized water bodies are returned to their owner without compensation for the expenses incurred during their illegal use.

**Article 117. Recovery of Damages Caused by Violation of Water Legislation**
