ASA SALVADORAN WATER AUTHORITY
General Water Resources Law

GOVERNMENT OF EL SALVADOR

GOVERNMENT OF EL SALVADOR
SALVADORAN WATER AUTHORITY
General Water Resources Law

DECREE NO. 253

THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF THE REPUBLIC OF EL SALVADOR,

CONSIDERING:

I. That Article 1 of the Constitution of the Republic establishes that the human person is the origin and the end of all State activity, which is organized to achieve justice, legal certainty, and the common good; likewise, it is the obligation of the State to ensure for the inhabitants of the Republic the enjoyment of liberty, health, culture, economic well-being, and social justice.

II. That the right to health and life is intrinsically related to having guaranteed access to drinking water and sanitation, for which reason the State must recognize the human right to water and sanitation, wherein all persons may have clean, sufficient, safe, acceptable, accessible, and affordable water, in terms of quantity, quality, continuity, and coverage.

III. That in accordance with Art. 117 of the Constitution of the Republic, it is the duty of the State to protect natural resources, as well as the diversity and integrity of the environment, to guarantee sustainable development and declares the protection, conservation, rational use, recovery, or replacement of natural resources to be of social interest.

IV. That El Salvador has ratified international treaties in which it directly or indirectly acquires responsibility for the integrated management of water; likewise, and within the international framework, important principles have been established for the sustainable management of water, such as the Principles contained in the Dublin Statement on Water and Sustainable Development; the Berlin Rules on Water Resources; and more recently, the recognition of the Human Right to Water and the Human Right to Sanitation by the United Nations General Assembly.

V. That water must be constituted as a national public good, inalienable, unseizable, and imprescriptible, for which reason its dominion, use, and enjoyment belong to all the inhabitants of the country; consequently, one of the main purposes that the State must pursue is the issuance of a regulation that allows for ensuring the integrated management of water, its sustainability, and the water security of the present and future population; which directly impacts the improvement of the quality of life of all inhabitants and the promotion of the country's economic and social development.

VI. That there are norms or articles scattered in different legal bodies that regulate the protection, conservation, and sectoral use of water; however, it is necessary to have a dedicated institutional framework for the issue of water resources that specifically regulates and orders its use and utilization, and establishes the legal framework for its administration and sustainable management.

VII. That increasing climate variability is already generating growing irregularity in rainfall patterns and excess precipitation that cause great damage to agriculture, infrastructure, and the population, as well as severe deficits at different times of the year that put the human water supply and food security at risk due to its lack or scarcity.

VIII. That women play a fundamental role in the supply, management, and protection of water, for which reason the recognition of this reality demands effective policies that address their needs and interests and that strengthen their capacities to participate at all levels in policies and programs on the use and management of water resources.

IX. That for all the considerations previously stated, it is imperative to have a regulation that establishes the institutional framework, provisions, procedures, and instruments to order and regulate the integrated management of the country's water resources.

THEREFORE,

in exercise of its constitutional powers and at the initiative of the President of the Republic, through the Ministers of Environment and Natural Resources and of Public Works and Transportation,

DECREES the following:

**GENERAL WATER RESOURCES LAW**

**TITLE ONE**
**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**CHAPTER I**
**RIGHT, OBJECTIVE, AND SCOPE OF APPLICATION**

**Human right to water and sanitation**
**Art. 1.-** El Salvador recognizes the human right to water, this being the right of all persons to have quality, sufficient, safe, accessible, and affordable water. Likewise, it also recognizes the human right to sanitation, this being the right of persons, without any discrimination, to sanitation that is healthy, hygienic, safe, social, and guarantees dignity. Both rights are components of the right to an adequate standard of living.

The State, in all its fundamental government bodies and its institutions, has the primary obligation and responsibility to guarantee its population the effective enjoyment of the human right to drinking water and the human right to sanitation, with gender equity and equality and without any discrimination, ensuring environmental sustainability for present and future generations, and must adopt all policies, legislation, and measures that lead to the full realization of this right.

In virtue of all the foregoing, water may not be privatized under any condition.

**Objective**
**Art. 2.-** This Law is of a public order and its objective is to regulate the integrated management of waters, their sustainability, guarantee the human right to water, and water security for a better quality of life for all the inhabitants of the country; and to promote human, social, and economic development through the sustainable use of water resources.

**Scope of application**
**Art. 3.-** The scope of application of this Law includes all continental, insular, estuarine, marine, underground, and atmospheric waters, whatever their location within the national territory, regardless of their physical state, quality, or natural condition, all in accordance with Article 84 of the Constitution.

**Purpose of the Law**
**Art. 4.-** This Law has the following purposes:
a) To guarantee the exercise of the human right to water.
b) To regulate the normative framework on the management of water as a national good, including the rights, use, utilization, protection, conservation, and recovery, the protection of river basins and ecosystems, respecting the phases of the hydrological cycle.
c) To carry out actions that promote water sanitation in order to guarantee water security that fosters hygiene and human dignity.
d) To create the institutional framework that orders and coordinates the uses and utilization of water resources, as well as the proper management of discharges.
e) To develop planning, technical, legal, and economic-financial instruments for the integrated management of the resource.
f) To establish coordination between state agencies, municipal governments, civil society, the academic sector, and productive sectors for proper water management.
g) To promote the active and co-responsible participation of Salvadoran society and, in particular, equity and equality between men and women in access to, use of, and decisions about water resources.

**National Public Good**
**Art 5.-** Water is a national public good, inalienable, unseizable, and imprescriptible; its dominion, use, and enjoyment belong to all the inhabitants of the country; consequently, it may not be privatized under any condition.

The regulation, integrated management, and administration of water resources correspond to the State, in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Rainwater directly collected and artificially stored by private individuals is excepted, provided that it is not subject to commercialization, does not cause prejudice to third parties, nor significantly affects other water sources.

**Public Water Domain**
**Art. 6.-** The public water domain is comprised of:
a) Continental, insular, estuarine, and marine waters, whether surface, atmospheric, or underground, including sub-surface waters and the subsoil strata that contain them, whatever their condition.
b) The beds and banks of natural, continuous, or discontinuous watercourses.
c) The beds of lakes, lagoons, reservoirs, estuaries, and marshes, whether natural or artificial.
d) The beaches of the sea, lakes, and lagoons.
e) Lands flooded during ordinary maximum floods for a return period of twenty-five years for lakes, lagoons, reservoirs, rivers, and desalinated waters.
f) Aquifers.
g) Waters originating from the desalination of seawater.
h) All saltwater forests/mangroves in the national territory.

**Declaration of public utility and social interest**
**Art. 7.-** The works and projects carried out by the State for the conservation, protection, improvement, utilization, and use of water resources are declared to be of public utility and social interest, especially activities aimed at:
a) Management, handling, protection, improvement, and conservation of river basins, aquifers, and surface receiving media; as well as those related to the infiltration of water for the recharge of aquifers.
b) Re-establishing and maintaining the hydrological balance of surface or subsoil waters, whether stored naturally or artificially, including limitations on extraction, closed seasons, and reserves, in accordance with this Law and its regulations.
c) Control and improvement of the quality of water bodies, as well as wastewater, its recirculation, and residues. In such a way that access to the water resource is guaranteed for present and future generations.
d) Timely and efficient management of the water resource in the face of extreme events of hydrometeorological origin, including prevention, mitigation, attenuation, and risk reduction measures; and adaptation measures in the face of climate change.

**CHAPTER II**
**PRINCIPLES AND DEFINITIONS**

**General principles of the Law**
**Art. 8.-** The management, use, utilization, protection, and conservation of the water resource shall be based on the principles established in the Constitution, in environmental law, and in international instruments. These are the following:
a) **Common, vital, finite, and vulnerable good:** Water is a common, finite, vulnerable, and essential good for human life and ecosystems, which, due to its interrelation with human activities and political, economic, social, cultural, and environmental aspects, becomes a strategic resource.
b) **Co-responsibility:** Shared responsibility and work between State bodies, municipal governments, community organizations (peasant, environmental, consumer, academic, women's, and user organizations at the local, regional, and national levels) in the integrated management of water resources.
c) **Efficiency:** To optimize the utilization in the different uses of water, considering its management, protection, and conservation.
d) **The polluter-pays principle:** It is the responsibility of every natural or legal person, whether public, municipal, or private, to assume the economic costs of repairing, mitigating, compensating for, and restoring any deterioration or damage they cause in the management of the water resource, regardless of its value.
e) **River basin approach:** The river basin is recognized as the primary unit in water resource management.
f) **Rights-based approach:** The design and execution of State policies and actions regarding water shall be carried out based on the constituent elements of the human right to water and the corresponding obligations of the State as recognized in the Constitution, International Human Rights Treaties, and relevant secondary laws.
g) **Equity:** Proper allocation of water resources among the members of society, according to the different conditions and needs of individuals and social groups and the availability of the resource; consequently, appropriate measures must be taken to equalize opportunities and allow all persons to enjoy the right.
h) **Risk management:** Water management must include the prevention of hydrometeorological risks that threaten the population, their property, and ecosystems, and the mitigation of the harmful effects they may cause.
i) **Equality and non-discrimination:** The enjoyment of the rights established in this Law may not be restricted based on differences of nationality, race, gender, or religion.
j) **In Dubio Pro Aqua:** Which establishes that, in case of doubt about the scope of legal provisions, the norm that most favors the preservation of the water resource, that guarantees a dignified life, health, culture, and the protection and conservation of ecosystems, shall be applied.
k) **Integrality:** The management of the water resource entails an integrated river basin approach that links the improvement of the population's quality of life with the protection of natural ecosystems, including their links with the territory and biological diversity, whether of the basins or the aquifers.
l) **Public Interest:** The prevalence of the collective interest over the private interest.
m) **Citizen participation:** The sustainable and integrated management of waters requires the equitable participation and responsible commitment of the entire population and, in particular, the recognition and participation of women at all levels as key actors in the process of water use, management, and conservation.
n) **Priority of water use for human consumption:** To allocate water uses primarily to fundamental human needs.
o) **Protection of the water resource:** The State and public, municipal, or private entities and individuals in general have the obligation to care for and promote the well-being of the water resource, regardless of its value to human beings, as well as to impose limitations on its use and utilization.
p) **Water security:** Defined as the capacity of a society to have water in acceptable quantity and quality for its survival and the performance of different recreational activities. It ensures the economic stability of a society, taking into account climate changes and environmental pollution produced by human beings that directly affect water.
q) **Water sustainability:** To satisfy the current needs of the population, conserving water resources in quality and quantity for the benefit of present and future generations, maintaining the stability of ecosystems.
r) **Valuation of water:** Water is a resource whose valuation must be subject to the social, cultural, economic, and environmental benefits derived from its use and utilization.

**Definitions**
**Art. 9.-** For the purposes of this Law and its Regulations, the following shall be understood as:

**AQUACULTURE:** The activity consisting of the cultivation and production of hydrobiological resources carried out in national waters and lands, private property under control, in natural or artificial aquatic environments.
**AQUIFER:** A body of groundwater existing in geologically interconnected formations, through which subsoil waters circulate or are stored.
**CONFINED AQUIFER:** A body of groundwater that is at pressures greater than atmospheric, between two impermeable layers or formations, and that is fully saturated throughout its thickness.
**CONTINENTAL WATERS:** Masses of water in any state, whether surface, sub-surface, underground, or atmospheric, existing in the continental portion of the country that are stored in continuous and discontinuous surface watercourses, reservoirs, or free or confined groundwater bodies.
**SUBSOIL WATERS:** Waters existing beneath the land surface in the national territory.
**ESTUARINE WATERS:** Brackish waters contained in estuaries and originating from the interaction of river waters with maritime waters, at the mouths of rivers and other surface currents into the sea.
**INSULAR WATERS:** Surface and underground waters existing on islands and islets.
**MARINE WATERS:** Waters comprised in the territorial sea and which include gulfs and bays.
**WATER FOR HUMAN CONSUMPTION:** Water that meets the values of microbiological, physical, chemical, and radiological parameters and can be used for all domestic purposes, including personal hygiene, and does not represent risks to health.
**DRINKING WATER:** Water suitable for human consumption that fully complies with the physical, chemical, and microbiological parameters established in the corresponding technical regulations.
**WASTEWATER:** Discarded water from the activities of different uses: public urban, public rural, industrial, commercial, service, agricultural, livestock, from treatment plants and, in general, from any use, as well as the mixture thereof. Its composition may be varied and may contain dissolved or suspended polluting substances. The corresponding regulations identify wastewater, special-type wastewater, and ordinary-type wastewater.
**ORDINARY-TYPE WASTEWATER:** Wastewater generated by human domestic activities, such as the use of sanitary services, sinks, drains, washing clothes, and other similar activities.
**SPECIAL-TYPE WASTEWATER:** Wastewater generated by agro-industrial, industrial, hospital, and all other activities not considered of the ordinary type.
**UNDERGROUND WATERS:** Water that occupies the saturated zone of the subsoil, is located below the water table, and makes up the aquifers.
**SANITARY SEWERAGE:** A system formed by collectors, sub-collectors, accessory works, pipes, or conduits, generally closed, that conduct ordinary, special, or both types of water.
**USE/UTILIZATION:** Use of water for the satisfaction of the diverse needs and demands of society, guaranteeing the maintenance of the stability of water ecosystems.
**ALLOCATION:** An administrative act by which the State grants to public entities, centralized and autonomous, the use or utilization of national waters originating from or extracted from specific sources and under determined conditions.
**AUTHORIZATION:** An administrative act by which the State grants authorizations for the use and utilization of the water resource to natural or legal persons, for their different uses, whether surface or underground, to be extracted under a specific regime at a defined geographic point, which may not be used for any purpose other than that authorized.
**WATER AUDIT:** A comprehensive review method that allows for verifying compliance with the conditions, requirements, and measures established in the authorizations issued by the water authority.
**NATIONAL WATER BALANCE:** The result of the dynamic interrelation that exists between the availability and the needs for water, or between supply and demand, supply, supply deficit, and water stress indicators, including its quantity and quality, as well as other factors derived from socioeconomic development, social well-being, and environmental sustainability.
**BENEFICIARY:** A natural or legal person, public or private, who in full use of their faculties and rights is the holder or person responsible for an allocation or permit.
**FOREST:** A land area of a minimum size of 0.5 hectares, with a canopy cover equal to or greater than 30%, with trees with the potential to reach a minimum height of four meters at maturity in situ. It does not include land subject to predominantly agricultural or urban use.
**SALTWATER FOREST/MANGROVE:** A forest composed of coastal woody vegetation comprised of salt-tolerant tree species.
**WATER QUALITY:** The physicochemical and biological characteristics that surface and underground waters present at a specific geographic point, at a specific time, and for a specific use.
**FEE:** The payment made by every natural or legal person who acquires the status of being authorized for the exploration, use, utilization, and discharge of water resources, to finance the public administration in the conservation and integrated management of water, the protection of the goods that form part of the public water domain, to prevent their degradation, and to reverse water pollution throughout the country.
**POLLUTANT LOAD:** Quantification of those substances that water contains and that change its physical, chemical, or biological conditions, either individually or in association.
**NET DISCHARGED POLLUTANT LOAD:** The pollutant load calculated considering the net discharged concentration of a polluting substance, the average discharge flow rate, and the discharge period.
**WATERCOURSE:** A clearly defined course of water through which water flows periodically or continuously.
**FLOW RATE:** Volume of water per unit of time that flows through a cross-section of a stream, river, channel, or emerges from a spring.
**ENVIRONMENTAL FLOW:** A specific water regime, characteristic of each river basin, that every water body must maintain, which respects its geomorphology, allows for the correct development of the biological cycles of species associated with the aquatic environment, conserves the sustainability of water ecosystems, and satisfies the needs of the population.
**ECOLOGICAL FLOW:** Flow of water that must be maintained in natural water sources for the protection or conservation of the ecosystems involved, the aesthetics of the landscape, or other aspects of scientific or cultural interest.
**AVERAGE DISCHARGE FLOW RATE:** The average value calculated from the flow rates measured during a specific period.
**HYDROLOGICAL CYCLE:** The permanent process of water circulation, continuous and interdependent, of movement and transfer of water in its different states between the atmosphere and the surface (land and water bodies).
**CONCENTRATION:** The mass of an element, substance, or compound per unit volume of the liquid that contains it.
**WATER POLLUTION:** The action and effect of introducing materials or forms of energy or inducing conditions in water that, directly or indirectly, imply a degradation of its quality in relation to subsequent uses and the preservation of the environment in the aquatic medium. Water pollution includes the degradation of its surroundings.
**POLLUTANT:** A substance that negatively affects, modifies, and alters the physical, chemical, or biological conditions of water, either individually or in association, causing environmental damage.
**RIVER BASIN:** A natural unit whose physical limits are defined by the surface water divide known as a watershed. The basin is an area of the Earth's surface where (if it were impermeable) raindrops falling on it would tend to be drained by the stream system towards the same outlet point, which could be a stream, a river, a lake, or another water body. That is, it has a single outlet for its surface runoff. The river basin is composed of sub-basins, which in turn are composed of micro-basins, and these together constitute the natural unit for planning.
**TRANSBOUNDARY RIVER BASIN:** Defined as the geographical area that extends through the territory of two or more States, determined by the dividing line of a hydrographic system of surface and groundwater that flows towards a common outlet.
**WATER BODY:** Any spring, river, stream, permanent or non-permanent brook, lake, marsh, natural or artificial reservoir, estuary, peat bog, swamp, wetland, fresh, brackish, or salt water.
**INTERNATIONAL WATERCOURSE:** A system of surface and underground waters, in which some of its parts are located in different countries.
**WATER ECOSYSTEMS:** A specific unit of a water mass (rivers, lakes, lagoons, or others) characterized by having specific physicochemical properties (biotope), in which animals or plants live.
**EFFLUENT:** The flow rate of wastewater that leaves the last conduction or treatment unit. It also refers to the diversion of a main watercourse.
**RESERVOIR:** The retention of surface waters within a natural channel through the construction of hydraulic works, whose waters can be used for various uses and purposes.
**GENERATING ENTITY:** A natural or legal person, public or private, responsible for the activity causing the discharge, treatment, and final disposal of wastewater.
**INTEGRATED WATER RESOURCES MANAGEMENT:** The development and exploitation of water resources in a sustainable manner, taking into account technical and hydrological aspects, as well as environmental, socioeconomic, and political constraints.
**WETLANDS:** Areas of marsh, fen, peatland, or water, whether natural or artificial, permanent or temporary, with water that is static or flowing, fresh, brackish, or salt, including areas of marine water the depth of which at low tide does not exceed six meters.
**WATER INVENTORY:** Systematized information on the availability of water in terms of quantity and quality, in a given geographical space, in both the dry and rainy seasons of the year; it includes information and estimates in relation to: (i) hydrometeorological phenomena, (ii) quantity and quality of water, (iii) temporal distribution of the rainfall regime, (iv) availability of surface and underground waters at the territorial level; and, (v) censuses on uses and users of national waters.
**WATER BOARDS:** A water board shall be understood as those non-profit social organizations with legal personality whose purpose is to provide drinking water and sanitation services to the community.
**BED:** The surface of the bottom of a water body, primarily constituted by sedimented solids of different physicochemical composition, which includes benthos and inert organic matter.
**PERMISSIBLE LIMIT:** Values, ranges, and concentrations of the parameters established in the corresponding technical regulations, technical regulations, or other current regulations that those responsible must comply with prior to their discharge, reuse, or disposal.
**RECEIVING MEDIUM OR BODY:** A river, stream, lake, lagoon, reservoir, sea, estuary, mangrove, swamp, where treated wastewater is discharged, excluding the sewerage system and the soil.
**HYDROGRAPHIC MICRO-BASIN:** Any area that develops its drainage directly to the main stream of a sub-basin. Several micro-basins can form a sub-basin. They are formed by the tributaries to secondary rivers, such as streams and brooks.
**MONITORING:** The set of technical and administrative actions to be able to determine, measure, observe, register, and forecast the behavior of water resources; comprising scientific research actions developed by public or private laboratories.
**DISCHARGE PERIOD:** The time span in which a discharge occurs, which can be expressed in hours per day, days per month, or months per year.
**PERMIT:** An administrative act through which the State, via ASA, authorizes a natural or legal person, public or private, for the exploitation and exploration of water resources and to make discharges into the receiving media that are part of the public water domain.
**WATER RESOURCE PLANNING:** Formulation and coordination of actions on the hydrological regime within the institutional and technological framework necessary to achieve the sustainability, socioeconomic, and environmental objectives of the law.
**SHALLOW OR ARTISANAL WELL:** An excavated well whose total depth can reach up to fifteen meters and which captures groundwater contained in a free aquifer.
**DEEP WELL:** A drilled or excavated well whose total depth is greater than fifteen meters, whether it captures water from a free, confined, semi-confined aquifer, or a combination thereof.
**INTAKE POINT:** The place where the user takes the water resource for any use.
**WATER RESOURCES:** Comprise both rainwater, surface, and underground waters, regardless of their physical state.
**HYDROGRAPHIC REGION:** A geographical demarcation that contains regions, basins, sub-basins, and hydrographic micro-basins, whose delimitation and denomination makes the natural component compatible with the objective of facilitating the application of this Law.
**WATER RESOURCES RESERVE:** Bodies of surface and underground waters in which restrictions or limitations are established for the uses and utilization of water for reasons of public interest or officially declared state of emergency and disaster.
**ARTIFICIAL RESERVOIR:** A structure built for the purpose of capturing, retaining, and storing rainwater.
**WASTEWATER REUSE:** Use or utilization of wastewater or recycled water that may or may not have been subject to prior treatment.
**BANK:** A strip of land contiguous to the beds of natural or artificial watercourses of the receiving medium that are part of the public water domain.
**RIVER:** A current of water, of variable flow, that flows into another body of water or into the sea.
**SANITATION:** Consists of methods and means to collect and eliminate excreta and wastewater from a community in a hygienic manner so as not to endanger the health of individuals and the community as a whole.
**WATER SECURITY:** Defined as the capacity of a society to have water of acceptable quantity and quality to preserve human and ecosystem health, and to effectively protect against water-related risks (pollution, floods, high waters, droughts, and landslides). It ensures the social and economic stability of a society, taking into account climate changes and environmental pollution that directly affect water.
**SUSTAINABLE:** The existence of ecological, social, and economic conditions that allow for meeting the needs of the present without compromising future generations.
**WATER SUSTAINABILITY:** The efficient and rational administration in the use of water resources, without thereby compromising the ecological balance. As such, the concept of water sustainability posits that our present use of our resources should not harm or limit the subsistence of water ecosystems.
**HYDROGRAPHIC SUB-BASIN:** Any area that develops its drainage directly to the main course of the basin. Several sub-basins can form a basin. They are formed by the secondary rivers that drain into the main river of the basin.
**WATER SUBSECTOR:** A component related to Integrated Water Resources Management, specifically with regard to different uses such as drinking water and sanitation, hydroelectric power generation, agricultural irrigation, tourism, and industry, among others.
**HOLDER:** A natural or legal person, public or private, to whom an allocation, authorizations for use or utilization of water resources, or permits for discharge and exploration are granted, for the goods that are part of the public water domain.
**TRANSFER:** The transfer of surface or underground waters from one river basin to another.
**CONSUMPTIVE USE:** The expenditure or consumption of water, which, once used, does not return to the medium from which it was captured; that is, the water is consumed in carrying out an activity.
**DOMESTIC USE:** The use of water for private and household purposes, irrigation of gardens and home vegetable plots, as well as watering troughs for domestic animals that do not constitute a commercial activity. It shall be understood that this use is intended for persons who do not have access to public drinking water distribution systems and do not commercialize said resource.
**NON-CONSUMPTIVE USE:** The water used is subsequently returned to the medium from which it was extracted, although not necessarily to the same place. Despite this, this water may present various alterations, physicochemical and biological, depending on the use it has been given.
**USER:** A natural or legal person, public or private, who makes use or utilization of the water resource or carries out activities that generate discharges.
**DISCHARGE OR OUTFALL:** Any final discharge of an element, substance, or compound that is contained in a residual liquid of any origin, to a receiving body, the soil, or the subsoil.
**POINT SOURCE DISCHARGE:** A discharge made at a fixed point where the generating entity of the discharge is identifiable.
**PROTECTION OR BANK ZONES:** Strips of land contiguous to the beds of rivers or watercourses, natural and artificial reservoirs, and other natural or artificial water bodies of the receiving medium that are part of the public water domain. These strips are measured horizontally from the level of ordinary maximum waters and are not part of the public water domain.
**AQUIFER RECHARGE ZONE:** An area of the basin in which, due to the characteristics of topography, vegetation cover, soil, and subsoil, there is infiltration of water towards an aquifer.
**HYDROGRAPHIC ZONE:** A geographical demarcation that contains regions, basins, and hydrographic micro-basins, whose delimitation and denomination makes the natural component compatible with the political one, with the objective of facilitating the application of this Law.

**TITLE TWO**
**INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK AND WATER ZONING**

**CHAPTER I**
**NATIONAL ADMINISTRATIVE REGIME**

**Creation of the Salvadoran Water Authority**
**Art. 10.-** The Salvadoran Water Authority, hereinafter may be referred to as ASA, is hereby created as an autonomous official institution of public law; its autonomy shall encompass technical, administrative, financial, and budgetary matters, with its own legal personality and assets; it shall be attached to the Executive Branch through the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources.
ASA shall be the superior, deliberative, governing, and normative body in matters of water resources policy.

**Purpose of ASA**
**Art. 11.-** The Salvadoran Water Authority shall be the governing body for the integrated management of water resources and other goods that are part of the public water domain, through the rational use, efficient utilization, management, protection, recovery, conservation, improvement, and restoration of the water resource to guarantee its sustainability with equity; it must incorporate in all its institutional actions, plans, and programs, a gender perspective, a basin approach, and the perspective of adaptation and mitigation in the face of climate change.

**Composition of ASA**
**Art. 12.-** ASA shall be constituted by:
a) A President, who shall be appointed by the President of the Republic and shall have its Legal Representation.
b) A Board of Directors, as a collegiate political body, for making strategic decisions on water.
c) An Executive Directorate, as an operational body, with its specialized and administrative units.
d) Zonal Basin Organizations.
e) A Sanctioning Tribunal whose members shall be appointed by the President of the Republic.

**Powers of the Salvadoran Water Authority**
**Art. 13.-** The powers, functions, and attributions of the Salvadoran Water Authority are the following:
a) To guarantee compliance with this Law, its policies, and its regulations.
b) To develop and ensure compliance with the National Policy for Integrated Water Resource Management.
c) To develop and approve the National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management, the zonal water plans, and their monitoring plans, which shall be updated every five years, as well as the inventories and balances on the use, utilization, and availability of water resources.
d) To develop guidelines on the efficient and sustainable use of water, which guarantee its preservation, conservation, and protection, to be complied with by the different subsectors that make use of water resources.
e) To administer the Water Information System (SIHI) in accordance with this Law.
f) To formulate and provide technical studies and contingency plans, which include preparing the logistics, equipment, and resources to prevent and address declarations of national or zonal emergencies related to critical situations arising from excess or deficit of water.
g) To issue authorizations for the use and utilization of water resources and permits for exploration, discharges, and authorizations for the goods established in the provisions of the public water domain of this Law and its regulations.
h) To issue public allocations to centralized and decentralized entities to utilize water resources.
i) To audit and sanction in water matters, related to compliance with the conditions set in allocations, authorizations, and permits in accordance with the requirements and procedures established in this Law.
j) To develop and approve the fees for use and utilization of water resources, for discharges, and for the use and utilization of the other goods that are part of the public water domain in accordance with this Law.
k) To prepare and submit for the approval of the Executive Branch, through the Ministry of Economy, the proposed rates for charging for the sale of administrative, technical, or scientific products and services provided to third parties, whether public or private.
l) To resolve conflicts over the use and utilization of water and discharges and matters related to the use of the other goods that are part of the public water domain in accordance with the procedure established in this Law.
m) To issue programs and promotional activities related to the sustainable use and utilization of water resources, ecosystems, and river basins, to contribute to their recovery, conservation, and improvement.
n) To establish the Public Registry of Water Resources, in accordance with this Law.
o) To submit for the consideration of the Ministry of Finance, through the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources, the draft special budget for the last year of its fiscal period, so that it may be presented and submitted for approval to the Legislative Branch.
p) To promote education and culture strategies and programs related to the sustainable utilization of water resources.
q) To seek from national and international organizations, credits, non-reimbursable technical cooperation, or other sources of financing, especially those aimed at institutional strengthening, training, and research for the personnel working at ASA.
r) To issue technical guidelines and directives related to the hydraulic works constructed on the goods that are part of the public water domain, prioritizing multi-purpose works, within the framework of integrated water resources management and climate change.
s) To issue technical and legal guidelines and directives aimed at incentivizing the use, recycling, and reuse of wastewater and sludge management.
t) To establish within its organization a specialized unit for the development of scientific research, adaptation, and technology transfer, in improvements to integrated water management, including the recovery, conservation, and protection of basins and the validation of technologies for wastewater treatment.
u) To publish and present annually to the Legislative Assembly the report of ASA's activities related to integrated water resource management.
v) To apply settlement strategies between users and other actors and implement measures and mechanisms for alternative conflict resolution in relation to the use and utilization of water resources, discharges, and other goods that are part of the public water domain of this Law and its regulations.
w) Any other powers and attributions conferred upon it in accordance with this Law.

ASA may delegate the exercise of its competence to hierarchical inferiors within the same institution. The internal organization shall be determined by regulation.

**Prohibitions**
**Art. 14.-** Persons in any of the following situations may not be officials of the Salvadoran Water Authority:
a) Having outstanding accounts with the State.
b) Being members of a board of directors or a governing body of a political party.
c) Being a member or shareholder of the boards of directors or governing bodies of companies that provide drinking water and sanitation services or those that generate direct economic benefits from activities related to water.
d) Being a holder of authorizations or permits of any type, or a representative, partner, shareholder, director, administrator, or agent of companies that hold and apply for permits.
e) The spouse or cohabiting partner, and persons who have a kinship relationship up to the third degree of affinity and fourth degree of consanguinity, with the officials or directors of the Salvadoran Water Authority or permit holders and those indicated in letters b) and c).

**Oversight**
**Art. 15.-** The Salvadoran Water Authority must annually contract the services of a specialized firm to conduct financial and management audits of that entity and shall be subject to the controls of the corresponding public entities.
The specialized firms must be subject to the provisions cited in the article regarding the prohibitions for ASA.

**Requirements to be President of ASA**
**Art. 16.-** To be president of ASA, the following requirements must be met:
a) Be a Salvadoran citizen.
b) Be over thirty years of age.
c) Have no criminal record.
d) Be of recognized honorability and probity.
e) Be a professional with extensive knowledge and proven experience related to water resource management.
f) Be free of all types of obligations with the State; and be in good standing with the treasury.
g) Not be the spouse or cohabiting partner, or a person with a kinship relationship up to the second degree of affinity and fourth degree of consanguinity, with other officials or directors of ASA or permit holders.

**Cessation of office**
**Art. 17.-** The President of ASA and members of the Board of Directors shall cease their functions for the following reasons:
a) By resignation.
b) By a final condemnatory judicial sentence for the commission of a crime.
c) By physical or mental incapacity, determined and confirmed by the Board of Directors, which prevents the exercise of the office.
d) For disclosing confidential information on matters handled by the institution or taking advantage of any information to which they have access for personal purposes or those of third parties, or to the detriment of the entity, the State, or third parties, without prejudice to any criminal and civil liability that may arise.
e) Non-compliance with the principles of the office required by Law.
f) For exercising influence by taking advantage of the position, thereby favoring non-compliance with this Law.
g) For the end of the term for which they were elected.

**Powers of the President of ASA**
**Art. 18.-** The President of ASA shall have the following powers:
1) To ensure compliance with this Law, its regulations, and norms in water matters.
2) To exercise the legal representation of ASA, and may grant general, judicial, administrative, and special powers of attorney, with prior authorization from the Board of Directors.
3) To sign agreements, contracts, and in general any commitment in water matters, with public or private, national or foreign entities, with prior authorization from the Board of Directors.
4) To issue declarations of national or zonal emergencies related to critical situations arising from excess or deficit of the water resource, with prior agreement of the Board of Directors.
5) To cast the deciding vote when there is a tie in the voting of the Board of Directors' sessions.
6) To ensure that information related to the updating and publication, every five years, of the National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management, the water inventory, the national registry of water resources, and the zonal water plans is consolidated, and to submit it for the consideration and approval of the Board of Directors; as well as to implement and monitor the related Plan.
7) To ensure compliance with the directives issued by the Board of Directors.
8) To collect and systematize the necessary information for its incorporation into the Water Information System (SIHI) in accordance with this Law.
9) To prepare technical studies and contingency plans, which include preparing the logistics, equipment, and resources to prevent and address declarations of national or zonal emergencies related to critical situations arising from excess or deficit of water.
10) To collect information related to authorizations, allocations, and permits for the use and utilization of water resources, for the approval of the Board of Directors.
11) To prepare information on the goods established in the public water domain and to ensure compliance with the conditions established in the authorizations, permits, and allocations granted.
12) To receive and evaluate the information related to water audits, concerning compliance with the conditions set in authorizations and permits for exploration, use, and utilization of water resources, discharges, and other goods that are part of the public water domain in accordance with the requirements and procedures established in this Law, submitted by zonal basin organizations.
13) To submit for the consideration of the Board of Directors matters related to the fees for the use and utilization of water resources and for discharges, and for the use and utilization of the other goods that make up the public water domain in accordance with this Law.
14) To resolve conflicts over the use and utilization of water and discharges that have not been resolved by the corresponding zonal hydrographic management, in accordance with the procedure established in this Law.
15) To formulate programs and activities of incentives and disincentives related to the sustainable use and utilization of water resources, ecosystems, and river basins, to contribute to their protection, recovery, conservation, and improvement.
16) To collect and prepare the directives, technical and legal guidelines aimed at promoting the use, recycling, and reuse of wastewater and sludge management, and to submit them for the consideration of the Board of Directors.
17) To propose to the Board of Directors for its approval the draft annual budget for the operation of ASA; as well as the work plans, Annual Operational Plans, and to prepare the Report of Activities and submit it to the Board of Directors.
18) To design and implement strategies and programs for education and culture related to the sustainable utilization of water resources.
19) To formulate and establish a bank of projects on integrated water management, following the criteria of national and international organizations, whether they be credits, non-reimbursable technical cooperation, or other sources of financing; especially those aimed at the training and education of the personnel working at ASA.
20) To collect information for the preparation of rates for administrative, technical, and scientific services provided to third parties, whether public or private, related to the water resource; to submit them for the consideration and approval of the Board of Directors.
21) To collect information and prepare the directives and technical guidelines related to the hydraulic works constructed on the goods established in the public water domain, prioritizing multi-purpose works, and to submit it for the consideration and approval of the Board of Directors; and to coordinate with the Ministries, autonomous entities, and municipalities, so that they comply with the technical directives and guidelines.
22) To design and implement the plans and programs related to the establishment of the specialized unit for the development of scientific research, adaptation, and technology transfer, in improvements to integrated water management, including the validation of technologies for wastewater treatment, and to provide it with financial, technical, and logistical resources.
23) To supervise and control the performance of the institution's operations.
24) To ensure compliance with the directives issued by the Board of Directors, on the efficient and sustainable use of water, which must be complied with by the different subsectors that make use of the water resource.
25) To prepare, consolidate, and implement the information related to water inventories and balances on the uses, utilization, and availability of water resources.
26) To ensure compliance with the authorizations for the use and utilization of water resources and to inform the board of directors every three months of their status.
27) To receive and consolidate information on the collection of the fee from the zonal basin organizations and to inform the board of directors in order to comply with matters related to the destination of ASA's financial resources.
28) To formulate programs and promotional activities related to the sustainable use and utilization of water resources, ecosystems, and river basins, to contribute to their protection, recovery, conservation, and improvement.
29) To ensure the efficient administration of the public registry of water resources, in accordance with this Law.
30) To collect information for the preparation of technical directives and guidelines for the efficient and sustainable use and utilization of water resources, as well as for the control of pollution, and to submit it for the consideration and approval of the board of directors.
31) To collect information and prepare the technical directives and guidelines for the design of hydraulic works aimed at determining the maximum floods of extreme events, considering the effects of climate change and climate variability.
32) To prepare information related to public allocations to centralized and decentralized entities to utilize water resources and to ensure their compliance.
33) To prepare and propose to the board of directors the technical directives and guidelines related to the hydraulic works constructed on the goods that are part of the public water domain, prioritizing multi-purpose works.
34) To supervise and apply the corresponding administrative or judicial measures.
35) Any other powers and attributions conferred upon it in accordance with this Law.

**Substitution of the President of ASA**
**Art. 19.-** In case of temporary absence or impediment of the president, one of the proprietary directors shall assume the office by agreement of the Board of Directors, for the duration of the president's absence or impediment. Likewise, in case of a vacancy in the office due to death, resignation, or other permanent disability of the president, the President of the Republic shall appoint a substitute, whose mandate shall extend until the end of the term for which the previous president was appointed.

**Composition of the Board of Directors of ASA**
**Art. 20.-** The Board of Directors of ASA shall be composed of the following directors:
a) The President-Director of ASA, who shall preside over it.
b) A representative of the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources (MARN).
c) A representative of the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock (MAG).
d) A representative of the Ministry of Economy (MINEC).
e) A representative of the Ministry of Tourism (MITUR).
f) A representative of the Ministry of Housing (MIVI).
g) A representative of the Ministry of Health (MINSAL).
h) A representative of the Ministry of Public Works and Transportation (MOP).
i) A representative of the Ministry of the Interior (MINGOB).
j) A representative of the University of El Salvador.
k) One representative for each of the three zonal basin organizations.
l) One representative of civil society dedicated to the protection and care of natural assets.

The proprietary and alternate members of the board shall be appointed by the head of the corresponding institution. In the case of alternates, they shall have a voice and vote only in the absence of the proprietary member they represent.

When it deems necessary, the Board of Directors may invite representatives of other public administration and private entities, both national and international, to its meetings; they may participate with a voice but without a vote.

The first Board of Directors of ASA shall serve until the end of the corresponding government term.

The representative from letter l) and their alternate shall be appointed by the President of the Republic and proposed by the corresponding sectors, in an assembly convened by ASA, from which it will form the respective shortlists, in the manner established in the internal regulations issued for that purpose. The representatives elected to form said shortlists shall be persons specialized in topics related to environmental sciences and sustainable development.

**Powers of the Board of Directors of the Salvadoran Water Authority**
**Art. 21.-** The powers of the Board of Directors are the following:
a) To develop and enforce the National Policy for Integrated Water Resource Management.
b) To approve and publish every five years the National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management and the zonal plans; as well as the inventories and balances on the use, utilization, and availability of Water Resources.
c) To issue directives on the efficient and sustainable use of water, to be complied with by the different subsectors that make use of the water resource.
d) To approve technical studies and contingency plans, which include preparing the logistics, equipment, and resources to prevent and address declarations of national or zonal emergencies related to critical situations arising from excess or deficit of water.
e) To approve the authorizations, allocations, and permits for the use and utilization of water resources established in this Law.
f) To approve public allocations to centralized and decentralized entities to utilize the water resource.
g) To be informed of the results of water audits, regarding compliance with the conditions set in allocations, authorizations, and permits for exploration, use, and utilization of water resources, discharges, and other goods that are part of the public water domain, in accordance with the requirements and procedures established in this Law.
h) To bring to the attention of the Sanctioning Tribunal facts constituting an infraction in accordance with this Law.
i) To consider and evaluate the fees for use and utilization of water resources, for discharges, and for use and utilization of the other goods that are part of the public water domain, in accordance with this Law, to submit them for approval to the Ministry of Economy.
j) To establish, evaluate, and submit for the approval of the Executive Branch, in the corresponding Department, the proposed rates for charging for the sale of administrative, technical, or scientific products and services provided to third parties, whether public or private.
k) To resolve conflicts of great strategic importance and national interest, over the use and utilization of water and discharges related to the use of the other goods that are part of the public water domain in accordance with the provisions of this Law.
l) To approve programs and promotional activities related to the sustainable use and utilization of water resources, ecosystems, and river basins, to contribute to their recovery, conservation, and improvement.
m) To submit for the consideration of the Ministry of Finance, through the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources, the draft special budget for its fiscal period, and for this to be submitted for approval to the Legislative Branch.
n) To approve the organizational and operational regulations and create a specialized unit for the development of scientific research, adaptation, and technology transfer, in improvements to integrated water management, including the validation of technologies for wastewater treatment; as well as the annual operational plans, and the annual management report of ASA.
o) To approve incentive and disincentive programs in water matters, especially to promote a change in society's behavior towards the proper use of the water resource.
p) To validate education and culture strategies and programs related to the sustainable utilization of water resources and climate change.
q) To approve projects on integrated water and sanitation management, taking into consideration the criteria of national and international organizations, whether they be credits, non-reimbursable technical cooperation, or other sources of financing, especially those aimed at the training and education of the personnel working at ASA.
r) To approve the necessary technical directives and guidelines for the efficient and sustainable use and utilization of water and for the control of pollution.
s) To approve the work plans, the annual operational plan, and the annual budget.
t) To approve the report of activities within two months following the end of each year.
u) To resolve conflicts between private parties over the use and utilization of water and discharges, in accordance with the procedure established in this Law.

**Board of Directors Sessions**
**Art. 22.-** The Board of Directors shall hold ordinary sessions at least once a month and extraordinary sessions when necessary, upon the call of the President of ASA, or failing that, the Head of the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources, at the proposal of three directors.

The President of the Salvadoran Water Authority shall preside over the sessions. In their absence, their substitute shall do so. In the absence of both, the proprietary directors shall elect from among themselves who will preside over the session.

**Quorum**
**Art. 23.-** For a quorum to exist in the sessions of the Board of Directors, the participation of the majority of the directors, that is, eight directors, acting as proprietary members or their substitutes, shall be required.

Resolutions shall be adopted by a simple majority of votes. In the special cases indicated by this Law, they shall be adopted by a qualified majority.

Alternate directors may attend sessions together with the proprietary members, but when the respective proprietary member participates, they shall have a voice but no vote. In the absence of the proprietary member, the alternate director shall have a voice and vote; in case of a tie, the president shall have the deciding vote.

**Executive Directorate of ASA**
**Art. 24.-** The Executive Directorate of ASA is hereby created as an operational body for the integrated management of water resources.
The Executive Director shall be appointed by the ASA Board of Directors upon the proposal of the President of ASA and shall serve for a period of five years; they shall ensure the efficient performance of the same and of its personnel.

**Requirements to be Executive Director of ASA**
**Art. 25.-** To be Executive Director of ASA, the following requirements must be met:
a) Be a Salvadoran citizen.
b) Be over thirty years of age.
c) Have no criminal record.
d) Be of recognized honorability and probity.
e) Be a professional with extensive knowledge and proven experience related to the management of public administration.
f) Be free of all types of obligations with the State; and be in good standing with the treasury.
g) Not be the spouse or cohabiting partner, or a person with a kinship relationship up to the second degree of affinity and fourth degree of consanguinity, with other officials or directors of ASA or permit holders.

**Cessation of office**
**Art. 26.-** The Executive Director of ASA shall cease their functions for the following reasons:
a) By resignation.
b) By a final condemnatory judicial sentence for the commission of a crime.
c) By physical or mental incapacity, determined and confirmed by the Board of Directors, which prevents the exercise of the office.
d) For disclosing confidential information on matters handled by the institution or taking advantage of any information to which they have access for personal purposes or those of third parties, or to the detriment of the entity, the State, or third parties, without prejudice to any criminal and civil liability that may arise.

When any of the causes for cessation, duly proven, exist or arise, the tenure of the Executive Director shall cease, and their immediate replacement shall proceed; however, acts authorized prior to the cessation shall be considered valid, except in the case of mental incapacity.

**Powers of the Executive Directorate**
**Art. 27.-** The Executive Directorate of ASA shall have the following functions:
a) To apply this Law, its regulations, and other normative instruments within the scope of its powers.
b) To be responsible for ensuring the good administrative management of ASA.
c) To execute the decisions emanating from the ASA Board of Directors.
d) To direct and execute actions leading to the fulfillment of agreements or covenants entered into by ASA.
e) To coordinate with the respective authorities the necessary actions to address declarations of national or zonal emergencies related to critical situations arising from excess or deficit of the water resource.
f) To collect the information and prepare the draft annual budget for the operation of ASA.
g) To coordinate with the organizational unit in charge of planning the preparation of work plans, the annual operational plan, and the annual budget to be presented to the board of directors for its approval.
h) To prepare the proposal for organizational and operational regulations, and the annual management report of ASA; and subsequently submit it for approval to the board of directors.
i) To hire, appoint, sanction, and remove institutional personnel, in accordance with the provisions of this Law.
j) To carry out the necessary actions to coordinate with the ministries, autonomous entities, and municipalities, so that they comply with the technical directives and guidelines related to the hydraulic works constructed on the goods that are part of the public water domain, prioritizing multi-purpose works, within the framework of integrated water resources management and climate change.
k) To coordinate with the corresponding areas the preparation of the report of activities for the water sector within two months following the end of each year, and present it for approval to the board of directors.
l) To implement the regulations related to the organization and functioning of ASA.
m) To implement and provide the necessary technical, financial, and logistical resources for the functioning of the specialized unit for the development of scientific research, adaptation, and technology transfer, in improvements to integrated water management.
n) To supervise and control the performance of the functioning of the zonal basin organizations, as well as to hold periodic meetings with their managers.
o) To prepare the internal regulations and submit them for the consideration of the board of directors for its approval.

**Zonal Basin Organizations**
**Art. 28.-** The zonal basin organizations are established as technical-administrative bodies of ASA at the zonal level, which will execute actions aimed at planning, coordinating, and facilitating the integrated management of water resources in each hydrographic zone, within the assigned territorial competence, including its use, utilization, protection, conservation, and recovery.

**Structure of the Zonal Basin Organizations**
**Art. 29.-** The zonal basin organizations shall represent ASA in matters related to the application of this Law in the respective hydrographic zone. They shall be led by a manager appointed by the Executive Director of ASA, with operational technical teams in each zone.
The internal structure, organization, and functioning of these organizations shall be determined by ASA through the corresponding internal regulations.

**Powers of the Zonal Basin Organizations**
**Art. 30.-** The powers of the zonal basin organizations are:
a) To monitor and control compliance with this Law, within the scope of their competencies, and to exercise acts of authority in water resource matters in their respective hydrographic zone.
b) To formulate and present to the Executive Directorate of ASA the proposals for zonal water plans, in accordance with the provisions of the national plan for integrated water resources management.
c) To participate in the development of the national policy for water resources and the National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management, in permanent consultation with the municipal governments of their corresponding zone.
d) To comply with and execute the requirements delegated to them by the Executive Directorate of ASA in their respective zone.
e) To execute activities for the protection and conservation of water resources, issued by ASA, in coordination with local authorities in their respective hydrographic zones.
f) To formulate and present to the ASA Board of Directors for its approval the zonal river basin plans, every five years, and to guarantee the execution of programs and projects that make these plans viable.
g) To monitor and control compliance with the powers related to the management of basins in their respective hydrographic zones.
h) To collect and generate water information for their zone in order to provide the necessary inputs for the normal functioning of the water information system (SIHI).
i) To propose and execute instruments to implement the programs and projects considered in the national water planning.
j) To hold periodic coordination and consultation meetings with representatives of communities, minority groups, civil society, universities, water boards, municipal governments, and public assignees of the corresponding hydrographic zone.
k) To support, strengthen, and train municipal governments in matters of integrated water resources management in their respective hydrographic zones.
l) To support the performance of water audits, concerning compliance with the conditions set in authorizations and permits for exploration, use, and utilization of water resources, discharges, and other goods that are part of the public water domain, in accordance with the requirements and procedures established in this Law.
m) To carry out inspections related to complaints, notices, or information on aspects related to the management of the water resource and the goods that are part of the public water domain.
n) To bring complaints and non-compliance due to infractions of this Law to the attention of the corresponding organizational unit.
o) To execute the necessary actions within their territorial scope to support compliance with the different agreements that ASA signs with other entities, whether public or private, national or foreign, in water matters, as appropriate.
p) To coordinate with the respective authorities the necessary actions to address declarations of national emergencies or in zones declared as water protection areas due to excess or deficit of the water resource; in their respective hydrographic zone.
q) Any other functions assigned to them by the Board of Directors and the Executive Director of ASA, in accordance with this Law.

**CHAPTER II**
**BASINS, HYDROGRAPHIC ZONES, AND TRANSBOUNDARY BASINS**

**Management of river basins**
**Art. 31.-** The management of river basins shall be subject to the directives of the National Water Resources Policy and the National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management.

**Hydrographic Zones**
**Art. 32.-** For administrative purposes of integrated water resources management, the three hydrographic zones are the following:
a) **Lempa,** constituted by the Lempa river basin within the national territory.
b) **Paz - Jaltepeque,** which comprises the river basins that exist in the space determined from the western borders of the country to the hydrographic zone of the Jaltepeque estuary; corresponding to the following hydrographic regions: Paz, Cara Sucia  San Pedro, Grande de Sonsonate  Banderas, Mandinga - Comalapa, and Jiboa - Estero de Jaltepeque.
c) **Jiquilisco - Goascorn,** which exists in the geographical space determined from the limits of the Lempa Hydrographic Zone to the eastern borders of the country; corresponding to the following hydrographic regions: Baha de Jiquilisco, Grande de San Miguel, Sirama, and Goascorn.

**Waters of an international drainage basin or in transboundary basins**
**Art. 33.-** The State shall negotiate and sign International Treaties and Agreements for international drainage basins or transboundary basins, these being bilateral or multilateral in relation to international watercourses, which includes surface and groundwater systems that, by virtue of their physical relationship, constitute a unitary whole whose parts are located in different States, that is, between El Salvador and other neighboring States, in which the provisions or limits established by the Constitution of the Republic and, where pertinent, the principles of international law on the matter must be respected.

**Management Committee for International Watercourses**
**Art. 34.-** To give applicability to the provisions of the preceding article, a Management Committee for International Watercourses shall be created, which shall be composed of a representative of ASA and of the following Ministries: Environment and Natural Resources, Ministry of Housing, Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock, National Defense, Local Development, and Foreign Affairs; the latter being the one to coordinate said Committee, whose functions and responsibilities shall be developed in the corresponding regulations.

**CHAPTER III**
**WATER SUBSECTORS**

**Subsectors and Competent Entities**
**Art. 35.** According to the uses made of water resources and the administrative management levels of the water sector, different public entities that administer national waters can be identified, and for the purposes of this Law, they shall have a regulatory character and shall be representative of the different subsectors, these being the following:
a) **Drinking water and sanitation:** the competent authority in this matter shall be the Ministry of Health with regard to drinking water; and with respect to the sanitation subsector, it shall be the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources, in accordance with the regulations of each institution.
b) **Water for agricultural, aquaculture, and fishing purposes:** the competent authority in this matter is the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock, through the Irrigation and Drainage Law, the Forestry Law, and the General Law for the Organization and Promotion of Fishing and Aquaculture.
c) **Water for hydroelectric purposes from the current reservoirs Cerrn Grande, 5 de Noviembre, and 15 de Septiembre, Guajoyo (Lake Gija), and the 3 de Febrero plant, and geothermal purposes:** the competent authority is the Lempa River Hydroelectric Executive Commission (CEL); and for particular cases of other hydroelectric uses, it shall be ASA.
d) **Water for industrial, agro-industrial, recreational, and other purposes:** the competent authority in this matter is ASA, through the application of this Law and its regulations.

All authorizations issued by the subsectors in accordance with the nature of their competencies shall be in line with the guidelines and directives issued by ASA for this purpose. Permits for discharges shall be issued exclusively by ASA.

**Powers of ASA in relation to the Subsectors**
**Art. 36.-** The relationships of ASA with the public entities that have a regulatory character for the different subsectors are established in the following terms:
a) To allocate the necessary water resource for the functioning of each of the subsectors; according to an analysis of resource availability and considering the order of priorities established in this Law.
b) To collect the corresponding fees.
c) To monitor and audit the efficient use of the water allocated by ASA, as well as to review and update annually the allocated volumes according to water availability and compliance with this Law.
d) To issue directives, be informed of, and supervise matters related to hydraulic works constructed on the goods established in the public water domain, prioritizing multi-purpose works.
e) To ensure compliance with the protection norms and directives in the use or utilization of the goods established in the public water domain.
f) To verify compliance with protection measures for water resources, ecosystems, and river basins.

**Obligations of the Public Regulatory Entities of the different Subsectors**
**Art. 37.-** The obligations of the Public Regulatory Entities of the different Subsectors are the following:
a) To strictly comply with the requirements and obligations established in the allocation of the water resource made by ASA.
b) To incorporate into the formulation of their subsectoral planning the provisions established in the different water planning instruments generated by ASA.
c) To request prior authorization from ASA to modify the conditions and characteristics of the allocated water resource.
d) To inform ASA annually about the use and utilization of the allocated water resource and to guarantee the efficiency of the same, in relation to its quantity and quality.
e) To allow duly accredited ASA officials access to the facilities or infrastructure under their responsibility to carry out monitoring and audits of the allocated resources.
f) To supply the information requested by ASA regarding the situation of the water resource under their responsibility.
g) To inform ASA of all matters related to the permits and authorizations they issue for the water resource they administer, for the purpose of incorporating the data into the Water Information System and the Registry of Water Resources.
h) To comply with the norms and regulations issued by ASA for the protection of water resources, ecosystems, and river basins.
i) To ensure that service operators and the inhabitants in general use appropriate practices in the use and utilization of water resources and sanitation.

**Specific Guidelines for the Drinking Water and Sanitation Subsector**
**Art. 38.-** The drinking water and sanitation Authorities must comply with the following guidelines:
a) To respect the human right to drinking water and sanitation, as established in this Law.
b) To ensure that the systems for drinking water supply, sanitary sewerage, and basic sanitation are optimized.
c) To design and implement risk prevention programs, vulnerability reduction, and have a protocol to address emergency cases.
d) To promote the reuse of treated wastewater.
e) To ensure the surveillance, monitoring, and follow-up of treatment plants and proper sludge management.
f) To raise awareness among the population on the use and management of water for human consumption, preservation of the environment, and to generate a culture of payment.
g) To promote that drinking water service must be provided in a continuous, safe manner, at an adequate quality and pressure, complying with the legal or technical regulations issued for that purpose.

**Specific guidelines for the Agricultural, Aquaculture, and Fishing Subsector**
**Art. 39.-** The competent authority of the Agricultural, Aquaculture, and Fishing Subsector must comply with the following guidelines:
a) To design and implement risk prevention programs, vulnerability reduction, and protocols for response in emergency cases.
b) To request from ASA the necessary annual allocation of water resources to comply with its obligations in granting the authorizations and permits required of it.
c) To comply with the directives issued by ASA on maintaining the environmental flow in the different water bodies.
d) To use and promote irrigation techniques that allow for the efficient use of water, in order to minimize losses and control erosion.
e) To incentivize practices of reusing water from other uses, such as recycling, aquaponics, hydroponics, or treatment systems that meet quality parameters.
f) To incentivize or promote the use of natural fertilizers, fungicides, and pesticides in agricultural activity, which maintain the balance in nitrogen levels and water ecosystems, in order to achieve the gradual substitution of agrochemicals with natural biotechnological products.
g) To promote water and soil conservation practices.
h) When authorizing aquaculture establishments, the carrying capacity of the water bodies must be evaluated, in order to protect the balance of the water ecosystems.
i) To monitor and prevent the construction of works and the performance of tasks in the natural beds or channels of rivers that affect the balance of water ecosystems.
j) When issuing permits and authorizations to carry out activities in the coastal-marine zone and especially in water ecosystems, their protection and conservation must be guaranteed.

**Specific guidelines for the Hydroelectric and Geothermal Subsector**
**Art. 40.-** The competent authorities of the Hydroelectric and Geothermal Subsector must comply with the following guidelines:
a) To guarantee the proper management of hydroelectric plants in order to minimize losses and damages in the face of hydrometeorological events.
b) To comply with the directives issued by ASA on maintaining the environmental flow in the different water bodies.
c) To establish measures for the protection and restoration of riparian ecosystems.
d) For geothermal generation, the provisions established in the corresponding technical regulations must be respected in order not to contaminate by discharge, infiltration, or outfalls, related to surface and underground water.
e) To request from ASA the necessary annual allocation of the water resource.

**Water Boards**
**Art. 41.-** A water board shall be understood as those non-profit social organizations with legal personality whose purpose is to provide drinking water service to the community.
The water boards in urban and rural areas, which provide drinking water service to the population, with the technical support of the corresponding State institutions, must respect the human right to water and sanitation established in this Law.
The water boards mentioned in the preceding paragraph must guarantee the treatment of their wastewater.
The State, through ASA, shall promote the development of the capacities of water boards in urban and rural areas for compliance with the corresponding regulations.

**Authorizations for use of marine waters**
**Art. 42.-** ASA may grant authorizations and permits for the use and utilization of marine waters to public and private entities, for the purpose of using it in desalination processes, cooling, aquaculture, and other uses related to the implementation of new technologies that are compatible with the object of this Law; the applicants must pay the corresponding fee for use, utilization, and discharge.

**TITLE THREE**
**POLICY, PLANNING, INFORMATION SYSTEM, AND NATIONAL REGISTRY OF WATER RESOURCES**

**CHAPTER I**
**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**Water Policy**
**Art. 43.-** The National Policy for Integrated Water Resources Management is the set of strategies, guidelines, and actions that will guide the actions of central, autonomous, and municipal public institutions in the development and implementation of sectoral policies, plans, programs, and projects related to the integrated management of the water resource.

**Guidelines of the Water Policy**
**Art. 44.-** The National Policy for Integrated Water Resources Management shall serve as the basis for the development of water plans at all levels and shall contain at least the following guidelines:
a) Sustainable and equitable utilization of the water resource.
b) Proper management of surface or underground waters in quantity and quality.
c) Priority use oriented to satisfy fundamental human needs; with emphasis on domestic use.
d) Respect for the hydrological cycle and the protection of territorial and marine waters.
e) Dissemination of a culture of water and sanitation that promotes sustainability and socio-environmental co-responsibility.
f) To promote actions for adaptation to the effects of climate change.
g) To foster or incentivize the use of rainwater.
h) Efficient use of the water resource, with emphasis on the agricultural, aquaculture, and fishing subsector.
i) To foster the reuse and recycling of treated wastewater.

**Water Planning**
**Art. 45.-** Water planning is the orderly, permanent, and sustainable projection of the utilization, use, and protection of water resources. It is based on water-related development policies and strategies and has a short, medium, and long-term perspective; as well as on the evolution of different uses, on the control of water quality, and on the sustainable management of water resources.
To carry out the Planning for the Integrated Management of Water Resources, in the water inventories and balances, the protection, conservation, and recovery of river basins and their adaptation to the effects of climate change must be considered.
Water planning shall respond to the guidelines of this Law and shall be consistent with sectoral policies related to environmental and land use planning and development matters.

**Compliance indicators**
**Art. 46.-** ASA, through the design, development, and application of planning instruments, may determine the effectiveness of compliance with the Law.
The regulations of this Law shall establish the compliance indicators.

**Objective of Water Planning**
**Art. 47.-** The objective of water planning is to achieve the normal functioning of aquatic ecosystems, in order to satisfy water demands, increase the availability of the resource, protect its quality, and optimize and rationalize its uses without detriment to the environment.
In the development of the Plans for Integrated Water Resources Management, measures and actions to address emergency situations must be included, based on technical and scientific studies on the matter.
The regulations of this Law shall develop the specific objectives and the content of water planning.

**National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management**
**Art. 48.-** The National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management is the planning instrument of the highest hierarchy with a strategic, public, and mandatory character, which will be carried out with a basin approach.
Said plan shall contain the framework of action that determines the directives for the integrated management of water resources, through the ordering of the use and utilization of said resources, which includes the protection, recovery, and conservation of water quality and quantity, in a sustainable and lasting manner.
The Plan shall be approved by the ASA Board of Directors and shall be updated every five years, and may be modified in shorter periods for reasons of public interest, as well as due to calamities or catastrophes that considerably affect water supply and demand.
The criteria for formulating the National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management shall be established in the regulations of this Law. Likewise, ASA shall prepare and propose said revision for its approval through the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources.
It shall mandatorily comprise, without being limited to, the following aspects:
a) Analysis of the application of the permit structure for water use, including the number of permits granted, the sectors to which permits were granted, and the availability of water for new and future uses.
b) Analysis of the application of the permit structure for wastewater discharges, including the number of permits granted, the sectors to which permits were granted, and the number of permits that did not comply with water quality standards.
c) Analysis of water quality and its integration with discharge permits, including the number and location of impaired waters and their main causes.

**CHAPTER II**
**WATER INFORMATION SYSTEM (SIHI)**

**Water Information System**
**Art. 49.-** All information on surface and underground waters shall be collected, stored, and systematized to form the Water Information System, hereinafter referred to as "SIHI".
The information on surface and underground waters includes the collection of data of a hydrometeorological type, extreme events, water demands, and its quality, which, once collected and verified, will proceed to its processing, storage, and dissemination.
ASA shall be responsible for the administration, management, and coordination of the SIHI and may establish with the different national and international institutions the cooperation mechanisms, protocols for exchange, and generation of information related to water resources, who must supply it to ASA when requested; said information shall be freely accessible to the public.

**Content of the SIHI**
**Art. 50.-** The SIHI shall contain at least the following information:
a) Maps of land use, land use planning, hydrometeorological data measurement stations, intake of surface and underground water sources, discharge sites of outfalls, including their characterization, as well as infrastructure, communication routes, towns, and others.
b) A map of protected natural areas and their buffer zones that contain sources of surface and underground waters.
c) A map of water resource reserve zones, including aquifer and spring recharge areas.
d) A map of inter-basin transfers.
e) A map of the geographical delimitation of protection zones for water resources, sources, and water bodies.
f) A hydrogeological map of aquifers and groundwater quality.
g) A map of water recharge.
h) The records of meteorological, hydrological, and hydrogeological data necessary for the preparation of water balances.
i) The runoff flow rates of surface channels, and the flow and storage of underground waters.
j) The flow rates and volumes of extraction, exploitation time, and intake of surface and underground waters.
k) Water supplies and consumption in the different hydrographic zones, specifying the origins of the source used and the uses to which they are destined.
l) Records on the quality of surface and underground waters, identifying the levels of existing pollutants.
m) Water balances and inventories.
n) Restrictions and incentives in basins and aquifers.
o) The demand for the water resource and its analysis according to the uses of the various socio-economic sectors, as well as future projections, integrating gender analysis transversally.
p) Loss of the water resource and its availability, identifying the causal agents, due to alteration in water quality and due to changes and conflicts in land use.
q) Identification of stretches of river bodies that experience a cut in continuous hydrological flow, including the causes of the cut.

**Information from Users**
**Art. 51.-** ASA may request information on the status of water resources management from natural or legal persons, who must provide it, according to the protocols and procedures that shall be established for that purpose by regulation.

**Annual report on the state of water**
**Art. 52.-** ASA shall prepare an annual report on the national state of water, which must be presented to the Legislative Assembly and disseminated in the main media outlets during the month of March of each year.

**CHAPTER III**
**WATER INVENTORIES AND BALANCES**

**Water Inventory**
**Art. 53.-** The water inventory shall be the technical instrument for decision-making regarding authorizations for the use or utilization of water resources, as well as to support actions for their protection and conservation.
The inventory must contain information related to the situation of water resources in quantity and quality, as well as censuses of uses and users.

**National Water Inventory**
**Art. 54.-** The national water inventory is the responsibility of ASA. For such purposes, it must coordinate with other institutions in the public and private sector, including users of water resources and other social actors interested in the integrated management of water resources.

**Content for the National Water Inventory**
**Art. 55.-** The national water inventory must contain at least, without being limited to, the following information:
a) Statistical data showing the evolution of the natural regime of flows, storage, and water quality throughout the hydrological year.
b) Existing uses and demands.
c) Location, name of the owner, annual volume extracted, location of the water source, including basic characteristics and water quality.
d) Information on wastewater discharges.
e) Information on the quality of surface water masses and seasonal changes.

**National Water Balance**
**Art. 56.-** ASA shall be responsible for conducting and updating the Water Balance, which will serve as the basis for determining authorizations, reserves, and measures for the protection and conservation of water resources at the national level and in each of the country's Hydrographic Zones.
The content, scope, and procedure for preparing the National and Zonal Water Balances shall be developed by regulation.

**CHAPTER IV**
**NATIONAL REGISTRY OF WATER RESOURCES**

**National Registry of Water Resources**
**Art. 57.-** ASA shall have a National Registry of Water Resources, whose objective will be the administrative control of the authorizations, allocations, and permits it issues, and will serve as a guarantee for authorized users and for the public disclosure of the registered instruments.
The National Registry of Water Resources shall be administered by a Registrar appointed by the Board of Directors and shall have the necessary personnel, resources, and organization for the normal functioning of the Registry.

**Requirements to be National Registrar of ASA**
**Art. 58.-** To be National Registrar of ASA, the following requirements must be met:
a) Be a Salvadoran citizen.
b) Be over 30 years of age.
c) Have no criminal record.
d) Be a professional with extensive knowledge and proven experience related to the integrated management of water resources.
e) Be free from claims of any kind in case of having been a contractor for public works executed with funds from the State or municipalities.
f) Not be the spouse or cohabiting partner, or a person with a kinship relationship up to the second degree of affinity and fourth degree of consanguinity with other directive officials of ASA or permit holders.

The National Registrar shall report to the Executive Directorate.

**Instruments to be registered in the National Registry of Water Resources**
**Art. 59.-** The following information, at a minimum, must be registered in the National Registry of Water Resources:
a) Allocations to public entities that are users of water resources.
b) Authorizations for use and utilization of water resources and of the goods established in the public water domain.
c) Permits for discharges and for exploration of underground waters.
d) The modifications, suspensions, and cancellations imposed on authorizations, allocations, and permits in accordance with the provisions of this Law.
e) The certification of the judgment by means of which an easement is authorized, granted in accordance with the corresponding regulations.

The information in the Registry shall be permanently updated and shall be available to the public under the conditions established by law.

**Relationship of the SIHI with the Registry**
**Art. 60.-** The SIHI shall have full and unrestricted information from the National Registry of Water Resources for the corresponding legal purposes.
ASA shall generate the information for the National Registry of Water Resources regarding authorizations, exploration and discharge permits; as well as information related to the fees for the use and utilization of water resources, discharges, and for the uses of the goods established in the public water domain.

**Competencies of the official in charge of the Registry**
**Art. 61.-** The official in charge of the Registry shall have the following competencies:
a) To incorporate the information related to the instruments to be registered in the National Registry of Water Resources.
b) To issue, notify, and certify the authorizations, allocations, and permits issued by ASA.
c) To supervise the terms and validity of authorizations, allocations, and permits and to communicate their expiration to ASA.
d) To provide relevant information to ASA about the files of authorizations, allocations, and permits when requested.
e) To present periodic reports and an annual report to ASA on the development of their powers.
f) To create and keep updated the database of uses and users of the water resource.
g) Any others assigned to them in the Internal Regulations.

**TITLE FOUR**
**USE OF THE PUBLIC WATER DOMAIN**

**SOLE CHAPTER**
**USES OF THE WATER RESOURCE**

**SECTION ONE**
**COMMON PROVISIONS**

**General criteria for use**
**Art. 62.-** All use of the water resource shall be made in accordance with the priority uses established in this Law, which constitute the way to establish preferences for the use and utilization of the water resource in order to manage supply and demand for its sustainability.

**Priority order for the use and utilization of the water resource.**
**Art. 63.-** ASA must guarantee the use and utilization related to the water resource according to the following priority order:
a) Water for human consumption and domestic use.
b) Use for ecosystem sustainability.
c) Agricultural use, seeking food security.
d) Use for the generation of electric power.
e) Industrial and commercial use.
f) Recreational uses.
g) Other uses.

The waters used and necessary for human consumption and domestic use have priority, and their use cannot be subordinated or conditioned to any other use; their only restriction shall be given by the environmental flow regime defined for the water source.

The priority uses establish a hierarchy and are not mutually exclusive, except in case of conflicts between different uses or due to availability, in which case the immediately superior use shall acquire preeminence.

**Right and obligations for domestic use**
**Art. 64.-** Every person may make common use of the surface waters of rivers, lakes, and lagoons, and of underground waters for domestic purposes; these uses shall be carried out in a way that does not cause deterioration of the quality and flow of the waters, nor damage to third parties, and without waste or misuse of the same, complying with the environmental and health norms in this regard.

Domestic use shall not require authorization, nor shall it be subject to the collection of a fee for use and utilization by ASA.

**Environmental Flow and Ecosystems**
**Art. 65.-** For every authorization, ASA must determine the environmental flow regime that allows for the proper development of the biological cycles of species associated with the aquatic environment, conserving the sustainability of water ecosystems, and satisfying the needs of the population.

**Utilization of rainwater**
**Art. 66.-** The State shall promote the utilization and harvesting of rainwater, for which it must promote incentive programs, national and international cooperation, both financial and technical.
ASA shall issue the guidelines or directives for the utilization and harvesting of rainwater.
Every natural person may use the rainwater that precipitates on their property, provided that it is not for commercial purposes, does not cause prejudice to third parties, nor significantly affects other water sources.

**Transfers**
**Art. 67.-** ASA may authorize the transfers of surface or underground waters from one basin to another, only in exceptional cases, when:
a) In the demanding basin, there is no other alternative that guarantees the environmental flow regime.
b) It is essential to guarantee the water supply for human, domestic, non-commercial, or non-profit consumption to already established populations.
c) To guarantee food security.
d) To guarantee the energy production of state hydroelectric plants.

For the approval of a transfer, it will be essential to conduct an environmental impact assessment in order to take all necessary prevention and mitigation measures that ensure the minimum ecological and social impact in both basins.
The regulations of this Law will develop the other requirements that must be met to authorize transfers.

**Rationing of water resource use**
**Art. 68.-** In a state of water scarcity, ASA may establish rationing measures to restrict the use of water not intended for human and domestic use.

**SECTION TWO**
**ALLOCATIONS AND AUTHORIZATIONS**

**Use and utilization of water**
**Art. 69.-** Every Natural or Legal Person, Public or Private, who intends to utilize or use water resources for purposes other than domestic use must obtain the corresponding consent from ASA. This may be of three classes: Public Allocations, authorizations, authorizations for the goods established in the public water domain, and exploration and discharge permits.

ASA will permit the use and utilization of waters, attending to the availability and priority uses of water, the provisions of the zonal water plans, and the objectives of the National Policy for Integrated Water Resource Management.

Every authorization for the use and utilization of water will include the conditions for the wastewater discharged to a receiving medium. The conditions of the discharge are specific to each authorization.

**Public Allocations**
**Art. 70.-** The organisms of the public administration, whether centralized or decentralized, that intend to utilize water resources, shall request from ASA the allocation of a specific volume of water, necessary for the fulfillment of their duties, for a period not exceeding five years, with the exception of uses for electricity generation. These may request their allocation for periods of up to fifteen years. And according to the update of the flow regime, the water balance of the basins, and compliance with the provisions established in this Law by the applicant during the period for which it was authorized.

The assignees, without exception, must pay annually the fees according to the volume of water granted to them, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and its Regulations.

The assigning public institutions may authorize the use or utilization of the allocated waters, in accordance with their corresponding regulations and in adherence to the provisions of this Law.

**Authorizations**
**Art. 71.-** ASA may issue authorizations for consumptive or non-consumptive use, or utilization of a certain quantity and quality of surface or underground water, of first use, whether fresh, brackish, or marine, including thermal waters, to be extracted under a specific regime, at a defined geographical point.

Level 1 authorizations for the use and utilization of water resources will be when the requested volume is equal to or greater than 365,000 cubic meters per year; and it may issue level 2 authorizations when the requested flow rate is less than 365,000 cubic meters per year. To guarantee this, the authorized parties must install meters to determine water consumption.

Level 1 authorizations will be granted for a validity period of up to fifteen years, and level 2 authorizations will have a validity period of up to five years; both authorizations may be renewed subject to a technical study, ordered and supervised by ASA, which proves that the aquifer conditions have not changed.

In the case of authorizations granted to water boards, they will have a validity period of up to fifteen years, which may be renewed subject to a technical study, ordered and supervised by ASA.

**Authorizations of National Goods that form the Public Water Domain**
**Art. 72.-** ASA will issue a technical opinion for the competent authority to authorize private individuals to use the national goods established in the public water domain.

**Scope of Authorizations**
**Art. 73.-** Authorizations will be subject to the uses indicated in the corresponding resolution, and the waters included therein may not be applied to uses other than those mentioned in said resolution. Likewise, they may not be subject to transfer, commercialization, or transmission.

Every authorization issued by ASA will include the conditions related to the disposal of discharges, as well as the infrastructure works for water retention or capture on goods that form the public water domain. Said authorizations will be governed by this Law and its Regulations.

**Requirements for granting Authorizations for use and utilization**
**Art. 74.-** To obtain an authorization for use and utilization of water resources, as well as its extensions, it will be necessary to submit an application to ASA, which will contain at least the following information:
a) The name and general information of the interested party, address, place or technical means, whether electronic, magnetic, or any other, designated for notifications and, where applicable, the name and general information of the person representing them.
b) Quantity of water requested.
c) Location of the respective source.
d) Type of activity for which the water is intended.
e) Favorable opinion from ASA for use and utilization.
f) Proof of payment for the processing fee.
g) The investments made by the interested parties, prior to or during the processing of the authorizations, do not constitute an obligation for the granting of the same.
h) Good standing with all types of obligations with ASA. In the case of legal entities, the good standing must include the legal representative, directors, and shareholders.

**Procedure for issuing authorizations**
**Art. 75.-** Once the application and the corresponding documentation have been submitted, ASA will resolve the request for level 1 authorizations within a period not exceeding ninety business days, counted from its receipt, subject to payment for the processing according to the corresponding service fee. For level 2 authorizations, the maximum period will be sixty business days. Both periods include the publication of the application, the presentation, review, and resolution of oppositions, if any, and the inspections made by ASA.

Exceptionally, due to the complexity and dimensions of the activity related to the use or utilization of the water resource, the periods mentioned in the first paragraph of this article may be extended by up to thirty additional business days, and for officially declared national emergency situations, for the duration of the emergency.

The investments made by the interested parties, prior to or during the processing of the authorizations, do not constitute an obligation for the granting of the same.

**Preventive notices**
**Art. 76.-** When the analysis of the corresponding application shows that it does not meet the requirements for granting authorizations established in this Law and reflects deficiencies in form or content, ASA must notify the interested party of the corresponding observations, specifying the aspects that need to be expanded or clarified, among others, so that they may be corrected within a period of twenty business days.

ASA may make observations on the said application only once, but if the interested party does not overcome the observations, their application will be filed without further processing, and their right to submit a new application, if appropriate under the Law, will be preserved.

ASA may make observations regarding new elements or facts that the interested party presents when trying to correct the observations mentioned in the preceding paragraph, for which the Salvadoran Water Authority will give them a period of ten business days. If they do not overcome said observations regarding the new elements, their application will be filed without further processing.

The times established in this article to overcome the observations suspend the period to resolve the application established in the preceding article.

**Publication**
**Art. 77.-** The publication of the application will be at the expense of the interested party, which must be done within a maximum period of ten business days, through a notice containing an extract of the same, which must be published in a newspaper of major national circulation, digital newspapers, or institutional digital platforms with intervals of two days between each publication; a copy of the same will also be sent to the corresponding Municipal Mayor's Office(s), so that it may be brought to the community's attention.

**Oppositions**
**Art. 78.-** Once the publication requirement is met, any Natural or Legal Person who has a legitimate interest, technically substantiated, and who is affected in the project's area of influence, may submit a request of opposition to the issuance of the corresponding authorization, within a period not exceeding fifteen business days, counted from the day following the last publication.

The opposition will be made through a formal written statement, addressed to ASA, explaining the reasons of fact and law for not granting the authorization; the written statement will be accompanied by the documentary evidence in their possession or indicating where it can be found.

Once the opposition is presented and when so requested, ASA will summon the parties to appear personally or through an attorney, in a joint or separate hearing; which must be scheduled within a period not exceeding ten business days counted from the day following the expiration of the period to present opposition.

Once the period established in the first paragraph has ended or when no hearing is requested, ASA will have ten business days to resolve the oppositions received. ASA may carry out inspections and gather necessary information during the procedure.

If ASA rules in favor of the opponent(s), the procedure will be terminated, leaving the applicant the opportunity to appeal said resolution; if the resolution is in favor of the applicant, the process will continue according to the provisions of this Law.

**Technical opinion for use and utilization of the water resource**
**Art. 79.-** Except for the exceptions established in this Law, every natural or legal person interested in making use and utilization of the water resource, prior to carrying out any activity, work, or project, must obtain a favorable technical opinion from ASA on the existence, availability, quality, and compatibility with the requested use and utilization of the water resource; for this purpose, they must submit an application and the necessary technical studies.

The favorable or unfavorable opinion will be issued within a period of fifteen business days, counted from the receipt of the corresponding application; if favorable, this opinion does not constitute any right, authorization, or permit, and will be valid for one year.
The technical opinion will warn about the possible overexploitation of aquifers or, in the case of the use of surface waters, will warn about possible conflicts of use.

**Final Resolution**
**Art. 80.-** ASA may not refrain from resolving a matter within its competence on the pretext of a vacuum or obscurity in the applicable legal provisions or in the issues that arise in the procedure.

The resolution will decide all questions of fact and law that result from the file, even if they have not been raised by the interested parties. In the latter case, it will be necessary to give the interested parties a prior hearing so that they may express their views on the matter and, if applicable, provide evidence. ASA will determine the period for this hearing, taking into account the circumstances of the case, but it may not be less than three business days.

In no case may the final resolution aggravate or harm the initial situation of the administrated party at whose instance the procedure was initiated.

**Reconsideration Appeal**
**Art. 81.-** Against all administrative resolutions issued by ASA, the interested natural or legal person may file a reconsideration appeal, which will be heard and resolved by the ASA Board of Directors, based on the record, within a period of fifteen business days.
The period for filing the reconsideration appeal is ten business days from the day following its notification.

**Renewal of authorizations**
**Art. 82.-** Authorizations may be renewed provided that the request is made six months before their expiration date and that the applicant has complied with the conditions set therein, in accordance with the provisions of the water audits. These renewals will be subject to the payment of a fee.

**Administrative silence**
**Art. 83.-** ASA is obliged to notify petitioners of the resolution regarding applications for allocation, authorizations, or permits, as well as their extensions and others related to the use and utilization of water, as well as discharges, in accordance with the periods established in this Law.

In the event that ASA fails to inform the petitioner of the resolution on their application, it is considered that the request has been denied. In such a case, the applicant may file the corresponding reconsideration appeal.

The absence of a resolution on the application may entail responsibilities for the officials to whom such resolution pertains, in accordance with the provisions contained in the applicable legal framework.

**Effects of Administrative Silence in Procedures Initiated Ex Officio**
**Art. 84.-** In procedures initiated ex officio, the expiration of the maximum established period without an express resolution having been issued and notified does not exempt the Administration from the fulfillment of the legal obligation to resolve. Once the said period has expired, the following effects will occur:
a) In the case of procedures from which the recognition or, where applicable, the constitution of individualized rights or other legal situations could be derived, the interested parties who have appeared may understand their claims to be dismissed by administrative silence.
b) In procedures in which the Administration exercises sanctioning powers or, in general, intervention powers, capable of producing unfavorable or burdensome effects, lapse will occur. In these cases, the resolution declaring the lapse will order the filing of the proceedings, with the effects provided for in this Law.

In cases where the procedure has been paralyzed for a cause attributable to the interested party, the computation of the period to resolve and notify the resolution will be interrupted.

**Authorizations issued by the subsectors**
**Art. 85.-** In the case of authorizations issued by the different competent entities of the subsectors, they will be carried out in accordance with the procedure established in this Law.

**Water Audits**
**Art. 86.-** To ensure compliance with the conditions set in the authorizations, permits, and accompanying documents, ASA may conduct periodic water audits, within a period not exceeding one year or randomly, according to the requirements and procedures established in the Regulations of this Law.

Water audits may be conducted during and outside of business hours, and without prior notice to the authorized parties.

**Modification of authorizations**
**Art. 87.-** Authorized parties may request ASA to modify the terms and conditions of each type of authorization granted. The procedure to be followed will be the same as that for the original authorization.

**Suspension of authorizations**
**Art. 88.-** Authorizations may be suspended, by means of a reasoned resolution, for any of the following reasons:
a) Cause of public interest.
b) Cause of force majeure or an officially declared state of emergency and disaster.
c) Failure to comply with the conditions established in the corresponding authorization.
d) Repeated failure to pay fees.
e) Denying entry to ASA delegates for conducting inspections, audits, and corresponding monitoring.

The suspension will last as long as the responsible party does not remedy the causes that motivated it, within the period established by ASA.

**Cancellation of authorizations**
**Art. 89.-** Authorizations may be canceled for any of the following reasons:
a) Non-compliance on more than two consecutive occasions or three alternate occasions with the conditions stipulated in the corresponding authorization.
b) Being sanctioned for having committed any of the serious or very serious infractions, according to this Law.
c) For water contamination due to the negligence of the holder.
d) Use of a volume or flow rate greater than that authorized.
e) Not using the water for two consecutive years after it was granted.
f) Utilization of the resource for unauthorized uses and on unauthorized properties.
g) For having provided false information to obtain the authorization.
h) The circumstances that motivated the suspension persist after the period established to correct them has passed.

**Procedure for recovery, modification, suspension, or cancellation of authorizations**
**Art. 90.-** For the purposes of recovery, modification, suspension, or cancellation of the authorizations referred to in this Law, the authorized party will be summoned to a hearing within a period of ten business days to appear personally or through an attorney to present evidence in their defense or justifications, as the case may be; with or without their presence, ASA will issue the final resolution within a period of five business days following.

**Duty to communicate acts that affect persons**
**Art. 91.-** Every administrative act that affects the rights or interests of persons must be duly notified in the administrative procedure.
Every notification must be made within a period of five business days counted from the day following the issuance of the administrative act.

**SECTION THREE**
**PERMITS**

**Permits**
**Art. 92.-** ASA may issue permits to natural or legal persons, which may be of two classes:
a) Discharge
b) Exploration.

**Discharge Permits**
**Art. 93.-** This is an administrative act by which the performance of discharges is authorized, upon application, which must be submitted by every natural or legal, public or private person, in accordance with the form issued for this purpose by ASA, incorporating the information as the case may be; ASA will have a period not exceeding thirty business days to resolve the said application.

The application mentioned in the first paragraph may be subject to observations in accordance with the provisions of this Law; the authorization issued for this purpose by ASA will allow the discharge into a receiving body located on public or private property, of wastewater altered in its physical, chemical, or biological characteristics, provided that it has been previously treated by virtue of the provisions of this Law or special regulations.

This permit will have a validity period of up to three years and may be renewed provided that the request is made six months before its expiration and, in addition, the conditions established therein have been met.

In case of an increase in the flow rate or modification of the characteristics of the discharges, the interested party must previously request the corresponding permits.
ASA may establish temporary restrictions in specific areas for carrying out discharges in order to contribute to the protection and recovery of water bodies.

**Guidelines for the issuance of discharge permits**
**Art. 94.-** To grant permits for the discharge of wastewater, at least the following must be taken into consideration:
a) Complying with the physicochemical and biological parameters established in the current Technical Regulation for wastewater, which the competent authority issues for this purpose, according to the type of use or utilization that corresponds and depending on the characteristics of the receiving medium of the discharges.
b) Gradually adapting to the decontamination goals contained in the programs designed for this purpose.
c) Respecting the measures issued by the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources, as the case may be.
d) Reviewing the permits at least every year in order to keep them updated.

**Prohibition of discharges into aquifers**
**Art. 95.-** When the discharge leads to the infiltration or storage of polluting substances in aquifers, it will not be authorized for any reason. ASA will provide the terms of reference for the formulation of the study to know that it does not cause harm to the receiving body, which will be done at the applicant's expense, within the procedure for granting the corresponding authorization.

**Definitive cessation of activities**
**Art. 96.-** ASA must order the definitive cessation of activities that give rise to unauthorized discharges or authorized discharges that do not comply with the conditions of said permit and order the measures it deems necessary for their correction, without prejudice to the civil, criminal, or administrative liability incurred by those who cause them.
For the suspension, cancellation, and modification of discharge permits, the provisions of the articles of this Law shall apply as the case may be.

**Treatment of discharges as a commercial activity**
**Art. 97.-** Natural or legal persons, public or private, who request authorization to carry out treatment of discharges as a commercial activity, must first provide a bond to ASA, to ensure compliance with the conditions established in the corresponding discharge permit.

The discharge permit granted in their favor will include, in addition to the general conditions required, the classification of the discharges to be treated by the company and the payment of the corresponding fees.

The authorized party will be obliged to submit periodic reports on the quality of the water before the discharge point and of treated sludge. The frequency will be established when the authorization is issued.

**Exploration permit**
**Art. 98.-** Every natural or legal person, public or private, who intends to use or utilize national underground waters, must first request from ASA the corresponding exploration permit, without this giving them exclusivity or a guarantee of being granted a utilization authorization.

Once the application is admitted, ASA will create a file and issue the corresponding resolution within a period not exceeding twenty business days from the admission of the application. If favorable, the permit will set the conditions and measures to which said permit will be subject.

Any damages and losses that the permit holder may cause must be covered at their own expense and risk.

The interested party may appeal the resolution that is issued, by filing a reconsideration appeal, within a period of ten business days from the day following the notification.

The validity of the underground water exploration permit may not be greater than one year; if the exploration is not carried out within said period, the permit process must be redone.

Regardless of whether or not the exploration is carried out, the beneficiary of the permit must provide ASA with the resulting information, within a period not exceeding sixty business days; the information on the result of the exploration must be in accordance with the provisions of the regulations of this Law.

**Registry of entities dedicated to exploration activities**
**Art. 99.-** Natural or legal persons, public or private, whose activity is the drilling of wells must register with ASA and provide detailed technical information about their equipment and drilling procedures.

Likewise, they must present annually or when required, a report of exploration activities, annexing the technical documentation of each drilling performed, in accordance with the regulations provided by ASA.

The commercial activity of well drilling may only be carried out when there is a prior exploration permit issued by ASA.

ASA may request information on the historical records of exploration activities carried out by the entities included in this article.

**To prevent or suspend exploration works**
**Art. 100.-** ASA may prevent exploration works from being carried out or suspend those already initiated, ordering their destruction and sealing, when such works are done without authorization or in a manner different from that authorized.

If the administrated party does not proceed with the destruction of the works, ASA will do so at their expense, and the certification of the costs of such destruction will be enforceable.

**SECTION FOUR**
**EASEMENTS**

**Easements**
**Art. 101.-** Prior to initiating the process for authorizations for the use or utilization of national waters, and in the event that it is necessary to occupy a property belonging to a different owner than the applicant, the applicant must manage the obtaining of the corresponding easement.

In case of no agreement between the owner of the dominant estate and the servient estate, the constitution of an easement may be requested before the competent judicial authority in accordance with common law.

**Registration of Easements**
**Art. 102.-** The instruments that support the establishment of an easement must be registered in the Land and Mortgage Registry of the National Registry Center and in the National Registry of Water Resources.

**TITLE FIVE**
**WATER CULTURE**

**SOLE CHAPTER**

**New Water Culture**
**Art. 103.-** ASA will coordinate efforts with the Ministry of Education and other organizations related to water issues to incorporate into the curriculum, plans, and programs of the national education system the promotion of a new Water Culture; taking into account the principles of this Law.

**Awareness in the use of the water resource**
**Art. 104.-** ASA, in coordination with the Ministry of Education, will promote with educational institutions, environmental non-governmental organizations, the business sector, and the media, the formulation and development of awareness programs with an emphasis on Integrated Water Resources Management, sustainable management of river basins, and the protection, conservation, and rational use of water.

**TITLE SIX**
**ECONOMIC REGIME**

**CHAPTER I**
**ESTABLISHMENT OF FEES**

**Collection of fees**
**Art. 105.-** ASA will be empowered to determine and authorize the fees for water use and utilization, for discharges, and for the use of the goods that are part of the public water domain, as well as for the administrative, technical, and scientific services provided to third parties, whether public or private, that it sells or provides.

The income from these items will be used to cover the administrative expenses of ASA and those related to the integrated management of water resources.

**Establishment of fees**
**Art. 106.-** The fee is established as a payment in legal tender, and is applicable in the following cases:
a) For the use and utilization of national waters, whether consumptive or non-consumptive;
b) For the discharge of wastewater into receiving media that are part of the public water domain; and
c) For the use of goods established in the public water domain.

The taxable base for the calculation of fees will be published in the Official Gazette for the purpose of its validity; the taxable base of said fees may be modified according to new environmental conditions and the availability of the water resource.

The payment of the fee must be made by every natural or legal person, public or private, authorized with an allocation, authorization, or permit; or who, according to this law, must obtain said authorization from ASA.

**Payment for administrative, technical, or scientific services of ASA**
**Art. 107.-** All natural or legal persons, public or private, who must, may, or are authorized in accordance with this law and who wish any type of administrative, technical, or scientific service provided by ASA, will be obliged to the corresponding payment by reason of this law.

ASA will issue the rates for the administrative, technical, or scientific services it provides.

**Purpose of the fee for use and utilization of the water resource**
**Art. 108.-** The purpose of the fee for the use and utilization of national waters is to contribute to regulating the integrated management of water and to provide the necessary financial resources to carry out activities related to said management.

**Collection of fee for use or utilization of the water resource**
**Art. 109.-** All natural or legal persons, public or private, who use the water resource for purposes other than domestic use in accordance with this law will be subject to the payment of the fee.
In the case of water supply networks, ASA will apply the collection of this fee to the entities that provide said service.

**Determination of the fee for use and utilization of the water resource.**
**Art. 110.-** The fee for use and utilization will be established based on the volume of water used in a specific period of time. This period of time will be expressed in hours per day, days per month, or months per year.

The formula for the establishment of the fee for use and utilization of the water resource will be the following:

CFU = VU x BP x C1 x C2 x C3 x C4 x C5

Where:
CFU = Fee for use and utilization of water in dollars ($)
VU = Volume of water used, expressed in cubic meters (m)
BP = Base price, expressed in dollars/m ($0.03-$0.2)
C1 = Coefficient based on the type of use and utilization in accordance with this Law (dimensionless)
C2 = Coefficient based on the origin of the waters at the intake point (dimensionless)
C3 = Coefficient for socio-environmental conditions of the Hydrological Analysis Unit (HAU) (dimensionless)
C4 = Coefficient based on the social interest or purpose of the use or utilization of the water resource (dimensionless)
C5 = Coefficient related to the consumptive or non-consumptive use condition of the water resource (dimensionless).

The base price (BP) may be updated according to the consumer price index or any other value of socioeconomic relevance to the country established by the Central Reserve Bank.

ASA will establish in the regulations of this law the values of the coefficients established in this article for the determination of the payment as a fee for use and utilization.

**Discharge fee**
**Art. 111.-** All natural or legal persons, public or private, who use water bodies to introduce, transport, or eliminate discharges that may cause modifications in the physical, chemical, or biological quality of the water will be subject to the payment of the discharge fee.

The circumstances in which a discharge-generating entity must be to be subject to the payment of the discharge fee are the following:
a) That there is a point source discharge.
b) That the discharge is made to a receiving body.
c) That the net discharged pollutant load in any of the parameters subject to the collection of the fee results in positive values.

Excluded from the payment of the discharge fee are all those generating entities that do not comply with the three previous requirements; as well as those whose domestic use wastewater is discharged into a septic tank with a drain for infiltration into the subsoil. Single-family homes that are under the situation described in this article will be excluded from the process of obtaining a discharge permit and therefore are exempt from the payment of this type of fee.

In the case of sanitary sewer networks, ASA will apply the collection of this fee to the entities that provide said service and not to those who discharge into them.

**Purpose of the discharge fee**
**Art. 112.-** The purpose of the discharge fee is to contribute to regulating the conditions, characteristics, and quality, as well as improving the conditions in the receiving media that are part of the public water domain, reducing risks to the environment and especially those related to people's health, ensuring its subsequent utilization by other uses and users, and incentivizing behavior patterns that contribute to reducing pollution.

The payment of the discharge fee will be made through payment in legal tender, based on the cost of removing polluting elements using the best available technology. This type of fee will provide financial resources to ASA for the execution of actions that allow for preventing, controlling, and reversing pollution and maintaining the adequate level of quality of water resources.

**Determination of the discharge fee**
**Art. 113.-** The total value expressed in dollars as a discharge fee will correspond to the sum of the values calculated for the individual fee for each parameter subject to collection according to the following expression:

DFC = n=1 FeePj

Where:
DFC = Discharge fee, expressed in dollars ($)
FeePj = Fee for each parameter used, expressed in dollars ($)
n = number of parameters to be used.

The calculation of the value for each parameter will be based on the relationship between the discharged concentration of said parameter and the maximum permitted concentration of it.

The scenarios and formulas to determine the fee for each parameter j will be the following:
a) When the discharged concentration of parameter j (Cvj) is less than or equal to the maximum permitted concentration for said parameter (Cpj), the following formula will be used:

FeePj = Q x DaF x t x Cnj x BPj x M x A x K1 x C3 x C5

Where:
FeePj = Fee for each parameter j used, expressed in dollars ($)
Q = Average discharged flow rate expressed in liters per second (l/s)
DaF = Dimensional adjustment factor based on the units of measurement of parameter j.
t = Average discharge time per day of the user, expressed in hours.
Cnj = Discharged concentration minus concentration at the intake point, of parameter j, in the measurement units established in the corresponding technical regulation.
BPj = Base price corresponding to parameter j, expressed in dollars per unit of measurement of said parameter ($/unit of measurement).
M = Monthly discharge period of the user, expressed in days.
A = Annual discharge period of the user, expressed in months.
K1 = Regulatory compliance coefficient (0.5-4) (dimensionless)
C3 = Coefficient for socio-environmental conditions of the Hydrological Analysis Unit (HAU) (dimensionless)
C5 = Coefficient related to the consumptive or non-consumptive use condition of the water resource (dimensionless).

a) When the discharged concentration of parameter j (Cvj) is greater than the maximum permitted concentration for said parameter (Cpj), the following formula will be used:

FeePj = Q x DaF x t x BPj x M x A x [(Cpj  Ca) + (Cvj  Cpj) x K1] x C3 x C5

Where:
FeePj = Fee for each parameter j used, expressed in dollars ($)
Q = Average discharged flow rate expressed in liters per second (l/s)
DaF = Dimensional adjustment factor based on the units of measurement of parameter j.
t = Average discharge time per day of the user, expressed in hours.
BPj = Base price corresponding to parameter j, expressed in dollars per unit of measurement of said parameter ($/unit of measurement).
M = Monthly discharge period of the user, expressed in days.
A = Annual discharge period of the user, expressed in months.
Cpj = Maximum permitted concentration of parameter j, as established in the corresponding technical regulation and in the measurement units established in said regulation.
Caj = Present concentration of parameter j at the intake point, in the measurement units established in the corresponding technical regulation.
Cnj = Discharged concentration minus concentration at the intake point, of parameter j, in the measurement units established in the corresponding technical regulation.
Cvj = Discharged concentration of parameter j, in the measurement units established in the corresponding technical regulation.
K1 = Regulatory compliance coefficient (0.5-4) (dimensionless)
C3 = Coefficient for socio-environmental conditions of the Hydrological Analysis Unit (HAU) (dimensionless)
C5 = Coefficient related to the consumptive or non-consumptive use condition of the water resource (dimensionless).

For the purposes of all the previous formulas, the maximum permitted concentration or permissible limits of each parameter will be those established in the corresponding technical regulations.

ASA will establish in the regulations of this law the values of the coefficients established in this article for the determination of the payment as a discharge fee.

**Obligation to submit information to ASA**
**Art. 114.-** All natural or legal persons, public or private, subject to the payment of fees in accordance with the provisions of this Law, must submit to ASA the information required of them in accordance with the stipulations of the corresponding authorization.

**Presumptive pollutant load**
**Art. 115.-** In the event that natural or legal persons, public and private, subject to the payment of the discharge fee, do not provide the information required by ASA regarding concentrations, flow rates, and discharge times, said institution will establish it based on technically adequate analyses and studies or by comparison with similar activities in the corresponding Hydrographic Zone, without prejudice to the imposition of the corresponding sanctions.

The costs resulting from obtaining the requested information and studies will be borne by the responsible user, with their reimbursement being made effective according to the procedure established in the regulations of this Law. In case of non-compliance, the certification issued by ASA will be enforceable.

**Fee for the use of goods that are part of the public water domain**
**Art. 116.-** The fee for the use of goods that are part of the public water domain will be developed in the regulations of this Law, based on the ecosystem value that said goods provide, which must be calculated by ASA.

**Fee update**
**Art. 117.-** The values of the coefficients and the base price for the calculation and determination of the fee will be published by ASA and must be updated within a maximum period of three years. As long as new values for the coefficients and the base price have not been approved, the last approved ones will be applied transitionally.

**CHAPTER II**
**PATRIMONIAL REGIME**

**Constitution of the assets of ASA**
**Art. 118.-** The constitution of the assets of ASA will be composed of:
a) Income from the collection of fees for use and utilization of the water resource, for discharges, and for the goods belonging to the public water domain.
b) Income from the collection of tariffs for the sale of products and services.
c) Complementary funds from the Central Government.
d) Funds from International Cooperation, contributions, legacies, subsidies, credits, and donations.
e) Movable and immovable property owned by it.
f) Rights derived from the application of this Law.

**Budgetary allocation**
**Art. 119.-** The Executive Branch, through the Ministry of Finance, must incorporate in the General Budget of the Nation the corresponding budgetary allocation for the operation and functioning of ASA, so that it may comply with the purpose and powers established in this Law.

**Destination of financial resources**
**Art. 120.-** The financial resources collected and administered by ASA will be destined for:
a) Financing activities, projects, and programs related to the integrated management of water resources.
b) Measurement, monitoring, and control of water resources and the goods that are part of the public water domain.
c) Inventory, water balance, and the national plan for the integrated management of water resources.
d) The operation and continuous improvement of the SIHI.
e) Strengthening of ASA's Human Capital.
f) For research, innovation, scientific and technological development.
g) Hiring of personnel for the implementation of this Law, its regulations, and complementary norms.

**Activities to be incentivized**
**Art. 121.-** ASA will promote the development of activities, projects, or programs that include:
a) Efficiency in the use of the water resource, both at the household and industrial levels.
b) The utilization and harvesting of rainwater.
c) The treatment of wastewater.
d) The restoration of water bodies.
e) The recharge of underground waters.
f) The reuse and recycling of wastewater.
g) Industrial reconversion processes oriented towards cleaner production in water resource matters.
h) Soil conservation and river basin protection projects.
i) Educational campaigns for the management of water resources.
j) Other activities oriented towards the improvement, protection, and conservation of water resources, ecosystems, aquifers, and river basins.

**TITLE SEVEN**
**PROTECTION AND CONSERVATION OF WATER RESOURCES**

**CHAPTER I**
**PROTECTION ACTIONS AND ENVIRONMENTAL FLOW REGIME**

**Protection and conservation of water resources**
**Art. 122.-** ASA must develop, directly or through third parties, actions that tend to protect, improve, or maintain the conditions of availability of surface and underground water resources in quantity and quality, in the terms of this Law.

Water Planning will especially incorporate in its regulation and control actions the directives and activities aimed at the protection and conservation of water resources, river basins, water ecosystems, and environmental flow regimes.

**Directives for the utilization and conservation of water resources**
**Art. 123.-** ASA will formulate the directives and carry out the necessary activities and actions oriented to protect water resources in terms of the use or utilization of said resources; for this purpose, it will consider the following limitations:
a) Groundwater extractions not greater than the aquifer recharge of the extraction zone and the aquifer as a whole, considering the maintenance of a minimum positive storage rate according to the provisions of the water plans and considering the already existing extractions, as protection and environmental reserve of the aquifer.
b) Water intakes not greater than the availability of the surface resource of the recharge zone, conserving the environmental flows and the authorized demands in the downstream zones.
c) Utilization of underground waters, incorporating directives aimed at preventing and avoiding pollution.

Natural and legal persons who use or utilize waters in any use or activity are obliged to take the necessary measures to prevent their pollution and, where appropriate, to return them in adequate conditions in order to allow their subsequent use in other activities or uses and to maintain the balance of the ecosystems.

The regulations of this Law will establish the other necessary activities and actions that must be complied with.

**Protection zones for surface sources**
**Art. 124.-** In order to adequately protect the quantity and quality of water, ASA may technically establish protection zones for the goods that are part of the public water domain.
ASA will be empowered to regulate land use and the activities that are developed in said zones.

**Declaration of aquifer protection zones**
**Art. 125.-** In order to prevent the overexploitation and depletion of aquifers, ASA may limit the uses and utilization of underground waters. If the aquifer is in a situation of extreme recovery or water stress, according to the exploitation index, ASA may declare the groundwater recharge zone as an aquifer protection zone with priority character, strictly restricting and controlling any activity, work, or project that by its interrelation could negatively affect the quality and quantity of the underground waters.

ASA may declare water recharge zones for aquifers or springs, without the need for a situation of water stress to have been reached, applying the precautionary principle and the other general principles of the Law.

Based on the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the declaration will contain the necessary actions and measures for the recovery of the adequate levels of balance of said underground water body.

From the entry into force of this Law, the protection zones declared by ASA will be made known to the offices of the Municipal Cadastre and the National Registry Center.

**Utilization of existing water resources in Protected Natural Areas**
**Art. 126.-** ASA may authorize the use and utilization of existing water resources in Protected Natural Areas, subject to a favorable technical opinion from the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources, in accordance with the provisions of the Management Plan of the Area in question or the guidelines issued for this purpose by said Ministry in accordance with the Law on Protected Natural Areas and the Environmental Law.

**Determination of the environmental flow regime**
**Art. 127.-** ASA will be responsible for determining the environmental flow regime, in order to maintain the stability of water ecosystems and satisfy the social and economic needs and demands of the population, guaranteeing a sustainable use of water resources. For this purpose, it will coordinate with other entities related to the theme of water resources.

For the determination of the environmental flow, ASA will establish the necessary instruments and methodologies for its calculation, which will be contained in the regulations of this Law.

**Monitoring in specific areas**
**Art. 128.-** The information on surface waters, underground waters, and rainfall in quality and quantity is necessary for its incorporation into the SIHI. For this purpose, ASA may carry out actions aimed at monitoring specific areas of the country, and thus obtain the necessary information to prepare the inventory and water balance at a regional and national level.

**Utilization of goods inherent to water resources**
**Art. 129.-** The following are goods inherent to water resources: stone materials, sands, sandstones, gravels, and organic soil found in the beds and channels of water that are part of the public water domain.
Said goods may be subject to use and utilization, subject to a favorable authorization issued by ASA, establishing as a guiding principle the preservation of the living conditions of water ecosystems and riparian zones, as well as the hydrological and hydraulic balance of water bodies and river channels.

**CHAPTER II**
**PREVENTION AND CONTROL OF WATER POLLUTION**

**Programs and Projects for the prevention and control of pollution**
**Art. 130.-** To guarantee the progressive recovery of quality in the goods that make up the public water domain, ASA will establish programs and projects aimed at the prevention, regulation, and control of pollution.

**Notice of accidental or fortuitous discharges**
**Art. 131.** When one or more accidental or fortuitous discharges of wastewater occur onto receiving media that are goods forming part of the public water domain, those responsible for or causing the discharge must activate the corresponding emergency protocol; as well as give immediate notice to ASA, specifying the volume and characteristics of the discharges so that the conducive measures may be promoted or adopted by ASA and other competent authorities.

ASA will deduce responsibilities from the person causing the discharge, who will be responsible for the damages caused to the water ecosystems and will be obliged to restore them. In case restoration is impossible or if the damage is irreversible, as determined by the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources, they will be condemned to the corresponding indemnities to the State and to the private parties affected for the damages and losses caused.

In the case that those responsible or causing the discharge do not give notice of the outfall, the response for damages caused or the pertinent indemnity will be double that calculated in normal cases with notice to the authorities.

**Strategic water reserves**
**Art. 132.-** The National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management will identify the water bodies that constitute strategic water reserves, establishing mechanisms and regulations that ensure environmental flows, sites, volumes, and their quality. For this purpose, ASA will carry out the necessary technical studies and investigations.

**TITLE EIGHT**
**REGIME OF INFRACTIONS AND SANCTIONING AUTHORITY**

**CHAPTER I**
**INFRACTIONS**

**Minor infractions**
**Art. 133.-** The following constitute minor infractions of this Law:
a) Not providing annually the information related to the exploration, use, and extraction of surface waters, underground waters, discharges, and of the other goods belonging to the public water domain, for the purpose of calculating the corresponding fee.
b) Not providing any other information that is required by ASA in order to comply with the functions or powers established in this Law.
c) Denying or preventing the entry of officials, employees, or personnel of ASA and the sanctioning authority, duly identified and in the exercise of the powers established in this Law.

These infractions will be sanctioned with a fine of one to one hundred monthly minimum wages of the current commerce and service sector at the time the fine is imposed.

ASA may also impose a fine of up to ten monthly minimum wages of the commerce and service sector for each day of delay on persons who deliberately or through negligence do not provide the required collaboration or who do so incompletely or inaccurately. The same fine may be imposed on anyone who does not comply with a precautionary measure ordered in accordance with this Law.

**Serious infractions**
**Art. 134.-** The following constitute serious infractions of this Law:
a) Carrying out, without the corresponding authorization, an agricultural activity or unauthorized occupation in the beds of rivers, lakes, and lagoons.
b) Preventing or obstructing the use of a water easement.
c) Non-compliance with the general guidelines for the actions that regulate the different subsectors involved in Water Resource Management.
d) Non-compliance with the directives and guidelines issued by ASA, regarding the theme of incentives for use, recycling, and reuse of wastewater and sludge management.

These infractions will be sanctioned with a fine of one hundred and one to one thousand monthly minimum wages of the current commerce and service sector at the time the fine is imposed.

**Very serious infractions**
**Art. 135.-** The following constitute very serious infractions of this Law:
a) Making use and utilization of an aquifer without authorization from ASA.
b) Making use of the strategic water reserves identified in the National Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management.
c) Discharging outfalls into receiving bodies without the corresponding permit or making discharges greater than those authorized.
d) Not complying with the payment of the fees.
e) Presenting false information or documentation to request authorizations and permits related to the integrated management of water resources.
f) Using volumes or flow rates of water in greater quantity than authorized, according to the uses.
g) Destining the authorizations for use or utilization of water to other uses, or on different properties than those mentioned in them.
h) Carrying out activities without the corresponding authorization for exploration, use, and extraction of surface waters, underground waters, discharges, and of the other goods that make up the public water domain.
i) Infringing the provisions for the protection zones of surface sources and declarations of aquifer protection zones established in this Law.
j) Carrying out transfers without the corresponding authorization.
k) Non-compliance with any of the elements contained in the authorizations issued by ASA.

These infractions will be sanctioned with a fine of one thousand and one to ten thousand monthly minimum wages of the current commerce and service sector at the time the fine is imposed.

**Proportionality and basis of the sanctions**
**Art. 136.-** In the imposition of the administrative sanctions regulated and established in this Law, the principle of proportionality of the sanction will be applied, taking into account the following circumstances:
a) The gravity of the damage caused to the water resource.
b) The actions that the infringer took to repair the damage caused.
c) The benefit obtained by the infringer.
d) The economic capacity of the infringer.
e) The repetition of the violation of this Law and its regulations.

**Valuation of damages to water resources**
**Art. 137.-** Whenever an administrative sanction is imposed, the infringer will be ordered to restore, restitute, or repair the damage caused to the water resources, being granted a period according to the gravity of the damage. In case of non-compliance, the value of the investment that must be destined for such objectives will be determined by experts appointed by ASA.

The certification of the valuation and of the resolution that orders the restoration, restitution, or repair of the damage will be enforceable against the infringer.

Without prejudice to the foregoing, as a consequence of the administrative sanction, the authorizations and permits issued in favor of the infringer will be canceled in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Payment of Fines**
**Art. 138.-** Administrative fines not paid after fifteen business days following a final resolution will be enforceable against the infringer; which will lead to the initiation of the process to suspend the existing permit.

**CHAPTER II**
**SANCTIONING TRIBUNAL**

**Sanctioning Tribunal**
**Art. 139.** For the fulfillment of its sanctioning power, ASA will have a Sanctioning Tribunal, hereinafter "the Tribunal". It will function permanently and will be composed of three members, one of whom will hold the position of president of the same and the other two members will hold the positions of first and second members. There will be an equal number of substitutes who will be appointed in the same way as the proprietary members.

**Powers of the Tribunal**
**Art. 140.-** The powers of the Sanctioning Tribunal are:
a) To conduct the Sanctioning Procedure and impose the corresponding sanctions for infractions of this Law.
b) To receive and attend to complaints regarding the violation, non-compliance, or lack of application of the provisions of this Law.
c) To assist the Public Prosecutor's Office in the criminal proceedings that are initiated for crimes against water resources, provided for in the Penal Code.
d) To hear and investigate acts, facts, or omissions that may constitute violations, non-compliance, or lack of application of the provisions of this regulation.
e) To conduct visits for the recognition of facts or omissions raised in the complaints it receives or the ex officio investigations it carries out, as well as to summon the involved persons to appear before the Tribunal to state what is convenient for their defense.
f) To impose, in a reasoned and motivated manner, the preventive measures that are appropriate, derived from the recognition of facts carried out by the Tribunal within the scope of its competence, and to issue resolutions that correspond to the procedures carried out as a result of attending to complaints and ex officio investigations.
g) To request from the competent authority the revocation and cancellation of authorizations, permits, and registrations when they are granted against the provisions of this Law.
h) To formulate and validate technical and expert opinions on damages to water resources and, where appropriate, on their restoration or compensation or on the adverse effects generated by violations, non-compliance, or lack of application of the provisions of this Law.
i) Any other matters that are attributed to it by Law.

**Requirements to be members of the Sanctioning Tribunal**
**Art. 141.-** To be members of the Sanctioning Tribunal, the following requirements must be met:
a) Be Salvadoran.
b) Be over 30 years of age.
c) Have no criminal record.
d) Be of recognized honorability and probity.
e) Have knowledge and work experience of at least five years, in relation to the application of environmental legislation and especially of Sanctioning Procedures.
f) Be free of claims of all kinds, in case of having been a contractor or consultant for acquisitions or contracts executed with public funds.
g) Be in good standing with the Treasury.

**Appointment**
**Art. 142.-** The president and the other members of the Tribunal will be appointed by the President of the Republic, and must be lawyers of the Republic who have obtained authorization to practice the profession ten years before their appointment, with a minimum of five years of work experience and knowledge in environmental topics; and especially of Sanctioning Procedures.

The members of the tribunal must perform their duties full-time.

**Term of office**
**Art. 143.-** The members of the tribunal will last four years in the exercise of their functions, and may be appointed for one more term. However, when the term for which they were appointed has concluded, and in case there is no new appointment, they will continue in the performance of their functions.

**Oath and taking of office**
**Art. 144.-** The members of the Tribunal will take an oath before the President of the Republic, prior to taking possession of their offices, and will solemnly declare that they will exercise their functions with all impartiality and respect for the legal order.

**Prohibitions**
**Art. 145.-** Persons who are in any of the following situations may not be members of the Sanctioning Tribunal:
a) Having outstanding accounts with the State.
b) Being members of the Board of Directors or a governing body of a political party or business associations.
c) Simultaneously holding public office or being a remunerated public employee, except of a teaching, cultural nature, and related to professional social assistance services.
d) Being a member of the Boards of Directors or sole administrators of commercial companies, or of governing bodies of service-providing companies related to the subject matter of this Law.
e) Not having been sanctioned in accordance with the Government Ethics Law in case of having been public servants, permanent or temporary; or having administered public goods or funds.

**Impediments**
**Art. 146.-** The members of the Tribunal may not participate in the decision of cases in which they have previously intervened as agents, counselors, consultants, or lawyers of any of the interested parties; when they have previously expressed their opinion on the matter or when there is a reason for impediment, recusal, or excuse established in the Law of Administrative Procedures.

**Excuses and Recusals**
**Art. 147.-** The members of the Tribunal must excuse themselves as soon as they become aware of any of the reasons indicated in the preceding articles that prevent them from hearing the matter and will immediately inform ASA, which will resolve what is appropriate. The decision does not admit any appeal.

The same procedure will be followed when the reason is invoked by one of the parties to recuse the members of the Tribunal.

When the excuse or recusal is admitted, the substitute will be called to hear the specific case, who will occupy the position of second member in the integration of the Tribunal. If it is the president of the Tribunal, they will be substituted by the first member.

**Causes for cessation of the members of the sanctioning tribunal**
**Art. 148.-** The members of the sanctioning tribunal will cease their functions when they incur in any of the causes for cessation of the office of the president of ASA.

**Secretary**
**Art. 149.-** The Tribunal will act with a secretary, who will authorize the resolutions adopted by it, receive documents, have under their responsibility the files and archives; and will be technically and administratively responsible for the performance of the Tribunal. In addition, the Tribunal will have a notifying secretary who will carry out the acts of communication, make the summonses and notifications that are ordered, in accordance with the provisions of the Law of Contentious-Administrative Jurisdiction and the Law of Administrative Procedures.

**Technical team**
**Art. 150.-** The Sanctioning Tribunal will have its own multidisciplinary technical team, composed of specialists in water resources, who will be in charge of preparing and issuing the technical reports or opinions that will serve as the basis for the resolutions that the Sanctioning Tribunal will issue.

**Voting method**
**Art. 151.-** Resolutions will be adopted by a simple majority; the member who opposes the decision will reason their vote.

**CHAPTER III**
**PREVENTIVE MEASURES**

**Preventive measures**
**Art. 152.-** The Tribunal may adopt at any time, through a reasoned agreement, the provisional measures that are necessary to ensure the effectiveness of the resolution that may be issued, as well as to prevent the maintenance of the effects of the infraction and in the presence or imminence of damage to human health, damage to water resources, or related ecosystems.

The preventive measures must be adjusted to the intensity, proportionality, and needs of the objectives sought to be guaranteed in each specific case.

**Application of preventive measures**
**Art. 153.-** The Tribunal may order, ex officio or at the request of the Attorney General's Office or any person, whether natural or legal, the preventive measures referred to in the preceding article in the presence or imminence of damage to human health, water resources, or related ecosystems, giving a period of fifteen business days for the affected party to appear and state their defense.

These measures will last as long as the person responsible for the threat of deterioration or the deterioration does not eliminate its causes and will be confined to the area, process, or product that directly threatens to deteriorate or deteriorates the water resources and related ecosystems.

The Tribunal must resolve on the compliance, continuation, modification, termination, or revocation of the preventive measures it has imposed within a term of ten business days counted from the expiration of the period granted to the affected party to state their defense or the one granted for the compliance with the measure.

**Non-compliance with preventive measures**
**Art. 154.-** If the Tribunal orders preventive measures based on the provisions of this Law, and they have been breached, it will certify the respective file to the Attorney General's Office and the Environmental Judge, so that the respective process may be initiated.

**CHAPTER IV**
**PROCEDURE FOR ADMINISTRATIVE SANCTIONS**

**Initiation of the Procedure**
**Art. 155.-** The procedure to impose sanctions for infractions of the legal provisions of the regulations object of this Law may be initiated ex officio or by complaint to ASA.

**Ex Officio Inspections**
**Art. 156.-** When the municipalities, the Attorney General's Office, the National Civil Police, or the Office of the Procurator for the Defense of Human Rights become aware by any means of an infraction of this Law and its Regulations, they will proceed within a maximum period of seventy-two hours to inspect the place where the infraction was committed and will draw up the corresponding record, which they will send to ASA within a period not exceeding five business days.

If the Tribunal has direct knowledge, it will proceed within seventy-two hours to carry out the inspection of the place and to gather the evidence.

The inspection record will constitute proof of the fact and, where appropriate, of the infraction committed, which will be assessed in the sanctioning procedure.

**Flagrante Delicto**
**Art. 157.** When agents of the National Civil Police or the Municipal Agents Corps of the respective jurisdiction surprise a person or persons in flagrante delicto at the moment of committing a fault or infraction against this Law and its Regulations, they will immediately proceed to ask for their identification and the place where they live or reside so that they can be summoned by the Tribunal once the corresponding sanctioning procedure has been initiated.

In all cases, if the act also constitutes a crime, fault, or municipal contravention, the procedure will be as provided by law.

**CHAPTER V**
**PROCESSING OF THE COMPLAINT PROCEDURE**

**Written or verbal complaint**
**Art. 158.-** The administrative sanctioning procedure may be initiated by written, verbal, or any electronic or digital means of complaint before ASA. The complaint will state the name of the person or persons allegedly accused and other data that identify them, if known. The complaint will state, as a minimum, the name of the complainant, the electronic means for notification, a relation of the facts, the alleged infringed norm, and the alleged infringer if they can be identified.

**Summons, Service, and Notification**
**Art. 159.** Once the complaint is admitted before the Tribunal, it will order the summons of the alleged infringer so that they may appear within a term of ten business days following to state their defense.

Every summons or service to answer the claim will be understood to be with the defendant or with their legal representative, if they have one.

Notifications and summonses must be made with the formalities indicated by the Law of Administrative Procedures.

**Declaration of default**
**Art. 160.-** If, having been legally notified, the alleged infringer does not appear within the legal term to state their defense, they will be declared in default ex officio and the procedure will continue.

The declaration of default may be interrupted at any stage of the administrative sanctioning process; the alleged infringer must resume it at the procedural stage in which it is found.

**Opening for evidence**
**Art. 161.-** Once the alleged infringer and the other parties, if any, have been summoned, the Tribunal will convene an oral hearing establishing the date, time, and place where all the evidence will be heard.

Within the hearing, if necessary, the Tribunal may previously carry out inspections, valuations, and expert examinations, accompanied by its multidisciplinary technical team for this purpose, who will give their results at the hearing.

The parties and the Tribunal may ask the relevant questions to the witnesses presented and to the alleged infringer, if the latter agrees to be interrogated.

**Administrative assistance**
**Art. 162.-** The Tribunal may request the support of the National Civil Police, the Attorney General's Office, the Office of the Procurator for the Defense of Human Rights, or the Municipalities in their respective jurisdictions, to assist the personnel of ASA and the multidisciplinary technical team of the Sanctioning Tribunal in the fulfillment of their functions.

**Records**
**Art. 163.-** The technical reports issued by ASA or the multidisciplinary technical team of the Sanctioning Tribunal will have probative value with respect to the investigated facts, unless there is evidence to the contrary, which will be weighed and evaluated by the Tribunal, according to the established rules.

**Resolutions**
**Art. 164.-** The final resolution or the one that resolves an incident will be signed by the Tribunal, and the certification of the final resolution that imposes a sanction or that contains an order to give will be enforceable; in the case of judgments that contain an order to do or not to do to repair the situation altered by the infraction, they will be forcibly executed.
The tribunal must issue the final resolution within a period not exceeding sixty business days counted from the date of initiation of the procedure.

The infringer must comply with the resolution within ten business days following the day on which they were notified.

If the resolution is not complied with voluntarily, the Tribunal will request the Attorney General of the Republic to make the sanction effective in accordance with common procedures.

The resolutions pronounced by the Tribunal will admit the reconsideration appeal, which it will hear and resolve based on the record within a period of ten business days. The period for filing it will be ten business days counted from the notification.

The reconsideration appeal will be optional for the purposes of the contentious-administrative action.

**TITLE NINE**
**TRANSITIONAL, REPEALING, AND FINAL PROVISIONS**

**CHAPTER I**
**TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS**

**Absence of regulations**
**Art. 165.-** As long as the General Regulations and the Special Regulations that develop the precepts of this Law are not approved, ASA is empowered to issue resolutions, provisions, or instruments that serve to comply with the object of this Law.

**Responsibility to inform**
**Art. 166.-** The entities responsible for Drinking Water and Sanitation must provide ASA with the information related to the registers of users, the volumes of exploitation of each system, in order to be able to develop the water balances and the information related to discharges and sanitation.

**Internal Regulation of Organization and Functioning**
**Art. 167.-** In the following one hundred and eighty days counted from the entry into force of this Law, ASA will formulate and approve its internal regulations of organization and functioning.

**Permits in Protected Natural Areas**
**Art. 168.-** Those who have authorized and are using the water resource within a Protected Natural Area in accordance with the corresponding authorization, must report said situation to ASA within a period not exceeding one year from the entry into force of this Law.
ASA, in accordance with the information received, will issue the authorizations for use and utilization, as well as permits for discharges as appropriate.

**Permits for wells and drilling companies**
**Art. 169.-** Natural and legal persons, public and private, who use drilled wells and the drilling companies, as well as the other users of national waters, who have done so before the entry into force of this Law, must legalize their situation before ASA, within a period not exceeding one year from the entry into force of this Law.

**Artisanal wells for domestic use**
**Art. 170.-** The owners of properties that have within them artisanal wells for domestic or common use will have the obligation to inform and register them with ASA, within the year following the entry into force of this Law.
With regard to artisanal wells that are to be excavated and their water resource utilized and that are located in an urban area, the interested natural or legal persons must request the corresponding exploration permit.

**Authorizations for Use and Utilization**
**Art. 171.-** Natural and legal persons, public and private, who wish to make use and utilization of national waters, must process the authorizations according to the annual volume necessary to comply with their activity, starting one year after the entry into force of this Law; likewise, users who enjoy permits for use and utilization of water will have to regularize their situation within the aforementioned period.

**Discharges to receiving media**
**Art. 172.-** Natural and legal persons, public and private, who make discharges into receiving media that are part of the goods established in the public water domain in accordance with this Law, must process and legalize their situation within a period not exceeding one year counted from the entry into force of this Law.

**Information on goods of the public water domain**
**Art. 173.-** The owners and possessors of properties in which goods belonging to the public water domain are located must inform ASA for their corresponding registration, within the year following the entry into force of this Law.

**Transfer of goods to ASA**
**Art. 174.-** ASA may identify those movable and immovable goods that are assigned to other government entities, in order to request the Council of Ministers that they be awarded to it for the purpose of guaranteeing its normal functioning, in accordance with the corresponding legal regulations.
Consequently, they are exempted from the payment of registration fees generated by the registrations of the transfer of all those immovable goods that are given in favor of ASA; they are also exempted from the payment of the tax on the transfer of real estate, as well as the presentation of the corresponding tax and municipal clearances.
Upon certification of the agreement of the Council of Ministers, the corresponding instruments will be granted through the head of the institution that owns the immovable property.

**CHAPTER II**
**REPEALS**

**Repeals**
**Art. 175.-** The provisions of the following legal bodies are expressly repealed:
a) The second paragraph of article 576 and articles 577, 836, 862 of the Civil Code, promulgated on April 10, 1860; published in the Official Gazette Number 85, of the 14th of the same month and year.
b) Articles 182 to 193 of Title VII of the Agrarian Law, dated August 26, 1941; published in the Official Gazette Number 66, Volume No. 132, of March 21, 1942.
c) From the Law of Irrigation and Drainage, issued by Legislative Decree number 153, dated November 11, 1970, published in the Official Gazette number 213, Volume No. 229, of the 23rd of that same month and year, as follows: Arts. 4 and 5 and Art. 6, letter a), regarding the planning of water resources; Chapter IV: Of Underground Waters, which comprises articles 20 to 28, inclusive; and, - Arts. 100 and 101.
d) The Regulation on water quality, discharge control, and protection zones. Decree number 50 dated October 16, 1987, published in the Official Gazette Number 191, Volume 297, of October 16, 1987.
e) The Law on Integrated Management of Water Resources, issued by Decree Law number 886 of the Revolutionary Government Junta, of December 2, 1981, published in the Official Gazette number 221, Volume 273, of the same date.
f) Arts. 36 and 37 of the Law of Highways and Local Roads, issued by Legislative Decree number 463, dated September 9, 1969; published in the Official Gazette number 196, Volume 225 of October 22, 1969.

**CHAPTER III**
**FINAL PROVISIONS**

**Special Law**
**Art. 176.-** This Regulation is considered a Special Law of an environmental nature. Consequently, it will prevail over any other that contradicts it.

**Exception**
**Art. 177.-** For the purposes of this Law, the provisions of Art. 72 of the Law of the National Administration of Aqueducts and Sewers (ANDA) will not be applicable.

**Suppletory application**
**Art. 178.-** For everything not provided for in this law, the provisions of the Law of Administrative Procedures will be applied in a suppletory manner.

**Entry into force**
**Art. 179.-** This Law will enter into force six months after its publication in the Official Gazette.
