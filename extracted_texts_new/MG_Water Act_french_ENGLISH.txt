January 27, 1999 OFFICIAL GAZETTE OF THE REPUBLIC OF MADAGASCAR 135

**LAW NO. 98-029**  
**Establishing the Water Code**

The National Assembly adopted the following at its session of December 19, 1998,

THE PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC,

Having regard to the Constitution,  
Having regard to the decision of the High Constitutional Court No. 04-HCC/D3 of January 13, 1999,

Promulgates the law with the following content:

**Article 1:** Water is part of the common heritage of the Nation. Each local authority is its guarantor within the scope of its powers.

The purpose of this Code is:  
*   The public domain status of water;  
*   The management, conservation, and development of water resources;  
*   The organization of the public service for drinking water and collective sanitation for domestic wastewater;  
*   Water policing;  
*   The financing of the water and sanitation sector;  
*   The organization of the water and sanitation sector.

**TITLE I**  
**THE PUBLIC WATER DOMAIN**

**Art. 2:** Water is a public good falling within the public domain. It may not be subject to private appropriation except under the conditions established by the provisions of civil law dealing with the matter, as well as the easements attached thereto in force in the territory of Madagascar.

**SECTION 1**  
**GENERAL PROVISIONS AND SCOPE OF APPLICATION**

**Art. 3:** This Code shall apply to all waters within the public domain, including surface waters and groundwaters.

**Art. 4:** The definition and nomenclature of waters belonging to the natural, artificial, or legal public domain shall respectively comply with the provisions of articles 4(a), 4(b), 4(c), and 5 of Ordinance No. 60-099 of September 2, 1960, regulating the public domain, as amended by Ordinance No. 62-035 of September 19, 1962.

**Art. 5:** Installations, structures, works, and activities carried out by any natural or legal person, public or private, that involve withdrawals from surface or groundwaters, whether returned or not, and that may have an impact on the level, quality, and flow of said waters, must be placed under the regular supervision of the administration. The same shall apply to chronic or occasional discharges, even if non-polluting.

**SECTION II**  
**SURFACE WATERS AND GROUNDWATERS**

**Art. 6:** Surface waters consist of all rainwater and running water on the ground surface, water bodies or canals, rivers and streams, navigation canals and canalized rivers, certain irrigation canals, saltwater ponds connected to the sea, lakes, ponds and the like, marshes, and wetlands.

Surface waters are part of the public domain.

**Art. 7:** Decrees shall determine the conditions for:  
*   The classification of a watercourse, a section of a watercourse, or a lake within the public domain;  
*   The concession of navigable or floatable watercourses, whether natural or artificial;  
*   The removal from the nomenclature of navigable or floatable waterways that are maintained in the public domain, with or without a concession.

**Art. 8:** Groundwaters consist of groundwater tables and sources.  
Groundwaters are part of the public domain.  
Sources, which are natural emergences of groundwater tables, shall continue to be part of the public domain.

**TITLE II**  
**MANAGEMENT OF WATER RESOURCES**

**Art. 9:** The purpose of the provisions of this title is to implement integrated water management policies, taking into account the relationships between quantitative and qualitative aspects, and between surface waters and groundwaters.

**CHAPTER I**  
**WATER PROTECTION**

**SECTION 1**  
**QUANTITATIVE PROTECTION**

**Subsection 1**  
**SURFACE WATER WITHDRAWALS**

**Art. 10:** No work may be carried out on the surface waters defined in Article 6 of this Code, whether or not it modifies their regime, and no diversion of waters from the public domain, in any manner and for any purpose whatsoever, by removing them temporarily or permanently from their course, may be made without authorization.

The conditions for obtaining such authorizations shall be established by decree upon the proposal of the National Water and Sanitation Authority (ANDEA) referred to in Title V of this Code.

However, authorization for surface water withdrawals not exceeding a volume threshold to be established by decree, for personal use, shall not be required.

**Subsection II**  
**GROUNDWATER WITHDRAWALS**

**ARTICLE 11:** Groundwater withdrawals may not be made without authorization, except for personal uses not exceeding a volume threshold to be established by decree and not posing a risk of pollution to the resource.

The conditions for obtaining such authorizations shall be established by decree upon the proposal of the National Water and Sanitation Authority.

**SECTION II**  
**QUALITATIVE PROTECTION**

**Subsection I**  
**WATER POLLUTION**

**ARTICLE 12:** Any natural or legal person, public or private, engaged in an activity that is a source of pollution or that may present dangers to water resources and environmental hygiene must consider all appropriate measures to stop or prevent the observed or presumed danger.

In the event of non-compliance with the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the polluter shall be required to pay, in accordance with the polluter pays principle, a sum, the amount of which shall be determined by regulation, in proportion to the degree of pollution caused.

**ARTICLE 13:** For the application of this Code, "pollution" shall mean any discharge, flow, release, or direct or indirect deposit of materials of any kind and, more generally, any act likely to cause or increase the degradation of waters by modifying their physical, chemical, biological, bacteriological, or radioactive characteristics, whether it be surface waters or groundwaters.

**Subsection II**  
**WASTE**

**ARTICLE 14:** Waste shall be considered any residue from a production, transformation, or utilization process; any substance, material, product, or more generally any movable property that is abandoned or that its holder intends to abandon.

For the application of this Code, primary consideration shall be given to waste which, by its conditions of production or possession, is likely to pollute water and, in general, to harm human health and degrade the environment.

In accordance with environmental requirements as provided for by Law No. 90-003 of December 21, 1990, establishing the Environmental Charter, and in order to reduce waste production at the source, the administration referred to in Title V of this Code must organize supervision of the activities of establishments that may cause nuisances or risks arising from waste produced or treated.

**ARTICLE 15:** Any natural or legal person who produces or holds waste of a nature to produce harmful effects on the soil, flora, and fauna, to pollute the air or waters, and, in general, to harm human health and degrade the environment, is required to ensure its disposal or treatment.

**ARTICLE 16:** The disposal of household waste shall be carried out under the responsibility of the municipalities, which may finance all or part of the service costs in accordance with the regulations in force.

Without prejudice to the provisions of other subsequent texts, the disposal of industrial, mining, and other waste is a matter for private initiative. Industrialists and other producers of waste of any kind must deliver it to channels that guarantee the protection of the environment and shall bear the costs of transport, disposal, or treatment.

**ARTICLE 17:** Within the disposal channels, companies that produce, import, or dispose of waste are required to provide the administration with all information concerning the origin, nature, characteristics, quantities, destination, and disposal methods of the waste they produce, deliver to a third party, or take charge of.

**ARTICLE 18:** Decrees shall determine the conditions under which discharges, flows, releases, and direct or indirect deposits of water or materials, and more generally any act likely to alter the quality of surface or groundwater, may be regulated or prohibited.

**Subsection III**  
**SANITATION**

**ARTICLE 19:** For the purposes of this Code, sanitation shall mean any measure intended to eliminate the causes of insalubrity in order to satisfy the protection of water resources, the convenience of the neighborhood, the health and safety of populations, public health, agriculture, the protection of nature and the environment, and the conservation of sites and monuments.

The sanitation of urban areas, as referred to in these provisions, aims to ensure the drainage of rainwater and wastewater and their discharge into natural outfalls in a manner compatible with public health requirements.

Collective sanitation for domestic wastewater concerns the drainage and treatment of wastewater from consumers after it has been distributed by drinking water supply systems.

The Regulatory Body for the public drinking water supply service may be tasked by decrees with the regulation of collective sanitation for domestic wastewater.

**ARTICLE 20:** It is the responsibility of any local authority or any establishment or enterprise referred to in Article 17 above to ensure the drainage of waters of all kinds that they receive, under conditions that respect the objectives set for maintaining and improving the quality of the receiving environments, in application of, in particular, the principles stated in this chapter.

In any event, wastewater of domestic origin, as well as rainwater, must be subject to collective sanitation under the conditions established by the implementing texts of this Code.

Individual sanitation may be authorized if the implementation of collective facilities involves excessive constraints from an economic or technical point of view or proves to be detrimental to the quality of the receiving surface waters.

However, the establishment of networks definitively reserved for the drainage of effluents from individual sanitation devices interposed between the connections of private buildings and public drainage works is prohibited.

**ARTICLE 21:** Any discharge of wastewater, other than domestic, into public sewers must be previously authorized by the local authority to which the works that will be used by this wastewater before it reaches the natural environment belong.

The authorization shall specify, according to the nature of the network to be used or the treatments implemented, the characteristics that this wastewater must have to be accepted.

In any event, the provisions of the texts in force regarding urban planning and housing concerning the discharge of used water and materials must be respected.

**ARTICLE 22:** Industrial wastewater of any kind to be treated must not be mixed with rainwater and industrial wastewater that can be discharged into the natural environment without treatment.

However, this mixing may be carried out if the resulting dilution does not cause any duly noted treatment difficulties by an approved control laboratory.

The discharge of industrial wastewater into the public sanitation network, if authorized, may be subject to, among other things, appropriate pre-treatment.

**CHAPTER II**  
**CONSERVATION OF WATER RESOURCES AND ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION**

**ARTICLE 23:** The implementation of developments, structures, or works, carried out by public or private persons, shall be preceded by a public inquiry and an environmental impact assessment subject to the provisions of this Code as well as those provided for in this regard by Law No. 90-003 of December 21, 1990, establishing the Environmental Charter, when, due to their nature, their consistency, or the character of the areas concerned, these operations are likely to affect the environment and could cause disturbances to the aquatic ecosystem.

**ARTICLE 24:** For the protection of rivers, lakes, ponds, all water bodies and watercourses, and groundwaters, it is prohibited to throw or dispose of any unhealthy materials or objects whatsoever in watersheds that would be likely to cause a quantitative and qualitative degradation of the characteristics of the water resource.

A watershed constitutes any surface area delimited topographically and geologically, drained by one or more watercourses. The watershed is a hydrological and hydrogeological unit that has been described and used as a physio-biological, socio-economic, and political unit for the planning and management of natural resources.

**ARTICLE 25:** In accordance with the provisions of the forestry policy, the eminently protective role of a forest cover, or at least that of a dense herbaceous cover on watersheds, as well as protection against erosion, silting, and sedimentation of hydroelectric infrastructure and downstream irrigated areas, are of public interest and shall be the subject of specific consultation measures, in order to maintain water quality standards, regulate hydrological regimes, and prevent severe floods.

**ARTICLE 26:** The protection of natural forests or reforested areas is subject to the provisions of Law No. 97-017 of July 16, 1997, revising forestry legislation, particularly in its Title II and those relating to the regulation of land clearing and vegetation fires.

**ARTICLE 27:** Regulatory texts shall establish specific measures concerning forests located in the catchment basins of torrents, and those that protect against harmful climatological influences, avalanches, landslides, and considerable variations in water regimes.

The protection measures referred to in the paragraph above are applicable to forests bordering watercourses and to any forested area important for protecting man from the forces of nature.

**CHAPTER III**  
**DEVELOPMENT OF WATER RESOURCES**

**ARTICLE 28:** The priorities for access to water resources, both surface and groundwater, shall be defined by decrees, upon the proposal of the National Water and Sanitation Authority, according to the specific conditions of the water resource in the regions concerned.

In the event of a limitation of available water resources, priority shall be given to the supply of drinking water, taking into account the consumption standards adopted in application of this Code.

**SECTION I**  
**IRRIGATION WATER**

**ARTICLE 29:** Water for land irrigation may come from surface waters or groundwaters.

All pumping installations intended for land irrigation shall respect the specific flow rate standards for crops, established by decree.

The quantities of water withdrawn must not harm other users of the available resource.

**ARTICLE 30:** Hydro-agricultural networks financed by the State are and shall remain governed by all legislative and regulatory texts relating to the management, maintenance, and policing of networks, in particular by the provisions of Law No. 90-016 of July 20, 1990.

**ARTICLE 31:** Any irrigation project initiated by a private legal or natural person requires the opinion of the National Water and Sanitation Authority concerning the use of water resources, both surface and groundwater.

In all cases, any project referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be subject to an impact study in accordance with the provisions of Article 23 of this Code and of Law No. 90-003 of December 21, 1990, establishing the Environmental Charter.

**SECTION II**  
**INDUSTRIAL WATER**

**ARTICLE 32:** The provisions of this section shall apply to any individual activity using water as the main source of energy, transformation, or income.

The establishment of an industry may be subject to the installation of an autonomous water supply system to avoid supply problems and not to disadvantage the population in terms of drinking water distribution.

However, where distribution and supply facilities and networks are already in place, the new establishment must harmonize its water withdrawal and supply policy with the existing one.

In any event, any industrial facility is subject to prior studies of the impact of its discharges on the environment, in accordance with Law No. 90-003 of December 21, 1990, referred to in Article 31 above.

**ARTICLE 33:** The operator of a classified installation must take all necessary measures, during the design and operation of the installation, to limit water consumption on the one hand, and to preserve the environment on the other, at the different stages of production, in accordance with the provisions of this Title II.

Regulatory texts shall specify, as necessary, the modalities for the application of this article.

**SECTION III**  
**HYDROELECTRIC USE OF WATER**

**ARTICLE 34:** Any natural or legal person under private law may be associated with the conduct of operations relating to the operation of enterprises and the hydroelectric production of water.

**ARTICLE 35:** No one may dispose of the energy of lakes and watercourses, regardless of their classification, without a concession or an authorization from the State, the conditions and modalities of granting of which are to be determined by regulation.

**ARTICLE 36:** Regulatory provisions shall define the technical conditions for the development of hydroelectric power plants, which shall first be subject to an impact study in accordance with Law No. 90-003 of December 21, 1990, establishing the Environmental Charter.

In case of necessity on certain watercourses or sections of watercourses, a list of which shall be established by decree, no authorization or concession shall be given for new hydroelectric enterprises. For existing enterprises, regularly established on the date of promulgation of this Code, a concession or an authorization may be granted provided that the height of the dam is not modified.

The procedure for granting authorizations by the prefect shall include a public inquiry and the publication of an environmental impact assessment report depending on the size of the structure.

The authorization shall require its holder to comply with water regulations specifying, in particular, the withdrawn and reserved flow rates.

**CHAPTER IV**  
**DRINKING WATER SUPPLY AND COLLECTIVE SANITATION FOR DOMESTIC WASTEWATER**

**Section I**  
**PUBLIC SERVICE FOR DRINKING WATER AND COLLECTIVE SANITATION FOR DOMESTIC WASTEWATER**

**ARTICLE 37:** The public service for drinking water supply and collective sanitation for domestic wastewater, referred to in this chapter as the "public water and sanitation service," is a service of drinking water supply and collective sanitation for domestic wastewater provided to the public, that is, to any user, whether a natural or legal person under public or private law, with public service obligations defined in specifications.

The universal service for drinking water supply is the attribute of the public service based on the obligation to provide all users with a minimum quantity and a minimum service of drinking water.

The principles and organization of this universal drinking water supply service shall be established by decree.

A drinking water supply and collective sanitation system for domestic wastewater, or "system," is the set of installations and infrastructure intended to provide drinking water and/or collective sanitation services for domestic wastewater over a given geographical area:  
*   intake, withdrawal, and treatment facilities considered as water production;  
*   transport facilities;  
*   distribution and connection infrastructure for drinking water;  
*   transport infrastructure such as sewers and treatment and purification infrastructure for sanitation.

**ARTICLE 38:** All water supplied for human consumption must be potable.  
Potable water is defined as water intended for human consumption which, through treatment or naturally, meets organoleptic, physico-chemical, bacteriological, and biological standards established by decree.

**ARTICLE 39:** The public supply of drinking water and access to collective sanitation for domestic wastewater are a municipal public service.

Self-production does not constitute a public service. However, in the absence or insufficiency of the public drinking water supply service in the area concerned, a self-producer may supply drinking water to the public, provided that express authorization is obtained within the framework of an agreement signed with the concerned contracting authority.

Self-production is defined as an activity that allows a natural or legal person to carry out the implementation and/or direct management and maintenance of a Drinking Water Supply System for the satisfaction of its own needs.

A decree shall regulate the conditions for carrying out self-production.

**ARTICLE 40:** Drinking water supply and collective sanitation systems for domestic wastewater are part of the public domain of the municipalities, with the exception of systems intended for self-production.

**Section II**  
**CONTRACTING AUTHORITY FOR THE PUBLIC WATER AND SANITATION SERVICE**

**ARTICLE 41:** The contracting authority is the public authority responsible to the users for the public water and sanitation service over a given geographical area.

Rural and urban municipalities are the contracting authorities for the drinking water supply and collective sanitation systems for domestic wastewater located within their respective territories. They shall exercise these powers through the municipal council.

However, as long as the municipalities do not meet the capacity criteria defined by decree for the exercise of all or part of the responsibilities incumbent upon contracting authorities, these shall be exercised by the Minister in charge of Drinking Water until they are empowered. During this period, the Minister in charge of Drinking Water shall act as the delegated contracting authority for the municipalities. At the end of this period, contracts concluded between the Minister in charge of Drinking Water and third parties shall be transferred by right to the contracting authorities.

By way of derogation from paragraph 2 of this article, communities and/or "Fokontany," may, at their request, exercise the delegated contracting authority for small rural drinking water supply systems located within their territory with the agreement of the Regulatory Body referred to in Section IV of this chapter and the parent municipality.

**ARTICLE 42:** Notwithstanding the provisions of Article 39 above, and in accordance with the conditions of the preceding Article 41, the drinking water supply and collective sanitation systems for domestic wastewater shall remain in the public domain of the State.

Upon the promulgation of this Code, the following functions shall be exercised by the municipalities:  
*   approval of investments for the drinking water supply systems in their territory.  
*   consultation on the development programs for the public drinking water and sanitation service concerning them.

The systems shall be transferred by right to the public domain of the municipalities according to modalities to be established by decree.

**ARTICLE 43:** When an integrated water supply and/or sanitation system extends over the territory of several municipalities, or when it appears necessary to extend the operating perimeter of the system for technical, economic, or public service quality reasons, the municipalities are free to associate in order to unify the contracting authority.

In the absence of initiative from the municipalities, the Regulatory Body may propose the merger of the contracting authority on the basis of a report justifying this action, after consulting the municipalities or communities concerned.

A decree shall establish the conditions and methods of organization for these associations of municipalities.

**ARTICLE 44:** The functions of the contracting authority shall be established by decree.

**Section III**  
**OPERATION OF DRINKING WATER SUPPLY AND COLLECTIVE SANITATION SYSTEMS FOR DOMESTIC WASTEWATER**

**ARTICLE 45:** The system operator is the operator, a natural person or a legal person under public or private law, Malagasy or foreign, to whom a contracting authority entrusts the implementation and/or direct management and maintenance of a system.

In the case of private law legal persons acting as system operators, they must be incorporated as a company under Malagasy law, in accordance with the legislative and regulatory provisions in force.

**ARTICLE 46:** The operation of the systems may be delegated to operators, through a management contract, a lease contract (affermage), or a concession contract, or may be carried out, on an exceptional basis, by the contracting authorities under direct management.

These contracts are subject to the prior approval of the Regulatory Body.

A decree shall establish the conditions for recourse to the different modes of delegated management and shall organize the regimes for management, lease, and concession contracts; it shall define the conditions and procedures for negotiation and calls for competition for these three types of contracts.

**Section IV**  
**THE REGULATORY BODY FOR THE PUBLIC WATER AND SANITATION SERVICE**

**ARTICLE 47:** A Body, a public establishment of an administrative nature, is hereby established, responsible for the regulation of the public drinking water and sanitation service, whose powers, composition, operating modalities, and financing method are established by this Code and the decrees issued for its application.

The Regulatory Body is a technical, advisory, and executive body specializing in the sector of drinking water supply and collective sanitation for domestic wastewater, endowed with legal personality and financial autonomy.

As necessary, it may consult any concerned administrations and organizations in the execution of its mission.

**ARTICLE 48:** The Regulatory Body is responsible, in particular, for:  
*   monitoring compliance with standards for service quality;  
*   determining and putting into effect, in accordance with the tariff provisions of this Code, water prices and sanitation fees, and monitoring their correct application;  
*   proposing specific standards adapted to each system, and submitting them for decision by the administration.  
*   designing, developing, and updating an information system on drinking water supply and collective sanitation facilities for domestic wastewater.

**ARTICLE 49:** The Regulatory Body shall be managed and administered by a board assisted by an executive office.

**ARTICLE 50:** The board of the Regulatory Body shall be composed of 7 members proposed according to criteria of specific expertise. They shall be appointed by decree issued by the Council of Ministers. Thus:  
*   a person competent in the engineering of drinking water intake, treatment, and distribution systems shall be proposed by the Minister in charge of Drinking Water;  
*   a person competent in sanitary engineering shall be proposed by the Minister in charge of Health;  
*   a person competent in the legal and institutional field shall be proposed by the Minister in charge of Justice;  
*   a person competent in economics and finance shall be proposed by the Minister in charge of Economy and Finance;  
*   a representative of the users of the public water and sanitation service shall be proposed by the Prime Minister;  
*   a representative of the municipal contracting authorities shall be proposed by the Minister in charge of Decentralized Local Authorities;  
*   a representative of the system operators shall be proposed by the Minister in charge of Drinking Water.

The term of office for a board member is five years, renewable. The appointments shall be made as follows:  
*   the first 4 shall be appointed upon publication of this Code;  
*   the other 3 shall be appointed 1 year later.

Except in the case of loss of their civic rights, board members are irremovable during their term of office.

The functions of Board members are incompatible with any function as a member of the Government or member of Parliament and any elected office. Accession to such functions shall automatically entail the termination of the mandate as a board member.

**ARTICLE 51:** The Board of the Regulatory Body shall elect from among its members a president for the duration of his/her term.

**ARTICLE 52:** The executive office shall be headed by an executive secretary. The executive secretary shall be appointed for a five-year term by the Board, upon the proposal of the president of the Regulatory Body.

**ARTICLE 53:** The organization, powers, operating mode, and financing method of the Regulatory Body shall be specified by a decree.

**Section V**  
**FINANCING AND TARIFF PRINCIPLES FOR THE PUBLIC WATER AND SANITATION SERVICE**

**ARTICLE 54:** The tariff and cost recovery policy for drinking water and sanitation services must respect the following principles:  
*   Access to the public water service, whether at collective water points or individual connections, is subject to payment;  
*   for each water and sanitation system, the applicable tariffs must allow for the financial equilibrium of the system operators and tend towards full cost recovery;  
*   investment and operating costs, on the one hand, and the ability of users to pay, on the other, are taken into account in the principles for water pricing and the setting of sanitation fees;  
*   the proceeds collected by the contracting authorities and operators for drinking water and sanitation services are revenues earmarked solely for these services;  
*   Tariff systems must include provisions allowing access to the universal drinking water service for domestic consumers with the lowest incomes.

**ARTICLE 55:** Due to the social component of the public water and sanitation service, the total taxes and surcharges levied by local authorities on the invoicing of these services may not exceed 10% of the pre-tax amount of said invoices.

**ARTICLE 56:** The local authority acting as the contracting authority shall maintain an auxiliary account in its budget for both the drinking water supply and collective sanitation services for domestic wastewater that it manages directly, and for the charges and revenues that concern it in the case of delegated management. It shall produce financial accounts in the forms defined by the Regulatory Body within 6 months following the end of each financial year.

**ARTICLE 57:** The modalities for the application of the provisions of this section shall be specified by regulation.

**TITLE III**  
**MONITORING AND WATER POLICING, DISPUTES, PENAL PROVISIONS AND SANCTIONS**

**SECTION I**  
**WATER QUALITY MONITORING**

**ARTICLE 58:** The monitoring of water quality shall be carried out systematically by the competent administration.  
Any operator is required to permanently monitor the quality of the water by means of regular checks which he must make available to the competent administration, and he must inform it of any variation from the imposed limit thresholds or of any incident likely to have consequences for public health.  
Regulatory texts shall specify the modalities for the application of this article.

**ARTICLE 59:** In addition to direct pollution controls, the practice known as self-monitoring refers to the measurements carried out by any operator, or those conducted under his responsibility, at the request of the administration, and under conditions specified to him.  
Self-monitoring, carried out under the responsibility of the operator, must be supplemented and validated by a control carried out at the initiative of the National Water and Sanitation Authority.

**ARTICLE 60:** Self-monitoring focuses, above all, on pollutant emissions; it may also include measurements in the rural environment, in the vicinity of the installation.  
To achieve the objectives assigned to it, self-monitoring implies regular and as frequent measurements as possible, the modalities of which shall be established by regulation, particularly concerning water withdrawals and consumption as well as discharges of any kind from installations classified for environmental protection.

**Section II**  
**WATER POLICING**

**ARTICLE 61:** Water policing shall mean any authority and/or other entity invested with the power to preserve the water resource, with regard to its qualitative, quantitative, and economic aspects, in the best general interest.  
The competence of the water police, as defined in the preceding paragraph, thus covers the balanced management of the resource in all its components and the preservation of the functioning of aquatic ecosystems and wetlands.

**ARTICLE 62:** Decrees issued by the Council of Ministers shall determine the principles of organization and intervention of the water police.

**Section III**  
**DISPUTES AND CONFLICTS OF USE**

**ARTICLE 63:** Disputes concerning challenges or other conflicts of use regarding water resources must be submitted to arbitration before any legal proceedings.

**ARTICLE 64:** In the event of failure of recourse to arbitration, the civil court of the place where the water resource subject to the dispute is located shall have jurisdiction in disputes where

**ARTICLE 65:** In the event of a dispute between landowners to whom the waters may be useful, the competent jurisdiction, in its ruling, must reconcile the interest of agriculture with the respect due to property; and in all cases, particular and local regulations must be observed.

**Section IV**  
**PENAL PROVISIONS AND SANCTIONS**

**ARTICLE 66:** Any polluter must bear the costs of their polluting activities.

**ARTICLE 67:** Without prejudice to the application of the Public Health Code relating to measures intended to prevent the pollution of drinking water and to the surveillance of consumption water, any infringement of Articles 12, 15, 17, 21, and 24 of this Code shall be punished by imprisonment of 1 year to 3 years and a fine of 2,500,000 Fmg to 250,000,000 Fmg, or by one of these two penalties only.

Any infringement of Articles 10, 11, 32, and 33 of this Code and of the texts adopted for its application, in particular the execution of works without authorization or contrary to the prescriptions of the authorization, shall be punished by 1 month to 6 months of imprisonment and a fine of 250,000 Fmg to 2,500,000 Fmg, or by one of these two penalties only.

Anyone who operates a water or hydroelectric installation without the required authorization shall be punished by a term of imprisonment of 2 months to 1 year and a fine of 2,500,000 Fmg to 25,000,000 Fmg, or by one of these two penalties only, without prejudice to any administrative sanctions relating to the closure of the installation.

**ARTICLE 68:** The amount of all financial penalties, imposed in application of the article above, are subject to modification according to the extent of the damage and/or pollution caused and according to indexation clauses to be determined by regulation.

In any event, decrees shall determine the administrative sanctions to be applied in case of infringement of this Code.

**TITLE IV**  
**FINANCING THE WATER AND SANITATION SECTOR**

**ARTICLE 69:** In order to participate in the financing of the conservation, mobilization, and protection of water resources, fees may be established for the withdrawal from resources, the deterioration of the quality of these resources, and the modification of water regimes.

**ARTICLE 70:** Fees on water resources, for water withdrawals or discharges or for modifications of water regimes, are due on an equal and equitable basis, for any natural or legal person, public or private, using these resources, according to the volumes concerned.

**ARTICLE 71:** The financing of works for the mobilization or protection of resources may also be ensured by specific fees for these works. These specific fees are due, on an equal and equitable basis, for any use and for any natural or legal person, public or private, benefiting from these works.  
Whenever possible, the management structure for these works must be an autonomous commercial enterprise structure under common law.

**ARTICLE 72:** The National Water and Sanitation Authority may resort, for the benefit of the water and sanitation sector, to the collection of funds, donations, and bequests of any kind, through regulatory procedures.

**ARTICLE 73:** A National Fund for Water Resources is hereby established, with the objective of meeting the specific financing needs for the conservation, mobilization, and protection of the quality of water resources. **TITLE V**  
**ORGANIZATION OF THE WATER AND SANITATION SECTOR**

**ARTICLE 75:** In order to ensure the integrated management of water resources and the rational development of the water and sanitation sector, the National Water and Sanitation Authority (ANDEA) is hereby created.

**ARTICLE 76:** The National Water and Sanitation Authority shall carry out its mission in close collaboration with the various departments concerned.  
The said Authority's mission shall include, in particular:  
*   to coordinate, plan, and program all hydraulic and sanitation projects and to monitor their execution;  
*   to develop and program master plans for the management of water resources;  
*   to develop and program master plans for sanitation and drainage;  
*   to establish priorities for access to water resources and to develop the relevant national standards;  
*   to have studies and works relating to sanitation and drainage networks carried out, if necessary;  
*   to collect data and information relating to water resources;  
*   to promote the use of watercourses for the purposes of animal protein production, transport, leisure, and energy production;  
*   to research new technologies to reduce the cost of water exploitation;  
*   to have economic and financial studies and analyses carried out in the short, medium, and long term with a view to:  
    1 - the optimal management of the financial resources of the water sector,  
    2 - the recovery of fees and taxes, and the economic evaluation of the return on investment in the water sector;  
*   to collect taxes and fees related to the use of water resources;  
*   to ensure awareness, information, and training in the industrial and agricultural sectors within the framework of the implementation of the national policy to combat water pollution;  
*   to monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of sanitation and water pollution prevention measures;  
*   to execute emergency plans for the prevention and control of floods and droughts.

**ARTICLE 77:** The National Water and Sanitation Authority is placed under the technical and administrative supervision of the Office of the Prime Minister, and under the financial supervision of the Ministry in charge of Economy and Finance.  
Decrees issued by the Council of Government shall determine the powers and functioning of the National Water and Sanitation Authority.

**ARTICLE 78:** In accordance with the provisions of Articles 28 and 77 of this Code, the National Water and Sanitation Authority is the sole point of contact for all stakeholders in matters of water resources.  
The relations of the said Authority with the different governmental structures, the Autonomous Provinces, and other Local Authorities, as well as non-state stakeholders, shall be specified within the framework of a decree.

**TITLE VI**  
**TRANSITIONAL AND FINAL PROVISIONS**

**Section I**  
**TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS**

**ARTICLE 79:** Until the establishment of the administrative and technical structures and bodies provided for by this Code in the field of Water and Sanitation, the structures and bodies provided for by the former legal or regulatory provisions shall continue to exist.

**ARTICLE 80:** For a period of 2 years from the date of entry into force of this Code, the state-owned company JIRAMA, created by Ordinance No. 75-024 of October 17, 1975, may legally continue its activities without holding a concession contract.  
Before the expiry of this 2-year period, the state-owned company JIRAMA must conclude concession contracts with the Conceding Authority for all or part of the facilities it operates on the date of promulgation of this Code, following specific requests by JIRAMA to the Ministry in charge of Drinking Water.  
The duration of these concession contracts shall be 10 years.

**ARTICLE 81:** The current Water Point Committees shall continue their usual activities until their respective parent municipalities are empowered as contracting authorities, with which they must enter into delegated management contracts.

**Section II**  
**FINAL PROVISIONS**

**ARTICLE 82:** Transfers of powers may be made to the Autonomous Provinces by regulation.

**ARTICLE 83:** Regulatory texts shall specify, as necessary, the modalities for the application of this Code.

**ARTICLE 84:** All prior provisions contrary to the stipulations of this Water Code are hereby repealed, in particular Ordinance No. 74-002 of February 4, 1974, concerning the orientation of water and electricity policy in its water component, Ordinance No. 60-173 of October 3, 1960, concerning the control of groundwaters and subsequent texts, as well as Article 3, paragraph 4, and Article 6 of Law No. 95-035 of October 3, 1995, authorizing the creation of organizations responsible for urban sanitation and establishing fees for urban sanitation.  
Until the effective implementation of the new sanitation fee regulations, Articles 4 to 12 of Law No. 95-035 shall remain in force; they shall be repealed upon the publication of the new texts.
