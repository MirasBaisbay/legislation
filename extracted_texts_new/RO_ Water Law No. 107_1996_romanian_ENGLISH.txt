**Water Law No. 107/1996**
*(Published in the Official Gazette, Part I, No. 244 of October 8, 1996)*

**The Parliament of Romania** hereby adopts this law.

**CHAPTER I**
**General Provisions**

**Art. 1.** - (1) Waters are part of the public domain.
(2) For the purposes of this law, waters shall mean:
a) surface waters, contained within the minor riverbeds of watercourses or in the basins of lakes and ponds, groundwater, inland maritime waters, the territorial sea, and the exclusive economic zone of the Black Sea;
b) the minor riverbeds of watercourses, the basins of lakes and ponds, and their banks and cliffs;
c) the sand and gravel beaches of the Black Sea, the sea cliffs and beaches with their natural riches and biological resources, the surface or underground waters in the coastal zone used for drinking water supply, balneotherapy, recreation, and other uses, as well as the strip of land necessary for the protection of the shore and for ensuring the normal use of the beach;
d) glaciers and permanent snow.
(3) For the purposes of this law, the terms and expressions below shall have the following meanings:
a) aquifer or aquifer layer - one or more subterranean layers of rock or other geological strata with porosity and permeability high enough to allow either a significant flow of groundwater or the abstraction of significant quantities of groundwater;
b) surface waters - inland still or flowing waters, transitional waters, and coastal waters, with the exception of groundwater; in the case of coastal waters, they also include the territorial sea;
c) groundwater - all waters located beneath the ground surface in the saturation zone and in direct contact with the soil or subsoil;
d) inland waters - all still and flowing waters on the surface of the land and all groundwater landward of the baseline from which the breadth of territorial sea waters is measured;
e) coastal waters - surface waters situated between the land and an imaginary line drawn at a distance of one nautical mile seaward from the baseline from which the breadth of territorial sea waters is measured, extending, where applicable, to the outer limit of transitional waters;
f) transitional waters - bodies of surface water in the vicinity of river mouths, which are partly saline due to their proximity to coastal waters but are substantially influenced by freshwater flows;
g) minor riverbed - the strip of land occupied by the waters at their highest flows, recorded in the last 20 years;
h) river basin - the area of land from which all surface runoff flows through a series of streams, rivers, and possibly lakes towards a specific point in a watercourse, typically a lake or the mouth of a river into a sea or ocean;
i) sub-basin - the area of land from which all surface runoff flows through a series of streams, rivers, and possibly lakes towards a specific point in a watercourse, typically a lake or a confluence;
j) water management works - the set of constructions, developments, and installations through which one or more water management functions are carried out;
k) water management - the activity of general interest comprising the set of measures and works for the knowledge, conservation, rational use, and protection of water resources against depletion and degradation, for the purpose of ensuring sustainable development;
l) water status monitoring - the process of observing, measuring, and recording the quality and quantitative parameters of waters, carried out over time, at specific points, and with a certain frequency;
m) protection zone - an area of land where prohibitions and restrictions are introduced for the purpose of protecting water quality or protecting hydraulic engineering structures, banks, and riverbeds;
n) sanitary protection zone - an area of land where a strict regime of restrictions and prohibitions is established, for the purpose of protecting water sources, and the related hydraulic engineering structures and installations against any activities that could lead to their pollution or deterioration;
o) hydrological protection zone - the strip of land adjacent to the banks of watercourses, necessary to ensure the flow of high waters without endangering human life and property;
p) program of measures - the set of basic and supplementary measures, identified for each river basin or sub-basin, developed for the purpose of achieving the established environmental objectives at the lowest cost and with maximum efficiency.

**Art. 2.** - (1) The right of ownership over the waters mentioned in Art. 1 belongs to the state and is inalienable.
(2) The right of ownership over water management works, regardless of their holder by any title, shall be exercised in accordance with the provisions of this law.

LAW no. 107 of September 25, 1996
Water Law
ISSUER □ THE PARLIAMENT
Published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 244 of October 8, 1996

Note
In accordance with Article XV of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 44 of May 24, 2023, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 467 of May 26, 2023, in all normative acts in force concerning the management and control of non-reimbursable European funds, applicable to the 2021-2027 programming period, the expression "operational program" is replaced with the term "program."

The Parliament of Romania adopts the present law.

**Chapter I**
**General Provisions**

**Article 1**
(1) Waters represent a renewable, vulnerable, and limited natural resource, an indispensable element for life and for society, a raw material for productive activities, a source of energy, and a means of transport, a determining factor in maintaining ecological balance.
(1^1) Water is not a commercial product like any other, but rather a natural heritage which must be protected, treated, and defended as such, being a strategic resource for national safety and security.
(on 16-07-2020, Paragraph (1^1) of Article 1, Chapter I was amended by Point 1, Article I of LAW no. 122 of July 10, 2020, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 613 of July 13, 2020)
(1^2) The groundwaters and surface waters of Romania are assets of public interest.
(on 16-07-2020, Article 1 of Chapter I was supplemented by Point 2, Article I of LAW no. 122 of July 10, 2020, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 613 of July 13, 2020)
(2) Waters are part of the public domain of the state. The knowledge, protection, development, and sustainable use of water resources are actions of general interest.
(on 29-08-2004, Para. (2) of Art. 1 was amended by pt. 2 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)
(3) The right of use, as well as the corresponding obligations resulting from the protection and conservation of water resources, shall be exercised in accordance with the provisions of this law, with the exception of geothermal waters for which specific regulations will be adopted.
(4) Waters, their banks and beds, regardless of the natural or legal person that administers them, are subject to the provisions of this law, as well as to the provisions of international conventions to which Romania is a party.
(5) Also subject to the provisions of this law are works constructed on waters or related to waters, which, directly or indirectly, cause temporary or permanent changes to the quality of waters or their flow regime.
(6) The conservation, protection, and improvement of the aquatic environment, under conditions of sustainable use of water resources, are based on the precautionary principle, the principle of preventive action, the principle that environmental damage should as a priority be rectified at source, and the polluter pays principle, and must take into account the vulnerability of the aquatic ecosystems located in the Danube Delta and the Black Sea, as their balance is closely influenced by the quality of the inland waters that flow into them.
(on 29-08-2004, Para. (6) of Art. 1 was introduced by pt. 3 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)

b) protect against any form of pollution and modification of the characteristics of water resources, their banks and beds or basins;
c) achieve the environmental objectives for surface and groundwater bodies;

f) manage water sustainably and rationally and equitably allocate this resource, while maintaining and improving the quality and natural regeneration of waters;

g) defend against floods and any other dangerous hydrometeorological phenomena;
g^1) manage flood risk, with the aim of reducing the negative consequences for human health, the environment, cultural heritage, and economic activity;

l) conserve, protect, and improve the aquatic environment through specific measures for the progressive reduction of discharges, emissions, or losses of priority substances and the cessation or phasing out of discharges, emissions, or losses of priority hazardous substances;
(on 29-08-2004, Letter l) of Art. 2 was introduced by pt. 5 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)
m) progressively reduce groundwater pollution and prevent further pollution;
(on 29-08-2004, Letter m) of Art. 2 was introduced by pt. 5 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)
n) achieve the objectives of the Convention on the Protection of the Black Sea Against Pollution regarding the cessation or phased elimination of discharges, emissions, or losses of priority substances to achieve concentrations of these substances in the marine environment close to natural background values and close to zero for synthetic substances;

o) prevent further deterioration, protect, and enhance the status of aquatic ecosystems and, with regard to their water requirements, of terrestrial ecosystems and wetlands directly depending on the aquatic ecosystems.

**Article 2^2**
(1) For the purpose of achieving effective and integrated protection of all waters and the aquatic environment and of achieving the environmental objectives for surface and groundwater bodies of this law, the delimitation of water bodies and the designation of artificial or heavily modified water bodies shall be carried out in accordance with the procedures provided in Annex no. 1^3.
(on 19-02-2010, Para. (1) of Art. 2^2 was amended by pt. 106 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010, by replacing the phrase "objectives for the protection of waters and the aquatic environment/aquatic ecosystems" with the phrase "environmental objectives for surface and groundwater bodies".)
(2) A surface water body is designated as artificial or heavily modified when:
a) the changes to the hydromorphological characteristics of this water body, necessary for achieving good ecological status, could have significant adverse effects on the wider environment, navigation, including port facilities or recreation, activities for the purpose of which water is stored, such as water supply, power generation or irrigation, water flow regulation, flood defense, land drainage, and other activities of equal importance to those provided for sustainable development;
b) the artificial or modified characteristics of the water body, imposed by the beneficial uses, cannot reasonably be achieved, for reasons of technical feasibility or disproportionate costs, by other means which are significantly better environmental options.
(3) The designation of artificial or heavily modified water bodies, including the reasons for designation, shall be presented in the management plans that constitute a qualitative water management component of the master plans for the development and management of river basins, hereinafter referred to as master plans, and subsequently reviewed every 6 years, in accordance with the provisions of Art. 43.
(on 19-02-2010, Para. (3) of Art. 2^2 was amended by pt. 4 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)

**Article 2^3**
The deadlines provided for in Art. 2^1 may be extended for the purpose of the phased achievement of the objectives for water bodies, only if no deterioration occurs in the status of the affected water bodies and when the following conditions are met:
a) the measures identified for improving the status of water bodies cannot be achieved by technically feasible means, as well as for economic reasons, by the deadline set in Art. 2^1, for at least one of the following reasons: (i) the level of improvement required can, for technical reasons, only be achieved in stages which exceed the deadline; (ii) completing the implementation of the measures by the deadline is extremely costly, leading to disproportionate costs; (iii) natural conditions do not allow for the improvement of the water body's status by the deadline;
(on 19-02-2010, Letter a) of Art. 2^3 was amended by pt. 5 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)
b) the extension of the deadline and the reasons for it are specifically set out and explained in the master plan;
c) the extension of the deadline must be limited to at most two subsequent updates of the master plan, except in cases where the natural conditions are such that the objectives cannot be achieved within this period;
d) the master plan presents a summary of the measures necessary to bring the water bodies progressively to the required status by the extended deadline, the reasons for any significant delay in making the measures operational, and the timetable for their implementation. Any review of the application of these measures and a summary of additional measures shall also be included in subsequent updates of the master plan.
(on 29-08-2004, Art. 2^3 was introduced by pt. 6 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)

**Article 2^4**
Where it is found that certain water bodies are so affected by human activity or their natural conditions identified in accordance with Art. 43 para. (1^4) are such that the objectives provided for in Art. 2^1 paras. (1) and (2) are technically unfeasible or entail disproportionate costs, less stringent objectives may be adopted only if the following conditions are cumulatively met:
a) the socioeconomic and environmental protection needs determined by such human activities cannot be achieved by other means which are a significantly better environmental option and which do not entail disproportionate costs;
b) the best possible ecological and chemical status for surface water bodies is ensured, taking into account the impact that cannot reasonably be avoided due to the nature, human activity, or pollution;
(on 16-07-2020, Letter b) of Article 2^4, Chapter I was amended by Point 3, Article I of LAW no. 122 of July 10, 2020, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 613 of July 13, 2020)
c) the slightest possible changes to good status for groundwater bodies are ensured, taking into account the impact that cannot reasonably be avoided due to the nature of human activity or pollution;
d) no further deterioration in the status of the water bodies occurs;
e) the less stringent objectives established, as well as the reasons for the decision to establish them, are specifically mentioned in the management plans provided for in Art. 43 para. (1), and these objectives are reviewed every 6 years.
(on 19-02-2010, Art. 2^4 was amended by pt. 6 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)

**Article 2^5**
Temporary deterioration in the status of water bodies is not considered a breach of the provisions of this law if it is the result of circumstances of natural cause or force majeure which are exceptional or could not reasonably have been foreseen, such as exceptional floods and prolonged drought, or if it is the result of circumstances due to accidents which could not reasonably have been foreseen, and only if all the following conditions are met:
a) all steps are taken to prevent further deterioration of the status of water bodies and not to compromise the achievement of the environmental objectives for other surface and groundwater bodies not affected by these circumstances;
b) the conditions under which exceptional or unforeseeable circumstances may be declared, including the adoption of appropriate indicators, are established in the river basin management plans;
c) the program of measures in the master plan includes the measures to be taken in such exceptional circumstances, which must not compromise the restoration of the water body's quality once the circumstances cease;
d) the effects of circumstances which are exceptional or could not reasonably have been foreseen are reviewed annually in accordance with the provisions of Art. 2^3 letter a), and all feasible measures are taken to restore the water body to its status prior to the effects of those circumstances, as soon as they can be implemented;
e) a summary of the effects of the circumstances and of the measures taken or to be taken in accordance with letters a) and d) is included in the master plan at its next update.
(on 19-02-2010, Art. 2^5 was amended by pt. 7 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)

**Article 2^6**
(1) Within each river basin district, all water bodies used for the abstraction of water intended for human consumption which provide more than 10 m³/day on average or serve more than 50 persons shall be identified, as well as those water bodies intended for such future use.
(2) Water bodies used for the abstraction of water intended for human consumption which provide more than 100 m³/day on average shall be monitored in accordance with the provisions of Annex no. 1^3.
(on 29-08-2004, Art. 2^6 was introduced by pt. 6 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)

**Article 2^7**
(1) The objectives provided for in Art. 2^1 paras. (1) and (2) are not considered to be unfulfilled when:
a) the failure to achieve good groundwater status, good ecological status or, where applicable, good ecological potential, or the failure to prevent deterioration in the status of a surface or groundwater body is the result of new modifications to the physical characteristics of a surface water body or alterations to the level of groundwater bodies;
b) the failure to prevent deterioration from very good status to good status of water bodies is the result of new sustainable development human activities.
(2) The provisions of para. (1) shall apply only when all the following conditions are met:
a) all practicable steps are taken to mitigate the adverse impact on the status of the water bodies;
b) the reasons for those modifications or alterations are specifically set out and explained in the management plan, and the objectives are reviewed every 6 years;
c) the reasons for those modifications or alterations are of overriding public interest and/or the benefits to the environment and to society of achieving the objectives set out in Art. 2^1 paras. (1) and (2) are outweighed by the benefits of the new modifications or alterations to human health, to the maintenance of human safety or to sustainable development;
d) the beneficial objectives served by those modifications or alterations of the water bodies cannot, for reasons of technical feasibility or disproportionate cost, be achieved by other means, which are a significantly better environmental option.
(on 19-02-2010, Para. (2) of Art. 2^7 was amended by pt. 8 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)
(on 29-08-2004, Art. 2^7 was introduced by pt. 6 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)

**Article 2^8**
(1) For the protection and conservation of surface water resources, discharges into these waters shall be regulated by December 22, 2012, through the use of a combined approach, by establishing and implementing emission controls based on the best available techniques, or emission limit values, or, in the case of diffuse pressures, controls and best environmental practices, established in accordance with Government Emergency Ordinance no. 152/2005 on integrated pollution prevention and control, approved with amendments and supplements by Law no. 84/2006, Government Decision no. 188/2002 approving certain norms on the conditions for discharging wastewater into the aquatic environment, as subsequently amended and supplemented, Government Decision no. 964/2000 approving the Action Plan for the protection of waters against pollution by nitrates from agricultural sources, as subsequently amended and supplemented, Government Decision no. 351/2005 approving the Program for the gradual elimination of discharges, emissions, and losses of priority hazardous substances, as subsequently amended and supplemented, and any other relevant national regulation compliant with Community legislation.
(2) Where a quality objective or quality standard, established in accordance with the provisions of this law and of Government Decision no. 351/2005, as subsequently amended and supplemented, or of any other relevant national regulation compliant with Community legislation, requires stricter conditions than those resulting from the application of the provisions of para. (1), stricter emission controls shall be established.

**Article 2^9**
(1) When applying the provisions of Arts. 2^2 - 2^5 and Art. 2^7, it must be ensured that their application will not permanently exclude or compromise the achievement of the objectives provided for in Art. 2^1 in other water bodies within the same river basin district and that this is consistent with other regulations in force.
(2) The steps for ensuring the application of the new provisions, including the application of the provisions of Arts. 2^2 - 2^5 and Art. 2^7, guarantee at least the same level of protection as that existing in the legislation in force.

**Article 2^11**

**Article 2^12**
To progressively reduce pollution by priority substances and phase out emissions, discharges, and losses of priority hazardous substances, as well as to prevent the deterioration of the status of all surface water bodies, the central public authority for water must introduce the necessary measures to:
a) avoid water pollution by one or more priority hazardous substances, provided for in Annex no. 5, which pose a significant risk to water or the aquatic environment, including water abstracted for drinking purposes, by developing water quality standards for surface waters affected by discharges containing these substances, and emission control measures for the main sources of these discharges, based, among other things, on considering all technical measures to reduce discharges;
b) transpose into national legislation, in accordance with the deadlines established at the community level, new water quality standards for surface waters, should such standards be adopted at the community level;
c) identify new priority hazardous substances and develop, within a maximum of 5 years of identification, appropriate surface water quality standards and measures for the control of emissions from the main point sources.
d) periodically update, by Government decision, the List of priority substances in the field of water provided for in Annex no. 5, as well as the quality standards provided for in letter b).

**Chapter II**
**Regime of Use of Waters and Beds**

**Section 1**
**Regime of Use of Waters**

**Article 9**
(1) The right to use surface or groundwaters, including artesian waters, is established by the water management permit and is exercised according to legal provisions. This right also includes the discharge into water resources of wastewater, water from dewatering or drainage, stormwater, mine water, or reservoir water, after use.
(2) Surface or groundwaters may be used freely, in compliance with sanitary and water quality protection norms, for drinking, watering, irrigation, washing, bathing, and other household needs, if this does not involve the use of installations or involves the use of small-capacity installations of up to 0.2 liters/second, intended exclusively for satisfying the needs of one's own household.

**Article 10**
(1) Satisfying the water needs of the population has priority over the use of water for other purposes. Priority is also given, over other uses, to water supply for animals, replenishing the untouchable water reserve after fires, as well as the flows necessary to maintain the ecological balance of the aquatic habitat.
(2) Restricting the use of drinking water for the population in favor of other activities is prohibited.
(3) Drinking water distributed in an organized manner in populated centers may be used for other purposes only if the needs of the population, animals, and activities requiring water of this quality have been fully satisfied. The supply of drinking water for other purposes will be limited or terminated only when new requirements arise in the water supply for the population.
(4) Groundwaters of appropriate quality are strategic resources intended primarily for the water supply of the population and animals, as well as for ensuring hygiene and public health. These waters may be used for other purposes only on the basis of a water management permit.

**Article 17**
For the purpose of rational use and protection of water resource quality, water users have the following obligations:
a) to adopt production technologies with reduced water requirements and as little pollution as possible, to save water through recirculation or repeated use, to eliminate waste and reduce water losses, to reduce pollutants discharged with wastewater, and to recover useful substances contained in wastewater and sludge;
a^1) to progressively reduce discharges, emissions, and losses of priority substances and to cease or gradually eliminate discharges, emissions, and losses of priority hazardous substances. The program for the gradual elimination of discharges, emissions, and losses of priority hazardous substances is approved by Government decision, at the proposal of the central public authority for water;

a^2) to adopt water treatment technologies for water abstracted from the source, which ensure the qualitative and quantitative requirements of the water uses;
(on 29-08-2004, Letter a^2) of Art. 17 was introduced by pt. 25 of Art. I of LAW no. 310 of June 28, 2004, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 584 of June 30, 2004.)
b) to ensure the implementation, maintenance, and operation of water quality treatment plants and installations at the authorized capacity, to monitor their efficiency through laboratory analyses, and to intervene promptly to bring emission indicators within the permissible limits for wastewater discharge, limits provided for by the water management permit;
c) to strictly respect technological discipline and norms in production activities that use water and discharge wastewater, as well as in water quality treatment plants and installations;
d) to monitor, through observation and control drillings, the quality status of groundwaters in the area of influence of treatment plants, industrial platforms, deposits of hazardous substances, petroleum products, and waste of any kind.

**Article 18**

In all cases, local pre-treatment of wastewater from these users is mandatory.

**Article 19**

**Article 21**

**Article 22**

**Section 2**

(3) Circulation on watercourses, natural lakes, or the sea with non-motorized pleasure boats is carried out freely, respecting the rights of riparian owners and legal regulations.
(4) The right to use minor beds, the beach, and the seashore for purposes other than those provided for in para. (1) shall be exercised only after obtaining the water management permit.

**Article 31**
(1) Forests with special protection functions in the reception basins of reservoirs, those in basins with a high degree of torrentiality and prone to erosion, in the major beds of rivers, in levee-bank areas, as well as the forest strips located along undiked rivers belong to the group of forests with special water protection functions and are managed as such through intensive treatments, with clear-cutting or treatments with a short regeneration period being prohibited.
(2) Forests for water protection, those for soil protection, located on rocky areas, screes, on eroded soils, on land with a slope greater than 35 degrees, and other such forests are managed under a special protection regime.
(3) In the perimeters mentioned in paras. (1) and (2), works to combat soil erosion, to extinguish torrents, and special rules for the maintenance of the executed works will be applied.
(4) Forests in mountain and hill areas must be managed in such a way as not to contribute to the formation of floods and the occurrence of soil erosion.

**Article 32**
(1) The use, transport, and handling of waste and hazardous substances in areas around waters and in other places from which they could reach surface, ground, or marine waters may be done only under such conditions that they do not cause water pollution.
(2) The storage of waste and hazardous substances in areas near waters is done in accordance with the water management notice.
(3) The storage of radioactive materials or waste in the major bed is prohibited.
(4) The transport of hazardous substances on inland waters, on the fluvial and maritime Danube, and on the territorial sea may be done only under the conditions of a joint notice, issued for each case by the central public authority for water and the central public authority for transport. **Article 43**
(1) In order to establish the fundamental guidelines for the sustainable, unitary, balanced, and complex management of water resources and aquatic ecosystems, as well as for the protection of protected areas, master plans are developed for river basins or groups of river basins. The master plan is the main instrument for planning, development, and water management at the river basin district level and consists of the river basin development plan, which also includes the flood risk management plan - the quantitative management component, and the river basin management plan - the qualitative management component. The master plan is developed according to the methodology and technical instructions approved by order of the head of the central public authority for water. The management plans for river basin districts are developed and adopted in accordance with the deadlines established in Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2000 establishing a framework for Community action in the field of water policy. The flood risk management plans for river basins/spaces are developed and adopted in accordance with the deadlines established in Directive 2007/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2007 on the assessment and management of flood risks.
(on 10-11-2017, Paragraph (1) of Article 43, Section 3, Chapter III was amended by Point 6, Article I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 78 of November 8, 2017, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 885 of November 10, 2017)
(1^1) The master plans set out in an integrated manner the environmental objectives for surface and groundwater bodies, aiming to ensure:
a) good surface water status or, for artificial or heavily modified water bodies, good ecological potential and good surface water chemical status;

c) the achievement of specially defined objectives for protected areas, with the aim of reducing the treatment required for the production of water intended for human consumption, respecting the requirements provided for in Law no. 458/2002, as subsequently amended and supplemented;
d) protection and defense against the destructive action of waters.

**Article 56**
(1) The water management permit may be amended or withdrawn by the issuer, without compensation, in the following cases:

c) in case of danger to the aquatic environment and, especially, if the aquatic environments are subject to critical conditions incompatible with their protection;
d) in cases of force majeure, due to natural changes in the water resource or natural disasters that have occurred to the users' installations;
e) if the works or installations are abandoned or are not properly maintained, in which case their holder is obliged, at the instruction of the "Romanian Waters" National Administration, to demolish them.

**Section 5^1**
**Flood Risk Management**
(on 19-02-2010, Section 5^1 of Chap. III was introduced by pt. 52 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)

**Article 76^1**
(1) For each river basin district provided for in Art. 6 para. (6), a preliminary flood risk assessment shall be carried out in accordance with para. (2).
(2) A preliminary flood risk assessment aims to assess potential risks. Based on information and on identifying the impact of climate change on the occurrence of floods, the assessment of potential risks shall include at least the following elements:
a) maps of the river basin, at an appropriate scale, including the boundaries of the river basins, of the coastal area, and information on topography and land use;
b) a description of floods that have occurred in the past and have had significant negative effects on human health, the environment, cultural heritage, and economic activity, for which the probability of similar future events is still relevant, both in terms of their magnitude, the flood-prone areas, and the assessment of the negative effects they could produce;
c) a description of significant historical floods, with a probability of recurrence of events, which would lead to similar negative future consequences;
d) an assessment of the potential negative consequences of future floods for human health, the environment, cultural heritage, and economic activity, analyzing issues such as topography, the position of watercourses and their general hydrological and geomorphological characteristics, including major riverbeds as natural retention areas, the efficiency of flood defense infrastructure, the location of populated areas, areas with economic activity, areas favorable for long-term development, taking into account the effects of climate change on the occurrence of floods.
(3) For each river basin district provided for in Art. 6 para. (6), shared with other member states, the central public authority for water shall ensure the exchange of information with the competent authorities of the member states concerned.
(4) The central public authority for water shall ensure the preliminary flood risk assessment and reporting to the European Commission by December 22, 2011.
(on 19-02-2010, Art. 76^1 was introduced by pt. 52 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)

**Article 76^2**
(1) Based on the preliminary flood risk assessment provided for in Art. 76^1, for each river basin district provided for in Art. 6 para. (6), the areas where there is a potential significant flood risk or where the materialization of this risk is probable shall be identified.
(2) For each river basin district provided for in Art. 6 para. (6), shared with other riparian states, the identification of areas and the preliminary flood risk assessment shall be coordinated between the competent authorities of the respective states.
(on 19-02-2010, Art. 76^2 was introduced by pt. 52 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)

**Article 76^3**
(1) For the areas identified under Art. 76^2 para. (1), flood hazard and flood risk maps shall be produced, respectively, according to the methodological norms elaborated by the central public authority for water, which are approved by Government decision.
(2) Pursuant to Art. 76^2 para. (2), in the case of transboundary rivers, the preparation of flood-prone area maps and flood risk maps shall be based on the exchange of information between riparian states.
(3) The flood hazard maps shall cover the geographical areas likely to be flooded in the following cases, in accordance with the norms provided for in para. (1):
a) low probability floods or extreme event scenarios;
b) medium probability floods for which the probable return period is greater than or equal to 100 years;
c) high probability floods, as appropriate.
(4) For each case provided for in para. (3), where there is a significant flood risk, the following elements shall be indicated in accordance with the methodological norms provided for in para. (1):
a) the extent of the flooded area;
b) the water depth or level;
c) the flow velocity or the corresponding flood flow.
(5) The flood risk maps indicating the potential adverse effects associated with the flood cases provided for in para. (3) shall be expressed in the following parameters:
a) the indicative number of inhabitants potentially affected;
b) the type of economic activity in the potentially affected area;
c) the type of drinking water supply for the population in the potentially affected area and the number of people served;
d) installations that may cause accidental pollution in the event of floods, in accordance with Annex no. 1 to Government Emergency Ordinance no. 152/2005, approved with amendments and supplements by Law no. 84/2006, as well as the protected areas in the potentially affected areas provided for in Annex no. 1^2;
e) indication of areas with high sediment transport, floods with coarse sediments, as well as information on other significant sources of pollution.
(6) The central public authority for water shall ensure the reporting to the European Commission of the flood hazard maps and flood risk maps by December 22, 2013, under the following conditions:
a) the preparation of the flood hazard maps is ensured by the central public authority for water;
b) the preparation of the flood risk maps is ensured by the county councils, with the support and coordination of the central public authorities with competences in the field of natural risk mapping;
c) all central public authorities carrying out activities in areas with significant flood risk shall make available, free of charge, all the information and data necessary for the creation of flood hazard maps and flood risk maps;
d) the creation of flood hazard maps and flood risk maps is financed from the state budget, through the budgets of the central public authorities provided for in letter b).
(on 19-02-2010, Art. 76^3 was introduced by pt. 52 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)

**Article 76^4**
(1) Based on the maps provided for in Art. 76^3 paras. (3) and (5), the central public authorities provided for in Art. 76^3 para. (6) letter b) shall prepare flood risk management plans, coordinated by the central public authority for water, at the level of the river basin district provided for in Art. 6, for the areas identified according to Art. 76^2 para. (1), and for the areas identified according to Art. 76^8 para. (1) letter b) in accordance with the provisions of paras. (2) and (3).
(1^1) The flood risk management plans provided for in para. (1), as well as their updating, shall be approved by Government decision.
(on 14-12-2016, Article 76^4 of Section 5^1, Chapter III was supplemented by Point 1, SOLE ARTICLE of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 94 of December 8, 2016, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 1005 of December 14, 2016)
(2) For the areas identified according to Art. 76^2 para. (1) and for the areas identified according to Art. 76^8 para. (1) letter b), the flood risk management objectives shall focus on reducing the potential adverse effects of floods on human health, the environment, cultural heritage, and economic activity, by adopting both non-structural and structural measures to reduce the probability of flooding.
(3) The flood risk management plans shall include measures to achieve the objectives established according to para. (2), as well as the components established by methodological norms elaborated by the central public authority for water and approved by Government decision. b) aspects of flood risk management, with an emphasis on prevention, protection, preparation, including flood forecasting, warning, and alarm under the specific conditions of a particular river basin or sub-basin;

(1) shall not include measures which, by their size and impact, would lead to a significant increase in flood risk upstream or downstream for neighboring countries, unless these measures have been agreed upon by the states involved according to the provisions of Art. 76^5.

**Article 76^5**
(1) For river basin districts provided for in Art. 6 that are entirely on Romanian territory, the central public authority for water shall ensure that a single flood risk management plan or a set of flood risk management plans, coordinated at the river basin district level, is prepared.
(2) For river basin districts that are entirely within the European Union, the central public authority for water shall ensure coordination in order to produce a single international flood risk management plan or a set of flood risk management plans coordinated at the international river basin district level. (2) shall apply to the portions of the international river basin of the Danube River that are part of the territory of the Member States of the European Community.
(4) If deemed necessary, the flood risk management plans provided for in paras. (2) and (3) are detailed and coordinated in collaboration with the competent authorities of neighboring countries at the level of international sub-basins.
(5) If the central public authority for water identifies a problem generated by a riparian country, which has an impact on the flood risk management of national waters and which cannot be solved at the national level, it shall report this problem to the European Commission, as well as to any other interested member state or riparian state to the international river basin of the Danube River and may make recommendations on how to resolve it.

**Article 76^6**
(1) The central public authority for water will take measures to correlate the flood risk management plans with the river basin district management plans, with the objectives of exchanging information and achieving a unitary and correlated approach to achieve a common benefit with regard to the environmental objectives for surface and groundwater bodies, according to Arts. 2^1 and 2^2, and the flood risk management objectives provided for in the National Flood Risk Management Strategy for the medium and long term.
(on 10-11-2017, Paragraph (1) of Article 76^6, Section 5^1-a, Chapter III was amended by Point 9, Article I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 78 of November 8, 2017, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 885 of November 10, 2017)
(2) In applying the provisions of para. (1), the following shall be particularly taken into account:
a) the development of the first flood hazard and risk maps and their subsequent revision, according to the provisions of Arts. 76^3 and 76^9, shall be carried out so that the information they contain is consistent with the relevant information presented in accordance with the methodological norms provided for in Art. 76^4 para. (3); this information is coordinated with the reviews provided for in Art. 43 para. (1^5) and may be integrated into them;
b) the development of the first flood risk management plans and their subsequent reviews, according to the provisions of Arts. 76^4 and 76^9, is carried out in coordination with the river basin district development plans;
c) the active involvement of all interested parties under Art. 76^7 is coordinated, where appropriate, with the active involvement of interested parties under Art. 43 para. (1^6).
(on 19-02-2010, Art. 76^6 was introduced by pt. 52 of Art. I of EMERGENCY ORDINANCE no. 3 of February 5, 2010, published in OFFICIAL GAZETTE no. 114 of February 19, 2010.)
