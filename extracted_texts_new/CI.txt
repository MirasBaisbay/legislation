TITLE ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS

CHAPTER I – DEFINITIONS

Article 1

For the purposes of this law on the Water Code, the following definitions shall apply:

Authority: any holder of power at both the national and local levels.

Water authority: the structure designated for the management of water resources.

Sanitation: collection, evacuation, and discharge or destruction according to sanitary requirements, with or without prior treatment, of rainwater, wastewater, or solid waste.

Watershed: a geographical area whose topography determines the flow of surface waters and effluents of various kinds towards a point of convergence; this point is called the basin outlet.

Abstraction:

the act of drawing water from a spring, lake, or river to supply a water main,

a device for the controlled withdrawal of water from a source,

a canal or conduit for diverting water from a lake or watercourse for agricultural, domestic, or industrial needs.

Water or water resources: water is a transparent, colorless, odorless, and tasteless liquid in its pure state. The terms water and water resources are used interchangeably.

Surface water: all bodies of water including their legal appurtenances in contact with the atmosphere (on the surface of the earth).

Mineral water: water from an underground source containing mineral salts and having chemical properties favorable to health.

Potable water: any water is considered potable if it does not affect the health of the consumer in the short, medium, and long term. Its physicochemical and microbiological characteristics are subject to regulatory provisions.

Sacred water: water considered or used, with or without its content, by a community that calls for absolute respect worthy of adoration and veneration.

Groundwater: all water contained in reservoir rocks in the subsoil, located below the hypodermic layer of the soil (unsaturated zone).

Territorial waters or seas: a zone of the sea extending from the coast of a country to a line considered its maritime border. This border is defined by the Montego Bay Convention of 10 December 1982 as 12 miles (1 mile = 1,609 meters).

Upstream/downstream property: a space or domain located upstream (superior) and downstream (inferior).

Borehole: the drilling of a circular hole of a predefined diameter, from the ground surface down to a layer, an aquifer zone, and equipped with a mechanical lifting system to draw water from it.

Freeboard: owner-free land, along the bank of a river or canal, the dimensions of which are subject to regulatory provisions.

Phreatic table: a shallow underground water table, easily reached by wells.

Protection perimeter:

immediate protection perimeter: a fenced area where any activity, installation, or deposit is prohibited except for those explicitly authorized.

close protection perimeter: an area where any activity or deposit likely to directly or indirectly harm the quality of the water may be prohibited or regulated. This land may be acquired through expropriation.

remote protection perimeter: an area where activities may be regulated if they present a risk of pollution.

Principle of information and participation: every person has the right to be informed of the state of water resources and to participate in the procedures prior to decision-making that may have prejudicial effects on water resources.

Principle of planning and cooperation: public authorities, international institutions, non-governmental associations, and private individuals shall work together to protect water resources at all possible levels and to participate in the development of a master plan for water resources.

Principle of precaution and prevention: preliminary measures taken to avoid or reduce any risk or danger to a given environment (water resources) during the planning or execution of activities likely to have an impact on that environmental setting.

Water pollution: the introduction into the aquatic environment of any substance likely to modify the physical, chemical, and/or biological characteristics of the water and to create risks to human health, to harm terrestrial and aquatic fauna and flora, to impair the amenity of sites, or to hinder any other rational use of the waters.

Polluter-pays principle: a set of defined rules that sanctions any natural or legal person who, directly or indirectly, causes an unfavorable modification in a given environment through the introduction of noxious substances. The damages caused are subject to a tax and/or a fee.

User-pays principle: a set of defined rules that allows for the tariffing of water use according to its purposes. These uses are subject to a tax and/or a fee.

Well: an excavation dug from the ground surface down to a layer, an aquifer, to draw water from it.

Sanitation and drainage network: a set of works intended to collect and evacuate wastewater or rainwater.

Hydrographic network: a set of permanent natural drainage channels where water from runoff or returned by groundwater tables flows, either in the form of springs or by continuous restitution along the bed of the watercourse.

Wetlands: lands, whether exploited or not, that are usually flooded or saturated with fresh, salt, or brackish water, either permanently or temporarily. The vegetation, when it exists, is dominated by hygrophilous plants for at least part of the year.

Environmental impact assessment: a set of procedures used to evaluate the effects of a given action or activity on the environment and to propose any measure or action to eliminate, reduce, or mitigate the harmful environmental effects likely to be generated by such an activity.

CHAPTER II – SCOPE OF APPLICATION

Article 2
This law on the Water Code determines the fundamental principles applicable:

to the legal regime for water, hydraulic developments, and works,

to the protection regime for water, hydraulic developments, and works,

to the management of water, hydraulic developments, and works.

It specifies the general rules for:

the preservation and distribution of water,

the preservation of the quality of hydraulic developments and works,

the harmonious use of sacred waters,

the policing of water, infractions, and sanctions.

The waters defined in this law on the Water Code include continental waters and the waters of the territorial sea.

Article 3
The following are subject to the provisions of this law:

natural or legal persons, under public or private law, carrying out an activity related to water resources,

hydraulic developments and works,

classified installations in accordance with the laws and regulations in force,

unclassified installations, works, and activities carried out for domestic or other purposes, by any natural or legal person, under public or private law, and entailing either withdrawals from surface or groundwater, whether returned or not, or a modification of chronic or episodic, direct or indirect discharges, flows, releases, or deposits, even if non-polluting.

Article 4
This law on the Water Code does not apply:

to situations of war,

to military activities. However, the authors of such activities are required to take into account the concerns for the protection of water resources, hydraulic developments, and works and to ensure for this purpose that they do not harm the public hydraulic domain as defined in Article 11 of this law on the Water Code.

CHAPTER III – OBJECTIVES

Article 5
The purpose of this law on the Water Code is the integrated management of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works.

This management aims to ensure:

the preservation of aquatic ecosystems, sites, and wetlands,

protection against all forms of pollution, the restoration of surface waters, groundwater, and sea waters within the limits of territorial waters,

the protection, mobilization, and management of water resources,

the development and protection of hydraulic developments and works,

the valorization of water as an economic resource and its distribution in a manner that satisfies or reconciles, during the various uses, activities, or works, the requirements:

of the supply of potable water to the population,

of health, public sanitation, and civil protection,

of the conservation and free flow of water and protection against floods,

of agriculture, fisheries, and marine cultures, freshwater fishing, industry, energy production, transport, tourism, leisure, and water sports, as well as all other legally exercised human activities,

the coherent planning of the use of water resources at both the hydrological watershed level and the national level,

the improvement of the living conditions of the different types of populations, while respecting the balance with the surrounding environment,

the conditions for a rational and sustainable use of water resources for present and future generations,

the establishment of an institutional framework characterized by the redefinition of the role of the stakeholders.

CHAPTER IV – PRINCIPLES

Article 6
This law on the Water Code adheres to the principles accepted in the integrated management of water resources, which are the principles of precaution, prevention, correction, participation, user-pays, polluter-pays, planning, and cooperation.

Article 7
Water is part of the common national heritage.

Its protection, mobilization, and development, in respect of natural balances, are of general interest.

It may only be appropriated under the conditions determined by the provisions of this law.

Article 8
The use of water resources is carried out under the conditions determined by the laws and regulations in force and the provisions of this law on the Water Code, subject to respect for rights previously acquired over the public hydraulic domain as defined in Article 11 of this law and the rights of third parties.

Article 9
The management and development of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works must involve at all levels:

planners, decision-makers, and specialists in the matter,

operators,

users.

Article 10
The existence of sacred waters is tolerated. However, their use must be in conformity with the general interest and meet the imperatives of maintaining and strengthening the cohesion of the social group and national unity.

TITLE II – LEGAL REGIME OF WATER, DEVELOPMENTS, AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

CHAPTER I – COMMON PROVISIONS

Article 11
The following are part of the public hydraulic domain, within the meaning of this law on the Water Code:

A - Water resources, including:

the waters of the territorial sea,

navigable or floatable watercourses within the limits determined by the high-water mark before overflowing, as well as a 25-meter wide right-of-way from these limits on each bank and on each side of the islands,

non-navigable and non-floatable springs and watercourses within the limits determined by the high-water mark before overflowing,

lakes, ponds, and lagoons within the limits determined by the level of the highest waters before overflowing, with a 25-meter wide zone from these limits on each outer bank and on each side of the islands,

underground aquifers.

B - Hydraulic developments and works installed on the public domain, including:

navigation canals and their towpaths, irrigation and drainage canals, and aqueducts executed for a public utility purpose, as well as the appurtenances of these works,

water pipes, sewer pipes, ports and roadsteads, maritime and fluvial dikes, lighting and beaconing works, as well as their appurtenances,

works declared of public utility for the use of hydraulic forces.

Article 12
Withdrawals from the waters of the public hydraulic domain and the realization of hydraulic developments or works are subject, as the case may be, to authorization or prior declaration.

Article 13
Any authorization must:

preserve the national heritage,

take into account previously established rights and uses,

reconcile the interests of the various categories of users.

Article 14
Authorization is granted, subject to the rights of third parties, for a determined period and, where applicable, after a public inquiry.

Article 15
The authorization may be withdrawn or modified with compensation:

in the interest of public health, and particularly when this withdrawal or modification is necessary for the supply of potable water,

to prevent or stop floods or in case of a threat to public safety,

in case of a major threat to the aquatic environment, and particularly when the environments are subject to critical hydraulic conditions incompatible with their preservation.

The authorization may be withdrawn at any time, without compensation, after formal notice has been sent in writing to the interested party:

if the purpose for which it was granted has not seen a commencement of execution within a period of two years,

when the works or installations are abandoned or are no longer subject to regular maintenance,

in case of non-observance of the conditions prescribed in the authorization.

Article 16
Any refusal, withdrawal, or modification of an authorization must be justified.

A decree issued in application of this law on the Water Code shall determine the conditions for granting, modifying, renewing, and withdrawing authorizations, and the thresholds relating to the flow rates withdrawn from the public hydraulic domain.

Article 17
The right to use water and the use of hydraulic developments and works are limited by the obligation not to infringe upon the rights of riparians and to return the water in such a way that it can be reused.

Article 18
Any operation or installation relating to the use of water resources for a general interest purpose encumbers intermediate lands with a servitude of passage, installation, support, and circulation, in accordance with the laws and regulations in force.

Article 19
Hydraulic developments and works must include devices that maintain a minimum quantity of water that permanently guarantees the life, circulation, and reproduction of species.

Article 20
In the event of water accumulation on private property, the operator of the property may be required to declare its capacity, nature, and purpose.

The conditions for the artificial accumulation of water on private properties are established by regulation.

CHAPTER II – THE REGIME OF WATERS

Article 21
Water resources include:

atmospheric or meteoric waters,

surface waters,

groundwater,

the waters of the territorial sea.

Section I: Atmospheric or meteoric waters

Article 22
Atmospheric or meteoric waters belong to the person who receives them on their property. They have the right to use and dispose of them.

Article 23
The artificial accumulation of water falling on private property is authorized provided that:

this water remains on this property,

its use complies with the prescriptions enacted by the laws and regulations in force.

Article 24
In accordance with the laws and regulations in force, every property owner must establish roofs or works in such a way that rainwater flows onto their land or onto the public way.

Section II: Surface waters and groundwater

Article 25
No one shall impede the free flow of surface and groundwater.

Article 26
Spring waters may be used by the person who has a spring on their private land, subject to compliance with the provisions of Articles 17, 18, and 32 of this law on the Water Code.

Section III: Sacred waters

Article 27
The management of sacred waters is ensured by the concerned community under the control of the State.

Article 28
The use of sacred waters must reconcile:

the imperatives of preserving the national heritage,

respect for the rights of third parties,

the concern for preserving and strengthening the cohesion of the social group and national unity.

CHAPTER III – APPLICABLE REGIME FOR DEVELOPMENTS AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

Article 29
Hydraulic developments and works subject to the authorization regime are the subject of a prior environmental impact assessment.

Article 30
The location, realization, and operation of hydraulic developments and works are subject, as the case may be, to authorization or prior declaration, in accordance with the provisions of Articles 31 and 32 of this law on the Water Code.

The implementation is preceded by the intervention:

of a hydrology or hydrogeology expert for the works and hydraulic developments subject to authorization,

of the services of the Water Authority and the competent Ministries for the hydraulic developments and works subject to declaration.

Article 31
The following are subject to prior authorization: installations, developments, works, and activities likely to hinder navigation, to present dangers to health and public safety, to harm the free flow of water, to degrade the quality and quantity of water resources, to significantly increase the risk of flooding, to seriously harm the quality or diversity of the aquatic environment.

The following are subject to prior declaration: installations, works, and activities which, while not likely to present such dangers, must nevertheless respect the prescriptions enacted by the legislation in force.

Article 32
Any development or work for the deviation or derivation of a water resource that deprives other users of its normal enjoyment is prohibited.

Article 33
Any operator of a hydraulic development or work must notify, in writing, the competent Authority of:

important events and accidents that have occurred,

a change of operator,

the cessation of activity.

TITLE III – PROTECTION REGIME FOR WATER, DEVELOPMENTS, AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

CHAPTER I – COMMON PROVISIONS

Article 34
The protection of water resources, hydraulic developments, and works is ensured by means of:

police measures,

standards,

protection perimeters,

classification and declassification measures,

the public utility regime.

Article 35
Any activity likely to degrade water resources, hydraulic developments, and works is subject to regulatory measures by the competent authority.

Article 36
In order to protect water resources, hydraulic developments, and works, standards and protection perimeters are established.

Article 37
The standards as specified in the preceding article are:

water resources quality standards,

discharge standards,

design, implementation, and protection standards for hydraulic developments and works.

These standards are determined according to the different uses, taking into account in particular:

the most recent scientific data in the matter,

the state of the receiving environment,

the self-purification capacity of the water,

the imperatives of national economic and social development,

financial viability constraints.

These standards are established by regulation.

Article 38
The protection perimeter, as a measure of public health, is mandatory.

There are three types of protection perimeters:

the immediate protection perimeter,

the close protection perimeter,

the remote protection perimeter.

The limits of these perimeters are determined by decree. They may be modified if new circumstances so require.

Article 39
Any activity other than that for which the immediate protection perimeter has been defined is prohibited.

Article 40
No underground work, no sounding may be carried out within the protection perimeter without prior authorization from the competent Authority.

Article 41
The discharge of wastewater into the public sanitation network must not harm either the management of this network or the conservation of water, developments, and hydraulic works.

Article 42
Water resources, hydraulic developments, and works may, for the purpose of general interest:

be the subject of classification or declassification measures,

be recognized as being of public utility.

A decree shall determine the conditions and modalities for classification, declassification, and the granting of public utility status.

CHAPTER II – PROTECTION OF WATERS

Article 43
The protection of water resources is ensured both quantitatively and qualitatively by the institution of specific standards.

Article 44
Sacred waters are protected by those to whom the community has conferred this power and who exercise it in the interest of the community under the control of the State.

They may, if the interest justifies it, be the subject of special protection measures.

Section I: Quantitative protection

Article 45
Any wastage of water is prohibited.

The authority may, by regulation, determine the conditions to be imposed on individuals, public and private networks, and installations in order to avoid this wastage.

Article 46
In the parts of the national territory where water resources are scarce and/or threatened, the Administration is empowered to enact stricter regulations to take account of this situation.

Section II: Qualitative protection

Article 47
The withdrawal points for water intended for human consumption must be surrounded by a protection perimeter. It is forbidden within these protection perimeters to carry out any act or activity of a polluting nature.

Article 48
Discharges, deposits of waste of any nature, or of radioactive effluents likely to cause or increase the pollution of water resources are prohibited.

Article 49
Any discharge of wastewater into the receiving environment must comply with the standards in force.

Article 50
The use of explosives, drugs, or toxic products as bait in surface waters, which are likely to harm the quality of the aquatic environment, is prohibited.

Article 51
It is forbidden to discharge into the sea, watercourses, lakes, lagoons, ponds, canals, groundwater, on their banks, and in alluvial plains, any used material, any fermentable residue of plant or animal origin, any solid or liquid, toxic or flammable substance likely to constitute a danger or a cause of insalubrity, to cause a fire or an explosion.

CHAPTER III – PROTECTION OF DEVELOPMENTS AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

Article 52
It is forbidden, except in cases of force majeure:

to degrade, destroy, or remove hydraulic developments and works,

to damage the temporary works carried out for the construction or maintenance of those referred to above.

Article 53
Classified or unclassified installations, developments or works, and sources of pollution are subject to an ecological audit under the conditions specified by decree.

The results of the ecological audit are transmitted to the competent authority and are communicable to third parties.

Article 54
Hydraulic developments and works of national interest, the list of which is determined by decree, are subject to special protection measures.

To this end, the Water Authority may, in agreement with the Ministries in charge of Defense and Security, have this protection ensured by the public forces.

TITLE IV – MANAGEMENT OF WATER, DEVELOPMENTS, AND HYDRAULULIC WORKS

CHAPTER I – THE INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK

Article 55
The national policy for the management of water, developments, and hydraulic works is defined by a decree issued in the Council of Ministers.

Article 56
The Water Authority ensures the implementation of this policy.

In this capacity, it receives the declarations and prior authorization requests relating to the use of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works.

It exercises its prerogatives jointly, and as the case may be, with the competent Ministries.

Article 57
A decree issued in the Council of Ministers defines the structures responsible for the management of water resources based on the principle of management by hydrological watershed, and determines the rules relating to the organization, attributions, and functioning of these structures.

Article 58
Under the terms of this law, the institutional framework is based on a principle characterized by the distinction between the manager and the different users of water.

Section I: The role of the manager

Article 59
The State ensures the management of water resources by preserving the quality of the sources, preventing waste, and guaranteeing availability.

Article 60
The State guarantees:

the supply of potable water,

the protection, conservation, and integrated management of water resources,

the satisfaction of other needs.
The State ensures:

the development and protection of hydraulic developments and works,

the prevention and control of waterborne diseases.

It exercises, through its competent services, the policing of water.

Section II: The rights and obligations of users

Article 61
The recognition of previously acquired rights over the public hydraulic domain is carried out with due diligence and by the care of the administration or at the request of the interested parties after a public inquiry, under conditions determined by regulation.

Article 62
Authorizations and concessions for water withdrawal, as well as acts recognizing acquired rights over water, may be registered in the land register.

Article 63
Owners whose rights have been duly recognized may only be dispossessed by way of expropriation. This measure shall only be taken under the conditions provided for by the laws and regulations in force.

Article 64
Any person who has knowledge of an incident or an accident presenting a danger to civil security, the quality, circulation, or conservation of water resources must inform the competent Authority thereof as soon as possible.

The competent Authority shall inform the populations by all appropriate means of the circumstances of the incident or accident, its effects, and the measures taken or to be taken to remedy it.

Article 65
Any person who is the source of an incident or an accident, and any operator or owner are required, as the case may be, as soon as they become aware of it, to take or have taken all possible measures to stop the danger or the harm to the environment. They must also take all necessary measures to remedy it.

Article 66
The competent Authority shall prescribe to the persons concerned the measures to be taken to put an end to the damage observed or to limit its seriousness, and in particular the analyses to be carried out.

Article 67
In case of default or if there is a risk of pollution or destruction of the natural environment, or for public health and the supply of potable water, the Authority may take or have executed the necessary measures at the expense of the responsible persons.

Article 68
Without prejudice to the compensation of victims for other damages suffered, the persons who have intervened materially or financially have the right to reimbursement, by the person or persons responsible for the incident or accident, of the costs incurred by them. To this end, they may seize the competent jurisdictions.

Article 69
The occupants of a watershed or the users of water may form an association for the protection of water resources and hydraulic works.

CHAPTER II – ORDERS OF PRIORITY

Article 70
The supply of water to populations remains, in all cases, the priority element in the distribution of water resources.

The allocation of water resources must, at all times, take into account the social and economic needs of the populations.

Article 71
When human needs for water have been met, the distribution of resources is carried out according to other uses.

Article 72
In the event of a conflict for the satisfaction of one or the other of the uses, other than human consumption, the distribution shall be made by the competent Authority.

Article 73
Decrees, issued in the Council of Ministers, shall establish the regimes and conditions for the use of waters other than those intended for human consumption.

Article 74
The order of priority may be temporarily modified when certain exceptional events occur, such as cases of force majeure, drought, and flooding.

Article 75
The Water Authority and the competent Ministries may entrust, to any natural or legal person, the public service of operating the waters, works, and hydraulic developments.

These modes of operation are approved, as the case may be, by decree issued in the Council of Ministers.

Article 76
The concession contract may confer on the beneficiary the right:

to establish, after approval of the projects by the conceding authority, all useful works,

to occupy the parts of the public domain necessary for its installations.

Article 77
Without prejudice to the particular clauses appearing in the concession contract, the forfeiture of the concessionaire may be pronounced for:

use of water different from that authorized or outside the fixed zone of use,

non-payment or non-remittance of fees,

non-compliance with obligations of a sanitary nature, particularly in the case of thermal springs.

In case of forfeiture of the concessionaire, the Water Authority and the competent Ministries may order the restoration of the site to its original state and, where applicable, have it carried out ex officio at the expense of the dispossessed concessionaire.

Section I: Consumption waters

Article 78
Water intended for human consumption must comply with the potability standards established by a joint order of the Water Authority and the Minister in charge of Health.

Article 79
Anyone who offers water to the public for human consumption, whether for a fee or free of charge and in whatever form, including food ice, is required to ensure that this water is potable and complies with the standards in force.

Article 80
The use of water for the preparation and consumption of any foodstuff and merchandise intended for both human and animal consumption must meet the standards of hygiene and public health.

Article 81
In areas provided with a public water distribution service, it is forbidden for natural or legal persons, and in particular for restaurateurs and hoteliers, to supply for consumption and for all uses related to food, any water other than the potable water provided by the aforementioned services.

Article 82
The use of private wells and springs is only authorized for human consumption if the water from them is potable, and if all precautions are taken to protect this water from any contamination due, in particular, to the proximity of latrines, manure deposits, garbage, filth, and cemeteries.

The water from these wells must constantly present the potability qualities required by the regulations and standards in force.

Article 83
In an area served by a potable water adduction network, the use of well water for human consumption may be prohibited.

Article 84
Any method of water correction or any recourse to a mode of treatment of these waters with the aid of chemical additives must be previously authorized under the conditions established by regulation. Any additives must in no case harm the potability of the water or alter its organoleptic properties.

Article 85
The measures intended to prevent the pollution of consumption waters are prescribed by a joint order of the Water Authority and the competent Ministries.

Section II: Mineral waters

Article 86
The surveillance and control of installation operations relating to the conservation, development of mineral waters, spring waters, and table waters, and even their packaging, are exercised by the competent services.

Article 87
Water sources such as those mentioned in Article 86 above may be declared of public interest by a decree issued in the Council of Ministers.

Article 88
The exportation, importation, and marketing of natural mineral waters and table waters are subject to a prior authorization issued jointly by the Water Authority and the competent Ministries.

Section III: Waters used for agro-pastoral, industrial purposes and for the satisfaction of other needs

Article 89
The use of water for agro-pastoral, industrial purposes, and for the satisfaction of other needs, particularly fishing, leisure, and transport, requires servitudes and must respect the texts and standards in force as well as the imperatives set forth in this law on the Water Code.

CHAPTER III – PLANNING AND COOPERATION

Article 90
Under the terms of this law on the Water Code, it is provided for:

the inventory of water resources, hydraulic developments, and works,

the development of a national network for collecting data on water resources, hydraulic developments, and works,

the establishment or institution of water quality objectives,

the Master Plans for the Development and Management of Water Resources (SDAGRE),

the institution of systems, zones, and an alert plan.

Article 91
An inventory of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works is carried out, according to a periodicity to be determined by decree.

This inventory is established under the direction of the Water Authority in collaboration with the competent Ministries and the various users.

Article 92
The inventory of water resources, hydraulic developments, and works must lead to the development of a short, medium, and long-term action plan.

Article 93
The data and information collected and elaborated by the water management structures must be communicated to the Water Authority.

Article 94
The Master Plans for the Development and Management of Water Resources (SDAGRE) are carried out by watershed or group of hydrological watersheds.

Article 95
The Master Plans for the Development and Management of Water Resources (SDAGRE) establish for each watershed or group of watersheds the global orientations for the integrated management of water resources.

They define the quality and quantity objectives for the waters, aquatic ecosystems, and wetlands, as well as the hydraulic developments and works to be realized.

Article 96
The project of the Master Plans for the Development and Management of Water Resources (SDAGRE) is elaborated by the national Water Authority.

After a public inquiry, it is submitted for advice to the basin committee comprising, in particular, representatives of the State, Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs), local elected officials, users, operators, and specialists in the matter.

Article 97
The Master Plans for the Development and Management of Water Resources (SDAGRE) are supplemented by Master Plans for the Development and Improvement of Hydraulic Works (PDADOH).

Article 98
In the event of a drought or an accident likely to cause a water shortage or a flood, the competent authorities are empowered to take all measures for the storage or withdrawal of water.

In these cases, an alert zone may be established, setting out the measures to be taken and the uses of water for essential needs.

Article 99
The State shall take the necessary measures to promote cooperation in the management and development of shared water resources with neighboring States.

This cooperation aims to ensure:

the exchange of information on all situations, particularly critical situations,

the implementation of joint projects and of bilateral and multilateral water management structures,

the integrated management of shared water resources.

CHAPTER IV - FINANCIAL MECHANISMS

Section I: Fees and premiums

Article 100
Any natural or legal person using the waters of the public hydraulic domain is subject to the payment of a fee, under the conditions established by this law on the Water Code and its implementing texts.

The State sets the fees.

It may allocate premiums for all activities aimed at a better exploitation of water, developments, and hydraulic works.

Article 101
The fees as provided for in the article referred to above are:

a fee related to quality,

a fee related to the quantity withdrawn,

a fee related to the use of the motive power of water,

a fee related to the use of water,

a fee related to the mobilization of water resources.

The competent Authority may, as needed, define other types of fees.

Article 102
The basis, rate, and method of collection of the fees are established in accordance with the legislation in force.

Article 103
The modes of concession as referred to in Article 75 above give rise, as the case may be, to the collection of fees.

Article 104
The conditions for the allocation of premiums are established by regulation.

Section II: The fund for the management of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works.

Article 105
A fund for the management of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works is created, intended to ensure the financing of the activities of:

integrated management of water resources,

planning and inventory of water resources, hydraulic developments, and works,

protection of water resources,

sanitary surveillance,

development, maintenance, and operation of hydraulic developments and works.

A decree issued in the Council of Ministers shall determine the rules of organization and functioning of the fund.

Article 106
The fund for the management of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works is financed by:

subsidies from the State,

fees,

the proceeds of transactions,

other liberalities.

TITLE V - WATER POLICING, INFRACTIONS, AND SANCTIONS

CHAPTER I – INVESTIGATION OF INFRACTIONS

Article 107
The following are responsible for investigating infractions of the provisions of this law on the Water Code and the texts issued for its application, for gathering evidence, and for identifying the authors:

Judicial police officers and agents,

Civil servants and agents of the various competent services.

Article 108
The civil servants and agents referred to in the article above shall take an oath before the court of first instance or the section of the court of the administrative district.

Article 109
For the purpose of identifying and investigating infractions, sworn civil servants and agents have access to the premises, installations, and places where the operations giving rise to the infractions are carried out. The owners and operators are required to grant them access.

Article 110
In the exercise of their functions, sworn civil servants and agents may request the assistance of the public force.

Article 111
Infractions of the provisions of this law on the Water Code and the texts issued for its application are established by official reports that are authoritative until proven otherwise.

Article 112
The investigation report includes, in particular, the identity of the offender, the circumstances and place of the infraction, the explanations of the presumed author, and the elements establishing the materiality of the infractions.

Article 113
Certain infractions, the list of which is determined by decree, may give rise to transactions. These are carried out by the Water Authority in liaison with the competent Ministries.

Article 114
In the event of the failure of the transaction or for serious infractions, the list of which is established by decree, the official reports must be addressed within fifteen (15) clear days following the finding to the Public Prosecutor or to the judge of the competent court section.

CHAPTER II – SANCTIONS

Article 115
In the event of a flagrant infraction of the provisions of this law on the Water Code, sworn civil servants and agents must have the work stopped and confiscate the objects that were used to commit the infraction.

Article 116
Any owner of an upstream property who, through specific works or developments, aggravates the servitude of water flow shall be punished by imprisonment of six (6) days to two (2) months and a fine of fifty thousand (50,000) to three hundred thousand (300,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.

Article 117
Anyone who withdraws water from the public domain, in excessive quantity, without authorization or prior declaration is liable to imprisonment of two (2) to six (6) months and a fine of three hundred and sixty thousand (360,000) francs to five million (5,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.
In case of a repeat offense, the penalty will be doubled.

Article 118
Anyone who:

continues an operation or the exploitation of an installation or a work without complying with the formal notice, at the end of a period fixed by the technical prescriptions contained in the authorization or the regulations issued in application of this law on the Water Code,

operates an installation or carries out works in violation of a measure to decommission, withdraw, or suspend an authorization or to suppress an installation or a measure of prohibition pronounced in application of this law on the Water Code,
shall be punished by imprisonment of two (2) months to two (2) years and a fine of one million (1,000,000) to five million (5,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.

Article 119
Anyone who undertakes underground work or a sounding within the protection perimeter without prior authorization is liable to imprisonment of one (1) month to six (6) months and a fine of five hundred thousand (500,000) francs to ten (10) million francs, or by only one of these two penalties.

Article 120
Any wastage of water is liable to imprisonment of one (1) month to six (6) months and a fine of three hundred and sixty thousand (360,000) francs to ten (10) million francs, or by only one of these two penalties.

Article 121
Anyone who engages in an activity likely to degrade the quality of water, developments, and hydraulic works is liable to imprisonment of six (6) months to two (2) years and a fine of one (1) million to one hundred million (100,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.

Article 122
Anyone who throws, discharges, or allows to flow into surface waters, groundwater, or the waters of the sea within the limits of the territorial waters, directly or indirectly, any waste or substances whose action or reactions have even temporarily caused harmful effects on health or damage to flora or fauna or significant modifications to the normal flow regime of the waters, is punished by imprisonment of two (2) months to two (2) years and a fine of two million (2,000,000) to one hundred million (100,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.

Article 123
Anyone who uses explosives, drugs, or toxic products in surface waters as bait, which are likely to harm the quality of the aquatic environment, is liable to imprisonment of two (2) to six (6) months and a fine of three hundred and sixty thousand (360,000) francs to one million (1,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two (2) penalties.

Article 124
Anyone who damages hydraulic developments or works by any means whatsoever is liable to imprisonment of two (2) months to two (2) years and a fine of five hundred thousand (500,000) francs to one hundred million (100,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two (2) penalties.

Article 125
Anyone who uses well water for human consumption in an area served by a potable water adduction network in case of prohibition is liable to imprisonment of six (6) days to one (1) month and a fine of fifty thousand (50,000) francs to three hundred thousand (300,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.
The judge may order the destruction of the well.

Article 126
Anyone who imports, exports, or markets mineral or table waters not in conformity with the standards in force is liable to imprisonment of two (2) months to one (1) year and a fine of five hundred thousand (500,000) francs to ten million (10,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.
The judge may order the confiscation and destruction of these products.

Article 127
Anyone who offers water to the public for human or animal consumption, whether free of charge or for a fee and in whatever form, that does not conform to the standards of hygiene and public health, is liable to imprisonment of two (2) months to two (2) years and a fine of three hundred and sixty thousand (360,000) francs to two million (2,000,000) francs, or by only one of these two penalties.

TITLE VI – TRANSITIONAL AND DIVERSE PROVISIONS

Article 128
Existing works must be brought into conformity within a period of two (2) years from the entry into force of this law on the Water Code.

Article 129
Subject to the elaboration of standards as provided for in this law on the Water Code, the standards in force are those of the World Health Organization (WHO).

Article 130
Industrial boreholes are subject to the provisions of Law No. 95-533 of 18 July 1995 on the Mining Code. The same applies to soundings and underground works.

Article 131
The provisions of Articles 117, 118, and 133 of the Penal Code relating to extenuating circumstances and suspended sentences are not applicable to the infractions provided for by Articles 121, 122, 123, 124, 125, and 126 of this law on the Water Code.

Article 132
The modalities of application of this law on the Water Code shall be determined by decrees issued in the Council of Ministers.

Article 133
This law on the Water Code repeals all prior contrary provisions.

Article 134
This law shall be published in the Official Journal of the Republic of Côte d'Ivoire and executed as a law of the State.

Done and adopted in public session
Abidjan, 16 December 1998
