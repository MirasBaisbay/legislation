Presidency of the Republic
Laws

The President of the Republic

Based on the provisions of the Constitution and Law No. /31/, and upon the approval of the People's Assembly in its session held on 17/9/1426 H, corresponding to 20/10/2005 AD,

Issues the following:

**Water Legislation**

**Chapter One**
**- Definitions -**

**Article - 1-** The following expressions contained in this Law shall have the meanings indicated next to each:
*   **Higher Water Committee:** A supreme national body that formulates the state's water policy for the short, medium, and long term, and approves the water strategy to achieve this policy.
*   **Chairman of the Higher Water Committee:** The Prime Minister or his authorized representative.
*   **Basin Management Committee:** A committee that implements the water policy established by the Higher Water Committee within the framework of the relevant basin to preserve water resources from depletion and pollution and to achieve the principle of sustainability.
*   **The Ministry:** The Ministry of Irrigation.
*   **The Minister:** The Minister of Irrigation.
*   **Ministry-Affiliated Body:** The public corporations and general directorates of irrigation for the water basins.
*   **The Corporation:** The entity responsible for the utilization of drinking water and sanitation, meaning the Public Corporation for Drinking Water and Sanitation and its affiliated companies and units in the governorates.
*   **Water Users' Association:** Legal, non-profit entities with corporate personality, formed by farmers or water beneficiaries within a specific hydrological unit, a known agricultural project, an independent irrigation system, or a geographical area with a recognized legal character.
*   **Association's Area of Operation:** A geographical area with a recognized legal character, to be determined by the Ministry.
*   **Hydrological Basin:** The catchment basin defined by surface water divides.
*   **Water Resource:** Traditional and non-traditional waters:
    1.  **Traditional Waters:** Surface water and groundwater:
        a.  **Surface Water:** Water of rivers, inland lakes, streams, torrents, marshes, salt flats, ponds, and pools.
        b.  **Groundwater:** Water from springs and aquifers.
    2.  **Non-traditional Waters:** Treated sewage and industrial wastewater, agricultural drainage water, in addition to desalinated water that meets the required standard specifications.
*   **Watercourse:** A river, stream, canal, drain, *fajjara* (underground channel), wadi, or torrent.
*   **Public Waters:** Any water resource or any water that is for public use for any purpose whatsoever.

*   **Standard Specifications:** The adopted standard water criteria issued by the competent authority.
*   **Water Pollution:** The presence of materials or pollutants in water that, directly or indirectly, alters its physical, chemical, biological, or bacteriological properties from the criteria adopted in the standard specifications.
*   **Polluter:** A natural or legal person, whether a public or private entity, that causes the pollution of water resources by any direct or indirect means.
*   **Protected Zone (Haram):** The designated area necessary to protect a water resource, for which specific conditions are established to preserve the integrity of the water resource and protect it from all forms of depletion and pollution, and for the implementation of maintenance and monitoring works. It is divided into:
    1.  **Direct Protected Zone:** The land immediately surrounding the water resource that allows access for its maintenance, preservation of its integrity, and prevention of its pollution.
    2.  **Indirect Protected Zone:** The lands surrounding the direct protected zone of water resources where the execution of certain works is prohibited or their use is restricted for reasons of public interest.
*   **Dam:** An artificial barrier that impounds water for the purpose of utilizing it for drinking, irrigation, industry, power generation, flood control, watercourse regulation, filtration, livestock watering, animal husbandry, fish farming, tourism, or climate moderation.

**Article - 2-** The following are considered public water properties:
1.  **Internal Waters, including:**
    a.  Watercourses of any kind within their defined boundaries, marked by the line of their high water level when full before overflowing.
    b.  Lakes, inland salt flats, pools, ponds, and marshes within their boundaries, defined by the highest level the water reaches before overflowing.
    c.  Waterfalls.
    d.  Groundwater that can be drawn from wells and all aquifers.
    e.  Springs of any kind.
    f.  Water in dam reservoirs and water installations up to their maximum storage level.
    g.  Water in irrigation, drainage, and drinking water networks.
    h.  Non-traditional waters.
2.  **Marine Waters, including:**
    a.  Seawater within the territorial limits of the Syrian Arab Republic and its derivative salt lakes, ponds, marshes, and pools, as well as ports, marine basins, bays, navigation channels, and their entire banks.
    b.  Waters resulting from the mixing of internal waters with seawater at the mouths of rivers, shores, streams, and sub-seabed waters.
3.  **The Seashore** up to the furthest point reached by waves on land, as determined by the plans approved by the Ministry of Transport.
4.  Syria's share of joint international watercourses within the borders of the Syrian Arab Republic.
5.  The direct protected zone of water resources and major public water installations, which shall not be less than six meters.

**Article - 3-**
1.  The Minister shall form a Committee for the Determination of Protected Zones for Water Resources, composed of representatives from the concerned authorities.
2.  Subject to the provisions of Law No. /10/ of 1989, the Minister shall, upon the proposal of the Committee for the Determination of Protected Zones for Water Resources, issue a decision specifying the following:
    a.  The determination of the direct protected zone for the public water properties listed in Article 2. It is forbidden to erect any building, establish any installation, or place any occupancy within the direct protected zone, with the exception of installations, buildings, and occupancies specific to public waters.
    b.  The determination of the indirect protected zone for water resources.
    c.  The determination of economic, tourist, agricultural, and residential activities in the indirect protected zone, and the conditions that must be met for these activities in accordance with the laws, regulations, and standard specifications in force.

**Chapter Three**
**- Affirmation of Acquired Rights to Public Waters -**

**Article - 4-** Without prejudice to previously affirmed acquired rights to public waters prior to the entry into force of this Law, the rights of persons who have acquired rights, rights of disposal, or usufructuary rights to public waters by custom or under legal deeds, and which have not been affirmed, shall be affirmed in accordance with the provisions included in this chapter from Article -5- to Article -13-. The utilization of such rights shall be subject to the provisions of this Law.

**Article - 5-** The process of affirming the rights mentioned in Article /4/ shall be initiated by a decision issued by the Minister, which shall include the following:
1.  The public waters to be covered by the affirmation process.
2.  The location for accepting applications from claimants of said rights, and the specified period for their submission, which shall not be less than sixty days from the date of the decision's publication.

**Article - 6-** The decision mentioned in the preceding article shall be published in a local newspaper and in a newspaper in the capital. It shall also be displayed on the notice boards at the governorate, district, and sub-district centers, and at farmers' associations and water users' associations. It shall also be circulated to the mukhtars of the villages covered by the affirmation process.

**Article - 7-**
1.  The process of affirming unconfirmed acquired rights to public waters shall be carried out by a committee formed within each of the public bodies affiliated with the Ministry by a decision of the Minister. The committee shall be chaired by a judge with the rank of counselor, appointed by the Minister of Justice, and shall include representatives from the concerned authorities as members.
2.  The members of the committee shall take the following oath before its chairman: "I swear by Almighty God to perform my duty with honesty and integrity."
3.  The chairman and members of the committee shall be granted compensation by a decision of the Prime Minister based on the Minister's proposal.

**Article - 8-** Any person claiming a right to public waters in the area specified by the decision provided for in Article /5/ of this Law must submit an application, supported by documents and records, within the specified period to the public body affiliated with the Ministry, otherwise their application shall not be considered.

**Article - 9-** The committee shall receive the applications and documents submitted by the claimants of the rights. It shall verify the documents and may seek the opinions and observations of persons and entities it deems capable of providing the necessary clarifications. The committee shall prepare a preliminary report attached to a schedule containing the names of the rights holders and the nature and extent of these rights.

**Article - 10-** The schedule mentioned in the preceding article shall be announced in the hall of the public body affiliated with the Ministry for a period of two months and published in a local newspaper, if any, and in a newspaper in the capital. This announcement shall be considered personal notification to all holders of rights to public waters in the area of the affirmation process.

**Article - 11-**
a.  Persons who submitted affirmation applications within the specified period have the right to submit their observations

**Article - 13-** When cadastral survey and registration works are conducted, the acquired rights to public waters shall be recorded in the land registry records in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Chapter Four**
**- Liquidation of Acquired Rights to Public Waters -**

**Article - 14-** When public interest necessitates the liquidation of acquired rights to public waters, a decision shall be issued by the Prime Minister upon the proposal of the Minister. The decision shall declare the existence of public utility based on justifying reasons, and this decision shall be final and not subject to any means of appeal or review. Its issuance shall be based on a plan showing the water resource and the real estate area covered by the liquidation process.

**Article - 15-** The Ministry shall announce the commencement of the liquidation of acquired rights in the halls of the governorate, district, and sub-district covered by the liquidation process, as well as in a local newspaper and a newspaper in the capital.

**Article - 16-**
a.  The Minister shall form a committee to assess the value of the acquired rights specified in Chapter Three of this Law, based on the principles established by the Ministry and the Ministry of Agriculture and Agrarian Reform.
b.  The Ministry shall notify all rights holders of the values assessed by the committee in accordance with the notification provisions contained in the Code of Civil Procedure. At the same time, it shall issue a public invitation to review the preliminary assessment records within a period of thirty days. This announcement shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Article /15/ of this Law.

**Article - 17-** All persons who submitted their applications in accordance with the provisions of Article /16/ of this Law have the right to submit their objections to the preliminary assessment within thirty days from the day following the date of the announcement or notification, whichever is later. The assessed values not objected to within the specified period shall be final. An objection by one of the partners in a right shall be considered an objection by the remaining partners.

**Article - 18-**
1.  The Minister shall form a committee to rule on objections submitted by the holders of acquired rights. The committee shall be chaired by a judge with the rank of counselor, appointed by the Minister of Justice, and shall include representatives from the concerned authorities and representatives of the holders of acquired rights as members.
2.  The chairman and members of the committee shall be granted compensation by a decision of the Prime Minister based on the Minister's proposal.
3.  The members of the committee shall take the oath provided for in paragraph /2/ of Article /7/ of this Law before its chairman.

**Article - 19-** The values of the acquired rights shall be paid to their holders in accordance with the provisions of Article /25/ of the Expropriation Law issued by Legislative Decree No. /20/ of 1983 and its amendments.

**Chapter Five**
**- Government Water Networks -**

**Article - 20-** Government water networks consist of the channels and pipelines, whether open or covered, intended for the transport, distribution, and drainage of water, as well as their appurtenant installations, which are established by the state and whose operation and maintenance it supervises, taking into consideration

the duties and tasks of water users' associations as per Chapter Ten of this Law.

**Article - 21-** The utilization and maintenance of water resources, water networks, watercourses, and dams shall be carried out in accordance with instructions issued by the Ministry or the competent Ministry.

**Article - 22-**
1.  Lands benefiting from irrigation networks are entitled to use the network's water according to distribution schedules, water quotas, and the instructions issued by the Ministry.
2.  The use of sewage and agricultural drainage water for irrigation is prohibited unless a prior license is obtained from the Ministry, based on the quantity and quality of water in the drain.

**Chapter Six**
**- Licenses for Well Drilling and Pumping Equipment -**

**Article - 23-** The utilization of public waters by public and private entities for any purpose whatsoever is subject to a prior license, with the exception of emergency cases for wells used for drinking water, provided that the Ministry is informed during drilling. These cases shall be specified in the implementing regulations.

**Article - 24-**
a.  The Minister shall issue regulations governing the granting of licenses for the utilization of public waters and the drilling of wells for various purposes, in coordination with the Ministry of Housing and Construction and the Ministry of Agriculture and Agrarian Reform. Priority in granting licenses shall be given to drinking water and public uses.
b.  The Minister's preliminary approval shall serve as an order to commence drilling for drinking water wells, provided that the licensing procedures are completed subsequently.

**Article - 25-** The Ministry, upon the request of the entity seeking a license, shall grant a license to drill one or more wells and provide it with technical assistance, all within the available water resources in each basin, on the condition that modern irrigation methods are used for agricultural purposes and that water use is rationalized for other purposes.

**Article - 26-**
1.  Licenses for well drilling shall be valid for a period of one year from the date of issuance, with the exception of public wells designated for drinking water belonging to the Corporation.
2.  An applicant for a license to use water from a well must submit the license application during the validity period of the well drilling license.
3.  The conditions for granting and using licenses, the application forms, the data to be attached, and the manner of their review and granting shall be determined by a decision of the Minister or his authorized representative.
4.  All persons, bodies, administrations, and institutions that obtain information useful for the study of water resources during exploration, geological, hydrogeological, or geophysical surveys, or other works, must deposit a copy thereof with the Ministry to be used when needed. The Ministry shall prepare a database divided into two levels: the first, whose circulation is permitted with the Minister's approval, and the second, whose use is permitted for interested parties and all those working in the field of public waters.

**Article - 27-** The entity applying for a license must be the owner of the property, a usufructuary thereof, or a lessee thereof. A majority of shares is sufficient, whether they are owners, usufructuaries, possessors, or their authorized agents or representatives. The Corporation shall be exempt from this requirement.

**Article - 28-** The Minister shall issue a decision determining:
1.  The water allocation that may be licensed based on the available water resources in each basin and sub-basin. The quantities of water permitted to be pumped from the water source shall be determined by meters installed on all pumping equipment for agriculture and irrigation purposes.

2.  The modification, whether increase or decrease, of the quantity of water specified in the license from the water source, in proportion to the available water resources in each basin.
3.  The conditions to be imposed on how to access public waters and the conditions for their utilization and protection from depletion and pollution.

**Article - 29-** Licenses for pumping equipment shall be granted for a period of ten years, renewable by a decision of the Minister upon the request of the concerned party and in light of the available water resources. The license shall be considered valid from the date of its issuance. Licenses for drinking water and public uses shall be exempt from this provision.

**Article - 30-** A license shall be considered automatically canceled in one of the following cases:
1.  If its holder does not install the licensed equipment within one year from the date of its issuance, taking into account cases deemed appropriate by the Ministry, which shall be specified in the implementing regulations.
2.  If the license holder fails to pay the fees due on their scheduled dates.

**Article - 31-** Licenses of all types shall be canceled without compensation by a decision of the Minister in the following cases:
1.  Causing harm to public waters in quantity or quality.
2.  Causing harm to third parties, provided that this is judicially proven.
3.  The license holder's second violation of the conditions contained therein.
4.  Diverting the licensed water to a purpose other than that for which it was licensed without prior approval from the Minister.
5.  Inclusion of the land licensed for irrigation within a government irrigation project.
6.  The license holder's violation of the laws and regulations in force concerning water utilization.
7.  If the license was issued based on fraud or granted based on false documents, and the person responsible shall be prosecuted judicially.

**Article - 32-** A license granted in the name of the owner or lessee for the use of public waters in agricultural projects shall remain in effect for the benefit of the properties to which it pertains, regardless of the person to whom ownership or the right of use is transferred.

**Article - 33-** A license must be renewed in the following cases:
1.  Upon the expiration of the previous license's term.
2.  When replacing the pumping equipment with a device of greater capacity.
3.  When adding new areas in excess of the area specified in the well license.

**Article - 34-** A one-time fee of /5,000/ five thousand Syrian pounds shall be imposed on holders of licenses for well drilling and the installation of pumping equipment thereon, for each license separately upon its issuance. This fee shall be reduced by half upon renewal. The fee shall be doubled if the legal renewal period is violated. This fee may be amended by a decision of the Chairman of the Higher Water Committee when necessary.

**Chapter Seven**
**- General Penalties -**

**Article - 35-** Without prejudice to the more severe penalties stipulated in the Penal Code or any other law, the perpetrators of the acts listed below shall be punished with the penalties specified in these paragraphs:
1.  a.  Anyone who intentionally demolishes or wholly or partially sabotages a major irrigation facility, such as dams and pumping stations, or intentionally pollutes water sources, shall be punished with imprisonment from one to three years and a fine of (50,000) fifty thousand to (200,000) two hundred thousand L.S.
    b.  Anyone who intentionally demolishes or wholly or partially sabotages a water utilization facility and its appurtenances, such as culverts and main drinking, irrigation, and drainage channels, shall be punished with imprisonment from six months to one year and a fine of (10,000) ten thousand L.S. to (100,000) one hundred thousand L.S.
    c.  Anyone who intentionally demolishes or wholly or partially sabotages secondary and tertiary irrigation, drainage, and drinking water networks shall be punished with imprisonment from one month to three months and a fine of (3,000) three thousand L.S. to (10,000) ten

    d.  The perpetrator in paragraphs (a), (b), and (c) above shall be obliged to pay for the value of the damages resulting from their act.
2. The compensation shall include the value of what was consumed, as estimated by the Corporation, in addition to the value of the damages incurred by it.
    e.  The Corporation shall receive requests for the settlement of infringements committed before the entry into force of this Law on public drinking water networks within a period of six months beginning from the date this Law enters into force. All infringements that occurred before the adoption of this Law shall be considered settled, criminal prosecution shall be halted, and the execution of any imposed sentence shall be stayed. The Corporation has the right to remove the violation if it cannot be maintained.
5.  Anyone who uses polluted water for irrigation purposes shall be punished with imprisonment from three months to one year or a fine of /25,000/ twenty-five thousand L.S. to /50,000/ fifty thousand L.S.
6.  The manner of disposal of the equipment, machinery, tools, and gear used in committing the violations mentioned in items (2 and 3) above shall be determined by a decision of the Minister.
7.  a.  Any member of the Water Police who facilitates the commission of one of the aforementioned violations, covers it up, or turns a blind eye to its recording or suppression shall be punished with the same penalty stipulated in one of the paragraphs above.
    b.  Employees in all other public entities of the state who issue orders or instructions that have no legal basis and lead to harm to public waters shall be punished with the same penalty stipulated in one of the paragraphs above.

**Chapter Eight**
**- The Water Police -**

**Article - 37-** Employees of the first and second categories working in the field of water resources and their utilization, who are tasked with recording the violations stipulated in this Law, shall have the capacity of the Water Police and shall be appointed by a decision of the Minister.

**Article - 38-** The employees mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall take the oath stipulated in paragraph /2/ of Article /7/ of this Law before the president of the Civil Court of First Instance in the governorate before commencing their work.

**Article - 39-** One or more Water Police units may be formed within the public body affiliated with the Ministry as necessity dictates.

**Article - 40-** The reports drawn up by the Water Police shall have the status of official judicial reports.

**Article - 41-** One or more Water Police units shall be formed in each of the Corporation's establishments and its affiliated units to suppress violations and remove infringements on drinking water and sanitation networks, by a decision of the competent Minister upon the proposal of the Director-General of the Corporation. The members of the Water Police shall take the oath stipulated in paragraph /2/ of Article /7/ of this Law before the president of the Civil Court of First Instance in the governorate before commencing their work. The reports drawn up by them shall have the status of official judicial reports.

**Article - 42-** The reports shall be sent to the Public Prosecution to take the necessary action thereon.

**Chapter Nine**
**- Exploration -**

**Article - 43-** Public entities may conduct exploration on the land of others with the exclusive approval of the Minister in cases of necessity that he deems appropriate, based on indications related to the quality of the land and aquifers, and provided that priority is given to drinking water, after observing the provisions for well drilling licenses contained in Chapter Six of this Law.

**Article - 44-** The entity that conducted the exploration shall be obligated to do the following:
a.  Clear infringements from the exploration site and adjacent land and compensate for damages resulting from the exploration.
b.  Provide compensation for the deprivation of use for the area and the period of time during which use was suspended due to exploration works.
c.  Form a committee by a decision of the competent authorizing officer, composed of at least three members, whose task is to consider compensation for damages resulting from exploration and to estimate the compensation for deprivation of use. The committee's decisions shall become effective after their ratification.
d.  Restore the situation to its previous state if no water is discovered.
e.  Expropriate the area necessary for the public use of the water source if the exploration results in the required quantity of water.

**Chapter Ten**
**- Water Users' Associations -**

**Article - 45-** Associations named "Water Users' Associations" shall be established in the Syrian Arab Republic by a decision of the Minister.

**Article - 46-** Membership in the associations shall be compulsory for beneficiaries of collectively used water resources within the association's area of operation.

**Article - 47-** Water Users' Associations shall be given concessional loans from a public bank, granted once upon establishment. The Minister of Finance shall issue the special instructions for granting these loans and the method of their repayment.

**Article - 48-** The Minister shall issue the implementing regulations, the model articles of association for the associations, and the document for the transfer of responsibilities from the Ministry to the association.

**Chapter Eleven**
**- General Provisions -**

**Article - 49-** The utilization of public waters shall be subject to:
a.  The provisions of Legislative Decree No. /8/ of 1996 and the decisions issued by the Chairman of the Higher Water Committee with respect to irrigation water.
b.  Decisions issued by the competent Minister that include the system of utilization and tariff decisions with respect to drinking water and sanitation.

**Article - 50-** Public waters shall be protected from pollution through cooperation and coordination between the Ministry, other ministries, and public entities in accordance with the laws and regulations in force.

**Article - 51-** Jurisdiction over public water properties and the authority to manage and represent them shall be the competence of the Ministry, with the exception of those that fall under the jurisdiction of another public entity by virtue of special laws.

**Article - 52-** Well owners are obligated to use advanced irrigation techniques in accordance with the principles and decisions issued by the Higher Water Committee.

**Article - 53-** The Minister or the competent Minister shall issue the necessary decisions to rationalize the use of public waters for various purposes. Beneficiaries of these waters must adhere to these decisions. In the event that waste by the beneficiary or violation of the issued decisions is proven, the Minister or the competent Minister has the right to withhold water from the violating beneficiary until they provide proof of their compliance with the decisions of the Ministry or the competent Ministry.

**Article - 54-** The Chairman of the Higher Water Committee shall issue the implementing regulations for both the Higher Water Committee and the Basin Management Committee.

**Article - 55-** In a manner not inconsistent with the provisions of the preceding Article (54), the Minister, in coordination with the competent authorities, shall issue the implementing regulations for the provisions of this Law.

**Article - 56-** All provisions contrary to the provisions of this Law are hereby repealed, wherever they may appear in the laws and regulations in force.

**Article - 57-** This Law shall be published in the Official Gazette and shall enter into force from the date of its issuance.
