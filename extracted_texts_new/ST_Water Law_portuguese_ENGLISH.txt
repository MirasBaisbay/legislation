Given the need to adopt a Law that enshrines the guiding principles, directives, and objectives of a National Water Policy to be implemented as set forth in the Government Programme; Highlighting its cross-cutting nature with other sectors, namely the Environment, Health, Fisheries, Agriculture, Forestry, Tourism, and the Coast Guard, among others, for which reason the active participation of the population and users in defining the Public Water Policy is recommended in its management; Reaffirming its importance for environmental, social, cultural, and economic sustainability; The National Assembly decrees, under the terms of Article 97(b) of the Constitution of the Republic, the following: CHAPTER I General Provisions Article 1 Purpose This Law establishes the framework for the management of public domain waters, whether surface waters, namely inland, transitional, and coastal waters, or groundwater, in order to: a) prevent the continued degradation and protect and improve the status of aquatic ecosystems and also of terrestrial ecosystems and wetlands directly dependent on aquatic ecosystems with regard to their water needs; b) promote the sustainable use of water, based on the long-term protection of available water resources; c) achieve enhanced protection and improvement of the aquatic environment, including through specific measures for the progressive reduction and the cessation or phasing out of discharges, emissions, and losses of priority substances; d) ensure the progressive reduction of groundwater pollution and prevent the deterioration of its pollution; e) mitigate the effects of floods and droughts; f) ensure the supply of a sufficient quantity of good-quality surface water and groundwater, as necessary for sustainable, balanced, and equitable water use; h) protect marine waters, including territorial waters; i) ensure compliance with the objectives of relevant international agreements, including those aimed at preventing and eliminating pollution in the marine environment. j) ensure that water is used by current and future generations in a rational manner and with satisfactory standards of quality and biodiversity protection; k) reconcile the use of water with the strategic objectives of social promotion, regional and district development, and environmental sustainability; l) apply and ensure measures for prevention and defense against environmental damage and critical hydrological events of natural origin or resulting from water use; m) ensure equity and the fair distribution of burdens and benefits from the use of water. n) promote and strengthen the exercise of citizenship, the self-determination of peoples, and solidarity for the construction of a sustainable society. 690 SÃO TOMÉ E PRÍNCIPE - OFFICIAL GAZETTE No. 55-02 May 2018 Article 2 Scope 1. This Law applies to all water resources referred to in the preceding article, regardless of their legal regime, encompassing, in addition to the waters, their respective beds and banks, as well as adjacent zones, maximum infiltration zones, and protected zones. 2. The provisions of this Law are without prejudice to the application of special regimes, namely, to water for human consumption, for irrigation, for energy production, to hydromineral and geothermal resources, spring waters, waters for therapeutic purposes, waters supplying swimming pools and other aquatic amusement facilities, and others. Article 3 Definitions For the purposes of this Law, the following definitions apply: a) Coastal waters: surface waters situated landward of a line every point of which is at a distance of 1 nautical mile on the seaward side from the nearest point of the baseline from which the breadth of territorial waters is measured, extending, where applicable, to the outer limit of transitional waters; b) Transitional waters: bodies of surface water in the vicinity of river mouths which are partly saline as a result of their proximity to coastal waters but which are also substantially influenced by freshwater flows; c) Water for human consumption: all water, either in its original state or after treatment, intended for drinking, cooking, food preparation, or other domestic purposes, regardless of its origin and whether it is supplied from a distribution network, from a tanker truck or ship, in bottles or other containers, with or without commercial purposes, as well as all water used in the food industry for the manufacture, processing, preservation, or marketing of products or substances intended for human consumption, except where the use of such water does not affect the wholesomeness of the foodstuff in its finished form; d) Inland waters: all standing or flowing surface waters and all groundwater on the landward side of the baseline from which the breadth of territorial waters is measured; e) Groundwater: all water which is below the surface of the ground in the saturation zone and in direct contact with the ground or subsoil; f) Surface waters: inland waters, with the exception of groundwater; transitional waters and coastal waters, and, in respect of chemical status, territorial waters are also included in this category; g) Territorial waters: the marine waters situated between the baseline and a line at a distance of 12 (twelve) nautical miles from the baseline; h) Wastewater: waters that, after human use, have their natural characteristics altered. Depending on the predominant use: commercial, industrial, or domestic, these waters have different characteristics and are generally designated as sewage, effluent, or greywater. i) Aquifer: one or more subsurface layers of rock or other geological strata of sufficient porosity and permeability to allow either a significant flow of groundwater or the abstraction of significant quantities of groundwater; Article 4 Principles In addition to the general principles enshrined in the Basic Law on the Environment and the principles set forth in the following chapters of this Law, the management of water resources must observe the following principles: a) Principle of the social value of water: which enshrines universal access to water for basic human needs, at a socially acceptable cost, and without constituting a factor of discrimination or exclusion; b) Principle of public operation and management of water: which applies imperatively to multi-municipal systems for public water supply and sanitation; 693 c) d) e) f) g) h) i) j) k) l) m) n) SÃO TOMÉ E PRÍNCIPE - OFFICIAL GAZETTE Principle of the environmental dimension of water: under which the need for a high level of water protection is recognized in order to guarantee its sustainable use; Principle of the economic value of water: by virtue of which the current or potential scarcity of this resource is acknowledged, as is the need to ensure its economically efficient use, with the recovery of the costs of water services, including environmental and resource costs, and based on the polluter-pays and user-pays principles; Principle of integrated management of waters and associated aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems and wetlands directly dependent on them: by virtue of which it is important to develop actions that simultaneously address quantitative and qualitative aspects, a condition for sustainable development; Principle of correction: of damage caused to the environment, primarily at the source, and of imposing on the polluting emitter the costs of corrective and recovery measures; Principle of cooperation: which is based on the recognition that the protection of waters is an attribution of the State and a duty of private individuals. Integration: water planning activity must be integrated horizontally with other administrative planning instruments, at the environmental, territorial, or economic level; Global consideration: economic, environmental, technical, and institutional aspects relevant to water management must be considered, guaranteeing its quantitative and qualitative preservation and its efficient, sustainable, and ecologically balanced use; Functional adaptation: water planning instruments must diversify their intervention in water resources management according to specific problems, needs, and public interests, without prejudice to the necessary unity and coherence of their planning content within each river basin; Sustainability: water planning must address the continuity and stability of the resource in question, protecting its ecological quality and regenerative capacity; Priority: in situations of scarcity, the priority use of water resources is for human and animal consumption; General principle of use: everyone is permitted to use water resources for the purposes for which they are intended, with the restrictions and under the conditions imposed by law or regulation; Principle of the need for a title of use: under the precautionary and prevention principles, activities that have a significant impact on the status of waters may only be carried out under a title of use issued in accordance with the terms and conditions provided for in this law and other legislation. Article 5 Guidelines The following constitute general guidelines for action for the implementation of the National Water Resources Policy: a) adapting water management to district and regional characteristics; b) coordination between the bodies that make up the National Water Management System (S.N.G.A.); c) compatibility of the planning and management of water use with national strategic objectives and the National Multi-annual Plan (P.N.P.); d) permanent development of programmes for the conservation and protection of waters against pollution and excessive and uncontrolled exploitation; e) encouragement and promotion of mobilization, participation, and social control for water management, 694 SÃO TOMÉ E PRÍNCIPE - OFFICIAL GAZETTE No. 55 02 May 2018 with special attention to the participation of the population and vulnerable social segments; f) integrated management, without dissociating quantitative and qualitative aspects, considering the phases of the hydrological cycle; g) integration of water management with the public policies of the central, regional, and district authorities in the sectors of the environment, health, sanitation, infrastructure, forestry, land use, urban and rural development, spatial planning, social and gender equality and equity, climate change, disaster risks, and others of relevant social interest that are interrelated with water management; h) interrelation of the management of river basins with groundwater domains and coastal zones; i) maximization of the social and economic benefits resulting from the multiple and integrated use of waters; j) the encouragement and strengthening of the integration of Environmental Education actions with science and sustainable technologies; k) prioritization of actions, services, and works aimed at ensuring the availability of water in regions of low availability and difficult access; l) promotion of environmental education for water use, with the objective of raising public awareness about the need for conservation and sustainable use of this resource and of capturing everyone's attention for active participation in favor of environmental justice; m) promotion of sustainable technologies aimed at the rational use, conservation, and channeling of water for reuse, recycling, and other forms of treatment of water and springs; n) use of economic and tax instruments to encourage the rational use and conservation of waters; CHAPTER V Use of Water Resources Section I On Uses Article 24 Purpose Water resources are intended to meet the population's needs for drinking water for domestic purposes, forestry, agriculture and livestock, industry, commerce, public services, tourism and recreation, culture and sport, responding to the demands of the country's sustainable development. Article 25 Right of use The private right to use water resources is a real right of use that confers powers and subjects its holder to obligations under the terms established by this legislation and the relevant regulations. Article 26 Basic duties of users 1. Users of water resources must act diligently, taking into account the circumstances, in order to: a) avoid any disturbance of the water status, as determined under this Law, and, in particular, any contamination or adverse alteration of its functional capacities; b) achieve a sustainable use of water, compatible with the maintenance of the integrity of water resources; c) avoid the creation of risks or dangers to its integrity, to the quality of the environment, or to public supply reserves. 2. Anyone who builds, exploits, or operates an installation capable of causing water pollution must, in case of an accident, take the adequate, necessary, and proportional precautions to, taking into account the nature and extent of the danger, prevent accidents and minimize their impacts. Article 28 Common and private use 1. Public domain waters, with regard to use and exploitation, are classified as waters for common use and waters for private use. 2. The uses of water must also be coordinated with other natural resources, taking into account their respective interdependencies. CHAPTER VIII Economic and Financial Regime SECTION I Charging for Water Use Service Article 41 Objective of charging Charging for the water use service is a management instrument that has the objective of: a) conferring rationality, economic and environmental sustainability to water use; b) improving the quality levels of effluents discharged into watercourses; c) contributing to the development of projects, programmes, and actions contemplated in the water policy instruments. d) carrying out activities or performing services and works without authorization or in disagreement with it, which may affect channels, beds, banks, marginal lands, water currents, springs, weirs, aquifers, groundwater tables, lakes, and dams, as well as the quantity, quality, and regime of surface and groundwater; e) defrauding the measurements of the volumes of water used or declaring values different from those measured; 708 SÃO TOMÉ E PRÍNCIPE - OFFICIAL GAZETTE No. 55 – 02 May 2018 f) carrying out interference in the beds of rivers and other water bodies for the extraction of minerals or other materials without the authorizations of the competent bodies; a) written warning, in which deadlines are established for the correction of irregularities; b) accessory sanctions; g) carrying out an activity that results in a change in the regime, quantity, or quality of the waters, without the authorization of the competent body; c) simple fines; d) h) infringing the rules established in this Law and in its regulatory provisions, including instructions and procedures set by the competent organs or entities; i) obstructing the oversight action of the competent authorities of the National Integrated Water Management System in the exercise of their functions; SECTION II Sanctions Article 56 Warning A warning is applicable for non-observance of the provisions of this Law, regulatory provisions, and Resolutions of the National Water Council, without prejudice to other legally provided sanctions. Article 57 Sanctions 1. Infractions of this Law are punished with the following sanctions, regardless of their numbered order: embargo or interdiction of works or activities; e) demolition of the work; f) closure of the well; g) seizure of instruments, tools, equipment, or machinery of any nature used in the infraction. 2. The Managing Entity for the National Water Policy is competent to draw up a notice of infraction, institute an administrative process, and apply penalties arising from infractions of the norms for water use, for violation of the water policy. 3. The Environmental entity, under the terms of the law in force, may cooperate with the other entities of the National Water System in the oversight of water resources. 4. The Regulatory Authority is competent to draw up a notice of infraction, institute a process, and apply penalties arising from infractions by operators in the water sector. 5. Infractions are determined in a specific administrative process, ensuring the right to a full defense and the adversarial principle, with the means and resources inherent to it, observing the legal provisions. 6. The simultaneous commission of infractions leads to the cumulative application of the sanctions provided for them. 7. In case of resistance by the infringer, the oversight and application of the penalties provided for in this Law may be carried out by requesting police force. 8. All expenses arising from the application of the penalties are borne by the infringer, without prejudice to compensation for the damages caused. Article 58 Recidivism and multi-recidivism 1. For the purposes of this Law, a recidivist is considered anyone who commits a new infraction within a 709 SÃO TOMÉ E PRÍNCIPE - OFFICIAL GAZETTE No. 55 – 02 May 2018 period of 3 (three) years, whether it is simple recidivism, when a new infraction of the same type is found, or multi-recidivism, when a new infraction of a different type is found. 2. In case of recidivism, the fine is applied in double. 3. In case of multi-recidivism, a daily fine corresponding to 5% (five percent) of the value of the simple fine is applied. Article 59 Accessory sanctions Accessory sanctions consist of: a) suspension of the authorization for water use; b) withdrawal or cancellation of the authorization for water use. Article 60 Simple fine 1. A simple fine shall be applied when the infringer: a) has been warned for irregularities that have been committed and fails to rectify them within the period established by the Managing Entity for the National Water Policy; b) obstructs the oversight of the Managing Entity for the National Water Policy and the other oversight entities of the National Water Management System. 2. The value of the fines is limited between a minimum of 10,000.00 nDbs (ten thousand new dobras) and a maximum of 50,000.00 nDbs (fifty thousand new dobras), periodically adjusted, as provided for in a Joint Order of the Ministers in charge of the finance and water sectors. 3. In the application of the fine, the following classification is observed: a) minor infractions; b) serious infractions; c) very serious infractions. Article 61 Embargo or interdiction 1. The penalty of embargo or interdiction is imposed in cases: a) of danger to public health or the environment; b) of non-compliance with an order to halt an irregular operation; c) defined in the regulations of this Law, upon the second recurrence. 2. The temporary embargo or interdiction ceases when the requirements for correcting the identified irregularities are met or upon the conclusion of a term of commitment that sets the conditions for the return of activities on a precarious basis. 3. A definitive embargo or interdiction is imposed when the activity does not meet the conditions to obtain authorization or an environmental license, as provided for in specific legislation. 4. A definitive embargo or interdiction entails the revocation of the authorization and, if temporary, its suspension, until the established requirements are met. Article 62 Demolition The penalty of demolition is imposed when the work, construction, or installation: a) is causing serious damage to the water regime; b) is contravening the legal provisions provided for in the water legislation. CHAPTER XII State of Environmental Emergency Article 65 Natural Disasters 1. In the event of natural disasters or man-made accidents that damage or cause a very significant risk of serious and irreparable damage to human health, the safety of persons and property, and the state of water quality, the Prime Minister may declare, in all or part of the national territory, a state of environmental emergency, upon the proposal of the member of the Government in charge of the environment sector, if it is not possible to restore the previous state by normal means. 2. If a state of environmental emergency is declared under the terms of the preceding paragraph, an environmental emergency council is created, chaired by the member of the Government in charge of the environment sector, composed of entities appointed by the latter which, depending on the exceptional circumstances verified, can contribute to the restoration of the previous ecological state or to the reduction of the created risks and damages. 3. During the period of the state of environmental emergency, the national water authority may: a) suspend the execution of water planning instruments; b) suspend acts that authorize the use of water resources; c) modify, respecting the principle of proportionality and taking into account the duration of the state of environmental emergency, the content of acts that authorize the use of water resources; d) define priorities for the use of water resources, derogating from the hierarchy established in the law or in the water planning instruments; e) impose behaviors or apply precautionary measures in response to ecological risks; 711 SÃO TOMÉ E PRÍNCIPE - OFFICIAL GAZETTE No. 55 – 02 May 2018 f) present recommendations to water resources users and inform the public about the evolution of the risk. 4. The environmental emergency acts referred to in the preceding paragraph must be ratified by the member of the Government in charge of the environment sector. 5. The state of environmental emergency has a maximum duration of three months.
