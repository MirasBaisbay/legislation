Law No. (50) of 2008
Law of the Ministry of Water Resources

Chapter One
Establishment and Objectives

Article 1 - A ministry named the (Ministry of Water Resources) shall be established, possessing a juridical personality and represented by the Minister of Water Resources or his authorized representative.

Article 2 - The Ministry aims to:
First - Plan for the investment of water resources in Iraq and the utilization of surface and groundwater to achieve the optimal use of water wealth.
Second - Develop and enhance water resources, and identify their sources and uses.
Third - Safeguard Iraq's rights in shared international waters and sustain communication and information exchange with neighboring and riparian states on river basins, in a manner that ensures the conclusion of fair agreements for the division of the quantity and quality of water entering Iraq.
Fourth - Protect surface and groundwater from pollution, giving priority to environmental aspects and the revival and sustenance of marshes and other water bodies.

Article 3 - The Ministry shall seek to achieve its objectives through the following:
First - Regulating water distribution, warding off the dangers of floods, and controlling torrents and river basins.
Second - Conducting studies related to projects for irrigation, reclamation, dams, and groundwater, and preparing the designs and related documents through its own formations or specialized consulting bodies.
Third - Managing, operating, and maintaining projects for dams, reclamation, irrigation, drainage, and groundwater.
Fourth - Implementing projects related to irrigation, reclamation, dams, and others through its executive bodies or by contracting with local or international contractors and companies.
Fifth - Coordinating with international, regional, and Arab organizations and non-governmental organizations specialized in water and environmental resources.
Sixth - Coordinating the Ministry's plans with planning authorities and water-consuming sectors in a manner consistent with sustainable development in Iraq and for all sectors.
Seventh - Introducing modern technologies and Geographic Information Systems (GIS) to develop work methods in the Ministry and training technical and administrative staff to achieve the management and utilization of water through advanced scientific methods.
Eighth - Raising public awareness of the importance of preserving and optimally investing water resources and protecting them from pollution, and expanding the base of public and civil society organization participation in the Ministry's activities.

Chapter Two
The Minister

Article 4 - First - The Minister is the highest head of the Ministry and is responsible for implementing its policies and tasks. He exercises supervision over its activities and performance, ensures its proper functioning, and has the authority to issue internal regulations, instructions, decisions, and orders in all matters related to his duties.
Second - The Minister may delegate some of his powers to an undersecretary of the ministry, to any of the advisors, to any of the directors-general of the departments subordinate to the Ministry, or to any of the Ministry's employees.

Article 5 - First - The Ministry shall have two undersecretaries, a senior undersecretary and a technical undersecretary, who shall exercise the tasks assigned to them by the Minister and assist him in managing the affairs of the Ministry.
Second - The undersecretary may delegate some of his powers to any of the directors-general or to any of the Ministry's employees.

Article 6 - The Ministry shall have two advisors who undertake the implementation of the tasks assigned to them by the Minister and provide advice regarding the work of the formations specified by the Minister.

Article 7 - The Ministry shall have an inspector general who undertakes his duties in accordance with the law, assisted by a number of employees.

Chapter Three
Organizational Structure of the Ministry

(This article was amended by Law No. 1 of 2012)

Article 8 - The Ministry shall be composed of the following formations:
First - Formations of the Ministry's headquarters:
a - Office of the Inspector General.
b - Planning and Follow-up Department.
c - Legal and Contracts Department.
d - Administrative Affairs Department.
e - Financial Affairs Department.
f - Minister's Office.
g - Internal Auditing and Control Section.
h - Relations and Media Section.
i - Water Research Section.

Second - Formations associated with the Ministry:
a - General Authority for the Operation of Irrigation and Drainage Projects.
b - General Authority for Irrigation and Reclamation Projects.
c - General Authority for the Maintenance of Irrigation and Drainage Projects.
d - General Authority for Dams and Reservoirs.
e - General Authority for Surveying.
f - General Authority for Groundwater.
g - Department for the Execution of River Dredging Works.
h - General Outfall Drain Department.
i - Al-Rafidain General Company for Dam Construction.
j - Al-Iraq General Company for the Execution of Irrigation Projects.
k - Al-Fao General Company for the Execution of Irrigation Projects.
l - National Center for Water Resources Management.
m - Center for Studies and Engineering Designs.
n - Center for the Revitalization of the Iraqi Marshes and Wetlands.
o - Center for Water Resources Studies for the Northern Region Projects.

Article 9 - The formations associated with the Ministry, as stipulated in clause (Second) of Article (8) of this law, shall have juridical personality and shall be represented by their director-general or his authorized representative, excluding the departments or centers associated with the Ministry.

(This article was amended by Law No. 4 of 2010)

Article 10 - The general authorities, departments, general companies, and centers stipulated in Article (8) of this law shall be managed by an employee with the title of director-general, who holds a primary university degree in his field of specialization, possesses expertise and competence, and has no less than (15) fifteen years of service.

Chapter Four
General and Final Provisions

(This article was amended by Law No. 4 of 2010)

Article 11 - Coordination between the Ministry and the Ministry of Water Resources in the Kurdistan Regional Government shall be carried out by virtue of instructions issued by the Council of Ministers in accordance with the law.

Article 12 - The tasks and divisions of the formations stipulated in Article (8) of this law shall be determined by instructions issued by the Minister.

Article 13 - First - The Law of the Ministry of Irrigation No. (8) of 1993 is hereby repealed, and the regulations, instructions, and internal systems issued thereunder shall remain in effect, provided they do not conflict with the provisions of this law, until such time as new provisions are issued to replace or repeal them.
Second - The rights, obligations, and assets of the departments formed under the Law of the Ministry of Irrigation No. (8) of 1993, along with their employees, shall be transferred to the Ministry's formations established by this law.

Article 14 - First - The rights, obligations, assets, and employees of the following companies and centers, established under the Law of the Ministry of Irrigation No. (8) of 1993, shall be transferred to the formations stipulated in this law:
a - Al-Hadbaa Company for the Maintenance of Irrigation Projects.
b - Al-Nasr Company for the Maintenance of Irrigation Projects.
c - Water Wells Drilling Company.
d - Machinery and Equipment Repair Company.
e - Al-Muthanna Company for River Dredging.
f - Tigris Center for Studies and Designs of Irrigation Projects.
g - Euphrates Center for Studies and Designs of Irrigation Projects.
h - Water Resources Research Center.

Second - The companies and centers stipulated in clause (First) of this Article are hereby dissolved.

Article 15 - The Minister may issue instructions and internal regulations to facilitate the implementation of the provisions of this law.

Article 16 - This law shall be effective from the date of its publication in the Official Gazette.

Justifying Reasons

In view of the establishment of a ministry named the (Ministry of Water Resources) and the changes that have occurred in the administrative formations associated with it, this law has been enacted.
