Preamble

For the purposes of harmonisation with the acts of the European Community entitled -
(a) Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2000 establishing a framework for Community action in the field of water policy (OJL 327 of 22.12.2000, p. 1)

(b) Decision No 2455/2001/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 20 November 2001 establishing the list of priority substances in the field of water policy and amending Directive 2000/60/EC (OJL 331 of 15.12.2001, p. 1).

"Directive 2008/105/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 16 December 2008 on environmental quality standards in the field of water policy, amending and subsequently repealing Council Directives 82/176/EEC, 83/513/EEC, 84/156/EEC, 84/491/EEC, and 86/280/EEC and amending Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council",

For the purposes of harmonisation with Article 32 of the act of the European Community entitled -
"Directive 2009/31/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 April 2009 on the geological storage of carbon dioxide and amending Council Directive 85/337/EEC, European Parliament and Council Directives 2000/60/EC, 2001/80/EC, 2004/35/EC, 2006/12/EC and 2008/1/EC, and Regulation (EC) No 1013/2006", and

for the purposes of more correct harmonisation with Article 10 paragraph 2 point (c) and Article 16 paragraph 8 of the act of the European Community entitled -
"Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2000 establishing a framework for Community action in the field of water policy", as last amended by Directive 2009/31/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 April 2009,

For the purposes of harmonisation with Article 2 and Annex II of the act of the European Union entitled -
"Directive 2013/39/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council of 12 August 2013 amending Directives 2000/60/EC and 2008/105/EC as regards priority substances in the field of water policy",

To authorise the competent authority to establish prohibitive and/or mandatory measures within the protection zones of surface and groundwater systems, as well as of water abstraction works, the water of which is intended or will be intended for human consumption, with the aim of preventing the deterioration of its quality and thus reducing the level of purification treatment required for the production of drinking water,

The House of Representatives enacts as follows:

PART I - INTRODUCTORY PROVISIONS

Short title

This Law shall be cited as the Protection and Management of Waters Law of 2003.
13(1)/2004

Interpretation
2.(1) In this Law, unless the context otherwise requires -
"measurement uncertainty" means the parameter, which does not take negative values, and characterises the dispersion of the quantitative values attributed to a measured quantity, based on the information used;
"direct discharge to groundwater" means the discharge of pollutants into groundwater without percolation through the soil or subsoil;
"competent authority" means the Minister of Agriculture, Natural Resources and Environment;
"available groundwater resource" means the long-term average annual rate of overall recharge of a body of groundwater less the long-term average annual rate of flow required to achieve the ecological quality objectives for associated surface waters as specified in Article 9, to avoid any significant diminution in the ecological status of such waters and to avoid any significant damage to associated terrestrial ecosystems;
"emission controls" means controls requiring a specific emission limitation, such as an emission limit value, or controls otherwise specifying limits or conditions on the effects, nature or other characteristics of an emission or operating conditions which affect emissions, and the use of the term "emission control" in this Law, in relation to the provisions of any other Law, shall not be considered as a new interpretation of the said provisions;
"hazardous substances" means substances or groups of substances that are toxic, persistent and liable to bio-accumulate, and other substances or groups of substances which give rise to an equivalent level of concern;
"Commission" means the Commission of the European Communities;
"surface water" means -
(a) inland waters, except groundwater; and
(b) transitional waters and coastal waters, except with respect to their chemical status, in which case they also include the waters within the territorial sea of the Republic as this is determined on the basis of the Territorial Sea Law of 1964.
"abstraction works" means the drilling or construction of a well or other works by which the abstraction of water from any water source is made possible and includes the widening, deepening or in any other way extending or modifying any natural spring;
"inland water" means all standing or flowing surface water and all groundwater on the landward side of the baseline from which the breadth of the territorial sea is measured;
"heavily modified water body" means a body of surface water which as a result of physical alterations by human activity is substantially changed in character, as designated by the competent authority in accordance with the provisions of Article 7 and Annex II;
"good surface water status" means the status achieved by a surface water body when its ecological and chemical status are at least "good";
"good groundwater status" means the status achieved by a groundwater body when its quantitative and chemical status are at least "good";
"good ecological status" means the status of a body of surface water so classified in accordance with Annex V;
"good quantitative status" means the status as defined in table 2.1.2 of Annex V;
"good surface water chemical status" means the chemical status required to meet the environmental objectives for surface waters established in Article 9, that is, the chemical status achieved by a body of surface water in which concentrations of pollutants do not exceed the environmental quality standards established in Annex IX and under other relevant Community legislation setting environmental quality standards at Community level or are established on the basis of any other provision of this or any other Law and concern the concentrations of priority substances in surface waters, sediments and biota;
"good groundwater chemical status" means the chemical status of a body of groundwater which meets all the conditions set out in table 2.3.2 of Annex V;
"good ecological potential" means the status of a heavily modified or an artificial water body, so classified in accordance with the relevant provisions of Annex V;
"Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009" means Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 21 October 2009 concerning the placing of plant protection products on the market and repealing Council Directives 79/117/EEC and 91/414/EEC, as last amended by Regulation (EU) No 652/2014;
"Regulation (EU) No 528/2012" means Regulation (EU) No 528/2012 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 22 May 2012 concerning the making available on the market and use of biocidal products, as last amended by Regulation (EU) No 334/2014;
"surface water status" means the general expression of the status of a body of surface water, determined by the poorer of its ecological status and its chemical status;
"groundwater status" means the general expression of the status of a body of groundwater, determined by the poorer of its quantitative status and its chemical status;
"Community" means the European Community;
"river basin" means the area of land from which all surface run-off flows through a sequence of streams, rivers and, possibly, lakes into the sea at a single river mouth, estuary or delta;
"lake" means a body of standing inland surface water;
"transitional waters" are bodies of surface water in the vicinity of river mouths which are partly saline in character as a result of their proximity to coastal waters but which are substantially influenced by freshwater flows;
"water intended for human consumption" has the meaning attributed to the term "water for human consumption" by the Quality of Water for Human Consumption (Monitoring and Control) Law of 2001;
"Directive" means Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2000 establishing a framework for Community action in the field of water policy;
"Directive 2008/105/EC" means Directive 2008/105/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 16 December 2008 on environmental quality standards in the field of water policy, amending and subsequently repealing Council Directives 82/176/EEC, 83/513/EEC, 84/156/EEC, 84/491/EEC and 86/280/EEC and amending Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council, as last amended by Directive 2013/39/EU;
"ecological status" is an expression of the quality of the structure and functioning of aquatic ecosystems associated with surface waters, classified in accordance with Annex V;
"emission limit values" means the mass, expressed in terms of certain specific parameters, concentration and/or level of an emission, which may not be exceeded during one or more periods of time;
"limit of detection" means the output signal or concentration value above which it can be affirmed, with a stated level of confidence, that a sample is different from a blank sample containing no determinand of interest;
"limit of quantification" means a stated multiple of the limit of detection at a concentration of the determinand that can reasonably be determined with an acceptable level of accuracy and precision. The limit of quantification can be calculated using an appropriate standard or sample, and may be obtained from the lowest calibration point on the calibration curve, excluding the blank;
"priority substances" means substances identified in accordance with Article 26 and listed in Annex X; amongst these substances there are "priority hazardous substances" which are substances identified in accordance with Article 16 paragraphs 3 and 6 of Directive 2000/60/EC;
"coastal water" means surface water on the landward side of a line, every point of which is at a distance of one nautical mile on the seaward side from the nearest point of the baseline from which the breadth of territorial waters is measured, extending, where appropriate, up to the outer limit of transitional waters;
"environmental objectives" means the objectives set out in Articles 9 to 17;
"river basin district" means the area of land and sea, made up of one or more neighbouring river basins together with their associated groundwaters and coastal waters, which is identified under Article 5(1) as the main unit for management of river basins;
"water source" means any surface and underground waters, including water from natural springs;
"environmental quality standard" means the concentration of a particular pollutant or group of pollutants in water, sediment or biota which should not be exceeded in order to protect human health and the environment;
"quantitative status" is an expression of the degree to which a body of groundwater is affected by direct and indirect abstractions;
"river" means a body of inland water flowing for the most part on the surface of the land but which may flow underground for part of its course;
"pollution" means the direct or indirect introduction, as a result of human activity, of substances or heat into the air, water or land which may be harmful to human health or the quality of aquatic ecosystems or terrestrial ecosystems directly depending on aquatic ecosystems, which result in damage to material property, or which impair or interfere with amenities and other legitimate uses of the environment;
"pollutant" means any substance liable to cause pollution, in particular those listed in Annex VIII;
"combined approach" means the control of discharges and emissions into surface waters according to the approach set out in Article 25;
"body of surface water" means a discrete and significant element of surface water such as a lake, a reservoir, a stream, river or canal, part of a stream, river or canal, transitional water or a stretch of coastal water;
"body of groundwater" means a distinct volume of groundwater within an aquifer or aquifers;
"taxon" means a particular aquatic taxonomic group at the rank of 'sub-phylum', 'class' or their equivalent;
"artificial water body" means a body of surface water created by human activity;
"aquifer" means a subsurface layer or layers of rock or other geological strata of sufficient porosity and permeability to allow either a significant flow of groundwater or the abstraction of significant quantities of groundwater;
"matrix" means an element of the aquatic environment, i.e. water, sediment or biota as appropriate;
"water services" means all services which provide, for households, public institutions or any economic activity -
(a) abstraction, impoundment, storage, treatment and distribution of surface water or groundwater,
(b) waste-water collection and treatment facilities which subsequently discharge into surface water.
"groundwater" means all water which is below the surface of the ground in the saturation zone and in direct contact with the ground or subsoil;
"sub-basin" means the area of land from which all surface run-off flows through a series of streams, rivers and, possibly, lakes to a particular point in a water course (normally a lake or a river confluence);
"natural spring" means the natural emergence of groundwater to the surface of the ground and the abstraction of this water for human consumption purposes, and includes the widening, deepening or otherwise extending or modifying it;
"water use", for the purposes of Article 3 and of the economic analysis carried out in accordance with Article 6 and paragraph (b) of Annex III, means water services together with any other activity identified under Article 6 and Annex II having a significant impact on the status of water.

(2) Any emission limit values established on the basis of this Law may also be set for certain groups, families or categories of substances, in particular for those identified on the basis of Article 26.

(3) The emission limit values for substances shall normally apply at the point where the emissions leave the installation, without any account being taken of any dilution thereof. With regard to indirect discharges into water, the effect of a waste-water treatment plant may be taken into account when determining the emission limit values of the installation concerned, provided that an equivalent level of protection of the environment as a whole is guaranteed and that this does not lead to higher levels of pollution in the environment.

Purpose of the Law
3. The purpose of this Law is the protection of inland surface waters, transitional waters, coastal waters and groundwater, by establishing appropriate provisions which -
(a) prevent further deterioration and protect and enhance the status of aquatic ecosystems and, with regard to their water needs, terrestrial ecosystems and wetlands directly depending on the aquatic ecosystems;
(b) promote sustainable water use based on a long-term protection of available water resources;
(c) aim at enhanced protection and improvement of the aquatic environment through the implementation of various measures, including specific measures for the progressive reduction of discharges, emissions and losses of priority substances and the cessation or phasing out of discharges, emissions and losses of priority hazardous substances;
(d) ensure the progressive reduction of pollution of groundwater and prevent its further pollution;
(e) contribute to mitigating the effects of floods and droughts and thereby contribute to -
(i) the provision of a sufficient supply of good quality surface water and groundwater as needed for sustainable, balanced and equitable water use,
(ii) a significant reduction in pollution of groundwater,
(iii) the protection of territorial and marine waters, and
(iv) achieving the objectives of relevant international agreements, including those which aim to prevent and eliminate pollution of the marine environment, through the specific measures of the European Community established on the basis of Article 16 paragraph 3 of Directive 2000/60/EC, which will contribute to the cessation or phasing out of discharges, emissions and losses of priority hazardous substances, with the ultimate aim of achieving concentrations in the marine environment near background values for naturally occurring substances and close to zero for man-made synthetic substances.

Responsibilities of the competent authority
4.(1) The competent authority is responsible for the faithful and effective implementation of the provisions of this Law and is the competent authority for the purposes of both paragraph 2 and paragraph 6 of Article 3 of the Directive.
(2) Without prejudice to the provisions of subsections (3), (4) and (5) of Article 22, the competent authority shall take appropriate action to encourage the active involvement of all interested parties and the public in the implementation of the provisions of this Law.
(3) The requirements of this Law for the achievement of the environmental objectives referred to in Articles 9, 10 and 11, and in particular the programme of measures, shall be coordinated by the competent authority for the entire river basin district.
(4) No later than six months from the entry into force of this Law, the competent authority shall forward to the Commission a report containing the information set out in Annex I. If any change occurs in this information after it has been sent, the competent authority shall inform the Commission accordingly, no later than three months after the said change.

PART II - WATER SYSTEMS AND ENVIRONMENTAL OBJECTIVES

River basin districts
5.(1) For the purposes of the Directive and this Law, the entire territory of the Republic is designated as one river basin district in which all individual river basins and the water systems referred to in subsection (2) are included.
(2) No later than six months from the entry into force of this Law, the competent authority shall identify all the river basins in the territory of the Republic and shall include in them all groundwater and coastal waters.
13(I)/2004

Analysis of the characteristics of water systems
6.(1) The competent authority shall ensure that, starting no later than one year from the entry into force of this Law, the following are completed -
(a) in accordance with the technical specifications set out in Annex II, -
(i) an analysis of the characteristics of all surface water systems and all groundwater systems in the Republic; and
(ii) a review of the impact of human activity on the status of surface waters and groundwater; and
(b) in accordance with the technical specifications set out in Annex III, an economic analysis of water use in the Republic.
(2) The analyses and reviews referred to in subsection (1) shall be reviewed and, where necessary, updated no later than ten years after the entry into force of this Law and every six years thereafter.
13(1)/2004

Designation of a water body as artificial or heavily modified
7.(1) Having identified all bodies of surface water, the competent authority shall designate, on the basis of the provisions of subsection (3), which of them are to be characterised, for the purposes of this Law, as artificial or heavily modified.
(2) The need for a body of surface water to be designated as artificial or heavily modified, as well as the justification for such designation, shall be stated in the river basin management plan drawn up on the basis of Article 22.
(3) A body of surface water may be designated as artificial or heavily modified when -
(a) the changes to the hydromorphological characteristics of that body which would be necessary for achieving good ecological status would have significant adverse effects on -
(i) the wider environment;
(ii) navigation, including port facilities;
(iii) recreation;
(iv) water supply, hydropower generation, irrigation or any other activity for which water is stored;
(v) water regulation;
(vi) flood protection;
(vii) land drainage; or
(viii) other equally important sustainable human development activities.
(b) the beneficial objectives served by the artificial or modified characteristics of the water body cannot, for reasons of technical feasibility or disproportionate cost, reasonably be achieved by other means, which are a significantly better environmental option.
13(1)/2004

Register of protected areas
8.(1) The competent authority shall ensure the establishment of a Register in which all areas designated under any Law as requiring special protection, either for the protection of surface or ground waters or for the conservation of habitats and species directly dependent on water, are recorded.
(2) The Register referred to in subsection (1) shall include all the information and data specified in Annex IV and shall be completed no later than one year after the entry into force of this Law.
(3) The competent authority shall ensure that the register is always kept correctly updated.
13(1)/2004

Environmental objectives for surface waters
9.(1) Subject to the provisions of Articles 15, 16 and 17, the competent authority shall ensure that all necessary measures are implemented to prevent the deterioration of the status of all bodies of surface water.
(2) Subject to the provisions of subsection (3), the competent authority shall ensure the protection, enhancement and restoration of all bodies of surface water with the aim of achieving good surface water status, in accordance with the provisions of Annex V, no later than twelve years from the entry into force of this Law.
(3) The competent authority shall ensure the protection and enhancement of all artificial and heavily modified water bodies, in accordance with the provisions of Annex V, with the aim of achieving good ecological potential and good chemical status of surface waters no later than twelve years from the entry into force of this Law.
(4) The competent authority shall implement the specific measures established by the Commission against pollution of water by individual pollutants or groups of pollutants presenting a significant risk to or via the aquatic environment, including risks to waters used for the abstraction of drinking water. For these pollutants, the measures shall aim at progressive reduction and, for priority hazardous substances, at the cessation or phasing out of discharges, emissions and losses.
(5) The above provisions of this Article shall not affect the provisions of any international convention to which the Republic is a party, including those aimed at the prevention or elimination of pollution of the marine environment through Community action under Article 16, paragraph 3 of the Directive, for the cessation or phasing out of discharges, emissions and losses of priority hazardous substances, with the ultimate aim of achieving concentrations in the marine environment which, for naturally occurring substances, are near background levels, and for man-made synthetic substances, are close to zero.
13(I)/2004

Environmental objectives for groundwater
10.(1) The competent authority shall take all necessary measures to prevent and limit the input of pollutants into groundwater and to prevent the deterioration of the status of all bodies of groundwater.
(2) The competent authority shall take the appropriate measures to protect, enhance and restore all bodies of groundwater and ensure a balance between abstraction and recharge of groundwater, with the aim of achieving good groundwater status no later than twelve years from the entry into force of this Law, in accordance with the provisions of Annex V.
(3) The competent authority shall implement the necessary measures to reverse any significant and sustained upward trend in the concentration of any pollutant resulting from the impact of human activity in order progressively to reduce pollution of groundwater. To this end, the competent authority shall adopt the specific measures and criteria established by the Community on the basis of Article 17 of the Directive. However, if these are not established within two years from the entry into force of this Law, then the criteria to be used for the purpose of assessing the good chemical status of groundwater and the criteria for the identification of significant and sustained upward trends and for the definition of starting points for trend reversal shall be determined by the competent authority.
(4) If for significant reasons it is not possible for the competent authority to establish the criteria referred to in subsection (3), then for the purposes of the same subsection, trend reversal shall take as its starting point 75%, at the most, of the level of the quality standards provided for in existing legislation of the European Community which applies to groundwater.
13(I)/2004

Environmental objectives for protected areas
11. The competent authority shall ensure compliance with all standards and objectives established by this Law no later than twelve years after its entry into force, unless otherwise provided for in any law or regulation harmonised with relevant legislation of the European Community, on the basis of which the respective protected areas have been established.
13(1)/2004

Application of the more stringent objective
12. Where two or more of the objectives set out in Articles 9, 10 and 11 apply to a specific water body, the competent authority shall ensure that the most stringent objective is applied to that body.
13(1)/2004

Extension of deadlines
13. The deadlines provided for in Articles 9, 10 and 11 may be extended for the purposes of phased achievement of the objectives for bodies of water, provided that this does not result in further deterioration of the status of the affected body of water and provided that the following conditions are met, namely-
(a) the competent authority, after investigation, establishes that all the required improvements in the status of the water body cannot reasonably be achieved within the above deadlines, for at least one of the following reasons, namely-
(i) the extent of the required improvements, for technical reasons, can only be achieved in phases exceeding the timescale;
(ii) completing the improvements within the timescale would be disproportionately expensive; or
(iii) natural conditions do not allow for the timely improvement of the status of the water body.
(b) the extension of the deadline and the corresponding justification are set out and explained specifically in the river basin management plan prepared on the basis of Article 22;
(c) extensions shall be limited to a maximum of two further updates of the river basin management plan, except in cases where such natural conditions prevail that the objectives cannot be achieved within the extended period;
(d) the river basin management plan shall set out -
(i) a summary of the measures required under Articles 19, 20 and 21 which are considered necessary to bring the bodies of water progressively to the required status within the extended deadline;
(ii) the reasons for any significant delay in making these measures operational; and
(iii) the expected timetable for their application.
13(I)/2004

Less stringent objectives
14. Notwithstanding the provisions of Articles 9, 10 and 11, the competent authority may, for specific bodies of water, aim to achieve environmental objectives less stringent than those imposed by the said Articles, in cases where these bodies are either affected by human activities, as identified by the review conducted on the basis of Article 6(1)(a)(ii), or their natural condition is such that the achievement of these objectives would be infeasible or disproportionately expensive, and provided that all the following conditions are met, namely -
(a) the environmental and socioeconomic needs served by the above human activities cannot be achieved by other means, which are a significantly better environmental option which does not entail disproportionate costs;
(b) appropriate care is taken to ensure that -
(i) for surface water, the highest ecological potential and the best possible chemical status are achieved, given impacts that could not reasonably have been avoided due to the nature of the human activity or pollution;
(ii) for groundwater, the least possible changes to good status occur, given impacts that could not reasonably have been avoided due to the nature of the human activity or pollution;
(c) no further deterioration in the status of the affected body of water occurs; and
(d) the establishment of less stringent environmental objectives, and the corresponding justification, are specifically set out in the river basin management plan referred to in Article 22, and those objectives are reviewed every six years.
13(1)/2004

Temporary deterioration due to unforeseen circumstances
15.(1) Any temporary deterioration in the status of any body of water resulting from -
(a) circumstances of natural cause or force majeure which are exceptional or could not reasonably have been foreseen, including extreme floods or prolonged droughts; or
(b) accidents which could not reasonably have been foreseen,
shall not constitute a breach of the requirements of this Law, provided that the conditions and terms of the following subsections of this Article are met.
(2) In any of the circumstances referred to in subsection (1), the competent authority shall ensure that all practicable steps are taken to prevent further deterioration in the status of the affected water body and not to compromise the achievement of the objectives of this Law in other bodies of water not affected.
(3) The measures referred to in subsection (2) shall be included in the programme of measures provided for in Article 19 and shall be of such a nature and extent as not to compromise the restoration of the quality of the water body after the end of the circumstances or accidents on account of which they were taken.
(4) The conditions under which the unforeseen or exceptional circumstances of subsection (1) may be declared by the competent authority shall be defined in the river basin management plan, which shall include for this purpose the appropriate indicators.
(5) The competent authority shall ensure that an annual review is carried out of the effects of any circumstances referred to in subsection (1) and, without prejudice to the provisions of Article 13, all practicable steps are taken for the reasonably earliest possible restoration of the water body to the status it had before the said effects.
(6) Any consequences for any water body due to the circumstances referred to in subsection (1), as well as the measures taken or to be taken for their prevention or restoration, shall be recorded in a summary which shall be included in the next update of the river basin management plan.
13(1)/2004

Permissible derogations
16. This Law shall not be deemed to be breached in any case where -
(a) failure to achieve good groundwater status, good ecological status or, where relevant, good ecological potential or to prevent deterioration in the status of any body of surface or ground water is the result of new modifications to the physical characteristics of a surface water body or alterations to the level of bodies of groundwater; or
(b) failure to prevent deterioration from high status to good status of any body of surface water is the result of new sustainable human development activities, and provided that the following conditions are met, namely -
(i) all practicable steps are taken to mitigate the adverse impacts on the status of the body of water;
(ii) the reasons for those modifications or alterations are specifically set out in the river basin management plan prepared in accordance with Article 22 and the objectives are reviewed every six years;
(iii) the reasons for those modifications or alterations are of overriding public interest, and/or the benefits to the environment and to society of achieving the objectives set out in Articles 9, 10, 11 and 12 are outweighed by the benefits of the new modifications or alterations to human health, to the maintenance of human safety or to sustainable development; and
(iv) the beneficial objectives served by those modifications or alterations of the water body cannot be achieved by other means, which are a significantly better environmental option, either for technical reasons or due to excessive cost.
13(1)/2004

Ensuring the objectives of the Law
17.(1) When applying Articles 7, 13, 14, 15 and 16, the competent authority shall ensure that the application does not permanently exclude or compromise the achievement of the objectives of this Law in other bodies of water and is consistent with the implementation of other environmental legislation and the Community environmental acquis.
(2) The competent authority shall take the appropriate measures to ensure that the application of Articles 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15 and 16 guarantees at least the same level of protection for water systems as provided for by the existing environmental legislation and the relevant environmental acquis.
13(1)/2004

Measures in case of non-achievement of objectives
18.(1) If, on the basis of the data collected during the monitoring carried out on the basis of Article 24 or on the basis of other data, it is established that the objectives set out in Articles 9 to 17 are unlikely to be achieved with regard to any water body, then the competent authority shall ensure that -
(a) the causes of the likely failure are investigated;
(b) the permits and any authorisations granted on the basis of the Control of Water Pollution Law or any other law, regulation or order are examined and, where deemed appropriate, reviewed;
(c) the monitoring programmes established on the basis of Article 24 are reviewed and adjusted, where deemed appropriate; and
(d) any additional measures necessary to achieve the said objectives are established, including, where deemed appropriate, the establishment of more stringent environmental standards in accordance with the procedures provided for in Annex V.
(2) If the causes of the likely failure referred to in subsection (1) are due to circumstances of natural cause or force majeure and are exceptional or could not reasonably have been foreseen, including cases of extreme floods or prolonged drought, the competent authority may, without prejudice to the provisions of Article 15, decide that the implementation of the additional measures referred to in subsection (1) is not feasible.

PART III - PROGRAMME OF MEASURES AND MANAGEMENT PLAN

Establishment of a programme of measures
19.(1) The Council of Ministers, after taking into account the results of the analyses and reviews referred to in Article 6(1), shall ensure the establishment of a programme of measures which will lead to the achievement of the objectives set out in Articles 9 to 17.
(2) The programme of measures established on the basis of subsection (1) may refer to measures which are implemented or must be implemented on the basis of the provisions of any other law or administrative act and may concern either the whole of the river basin district or part of that district and shall include -
(a) the measures specified in Article 20, hereinafter referred to as "basic measures",
(b) where necessary, the measures specified in Article 21, hereinafter referred to as "supplementary measures", and
(c) the measures established by the European Community on the basis of Article 17 paragraph 1 of the Directive.
(3) The programme of measures referred to above shall be established no later than six years from the date of entry into force of this Law and all measures referred to therein shall be made operational no later than nine years after that date.
(4) The programme of measures shall be reviewed and, if necessary, updated no later than twelve years from the date of entry into force of this Law and every six years thereafter. Any new or revised measure established on the basis of an updated programme must be ready for implementation no later than three years after its establishment.
13(1)/2004

Basic measures
20.(1) The basic measures included in the programme of measures established on the basis of Article 19 shall constitute minimum requirements and shall consist of the following, namely -
(a) in measures required to implement the Community acquis concerning the protection of water, including measures required on the basis of the legislation referred to in Article 25 and in Part A of Annex VI.
(b) in measures deemed appropriate for the purposes of Article 27.
(c) in measures to promote efficient and sustainable water use in order not to compromise the achievement of the objectives set out in Articles 9 to 17.
(d) in measures aimed at complying with the requirements of Article 23, including measures to safeguard water quality in order to reduce the level of purification treatment required in the production of drinking water.
(e) in controls over the abstraction of fresh surface water and groundwater, and impoundment of fresh surface water, including a register or registers of water abstractions and a requirement for prior authorisation for abstraction and impoundment; these controls shall be reviewed periodically and, where necessary, updated:
Provided that the competent authority may exempt from the above controls abstractions or impoundments which do not have a significant impact on water status;
(f) in controls of groundwater systems, including a requirement for prior authorisation for artificial recharge or augmentation of such systems; the water used for these purposes may be derived from any surface water or groundwater, provided that the use of any source does not compromise the achievement of the environmental objectives established for that source or for the recharged or augmented body of groundwater; these controls shall be reviewed periodically and, where deemed necessary, updated;
(g) for point source discharges liable to cause pollution, in regulatory measures taken on the basis of the Control of Water Pollution Law or any other law or administrative act, by which controls are imposed on the discharge of the relevant pollutants, including a prohibition on the introduction of pollutants into water or a requirement for prior authorisation which is accompanied by binding conditions or is based on general binding rules; these controls shall be reviewed periodically and, where deemed necessary, updated;
(h) for diffuse sources liable to cause pollution, in measures to prevent or control the input of pollutants, including a prohibition on the introduction of pollutants into water or a requirement for authorisation which is accompanied by binding conditions or is based on general binding rules in accordance with the Control of Water Pollution Law or any other law or regulatory administrative act; these controls shall be reviewed periodically and, where deemed necessary, updated;
(i) for cases where significant adverse impacts on the status of water are observed following the relevant analyses and reviews provided for in Article 6 and Annex II, in measures to ensure that the hydromorphological conditions of the water bodies are consistent with the achievement of the required ecological status or good ecological potential for water bodies designated as artificial or heavily modified; the controls required for this purpose shall be carried out on the basis of the Control of Water Pollution Law or any other law or regulatory administrative act and shall include a requirement for authorisation which is accompanied by binding conditions or is based on general binding rules; these controls shall be reviewed periodically and, where deemed necessary, updated;
(j) in measures aimed at eliminating pollution of surface water by priority substances and at progressively reducing pollution from other substances, if this would impede the achievement of the objectives set out in Article 9.
(k) in measures to prevent significant leakages of pollutants from technical installations and to prevent or reduce the impacts of accidental pollution incidents, such as in cases of floods, including measures for the introduction of detection or early warning systems for such incidents, as well as measures to reduce the risks to aquatic ecosystems which could not reasonably have been foreseen.
(2) The programme of measures referred to in subsection (1) shall also include measures prohibiting the direct discharge of pollutants into groundwater, except for the following cases, namely -
(a) the reinjection into the same aquifer of water used for geothermal purposes;
(b) the injection of water containing substances resulting exclusively from the exploration for and extraction of hydrocarbons or from mining activities, provided that such injection does not contain substances other than those resulting from these activities;
(c) the injection of water for technical reasons into geological formations from which hydrocarbons or other substances have been extracted or into geological formations which for natural reasons are permanently unsuitable for other purposes, provided that such injection does not contain substances other than those resulting from these activities;
(d) the reinjection of groundwater abstracted from mines and quarries or associated with the construction or maintenance of civil engineering works;
(e) the injection of natural gas or liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) for storage purposes into geological formations which, for natural reasons, are permanently unsuitable for other purposes;
(f) the injection of natural gas or liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) for storage purposes into other geological formations where there is an overriding need for security of gas supply, and where the injection is such as to prevent any present or future danger of deterioration in the quality of any receiving groundwater;
(g) the carrying out of construction and civil engineering works and similar activities on or in the ground which come into contact with groundwater, provided that the provisions of any law or regulatory administrative act concerning these works or activities are complied with;
(h) discharges of small quantities of substances for scientific purposes for characterisation, protection or remediation of water bodies, provided that they are strictly limited to the quantity required;
(i) the injection of carbon dioxide streams for storage purposes into geological formations which for natural reasons are permanently unsuitable for other purposes, provided that such injection is made in accordance with the provisions of the Law on the Storage of Carbon Dioxide in Geological Formations or is exempted from the scope of that law in accordance with subsection (2) of that Article.
(3) The exceptions specified in subsection (2) shall apply provided that the discharges do not compromise the achievement of the environmental objectives established for the water body into which these discharges are made.
(4) The implementation of the measures established on the basis of this Article shall not lead to an increase in the pollution of marine waters and, subject to the provisions of any other law, shall not directly or indirectly lead to an increase in the pollution of surface waters:
Provided that the above requirements shall not apply where compliance with them would result in an increase in the pollution of the environment as a whole.

Recovery of costs for water services
27.(1) Notwithstanding the provisions of any other law, the competent authority shall determine the pricing policy for water services in the Republic and for this purpose shall take account of the principle of recovery of the costs of water services, including environmental and resource costs, having regard to the economic analysis conducted in accordance with Annex III and in particular in accordance with the principle that the polluter pays.
(2) For the purposes of applying subsection (1), the competent authority shall ensure that by 2010 -
(a) water-pricing policies provide adequate incentives for users to use water resources efficiently and thereby contribute to the environmental objectives of this Law;
(b) it is ensured that in the recovery of the costs of water services, which will be based on the economic analysis to be carried out in accordance with Annex III and taking into account the 'polluter pays' principle, the various uses of water, which should be broken down at least into industry, households and agriculture, will contribute appropriately.
(3) In implementing the above, the competent authority may have regard to the social, environmental and economic effects of the recovery as well as the geographic and climatic conditions of the region or regions affected.
(4) The competent authority may decide not to apply the provisions of paragraph (b) of subsection (2) and consequently not to apply the relevant provisions of subsection (6)(a), if it considers that there are special circumstances which justify this for a specific water use activity and provided that the decision does not compromise the purposes of this Law and the achievement of its objectives.
(5) The provisions of this Article shall not prevent the funding of particular preventive or remedial measures in order to achieve the objectives of this Law.
(6) The river basin management plan prepared on the basis of Article 22 shall state -
(a) the planned actions for the implementation of subsections (1), (2) and (3); and
(b) in the event that a decision is taken on the basis of subsection (4), the reasons on which the decision is based.

The following information for the competent authority shall be included in a report to the European Commission:
i) Name and address of the competent authority: Official name and address.
ii) Geographical coverage of the river basin district: The names of the main rivers in the river basin district, together with a precise description of the boundaries of the river basin district. This information should, as far as possible, be available for introduction into the Geographic Information System (GIS) and/or the Geographic Information System of the Commission (GISCO).
iii) Legal status of the competent authority: Description of the legal status of the competent authority.
iv) Responsibilities: A description of the legal and administrative responsibilities of the competent authority and of its role in the river basin district.

ANNEX II
(Articles 1, 6, 20)
1. SURFACE WATERS
1.1. Characterisation of surface water body types

The competent authority shall identify the location and boundaries of bodies of surface water and shall carry out an initial characterisation of all such bodies in accordance with the following methodology. For this initial characterisation, the competent authority may group several bodies of surface water.
i) Bodies of surface water within the river basin district shall be categorised as either rivers, lakes, transitional waters or coastal waters, or as artificial surface water bodies or heavily modified water bodies.
ii) For each surface water category, the relevant surface water bodies within the river basin district shall be differentiated into types. These types are those defined using either "System A" or "System B", which are described in section 1.2.
iii) If System A is used, surface water bodies within the river basin district shall first be differentiated into the relevant ecoregions according to the geographical areas described in section 1.2 and shown on the relevant map in Annex XI. Subsequently, the water bodies within each ecoregion shall be differentiated into surface water body types based on the descriptors in the tables for System A.
iv) If System B is used, the competent authority must achieve at least the same degree of differentiation as would be achieved with System A. Accordingly, the surface water bodies within the river basin district shall be differentiated into types using the values of the obligatory descriptors and of such optional descriptors or combinations of descriptors as are required to ensure that the biological reference conditions are reliably derived.
v) For artificial and heavily modified surface water bodies, the differentiation shall be carried out in accordance with the descriptors for whichever of the surface water categories most closely resembles the heavily modified or artificial water body concerned.
vi) The competent authority shall submit a map or maps (in a GIS format) of the geographical location of the types corresponding to the degree of differentiation required under System A.

1.3. Establishment of type-specific reference conditions for the different types of surface water bodies
i) For each type of surface water body characterised in accordance with section 1.1, type-specific hydromorphological and physicochemical conditions shall be established representing the values of the hydromorphological and physicochemical quality elements specified in section 1.1 of Annex V for that surface water body type at high ecological status as defined in the relevant table in section 1.2 of Annex V. Type-specific biological reference conditions shall be established representing the values of the biological quality elements specified in section 1.1 of Annex V for that surface water body at high ecological status as defined in the relevant table of section 1.2 of Annex V.
ii) In applying the procedures of this section to heavily modified or artificial surface water bodies, references to high ecological status shall be construed as references to maximum ecological potential as defined in table 1.2.5 of Annex V. The values for maximum ecological potential for a water body shall be reviewed every six years.
iii) The type-specific conditions for the purposes of points i) and ii) and the type-specific biological conditions may be either spatially based or based on modelling, or may be derived using a combination of these methods. Where it is not possible to use these methods, the competent authority may use expert judgement to establish these conditions. In defining high ecological status with respect to the concentrations of specific synthetic pollutants, the detection limits are those that can be achieved with the techniques available at the time of establishing the type-specific conditions.
iv) For spatially based type-specific biological reference conditions, the competent authority shall establish a reference network for each surface water body type. The network shall contain a sufficient number of sites of high status to provide a sufficient level of confidence in the values for the reference conditions, given the variability in the values of the quality elements corresponding to high ecological status for that surface water body type and the modelling techniques that will be applied in accordance with point v).
v) Type-specific biological reference conditions based on modelling may be derived using either predictive models or hindcasting methods. The methods shall use historical, palaeological and other available data and shall provide a sufficient level of confidence in the values of the reference conditions to ensure that the conditions so derived are consistent and valid for each surface water body type.
vi) Where it is not possible to establish reliable reference conditions for a quality element in a surface water body type due to high natural variability and not just due to seasonal variations of that element, that element may be excluded from the assessment of ecological status for that surface water body type. In such circumstances, the competent authority shall state the reasons for this exclusion in the river basin management plan.

1.4. Identification of pressures
The competent authority shall collect and maintain information on the type and magnitude of the significant anthropogenic pressures to which the surface water bodies in each river basin district are liable to be subject, in particular:
calculates and identifies significant point source pollution, in particular by substances listed in Annex VIII, from urban, industrial, agricultural and other installations and activities, based, inter alia, on information collected under the legislation enacted for the purposes of harmonisation with Articles -
i) 15 and 17 of Directive 91/271/EEC,

and, for the purposes of the initial river basin management plan:

calculates and identifies significant diffuse source pollution, in particular by substances listed in Annex VIII, from urban, industrial, agricultural and other installations and activities, based, inter alia, on information collected under the legislation enacted for the purposes of harmonisation with -
i) Articles 3, 5 and 6 of Directive 91/676/EEC,
ii) Articles 7 and 17 of Directive 91/414/EEC,
iii) Directive 98/8/EC,
and, for the purposes of the first river basin management plan:
iv) with Directives 75/440/EEC, 76/160/EEC, 76/464/EEC, 78/659/EEC and 79/923/EEC.
calculates and identifies significant water abstraction for urban, industrial, agricultural and other uses, including seasonal variations and total annual demand, and the loss of water in distribution systems;
calculates and identifies the impact of significant water flow regulation, including water transfer and diversion, on the general flow characteristics and water balances;
calculates and identifies other significant anthropogenic impacts on the status of surface waters; and
calculates the land use patterns, including the identification of the main urban, industrial and agricultural areas and, where appropriate, fisheries and forests.

1.5. Assessment of impact
The competent authority shall assess the susceptibility of the status of surface water bodies to the aforementioned pressures.

2. GROUNDWATER
2.1. Initial characterisation
The competent authority shall carry out an initial characterisation of all groundwater bodies in order to assess their uses and the risks that they will not meet the objectives for each groundwater body in accordance with Articles 9-17. For this initial characterisation, the competent authority may group several groundwater bodies. During this analysis, existing hydrological, geological, pedological, land use, discharge, abstraction and other data may be used to identify:

the location and boundaries of the groundwater body or bodies,
the pressures to which the groundwater body or bodies are liable to be subject, including inter alia:

diffuse sources of pollution,

point sources of pollution,

abstraction,

artificial recharge,

the general character of the overlying strata in the catchment from which the groundwater body receives its recharge,

groundwater bodies for which there are directly dependent surface water ecosystems or terrestrial ecosystems.

2.2. Further characterisation
Following this initial characterisation, the competent authority shall carry out a further characterisation of those groundwater bodies or groups of bodies which have been identified as being at risk in order to establish a more precise assessment of the significance of the risk and to identify any measures to be required under Articles 18-21. This characterisation shall therefore include relevant information on the impact of human activities and, where relevant, information on:

the geological characteristics of the groundwater body, including the extent and type of geological units,

the hydrogeological characteristics of the groundwater body, including hydraulic conductivity, porosity and confinement,

the characteristics of the superficial deposits and soils in the catchment from which the groundwater body receives its recharge, including their thickness, porosity, hydraulic conductivity and absorptive properties of the deposits and soils,

the stratification characteristics of the water within the groundwater body,

an inventory of associated surface systems, including terrestrial ecosystems and bodies of surface water, with which the groundwater body is dynamically linked,

estimates of the directions and rates of exchange of water between the groundwater body and associated surface systems, and

sufficient data to calculate the long-term annual average rate of overall recharge,

the characterisation of the chemical composition of the groundwater, including the specification of the contributions from human activity. When establishing natural background levels for these bodies of groundwater, the competent authority may use typologies for groundwater characterisation.

2.3. Review of the impact of human activity on groundwater
For those groundwater bodies that are identified in the initial characterisation carried out in accordance with section 2.1 as being at risk of failing to meet the objectives set for each body under Articles 9-17, the following information shall, where relevant, be collected and maintained for each groundwater body:
a) the location of abstraction points in the groundwater body, except for:

abstraction points providing less than 10 m3 per day on average, or

points for the abstraction of water intended for human consumption providing less than 10 m3 per day on average or serving fewer than 50 persons;
b) the mean annual rates of abstraction from such points;
c) the chemical composition of water abstracted from the groundwater body;
d) the location of points in the groundwater body where water is directly discharged;
e) the rates of discharge at such points;
f) the chemical composition of the water discharged into the groundwater body; and
g) land use in the catchment or catchments from which the groundwater body receives its recharge, including pollutant inputs and man-made alterations to the recharge characteristics such as rainwater run-off diversion and percolation due to land sealing, artificial recharge, damming or drainage.

2.4. Review of the impact of changes in groundwater level
The competent authority shall also identify those groundwater bodies for which lower objectives are specified under Articles 9-17, inter alia as a result of the impact of the status of the body on:
i) surface water and associated terrestrial ecosystems;
ii) water regulation, flood protection and land drainage;
iii) human development.

2.5. Review of the impact of pollution on groundwater quality
The competent authority shall also identify those groundwater bodies for which less stringent objectives are specified under Article 14 where, as a result of the impact of human activity as set out in Article 6(1), the groundwater body is so polluted that achieving good groundwater chemical status is infeasible or disproportionately expensive.

ANNEX III
(Articles 2, 6, 27)
ECONOMIC ANALYSIS
The economic analysis shall contain sufficient information in sufficient detail (taking account of the costs associated with collecting the relevant data) in order to:
a) make the calculations required to take account, in accordance with Article 27, of the principle of recovery of the costs of water services, taking account of long-term forecasts of supply and demand for water in the river basin district and, where necessary:

of estimates of the volume, prices and costs associated with water services, and

of estimates of relevant investment, including forecasts of such investment;
b) make a judgement about the most cost-effective combination of measures in relation to water uses to be included in the programme of measures under Articles 19-21, based on estimates of the potential costs of such measures.

ANNEX IV
(Article 8)
REGISTER OF PROTECTED AREAS

The register of protected areas required under Article 8 shall include the following types of protected areas:
i) Areas designated for the abstraction of water intended for human consumption under Article 23.
ii) Areas designated for the protection of economically significant aquatic species.
iii) Water bodies designated as recreational waters, including areas designated as bathing waters, under the legislation enacted for the purposes of harmonisation with Directive 76/160/EEC.
iv) Nutrient-sensitive areas, including areas designated as vulnerable zones under the legislation enacted for the purposes of harmonisation with Directive 91/676/EEC and areas designated as sensitive areas under the legislation enacted for the purposes of harmonisation with Directive 91/271/EEC.
v) Areas designated for the protection of habitats or species where the maintenance or improvement of the status of water is an important factor in their protection, including relevant Natura 2000 sites designated under the legislation enacted for the purposes of harmonisation with Directives 92/43/EEC and 79/409/EEC.

The summary of the register required as part of the river basin management plan shall include maps showing the location of each protected area and a description of the legislation under which they have been designated.

2. GROUNDWATER

2.1. Quantitative status of groundwater

2.1.1. Parameter for the classification of quantitative status
Groundwater level regime

2.1.2. Definition of quantitative status

Element	Good status
Groundwater level	The level of groundwater in the groundwater body ensures that the available groundwater resource is not exceeded by the long-term annual average rate of abstraction. <br><br> Accordingly, the level of the groundwater is not subject to anthropogenic alterations such as would result in: <br> - failure to achieve the environmental objectives set out in Articles 9-17 for associated surface waters, <br> - any significant diminution in the status of such waters, <br> - any significant damage to terrestrial ecosystems which depend directly on the groundwater body, <br> and temporary, or continuous but spatially limited, alterations to the direction of flow due to changes in level may occur, but such reversals do not lead to saline or other intrusions and do not constitute a permanent and clearly identified trend, resulting from anthropogenic causes, in the alteration of the direction of flow capable of leading to such intrusions.

2.2. Monitoring of the quantitative status of groundwater
2.2.1. Groundwater level monitoring network
The groundwater monitoring network shall be established in accordance with the requirements of Articles 7 and 8. The monitoring network shall be designed so as to provide a reliable assessment of the quantitative status of all groundwater bodies or groups of bodies, including an assessment of the available groundwater resource. The competent authority shall provide one or more maps in the river basin management plan showing the groundwater monitoring network.

2.2.2. Density of monitoring points
The network shall include sufficient representative monitoring points to estimate the groundwater level in each groundwater body or group of bodies, taking account of short- and long-term variations in recharge, and in particular:

for groundwater bodies identified as being at risk of failing to achieve the environmental objectives of Articles 9-17, a sufficient density of monitoring points shall be ensured to assess the impact of abstractions and discharges on the groundwater level.

2.2.3. Frequency of monitoring
The frequency of observations shall be sufficient to allow for the assessment of the quantitative status of each groundwater body or group of bodies, taking into account short- and long-term variations in recharge, and in particular:

for groundwater bodies identified as being at risk of failing to achieve the environmental objectives of Articles 9-17, a sufficient frequency of measurements shall be ensured to assess the impact of abstractions and discharges on the groundwater level.

2.2.4. Interpretation and presentation of the quantitative status of groundwater
The results obtained from the monitoring network for a groundwater body or group of bodies shall be used to assess the quantitative status of that body or bodies. Subject to section 2.5, the competent authority shall prepare a map based on this assessment of the quantitative status of groundwater, using the following colour codes:
Good: green
Poor: red

2.3. Interpretation and presentation of the chemical status of groundwater

2.3.1. Parameters for the determination of the chemical status of groundwater

Conductivity

Concentrations of pollutants

2.3.2. Definition of good groundwater chemical status

Element	Good status
General	The chemical composition of the groundwater body is such that the concentrations of pollutants: <br> - as specified below, do not exhibit the effects of saline or other intrusions, <br> - do not exceed the quality standards applicable under other relevant Community legislation in accordance with Article 17 of Directive 2000/60/EC, <br> - are not such as would result in failure to achieve the environmental objectives of Articles 9-17 for associated surface waters, nor in any significant diminution of the ecological or chemical quality of such bodies, nor in any significant damage to terrestrial ecosystems which depend directly on the groundwater body.
Conductivity	Changes in conductivity do not indicate saline or other intrusion into the groundwater body.

2.4. Monitoring of the chemical status of groundwater

2.4.1. Groundwater monitoring network
The groundwater monitoring network shall be established in accordance with the requirements of Articles 23 and 24 of the Law. The monitoring network shall be designed so as to provide a coherent and comprehensive overview of the chemical status of groundwater within each river basin and to detect the presence of long-term anthropogenically induced upward trends in pollutants.

On the basis of the characterisation and impact assessment carried out in accordance with Article 6 and Annex II, the competent authority shall, for each period of a river basin management plan, establish a surveillance monitoring programme. The results of this programme shall be used to establish an operational monitoring programme to be applied for the remaining period of the plan.

The plan shall provide estimates of the level of confidence and precision of the results provided by the monitoring programmes.

2.4.2. Surveillance monitoring
Objective
Surveillance monitoring shall be carried out in order to:

supplement and validate the impact assessment procedure,

provide information for use in the assessment of long-term trends due to changes in natural conditions and to human activity.

Selection of monitoring points
Sufficient monitoring points shall be selected for:

bodies which are identified as being at risk following the characterisation carried out in accordance with Annex II,

bodies which cross a Member State boundary.

Selection of parameters
The following series of core parameters shall be monitored in all selected groundwater bodies:

oxygen content,

pH value,

conductivity,

nitrate,

ammonium.
Those bodies which are identified, in accordance with Annex II, as being at significant risk of failing to achieve good status shall also be monitored for those parameters which are indicative of the impacts of those pressures.

2.4.3. Operational monitoring
Objective
Operational monitoring shall be undertaken in the periods between surveillance monitoring programmes in order to:

establish the chemical status of all groundwater bodies or groups of bodies determined to be at risk,

establish the presence of long-term anthropogenically induced upward trends in the concentration of pollutants.

Selection of monitoring points
Operational monitoring shall be carried out for all those groundwater bodies or groups of bodies which, on the basis of both the impact assessment carried out in accordance with Annex II and surveillance monitoring, are identified as being at risk of failing to meet the objectives of Articles 9-17. The selection of monitoring points shall also reflect an assessment of how representative the monitoring data from the particular point are in terms of the quality of the relevant one or more groundwater bodies.

Frequency of monitoring
Operational monitoring shall be carried out in the periods between surveillance monitoring programmes at a frequency sufficient to detect the impacts of the relevant pressures and, in any event, at least once a year.

2.4.4. Trend identification
The competent authority shall use data from both surveillance and operational monitoring to identify long-term anthropogenically induced upward trends in pollutant concentrations and the reversal of these trends. The base year or period from which such trends are calculated shall be identified. The calculation of trends shall be carried out for a body or, where appropriate, a group of groundwater bodies. The reversal of a trend shall be demonstrated statistically and the level of confidence in the identification shall be stated.

2.4.5. Interpretation and presentation of the chemical status of groundwater
In assessing status, the results of individual monitoring points within a groundwater body shall be aggregated for the body as a whole. Subject to the relevant directives, for a groundwater body to be in good status, with respect to the chemical parameters for which Community-wide environmental quality standards are set:

the mean value of the results of monitoring at each point in the body or group of groundwater bodies is calculated; and

in accordance with Article 17 of Directive 2000/60/EC, these mean values are used to demonstrate compliance with good groundwater chemical status.

Subject to section 2.5, the competent authority shall produce a map of the chemical status of groundwater, using the following colour codes:
Good: green
Poor: red

The competent authority shall also indicate on the map with a black dot those groundwater bodies which are subject to a significant and sustained upward trend in the concentrations of any pollutant resulting from the impact of human activity. The reversal of a trend shall be indicated on the map with a blue dot.

These maps shall be included in the river basin management plan.

2.5. Presentation of groundwater status
The competent authority shall provide in the river basin management plan a map showing, for each groundwater body or group of bodies, both the quantitative and the chemical status of that body or group of bodies, using colour codes in accordance with the requirements of sections 2.2.4 and 2.4.5. The competent authority has the option of not producing separate maps for sections 2.2.4 and 2.4.5, but in that case, it shall also indicate, in accordance with the requirements of section 2.4.5, on the map required by this section, those bodies that are subject to a significant and sustained upward trend in the concentration of any pollutant or any reversal of that trend.
