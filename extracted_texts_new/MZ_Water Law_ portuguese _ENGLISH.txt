Law No. 16/91: Approves the Water Law.
Law No. 17/91: Approves the Law on Public Enterprises.

ASSEMBLY OF THE REPUBLIC
Law No. 16/91
of 3 August

The importance of water resources in all sectors of life has led to an ever-increasing need for their use.
Water is used for various purposes according to the needs and quantities that each user deems necessary. In order that the use of water by multiple interested parties does not prejudice the needs of some, it is essential to create mechanisms conducive to its distribution or supply in accordance with the needs of each one.
This Water Law establishes the water resources that belong to the public domain, the principles of water management, the need for an inventory of all existing water resources in the country, the general regime for their use, the priorities to be taken into account, the general rights of users and their corresponding obligations, among others.
The right to use waters of the public domain will be recognized under a free use regime, in certain cases, and by means of use authorizations or development concessions, in specially regulated cases.
The Water Law emerges as a fundamental instrument for the realization and satisfaction of the interests of the Mozambican people.
In these terms, under the provisions of Article 135(1) of the Constitution of the Republic, the Assembly of the Republic determines:

CHAPTER I
Preliminary Provisions

ARTICLE 1
(Ownership of Waters)
1. Inland waters, both surface waters and their respective beds, and groundwaters, whether they spring naturally or not, are the property of the State, constituting the public water domain.
2. The public water domain also includes works, hydraulic equipment, and their appurtenances created by the State or on its behalf for the purpose of public utility.
3. The public water domain is inalienable and imprescriptible, and the right to its use and enjoyment shall be granted in a manner that guarantees its preservation and management for the benefit of the national interest.

ARTICLE 2
(Objective)
1. The objective of this law is to define, in relation to inland waters:
a) The public water domain of the State and the general policy for its management;
b) The general legal regime for the activities of protection and conservation, inventory, use, control, and oversight of water resources;
c) The powers attributed to the Government in relation to the public water domain.
2. Natural mineral, mineral-medicinal, and thermal waters are regulated by specific legislation.
214-(12)
3. The protection, use, and exploitation of fishery resources in inland waters shall be regulated by their own legislation, as well as navigation and floating.
4. The exploration and exploitation of mineral resources in beds, banks, and floodplains shall be subject to their own legislation.

ARTICLE 3
(Regarding Beds)
1. The bed of inland waters is limited by the bank line. The bank line is defined by the waters when they reach their ordinary high-water mark. The bed includes islets, mudflats, and sandbanks formed therein by alluvial deposition.
2. It shall be the responsibility of the regional water administrations to determine the legal bank line of the country's water bodies and watercourses and to enter them in the water cadastre. It shall also be their responsibility to adopt the necessary measures for the protection of beds and bank lines.
3. The use of beds is subject to the licensing and concession regime of this law.

ARTICLE 4
(Regarding Banks)
1. A bank is the strip of land adjoining or overlooking the line that limits the bed of the waters. Throughout their entire longitudinal extent, banks are subject to the partial protection regime defined in the Land Law.
2. It shall be the responsibility of the regional water administrations, without prejudice to the provisions of the Land Law, to ensure the preservation, conservation, and defense of the partial protection zones defined in the preceding paragraph.

ARTICLE 5
(Floodplains)
1. Floodplains are areas that may be flooded during extraordinary floods of natural, continuous, or discontinuous water bodies and watercourses, and are defined as such in the cadastre.
2. The lands comprised by floodplains maintain the legal status and ownership they have, but may, however, be declared partial protection zones or be subject to other restrictions to ensure the safety of persons and property.

ARTICLE 6
(Groundwater)
For the purposes of this law, groundwater is understood as that which, being beneath the earth's surface, is or can be brought to the surface by human action. Measures for its protection and use may include the solid and liquid parts of aquifers and any necessary protection zones.

CHAPTER II
General Water Management Policy

SECTION I
Principles and Guidelines

ARTICLE 7
(Principles of Water Management)
1. The action of the State in the water management sector shall be carried out by the Ministry of Construction and Water with recourse to the National Water Council and is inspired by the following principles:
a) Unity and coherence in the management of the country's river basins, that is, the set of watercourses that flow into the same main watercourse and the areas they drain, as well as groundwater aquifers;
b) Institutional coordination and participation of the populace in major decisions relating to water management policy;
c) Harmonization of the water management policy with the general policy of land use planning and conservation of the environmental balance.
2. Hydraulic works may not be approved without prior analysis of their social, economic, and environmental effects and impacts.
3. Studies on the effects referred to in the preceding paragraph shall be the responsibility of the owners of large-scale works. Regulations shall define the criteria for classifying works for the purpose of imputing the cost of the studies.

ARTICLE 8
(Guidelines of the Water Management Policy)
The State shall be responsible for progressively implementing, in regions defined as priority for intervention, a water management policy aimed at achieving the following objectives:
a) Better use of available water for all purposes through its rational and planned utilization, with a view to satisfying the needs of the population and the development of the national economy;
b) Continuous and sufficient supply of potable water to the population to meet domestic and hygiene needs;
c) Promotion, frameworking, and regulation of the use of water for agricultural, industrial, and hydroelectric purposes;
d) Better use of public domain waters, namely, by combating waste, the possibility of using water for multiple purposes through recycling, controlling losses to the sea, and carrying out works and equipment for water retention and storage and for flow regulation;
e) Promotion, according to the needs and priorities of government action, of investigation, exploration, and abstraction actions aimed at increasing the overall volume of available water resources;
f) Improvement of sanitation, the fight against pollution, and against the deterioration of water by saline intrusion;
g) Prevention and combat of the harmful effects of water, namely, in the sectors of combating soil erosion and flood control;
h) Seeking balances for all users in cases of multiple and conflicting uses of public domain waters;
i) Safeguarding the interests of promoting river navigation;
j) Improving the management of hydraulic infrastructures;
k) Promoting training, education, and awareness campaigns, for both the populace and administration agents, regarding the main problems of water management;

l) Progressive development of legislation intended to regulate the use and protection of water resources;
m) Ensuring the overall balance between the total available water resources and total consumption.

ARTICLE 9
(Inventory of Water Resources and Needs)
1. It shall be the responsibility of the Ministry of Construction and Water to carry out a general inventory of water resources in their quantitative and qualitative aspects and to update it periodically, in order to support the planning and integrated management of water resources and the implementation of hydraulic works.
2. The general inventory shall include an inventory of water resources, both available and potential, surface and groundwater, as well as present and future needs and the balances of water resources and needs.
3. The Government shall define, as needed, the modalities for carrying out the inventories as well as the functions to be performed by the local bodies of the State apparatus.

ARTICLE 10
(Water Cadastre)
1. A National Water Cadastre is hereby created, covering the entire national territory and to be implemented progressively and with priority for the main river basins.
2. The National Water Cadastre shall be especially responsible for:
a) The registration of concessions and licenses for water use, their characteristics, and subsequent modifications, as well as authorizations for effluent discharge, including those granted under previous legislation, when recognized under the terms of Articles 69 and 70 of this law;
b) The registration of common uses traditionally recognized under the provisions of Article 71 of this law.
3. The organization and functioning of the National Water Cadastre shall be regulated by a ministerial diploma.

ARTICLE 11
(Mandatory Registration)
1. Concessions, licenses for water use, and authorizations for effluent discharge are subject to registration.
2. Registration is mandatory, and it is the responsibility of the beneficiary to apply for it within three months from the date on which the right to use was granted or recognized.
3. Rights to water use subject to mandatory registration only produce effects with respect to third parties after registration has been completed.

ARTICLE 12
(Information Contained in the Register)
The registration entry shall contain, without prejudice to what may be established in its own regulation, the following information:
a) Name, domicile, and identity card number of the beneficiary or beneficiaries;
214-(13)
b) Type and location of the use, volumes of water to be used, methods, equipment, and works carried out for the use;
c) Established servitudes;
d) Obligations of the beneficiaries;
e) Defined treatment for effluents.

ARTICLE 13
(General Scheme for the Use of Water Resources)
1. The General Scheme for the Use of Water Resources aims, namely, to:
a) Improve the satisfaction of water needs through the correct use of available resources and the rationalization of their use;
b) Balance and harmonize national, regional, and sectoral development;
c) Protect the environment, ensuring that water uses are carried out without prejudice to the minimum flow and the ecological flow, and respecting, as far as possible, the natural regime of water bodies and watercourses;
d) Protect water quality.
2. It shall be the responsibility of the Council of Ministers to approve the General Scheme for the Use of Water Resources and its periodic adjustments, to be made according to needs.

SECTION II
Institutional Coordination

ARTICLE 14
(International Cooperation)
1. The participation of the Republic of Mozambique in international cooperation organizations in the domain of water shall aim at the following objectives:
a) Adoption of coordinated management measures for watercourses of the same river basin, taking into account the interests of all interested States;
b) Sharing of waters of common interest and their joint use;
c) Joint preparation or implementation of research programs, projects, and construction of infrastructures;
d) Control of water quality, pollution, and soil erosion;
e) Exchange of information on matters of common interest.
2. It shall be the responsibility of the Minister of Construction and Water to promote the necessary international cooperation actions with neighboring States or States of the region, with a view to ensuring the best management of river basins of common interest and safeguarding national interests, as well as ensuring the participation of the Republic of Mozambique in the work of cooperation bodies that may be created.
3. It shall be the responsibility of the Council of Ministers to adopt the necessary measures to ensure the implementation of the recommendations and decisions made in these organizations.

ARTICLE 15
(Decentralizing Initiatives)
1. The Ministry of Construction and Water shall encourage initiatives from its bodies, the populace, and public and private enterprises in the domain of water management that are compatible with the guidelines of the general policy of the State.
214-(14)
2. The carrying out, by these entities and under terms to be defined in a regulatory diploma, of activities and operations of exploration, abstraction, equipping, and provision of water shall also be encouraged. It shall also be the responsibility of the Ministry of Construction and Water to ensure the technical oversight of projects and their execution.

ARTICLE 16
(Intersectoral Cooperation)
In implementing the general guidelines of the water management policy and without prejudice to its own powers, the Ministry of Construction and Water shall promote the necessary coordination with the other Ministries interested in water management, namely Agriculture, Foreign Affairs, Cooperation, Industry and Energy, Mineral Resources, State Administration, and Health, the National Planning Commission, and the executive councils.

ARTICLE 17
(National Water Council)
1. The National Water Council is hereby created, an advisory body to the Council of Ministers and for inter-ministerial coordination, charged with opining on relevant aspects of the general water management policy and ensuring its compliance.
2. In addition to its advisory functions, the National Water Council is responsible, namely, for:
a) Proposing the objectives of the Government's water policy in the social, economic, and environmental domain;
b) Identifying the institutional, human resource, financial, and economic limitations that affect the pursuit of the water policy objectives and proposing appropriate solutions;
c) Keeping the Council of Ministers informed about critical and recurring aspects that affect the development and conservation of water resources, proposing the most appropriate measures;
d) Proposing programs, projects, and measures necessary for the development and conservation of water resources;
e) Detecting macroeconomic and macro-institutional factors that affect the development and conservation of the country's water resources and proposing appropriate solutions;
f) Issuing an opinion on water projects and programs before they are submitted for international financing or before funds from the State budget are allocated;
g) Requesting, from public and private bodies, the information or clarifications necessary for the performance of its duties;
h) Recommending the adoption of specific measures or the development of necessary actions by the central and local bodies of the State and other organisms with territorial or functional competence in the area of water resources.
3. The National Water Council may propose to ministries and other public bodies lines of study and research for the development of technical innovations regarding the procurement, employment, conservation, recovery, integral treatment, and economy of water.

4. Its composition, organic structure, and functioning shall be regulated by decree of the Council of Ministers.

ARTICLE 18
(Regional Water Administrations)
1. The management of water resources shall be carried out by regional water administrations organized on the basis of river basins and fundamentally oriented towards the administration of the region's water resources.
2. The regional water administrations are public institutions endowed with legal personality and administrative, patrimonial, and financial autonomy, under the tutelage of the Ministry of Construction and Water, through the National Directorate of Water. Their territorial scope may comprise one or several river basins.
3. They are responsible, namely, for:
a) Participating in the preparation, implementation, and review of the hydrological occupation plan for the basin;
b) The administration and control of the public water domain and the creation and maintenance of the water cadastre and the register of private uses, as well as the assessment and collection of fees for water use;
c) The licensing and concession of the use of public domain waters, the authorization of discharges, the imposition of administrative servitudes, as well as the inspection and oversight of compliance with the requirements to which they are subject;
d) The approval of hydraulic works to be carried out and their oversight;
e) Declaring the expiration of authorizations, licenses, and concessions and their termination or revocation;
f) The design, construction, and operation of works carried out with their own means, as well as those assigned to them;
g) The provision of technical services related to their duties and advising local State bodies, public and private entities, and individuals;
h) Collecting and keeping updated the hydrological data necessary for the management of river basins;
i) Reconciling conflicts arising from the use of water;
j) Carrying out the policing of waters, applying sanctions, ordering the demolition of works, the elimination of unauthorized uses, and the closure of sources of contamination.
4. The statutes of the regional water administrations shall be approved by ministerial diploma.

ARTICLE 19
(Bodies of the Regional Water Administrations)
1. The regional water administrations, in addition to the bodies that may be statutorily defined, shall include a management council composed of representatives from the Ministries of Construction and Water, Agriculture, Industry and Energy, Mineral Resources, local State bodies, and user organizations.
2. The management council, without prejudice to what may be established, shall be responsible, namely, for reviewing the program of activities, works, and the budget.

ARTICLE 20
(User Associations)
1. Users of water resources may voluntarily form associations, namely irrigators' associations.
2. The regional water administrations shall be responsible for promoting the establishment of user associations, and the use of certain resources may be conditioned on their creation.

CHAPTER III
Use of Waters

SECTION I
General Regime

ARTICLE 21
(Common and Private Uses)
1. Waters of the public domain, with regard to their use, are classified as waters for common use and waters for private use. The private use of waters may result from law, from a license, or from a concession.
2. Common uses are those that aim, without the use of a siphon or mechanized means, to satisfy the domestic, personal, and family needs of the user, including the watering of livestock and small-scale irrigation.
3. Private uses resulting from law are those that may be directly carried out by the holders of the right to use land, unless otherwise provided.
4. Access to private uses resulting from a license or concession shall be available to any persons, whether natural or legal, public or private, national or foreign, duly authorized to operate in the national territory, under the terms of this law and provided that they do not jeopardize the ecological balance and the environment.

ARTICLE 22
(Freedom of Use)
1. Common uses of water are free of charge and unrestricted, that is, they are carried out without the need for a prior license or concession. Regulations may specify the conditions to which, generally or locally, common use must adhere, namely in cases of exceptional scarcity.
2. Common uses are carried out in accordance with the traditional regime of use and without altering the quality of the water or significantly changing its flow. Waters may not be diverted from their beds nor may the banks be altered.

SECTION II
Uses Resulting from Law

ARTICLE 23
(Uses Established by Law)
1. The holders of the right to use land, for the satisfaction of their domestic needs and the normal and foreseeable needs of agriculture, may use, without a license and without affecting pre-existing common uses when traditionally established and the rights of third parties:
a) The waters of water bodies, that is, of lakes, lagoons, and swamps existing on the respective plot;
b) The waters of springs that do not cross, flowing freely, the boundaries of the respective plot or do not flow into a current;
214-(15)
c) Groundwater not included in protection zones, provided they do not disturb its regime or deteriorate its quality;
d) Rainwater.
2. Users of plots surrounding lakes, lagoons, and swamps may use their respective waters under the conditions established in the preceding paragraph, unless by their volume and importance they require a license or concession in accordance with what is established in the cadastre. An identical requirement may be imposed on the use provided for in paragraph 1.
3. The artificial accumulation of rainwater by land users, beyond the limits to be defined in regulations, shall be conditioned on the private use regime.

ARTICLE 24
(Requisition)
1. In cases of force majeure, namely droughts, floods, or other natural calamities, and for as long as they persist, administrative authorities may impose the common use of the waters referred to in the preceding article for the benefit of the population.
2. It shall be the responsibility of the administrative authorities to define the access routes, timetable for use, and other conditions.
3. The user of the plot shall have the right to be compensated for any damages caused.

SECTION III
Uses Resulting from a License or Concession

ARTICLE 25
(Private Use)
Waters of the public domain, except as provided in Article 23, may be subject to private use by means of a license or concession under the terms of this law and its regulations.

ARTICLE 26
(Priority of Private Uses)
1. The supply of water to the population, for human consumption and for the satisfaction of sanitary needs, has priority over other private uses.
2. Private uses of water are not authorized to the detriment of the quantities necessary for environmental protection.
3. Conflicts arising from a lack of water for the satisfaction of different objectives shall be resolved based on the socio-economic profitability of the respective uses.

ARTICLE 27
(Regarding Applications for Licenses and Concessions)
1. Applications for a license or concession may only be denied when one of the following circumstances occurs:
a) There is no available water or the needs to be satisfied are not justified;
b) The satisfaction of the needs compromises the quantitative or qualitative protection of the water, unless the utility of the use, the scale of its impact, or the impossibility or economic unfeasibility of alternative uses dictate otherwise;
c) They are incompatible with uses contained in approved plans or they are uses that must be carried out by public entities;
214-(16)
d) The requested uses result in prejudice to third parties whose rights must be respected.
2. An appeal may be lodged by third parties against the granting of the application, based on the provisions of subparagraph d) of the preceding paragraph.

ARTICLE 28
(Rights of Users)
1. The right to private use confers upon its holder the possibility, within the stipulated period, of making the use determined for them, and for that purpose, they may carry out the appropriate works and, under terms to be established, temporarily occupy neighboring lands and establish the necessary servitudes.
2. This right is granted with reservation of pre-existing common uses and the rights of third parties.
3. The possibility of use may be reviewed if there is an insufficiency of abstraction and adduction equipment, an unforeseeable decrease in the flow or volume of water subject to the right of use, or a calculation error in the assessment of the flow.
4. Modification of the characteristics of the license or concession may only be made with the prior and express authorization of the granting entity.

ARTICLE 29
(Transfer of the Right to Use)
1. Waters granted for agricultural or industrial purposes are transferred together with the right to use the land where these operations are located and under the same conditions.
2. Without prejudice to the provisions of the preceding paragraph, the right to private use of waters is transferred, between living persons, by means of express authorization from the Minister of Construction and Water and, upon the death of the holder, in favor of the spouse and heirs under the terms of civil law.
3. The transfer of the right to use water does not entail an extension of the term of the license or concession.

ARTICLE 30
(General Obligations of Users)
The general obligations of users are:
a) To respect the conditions established in the act constituting the right;
b) To use the water in a rational and economical manner, giving it only the defined purpose;
c) To make timely payment of the stipulated tariffs and financial charges;
d) To participate in tasks of common interest, namely those intended to prevent the deterioration of the quantity and quality of water and soil;
e) To provide requested information, comply with the guidelines transmitted by the competent authorities, and submit to necessary inspections;
f) To ensure the minimization of environmental impact and, in particular, to safeguard water quality;
g) To respect the rights of other legitimate users of the waters.

ARTICLE 31
(Abuse of Right)
The exercise of the right to use water is abusive and consequently illegitimate if it manifestly exceeds the limits imposed by the social or economic purpose of that right, by good faith, and by good customs, namely due to waste or misuse of water, whatever the title asserted.

SUBSECTION 1
Licensing

ARTICLE 32
(Licenses)
1. The private use of water shall depend on a license when practiced through works of a non-permanent nature that do not alter the banks or beds of currents, lakes, lagoons, or swamps.
2. A license is also required for:
a) The prospecting, abstraction, and use of groundwater included in protection zones;
b) The installation of reservoirs, the establishment of crops or plantations, and the felling of trees in the beds and on the banks of natural, continuous or discontinuous currents, and of lakes, lagoons, and swamps;
c) The extraction of inert materials, namely sand and gravel, from the beds and banks of natural, continuous or discontinuous currents, and of lakes, lagoons, and swamps.

ARTICLE 33
(Nature of the Right Recognized by the License)
1. The right to the private use of water by means of a license is granted for a period of five years, subject to renewal.
2. Licenses are precarious and revocable, and cannot serve as grounds for opposition to applications for a concession. Their respective holders shall not be entitled to any compensation for damages that may arise from such concessions.

ARTICLE 34
(Revocation of the License)
1. Licenses terminate at the end of their term or its renewals and are revocable, namely on the following grounds:
a) Non-compliance with the essential obligations fixed in the license, abuse of the exercise of the right, or repeated violation of the rights of third parties;
b) Public interest in allocating the water to other private uses;
c) Force majeure, namely droughts, floods, or other natural calamities of lasting effect.
2. The grounds provided for in subparagraphs b) and c) of the preceding paragraph only determine the revocation of the license when the needs cannot be met by the simple requisition of part of the granted flows.
3. The requisition of part of the flows, as well as the revocation of the license, imply a duty for the State to compensate when determined by virtue of the provisions of subparagraph b) of paragraph 1.

SUBSECTION II
Concessions

ARTICLE 35
(Concession)
The private use of water is subject to the concession regime in all cases not provided for in Article 32.

3 AUGUST 1991
ARTICLE 36
(Nature of the Right Recognized by Concessions)
1. Concessions are granted temporarily for a period of up to fifty years, subject to renewal. The term may be extended when there is a need to carry out works whose cost cannot be amortized within the remaining period.
2. The granting of a concession implies authorization to use, in accordance with the approved projects, the lands necessary for the execution of the works and the proper exploitation of the concession, upon payment of the fees and indemnities that are due.
3. The rights arising from the concession and the set of things over which these rights are exercised cannot be encumbered without the authorization of the entity that granted it.
4. Once the concession is terminated, all installations and assets that comprise it shall revert to the State, except in the case of the exhaustion of the resource.

ARTICLE 37
(Application for a Concession)
The application for a concession is accompanied by a justificatory memorandum with the economic and technical reasons for the undertaking.

ARTICLE 38
(Review of the Concession)
1. The concession may be reviewed:
a) When the determining assumptions for its granting have changed;
b) In case of force majeure and at the request of the concessionaire;
c) When there is a need to adapt it to water management plans.
2. When the review is made under the provisions of subparagraph c) of the preceding paragraph, the concessionaire is entitled to be compensated in accordance with the regime of expropriations for public utility.
3. The expenses, including those arising from the replacement of all or part of the allocated flows with others from a different source, may be passed on to the new beneficiaries.

ARTICLE 39
(Termination)
The concession is terminated:
a) at the end of the term of validity or its renewals;
b) by agreement between the parties or by rescission by its holder;
c) upon the disappearance of the need for water use or the exhaustion of the resource, that is, the irreversible sharp decline in flow or the degradation of its characteristics;
d) by revocation and by redemption.

ARTICLE 40
(Grounds for Revocation)
1. The entity that granted the concession shall be responsible for revoking it when any of the following circumstances occur:
a) Non-compliance with the essential obligations or the deadlines provided for in the concession;
214-(17)
b) Abuse of the exercise of the right to use water or repeated violation of the rights of third parties;
c) Permanent interruption of the exploitation of the concession for three consecutive years for reasons attributable to its holder;
d) Hindrance of the exercise of oversight by the State;
e) Contamination of the returned waters beyond the established values.
2. The revocation order is subject to appeal, and the appeal has suspensive effect, except when such effect could result in serious prejudice.

ARTICLE 41
(Redemption)
1. The entity that granted the concession may proceed with its redemption when there is a need to make the granted waters available, namely for the benefit of a more profitable use under the terms of Article 26.
2. The redemption shall be made upon compensation and after a certain period has elapsed from the start of the concession, to be fixed on a case-by-case basis, and comprised between one-third and one-half of its duration.
3. The redemption shall be notified to the concessionaire one year in advance and, after notification, the latter may not, without prior authorization, alter the contracts included in the objectives of the concession and previously concluded.

SUBSECTION III
Financial Charges

ARTICLE 42
(Fees)
1. Beneficiaries of water rights, de facto users, and users of public water services shall be subject to the payment of fees which may have as their object, in addition to the provisions of subparagraph a) of Article 44, the promotion of practices suitable for the correct use and conservation of water and the prevention of contamination or the reduction of its level.
2. Credits for debts of fees enjoy a real property lien over the lands, buildings, or installations where the waters are used and shall be collected coercively through the fiscal execution process.
3. The fees shall be fixed by decree upon the proposal of the National Water Council and shall constitute own revenues of the regional water administrations.

ARTICLE 43
(Amount of Fees)
1. The amount of the fees shall be established according to the measured or estimated volume of water required, based on the type and size of the activity exercised and the expected quantity of consumptive use, the nature of the user, and the type and volume of the discharged contaminant.
2. As an incentive for certain activities, preferential fees or exemptions may be established.

ARTICLE 44
(Infrastructure Use Fee)
1. Beneficiaries of hydraulic or basic sanitation infrastructures built by public entities are subject to the payment of a fee which shall be fixed by ministerial diploma upon the proposal of the National Water Council, taking into account, among other factors it deems pertinent, the following:
a) The costs incurred with the construction, operation, and conservation of the works;
b) The total number of beneficiaries;
c) The average contributory capacity of the same.
2. The amount of the fee shall be reduced when the works and installations are fully amortized.
3. Unless otherwise provided, infrastructure use fees shall constitute own revenues of the regional water administrations, with the provisions of Article 42(2) being applicable.

SECTION IV
Special Regimes

ARTICLE 45
(Potable Water Supply)
1. For the purposes of this Law, potable water is understood as that intended for food, the preparation and preservation of food and products intended for food, personal hygiene, domestic use, and the manufacture of carbonated beverages, mineral waters, and ice.
2. Private uses of water may not be granted or maintained to the detriment of the population's right to potable water.
3. Holders of private use rights must allow the neighboring population to obtain potable water, through the establishment of the respective administrative servitudes, when they cannot, without great difficulty, obtain it otherwise.
4. The supply of potable water is subject to compliance with the standards established in Articles 56 and 57 to ensure water quality.

ARTICLE 46
(Irrigation)
1. Users of water for irrigation must proceed with the intensive use and maximum valorization of water resources.
2. The beneficiaries of irrigation systems are responsible for adopting appropriate and economically justifiable measures to reduce water losses, namely through infiltration, evaporation, and leaks. It is also their responsibility to ensure that the most advisable soil moisture regime is practiced.
3. It shall be the responsibility of the entities that supervise irrigation systems to propose regulations that, obeying the principles enshrined in this law and its regulations, address the specificities of each system.

ARTICLE 47
(Fishing and Pisciculture)
1. The need for the maintenance and reproduction of fish species or other aquatic resources for industrial use may impose, for the benefit of the fishing economy, restrictions on the private use of water.
2. The transfer of public domain water for piscicultural purposes is subject to the concession regime.

ARTICLE 48
(Industry and Energy)
1. The waters of the public domain, by means of a concession and in addition to energy production, may be used for ore treatment, gravel washing, vegetable fiber treatment, and any other industrial purposes.
214-(18)
2. The concession diploma shall state the location of the hydraulic works, the power plants or factories and workshops to be built, the volume of water granted, and the industry or industries to be exploited.
3. When the industrial or energy exploitation itself is subject to a concession regime, the durations, terms, and other requirements of the two concessions must be harmonized.
4. It shall be the responsibility of the users to respect the provisions of this Law and its respective regulations on the rational use and protection of waters.

ARTICLE 49
(Obligations of the Concessionaire for Energy Production)
Concessionaires of hydroelectric projects shall be especially obliged to:
a) Allow the permanent flow downstream of the dams of the discharges that, in accordance with the approved operation scheme, are deemed necessary to safeguard the public interest or the legitimate interests of third parties;
b) Cede, without right to any compensation, the water necessary for the irrigation of areas covered by hydro-agricultural development works plans and for the supply of urban centers;
c) Take the pisciculture protection measures that are determined by higher authority;
d) Organize operation diagrams for the hydroelectric plant and for the use of water impounded in the reservoir and make the hydrometeorological observations that are determined by the National Directorate of Water, which may install and maintain in operation, at the concessionaire's expense, the devices and other installations it deems convenient for this purpose.

ARTICLE 50
(Navigation and Transport)
1. Navigation and transport on public domain watercourses and lakes, as well as the construction of wharves, ramps, and other complementary navigation facilities, are governed by their own legislation.
2. Regular transport service on these waters may only be authorized after obtaining the opinion of the respective regional water administration.

CHAPTER IV
Water Quality Protection

SECTION I
Prevention and Control of Water Contamination

ARTICLE 51
(Contamination)
For the purposes of this Law, water contamination consists of the action and effect of introducing materials, forms of energy, or creating conditions that, directly or indirectly, imply a prejudicial alteration of its quality in relation to subsequent uses or its ecological function.

3 AUGUST 1991
ARTICLE 52
(Protection Objectives)
The protection of the public water domain against contamination essentially aims to:
a) Achieve and maintain an adequate level of water quality;
b) Prevent the accumulation, in the subsoil, of toxic or dangerous compounds capable of contaminating groundwater;
c) Avoid any other action that could be a cause of its degradation.

ARTICLE 53
(Prohibited Activities)
Without prejudice to the provisions of Article 54, it is, in general, prohibited to:
a) Directly or indirectly carry out discharges that contaminate the waters;
b) Accumulate solid waste, refuse, or any substances that contaminate or create a danger of contaminating the waters;
c) Act upon the physical or biological environment linked to the water in a way that degrades it or creates a danger of its degradation;
d) Carry out, in the protection zones established in water management plans, any activities that involve or may involve a danger of contamination or degradation of the public water domain.

ARTICLE 54
(Prevention and Control)
1. Any activity likely to cause the contamination or degradation of the public water domain, and in particular the discharge of wastewater, excreta, or other substances into public domain waters, is subject to a special authorization to be granted by the regional water administrations and the payment of a fee.
2. Regulations shall establish quality standards for effluents, receiving water bodies, technological systems, and methods for joint and individual water treatments, and contaminating activities may be suspended or establishments closed until these measures are implemented.
3. The qualitative and quantitative limits from which discharge operations become dependent on authorization from the Minister of Construction and Water, to be granted after hearing the interested entities, shall also be fixed by regulation.
4. Discharge concessions and licenses are subject to modifications and restrictions according to public, environmental, and ecological needs. With respect to contamination, no acquired rights are recognized, and, when necessary, deadlines will be set for the progressive adaptation of the characteristics of the discharges.

ARTICLE 55
(Polluter's Liability)
Whoever, beyond the permitted limits, causes the contamination or degradation of the public water domain, regardless of the applicable sanction, shall be under the obligation, at their own expense, to restore the situation that would have existed if the event requiring reparation had not occurred.

214-(19)
SECTION II
Potable Water

ARTICLE 56
(Quality Control)
1. Natural or legal persons responsible for supplying water for consumption shall be responsible for ensuring that the facilities used and the water supplied comply with the requirements to be defined by ministerial diploma.
2. It shall be the responsibility of the Minister of Health to carry out the oversight and control of the quality of potable water and to define, namely:
a) The modalities for carrying out controls of the works and installations for water abstraction, treatment, storage, transport, and distribution;
b) The bacteriological, physical, and chemical parameters of potable water and the modalities for carrying out controls or analyses, as well as the methods and products used in the treatment and correction of waters;
c) The special protection measures that must be adopted in exceptional situations;
d) The sanitary control to which workers assigned to the water treatment, transport, and distribution sector for consumption will be subject.

ARTICLE 57
(Protection Zones)
1. The places where water abstraction points for the consumption of cities or other urban centers are installed, the banks of artificial lakes, as well as their respective adjacent areas, shall be subject to the regime of protection zones defined in the Land Law and its Regulation. The adjacent areas of water springs and wells shall be subject to the same regime.
2. The legal diploma that establishes the protection zone shall define the limits within which such protection is to be exercised and shall enumerate the restrictions and conditions on land use that must be observed.
3. In the aforementioned protection zones, and in addition to the restrictions and conditions dictated by the specificities of each case, it is prohibited to:
a) Construct dwellings or buildings whose use may lead to the degradation of water quality;
b) Install industrial or commercial establishments, slaughterhouses, or livestock enclosures;
c) Install graves or make excavations;
d) Install dumps or spoil heaps resulting from mining activity;
e) Introduce animals, deposit or bury garbage or filth of any kind;
f) Install pipelines and reservoirs for hydrocarbons or used waters of any kind;
g) Establish cultivated land and spread manure, fertilizers, or any other product intended for soil fertilization or crop protection.

CHAPTER V
Harmful Effects of Waters

SECTION I
Soil Protection

ARTICLE 58
(Soil Protection)
1. Outside of nature protection zones and without prejudice to the provisions of other legal diplomas, on sloping lands near springs, watercourses, or where erosion is to be prevented or combated, the execution of works, installation of equipment, or the development of any activities likely to alter the existence, flow, or reserves of springs, lakes, lagoons, or watercourses is subject to prior authorization from the regional water administrations.
2. The authorization shall only be granted after hearing the interested entities, namely those that supervise agricultural and forestry activities, land use planning, and mineral resources.

SECTION II
Sanitation

ARTICLE 59
(Sanitation)
The sanitation of population centers aims to ensure, under conditions compatible with the requirements of public health and in safeguarding the environment, the rapid and non-stagnant evacuation of rainwater and domestic and industrial wastewater.

ARTICLE 60
(Sanitation Obligation)
1. Owners of existing or to-be-built buildings on plots served by a public domestic sewage collector are obliged to connect their sanitary installations to the said collectors and to ensure, by this process, the drainage of rainwater that cannot be infiltrated without inconvenience.
2. When the plot is considered not to be served by a public collector and it is not used, it shall be the responsibility of the owners of existing or to-be-built buildings to ensure that domestic sewage is conducted to installations that guarantee the purification required for each case, in accordance with the conditions for the final disposal of the effluent.
3. It shall be the responsibility of the executive councils to fix the required characteristics of the effluent.
4. As soon as the public sewage service is ensured, the provisions of paragraph 1 shall become enforceable, and the installations referred to in paragraph 2 must be demolished or filled in after being carefully disinfected.
5. The sanitation of wastewater of non-domestic origin, through the public sewage network, is dependent on special authorization.

ARTICLE 61
(Pre-treatment of Wastewater)
Wastewater may not be evacuated without prior treatment when, in its raw state, it may affect the proper functioning of the public sanitation network or the purification plants.

CHAPTER VI
Groundwater

ARTICLE 62
(Exploration, Abstraction, Use)
1. The exploration, abstraction, or use of groundwater, whether it springs forth or not, is subject to the regime of private uses established in this law.
2. The technical requirements to which exploration, abstraction, and use must adhere shall be fixed by regulation.
3. The provisions of paragraph 1 of this article do not apply to the special uses regulated in Article 23 when carried out outside of urban perimeters or in urban areas that do not have a public water distribution network. Where a public network exists, the executive councils may, considering the particularities of the urban area, establish special regimes.
4. The provisions of this article do not apply to the exploration, abstraction, and use of water for supplying the population, carried out in areas that do not have a public water distribution network, unless a ministerial diploma provides otherwise.
5. The exception provided for in the preceding paragraph shall be established based on the estimated potential of the groundwater or its importance.

ARTICLE 63
(Special Conditions for Use)
The use of groundwater shall also be conditioned on:
a) The maintenance, in renewable aquifers, of the balance between the renewal of fresh water and extractions, in order to ensure continued use under the same physical and chemical use conditions;
b) The optimization, in non-renewable aquifers, of their use over time, in order to extract the maximum benefit from them;
c) The creation of rainfall protection zones for the reservation and maintenance of aquifers;
d) The joint management of surface and ground waters.

ARTICLE 64
(Water from Mining Operations)
1. Holders of rights to the use of mineral resources may, observing the conditions established in this law, use the water they abstract during mining operations.
2. Surplus water shall be made available to the regional water administrations, which shall be responsible, paying special attention to its quality, for defining its destination or the conditions to which dewatering must adhere.
3. The costs of dewatering the mining operation shall be borne by the holder of the operation.

CHAPTER VII
Infractions, Sanctions, and Oversight

ARTICLE 65
(Infractions and Duty to Compensate)
1. Without prejudice to the institution of any criminal proceedings that may be applicable, the following constitute administrative infractions to be punished under terms to be regulated:
a) Acts that cause damage to assets of the public water domain;
3 AUGUST 1991
b) Non-compliance with the conditions imposed for the use of water, namely in licenses and concessions;
c) The diversion of water from its beds and the exploration, abstraction, and use of groundwater in violation of the provisions of this law;
d) The execution, without prior authorization, of works, labors, crops, or plantations in the beds and in zones subject to restrictions;
e) The extraction or deposit of inert materials without prior authorization;
f) Non-compliance with the prohibitions established in this law or the omission of the imposed conditions.
2. Infraction of the provisions of this law determines the obligation to compensate the injured parties, under the terms of civil liability.

ARTICLE 66
(Sanctions)
1. The regulations of this law shall establish the sanctions corresponding to the infractions provided for.
2. Works that are carried out without a license or contrary to what has been established and to the prejudice of the conservation, regulation, and regime of watercourses, lakes, lagoons, swamps, and groundwater, or to the prejudice of third parties, shall be ordered to be demolished at the expense of the offenders.

ARTICLE 67
(Oversight and Policing)
It shall be the responsibility of the Ministry of Construction and Water and the regional water administrations to:
a) Ensure the proper regime and policing of the waters and prevent the violation of the rights of third parties, without prejudice to the right recognized to the latter to resort to the competent courts;
b) Inspect sites, buildings, and equipment and request the necessary information and clarifications;
c) Impose the demolition of works, the closure of establishments and sources of contamination, and the cessation of unauthorized activities;
d) Oversee the execution of works, their conservation and safety, as well as the exploitation of licenses and concessions, obliging their holders to comply with the conditions imposed on the use of waters.

CHAPTER VIII
General, Final, and Transitional Provisions

ARTICLE 68
(Duties of Holders of Riparian Parcels)
1. Holders of the right to use parcels bathed by continuous or discontinuous watercourses may not obstruct the free course of the waters and are obliged to remove obstacles that oppose it when they originate from their parcels, except in the case of an alteration to the water regime, its retardation, or loss, due to lawful application.
2. The conservation of the free course of the waters includes, namely, the duty to:
a) Not change the course of the water without prior authorization and, having obtained it, ensure that the new bed has adequate dimensions, does not obstruct the course of the waters, nor offends the rights of third parties;
214-(21)
b) Not execute works or labors that alter the width and arrangement of the bed;
c) Proceed with the cutting or uprooting, according to the circumstances, of trees and shrubs, trunks, and roots that hang over the bed.

ARTICLE 69
(Acquired Rights)
1. This law does not affect acquired and non-extinguished rights under previous legislation, namely Decree No. 35 463 of 23 January, and its regulation, provided that no cause of expiration has occurred in the meantime, namely abandonment for more than three years not determined by reason of force majeure or fortuitous event.
2. The recognition of acquired rights shall be claimed within a period of one year from the entry into force of this law, with the interested parties being responsible for providing the necessary information and clarifications.
3. Claims submitted after the period established in the preceding paragraph has elapsed shall be considered as new applications for water use, being subject to the regime established in Chapter III of this law.

ARTICLE 70
(Recognition of Acquired Rights by Virtue of Previous Legislation)
1. The regional water administrations shall proceed with the verification of the rights claimed based on the elements provided by the applicant and on other data they may have been able to collect.
2. The claimed rights may be restricted so that the incompatibilities referred to in Article 27 of this law do not occur.
3. Duly recognized rights shall be subject to registration under the terms and conditions that have been prescribed.

ARTICLE 71
(Recognition of Traditional Uses)
1. Traditionally established and de facto existing common uses shall be recognized and registered when they may compete with private uses resulting from law, license, or concession and translate into a useful and beneficial application of water.
2. Uses that imply the contamination of waters shall not be recognized unless the contamination is ceased.
3. It shall be the responsibility of the regional water administrations to proceed with the recognition and promote the registration of common uses that conform to the provisions of the preceding paragraphs.

ARTICLE 72
(Reservation of Obligations Resulting from International Commitments)
The provisions of this law do not prejudice the obligations resulting from normally recognized principles of international law, as well as the obligations arising from international commitments undertaken with neighboring States, under regularly concluded and ratified agreements and treaties.

ARTICLE 73
(Regulation of the Register)
The Minister of Construction and Water shall define, by ministerial diploma, the rules that must govern the registration of rights to use water, and it is also their responsibility to fix the date from which registration becomes mandatory.

(Creation of the Regional Water Administrations)
It shall be the responsibility of the Ministry of Construction and Water to promote the creation and entry into operation of the regional water administrations and, in the meantime, to ensure the exercise of their respective functions.

ARTICLE 75
(Regulations)
It shall be the responsibility of the Council of Ministers to approve the regulations of this law.

Law No. 2/81 of 30 September established the rules for the organization and functioning of state enterprises.
The philosophy underlying the principles enshrined in the said law, due to recent economic-financial circumstances, namely the Economic Rehabilitation Program, shows that the legal regime applicable to state enterprises is considerably outdated.
Thus, it is necessary to introduce new legal mechanisms in order to ensure ever-increasing efficiency and profitability of the public enterprise sector.
Simultaneously, the opportunity should be taken to change the designation from "state enterprise" to a new denomination that, beyond the purely formal aspect, entails a profound change in the management of enterprises endowed with State capital.
In these terms, under the provisions of Article 135(1) of the Constitution of the Republic, the Assembly of the Republic determines:

CHAPTER I
General Principles

ARTICLE 1
(Objectives)
Public enterprises created by the State, with their own capital or capital provided by other public entities, carry out their activity within the framework of the socio-economic objectives of the same.

ARTICLE 2
(Legal Personality and Capacity)
1. Public enterprises enjoy legal personality and are endowed with administrative, financial, and patrimonial autonomy.
2. The legal capacity of public enterprises includes all rights and obligations necessary for the pursuit of their object, as fixed in their respective statutes.

ARTICLE 3
(Creation and Subordination)
1. Public enterprises are created by decree of the Council of Ministers.
2. The diploma creating the public enterprises shall define the body of the State apparatus to which they are subordinate.
3. The proposals for creation must be accompanied by adequate technical, economic, and financial studies, as well as the project for the organic structuring of the enterprise and also the opinions of the National Planning Commission and the Ministry of Finance.

ARTICLE 4
(Statutes)
The diploma creating the public enterprises shall have as its annex the statutes of the enterprise, the content of which constitutes an integral part thereof.

ARTICLE 5
(Mandatory Provisions of the Statutes)

a) Name;
b) Headquarters and geographical area of its activity;

e) Subordinating body;
f) Constitution, competence, and functioning of its bodies.
2. The name of the public enterprises must be followed by the words "Empresa Pblica" or the initials "E. P.".
3. The public enterprise may open branches whenever it proves necessary and under statutory terms.
