It comprises surface water, groundwater, continental water, and the property associated therewith. It extends to maritime and atmospheric water where applicable. Article II.- Purpose The purpose of this Law is to regulate the integrated use and management of water, the actions of the State and private parties in said management, as well as in the property associated therewith. Article III.- Principles The principles governing the integrated use and management of water resources are: 1. Principle of water valuation and integrated water management Water has sociocultural value, economic value, and environmental value; therefore, its use must be based on integrated management and a balance among these values. Water is an integral part of ecosystems and is renewable through the hydrological cycle. 2. Principle of priority in access to water Access to water for the satisfaction of the primary needs of the human person is a priority as a fundamental right over any other use, including in times of scarcity. 3. Principle of population participation and water culture The State shall create mechanisms for the participation of users and the organized population in decision-making that affects water in terms of quality, quantity, opportunity, or any other attribute of the resource. It shall promote the institutional strengthening and technical development of water user organizations. El Peruano educational system and civil society, on the importance of water for humanity and ecological systems, generating awareness and attitudes that promote its proper use and valuation. 4. Principle of legal certainty The State shall establish a system of rights for the use of water. It shall promote and ensure respect for the conditions that grant legal certainty to investments related to its use, whether public, private, or in partnership. 5. Principle of respect for water uses by peasant and native communities The State shall respect the uses and customs of peasant communities and native communities, as well as their right to use the waters that flow through their lands, provided they do not conflict with the Law. It shall promote ancestral knowledge and technology related to water. 6. Principle of sustainability The State shall promote and control the sustainable use and conservation of water resources, preventing adverse effects on their environmental quality and the natural conditions of their surroundings, as part of the ecosystem where they are found. The sustainable use and management of water implies the balanced integration of sociocultural, environmental, and economic aspects in national development, as well as the satisfaction of the needs of current and future generations. 7. Principle of decentralization of public water management and of a single authority For effective public water management, the direction of the National Water Resources Management System is the responsibility of a single, decentralized authority. The public management of water also includes that of its associated property, whether natural or artificial. 8. Precautionary principle The absence of absolute certainty about the danger of serious or irreversible harm threatening water sources shall not constitute an impediment to adopting measures to prevent their degradation or extinction. 9. Principle of efficiency The integrated management of water resources is based on their efficient use and conservation, encouraging the development of a culture of efficient use among users and operators. 10. Principle of participatory integrated management by hydrographic basin The use of water must be optimal and equitable, based on its social, economic, and environmental value, and its management must be integrated by hydrographic basin and with the active participation of the organized population. Water constitutes part Water Resources Law It shall promote education, dissemination, and awareness programs, through the authorities of the Start El Peruano UPDATED LEGAL STANDARDS Water Resources Law of ecosystems and is renewable through the processes of the hydrological cycle. 11. Principle of legal protection The State shall protect, supervise, and oversee water in its natural or artificial sources and in whatever state it may be found: liquid, solid, or gaseous, and at any stage of the hydrological cycle. TITLE I GENERAL PROVISIONS Article 1.- Water Water is a renewable natural resource, indispensable for life, vulnerable, and strategic for sustainable development, the maintenance of the systems and natural cycles that sustain it, and the security of the Nation. Article 2.- Public domain and use of water Water constitutes the patrimony of the Nation. Dominion over it is inalienable and imprescriptible. It is a good for public use, and its administration can only be granted and exercised in harmony with the common good, environmental protection, and the interest of the Nation. There is no private property over water. Article 3.- Declaration of national interest and public necessity The integrated management of water resources is declared to be of national interest and public necessity for the purpose of achieving efficiency and sustainability in the management of hydrographic basins and aquifers for the conservation and increase of water, as well as to ensure its quality by fostering a new water culture, to guarantee the satisfaction of the demand of current and future generations. Article 4.- Denominations When reference is made to "the Law" or "the Regulations," it is understood to mean this Law or its Regulations. The National Authority shall be understood as the National Water Authority (ANA) and the Basin Council as the Hydrographic Basin Resources Council. Article 5.- Water included in the Law The water whose regulation is the subject of this Law comprises the following: 1. That of rivers and their tributaries, from their natural source; 2. that which flows through artificial channels; 3. that which is accumulated naturally or artificially; 4. that found in inlets and estuaries; 5. that found in wetlands and mangroves; 6. that found in springs; 7. that of snowcaps and glaciers; 8. wastewater; 9. groundwater; 10. that of medicinal mineral origin; 11. geothermal water; 12. atmospheric water; and 13. water from desalination. Article 6.- Property associated with water The following are properties associated with water: 1. Natural property: 5 a. The area between the low and high tide lines, plus a strip parallel to the high tide line to an extent determined by the competent authority; b. the channels or beds, riverbeds, and banks of water bodies, including beaches, mudflats, sandbars, and shoals, in the case of the Amazon, as well as the protective vegetation; c. the materials carried and deposited by water in the channels; d. the areas occupied by snowcaps and glaciers; e. the strata or deposits through which groundwater flows or is found; f. existing islands and those that may form in seas, lakes, lagoons, or estuaries, or in rivers, provided they do not originate from a bifurcation of the watercourse as it crosses private lands; g. lands gained from the sea, rivers, lakes, lagoons, and other watercourses or reservoirs by natural causes or artificial works; h. riparian vegetation and that of the headwaters of basins; and i. the marginal strips referred to in this Law; j. others specified by Law. 2. Artificial property: The property used for: a. The capture, extraction, desalination, storage, regulation, conveyance, measurement, control, and use of water; b. the sanitation, purification, treatment, and reuse of the resource; c. the artificial recharge of aquifers; d. the channeling of rivers and defense against floods; e. the protection of property that is part of the public hydraulic domain; and f. the surveillance and maintenance paths used for water use in accordance with the law. Article 7.- Property of the public hydraulic domain The water listed in Article 5 and the natural property associated with it, as indicated in item 1 of Article 6, constitute property of the public hydraulic domain, subject to the provisions of this Law. Any intervention by private parties that affects or alters the characteristics of this property must be previously authorized by the Administrative Water Authority, with the exception of the primary use of water and matters related to navigation. TITLE II NATIONAL WATER RESOURCES MANAGEMENT SYSTEM CHAPTER I PURPOSE AND MEMBERS Article 9.- Creation of the National Water Resources Management System The National Water Resources Management System is hereby created with the objective of coordinating the actions of the State to conduct the processes of integrated management and conservation of water resources within the scope of basins, the ecosystems that comprise them, and their associated property; as well as to establish spaces for coordination and consensus-building among public administration entities and the stakeholders involved in said management, in accordance with this Law. Article 10.- Purpose of the National Water Resources Management System The National Water Resources Management System is part of the National Environmental Management System and its purpose is the sustainable use, conservation, and increase of water resources, as well as compliance with the national water resources policy and strategy and the national water resources plan at all levels of government and with the participation of the various users of the resource. Article 11.- Composition and members of the National Water Resources Management System The National Water Resources Management System is comprised of the set of institutions, principles, norms, procedures, techniques, and instruments through which the State develops and ensures the integrated, participatory, and multi-sectoral management, sustainable use, conservation, preservation of quality, and increase of water resources. The following are members of the National Water Resources Management System: 1. The National Authority; 2. the Ministries of the Environment; Agriculture; Housing, Construction and Sanitation; Health; Production; and Energy and Mines; 3. regional governments and local governments through their competent bodies; 4. agricultural and non-agricultural user organizations; 5. the operating entities of the hydraulic sectors, both sectoral and multi-sectoral; 6. peasant communities and native communities; and El Peruano 7. public entities linked to the management of water resources. Article 12.- Objectives of the National Water Resources Management System The objectives of the National Water Resources Management System are as follows: a. To coordinate and ensure the integrated and multi-sectoral management, sustainable use, conservation, efficient use, and increase of water resources, with quality standards according to their respective use. b. To promote the development of studies and the execution of research and training projects and programs in water resources management. Article 13.- Scope of the National Water Resources Management System The National Water Resources Management System develops its policies in coordination with the Ministry of the Environment, the Ministry of Agriculture, the Ministry of Energy and Mines, the Ministry of Health, the Ministry of Production, and the Ministry of Housing, Construction and Sanitation, as well as with regional and local governments, within the framework of the national water resources policy and strategy. Access to the position of member of the National Tribunal for the Resolution of Water Disputes is through a public merit-based competition approved by the National Authority in accordance with the law. The members are appointed by supreme resolution. SUBCHAPTER IV BASIN COUNCIL Article 24.- Nature of the Basin Councils The Basin Councils are permanent bodies, members of the National Authority, created by supreme decree at the initiative of the regional governments, with the objective of participating in the planning, coordination, and consensus-building for the sustainable use of water resources in their respective jurisdictions. TITLE III USES OF WATER RESOURCES Article 34.- General conditions for the use of water resources The use of water resources is conditioned by their availability. The use of water must be carried out efficiently and with respect for the rights of third parties, in accordance with the provisions of the Law, promoting the maintenance or improvement of the physical-chemical characteristics of the water and the hydrological regime for the benefit of the environment, public health, and national security. Article 35.- Classes of water uses and order of priority The Law recognizes the following classes of water use: 1. Primary use. 2. Population use. 3. Productive use. The priority for the granting and exercise of the aforementioned uses follows the order in which they have been listed. Article 36.- Primary use of water Primary use consists of the direct and effective utilization of water, in natural sources and public Water Resources Law Start 10 UPDATED LEGAL STANDARDS Water Resources Law public water channels, for the purpose of satisfying primary human needs. It includes the use of water for food preparation, direct consumption, and personal hygiene, as well as its use in cultural, religious, and ritual ceremonies. Article 37.- Characteristics of primary use The primary use of water does not require administrative authorization and is exercised by the sole provision of the Law. It is harmless to the environment and to third parties, has no lucrative purpose, and is exercised free of charge by individuals, under their own responsibility, restricted only to manual means and conditioned on: 1. Not altering the water sources in their quantity and quality, and 2. Not affecting the property associated with water. Article 38.- Zones of free access for primary use The State guarantees free access to natural sources and public artificial channels, without altering them and avoiding their contamination, to directly satisfy the primary needs of the population. The National Authority shall establish, when necessary, places or zones of free access. Article 39.- Population use of water Population use consists of the capture of water from a source or public network, duly treated, for the purpose of satisfying basic human needs: food preparation and personal hygiene habits. It is exercised through water use rights granted by the National Authority. Article 40.- Access of the population to drinking water networks The State guarantees all persons the right of access to drinking water services, in sufficient quantity and under conditions of safety and quality to meet personal and domestic needs. Article 41.- Restrictions on population water use In states of water scarcity, local, regional, and national authorities responsible for regulating drinking water supply services must issue rationing measures to restrict water use not intended for satisfying personal needs. Article 42.- Productive use of water The productive use of water consists of its utilization in production processes or processes prior to them. It is exercised through water use rights granted by the National Authority. Agrarian: livestock and agricultural; 2. Aquaculture and fishing; 3. Energy; 4. Industrial; 5. Medicinal; 6. Mining; 7. Recreational; 8. Touristic; and 9. Transportation. Water may be granted for unforeseen uses, respecting the provisions of this Law. TITLE IV WATER USE RIGHTS CHAPTER I GENERAL PROVISIONS Article 44.- Water use rights To use the water resource, except for primary use, a use right granted by the Administrative Water Authority with the participation of the Regional or Interregional Basin Council, as appropriate, is required. Water use rights are granted, suspended, modified, or extinguished by administrative resolution of the National Authority, in accordance with the law. Article 45.- Classes of water use rights Water use rights are as follows: 1. Use license. 2. Use permit. 3. Water use authorization. Article 46.- Guarantee in the exercise of use rights It is prohibited to alter, modify, disturb, or impede the legitimate use of water. The State guarantees the fulfillment of the granted use rights. CHAPTER II WATER USE LICENSE Article 47.- Definition A water use license is a use right by which the National Authority, with the opinion of the respective Basin Council, grants its holder the power to use this natural resource, for a specific purpose and in a specific place, under the terms and conditions provided for in current legal provisions and in the corresponding administrative resolution that grants it. Article 48.- Classes of use license A water use license may be granted for consumptive and non-consumptive use. 11 Article 49.- Reversion of water resources The National Authority, with the opinion of the Basin Council, shall promote the reversion of surplus water resources obtained by virtue of compliance with this norm, considering for this purpose the regulations established by the Ministry of the Environment in its area of competence. Users or operators of hydraulic infrastructure who generate surplus water resources and who have an efficiency certificate shall have preference in the granting of new water use rights for the surplus resources. The Regulations shall establish the conditions for the application of the provisions of this article. Article 50.- Characteristics of the use license The characteristics of a use license are as follows: 1. It grants its holder the power to use and register an annual water allocation expressed in cubic meters, extracted from a source, and to take legal action for its defense; 2. it is extinguished for the causes provided for in the Law; 3. its term is indefinite as long as the activity for which it was granted continues; 4. it grants the holder the power to carry out, directly or in partnership, as the case may be, investments in treatment, transformation, and reuse for the granted use. Surplus water is delivered to the National Authority for distribution; 5. it empowers the holder to exercise the easements provided for in this Law and in accordance with the activities and type of water use carried out by the holder; 6. it is inherent to the object for which it was granted; and, 7. use licenses are not transferable. If the holder does not wish to continue using it, they must revert it to the State, through the National Authority. Article 51.- Block use license A block water use license may be granted to a recognized water user organization, composed of a plurality of natural or legal persons who use a water source with a common intake point. The organizations holding block water use licenses shall issue nominative certificates representing the portion of the license corresponding to each of their members. Article 52.- Provisional licenses The National Authority, with the opinion of the Basin Council, at the request of a party, provided that there are surplus water resources and the use rights of third parties are not affected, shall grant provisional licenses to the holders of concessions granted by the competent public entities for the purpose of carrying out studies in any activity. It is granted in accordance with the conditions, acts, and requirements provided for in the Law, the Regulations, and in the granting resolution itself. Once the conditions under which the provisional license was granted are met, the granting of the use license that empowers its holder to make effective use of the water shall proceed, at the request of a party. Article 53.- Granting and modification of the use license The granting, suspension, or modification of a water use license is processed according to the procedure established in the Regulations. To be granted, the following is required: 1. That there is availability of the requested water and that it is appropriate in quality, quantity, and opportunity for the intended use; 2. that the water source to which the request pertains has an available volume of water that ensures ecological flows, minimum reserve or storage safety levels, and navigability conditions, when applicable and according to the hydrological regime; 3. that it does not endanger public health and the environment; 4. that the rights of third parties are not affected; 5. that it is consistent with the water management plan of the basin; 6. that the interested party presents the pertinent environmental instrument approved by the competent sectoral environmental authority; and 7. that the easements, as well as the works for capture, extraction, production or regeneration, conveyance, utilization, drainage, measurement, and any others that may be necessary, have been approved. Article 54.- Requirements for the use license application The application shall be submitted to the National Authority, containing, in addition to the requirements indicated in Article 113 of Law No. 27444, the General Administrative Procedure Law, the following: 1. The use for which the water is intended; 2. the intake source, watercourse, or water body to be used, indicating the hydrographic basin to which it belongs, its political and geographical location, and main characteristics of interest; 3. the location of the intake and return points or the delimitation of the source use area, as appropriate, with the corresponding plans; 4. the required annualized volume and the estimated discharge, when applicable, and other characteristics, according to the license requested; 5. environmental certification issued in accordance with the respective legislation, when applicable; El Peruano 6. the specification of the easements required; and 7. accreditation of ownership or legitimate possession of the property where the requested water will be used, when applicable. Negative administrative silence applies to water use applications. Article 55.- Priority for granting water use There is a concurrence of applications when, at any stage of the administrative procedure for granting a water use right, more than one application is submitted for the same water source. When the availability of the resource is not sufficient to meet all concurrent applications, the granting must be carried out according to the following rules: 1. The general order of priority established in the Law; 2. the order of preferences for productive uses established by the National Authority, taking into account the provisions of Articles 35 and 43; and, 3. in the case of the same productive use, the one that is of greater public interest, according to the following criteria: a) The greatest efficiency in water utilization; b) the greatest generation of employment; and, c) the least environmental impact. Third.- Navigation, flotation, and activities with seawater The use of water as a means of maritime, river, and lake transport and flotation, as well as the use of and activity with seawater, are governed by the special legislation on the matter and in accordance with the provisions of this Law. Fourth.- Other entities that are part of the National Water Resources Management System The special projects; regional, national, and binational special hydraulic and hydro-energy projects; the competent environmental authorities; the sanitation service providers; the National Service of Meteorology and Hydrology; and the Maritime Authority of Peru are part of the National Water Resources Management System. These entities must coordinate their actions in accordance with the norms of this Law and the Regulations. Fifth.- Regulations of the Water Resources Law The Executive Branch, with the countersignature of the President of the Council of Ministers and the Minister of Agriculture, shall approve the Regulations of this Law, and shall adapt the Organizational and Functional Regulations of the National Authority, within a period not exceeding sixty (60) calendar days. Water Resources Law Start 22 UPDATED LEGAL STANDARDS Water Resources Law Sixth.- Attachment to the Ministry of the Environment The National Authority may be attached to the Ministry of the Environment once the process of implementation and operation of said Ministry is completed. Seventh.- Major public hydraulic infrastructure The regional governments to which the operation and maintenance of major public hydraulic infrastructure are transferred shall carry out their activities in strict compliance with the national water resources policy and strategy, as well as the National Water Resources Management Plan, and shall be subject to the norms, guidelines, directives, and procedures issued by the National Authority within its sphere of competence. Eighth.- Sanitation service provider entities The groundwater reserved in favor of sanitation service provider entities is governed in each case by the law that authorizes the corresponding reservation. Ninth.- Promotion mechanisms The Regulations shall establish mechanisms for the promotion of private investment in hydraulic infrastructure, different from those contemplated in Article 8 of the Law. Tenth.- Exception for water use The use of water for national defense purposes, approved by the National Water Authority and the Basin Council as a decentralized body, is exempted from this Law. Eleventh.- Social responsibility The beneficiaries of water, grouped in companies and associations, within the framework of corporate social responsibility, shall develop projects for the establishment of afforestation, irrigation improvement, seed improvement, and other programs, in order to maintain ecological balance. Twelfth.- Environmental adaptation and management program Public and private entities that do not have an Environmental Adaptation and Management Program (PAMA), in the case of wastewater discharge, and that fail to comply with the provisions of Title V of the Law, must present their PAMA to the competent Environmental Authority, establishing the deadlines for remediation, mitigation, and environmental control. TRANSITIONAL COMPLEMENTARY PROVISIONS El Peruano For the procedures that begin from the entry into force of this Law and until the Administrative Water Authorities and the National Tribunal for the Resolution of Water Disputes are implemented, the functions of the first instance shall be assumed by the local water administrations and the second instance by the Chief of the National Authority. The procedures initiated before the entry into force of this Law shall be governed by the regulations in force at that time until their conclusion, except for the second-instance functions exercised by the autonomous hydrographic basin authorities, which shall be assumed by the National Authority.
