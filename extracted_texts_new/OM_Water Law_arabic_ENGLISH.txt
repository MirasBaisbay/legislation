Royal Decree No. 29/2000
Promulgating the Law for the Protection of Water Resources

We, Qaboos bin Said, Sultan of Oman,

After reviewing the Basic Statute of the State issued by Royal Decree No. 101/96,
And Royal Decree No. 8/82 considering water reserves a national resource,
And Royal Decree No. 3/94 transferring the responsibilities and competencies related to dams and aflaj to the Ministry of Water Resources,

And in accordance with the public interest,

Have decreed as follows:

**Article (1):** The attached Law for the Protection of Water Resources shall be enforced.

**Article (2):** The Minister of Water Resources shall issue the regulations and decisions necessary for the implementation of the provisions of the attached law. Until they are issued, the existing regulations and decisions shall continue to be in force, provided they do not conflict with its provisions.

**Article (3):** Royal Decree No. 88/82 is hereby repealed, as is anything that contravenes or conflicts with the provisions of the attached law.

**Article (4):** This Decree shall be published in the Official Gazette and shall come into force from the date of its publication.

Qaboos bin Said
Sultan of Oman

Issued on: 10 Muharram 1421 H
Corresponding to: 15 April 2000 AD

*This Decree was published in the Official Gazette No. (670) issued on 1/5/2000.*

**Law for the Protection of Water Resources**

**Article (1):** For the purposes of applying the provisions of this Law, the following words and phrases shall have the meaning indicated opposite each, unless the context requires otherwise:
**The Ministry:** The Ministry of Water Resources.
**The Minister:** The Minister of Water Resources.
**Water:** Groundwater and surface water—excluding seawater—and includes water from wells, wadis, aflaj, dams, springs, and rainwater, as well as other water impounded or produced in water installations.
**Water Pollution:** Any physical, chemical, or organic change in the properties of water that renders it harmful to public health.
**Protection Zones:** Areas designated by the Ministry to protect water sources and public water supply fields from pollution, depletion, and saltwater intrusion.
**Aquifer:** A geological layer of rock or sediment that contains water.

**Article (2):** Water in the Sultanate of Oman is considered a national resource. Its use is subject to the controls established by the Ministry to regulate its optimal utilization in a manner that serves the state's comprehensive development plans. These controls must, in particular, include the designation of protection zones and the activities prohibited therein without a permit, the rules governing the drilling and maintenance of wells and the use of their water, the maintenance and extension of aflaj, and the rules and procedures for monitoring water to detect any occurrence of water pollution.

**Article (3):** The Ministry must take the necessary measures and actions to prevent the deterioration of the quantity and quality of water in any area of the Sultanate. Should such deterioration occur, it must take the necessary steps to mitigate and remedy it.

**Article (4):** Government bodies must provide the Ministry with the data available to them concerning:
a) The quantities of water consumed for uses falling within their competencies.
b) Their water requirements for the implementation of their future plans and projects.

**Article (5):** It is prohibited to carry out any works that negatively affect the groundwater recharge of an aquifer, regardless of the owner of the land where the aquifer is located. It is not permissible to carry out any works that would alter the courses or benefits of aflaj without a permit from the Ministry.

**Article (6):** Employees designated by a decision of the Minister shall have the capacity of judicial enforcement officers in applying the provisions of this Law and its implementing regulations and decisions.

**Article (7):** The fees for licensing companies or individuals to drill any wells or carry out any works thereon, including their maintenance, deepening, and widening, and the installation or replacement of a pump or a water desalination unit, shall be determined by a decision of the Minister. The fees for issuing inventory (1) and registration certificates, the fees for registering contractors working in the field of well drilling or carrying out any works thereon, and the fees for renewing this registration shall also be determined by a decision of the Minister. Anyone receiving a pension in accordance with the Social Security Law shall be exempt from these fees.

**Article (8):** Without prejudice to any more severe penalty stipulated in any other law, anyone who violates the provisions of Article (2) and its implementing regulations and decisions, and Article (5) of this Law, shall be punished with imprisonment for a term not exceeding one year and a fine not exceeding two thousand Omani Rials, or one of these two penalties. The penalty shall be doubled in the event of a repeat offense.

(1) This word was amended by virtue of the correction published in the Official Gazette No. (672) issued on 3/6/2000.
