Water Affairs Management Law
Chapter One
General Provisions

Article One: Basis
This law is enacted in light of the provision of Article Nine of the Constitution of Afghanistan.

Article Two: Objectives
The objectives of this law are as follows:

Regulating existing waters and maximizing their just, effective, and economic use.

Developing and utilizing water resources in a comprehensive and sustainable manner.

Improving the quantity and quality of water resources and related services.

Improving and developing water supply, irrigation, and wastewater systems based on appropriate and modern technologies.

Ensuring good governance for the management of water affairs.

Protecting water resources for present and future generations.

Article Three: Terminology
The following terms in this law shall have the meanings ascribed to them below:

Water Resources: Water existing permanently or seasonally in the atmosphere, on the surface, underground, or in other reservoirs or channels, which flows or emerges therefrom.

Atmospheric Water: Water that appears in the form of rain, snow, and hail.

Surface Water: Water existing on or near the bed of a river, stream, canal, pond, glacier, or natural lake, or in any other type of reservoir.

Groundwater: Water stored or present at various depths of the earth (in aquifers and saturated layers), including water from springs, karezes, and deep and ordinary wells.

Wetland: A relatively shallow area where water collects permanently or temporarily and has environmental and ecological importance.

Lake: A large area of standing water where water has collected naturally and its surroundings are enclosed by dry land.

Spring: A source of water from which water naturally flows from underground to the surface.

Karez: A traditional ancient structure where underground water is collected from aquifer layers through a horizontal tunnel and flows to the earth's surface.

Runoff: A part of a catchment area from which water, due to the slope of the land, flows to lower areas.

Catchment Area: An area where water from atmospheric precipitation collects and drains into a reservoir, river, stream, or other water channels.

Storage Dam: A structure built in the course of water for the purpose of storing, controlling, and increasing its elevation by forming a reservoir basin.

Diversion Dam: A structure built in the course of a river which, by raising the water level, causes water to flow into main canals.

River Basin: Catchment areas whose water naturally flows into a common channel.

Sub-River Basin: A branch of a river basin.

Transboundary Waters: Waters that originate from the territory of Afghanistan and, by crossing the border, enter neighboring countries, or enter Afghanistan from neighboring countries.

River Basin Approach: Comprehensive management of water resources, taking into account the natural boundaries of a river basin within the country.

Integrated Water Resources Management (IWRM): A method that promotes the integrated and coordinated management of water, land, and related resources to foster economic growth and social welfare in an equitable manner without compromising the sustainability of vital ecosystems.

Permit: An official document issued by the Ministry of Water and Energy to an applicant for the purpose of activity in and utilization of water resources, in accordance with the provisions of this law.

Water Users: Real and legal persons who use water for various purposes, directly or indirectly.

Water Users' Association: A voluntary association of real and legal persons established to secure the social and economic objectives of water use, in accordance with the provisions of this law.

Water Service Providers: Governmental and non-governmental administrations, companies, and associations related to water that operate for the purpose of providing services for water use, collecting excess water, and other related services.

Irrigation System: The change of direction, division, transfer, control, distribution, and collection of water, which includes diversion dams, main canals, and control, transitional, and divisional structures for irrigation and drainage networks, and other related structures.

Irrigation Network: The sum of all canals, channels, and drains with their related structures, which, for the purpose of using water in agriculture, begin at the main canals and continue to the farm.

Water Right (Haqaba): The turn for using water that is determined for the purpose of irrigating crops or trees.

Riparian Zone: A part of the land on the banks of a river confluence, river, floodway, dam, natural and traditional canal, and other water channels and reservoirs that is necessary for their protection and use.

Bed: The bottom of a river confluence, river, floodway, dam, natural and traditional canal, and other water channels and reservoirs, which forms the physical limit of the maximum water flow and is determined based on hydrological information in the area.

Water Allocation: The amount of water that, considering the available water in the source, is determined and fixed for users according to need and priority.

Drinking Water: Water whose quality is determined based on accepted standards and whose consumption is not harmful to health.

Water Infrastructure: Structures for the regulation, control, transfer, and use of water resources (including storage dams, diversion dams, canals, riverbank reinforcement structures, hydropower generation structures, and other related facilities).

Water Supply System: Consists of facilities for supplying drinking water, and for domestic, industrial, and other uses in urban, semi-urban, rural, and other areas.

Wastewater: Used water that has been generated from domestic, sanitary, industrial, mining, agricultural, and other uses, containing materials in dissolved or solid states.

Article Four: Implementing Authority
The Ministry of Water and Energy is the authority responsible for implementing the provisions of this law.

Article Five: Right of Access to Clean and Potable Water
(1) Access to clean and potable drinking water is the right of the people.
(2) Access for the people to clean and potable drinking water shall be provided in accordance with the provisions of this law and other legislative documents.

Article Six: Integrated Water Resources Management
Integrated water resources management shall be carried out for the purpose of protection, sustainable use, and preservation of the environment, taking into account hydrological boundaries and the river basin approach, in accordance with the provisions of this law.

Article Seven: Prioritization of Water Resources Use and Allocation
(1) The use and allocation of water from resources for humans and animals shall have the highest priority, based on their importance.
(2) The use and allocation of water from resources for other purposes shall be regulated with consideration for economic and regional rationality.

Article Eight: Service Fees
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy shall collect a fee, directly and indirectly, from users for the provision, storage, transfer, operational affairs, preservation, and maintenance of water systems and other related services.
(2) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to determine the price of water use from the source and the fee for its services every three years based on a separate regulation.

Chapter Two
Water Affairs Governance

Article Nine: Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment
(1) The Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment is the highest leading, policy-making, and decision-making authority in the water sector and for the regulation of water resources.
(2) The members of the Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment are appointed by the President of the Republic and under his leadership, with the following composition:
1. Minister of Water and Energy
2. Minister of Foreign Affairs
3. Minister of Mines and Petroleum
4. Minister of Agriculture and Livestock
5. Minister of Rural Rehabilitation and Development
6. Minister of Economy
7. Minister of Justice
8. Minister of Finance
9. Minister of Interior Affairs
10. Minister of Urban Development and Land
11. Director General of the National Statistics and Information Authority
12. Director General of the National Environmental Protection Agency
13. Director General of the National Standards Authority
14. President of the Afghanistan Urban Water Supply and Sewerage Corporation
(3) The operational procedure of the Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment is regulated by a procedure that is approved by the council itself.
(4) A coordinating committee for the management of water affairs is established under the supervision of the Minister of Water and Energy with the participation of relevant ministries and administrations, and the operational procedure of the said committee is regulated by a procedure that is approved by the Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment.

Article Ten: Powers of the Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment
(1) The Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment has the following powers:
1. Approving policies and strategies for the regulation and development, effective use, and monitoring of the implementation of water resources.
2. Approving the policy plan and management of transboundary waters.
3. Approving the diplomatic plan for negotiations and discussions on transboundary waters.
4. Approving the plan for regulation, allocation, supply, distribution, and utilization of water resources for various uses based on economic and regional rationality.
5. Making decisions regarding the plan for regulation and transfer of water between river basins.
6. Making decisions regarding transboundary waters.
(2) The secretariat of the Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment is the responsibility of the Water Affairs Management Authority of the Ministry of Water and Energy.

Article Eleven: Duties and Powers of the Ministry of Water and Energy
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy has the following duties and powers:
1. Designing and preparing water policy, strategy, and related legislative documents.
2. Analyzing the capacity of existing and accessible water and managing catchment areas.
3. Designing and preparing the plan for the allocation and proper use of water resources and proposing it to the Supreme Council for Water, Land, and Environment.
4. Designing, preparing, implementing, and monitoring the plan for the rehabilitation, development, utilization, safety, preservation, and maintenance of water facilities and other related structures associated with water resources.
5. Expanding the hydrological and hydrogeological network in cooperation with relevant administrations for the collection and analysis of data and information.
6. Creating a national water resources information bank for the purpose of designing and preparing the plan for the development and regulation of water resources, taking into account the adverse effects of climate change, including the reduction of flood and drought impacts, and sharing it with the public sector according to a separate procedure.
7. Providing the groundwork for private sector investment for the development of water resources, taking into account economic and regional rationality.
(2) For the purpose of advancing the duties listed in paragraph (1) of this article, the Water Affairs Management Authority is established within the framework of the Ministry of Water and Energy.
(3) The Water Affairs Management Authority establishes general directorates at the level of five river basins, sub-directorates at the provincial level, and administrative units and runoff areas at the district level for the better management of river basin affairs.
(4) The method of execution and operation of the Water Affairs Management Authority, the general and sub-directorates of river basins, and the administrative units mentioned in paragraph (3) of this article are regulated by a separate regulation.

Article Twelve: River Basin Advisory Council and its Duties and Powers
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy establishes river basin advisory councils, composed of representatives of water users, relevant government administrations, and other involved parties in the respective basins.
(2) The river basin advisory councils have the following duties and powers:
1. Providing advice for the formulation of a strategy for the regulation of water resources in the relevant river basin in accordance with water laws and national policy, and considering the characteristics and needs of the river basin, and monitoring the observance of the provisions of this law and related regulations.
2. Providing advice and agreement on the determination of water allocation, which is prepared by the river basin administrations in consideration of the national water policy.
3. Providing advice on resolving disputes arising from the allocation and use of water in the relevant river basin.
4. Monitoring the provision of water rights in the relevant river basin and supervising the performance of the duties of the sub-basin advisory councils in the river basin.
5. Confirming, rejecting, or amending the decisions of the sub-river basin advisory councils and the water service providers in cooperation with the relevant administration.
(3) The river basin advisory councils may, for the advancement of relevant affairs, delegate some of their duties and powers to the sub-river basin advisory councils.

Article Thirteen: Sub-River Basin Advisory Councils and their Duties and Powers
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy establishes sub-river basin advisory councils, composed of representatives of water users, relevant government administrations, and other involved parties in the respective basins.
(2) The sub-river basin advisory councils have the following duties and powers:
1. Providing advice for the regulation of water resources in the relevant basin, taking into account the characteristics and needs of the sub-river basin, and monitoring the observance of the provisions of this law and related regulations.
2. Providing advice and agreement regarding the water allocation plan that is prepared by the sub-river basin administrations in consideration of the national water policy.
3. Monitoring the provision of water rights and supervising the performance of the duties of the sub-basin councils in the river basin.
4. Providing advice for resolving disputes arising from the allocation and use of water in the sub-river basin.

Article Fourteen: Establishment of Water Users' Associations
(1) Water users' associations are established to ensure the legal right of water users and to cooperate with the water affairs management authority.
(2) The mode of operation, scope of duties, limits of powers, and other related affairs of the associations are regulated according to a separate regulation.
(3) The Ministry of Water and Energy shall register and certify associations related to water.

Article Fifteen: Formalization of the Mirab System
The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to study the traditional irrigation system, the Mirab system, at the national level for the purpose of better regulation, and to take steps towards its formalization in accordance with the relevant regulation.

Article Sixteen: New Irrigation Systems
The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to create and develop new technology-based irrigation systems for lands that are newly brought under irrigation, taking into account their economic rationality.

Chapter Three
Water Resources Regulation

Article Seventeen: Design and Preparation of the Water Resources Regulation Plan
(1) The design and preparation of the water resources regulation plan shall include the following items:
1. Analysis of the capacity of existing and accessible water resources in the main and sub-river basins and runoff areas.
2. Modeling the effects of climate change on water resources, considering the results of floods, droughts, and other natural events arising from water.
3. Allocation and effective utilization of water resources for various uses.
4. Monitoring and evaluation of water use.
(2) The government, in cooperation with relevant ministries and administrations, shall design and implement necessary programs for the purpose of providing the groundwork for private sector investment for the development of water resources and related services, for use in various fields.

Article Eighteen: Effective and Economic Use of Water Resources
(1) The effective and economic use of water resources includes the following main items:
1. Construction, rehabilitation, and development of water resource infrastructure and facilities.
2. Increasing the effectiveness of water resource use for various purposes, considering economic and regional rationality.
3. Utilizing appropriate and modern methods and technologies for irrigation.
4. Collecting rainwater in small water reservoirs in urban and rural areas.
5. Improving the recharge of groundwater.
6. Reusing wastewater for non-drinking purposes.
(2) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to determine the effectiveness and economic viability of water based on its rational consumption.

Article Nineteen: Provision of Water Services
(1) The change of the traditional water system to a standard and technical system shall occur gradually, taking into account the irrigation standards in the regulation of water resources.
(2) The improvement of water service provision shall be carried out considering its importance for drinking, agriculture, industry and commerce, the environment, energy production, tourism, and navigation.

Chapter Four
Water Distribution

Article Twenty: Water for Drinking
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to design, build, and reconstruct the infrastructure and systems for the regulation and distribution of water for drinking.
(2) The mechanism for distributing water for drinking shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy in technical cooperation with the Ministry of Urban Development and Land, the Ministry of Rural Rehabilitation and Development, and the Afghanistan Urban Water Supply and Sewerage Corporation, according to a separate regulation.
(3) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to provide and distribute water for livestock in public and private pastures in cooperation with the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock.

Article Twenty-One: Water for Irrigation
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to design, build, and reconstruct the infrastructure, systems, and networks for irrigation.
(2) The mechanism for using water in irrigation shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy in technical cooperation with the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock, according to a separate regulation.

Article Twenty-Two: Water for Industry and Commerce
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to design, build, and reconstruct the water infrastructure and facilities for industrial, commercial, mining, and fish farming activities, in cooperation with the relevant ministries.
(2) The mechanism for using water for the activities mentioned in paragraph (1) of this article shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy in cooperation with the ministries of industry and commerce, agriculture and livestock, and the chamber of industries and mines, according to a separate regulation.

Article Twenty-Three: Water for Environmental Protection
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to design, build, and reconstruct the water infrastructure and facilities for environmental protection.
(2) The mechanism for using water resources for environmental protection shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy in cooperation with the National Environmental Protection Agency, according to a separate regulation.

Article Twenty-Four: Water for Energy Production
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to design, build, and reconstruct the water infrastructure and facilities for energy production.
(2) The mechanism for using water resources for energy production shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy according to a separate regulation.

Article Twenty-Five: Water for Tourism
(1) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to design, build, and reconstruct the water infrastructure and facilities for tourism activities.
(2) The mechanism for using water resources for tourism activities shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy in cooperation with the Ministry of Information and Culture, the relevant municipality, and the General Directorate of Physical Training and Sports, according to a separate regulation.

Article Twenty-Six: Transboundary Waters
(1) Transboundary waters shall be regulated based on existing treaties, taking into account the characteristics of the water resources.
(2) Transboundary waters for which no treaty exists regarding their regulation shall be regulated according to the transboundary water policy.

Article Twenty-Seven: Water Allocation
(1) The allocation of water to the sectors listed from Article Twenty to Article Twenty-Five of this law shall be determined by the Ministry of Water and Energy every three years, based on the availability of water in the resources and the rational and economic consumption and regional rationality thereof.
(2) The modality of water allocation to the relevant sectors shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy according to a separate regulation.

Article Twenty-Eight: Water Reuse
The reuse of used water for non-drinking purposes shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy according to a separate regulation.

Article Twenty-Nine: Wastewater Discharge
Matters of wastewater discharge shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy in cooperation with the relevant technical administrations, according to a separate regulation.

Article Thirty: Protocol Agreement
The gradual transfer of responsibility for the design, construction, and reconstruction of water resource infrastructure and facilities, and the systems and networks for water regulation and distribution, to various sectors from the relevant ministries and administrations, including projects and programs under their implementation, shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy through a protocol.

Chapter Five
Coordination of Relevant Administrations

Article Thirty-One: Obligations of the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock
The Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock has the following obligations:

Promoting new and appropriate technology for the purpose of increasing irrigation efficiency and reducing surface and groundwater losses.

Promoting and selecting cultivable plants based on the rational amount of water consumption.

Providing awareness and capacity building for farmers in the rational use of water.

Conducting studies on climate change for the purpose of predicting and warning about agricultural droughts and reducing their effects, in cooperation with relevant administrations.

Studying soil evaporation and plant transpiration to maximize irrigation productivity.

Strengthening plant cover to combat natural disasters before their occurrence, and restoring damaged natural areas after the event, in cooperation with the relevant administration.

Improving and enhancing areas under irrigated cultivation using modern methods for the effective use of water.

Article Thirty-Two: Obtaining Written Agreement and Permit
(1) Any type of activity in and use of water resources requires obtaining a written agreement and permit from the Ministry of Water and Energy, as circumstances may require.
(2) The ministries of Mines and Petroleum, and Industry and Commerce are obligated to obtain the written agreement of the Ministry of Water and Energy regarding the regulation and distribution of the water source before commencing mining, industrial, or commercial activities in the relevant areas.
(3) The Ministry of Urban Development and Land is obligated to obtain the written agreement of the Ministry of Water and Energy regarding the regulation and distribution of the water source before urban planning and the expansion of cities.
(4) Municipalities are obligated to obtain the written agreement of the Ministry of Water and Energy regarding the regulation and distribution of the water source before undertaking construction, urban development, and landscaping activities.
(5) The private sector is obligated to obtain a permit from the Ministry of Water and Energy before carrying out any activity in surface and groundwater resources and using them.
(6) The Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated, after technical studies, to express its agreement or disagreement on the matters listed in paragraphs (2) to (5) of this article.

Article Thirty-Three: Water Quality
The quality standard for drinking and domestic, irrigation, industrial, and wastewater shall be determined by the Ministry of Public Health in cooperation with the National Environmental Protection Agency, the ministries of Water and Energy, Agriculture and Livestock, Mines and Petroleum, and the National Standards Authority, in compliance with accepted national and international norms.

Article Thirty-Four: Water Resources Research Center
(1) The Ministry of Higher Education is obligated to establish a national center for water resources research within the framework of the national research and development center.
(2) Matters related to the regulation of the water resources research center shall be regulated according to a separate regulation.

Chapter Six
Miscellaneous Provisions

Article Thirty-Five: Addressing Water Disputes
The mechanism for addressing disputes arising from water shall be regulated by the Ministry of Water and Energy in cooperation with the relevant technical administrations through a separate regulation.

Article Thirty-Six: Bed and Riparian Zone of Water Resources
(1) The bed and riparian zone of water resources are protected from encroachment.
(2) The Ministry of Water and Energy, based on technical standards and in cooperation with relevant administrations, shall determine and regulate the riparian zone of water resources (water facilities, storage dams, diversion dams, rivers, wetlands, lakes, canals, irrigation systems and networks, karezes, springs, wells, and other natural water channels) according to the relevant regulation.

Article Thirty-Seven: Preservation and Maintenance of Traditional Irrigation Systems
The preservation and maintenance of the traditional irrigation system by the people shall be carried out taking into account local customs.

Article Thirty-Eight: Water-Related Crimes and Penalties
The handling of crimes related to water shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions of the law.

Article Thirty-Nine: Water Resources Code
For the purpose of better integration and regulation of all legal and technical documents related to water resources, the Ministry of Water and Energy is obligated to codify and process the water code in cooperation with the relevant administrations.

Article Forty: Establishment of Regulations and Procedures
The Ministry of Water and Energy may, for the purpose of better implementation of the provisions of this law, propose regulations and prepare procedures in cooperation with the relevant ministries and administrations.

Article Forty-One: Enforcement
This law shall enter into force from the date of its publication in the Official Gazette, and with its enforcement, the Water Law published in the Official Gazette No. (980) of 1388 S.H., the amendment to certain articles of the Water Law published in Official Gazette No. (1206) of 1394 S.H., and the provision of Article Eight of the Land Management Law published in Official Gazette No. (1254) of 1396 S.H. are hereby repealed.
