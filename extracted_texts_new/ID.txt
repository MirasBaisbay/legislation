PRESIDENT
OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA

LAW OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA
NUMBER 17 OF 2019
CONCERNING
WATER RESOURCES

BY THE GRACE OF GOD ALMIGHTY

THE PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA,

Considering:

a. that water is a basic necessity for human life, bestowed by God Almighty upon the entire Indonesian nation;
b. that water as a part of water resources constitutes a vital branch of production that affects the lives of many people, controlled by the state to be used for the greatest prosperity of the people in accordance with the mandate of the 1945 Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia;
c. that in facing the imbalance between water availability, which tends to decrease, and water demand, which is increasing, water resources must be managed by considering social, environmental, and economic functions in a harmonious manner to create synergy and integration between regions, sectors, and generations to fulfill the people's need for water;
d. that with the reenactment of Law Number 11 of 1974 concerning Irrigation after Law Number 7 of 2004 concerning Water Resources was annulled by the Constitutional Court, there are still many shortcomings, and it is no longer able to comprehensively regulate water resources management in accordance with the development and legal needs of the community, and thus it needs to be replaced;
e. that based on the considerations as referred to in letters a, b, c, and d, it is necessary to establish a Law concerning Water Resources;

In view of:

Article 18A, Article 18B, Article 20, Article 21, and Article 33 of the 1945 Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia;

With the Joint Approval of
THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA
and
THE PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA

HAS DECIDED:

To enact: THE LAW CONCERNING WATER RESOURCES.

CHAPTER I

GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1

In this Law, what is meant by:

Water Resources are water, water sources, and the water potential contained therein.

Water is all water found on, above, or below the land surface, including in this definition surface water, groundwater, rainwater, and seawater on land.

Surface Water is all Water found on the land surface.

Groundwater is Water found in the soil or rock layers below the land surface.

Drinking Water is water that, through processing or without processing, meets health requirements and can be consumed directly.

Water Source is a natural and/or artificial place or container for Water found on, above, or below the land surface.

Water Potential is the potential contained in Water and/or at a Water Source that can provide benefits or cause harm to human life and livelihood as well as the environment.

Water Resources Management is the effort to plan, implement, monitor, and evaluate the implementation of Water Resources Conservation, Water Resources Utilization, and Control of the Destructive Force of Water.

Water Resources Management Master Plan is the basic framework for planning, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating activities of Water Resources Conservation, Water Resources Utilization, and Control of the Destructive Force of Water.

Water Resources Management Plan is the result of comprehensive and integrated planning required to carry out Water Resources Management.

River Basin is an integrated area for Water Resources Management within one or more River Catchment Areas and/or small islands with an area of less than or equal to 2,000 (two thousand) square kilometers.

River Catchment Area is a land area that forms a single unit with a river and its tributaries, which functions to naturally collect, store, and channel Water originating from rainfall to a lake or the sea, with its land boundary being a topographic divide and its sea boundary extending to the waters still influenced by land activities.

Groundwater Basin is an area bounded by hydrogeological limits, where all hydrogeological events, such as recharge, flow, and discharge of Groundwater, take place.

Water Resources Conservation is the effort to maintain the existence and sustainability of the state, properties, and functions of Water Resources so they are always available in sufficient quantity and quality to meet the needs of humans and other living beings, both in the present and in the future.

Water Resources Utilization is the effort of arranging, providing, using, and developing Water Resources optimally to be effective and efficient.

Destructive Force of Water is the Water Potential that is detrimental to life.

Control of the Destructive Force of Water is the effort to prevent, mitigate, and restore the damage to environmental quality caused by the Destructive Force of Water.

Planning is a process of activities to determine actions that will be carried out in a coordinated and directed manner to achieve the objectives of Water Resources Management.

Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources are activities that include regulation, implementation, care, monitoring, and evaluation to ensure the existence and preservation of the functions and benefits of Water Resources and their infrastructure.

Water Resources Infrastructure is Water structures along with other supporting structures that support Water Resources Management activities, both directly and indirectly.

Water Resources Manager is an institution given the duty and responsibility by the Central Government or a Regional Government in Water Resources Management based on the provisions of laws and regulations.

Indigenous Peoples are customary law communities and/or traditional communities that have lived for generations in a specific geographical area and are bound by a cultural identity, a strong relationship with the land, as well as their customary territory and the natural resources within it.

Ulayat Right is the communal right held by a specific Indigenous People over a particular territory that constitutes the living environment of its members, which includes the right to utilize the land, forest, and Water along with its contents in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.

Every Person is an individual or a corporation, whether a legal entity or not.

Central Government is the President of the Republic of Indonesia who holds the governmental power of the state of the Republic of Indonesia, assisted by the Vice President and ministers as referred to in the 1945 Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia.

Regional Government is the head of a region as an element of the Regional Administration organizer who leads the implementation of governmental affairs that are the authority of the autonomous region.

Minister is the minister who organizes governmental affairs in the field of Water Resources.

Water Resources Management Service Fee, hereinafter abbreviated as BJPSDA, is the fee charged, either partially or in full, to users of Water Resources which is used for sustainable Water Resources Management.

Drinking Water Supply System is a unified system of facilities and infrastructure for providing drinking water.

Article 2

Water Resources Management shall be conducted based on the principles of:
a. public benefit;
b. affordability;
c. justice;
d. balance;
e. self-reliance;
f. local wisdom;
g. environmental insight;
h. preservation;
i. sustainability;
j. integration and harmony; and
k. transparency and accountability.

Article 3

The regulation of Water Resources aims to:
a. provide protection and guarantee the fulfillment of the people's right to Water;
b. guarantee the sustainable availability of Water and Water Sources to provide benefits to the community in a just manner;
c. guarantee the preservation of the function of Water and Water Sources to support sustainable development;
d. guarantee the creation of legal certainty for the implementation of community participation in overseeing the utilization of Water Resources, starting from planning, implementation, and evaluation of utilization;
e. guarantee the protection and empowerment of the community, including Indigenous Peoples, in the effort of Water and Water Source conservation; and
f. control the Destructive Force of Water comprehensively, covering efforts of prevention, mitigation, and recovery.

CHAPTER II

SCOPE OF REGULATION

Article 4

The scope of regulation of Water Resources includes:
a. state control and the people's right to Water;
b. duties and authorities in Water Resources Management;
c. Water Resources Management;
d. permitting;
e. water resources information systems;
f. empowerment and supervision;
g. funding;
h. rights and obligations;
i. community participation; and
j. coordination.

CHAPTER III

STATE CONTROL AND THE PEOPLE'S RIGHT TO WATER

Part One
State Control

Article 5

Water Resources are controlled by the state and used for the greatest prosperity of the people.

Article 6

The state guarantees the people's right to Water to fulfill the minimum basic daily needs for a healthy and clean life with sufficient quantity, good quality, safety, sustained continuity, and affordability.

Article 7

Water Resources may not be owned and/or controlled by individuals, community groups, or business entities.

Part Two
The People's Right to Water

Article 8

(1) The people's right to Water, the fulfillment of which is guaranteed by the state as referred to in Article 6, constitutes the minimum basic daily need.
(2) In addition to the people's right to Water, the fulfillment of which is guaranteed by the state as referred to in paragraph (1), the state prioritizes the people's right to Water as follows:
a. basic daily needs;
b. smallholder agriculture; and
c. use of Water Resources for business purposes to fulfill basic daily needs through the Drinking Water Supply System.
(3) In the event that the availability of Water is insufficient for the priority of fulfillment as referred to in paragraph (2), the fulfillment of Water for basic daily needs is prioritized over others.
(4) In the event that the availability of Water is sufficient, after the priority sequence of fulfillment as referred to in paragraph (2), the subsequent priority sequence is:
a. use of Water Resources for non-business activities for the public interest; and
b. use of Water Resources for other business needs for which a permit has been established.
(5) The Central Government or a Regional Government shall establish the priority sequence for Water fulfillment in a River Basin according to its authority based on the provisions as referred to in paragraph (2), paragraph (3), and paragraph (4).
(6) In establishing the priority for Water fulfillment as referred to in paragraph (5), the Central Government or a Regional Government must first consider the need for Water for the maintenance of Water Sources and the living environment.
(7) The people's right to Water is not a right of ownership over Water, but is limited only to the right to obtain and use a certain quota of Water in accordance with the allocation, the determination of which is regulated by a Government Regulation.
(8) Further provisions regarding the use of Water Resources to fulfill basic daily needs, smallholder agriculture, and business needs to fulfill basic daily needs through the Drinking Water Supply System, as referred to in paragraph (2), as well as to fulfill non-business activities for the public interest and other business needs as referred to in paragraph (4), shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

CHAPTER IV

DUTIES AND AUTHORITIES

Article 9

(1) Based on state control over Water Resources as referred to in Article 5, the Central Government and/or Regional Governments are given the duty and authority to regulate and manage Water Resources.
(2) The control of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1) is exercised by the Central Government and/or Regional Governments while still recognizing the Ulayat Right of local Indigenous Peoples and similar rights, as long as they do not conflict with national interests and the provisions of laws and regulations.
(3) The Ulayat Right of Indigenous Peoples over Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (2) remains recognized as long as it verifiably still exists and has been regulated by a Regional Regulation.

Article 10

In regulating and managing Water Resources, the Central Government, as referred to in Article 9 paragraph (1), is tasked with:
a. formulating the national policy on Water Resources;
b. formulating the Water Resources Management Master Plan for trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins, including the Groundwater Basins within those River Basins;
c. formulating the Water Resources Management Plan for trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins, including the Groundwater Basins within those River Basins;
d. implementing Water Resources Management in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins, including the Groundwater Basins within those River Basins;
e. managing Water Source protection areas in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
f. conducting the permitting process for the use of Water Resources in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
g. developing and managing trans-provincial Drinking Water Supply Systems and Drinking Water Supply Systems for national strategic interests;
h. guaranteeing the provision of raw Water that meets quality standards for the fulfillment of the minimum basic daily needs of the community in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
i. developing and managing irrigation systems as a unified system in irrigation areas that are the authority of the Central Government;
j. maintaining the effectiveness, efficiency, quality, and orderliness of the implementation of Water Resources Management in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
k. providing technical assistance and technical guidance in Water Resources Management to provincial Regional Governments and regency/city Regional Governments;
l. developing technology in the field of Water Resources;
m. conducting supervision of the implementation of the duties and authorities of Water Resources Management by provincial Regional Governments and/or regency/city Regional Governments;
n. conducting supervision of the implementation of the duties and authorities of the development and management of trans-provincial Drinking Water Supply Systems;
o. conducting supervision of the implementation of the duties and authorities of the development and management of irrigation systems in irrigation areas under the authority of provincial Regional Governments and regency/city Regional Governments; and
p. facilitating the resolution of inter-provincial disputes in Water Resources Management.

Article 11

In regulating and managing Water Resources, the Central Government, as referred to in Article 9 paragraph (1), has the authority to:
a. establish the national policy on Water Resources;
b. establish the Water Resources Management Master Plan for trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
c. establish the Water Resources Management Plan for trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
d. establish Water Source protection areas in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
e. establish Groundwater conservation zones in Groundwater Basins within trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
f. establish the status of irrigation areas;
g. regulate, establish, and grant permits for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs and permits for the use of Water Resources for business needs at specific locations in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
h. form a coordination forum for Water Resources Management in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins;
i. establish norms, standards, procedures, and criteria for Water Resources Management;
j. form a Water Resources Manager;
k. establish the unit value of BJPSDA by involving relevant stakeholders;
l. establish national policies and strategies for the implementation of the Drinking Water Supply System; and
m. collect, receive, and use BJPSDA in trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, and national strategic River Basins.

Article 12

The duties and authorities of Regional Governments as referred to in Article 9 paragraph (1) include the duties and authorities of provincial Regional Governments and/or regency/city Regional Governments.

Article 13

In regulating and managing Water Resources, the provincial Regional Government, as referred to in Article 12, is tasked with:
a. formulating the provincial Water Resources Management policy based on the national Water Resources policy, taking into account the interests of neighboring provinces;
b. formulating the Water Resources Management Master Plan for trans-regency/city River Basins;
c. formulating the Water Resources Management Plan for trans-regency/city River Basins;
d. implementing Water Resources Management in trans-regency/city River Basins, including the Groundwater Basins within those River Basins;
e. managing Water Source protection areas in trans-regency/city River Basins;
f. conducting the permitting process for the use of Water Resources in trans-regency/city River Basins;
g. guaranteeing the provision of raw Water that meets quality standards for the fulfillment of the minimum basic daily needs of the community in trans-regency/city River Basins;
h. developing and managing irrigation systems as a unified system in irrigation areas that are the authority of the provincial Regional Government;
i. developing and managing trans-regency/city Drinking Water Supply Systems;
j. maintaining the effectiveness, efficiency, quality, and orderliness of the implementation of Water Resources Management in trans-regency/city River Basins;
k. providing technical assistance and technical guidance in Water Resources Management to regency/city Regional Governments;
l. facilitating the resolution of inter-regency and/or inter-city disputes in Water Resources Management; and
m. conducting supervision of the implementation of the duties and authorities of Water Resources Management by regency/city Regional Governments.

Article 14

In regulating and managing Water Resources, the Provincial Regional Government, as referred to in Article 12, has the authority to:
a. establish the Water Resources Management policy in its territory based on the national Water Resources policy, taking into account the interests of neighboring provinces;
b. establish the Water Resources Management Master Plan for trans-regency/city River Basins;
c. establish the Water Resources Management Plan for trans-regency/city River Basins, taking into account the interests of neighboring provinces;
d. establish Water Source protection areas in trans-regency/city River Basins;
e. establish Groundwater conservation zones in Groundwater Basins within trans-regency/city River Basins;
f. establish provincial policies and strategies for the implementation of the Drinking Water Supply System;
g. regulate, establish, and grant permits for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs and permits for the use of Water Resources for business needs at specific locations in trans-regency/city River Basins;
h. form a coordination forum for Water Resources Management in trans-regency/city River Basins;
i. establish the unit value of BJPSDA by involving relevant stakeholders; and
j. collect, receive, and use BJPSDA in trans-regency/city River Basins.

Article 15

In regulating and managing Water Resources, the regency/city Regional Government, as referred to in Article 12, is tasked with:
a. formulating the regency/city Water Resources Management policy based on the national Water Resources policy and the provincial Water Resources Management policy, taking into account the interests of neighboring regencies/cities;
b. formulating the Water Resources Management Master Plan for River Basins within a single regency/city;
c. formulating the Water Resources Management Plan for River Basins within a single regency/city;
d. developing and managing irrigation systems as a unified system in irrigation areas that are the authority of the regency/city Regional Government;
e. managing Water Source protection areas in River Basins within a single regency/city;
f. conducting the permitting process for the use of Water Resources in River Basins within a single regency/city;
g. guaranteeing the provision of raw Water that meets quality standards for the fulfillment of the minimum basic daily needs of the community in River Basins within a single regency/city;
h. endeavoring to provide water for the fulfillment of smallholder agriculture, non-business activities, and/or business activities in river basins within a single regency/city;
i. fulfilling the minimum basic daily needs for Water for the community in the regency/city territory;
j. implementing Water Resources Management in River Basins within a single regency/city, including the Groundwater Basins within those River Basins;
k. developing and managing the Drinking Water Supply System in the regency/city area;
l. maintaining the effectiveness, efficiency, quality, and orderliness of the implementation of Water Resources Management in River Basins within a single regency/city;
m. providing technical assistance and technical guidance in Water Resources Management to village governments; and
n. facilitating the resolution of disputes within a single regency/city in Water Resources Management.

Article 16

In regulating and managing Water Resources, the regency/city Regional Government, as referred to in Article 12, has the authority to:
a. establish the Water Resources Management policy in its territory based on the national Water Resources policy and the provincial Water Resources Management policy, taking into account the interests of neighboring regencies/cities;
b. establish the Water Resources Management Master Plan for River Basins within a single regency/city, taking into account the interests of neighboring regencies/cities;
c. establish the Water Resources Management Plan for River Basins within a single regency/city, taking into account the interests of neighboring regencies/cities;
d. establish Water Source protection areas in River Basins within a single regency/city;
e. regulate, establish, and grant permits for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs and permits for the use of Water Resources for business needs at specific locations in River Basins within a single regency/city;
f. form a coordination forum for Water Resources Management in River Basins within a single regency/city;
g. establish the unit value of BJPSDA by involving relevant stakeholders;
h. collect, receive, and use BJPSDA in River Basins within a single regency/city; and
i. establish regency/city policies and strategies for the implementation of the Drinking Water Supply System.

Article 17

The village government or what is known by another name has duties that include:
a. assisting the Central Government and/or Regional Governments in managing Water Resources in the village area based on the principle of public benefit and with due regard to the interests of other villages;
b. encouraging the initiative and participation of the village community in the Management of Water Resources in its territory;
c. participating in maintaining the effectiveness, efficiency, quality, and orderliness of the implementation of Water Resources Management; and
d. assisting the regency/city Regional Government in fulfilling the minimum basic daily needs for Water for village residents.

Article 18

In implementing the duties and authorities as referred to in Article 10 and Article 11, the Central Government may carry them out itself or may delegate a portion of its authority to Central Government apparatus or representatives of the Central Government in the regions, or may assign it to a Regional Government in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.

Article 19

(1) A portion of the duties and authorities of the Central Government and/or Regional Governments as referred to in Article 10, Article 11, Article 13, Article 14, Article 15, and Article 16 in managing Water Resources covering a River Basin may be assigned to a Water Resources Manager.
(2) The Water Resources Manager as referred to in paragraph (1) may be in the form of a ministerial technical implementation unit/regional technical implementation unit or a state-owned enterprise/regionally-owned enterprise in the field of Water Resources Management.
(3) A portion of the duties and authorities as referred to in paragraph (1) does not include:
a. establishing policies;
b. establishing a Water Resources Management Master Plan;
c. establishing a Water Resources Management Plan;
d. establishing Water Source protection areas;
e. establishing permits;
f. forming coordination forums;
g. establishing norms, standards, procedures, and criteria;
h. forming a Water Resources Manager; and
i. establishing the unit value of BJPSDA.
(4) A state-owned enterprise/regionally-owned enterprise in the field of Water Resources Management as referred to in paragraph (2) must meet the following provisions:
a. have the duty to carry out a part of the Water Resources Management functions, namely development, operation, and maintenance;
b. have the duty to use Water Resources for business needs only within its working area;
c. provide quality services with the principle of sound company management;
d. have the duty to collect, receive, and use BJPSDA;
e. receive special assignments given by the Central Government and/or a Regional Government; and
f. not be solely oriented towards pursuing profit.
(5) The assignment by the Central Government to a state-owned enterprise in the field of Water Resources Management as referred to in paragraph (2) shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
(6) The assignment by a Regional Government to a regionally-owned enterprise in the field of Water Resources Management as referred to in paragraph (2) shall be regulated by a regulation of the head of the region.

Article 20

(1) In the event that a provincial Regional Government and/or a regency/city Regional Government is not yet able to carry out a portion of the duties and authorities as referred to in Article 13 through Article 16, the provincial Regional Government and/or the regency/city Regional Government may surrender it to the government above it in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
(2) The implementation of a portion of the duties and authorities of Water Resources Management by a provincial Regional Government and/or a regency/city Regional Government as referred to in Article 13 through Article 16 must be taken over by the government above it in the event of:
a. the provincial Regional Government and/or the regency/city Regional Government fails to carry out a portion of the duties and authorities of Water Resources Management, which could endanger the public interest;
b. the provincial Regional Government and/or the regency/city Regional Government fails to carry out a portion of the duties and authorities of Water Resources Management, which could disrupt public services; and/or
c. the existence of inter-provincial or inter-regency and/or inter-city disputes that cannot be resolved.
(3) Further provisions regarding the surrender and takeover of duties and authorities shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.


CHAPTER V
MANAGEMENT OF WATER RESOURCES
Part One
General
Article 21
(1) Water resources have social, environmental, and economic functions that are organized and realized in a harmonious manner.
(2) Water Resources are managed in an integrated, sustainable, and environmentally-sound manner.
Article 22
(1) The Management of Water Resources is based on the River Basin, taking into account the interrelationship between the use of Surface Water and Groundwater, with an emphasis on the utilization of Surface Water.
(2) The Management of Water Resources based on the River Basin as referred to in paragraph (1) shall at least consider:
a. the River Catchment Area in its natural state;
b. the functional characteristics of the Water Source;
c. the carrying capacity of the Water Resources;
d. the distinct characteristics and aspirations of the region and surrounding community by involving relevant stakeholders;
e. funding capacity;
f. climate change;
g. the conservation of biological natural resources and their ecosystems;
h. technological development; and
i. the population size and distribution as well as its growth projections.
(3) Groundwater as referred to in paragraph (1) constitutes the Groundwater in the Groundwater Basin located within the respective River Basin.
(4) The River Basin as referred to in paragraph (1) includes trans-state River Basins, trans-provincial River Basins, national strategic River Basins, trans-regency/city River Basins, and River Basins within a single regency/city.
(5) Further provisions concerning the criteria and procedures for the designation of a River Basin shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
(6) The River Basin as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be designated by a Presidential Decree.
Article 23
(1) Water Resources Management is carried out comprehensively, integratedly, and with an environmental perspective, with the aim of realizing the sustainable benefits of Water for the greatest prosperity of the people.
(2) Water Resources Management activities include Water Resources Conservation, Water Resources Utilization, and Control of the Destructive Force of Water.
Part Two
Conservation of Water Resources
Article 24
(1) Water Resources Conservation is aimed at maintaining the continuity of the existence, carrying capacity, holding capacity, and function of Water Resources.
(2) Water Resources Conservation as referred to in paragraph (1) is carried out by the Central Government and/or Regional Governments in accordance with their authorities based on the provisions of laws and regulations.
(3) Water Resources Conservation as referred to in paragraph (1) is carried out by referring to the Water Resources Management Plan through the activities of:
a. protection and preservation of Water Sources;
b. water preservation;
c. water quality management; and
d. water pollution control.
(4) The protection and preservation of Water Sources as referred to in paragraph (3) letter a is aimed at protecting and preserving Water Sources and their surrounding environment from damage or disturbances caused by natural forces and by human actions.
(5) Water preservation as referred to in paragraph (3) letter b is aimed at maintaining the existence and availability of Water or the quantity of Water in accordance with its function and benefits.
(6) Water quality management as referred to in paragraph (3) letter c is carried out by improving the quality of Water at the Water Source and in Water Resources Infrastructure.
(7) Water pollution control as referred to in paragraph (3) letter d is carried out by preventing the entry of water pollution into Water Sources and Water Resources Infrastructure.
(8) The Water Resources Conservation activities as referred to in paragraph (3) shall become one of the references in spatial planning.
Article 25
Every Person is prohibited from carrying out activities that result in:
a. disruption of the water system condition of a River Catchment Area;
b. damage to Water Sources and/or their infrastructure;
c. disruption of water preservation efforts; and
d. water pollution.
Article 26
(1) Water Resources Conservation shall be implemented at springs, rivers, lakes, reservoirs, swamps, Groundwater recharge areas, Groundwater Basins, water catchment areas, nature sanctuaries, nature conservation areas, forest areas, and coastal areas.
(2) Water Resources Conservation that is located within nature sanctuaries, nature conservation areas, forest areas, and coastal areas as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be implemented based on the provisions of laws and regulations.
Article 27
Further provisions concerning Water Resources Conservation as referred to in Article 24 up to Article 26 shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
Part Three
Utilization of Water Resources
Article 28
(1) The Utilization of Water Resources is aimed at sustainably capitalizing on Water Resources, with the main priority being the fulfillment of Water for the basic daily needs of the community.
(2) In the event that there is still sufficient availability of Water Resources for the needs as referred to in paragraph (1), the subsequent priority for the fulfillment of Water needs is for the irrigation needs of smallholder agriculture.
(3) The priority sequence for the fulfillment of Water needs is established in the Water Resources Management Master Plan and the Water Resources Management Plan, which includes the priority for the fulfillment of Water for the needs as referred to in paragraph (1) and paragraph (2) and the sequence for the fulfillment of Water for the needs of non-business activities and business activities.
Article 29
(1) The Utilization of Water Resources as referred to in Article 28 includes:
a. Surface Water at springs, rivers, lakes, reservoirs, swamps, and other Surface Water Sources;
b. Groundwater in Groundwater Basins;
c. rainwater; and
d. seawater located on land.
(2) The Utilization of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1) is carried out through the activities of:
a. water resources allocation;
b. water resources provision;
c. water resources use; and
d. water resources development.
(3) The Utilization of Water Resources activities as referred to in paragraph (2) shall refer to the Water Resources Management Master Plan and the Water Resources Management Plan, taking into account the balance of ecological, economic, and socio-cultural aspects.
Article 30
(1) The Utilization of Water Resources for business activities as referred to in Article 28 paragraph (3), conducted within a River Basin by building and/or using a transmission channel, may only be done for other River Basins if the availability of Water exceeds the needs of the population in the respective River Basin.
(2) The Utilization of Water Resources for business activities as referred to in paragraph (1) is based on the Water Resources Management Master Plan and the Water Resources Management Plan for the respective River Basin, involving relevant stakeholders.
Article 31
In a state of emergency, the Central Government and/or Regional Governments shall regulate and establish the use of Water Resources as referred to in Article 29 paragraph (2) letter c for the purposes of conservation, preparation for construction implementation, and fulfillment of priority uses of Water Resources.
Article 32
Every Person who uses Water Resources as referred to in Article 29 paragraph (2) letter c is prohibited from causing pollution and/or damage to the Water Source, the environment, and the surrounding Water Resources Infrastructure.
Article 33
(1) Every Person is prohibited from carrying out the Utilization of Water Resources in nature sanctuaries and nature conservation areas.
(2) The prohibition on the Utilization of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1) is exempted for individuals for the fulfillment of basic daily needs that are not utilized as a form of business.
Article 34
Further provisions concerning the Utilization of Water Resources as referred to in Article 28 up to Article 33 shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
Part Four
Control of the Destructive Force of Water
Article 35
(1) The Control of the Destructive Force of Water is carried out comprehensively, encompassing efforts of prevention, mitigation, and recovery.
(2) The Control of the Destructive Force of Water as referred to in paragraph (1) is prioritized on prevention efforts through the Control Plan for the Destructive Force of Water, which is formulated in an integrated and comprehensive manner within the Water Resources Management Master Plan.
(3) The prevention of the Destructive Force of Water as referred to in paragraph (1) is aimed at preventing the occurrence of disasters caused by the Destructive Force of Water.
(4) The mitigation of the Destructive Force of Water as referred to in paragraph (1) is aimed at alleviating suffering resulting from disasters through disaster mitigation.
(5) Mitigation efforts for the Destructive Force of Water that are declared as a disaster shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
(6) In a hazardous situation, the governor and/or regent/mayor are authorized to take emergency actions for the purpose of mitigating the Destructive Force of Water.
(7) Recovery efforts for the Destructive Force of Water are carried out through reconstruction and rehabilitation activities.
Article 36
Every Person is prohibited from carrying out activities that may result in the occurrence of the Destructive Force of Water.
Article 37
Further provisions concerning the Control of the Destructive Force of Water as referred to in Article 35 shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
Part Five
Stages of Water Resources Management
Paragraph 1
General
Article 38
The stages of Water Resources Management include:
a. Water Resources Management Planning;
b. implementation of Water Resources Infrastructure construction and non-construction implementation;
c. implementation of Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources; and
d. monitoring and evaluation of Water Resources Management.
Paragraph 2
Water Resources Management Planning
Article 39
(1) The Central Government and/or Regional Governments, in accordance with their authorities, shall formulate a Water Resources Management Master Plan for the implementation of Water Resources Management that can provide the greatest possible benefit for the interests of the community.
(2) The Water Resources Management Master Plan as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be formulated based on the River Basin with the principle of inter-sectoral and inter-regional integration, as well as the interrelationship of use between Surface Water and Groundwater.
(3) The Water Resources Management Master Plan as referred to in paragraph (2) is further elaborated in the Water Resources Management Plan as a reference for the long-term implementation of Water Resources Management.
(4) The Water Resources Management Plan as referred to in paragraph (3) serves as a reference for the formulation of the Water Resources Management program and the programs of relevant non-ministerial government ministries or agencies.
(5) The Water Resources Management Plan serves as the basis and one of the elements in the formulation, review, and/or refinement of the regional spatial plan.
(6) The Water Resources Management Program as referred to in paragraph (4) serves as a reference in the formulation of the Water Resources Management activity plan and the activity plans of relevant non-ministerial government ministries or agencies.
(7) The implementation of the Water Resources Management activity plan includes the construction of Water Resources Infrastructure, non-construction activities, and Operation and Maintenance activities of Water Resources.
(8) Further provisions regarding the formulation of the Water Resources Management Master Plan, the Water Resources Management Plan, the Water Resources Management program, and the Water Resources Management activity plan as referred to in paragraph (2) up to paragraph (6), shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
Paragraph 3
Implementation of Water Resources Infrastructure Construction and Non-Construction Implementation
Article 40
(1) The implementation of Water Resources Infrastructure construction and non-construction implementation is carried out by the Central Government and/or Regional Governments in accordance with their authorities based on the program and activity plan.
(2) The implementation of Water Resources Infrastructure construction and non-construction implementation as referred to in paragraph (1) may be carried out with the involvement of the community.
(3) Every Person or community group, on their own initiative, may carry out construction activities of Water Resources Infrastructure and non-construction implementation for their own interests based on a permit from the Central Government and/or a Regional Government in accordance with their authority.
(4) The implementation of Water Resources Infrastructure construction and non-construction implementation shall be carried out by:
a. following norms, standards, procedures, and criteria;
b. utilizing local technology and resources; and
c. prioritizing safety, work security, and the sustainability of ecological functions, in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
(5) The obligation to obtain a permit as referred to in paragraph (3) is exempted for non-construction activities that do not result in physical changes to the Water Source.
(6) Further provisions regarding the permit as referred to in paragraph (3) shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
Paragraph 4
Implementation of Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources
Article 41
(1) The implementation of Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources consists of the maintenance of Water Sources and the operation and maintenance of Water Resources Infrastructure.
(2) The implementation of Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1) includes regulation, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation to ensure the preservation of the functions and benefits of Water Resources and their infrastructure.
(3) The implementation of Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources is carried out by the Central Government and/or Regional Governments in accordance with their authorities and may involve the community.
(4) The implementation of operation and maintenance of Water Resources Infrastructure built by any Person or community group becomes the duty and responsibility of the constructing parties.
(5) Further provisions regarding the implementation of Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
Article 42
Every Person is prohibited from carrying out activities that result in damage to Water Resources Infrastructure.
Paragraph 5
Monitoring and Evaluation of Water Resources Management
Article 43
(1) Monitoring of Water Resources Management is conducted on:
a. Water Resources Management Planning;
b. the implementation of Water Resources Infrastructure construction and non-construction implementation; and
c. the implementation of Operation and Maintenance of Water Resources.
(2) The evaluation of Water Resources Management is conducted based on the results of monitoring Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1) against the objectives of Water Resources Management.
(3) The results of the evaluation of Water Resources Management are used as consideration in making improvements to the implementation of Water Resources Management.
(4) The implementation of monitoring and evaluation of Water Resources Management as referred to in paragraph (1) and paragraph (2) is carried out by the Central Government and/or a Regional Government in accordance with their authority.
(5) Further provisions regarding the monitoring and evaluation of Water Resources Management shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.
CHAPTER VI
PERMITTING
Part One
General
Article 44
(1) The use of Water Resources as referred to in Article 29 paragraph (2) letter c for business and non-business needs shall be carried out based on a permit.
(2) The permit for the use of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1) is granted with due regard to the function of the area and the preservation of the living environment.
(3) The permit for the use of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1) is granted by the Central Government and/or a Regional Government in accordance with their authority.
(4) The permit for the use of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (2) cannot be leased or transferred, either partially or in whole.
Part Two
Permit for the Use of Water Resources for Non-Business Needs
Article 45
The permit for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs consists of:
a. a permit for the use of Water Resources for the fulfillment of basic daily needs is required if:
1) the method of use is carried out by changing the natural condition of the Water Source; and/or
2) its use is intended for group purposes that require Water in large quantities.
b. a permit for the use of Water Resources for the fulfillment of smallholder agriculture needs is required if:
1) the method of its use is carried out by changing the natural condition of the Water Source; and/or
2) its use is for smallholder agriculture outside the existing irrigation system.
c. a permit for the use of Water Resources for the fulfillment of needs for activities other than to meet basic daily needs and smallholder agriculture which do not constitute business activities.
Part Three
Permit for the Use of Water Resources for Business Needs
Article 46
(1) The use of Water Resources for business needs is organized by observing the principles of:
a. not disturbing, not overriding, and not negating the people's right to Water;
b. state protection of the people's right to Water;
c. preservation of the living environment as a human right;
d. absolute state supervision and control over Water;
e. primary priority for the use of Water Resources for business activities is given to state-owned enterprises, regionally-owned enterprises, or village-owned enterprises; and
f. the granting of a permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs to the private sector may be carried out with specific and strict conditions after the principles as referred to in letters a through e are fulfilled and there is still water availability.
(2) The use of Water Resources for business needs is aimed at increasing the benefits of Water Resources for the welfare of the people.
(3) The use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in paragraph (2) is carried out by prioritizing the public interest.
Article 47
(1) The use of Water Resources for business needs may be organized if the Water for basic daily needs and smallholder agriculture has been fulfilled and as long as the availability of Water is still sufficient.
(2) The use of Water Resources for business needs is carried out by taking into account social and environmental functions as well as ensuring the security of state assets and environmental preservation.
Article 48
The use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in Article 47 is organized based on the plan for the provision of Water and/or the spatial utilization zone at the Water Source contained in the Water Resources Management Plan, involving relevant stakeholders.
Article 49
(1) The use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in Article 47 may be in the form of the use of:
a. Water Resources as a medium;
b. Water and Water Potential as materials;
c. Water Sources as a medium; and/or
d. Water, Water Sources, and/or Water Potential as a medium and material.
(2) The use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in paragraph (1) must have a permit.
(3) The granting of a permit is carried out strictly with the following order of priority:
a. fulfillment of basic daily needs for groups that require Water in large quantities;
b. fulfillment of basic daily needs that change the natural condition of the Water Source;
c. smallholder agriculture outside the existing irrigation system;
d. use of Water Resources for business purposes to fulfill basic daily needs through the Drinking Water Supply System;
e. non-business activities for the public interest;
f. use of Water Resources for business needs by state-owned enterprises, regionally-owned enterprises, or village-owned enterprises; and
g. use of Water Resources for business needs by private business entities or individuals.
(4) A permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in paragraph (2) may be granted for:
a. a specific point or place at a Water Source;
b. a specific segment of a Water Source; or
c. a specific part of a Water Source.
(5) A permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in paragraph (4) may be granted to:
a. a state-owned enterprise;
b. a regionally-owned enterprise;
c. a village-owned enterprise;
d. a cooperative;
e. a private business entity; or
f. an individual.
Article 50
A permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs using Water and Water Potential as materials as referred to in Article 49 paragraph (1) letter b which produces a product in the form of Drinking Water for basic daily needs is granted to a state-owned enterprise, a regionally-owned enterprise, or a village-owned enterprise that organizes the Drinking Water Supply System.
Article 51
A permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs may be granted to a private party after fulfilling specific and strict requirements as referred to in Article 46 paragraph (1) letter f, at a minimum:
a. in accordance with the Water Resources Management Master Plan and the Water Resources Management Plan;
b. fulfilling administrative technical requirements;
c. obtaining approval from stakeholders in the area of the Water Source; and
d. fulfilling the obligation of Water Resources Conservation fees which are a component in the BJPSDA and other financial obligations in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
Article 52
(1) The use of Water Resources for other countries is prohibited, except for humanitarian purposes.
(2) The exception as referred to in paragraph (1) must meet the requirement that the need for the use of Water Resources in the respective River Basin and surrounding areas has been fulfilled.
(3) The use of Water Resources for other countries as referred to in paragraph (1) must be based on the Water Resources Management Master Plan and the Water Resources Management Plan for the respective River Basin and must take into account the interests of the surrounding areas.
(4) The plan for the use of Water Resources for other countries is carried out through a public consultation process by the Central Government and/or a Regional Government in accordance with their authority.
(5) The use of Water Resources for other countries as referred to in paragraph (3) and paragraph (4) must obtain a permit from the Central Government based on recommendations from the Regional Government and in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
Article 53
Further provisions concerning the permitting for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs as referred to in Article 45 and the permitting for the use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in Article 46 up to Article 51, as well as the permitting for the use of Water Resources for other countries as referred to in Article 52, shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

CHAPTER VII

WATER RESOURCES INFORMATION SYSTEM

Article 54

(1) To support the Management of Water Resources, the Central Government and Regional Governments shall organize the management of the water resources information system in accordance with their respective authorities.
(2) The water resources information system as referred to in paragraph (1) constitutes a network of water resources information that is distributed and managed by various institutions.
(3) The water resources information network as referred to in paragraph (2) must be accessible to various parties with an interest in the field of Water Resources.
(4) The Central Government, Regional Governments, Water Resources Managers, legal entities, organizations, institutions, and individuals are responsible for ensuring the accuracy, truth, and timeliness of the information they provide.
(5) Water Resources Information includes information concerning hydrological, hydrometeorological, and hydrogeological conditions, Water Resources policies, Water Resources Infrastructure, Water Resources technology, the environment at and around the Water Resources, as well as the social, economic, and cultural activities of the community related to Water Resources.
(6) To improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the water resources information system as referred to in paragraph (1), each institution, in accordance with its authority, shall carry out:
a. optimization of the utilization of data and information related to Water Resources, including the Hydrological, Hydrometeorological, and Hydrogeological Information Systems;
b. integrated management;
c. a clear and proportional division of roles between institutions;
d. regulation of data access;
e. regulation of data flow; and
f. regulation of data utilization.
(7) Further provisions concerning the water resources information system as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

CHAPTER VIII

EMPOWERMENT AND SUPERVISION

Article 55

(1) The Central Government and/or Regional Governments, in accordance with their authorities, shall organize the empowerment of stakeholders and Water Resources institutions in a planned and systematic manner to improve the performance of Water Resources Management.
(2) The empowerment as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be implemented in the activities of Planning, implementation, operation and maintenance, as well as monitoring and evaluation of Water Resources Management.
(3) The empowerment as referred to in paragraph (1) may involve the role of the community.
(4) The empowerment as referred to in paragraph (1) may be carried out through cooperation between the Central Government and/or Regional Governments with institutions in the field of Water Resources development from within the country or from abroad that are competent.
(5) Stakeholders, on their own initiative, may also carry out empowerment efforts for the benefit of the community, guided by the empowerment objectives as referred to in paragraph (1).

Article 56

(1) Supervision of Water Resources Management shall be carried out by the Central Government and/or Regional Governments in accordance with their authorities over the Management of Water Resources.
(2) The supervision of Water Resources Management as referred to in paragraph (1) may be carried out with the involvement of the community.
(3) Further provisions concerning the supervision of Water Resources Management shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

CHAPTER IX

FUNDING

Article 57

(1) Funding for Water Resources Management shall be determined based on the actual needs of Water Resources Management.
(2) The funding for management as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be carried out by taking into account the upstream and downstream linkages of the River Catchment Area and the function of the area.
(3) Funding for Water Resources Management may be sourced from:
a. the State Budget;
b. the Regional Budget; and/or
c. other legitimate sources in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
(4) Funding for Water Resources Management sourced from the State Budget and/or the Regional Budget as referred to in paragraph (3) letters a and b, which is the responsibility of the Central Government or a Regional Government, shall be based on their respective authorities in Water Resources Management.
(5) In the event of an urgent interest in the Management of Water Resources in trans-provincial, trans-regency/city, and national strategic River Basins, its management funding shall be carried out through an agreement between the concerned Central Government and/or Regional Governments.
(6) In the case of a state-owned enterprise, regionally-owned enterprise, village-owned enterprise, cooperative, private business entity, and individual who carries out the use of Water Resources for business activities, the funding shall be borne by each party that carries out said activity.
(7) The provision of Water Resources Infrastructure may be carried out through funding cooperation with a private business entity or another country's government.
(8) The funding cooperation as referred to in paragraph (7) does not include cooperation in the implementation of Operation and Maintenance activities of Water Resources.

Article 58

(1) Users of Water Resources are not charged a BJPSDA if they use Water Resources for:
a. fulfillment of basic daily needs;
b. smallholder agriculture;
c. activities other than to fulfill basic daily needs and smallholder agriculture that do not constitute business activities; and
d. construction activities at a Water Source that do not use Water.
(2) Users of Water Resources other than as referred to in paragraph (1) shall bear the BJPSDA.
(3) The Central Government and/or Regional Governments, in accordance with their authorities, have the right to the revenue from the BJPSDA collected from the users of Water Resources.
(4) The BJPSDA as referred to in paragraph (3) shall be used for the sustainability of Water Resources Management in the respective River Basin.

Article 59

The payment of BJPSDA as referred to in Article 58 must observe the beneficiary-pays principle.

Article 60

Further provisions concerning the funding of Water Resources Management as referred to in Article 57 up to Article 59 shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

CHAPTER X

RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS

Article 61

(1) In implementing Water Resources Management, the community has the right to:
a. obtain access to utilize Water Resources;
b. use Water for the fulfillment of minimum basic daily needs, smallholder agriculture, and non-business activities;
c. obtain benefits from Water Resources Management;
d. obtain appropriate compensation for losses incurred as a result of the implementation of Water Resources Management;
e. obtain information related to Water Resources Management;
f. express opinions on the Water Resources Management Plan that has been announced within a certain period according to local conditions;
g. submit reports and complaints to the competent authorities regarding losses suffered that are related to the implementation of Water Resources Management; and/or
h. file a lawsuit with the court against various Water Resources problems that are detrimental to their lives.
(2) The report and complaint as referred to in paragraph (1) letter g shall be submitted to the Central Government, a Regional Government, and/or a coordination forum at the River Basin level.
(3) Further provisions concerning the procedures for reporting and complaints as referred to in paragraph (2) shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

Article 62

(1) In using Water Resources, the community is obliged to:
a. protect and maintain the continuity of the function of Water Resources;
b. protect and secure Water Resources Infrastructure;
c. practice water conservation in its use;
d. undertake efforts to control and prevent the occurrence of water pollution;
e. repair environmental damage caused by the activities it has generated;
f. provide access for the use of Water Resources from a Water Source located on the land it controls for the community;
g. provide opportunities for other Water users to channel Water through the land it controls;
h. pay attention to the public interest; and
i. fulfill other obligations in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
(2) Further provisions concerning the obligations of the community as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

CHAPTER XI

COMMUNITY PARTICIPATION

Article 63

(1) The community has an equal opportunity to participate in the Management of Water Resources.
(2) The participation as referred to in paragraph (1) is carried out to channel the aspirations, thoughts, and interests of the community in the Management of Water Resources.
(3) The participation as referred to in paragraph (2) is carried out in the form of:
a. public consultation;
b. deliberation;
c. partnership;
d. submission of aspirations;
e. supervision; and/or
f. other involvement in accordance with the provisions of laws and regulations.
(4) Further provisions concerning community participation in the Management of Water Resources as referred to in paragraph (1), paragraph (2), and paragraph (3) shall be regulated by a Government Regulation.

CHAPTER XII

COORDINATION

Article 64

(1) Water Resources Management includes cross-sectoral and cross-regional interests that require integrated action to maintain the continuity of the function and benefits of Water and Water Sources.
(2) Water Resources Management as referred to in paragraph (1) is carried out through coordination by integrating the interests of various sectors, regions, and stakeholders in the field of Water Resources.
(3) The coordination as referred to in paragraph (2) is carried out at the level of:
a. national;
b. provincial;
c. regency/city; and
d. River Basin.

Article 65

(1) Coordination at the national level as referred to in Article 64 paragraph (3) letter a shall be carried out by the National Water Resources Council.
(2) Coordination at the national level as referred to in paragraph (1) is organized to:
a. formulate the national-level Water Resources Management policy;
b. prepare the draft designation of a River Basin and changes to the designation of a River Basin; and
c. formulate the policy for the management of the hydrological, hydrometeorological, and hydrogeological information systems at the national level.
(3) The National Water Resources Council as referred to in paragraph (1) shall consist of government representatives as permanent members and non-government representatives as non-permanent members.
(4) The National Water Resources Council as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be established by the President.
(5) Coordination at the provincial or regency/city level as referred to in Article 64 paragraph (3) letters b and c shall be carried out by a regional Water Resources council formed according to needs and consisting of representatives of the Regional Government as permanent members and representatives of the non-Regional Government as non-permanent members.
(6) In the event that a Water Resources council at the provincial or regency/city level has not yet been formed or is not formed, the coordination as referred to in paragraph (5) shall be carried out by the agency that organizes governmental affairs in the field of Water Resources.
(7) Coordination at the provincial or regency/city level as referred to in paragraph (5) is organized for the formulation of the Water Resources Management policy at the provincial or regency/city level.
(8) The provincial or regency/city Water Resources Council as referred to in paragraph (5) shall consist of representatives of the Regional Government as permanent members and representatives of the non-government of the Region as non-permanent members.
(9) Further provisions concerning the organizational structure and working procedures of the National Water Resources Council as referred to in paragraph (4) shall be regulated by a Presidential Regulation.
(10) Further provisions concerning guidelines for the organizational structure, working procedures, and formation of the provincial or regency/city Water Resources council as referred to in paragraph (5) shall be regulated by a Ministerial Regulation.

Article 66

(1) Coordination at the River Basin level as referred to in Article 64 paragraph (3) letter d shall be carried out by a coordination forum at the River Basin level.
(2) The coordination forum at the River Basin level as referred to in paragraph (1) has the main tasks of:
a. harmonizing the interests of inter-sectoral, inter-regional, and inter-stakeholder groups in the Management of Water Resources within the River Basin;
b. providing advice to the Central Government and/or Regional Governments regarding the implementation of Water Resources Management in accordance with the results of coordination as referred to in letter a; and
c. monitoring and evaluating the implementation of programs and activity plans for Water Resources Management in the River Basin.
(3) The coordination forum at the River Basin level as referred to in paragraph (1) shall consist of representatives from government agencies and the community representing the stakeholders of Water Resources in the respective River Basin.
(4) In carrying out its main tasks as referred to in paragraph (2), the coordination forum shall present community representatives related to the issues that need to be coordinated.
(5) The community representatives as referred to in paragraph (4) are representatives of the parties agreed upon by the Central Government, Regional Governments, and/or the stakeholders of Water Resources.
(6) Further provisions concerning the organizational structure, working procedures, and guidelines for the formation of the coordination forum as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be regulated by a Ministerial Regulation.

CHAPTER XIII

INVESTIGATION

Article 67

(1) In addition to investigators from the police of the Republic of Indonesia, certain civil servant officials within the government agency whose scope of duties and responsibilities is in the field of Water Resources are given special authority as investigators as referred to in the Criminal Procedure Code to conduct investigations into criminal acts related to Water Resources.
(2) The civil servant investigator officials as referred to in paragraph (1) are authorized to:
a. conduct examinations on the truth of a report or information regarding the existence of a criminal act related to Water Resources;
b. conduct examinations of persons or business entities suspected of committing a criminal act related to Water Resources;
c. summon persons to be heard and examined as witnesses or suspects in a criminal case related to Water Resources;
d. conduct inspections of Water Resources Infrastructure and stop equipment suspected of being used to commit a criminal act;
e. carry out arrests, detentions, and searches;
f. seal and/or confiscate equipment used to commit a criminal act as evidence;
g. request expert assistance in the framework of carrying out the duties of investigating a criminal act related to Water Resources;
h. create and sign an official report and send it to the investigators of the National Police of the Republic of Indonesia; and/or
i. stop an investigation if there is insufficient evidence or the event is not a criminal act.
(3) The civil servant investigator official as referred to in paragraph (2) shall notify the commencement of the investigation to the investigators of the National Police of the Republic of Indonesia.
(4) The civil servant investigator official as referred to in paragraph (2) shall submit the results of the investigation to the public prosecutor through the investigators of the National Police of the Republic of Indonesia in accordance with the Criminal Procedure Code.
(5) In carrying out their authority as referred to in paragraph (2), the civil servant investigator officials are under the coordination and supervision of the investigators of the National Police of the Republic of Indonesia in accordance with the Criminal Procedure Code.

CHAPTER XIV

CRIMINAL PROVISIONS

Article 68

Any Person who intentionally:
a. carries out activities that result in damage to Water Sources and their infrastructure and/or water pollution as referred to in Article 25 letters b and d; or
b. carries out activities that result in the occurrence of the Destructive Force of Water as referred to in Article 36,
shall be punished with imprisonment for a minimum of 3 (three) years and a maximum of 9 (nine) years and a fine of at least Rp5,000,000,000.00 (five billion rupiah) and at most Rp15,000,000,000.00 (fifteen billion rupiah).

Article 69

Any Person who intentionally:
a. disrupts water preservation efforts as referred to in Article 25 letter c;
b. uses Water Resources, causing damage to the Water Source, the environment, and/or the surrounding Water Resources Infrastructure as referred to in Article 32;
c. carries out the Utilization of Water Resources in nature sanctuaries and nature conservation areas as referred to in Article 33 paragraph (1); or
d. carries out activities that result in damage to Water Resources Infrastructure as referred to in Article 42,
shall be punished with imprisonment for a minimum of 18 (eighteen) months and a maximum of 6 (six) years and a fine of at least Rp2,500,000,000.00 (two billion five hundred million rupiah) and at most Rp10,000,000,000.00 (ten billion rupiah).

Article 70

Any Person who intentionally:
a. carries out construction and non-construction activities on a Water Source without obtaining a permit from the Central Government or a Regional Government as referred to in Article 40 paragraph (3);
b. leases or transfers, either partially or wholly, a permit for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs or a permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs as referred to in Article 44 paragraph (4); or
c. uses Water Resources for business needs without a permit as referred to in Article 49 paragraph (2),
shall be punished with imprisonment for a minimum of 1 (one) year and a maximum of 3 (three) years and a fine of at least Rp1,000,000,000.00 (one billion rupiah) and at most Rp5,000,000,000.00 (five billion rupiah).

Article 71

Any Person who, due to negligence:
a. carries out activities that result in the disruption of the water system condition of a River Catchment Area, damage to Water Sources and their infrastructure, and/or water pollution as referred to in Article 25 letters a, b, and d; or
b. carries out activities that result in the occurrence of the Destructive Force of Water as referred to in Article 36,
shall be punished with imprisonment for a minimum of 6 (six) months and a maximum of 18 (eighteen) months and a fine of at least Rp1,000,000,000.00 (one billion rupiah) and at most Rp3,000,000,000.00 (three billion rupiah).

Article 72

Any Person who, due to negligence:
a. disrupts water preservation efforts as referred to in Article 25 letter c;
b. uses Water Resources, causing damage to the Water Source, the environment, and/or the surrounding Water Resources Infrastructure as referred to in Article 32;
c. carries out the Utilization of Water Resources in nature sanctuaries and nature conservation areas as referred to in Article 33 paragraph (1); or
d. carries out activities that result in the damage of Water Resources Infrastructure as referred to in Article 42,
shall be punished with imprisonment for a minimum of 4 (four) months and a maximum of 1 (one) year and a fine of at least Rp500,000,000.00 (five hundred million rupiah) and at most Rp2,000,000,000.00 (two billion rupiah).

Article 73

Any Person who, due to negligence:
a. carries out construction and non-construction activities on a Water Source without a permit from the Central Government or a Regional Government as referred to in Article 40 paragraph (3); or
b. uses Water Resources for business needs without a permit as referred to in Article 49 paragraph (2),
shall be punished with imprisonment for a minimum of 3 (three) months and a maximum of 6 (six) months and a fine of at least Rp300,000,000.00 (three hundred million rupiah) and at most Rp1,000,000,000.00 (one billion rupiah).

Article 74

(1) In the event that a criminal act related to Water Resources as referred to in Article 68 up to Article 73 is committed by a business entity, the criminal penalty shall be imposed upon the business entity, the person who gave the order to commit the criminal act, and/or the leader of the concerned business entity.
(2) The penalty imposed upon the business entity as referred to in paragraph (1) shall be in the form of:
a. a criminal fine against the business entity amounting to two times the criminal fine as referred to in Article 68 up to Article 73;
b. imprisonment against the person who gave the order to commit the criminal act for a duration as referred to in Article 68 up to Article 73; and/or
c. imprisonment against the leader of the business entity with the same magnitude as regulated in Article 68 up to Article 73.

CHAPTER XV

TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS

Article 75

At the time this Law comes into force:
a. permits for the Use of Water Resources or permits for the Concession of Water Resources and permits for the Concession of Groundwater that were issued before the enactment of this Law shall remain valid until the permit expires.
b. applications for a permit for the Use of Water Resources or a permit for the Concession of Water Resources and a permit for the Concession of Groundwater that were submitted before the enactment of this Law and for which a permit has not yet been issued must be adjusted in accordance with this Law.

CHAPTER XVI

CLOSING PROVISIONS

Article 76

At the time this Law comes into force,
a. Law Number 11 of 1974 concerning Irrigation (State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia of 1974 Number 65, Addendum to the State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia Number 3046) is revoked and declared no longer valid; and
b. all implementing regulations that regulate Water Resources are declared to remain valid as long as they do not conflict with and have not been replaced based on this Law.

Article 77

At the time this Law comes into force, the provisions concerning the division of concurrent governmental affairs between the Central Government and provincial regional governments and regency/city regional governments, Number I Matrix of the division of concurrent governmental affairs between the central government and provincial regional governments and regency/city regional governments:
a. letter C Division of Governmental Affairs in the Field of Public Works and Spatial Planning Number 1 Sub-Affair of Water Resources (SDA) column 3 letter b, column 4 letter b, and column 5 letter b;
b. letter CC Division of Governmental Affairs in the Field of Energy and Mineral Resources Number 1 Sub-Affair of Geology column 3 letter a, column 4 letter b, and column 5,
which are contained in the appendix that is an inseparable part of Law Number 23 of 2014 concerning Regional Government (State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia of 2014 Number 244, Addendum to the State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia Number 5587) as amended several times, most recently by Law Number 9 of 2015 concerning the Second Amendment to Law Number 23 of 2014 concerning Regional Government (State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia of 2015 Number 58, Addendum to the State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia Number 5679), are revoked and declared no longer valid.

Article 78

The implementing regulations of this Law must be established no later than 2 (two) years from the date this Law is promulgated.

Article 79

This Law shall come into force on the date of its promulgation.

In order that every person may know of it, the promulgation of this Law is ordered by its placement in the State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia.

LAW OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA
NUMBER 17 OF 2019
CONCERNING
WATER RESOURCES

I. GENERAL

Water is a basic necessity for human life bestowed by God Almighty upon the entire Indonesian nation. Water as a part of Water Resources is a vital branch of production that affects the lives of many people and is controlled by the state to be used for the greatest prosperity of the people in accordance with the mandate of Article 33 paragraph (2) and paragraph (3) of the 1945 Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia. In that article, it is stated that the earth, water, and the natural resources contained therein are controlled by the state and are used for the greatest prosperity of the people. Therefore, the formulation of the Law concerning Water Resources must be aimed at optimizing the Management of Water Resources to achieve the mandate of the 1945 Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia.

Water is a very important necessity for life. With the imbalance between the availability of Water, which tends to decrease, and the demand for Water, which is increasing, water resources must be managed by considering social, environmental, and economic functions in a harmonious manner to create synergy and integration between regions, sectors, and generations to fulfill the people's need for Water.

Since its enactment in 2004, Law Number 7 of 2004 concerning Water Resources has been subjected to judicial review at the Constitutional Court several times, namely Case Number 058-059-060-063/PUU-II/2004, Case Number 008/PUU-III/2005 dated July 19, 2005, and Case Number 85/PUU-XI/2013 dated February 18, 2015. Ultimately, the Decision of the Constitutional Court Number 85/PUU-IX/2013 revoked the validity of Law Number 7 of 2004 concerning Water Resources and reinstated Law Number 11 of 1974 concerning Irrigation, which in reality is no longer in accordance with the current conditions of Water Resources Management.

The re-enacted Law on Irrigation still has many shortcomings and is not yet able to comprehensively regulate the Management of Water Resources in accordance with the developments and legal needs of the community today, and thus it needs to be replaced.

The regulation concerning Water Resources is made so that the Management of Water Resources is organized based on the principles of public benefit, affordability, justice, balance, self-reliance, local wisdom, environmental insight, preservation, sustainability, integration and harmony, as well as transparency and accountability. The regulation of Water Resources aims to provide protection and guarantee the fulfillment of the people's right to Water; guarantee the sustainable availability of Water and Water Sources to provide benefits to the community in a just manner; guarantee the preservation of the function of Water and Water Sources to support sustainable development; guarantee the creation of legal certainty for the implementation of community participation in overseeing the utilization of Water Resources starting from Planning, implementation, and evaluation of utilization; guarantee the protection and empowerment of the community, including Indigenous Peoples, in the effort of Water Resources conservation and utilization; and control the Destructive Force of Water.

The principal materials regulated in this Law concerning Water Resources include state control and the people's right to Water; the authority and responsibility of the Central Government and Regional Governments in the Management of Water Resources; Management of Water Resources; permitting for the use of Water Resources; the water resources information system; empowerment and supervision; funding; rights and obligations; community participation; and coordination. In addition, provisions concerning investigation and criminal provisions for violations of the provisions in this Law are also regulated.

This Law expressly states that Water Resources are controlled by the state and used for the greatest prosperity of the people. For that, the state guarantees the people's right to Water to meet the minimum basic daily needs for a healthy and clean life with a sufficient quantity, good quality, safety, sustained continuity, and affordability. In addition, the state prioritizes the people's right to Water for (1) basic daily needs, (2) smallholder agriculture, (3) business needs to meet basic daily needs through the Drinking Water Supply System, (4) non-business activities for the public interest; and (5) other business needs for which a permit has been established.

The limited availability of Water Resources on one hand and the increasing demand for Water on the other hand give rise to competition among users of Water Resources, which has an impact on the strengthening of the economic value of Water. This condition has the potential to cause conflicts of interest between sectors, between regions, and between various parties related to Water Resources. For that, regulations are needed that can provide protection for the interests of the community to meet basic daily needs and irrigation for smallholder agriculture. Therefore, the provision of Water to meet basic daily needs and irrigation for smallholder agriculture in the existing irrigation system is the main priority above all other Water needs.

Based on state control over Water Resources, the Central Government and/or Regional Governments are given the duty and authority to regulate and manage Water Resources, including the duty to fulfill the minimum basic daily needs for Water for the community. In addition, this Law also provides authority for Water Resources Management to the village government, or what is known by another name, to assist the government in the Management of Water Resources and to encourage the initiative and participation of the village community in the Management of Water Resources in its territory.

A portion of the duties and authorities of the Central Government and/or Regional Governments in managing Water Resources covering one River Basin may be assigned to a Water Resources Manager, which may be in the form of a ministerial technical implementation unit/regional technical implementation unit or a state-owned enterprise/regionally-owned enterprise in the field of Water Resources Management.

The existence of Water as a source of community life, naturally, is dynamic and flows to a lower place without recognizing administrative boundaries. The existence of Water follows the hydrological cycle, which is closely related to weather conditions in a region, thus causing the availability of water to be uneven in every time and every region. This requires that the Management of Water Resources be carried out holistically from upstream to downstream on a River Basin basis.

Based on this, the regulation of the authority and responsibility for the Management of Water Resources by the Central Government, provincial governments, and regency/city governments is based on the existence of the River Basin. To achieve integrated management of Water Resources, a joint reference needs to be formulated for stakeholders in one river basin in the form of a Water Resources Management Master Plan with the principle of integration between Surface Water and Groundwater. The Water Resources Management Master Plan is formulated in a coordinated manner among relevant agencies.

The Water Resources Management Master Plan is then elaborated into the Water Resources Management Plan. The Water Resources Management Plan is the master plan for Water Resources Conservation, Water Resources Utilization, and Control of the Destructive Force of Water, which is formulated in a coordinated manner and is based on the River Basin. The plan becomes the basis for the formulation of the Water Resources Management program, which is further elaborated in the activity plan of each relevant agency.

In principle, the use of Water Resources to meet basic daily needs and for smallholder agriculture can be carried out without a permit for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs. However, in the case that the use of Water Resources to meet basic daily needs involves changing the natural condition of the Water Source or is intended for the needs of a group that requires Water in a large quantity, its use must be based on a permit for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs. The use of Water to meet the needs of irrigation for smallholder agriculture must also be based on a permit for the use of Water Resources for non-business needs if it is done by changing the natural condition of the Water Source or is used for smallholder agriculture outside the existing irrigation system.

All types and forms of use and development of Water Resources for business activities must be carried out based on a permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs. The amount of the Water quota specified in the permit is the maximum volume of Water that can be given to the permit holder, which is not absolute and does not constitute a permit to control the Water Source.

The use of Water Resources for business needs is organized by observing the principles of (a) not disturbing, not overriding, and not negating the people's right to Water; (b) state protection of the people's right to Water; (c) preservation of the living environment as a human right; (d) absolute state supervision and control over Water; (e) primary priority for the use of Water Resources for business activities is given to state-owned enterprises, regionally-owned enterprises, or village-owned enterprises; and (f) the granting of a Permit for the Use of Water Resources for business needs to the private sector may be carried out with specific and strict conditions after the principles as referred to in letters a through e are fulfilled and there is still water availability.

The use of Water Resources for business needs is aimed at increasing the benefits of Water Resources for the welfare of the people by prioritizing the public interest and still paying attention to the social function of Water Resources and the preservation of the living environment. The use of Water Resources for business activities at a certain place can be granted to a state-owned enterprise or a regionally-owned enterprise, a private business entity, and/or an individual based on the plan for the implementation of the business activity that has been formulated through public consultation and a permit for the use of Water Resources for business needs from the government.

The use of Water Resources for such business activities can be in the form of using Water Resources for business activities that require raw Water as a production raw material, as one of the media or main elements of a business activity, such as regional drinking water companies, bottled drinking water companies, hydroelectric power plants, rafting sports, and as an auxiliary material in the production process, such as Water for a machine cooling system or Water for washing mining exploration products. The use of Water Resources for business activities does not include the control of the Water Source, but is limited to the use of Water in accordance with the established Water quota and the use of a part of the Water Source for the needs of the required infrastructure facilities, for example, the use of Water Resources for the business activity of building infrastructure facilities at the Water Source.

For the sustainable implementation of Water Resources Management, the beneficiaries of Water Resources Management services, in principle, are obliged to bear the management costs in accordance with the benefits received. This obligation does not apply to users of Water for basic daily needs, smallholder agriculture, and activities other than to meet basic daily needs and smallholder agriculture that are not business activities.

The Management of Water Resources involves the interests of many parties who are often not aligned and create potential conflicts. To address this, coordination is needed to integrate the interests of inter-sectoral and inter-regional parties and to formulate Water Resources Management activities in a synergistic manner.

Coordination at the national, provincial, and regency/city levels is necessary in the formulation of Water Resources Management policies. At the River Basin level, coordination needs to be carried out related to operational activities that involve various interests. Coordination at the River Basin level needs to be accommodated in a permanent institution in the form of a coordination forum at the River Basin level.

To ensure the certainty and enforcement of the law in matters related to the Management of Water Resources, in addition to investigators from the National Police of the Republic of Indonesia, it is necessary to have civil servant investigators who are given the authority for investigation.

Article 2
Letter a
What is meant by the "public benefit" principle is that the Management of Water Resources is implemented to provide the greatest possible benefit for the public interest.

What is meant by the "affordability" principle is that in the Management of Water Resources, the availability of Water must be accessible to every individual, both in terms of location and economy.
Letter c

Letter e

Letter f

Letter g
What is meant by "environmental insight" is that the Management of Water Resources pays attention to the balance of the ecosystem and the carrying capacity of the environment.

Letter i

The recognition of the existence of the Ulayat Right of Indigenous Peoples, including rights similar to it, is understood that what is meant by Indigenous Peoples is a group of people who are bound by their customary law order as joint citizens of a customary law community based on the similarity of residence or based on descent. The Ulayat Right of Indigenous Peoples is considered to still exist if it meets three elements, namely:
a. the element of Indigenous Peoples, namely the existence of a group of people who still feel bound by their customary law order as joint citizens of a certain customary law community, who recognize and apply the provisions of the community in their daily lives;
b. the element of territory, namely the existence of certain ulayat land which is the living environment of the members of the customary law community and the place to take their daily needs; and
c. the element of the relationship between the community and its territory, namely the existence of a customary law order regarding the management, control, and use of its ulayat land which is still valid and adhered to by the members of the customary law community.

b. controlling the utilization of Water Sources;
c. refilling Water into Water Sources;
d. regulating sanitation infrastructure and facilities;
e. protecting Water Sources in relation to development activities and land use at the Water Source;
f. controlling land cultivation in upstream areas;
g. regulating riparian zones of Water Sources;
h. rehabilitating forests and land; and/or
i. preserving protected forests, nature sanctuaries, and nature conservation areas.
The protection and preservation of Groundwater Sources are carried out through activities:
a. maintaining the carrying capacity and function of Groundwater recharge areas;
b. maintaining the carrying capacity of aquifers; and/or
c. restoring the condition and environment of Groundwater in critical zones and damaged zones.

What is meant by "water preservation" is the effort made to:
a. store excess Water during the rainy season so that it can be utilized when needed;

Article 45
Letter a
Number 1)
What is meant by "changing the natural condition of a Water Source" is to raise, lower, and divert a Water Source.
To raise is an act that can cause the Water at a Water Source to become higher, for example, building a weir or a dam.
Included in the definition of raising is pumping Water from a Water Source.
To lower is an act that can cause the Water at a Water Source to become lower or drop from its proper level, for example, digging or dredging a river.
To divert is an act that can cause the flow of Water and the channel of a Water Source to turn from its actual course.
The use of Groundwater that changes the natural condition by using human power from a dug well is not included in what requires a permit for the use of Water Resources.
Number 2)
What is meant by "Water in a large quantity" for Surface Water is a quota of Surface Water whose amount exceeds the basic daily needs for 150 (one hundred and fifty) people from one point of withdrawal or more than 60 (sixty) liters per person per day.
What is meant by "water in a large quantity" for Groundwater is if the Groundwater is taken from a drilled well with a diameter of more than 2 (two) inches or more than 5 (five) centimeters or more than 25 (twenty-five) cubic meters per month per head of family.
