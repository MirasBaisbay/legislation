LIBERTAS REPUBLIC OF SAN MARINO REGULATION No. 1 of January 22, 2019 We, the Captains Regent of the Most Serene Republic of San Marino Having regard to paragraphs 5, letter b) and 5-bis of Article 5 of Delegated Decree No. 44 of April 27, 2012, introduced by Article 4 of Delegated Decree No. 16 of January 31, 2017; Having regard to Decree-Law No. 181 of December 28, 2018, by which the term referred to in paragraph 5-bis of Article 5 of Delegated Decree No. 44 of April 27, 2012, introduced by Article 4 of Delegated Decree No. 16/2017, was extended; Having regard to the resolution of the Congress of State No. 17, adopted in the session of January 8, 2019; Having regard to Article 5, paragraph 5, of Constitutional Law No. 185/2005 and Article 13 of Qualified Law No. 186/2005; We promulgate and order the publication of the following regulation: REGULATION FOR THE MANAGEMENT OF THE INTEGRATED WATER SERVICE TITLE I COMMON PROVISIONS FOR THE INTEGRATED WATER SERVICE CHAPTER I GENERAL PROVISIONS 1. Art. 1 (Subject of the Regulation) This Regulation for the Management of the Integrated Water Service (hereinafter "Regulation") governs the Integrated Water Service based on the principles and provisions contained in Delegated Decree No. 44 of April 27, 2012, "Environmental Code," and subsequent amendments (hereinafter "Environmental Code"). 2. The integrated water service is the set of public services for the collection, conveyance, and distribution of water for civil use, sewerage, and wastewater treatment, and it is managed according to principles of efficiency, effectiveness, and economy, in compliance with current laws and community standards. 3. Pursuant to the Environmental Code, the "Manager of the Integrated Water Service" is the Autonomous State Agency for Services (hereinafter "Manager"). 4. The conditions for the supply of the integrated water service and the relationship between the Manager and the user are established in this Regulation, in the supply contract, and in the laws and provisions issued by the competent public bodies concerning public services. 5. This Regulation contains the provisions for the use of the aqueduct and sewerage networks and plants intended for the provision of the integrated water service. 6. This Regulation does not apply to the supply of water not originating from the aqueduct network. 7. Special provisions established by the competent health authorities shall remain unaffected. 1. Art. 2 (Correct and Rational Use of Water) Water is a resource to be protected and used according to principles of solidarity; any use shall be carried out safeguarding the expectations and rights of future generations to enjoy an intact environmental heritage. 2. The use of water is considered correct and rational when it aims to avoid waste and promote the renewal of resources, so as not to jeopardize the water heritage, the livability of the environment, agriculture, aquatic fauna and flora, geomorphological processes, and hydrological balances. 3. Uses other than for human consumption are permitted to the extent that water resources are sufficient and on the condition that they do not impair its quality. 4. For this purpose, the Manager undertakes to carry out with the utmost diligence the activities related to the management of water resources connected with the supply of drinking water, the collection and treatment of wastewater, and stormwater. 5. The Manager organizes and carries out the activities of water collection and potabilization from the environment, taking into account the quantity and quality of available resources and the intended use of the water (civil, industrial, and irrigation). 6. For the distribution of the resource, the Manager employs the most appropriate technologies and carries out all activities deemed useful for saving the water resource. To this end, it conducts scheduled leak detection activities, optimizes network pressure, rehabilitates obsolete networks, and promotes the installation of systems that reduce water waste. 7. In the context of collecting and treating wastewater and stormwater, the Manager is committed to performing the services by applying the best techniques in full compliance with current legislation and by promoting research and development of new technologies. 8. The user undertakes to use water to meet their needs by adopting techniques and behaviors useful for reducing the waste of the resource and for reusing it, where possible, in accordance with the provisions of this Regulation. 9. Likewise, the user undertakes not to discharge prohibited substances and waste into the sewerage network that could compromise the proper functioning of the sewerage networks and treatment plants. Art. 3 (Tasks and Powers of the Manager of the Integrated Water Service) 1. The Manager of the Integrated Water Service carries out the activities and services indicated in Article 5, paragraph 4, of Delegated Decree No. 44/2012. 2. The Manager may also perform any other activity, operation, and service pertaining or related to the management of the services referred to in paragraph 1, including the study, design, construction, and management of specific plants, either directly or indirectly, according to the guidelines contained in the Water Management and Protection Plan. Art. 4 (Definitions) 1. For the purposes of this Regulation, the following definitions shall apply: a) aqueduct: the entire complex of pipes, plants, and installations intended for the procurement, potabilization treatment, transport, and distribution of water up to the point of delivery to users; b) distribution network: the set of pipes and their respective accessories laid in public or private areas; c) water connection or service line: a water pipe derived from the distribution network, dedicated to supplying one or more users; it begins at the point of connection to the distribution main (tapping point) and ends, including the user meter, with one or more outlets housed within a special enclosure; d) tapping point: the point of connection of the service line to the main water pipe; e) user meter: an instrument for measuring the quantity of water supplied; f) point of delivery: the outlet section of the user meter; g) internal water system: the water distribution system located within the user's property, downstream of the point of delivery; h) centralized water system: a water system in which the water supplied to a condominium is measured by a single meter, and a single water supply contract is stipulated with the Manager of the Water Service; i) hydraulic backflow preventer: a safety device designed to prevent backflow from the user's internal system into the aqueduct network; l) use category: the category to which a supply contract is assigned in relation to the main use of the water and to which a specific tariff is applied; m) hidden leak: a dispersion that occurs in a non-inspectable section of the internal system; n) contract holder: the natural or legal person in whose name the water supply and/or sewerage service contract is registered; o) user: a natural or legal person who uses the services provided by the Manager; p) tariff schedule: the list of charges and prices for specific services, both ordinary and ancillary, as well as indemnities and penalties related to the services provided by the Manager; q) wastewater comparable to domestic wastewater: discharges of wastewater from the activities referred to in Article 70, paragraph 6, of the Environmental Code; r) first flush rainwater: the depth in millimeters of stormwater runoff from uncovered impermeable surfaces contaminated by hazardous substances or substances that may jeopardize the achievement of quality objectives for water bodies. Only water resulting from meteorological events where the time between the end of one atmospheric event and the beginning of the next is greater than 72 hours can be classified as first flush rainwater; s) second flush rainwater: stormwater runoff from uncovered impermeable surfaces that cannot be classified as first flush rainwater; t) stormwater sewerage networks: intended for the collection of stormwater runoff from roofs, yards, or drainage systems, as referred to in points g) and z) of Article 62 of the Environmental Code, if not polluted by human activity, provided they meet the requirements for direct discharge into surface water bodies in compliance with legal regulations, without treatment; u) combined sewerage networks: pipe systems that collect and convey both stormwater and urban wastewater together; v) provisional discharge: a discharge made by means of a temporary connection to the sewerage network (e.g., construction sites, kiosks, and sanitary facilities for festivals or events, connections for the remediation of contaminated sites); z) sewer connection: the connection between the public sewerage network and the connection manhole to which the user's or multiple users' private network is connected; aa) connection manhole: the arrival point of the internal network and the starting point of the pipes connecting to the sewer. It constitutes the boundary of the responsibilities and liabilities of the Discharge Holder and the Manager and is located on private property; bb) point of delivery: constitutes the point where the user's wastewater is delivered to the Manager; it is identified as the outlet point of the connection manhole; cc) internal network: systems and networks owned by and under the responsibility of the user up to the delivery manhole, including wastewater or stormwater discharge and collection pipes, lifting stations, pre-treatment plants, sectioning and inspection systems, flow measurement, and sampling equipment; dd) discharge permit: the consent, given by the competent Authority, for the discharge of wastewater into the Manager's sewer system or other receiving bodies. 2. For the purposes of this Regulation, the definitions contained in the Environmental Code and its subsequent amendments are reported below: a) stormwater runoff: rainwater that, having fallen to the ground, washes over surfaces and indirectly reaches receiving bodies; b) stormwater: stormwater runoff or groundwater, provided it has not been contaminated by production or service activities; c) urban wastewater: domestic wastewater or a mixture of domestic wastewater, industrial wastewater, or stormwater runoff conveyed in sewerage networks, including separate ones, and originating from an agglomeration; d) industrial wastewater: any type of wastewater discharged from buildings or plants where commercial or goods production activities take place, other than domestic wastewater and stormwater runoff; e) domestic wastewater: wastewater from residential and service settlements, primarily deriving from human metabolism and domestic activities; f) agglomeration: the area in which the population, or productive activities, are concentrated to such an extent that the collection and conveyance of urban wastewater to a treatment system or a final discharge point is admissible, both technically and economically, also in relation to the achievable environmental benefits; g) sewerage network: the system of channels, generally underground, for the collection and conveyance of domestic, industrial, and urban wastewater to the final receiving body; h) separate sewer system: a sewerage network consisting of two channels, the first called the "stormwater" sewer, used for the collection and conveyance of only stormwater, and which may or may not be equipped with devices for the collection and separation of first flush rainwater, and the second called the "foul" sewer, used for the collection and conveyance of urban wastewater together with any first flush rainwater; i) discharge: any discharge made exclusively through a stable collection system that connects the wastewater production cycle to the receiving body, such as surface water, soil, subsoil, and sewerage networks, without interruption, regardless of its polluting nature, including wastewater that has undergone prior purification treatment; wastewater: all wastewater coming from a discharge; l) m) existing discharges: discharges of urban wastewater, domestic wastewater, and industrial wastewater that are in operation, compliant with the pre-existing authorization regime, and authorized at the date of entry into force of the Environmental Code; n) appropriate treatment: the treatment of urban wastewater by means of a process or disposal system that, after discharge, ensures the compliance of the receiving water bodies with their respective quality objectives or complies with the provisions of Chapter VI, Title III; o) primary treatment: the treatment of wastewater that involves the sedimentation of suspended solids through physical and/or physico-chemical processes and/or other processes, as a result of which, before discharge, the BOD5 of the treated water is reduced by at least 20 percent and the total suspended solids by at least 50 percent; p) secondary treatment: the treatment of wastewater by means of a process that generally involves biological treatment with secondary sedimentation, or by another process that in any case meets the requirements of Table 1/J of Annex J of the Environmental Code; q) population equivalent (P.E.): the biodegradable organic load having a biochemical oxygen demand at 5 days (BOD5) of 60 grams of oxygen per day. 1. Art. 5 (Scope and Effectiveness of the Regulation) This Regulation is valid for the Integrated Water Service entrusted to the Manager. 2. This Regulation is mandatory for all users in relation to the Integrated Water Service (aqueduct, sewerage, and treatment) provided to them. 3. This Regulation shall be considered an integral part of every water supply and sewerage service contract, without the need for its physical transcription, without prejudice to the user's right to receive a free copy upon signing the contract, upon its renewal, or whenever requested. 4. The adoption of this Regulation and any subsequent amendments and/or integrations are made known by notice published on the Manager's website. CHAPTER II PROVISIONS ON CONTRACTS Art. 6 (General Rules on Contracts) 1. Any natural or legal person who intends to use the services included in the integrated water service pursuant to this Regulation is required to sign a contract with the Manager for the requested services. 2. The contract is drawn up on a form conforming to Annex A of this Regulation. 3. The contract is stipulated with the applicant. If the applicant is a legal person, the contract is stipulated with the legal representative. The contract is concluded on the date the meter is activated, as indicated on the first invoice. 4. The contract holder has the right to indicate, at the time of signing or subsequently, for the sole purpose of receiving correspondence, a different person as the recipient of invoices and any communications, without this creating any contractual obligation towards the latter. 1. Art. 7 (Contents of the Contract) The contract may concern: a) only the supply of water; b) only the sewerage service; c) the supply of water and the sewerage service. 2. The contract must indicate its duration, the tariff in force, payment terms and methods, a declaration by the holder of acceptance of the Regulation, and the Manager's right to vary the Regulation, and shall provide that during the course of the water supply or sewerage service contract, the tariffs may vary based on the resolutions of the Authority for Services and Energy pursuant to Article 13. 3. The water supply contract must indicate the type of utility and the type of supply, while the sewerage service contract must indicate the type of discharge. 4. The water supply provided by the Manager may not be used in premises and for purposes other than those specified in the contract, nor transferred in any form to third parties. 5. The user is obliged to notify the Manager of any change that results in a variation of the original contractual conditions. 6. The contract refers to a single type of water use, as multiple contracts for the same property unit are not permitted, except for fire-fighting use. 7. All stamp duty and registration expenses related to the contract are borne by the user. 1. Art. 8 (Security Deposit) Upon signing the supply contract, the Manager shall require the user to pay a security deposit to guarantee the supply. 2. The security deposit, the amount of which is determined by the Regulatory Authority for Public Services and Energy pursuant to Article 13, shall be charged to the user on the first invoice issued. 3. In the event of the user's insolvency, the Manager reserves the right to retain the amount of the security deposit up to the value of its credits, without prejudice to any other actions arising from non-compliance with this Regulation and the law. 4. In the event of termination of the contract, the amount of the security deposit shall be refunded and credited on the last available invoice, after deducting any amounts due by the user that remain unpaid. 1. Art. 9 (Duration of Contracts) Supply contracts are normally for an indefinite term, without prejudice to the possibility of withdrawal as provided for in the following Article 10. 2. For sewer connections of industrial utilities referred to in Annex K of the Environmental Code, the contract has a duration of four years from the date the permit is issued and is renewed in the event of permit renewal for the duration of the same. 3. For supplies for temporary use, the provisions of Article 30 apply. 1. Art. 10 (Methods of Withdrawal) The contract holder may give notice of cancellation by sending a communication by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt, or by signing a form conforming to Annex A of this Regulation. 2. The Manager will take the final reading and shut off the meter. The contract holder will pay for the consumption recorded up to the final reading as well as the fixed fees and any other charges due up to the cancellation, or until the date of the regular sub-entry of another contract holder. 3. In the absence of a notice of cancellation, the contract holder remains solely responsible towards the Manager and third parties and is liable for all civil and criminal implications. 4. The contract holder who does not comply with the above specifications remains directly responsible for the payment of the water that may be consumed by any successors who have not regularized their contractual relationship, as well as for any other expenses and damages connected with and resulting from the use of the systems. Art. 11 (Sub-entry) 1. Sub-entry into the contract is permitted only following express acceptance by the Manager. 2. Until the sub-entry of the new holder into the contract is formalized, the previous holder is responsible for all contractual obligations. 3. Requests for sub-entry into contracts where there are outstanding debts will be accepted only after the settlement of the previous debt situation. 4. The sub-entry takes effect from the date of the final reading of the user meter by the Manager or from the date of a joint reading by the contract holder and the sub-entrant. 5. In the event of the death of the contract holder, the heirs or successors are required to inform the Manager within six months of the death and to arrange for the sub-entry into the contract. 6. After the term referred to in paragraph 5 has elapsed, the provisions of Article 12 shall apply. 1. Art. 12 (Termination of the Contract) The contract is terminated by right in the event of: - failure to remove the causes of suspension referred to in Article 35; - non-compliance with the prohibitions referred to in Article 29. 2. The connection contract for industrial discharges referred to in Annex K of the Environmental Code is terminated by right in the event of revocation of the discharge permit by the competent Authority. 3. The termination of the water supply contract entails the shut-off and possible removal of the user meter, with any works for total and definitive disconnection, for the part in a public area, to be carried out by the Manager. 4. The termination of the sewerage contract entails the deactivation of the connection to the public sewer, with possible demolition, for the part in a public area, to be carried out by the Manager. CHAPTER III TARIFFS AND PAYMENT METHODS 1. Art. 13 (Tariffs for the Integrated Water Service) The tariffs for the water supply and sewerage services provided by the Manager are determined by the Regulatory Authority for Public Services and Energy pursuant to Article 7 of Law No. 120 of November 20, 2001, and Delegated Decree No. 99 of July 3, 2008, and are applied by the Manager. 2. The tariffs for the services provided by the Manager listed below are determined by the Regulatory Authority for Public Services and Energy: a) connection; b) fire-fighting connection; c) temporary supplies; d) installation of the user meter; e) rental of the user meter for water supply; f) security deposit. 3. For the water supply, the tariff is established based on the type of utility, consumption brackets, and any water emergency situations, while for the sewerage service, the tariff is divided into civil and industrial utilities. 4. The user is informed of the tariff in force at the time of signing the contract. 5. During the course of the supply contract, the tariffs may vary and will be communicated through publication on the Manager's website. 6. The tariffs and charges due are paid to the Manager by the deadlines established in this Regulation. 1. Art. 14 (Contributions and Quotations) Requests for modification or relocation of the networks, equipment, plants, and structures of the Integrated Water Service under the responsibility of the Manager may also be submitted by private users. The related works are carried out by the Manager at the applicant's expense, subject to verification of technical compatibility, acceptance of the quotation, and payment according to the methods set out in Article 19, without prejudice to the payment of adjustments in case of higher costs. 2. The Manager shall establish the fees for the services indicated below: a) construction of new networks or upgrading of existing networks; b) modifications to the Manager's networks, equipment, plants, and structures; c) modifications to existing connections; d) relocation of the user meter; e) reactivation of the user meter in case of default. 3. The contribution that the applicant is required to pay to the Manager, in the cases referred to in paragraph 2, includes: a) the costs incurred by the Manager for the technical investigation, including surveys, verifications, design, and preparation of the quotation; b) the costs of executing the works carried out by the Manager. 4. Any quotation is valid for twelve months from the date of its communication to the applicant. The applicant, even if the works are not executed, is required to pay the costs related to the processing of the file, design, and preparation of the quotation. 5. The payment of contributions does not grant the user any title of ownership over the works carried out by the Manager. 6. The services referred to in letters a) and b) of paragraph 2 are carried out by the Manager compatibly with the budget provisions and the works planning of the Agency itself. 1. Art. 15 (Environmental Tariff and Collection Methods) The environmental tariff is governed by Article 95 of the Environmental Code and is established by the Regulatory Authority for Public Services and Energy. The tariff is payable to the Manager by all users of the water service. 2. For the purpose of calculating the environmental tariff, the volume of wastewater discharged into the sewer is determined according to the following criteria: a) for discharges of domestic, comparable, and industrial wastewater from utilities connected to the aqueduct, the quantity of discharged wastewater is determined to be equal to the volume of water consumed; b) for discharges from utilities that use water sources other than the public aqueduct, the tariff is applied to the volume of discharged wastewater determined by the flow meter referred to in Article 66. 3. The environmental tariff is charged on the same bill used for invoicing the aqueduct service, without prejudice to the possibility of a different application. 4. In case of irregular or non-functioning of the user meter or flow meter, the calculation of the environmental tariff is based on the average consumption of the previous two years; in the absence of historical consumption data, reference will be made to the consumption recorded on the new meter installed. 1. Art. 16 (Meter Reading) The meter is normally read twice a year; additional readings may be taken for technical or administrative reasons. The user is required to permit and facilitate, at any time, access to the meter for readings by the Manager's staff or other persons appointed by the Manager. In case of absence, the user is also required to arrange, in the manner indicated by the Manager, to communicate the meter reading or to allow it to be read by their representative. 2. The Manager gives the user the option to perform a self-reading and to communicate it by filling out the form in Annex B to this Regulation or through the tools specifically made available, with transmission costs borne by the recipient. Failure to communicate the self-reading to the Manager's designated offices will result in the charging of an estimated consumption. 3. The Manager is not required to perform a simultaneous reading of the meters in the event of changes in tariffs or taxes levied on the tariffs themselves. 4. In the case of meters showing a measurement multiplier coefficient on the totalizer, the indicated quantities will be increased according to the multiplier itself. 5. For consumption-based supplies for other uses referred to in Article 28, paragraph 3, letter b), the meter is read at the end of the usage period and in any case at least once a year. 6. The Manager is obliged to inform the user in case of detection of anomalous readings and to be available for additional verifications. 1. Art. 17 (Consumption) Water consumption is measured by a meter and is expressed in cubic meters. In the absence of a reading, the Manager may estimate consumption based on the values of the previous year. 2. In case of anomalies or failures of the meter, the Manager will determine the water consumption for the period of the device's incorrect functioning as follows: a) based on the average consumption of the previous two years; b) in the absence of historical consumption data, reference will be made to that recorded on the new meter installed. 3. In proven cases of meter tampering by the user and in the absence of reference data from previous consumption, the consumption will be determined by the Manager based on technical assessments. 1. Art. 18 (Billing Methods for the Integrated Water Service) The Manager shall issue invoices for the services offered to the user based on the contract, applying the tariffs referred to in Article 13. 2. Domestic utilities that are not connected to the public sewer system and that provide for their own wastewater treatment pursuant to Article 69 are not required to pay the environmental tariff, subject to notification to the Manager by filling out the appropriate form in Annex C to this Regulation. 3. The billing frequency is normally quarterly and in any case carried out four times a year. The Manager reserves the right to change the billing frequency, providing adequate written notice to the users. 4. For the services referred to in Article 14, the invoice is normally issued upon acceptance of the quotation. 5. Supplies are normally billed with a charge for estimated consumption and a subsequent adjustment. If a reading is not provided, the Manager will bill an estimated consumption, subject to adjustment at the next reading. 6. For supplies with consumption exceeding 200 mÂ³ per month, monthly billing is applied with a charge for actual consumption, with the reading carried out by the Manager through its representative, in the event of a declaration of a water emergency as per Article 37 or in cases established by the regulatory authority for services or by special regulations. 7. Concurrently with the amounts for consumption, other due amounts are charged, such as: a) fixed fees; b) tariffs for sewerage and treatment services; c) annual fee based on the number and diameter of hydrants installed for fire-fighting supplies; d) fees for ancillary services provided by the Manager; e) expense reimbursements; f) security deposit; g) stamp duty; h) rounding of invoiced amounts; i) contribution for hidden leak insurance; l) late payment penalty for overdue invoices. 8. In the case of a centralized water system, the Manager does not apportion the consumption; the invoice, however, takes into account the number of property units. 9. The Manager may vary the billing items and the billing system by providing prior notice to the users. 1. Art. 19 (Payments) Invoices are sent to the address indicated by the contract holder or to another person indicated by them. 2. Payment is made by the due date indicated on the invoice using the pre-printed form attached to it, according to the instructions on the document, or by other provided methods such as direct debit. 3. Payments for the services referred to in Article 30, paragraph 3, are made in advance. 4. For the services referred to in Article 14, upon acceptance of the quotation for the execution of the works, the applicant is required to pay the entire amount quoted by the Manager before the service is provided, without prejudice to the payment of adjustments in case of higher costs. 5. In the case of bills with a credit amount, they are refunded on subsequent invoices. The Manager may carry out accounting set-offs between debts and credits of the same holder referring to different contracts. 6. Art. 20 (Late Payment and Formal Notice) In case of late payment, thirty days after the due date of the invoice, the user is put on formal notice by means of a registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt containing an indication of the final payment deadline and a notice that, after said deadline, the Manager may proceed to suspend the service, without prejudice to the provisions of paragraph 7 of Article 35 and the Manager's right to proceed according to the procedure governed by Delegated Decree No. 131 of August 7, 2014. 1. If payment is delayed beyond the due dates indicated on the invoice, without prejudice to the right to suspend the supply and terminate the contract, the Manager will apply a late payment penalty, calculated on the amount of the sums due, as follows: a) up to 10 days, a late payment penalty of 1%; b) from 10 to 30 days, a late payment penalty of 3%; c) over 30 days, a late payment penalty of 5%. 1. Art. 21 (Information and Complaints) Information on consumption, payments, and supply amounts is provided only to the contract holders. If the contract holder is a condominium, the information may also be given, upon written request, to the actual members of the condominium. 2. Any complaint that the user wishes to make regarding the invoices must be addressed to the Manager by the due date of the invoice. The Manager will respond to written complaints within fifteen days from the date of receipt. 3. Any pending complaints or disputes do not entitle the user to defer or suspend payments. 4. The payment of an invoice does not release the user from the charge for any previous consumption or services not invoiced or from the payment of charges erroneously not imputed. CHAPTER IV PROVISIONS ON THE CONSTRUCTION OF NETWORKS AND CONNECTIONS Art. 22 (Construction of Networks) 1. The networks and connections, even if on private land and/or with contributions from private individuals, are the property of the Manager, and the subsequent operation and maintenance activities are the responsibility of the same Manager. 2. Water pipes are normally laid along road routes to allow for maintenance and to ensure greater stability of the laid networks. 3. The Manager shall construct the main and secondary sewer collectors and related connections, taking into account the provisions of Article 69 of the Environmental Code, also making use of private companies. 4. The laying of networks on private land takes place following the establishment of an underground occupation easement; in case of refusal, the Manager shall initiate the procedures for the granting of a public predial easement pursuant to Chapter II, Section VII of Law No. 140/2017. 1. Art. 23 (Ordinary and Extraordinary Maintenance, Modifications, and Relocation of the Manager's Networks, Equipment, Plants, and Structures) The ordinary and extraordinary maintenance of the Manager's networks, equipment, plants, and structures is carried out directly by the Manager or by entities appointed by it. 2. Should the Manager identify needs for the modernization, modification, or relocation of networks, equipment, plants, and structures, or for their adaptation to current regulations, it may proceed, at its own care and expense, with the said adaptation, with the obligation to give prior notice to the affected users. 3. Modifications or relocations of utility networks and connections due to public works are carried out by the Manager, subject to verification of technical compatibility. 4. The works referred to in this article are carried out by the Manager compatibly with the budget provisions and the works planning of the Agency itself. 1. Art. 24 (Distribution Networks on Private Property) For the purpose of extending and upgrading distribution networks on private areas, the applicant must sign a specific agreement with the Manager to define the methods and timing of the works, as well as the financial consideration for the service, defined pursuant to Article 14. The execution of the aforesaid works is subject to the establishment of an easement for the laying and maintenance of the networks. 1. Art. 25 (Execution of Connections) The Manager shall carry out the connection works and the installation of the user meter directly or through entities appointed by it. 2. The Manager reserves the right to connect other users even to lines built with private contributions, without this giving rise to claims for reimbursement or compensation. 3. Multiple connections may be made for the same building if this is necessary for technical or safety reasons assessed by the Manager. 4. In "row house" type settlements or in those where the buildings are located at a distance of less than ten meters for the above-ground part, no more than one connection shall be made for every two property units. 5. The execution of a connection on private property is subject to obtaining the easements or authorizations necessary for the maintenance of the networks. 6. In the case of connections for condominium utilities, the Manager may execute a single connection from the street main and construct the collector for the various branches of the individual utilities within the private property. 7. For connections of condominium utilities, all user meters are grouped together and located in spaces under common ownership of all the served utilities, in the position already defined during the design phase. 8. The Manager has the right to insert a flow limiter in the connection, commensurate with the maximum performance of the user meter. 1. Art. 26 (Interference of Works with Networks and Issuance of Opinion) In the case of projects involving the execution of works on public or private property that may interfere with the Manager's networks, or where it is deemed necessary to verify the presence of networks, the applicant shall submit a request for an inspection to the Manager by filling out the appropriate form in Annex D to this Regulation. 2. The following documentation shall be attached to the request referred to in paragraph 1: a) a plan of the affected area; b) a graphic drawing of the planned works. 3. The Manager, having evaluated the request and the related documentation, shall issue an opinion and any requirements, reserving the right to carry out inspections during the course of the works. A copy of the notice of commencement of work provided for under Article 68 of Law No. 140/2017 shall also be sent to the Manager. 4. Should the works involve relocations or modifications of the Manager's networks, they will be carried out by applying the provisions of Article 14. 5. Should damage occur to public utility networks during the execution of works on public or private property, the Manager will carry out the necessary restoration works, charging the expenses to the executor of the works. TITLE II PROVISIONS ON THE AQUEDUCT SERVICE WATER SUPPLY CHAPTER I GENERAL PROVISIONS 1. Art. 27 (Subject of the Aqueduct Service) This Regulation governs the methods of providing the aqueduct service and the relationship between the Manager and the user of said service. The Manager provides the aqueduct service to applicants within the limits of the extent and capacity of the managed networks and plants. The Manager guarantees that the supplied water has chemical-physical and hygienic-sanitary characteristics such that it is classified as suitable for human consumption (potable water) in compliance with current regulations. 2. The aqueduct service is provided continuously in the manner prescribed by current regulations. Supply interruptions are related only to ongoing works and maintenance on the production, conveyance, and distribution network and plants, or to force majeure events. Programmed interruptions are communicated to the users, including through a notice published on the Manager's website. 1. Art. 28 (Types of Supply) Water supplies are divided into supplies for public use and supplies for private use. 2. Supplies for public use, intended to meet common water needs and requirements related to the community, are those connected to the following facilities: a) public fountains; b) supplies to state buildings and facilities intended for public services (schools, educational institutions, museums, and public sports facilities); c) offices and headquarters of public activities (depots, warehouses, etc.); d) irrigation systems for public green spaces; e) public service fire-fighting systems; f) supply points for the activities referred to in Article 28, paragraph 3. 3. The following are considered supplies for private use: a) domestic uses: these include consumption for human consumption and other uses within property units with a residential function; b) supplies for other uses: these include water supplies in property units intended for commercial, artisanal, industrial, hotel, tertiary, agricultural, livestock use, and private fire-fighting systems. This category also includes cases of mixed-use supply (domestic use and other use). 1. The supply of water, for both public and private use, is carried out by the Manager upon express request of the interested party and is normally provided on a free-flow basis, measured by a suitable device (user meter). 1. Art. 32 (Conditions for Water Supply and Right to Supply) The Manager supplies water for the uses provided for in the Regulation, in areas already served by the distribution network, in return for the contributions established for the activation and supply of water and following the stipulation of a specific contract according to the procedures provided for in this Regulation. 2. The aqueduct works are carried out by the Manager. In cases where supply requests in areas already served by aqueduct networks cannot be met as a result of changes in land use or urban transformation interventions, the upgrading works shall be at the total expense of the parties implementing the interventions. Upgrading works for networks and plants in areas already served and characterized by structural deficiencies shall be at the expense of the applicant. 3. In areas not served by a distribution network and for which the Manager's works program does not foresee extension interventions, the Manager shall carry out the works related to the provision of the service, which shall be charged to the applicant as indicated in the quotation referred to in Article 14. 4. TITLE III SEWERAGE SERVICE CHAPTER I GENERAL PROVISIONS 1. Art. 51 (Preliminary Provisions) This Regulation establishes the rules for the discharge of wastewater into the sewerage networks. Discharges of domestic or comparable wastewater are permitted in compliance with Article 70 and Article 78, paragraph 9 of the Environmental Code and this Regulation. 2. Discharges of industrial wastewater must be previously authorized by the Technical Board (CT) referred to in Article 4 of the Environmental Code, as established by Article 78 of the Environmental Code. 3. The sewerage networks consist of two separate channels, as defined in Article 62, paragraph 1, letter z) of the Environmental Code. 4. The collection of stormwater must be carried out separately from that of domestic, comparable to domestic, and industrial wastewater on private land, as established by Article 126 of the Consolidated Act. 1. Art. 52 (Obligation to Connect to the Sewerage Network) Discharges of domestic wastewater and wastewater comparable to domestic must be connected to the sewerage network. The AASS issues an opinion in the cases referred to in Article 57, paragraph 8, of the Consolidated Act. 2. Discharges of industrial wastewater must be authorized by the CT pursuant to Article 78 of the Environmental Code. As a rule, discharges of industrial wastewater are connected to the sewerage network. 3. The provisions of paragraph 1 do not apply to isolated buildings, i.e., those located at a distance from the sewer collector greater than 50+S/4 meters; where S is the plan area of the building in square meters. For isolated buildings, the technical requirements of Article 69 of this Regulation apply to permit discharge onto land. 4. In the cases referred to in paragraphs 1 and 2, the possibility of discharge onto land pursuant to Article 71, paragraph 1, letter c, of the Environmental Code is reserved. 5. Stormwater runoff may be discharged onto land if surface delivery is possible, and it must be prevented from flowing onto neighboring properties and/or the public roadway. 1. Art. 53 (Provisions for Existing Sewer Discharges) The Manager, pursuant to Article 79, paragraph 3-ter, of the Environmental Code, shall define the intervention methods for the connection of residential areas for which sewerage networks do not exist or are not correctly separated, by establishing Intervention Plans based on the Water Management and Protection Plan referred to in Article 94 of the Environmental Code and on budget availability. 2. In the case of existing discharges, within the times indicated in the Water Management and Protection Plan and within the term provided for by Article 79, paragraph 3-quater of the Environmental Code: a) if the public sewer system is existing and separate, the Manager shall appoint personnel from the administration and/or private structures to verify the correct connection to the public sewerage network and the separation of the sewerage networks within the private property; b) if the public sewer system is not existing or is not separate, the Manager shall complete the construction or upgrading works based on the Intervention Plans, requiring the adaptation of the connection; c) in the case of isolated buildings as referred to in Article 52, paragraph 3, the Manager shall require the adaptation of the connection according to the requirements of Article 69. 3. The costs of adaptation referred to in paragraph 2 are the responsibility of the network owner, as specified in Article 54. Art. 54 (Limits of Responsibility) 1. The sewerage network under the responsibility of the discharge holder begins at the collection points and ends at, and includes, the connection manhole to the Manager's network. 2. The sewerage network under the responsibility of the Manager begins at the discharge holder's connection manhole and includes all the channels and plants up to the treatment plants for discharge into the environment. Art. 55 (Execution of Connections) 1. The Manager shall carry out the connection works to the manhole directly or through authorized entities. 2. For discharges of domestic and comparable to domestic wastewater as referred to in Article 70, paragraph 6 of the Environmental Code, a single connection point to the Manager's sewerage network is provided for each building, located within the private property and on its boundary, pursuant to Article 126 of Law No. 140 of December 14, 2017 (Consolidated Act) if the sewer collector is outside the private property, while it will be located near the sewer collector if the latter is on the same private property. 3. Multiple connections may be made for the same building if this is necessary for technical or safety reasons assessed by the Manager, based on the diagram shown in type 1 of Annex F to this Regulation. 4. In "row house" type settlements or in those where above-ground buildings are located at a distance of less than ten meters from each other, no more than one connection shall be made for every two property units, based on the diagram shown in type 2 of Annex F to this Regulation. 5. For the purpose of allowing inspection and sampling, industrial discharges must have a separate connection point from that of discharges comparable to domestic, and a dedicated inspection and sampling manhole. The sampling manhole shall be installed on the discharge holder's land, near the property boundary, upstream of the point of discharge into the public sewer, in a position easily accessible to the authorized personnel. 6. The same requirements as in paragraphs 3 and 4 relating to wastewater apply to stormwater runoff. Art. 56 (Discharge Limits) 1. Art. 63 (First Flush Rainwater) For activities where the spillage of processed materials onto yards or access roads and/or the direct runoff of materials and equipment is foreseeable, it is mandatory to separate first flush rainwater from stormwater runoff. 2. The obligation referred to in the preceding paragraph concerns the following activities: a) petroleum industry: extraction, processing, deposit, storage, and sale, including fuel distributors; b) non-energy mineral industry: extraction, processing, deposit, storage, including salt production; c) metallurgical industry: extraction, processing, surface treatment, deposit, storage, and sale of metallic materials; d) food and tobacco industry; e) textile industries; f) tanning industries and dyeing activities; g) paper industry; h) chemical industry, including the production of synthetic and artificial fibers, the processing of rubber and plastic materials; i) l) manufacture of mechanical and electrical machinery and apparatus; m) manufacture, repair, deposit, scrapping, and demolition of means of transport; n) places for the collection, deposit, storage, and processing of waste. 3. First flush rainwater is collected and discharged to the Manager separately from stormwater. Second flush rainwater and stormwater are discharged into the Manager's stormwater network. 4. The CT, pursuant to Article 77 of the Environmental Code, shall issue requirements on the treatment of first flush rainwater and on the quantity of water that can be qualified as first flush. 5. The separation of first flush rainwater is carried out by installing special accumulation systems, rain control systems, lifting stations, or by systems that guarantee equivalent treatment for the purpose of environmental protection. 6. All works related to the accumulation and treatment systems for first flush rainwater are carried out and managed at the care and expense of the discharge holder, and the structures are positioned within private areas. 7. The design documents relating to first flush treatments are attached to the application for connection to the public sewerage network submitted to the Manager. 1. Art. 64 (Technical and Construction Requirements for the Execution of Connections) Connections to the sewerage network are executed in the positions indicated by the Manager based on orographic conditions, the technical characteristics of the network, and the needs for maintenance, repair, and control of the networks, as well as the consequent restoration works on public roads. 2. The networks, in the section affecting the roadway or public area, must follow the shortest path to reach the discharge points, or be perpendicular to the axis of the street pipe. At the point of discharge into the public sewer, the pipe must not occupy the useful section of the main conduit. 3. The connection channels to the public sewer within private properties must have characteristics of resistance to corrosion, crushing, abrasion, and temperatures up to 100 ÂºC, have absolute impermeability, and be installed so as to guarantee a correct passage of wastewater. 4. In addition to the requirements of Article 70, paragraph 2, the connection manhole to the Manager's sewerage network must be equipped with "Florence-type" traps with double inspection. A manhole is constructed for each type of water discharged (stormwater, domestic or comparable wastewater, industrial wastewater). 5. The position of the connection manhole and its accessibility conditions are indicated at the time of submitting the connection request. The delivery point is agreed upon with the discharge holder, based on the configuration of the public sewerage network, the need to respect existing utilities, and in compliance with the discharge limits of Article 56. 6. The construction and maintenance of the manhole are the responsibility of the discharge holder, who also remains responsible for its correct functionality. 7. The sizing of the pipe between the public sewerage network and the connection manhole is determined by the Manager based on the discharge flow rate requested in the application. 8. The lifting of wastewater or stormwater is permitted, with installation and management costs entirely borne by the applicant, with discharge upstream of the connection manhole, in compliance with the discharge limits of Article 56. In order to avoid pressurization of the discharge pipes, downstream of the lift station and before the discharge manhole, the use of a disconnection manhole maintained at atmospheric pressure, used as an open vessel, is mandatory. 9. A lift station equipped with a backflow prevention device shall be provided, at the care and expense of the applicant, if gravity discharge of the wastewater into the public sewer is not possible. 10. The elimination of the septic tank or Imhoff tank is possible for domestic or comparable to domestic discharges that flow into a separate sewer system. CHAPTER V RULES FOR INTERNAL SYSTEMS 1. The deactivation of treatment plants for maintenance work shall be communicated in advance to the Manager and the Department of Prevention, by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt, indicating the dates of suspension and reactivation of the plants, as well as any variation in the communicated times. 1. Art. 66 (Flow Meter) For the utilities referred to in paragraphs 5 and 6 of Article 95 of Delegated Decree No. 44/2012, a flow meter as referred to in Article 97, paragraph 2, shall be installed for the purpose of determining the volume of discharged wastewater. 2. The Manager, upon issuing the discharge permit, shall indicate the position in which to install the flow meter and shall formulate any requirements relating to its installation. 3. The meter is installed according to the manufacturer's technical specifications. 5. The Manager, having verified the conditions of paragraphs 2, 3, and 4, shall affix the seals to the meter. 6. CHAPTER VI RULES FOR THE TREATMENT OF DOMESTIC WASTEWATER FOR DISCHARGE ONTO LAND 1. Art. 69 (Treatment of Domestic Wastewater for Discharge onto Land) Pursuant to paragraph 3 of Article 69 of the Environmental Code, the discharge of domestic wastewater onto land is permitted based on the plant diagram in Annex P, adopting, according to the type of soil present, the following plant solutions: a) a system composed of an Imhoff tank and sub-irrigation networks for dispersion or sub-irrigation with drainage; b) a system composed of an Imhoff tank and a horizontal flow phytoremediation system or a vertical flow phytoremediation system. 2. The project for the treatment plant for discharge onto land as referred to in the preceding paragraph is drawn up based on the indications contained in this Regulation and submitted to the Building Office together with the building project or, in the case of an existing sewer discharge, according to the times set out in Article 79, paragraph 3-quater of Delegated Decree No. 44/2012. The Manager shall issue an opinion as prescribed by Article 126 of Law No. 140/2017. 1. Art. 70 (Imhoff Tank) The Imhoff tank is mandatory for the treatment of domestic wastewater discharged onto land. 2. The Imhoff tank precedes the sub-irrigation or phytoremediation network; for over 50 P.E., an oxidative phase with a BOD5 reduction of not less than 60% is included. 3. The Imhoff tank must have a minimum capacity of 250 liters per P.E., of which: - 50 liters for the sedimentation compartment; - 200 liters for the digestion compartment. 4. The Imhoff tank must be inspectable and, as far as possible, located outside the perimeter walls of the building, far (at least 10 meters) from wells, tanks, and water pipes, in order to allow for the operations of residue extraction and cleaning at least annually. An inspection manhole is provided for before the Imhoff tank. 5. Water from kitchen sinks and dishwashers is treated with a grease trap before being discharged into the Imhoff tank. 6. Stormwater must not be discharged into the Imhoff tank. 1. Art. 71 (Dispersion Sub-irrigation Networks) The dispersion sub-irrigation system is normally used for permeable soils. 2. Discharge by dispersion sub-irrigation onto land is permitted for discharges from single- or two-family residential buildings and from buildings intended for civil habitation. 3. For settlements with a higher potential, such as condominiums, schools, hotels, restaurants, and small residential nuclei, the sub-irrigation discharge system is permitted only in cases where: a) the technical impossibility of discharging into surface water bodies has been ascertained; b) for building types with a consistency of less than 50 P.E. 4. Wastewater from the Imhoff tank and grease traps is discharged, through a sealed pipe, into the dispersion network. 5. The dispersion network consists of pipes made of terracotta, stoneware, concrete, or plastic materials with a diameter of 100-120 mm, laid with a slope between 0.2% and 0.5%, equipped with slots made at the bottom and perpendicular to the axis of the pipe, spaced 200â400 mm apart and 10 to 20 mm wide. The network is placed in a trench at a depth of not less than 600 mm and not more than 800 mm and with a width at the base of at least 400 mm. The bottom of the trench, for at least 300 mm, is occupied by a bed of washed gravel of size 40/70 mm. The dispersion network is placed in the center of the gravel bed. The upper part of the gravel mass, before being covered with the excavated soil, is protected with a layer of suitable material that prevents it from being clogged by the overlying soil and, at the same time, ensures the aeration of the drainage system. When the work is completed, the top of the trench must be raised with respect to the adjacent ground to avoid the formation of depressions above it. The trench may have the dispersion network in a single line, a single line with branches, or multiple lines; the trench follows the contour lines to maintain the dispersion pipe at a suitable slope. 6. For hygienic-sanitary and functional reasons, the trenches with dispersion networks are located far from buildings (about 10 meters), paved areas, or other arrangements that prevent the passage of air into the soil. The distance between the bottom of the trench and the maximum water table level must not be less than one meter; the water table cannot be used downstream for domestic use or for irrigating products to be eaten raw, unless chemical and microbiological tests are carried out by the health unit. 7. The length of the dispersion network, to be determined preferably with percolation tests, must be a function of the nature of the soil, specifically: a) fine sand, light backfill material: 2 m per P.E.; b) coarse sand and gravel: 3 m per P.E.; c) fine sand with clay: 5 m per P.E.; d) clay with some sand: 10 m per P.E.; e) compact clay: not suitable. The soil characteristics are documented by a geological report. 8. There must be a minimum distance of 30 meters between the trench and any pipe, tank, or other work intended for water service. The minimum distance between the trench and wells, pipes, tanks, or other public works intended for water service is 200 meters. 9. The user must periodically check: a) the absence of clogging of the gravel or the underlying soil; b) the absence of surface waterlogging; c) the proper functioning of the trap regularly. 10. The sub-irrigation networks can be served by a lifting system with hydraulic pumps. 1. Art. 72 (Sub-irrigation Networks with Drainage) The system of sub-irrigation networks with drainage is normally used for impermeable soils. Wastewater from clarification, through sealed pipes, flows into the dispersion pipe. The system consists of a trench, generally 1-1.5 meters deep, with a layer of clay at the bottom, on which the drainage pipe is laid, topped vertically with fine and coarse gravel; within the last layer, the dispersion network is placed. The two pipes, generally having a slope between 0.2% and 0.5%, are made of tubular elements of terracotta, stoneware, or concrete with a diameter of about 100-120 mm with straight-cut ends spaced 10 to 20 mm apart, covered on top by tiles or stone elements to prevent the entry of gravel and excavated soil, which will cover the trench with suitable material to avoid depressions; precautions must be taken so that the backfill soil does not fill the voids before settling. Aeration pipes of a suitable diameter are placed vertically, from the ground level down to the lower layer of coarse gravel, arranged alternately to the right and left of the pipes and spaced two to four meters apart. The drainage pipe opens into a suitable receptor (stream, channel, gully, etc.), while the dispersion pipe is closed 5 meters before the outlet of the drainage pipe. The trench can have pipes in a single line, a branched line, or multiple lines. The length of the pipes is generally calculated at two to four meters per user. 2. There must be a minimum distance of 30 meters between the trench and any pipe, tank, or other work intended for water service. The minimum distance between the trench and wells, pipes, tanks, or other public works intended for water service is 200 meters. 3. The sub-irrigation networks can be served by a lifting system with hydraulic pumps. 1. Art. 73 (Phytoremediation) For isolated settlements, installations, or buildings that discharge domestic or comparable wastewater, the use of natural purification technologies such as phytoremediation, or other systems that achieve the same level of environmental protection, is permitted. 2. Phytoremediation systems are distinguished into: a) sub-surface horizontal flow; b) sub-surface vertical flow. 3. The choice of the horizontal flow system is recommended for settlements with a maximum potential of 15 P.E., while the vertical flow system is recommended for a maximum of 100 P.E. 1. Art. 74 (Horizontal Flow Phytoremediation) In horizontal flow phytoremediation, the absorption bed consists of a watertight tank or basin made of masonry, concrete, or prefabricated plastic material, with a perfectly level bottom, placed at a depth of about 700/800 mm. 2. The tank or basin is filled with a layer of washed gravel, size 40/70 mm, for a thickness of 150/200 mm, to facilitate the distribution of the wastewater, and then a layer of washed fine gravel, size 10/20 mm, with a thickness of 15 mm, as a support for the roots. Above the fine gravel layer, a sheet of "non-woven fabric" is placed, and 400/500 mm of a mixture consisting of 50% topsoil and 50% peat, on which the plants will be planted. 3. Depending on the sizing of the absorption bed, there may be either an outflow of wastewater from the system or no discharge due to the complete elimination of wastewater through evaporation and plant transpiration. A surface area of the absorption bed of about 5 sq m per P.E. can be indicated to ensure complete elimination of the wastewater. Upstream of the absorption bed and in any case downstream of the Imhoff tank, a manhole is placed to control the water level in the system. Downstream of the absorption bed, a manhole is placed which will be used to control the water level in the basin, if the system is a closed circuit, and as an official sampling manhole if the system provides for discharge. 1. Art. 75 (Vertical Flow Phytoremediation) Vertical flow phytoremediation requires, downstream of the tank, an accumulation tank equipped with a lift pump to regulate the inlet flow rate. The tank, used both as a substrate for planting and as a filter, must have appropriate slopes to facilitate the discharge of the treated wastewater. The tank can be made of cast-in-place concrete, prefabricated concrete, or synthetic membranes (geomembranes) of adequate thickness, which must be protected against rupture by placing layers of "non-woven fabric" below and above. At the bottom of the tank, the collection pipe is placed, consisting of a perforated drain-type pipe that collects the purified water. This pipe conveys the purified water into a manhole, located at the outlet of the tank, where a water level regulation system (siphon) is placed to prevent water stagnation within the system. Inside the tank is the filling material, consisting of fine washed gravel with a grain size of 4/8 mm, for a height of about 1 m. Above this drainage layer, the supply pipes are laid, 1 m apart from each other, consisting of PVC or polyethylene pipes with a diameter of 100/120 mm, on which holes are made. The pipes are covered with a further layer of fine washed gravel, for a thickness of 100/150 mm. The plants are planted in this layer of gravel. When forming the final leveling of the tank, it is advisable to construct small perimeter banks to prevent the entry of stormwater. 2. For the sizing of the tank surface, from 2.5 sq m to 3.5 sq m per P.E. should be provided. TITLE IV COMMON PROVISIONS CHAPTER I FINAL PROVISIONS 1.
