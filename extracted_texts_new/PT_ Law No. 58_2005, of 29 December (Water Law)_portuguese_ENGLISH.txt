the Water Law, transposing into national law Directive No. 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October, and establishing the basis and the institutional framework for the sustainable management of waters The Assembly of the Republic hereby decrees, under the terms of Article 161(c) of the Constitution, as follows: Chapter I General Provisions Article 1 Objectives 1 - This law establishes the framework for the management of surface waters, namely inland, transitional, and coastal waters, and of groundwater, in order to: a) Prevent further deterioration and protect and enhance the status of aquatic ecosystems and also of terrestrial ecosystems and wetlands directly dependent on aquatic ecosystems with regard to their water needs; b) Promote sustainable water use based on long-term protection of available water resources; c) Achieve enhanced protection and improvement of the aquatic environment, inter alia through specific measures for the progressive reduction and cessation or phasing out of discharges, emissions, and losses of priority substances; d) Ensure the progressive reduction of pollution of groundwater and prevent its further pollution; e) Mitigate the effects of floods and droughts; f) Ensure the sufficient supply of good quality surface water and groundwater as needed for sustainable, balanced, and equitable water use; g) Protect marine waters, including territorial waters; h) Ensure compliance with the objectives of relevant international agreements, including those intended to prevent and eliminate pollution in the marine environment. 2 - This Water Law ensures the transposition of Directive No. 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October, establishing a framework for Community action in the field of water policy. Article 2 Scope 1 - This law applies to all water resources referred to in Article 1(1), whatever their legal regime, covering, in addition to the waters, their respective beds and banks, as well as adjacent zones, maximum infiltration zones, and protected areas. 2 - The provisions of this law are without prejudice to the application of special regimes relating, in particular, to water for human consumption, hydromineral resources, geothermal resources and spring waters, waters for therapeutic purposes, and waters that supply swimming pools and other facilities with aquatic amusements. Amendments Amended by Article 2 of Decree-Law No. 130/2012 - Diário da República No. 120/2012, Series I of 2012-06-22, in force from 2012-06-23 INCM IMPRENSA NACIONAL CASA DA MOEDA Article 3 Version as of 29-12-2023 Page 4 of 51 OFFICIAL GAZETTE OF THE REPUBLIC WATER LAW CONSOLIDATED LEGISLATION Principles 1 - In addition to the general principles enshrined in the Basic Law on the Environment and the principles established in the following chapters of this law, water management shall observe the following principles: a) The principle of the social value of water, which enshrines universal access to water for basic human needs, at a socially acceptable cost, and without being a factor of discrimination or exclusion; b) The principle of public operation and management of water, which applies mandatorily to multi-municipal systems for public water supply and sanitation; c) The principle of the environmental dimension of water, under which the need for a high level of protection of water is recognized, in order to ensure its sustainable use; d) The principle of the economic value of water, by virtue of which the current or potential scarcity of this resource is recognized, as is the need to ensure its economically efficient use, with the recovery of the costs of water services, including environmental and resource costs, and based on the polluter-pays and user-pays principles; e) The principle of integrated management of waters and associated terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems and directly dependent wetlands, by virtue of which it is important to develop actions that simultaneously address quantitative and qualitative aspects, a condition for sustainable development; f) The precautionary principle, according to which measures intended to prevent the negative impact of an action on the environment should be adopted, even in the absence of scientific certainty of a cause-and-effect relationship between them; g) The principle of prevention, by virtue of which actions with negative effects on the environment must be considered in advance in order to eliminate the very causes of environmental change or to reduce their impacts when this is not possible; h) The principle of correction, preferably at source, of damage caused to the environment and the imposition on the polluting emitter of corrective and recovery measures and their respective costs; i) The principle of cooperation, which is based on the recognition that the protection of waters is a duty of the State and a responsibility of individuals; j) The principle of reasonable and equitable use of shared river basins, which recognizes the right and obligation of riparian States to use the watercourse in a reasonable and equitable manner with a view to the optimized and sustainable use of resources, consistent with their protection. 2- The river basin district is the main unit for water planning and management, based on the river basin. Chapter II Institutional Framework WATER LAW CONSOLIDATED LEGISLATION Article 5 Public Administration It is the duty of the State to promote the sustainable management of waters and to carry out the activities necessary for the application of this law. Article 6 River basin districts 1 - Within the framework of the specificity of the river basins, the national aquifer systems, and the basins shared with Spain, as well as the unique characteristics of the Autonomous Regions of the Azores and Madeira, the following river basin districts are created: a) Minho and Lima (RH 1), which comprises the river basins of the Minho and Lima rivers and the coastal streams between their respective estuaries and other small adjacent streams; b) Cávado, Ave and Leça (RH 2), which comprises the river basins of the Cávado, Ave and Leça rivers and the coastal streams between their respective estuaries and other small adjacent streams; c) Douro (RH 3), which comprises the river basin of the Douro river and other small adjacent streams; d) Vouga, Mondego and Lis (RH 4), which comprises the river basins of the Vouga, Mondego and Lis rivers, and the coastal streams between the estuary of the Douro river and the mouth of the Lis river; e) Tagus and Western Streams (RH 5), which comprises the river basins of all watercourses south of the mouth of the Lis up to, but not including, the Tagus river estuary, and the river basin of the Tagus river and other small adjacent streams; f) Sado and Mira (RH 6), which comprises the river basins of the Sado and Mira rivers and other small adjacent streams; g) Guadiana (RH 7), which comprises the river basin of the Guadiana river; h) Algarve Streams (RH 8), which comprises the river basins of the Algarve streams; i) Azores (RH 9), which comprises all river basins of the archipelago; j) Madeira (RH 10), which comprises all river basins of the archipelago. 2 The river basin districts of Minho and Lima, Douro, Tagus and Western Streams, and Guadiana are part of international river basin districts as they comprise river basins shared with the Kingdom of Spain. 3 - The Government shall define by specific regulation, under the terms of Article 102(3), the geo-referenced delimitation of the river basin districts. Article 15 Scope of intervention 1 - Measures for the management and planning of water resources have as their scope of intervention, beyond their own geographical limits, the surrounding territory that impacts these resources and the zones subject to measures for their protection. 2 - "Surrounding territory that impacts water resources" is understood as the banks of public water lakes and reservoirs and the coastal and estuarine shores where it is important to impose rules to harmonize their diverse uses with the preservation of water resources and media. 3 - The zones subject to measures for the protection of water resources include the protection perimeters and adjacent areas of water abstractions for human consumption, the maximum infiltration areas for aquifer recharge, and areas vulnerable to pollution by nitrates from agricultural sources. 4 - Certain areas, namely parts of basins, aquifers, or water bodies, which, due to their natural characteristics and environmental, economic, or social value, are of special public interest, may also be subject to these protection measures. Section III Planning Article 23 Water planning It is the responsibility of the State, through the National Water Authority, to establish an integrated water planning system adapted to the specific characteristics of the basins and river basin districts. 1 - Article 24 Objectives and instruments of planning Water planning aims to substantiate and guide the protection and management of waters and the reconciliation of their uses with their availability in order to: a) Guarantee their sustainable use, ensuring the satisfaction of the needs of current generations without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs; b) Provide criteria for allocation to the various types of intended uses, taking into account the economic value of each, as well as ensuring the harmonization of water management with regional development and sectoral policies, individual rights, and local interests; c) Set environmental quality standards and criteria relating to the status of waters. 2 - Water planning is implemented through the following instruments: a) The National Water Plan, with a territorial scope, covering the entire national territory; b) River basin management plans, with a territorial scope, covering the river basins integrated into a river basin district and including their respective programmes of measures; c) Specific water management plans, which are complementary to the river basin management plans and which may be of a territorial scope, covering a sub-basin or a specific geographical area, or of a sectoral scope, covering a problem, type of water, specific aspect, or economic activity sector with a significant interaction with waters. INCM IMPRENSA NACIONAL CASA DA MOEDA Version as of 29-12-2023 Page 16 of 51 OFFICIAL GAZETTE OF THE REPUBLIC WATER LAW CONSOLIDATED LEGISLATION Article 25 Principles of water planning Water planning shall obey the following specific principles: a) Integration - the activity of water planning must be integrated horizontally with other administrative planning instruments, at the environmental, territorial, or economic level; b) Global consideration - economic, environmental, technical, and institutional aspects relevant to water management must be considered, guaranteeing their quantitative and qualitative preservation and their efficient, sustainable, and ecologically balanced use; c) Functional adaptation - water planning instruments must diversify their intervention in water resource management according to specific problems, needs, and public interests, without prejudice to the necessary unity and coherence of their planning content within each river basin; d) Durability - water planning must attend to the continuity and stability of the resource in question, protecting its ecological quality and regenerative capacity; e) Participation - any private individuals, users of water resources, and their associations may intervene in water planning and, specifically, in the procedures for preparing, implementing, and amending its instruments; f) Information - water planning instruments constitute a means of managing information about the administrative activity of water resources management in each river basin; g) International cooperation - within the scope of an international river basin district, water planning must address, in a concerted manner, the problems of water resources management. Article 26 Participation in planning In the preparation, review, and evaluation of water planning instruments, the following shall be guaranteed: a) The intervention of the various ministerial departments that oversee the activities interested in the use of water resources and of the public bodies to which the administration of the areas involved is assigned; b) The participation of interested parties through the public consultation process and the representation of users in the advisory bodies for water management; c) The prior publication, namely on the website of the National Water Authority, of all relevant information under the terms of Article 85, including the draft plan and all proposals and opinions received throughout the consultation process. Article 27 Regulations In the event that a water planning instrument concludes that it is necessary to subject certain activities of the administered parties to conditions or restrictions authorized by law, imposed by the protection and good management of waters, the rules establishing such conditions and restrictions shall be set out in a regulation, approved by an ordinance of the member of the Government responsible for the environment. Article 42 Measures for protection against major pollution accidents 1 - In programmes for the prevention of and response to major pollution accidents, particularly those contained in water resources plans, the following must be done: a) Identify and assess the risks of pollution from all potential sources, namely industrial units, wastewater treatment plants, old abandoned mines, waste deposits, and the circulation of vehicles transporting hazardous substances; b) Identify all uses that may be put at risk by possible pollution accidents, especially the sources for water supply serving more populous areas; c) Define the measures intended for the various foreseeable situations in the sectors of activity with the highest risk and their respective implementation mechanisms, structured according to the levels of severity of the occurrence and the importance of the resources at risk. 2 - An warning and alert system must be established, with levels of action as provided for in the programmes, with the entity responsible for the accident being primarily obliged to alert the competent authorities. 3 - Waters must be specially protected against major pollution accidents in order to safeguard the quality of water resources and ecosystems, as well as the safety of people and property. Article 46 Objectives for surface waters 1 - The necessary measures shall be implemented to prevent the deterioration of the status of all surface water bodies, without prejudice to the following provisions. 2 - With the objective of achieving good status of surface water bodies, with the exception of artificial and heavily modified water bodies, measures shall be taken for their protection, improvement, and restoration. 3 - With the objective of achieving good ecological potential and good chemical status of artificial or heavily modified water bodies, measures shall be taken for the protection and improvement of their status. 4 - The gradual reduction of pollution from priority substances and the cessation of emissions, discharges, and losses of priority hazardous substances shall also be ensured. 5 - The classification and presentation of the ecological status of surface waters and the monitoring of the ecological and chemical status of surface waters shall be defined in regulations to be approved under the terms of Article 102(3). Article 47 Objectives for groundwater 1 - Measures shall be implemented to prevent or limit the input of pollutants into groundwater and to prevent the deterioration of the status of all water bodies. 2 - Good groundwater status must be achieved, for which purpose one must: a) Ensure the protection, enhancement, and restoration of all groundwater bodies, ensuring a balance between abstractions and recharge of such waters; b) Reverse any significant and sustained upward trend in the concentration of any pollutant resulting from the impact of human activity in order to progressively reduce pollution of groundwater. 3 - The quantitative and chemical status of groundwater and its monitoring are regulated by norms to be approved under the terms of Article 102(3). 4 - The direct discharge of pollutants into groundwater is prohibited, with the exception of discharges that do not compromise the achievement of the specific objectives established in this law, which may be authorized under the conditions defined by regulations to be approved under the terms of Article 102(3). Article 48 Objectives for protected areas 1 - The objectives that justified the creation of the protected areas must be ensured, fully observing the legal provisions established for this purpose and which guarantee the control of pollution. 2 - A register shall be compiled of all areas included in each river basin district which have been designated as requiring special protection with regard to the protection of surface and groundwaters or the conservation of INCM IMPRENSA NACIONAL CASA DA MOEDA Version as of 29-12-2023 Page 29 of 51 OFFICIAL GAZETTE OF THE REPUBLIC WATER LAW CONSOLIDATED LEGISLATION habitats and species directly depending on water. 3 The register of protected areas for each river basin district shall include maps indicating the location of each protected area and a description of the legislation under which these areas have been created. 4 - All bodies of water within each river basin district used for the abstraction of water intended for human consumption providing more than 10 m3 a day as an average or serving more than 50 persons shall be identified, as well as those bodies of water intended for such future use, and their classification as protected areas shall be mentioned, where appropriate. Article 49 Artificial or heavily modified water bodies 1 - A body of surface water may be designated as artificial or heavily modified when both of the following conditions occur cumulatively: a) If the changes to the hydromorphological characteristics of that water body which would be necessary for achieving good ecological status would have significant adverse effects on: i) The wider environment; ii) The ability to regulate flows, protect against floods, and drain land; iii) Specific uses, namely navigation, port facilities, recreation, activities for which water is stored, including drinking water supply, power generation or irrigation; or iv) Other equally important sustainable development activities; b) If the beneficial objectives served by the artificial or modified characteristics of the water body cannot, for reasons of technical feasibility or disproportionate cost, reasonably be achieved by other means, which are a significantly better environmental option. 2 - The designation of a water body as artificial or heavily modified, and the respective justification, shall be included in the river basin management plan, and its review every six years is mandatory. Article 50 Extension of deadlines The deadline established in Article 45(2) may be extended for the purposes of a phased achievement of the objectives for water bodies, provided that the requirements of Article 52 are met, as long as, alternatively, no further deterioration occurs in the status of the affected water body or all of the following conditions are met: a) The necessary improvements in the status of water bodies cannot all reasonably be achieved within the timeframes due, at least, to one of the following reasons: i) The scale of improvements required can only, for reasons of technical feasibility, be achieved in phases exceeding the timescale; ii) It is disproportionately expensive to complete the improvements within the timescale; or iii) Natural conditions do not allow for timely improvements in the status of the water body; and b) The extension of the deadline and the reasons for it are specifically set out and explained in the river basin management plan; and also c) The extensions shall be limited to periods not exceeding the period covered by two updates of the river basin management plan, except in cases where the natural conditions are such that the objectives cannot be achieved within that period; and finally d) A summary of the measures required to bring the bodies of water progressively to the required status by the end of the extended deadline, the reasons for any significant delay in making these measures operational, and the expected timetable for their implementation have been set out in the river basin management plan, and a review of the implementation of the planned measures and a summary of any additional measures have been included in the update of the river basin management plan. Chapter V Use of Water Resources Article 56 Principle of the need for a title of use Under the precautionary and prevention principle, activities that have a significant impact on the status of waters may only be carried out under a title of use issued under the terms and conditions provided for in this law and in a decree-law to be approved under Article 102(2), which shall also regulate the matters covered in Article 63(1)(a), Article 66(3), Article 67(5), Article 68(9), and Article 69(1). Article 57 Basic duties of users 1 - Users of water resources must act diligently, taking into account the circumstances, in order to: a) Avoid any disturbance of the status of water, as determined under this law, and, in particular, any contamination or adverse alteration of its functional capacities; b) Achieve an economic use of water that is sustainable and compatible with maintaining the integrity of water resources. 2 - Waters shall be used in such a way as to avoid the creation of unreasonable risks or dangers to their integrity, to the quality of the environment, or to public supply reserves. Article 58 Common use of public domain water resources The water resources of the public domain are for common use and enjoyment, namely in their functions of recreation, stay, and watering of livestock, and this use and enjoyment is not subject to a title of use, provided that it is done in compliance with general law and the conditions defined in the applicable plans and does not produce a significant alteration in the quality and quantity of the water. Article 59 Private use of public domain water resources 1 - Private use of public domain water resources is considered to be that in which someone obtains for themselves a greater use of these resources than the generality of users, or that which implies an alteration in the status of these resources or endangers that status. 2 - The right of private use of the public domain may only be granted by license or by concession, whatever the nature and legal form of its holder, and cannot be acquired by usucaption or by any other title. Article 61 Public domain uses subject to concession The following private uses of public domain water resources are subject to a prior concession: a) Water abstraction for public supply; b) Water abstraction for irrigation of an area greater than 50 ha; c) Use of public water domain land for the construction of tourist and similar developments; d) Water abstraction for energy production; e) Installation of hydraulic infrastructures intended for the purposes referred to in the preceding subparagraphs. Article 62 Use of private water resources 1 - The following activities are subject to prior authorization for the use of water resources when they affect private beds, banks, and waters: a) Carrying out constructions; b) Installing hydraulic infrastructures; c) Abstracting water; d) Other activities that alter the status of water bodies or endanger that status, beyond those referred to in the following paragraph. 2 - The following activities are subject to a prior license for use and to compliance with the provisions of the river basin management plan when they affect private beds, banks, and waters: a) Discharge of wastewater; b) Immersion of waste; c) Artificial recharge and injection into groundwater; d) Extraction of aggregates; e) Landfilling and excavation. 3 - To the extent that it does not jeopardize the objectives of this law, the need for prior authorization as provided for in paragraph 1 may be waived by the regulation annexed to the river basin management plan or by the regulation annexed to the applicable special land-use plan, or replaced by mere communication to the authorities that supervise the use of water resources. 4 - The abstraction of private waters requires simple communication from the user to the entity competent for supervising the use of water resources when the means of extraction do not exceed 5 hp, unless the said abstraction is characterized by the competent licensing authority as having a significant impact on the status of the waters. Article 63 Requirements and conditions for titles of use 1 - The granting of titles of use must ensure: INCM IMPRENSA NACIONAL CASA DA MOEDA Version as of 29-12-2023 Page 35 of 51 OFFICIAL GAZETTE OF THE REPUBLIC WATER LAW CONSOLIDATED LEGISLATION a) Compliance with the norms and principles of this law and with the regulations to be approved, as provided for in Article 56; b) Respect for the provisions of the applicable river basin management plan; c) Respect for the provisions of territorial management instruments, specific water management plans, and the regulations provided for in Article 27; d) Compliance with quality standards and discharge standards; e) The granting of precedence to the use considered a priority under the terms of this law, in the event of a conflict of uses. 2 - The title of use must determine that the user shall refrain from practicing acts or activities that cause the degradation of the status of water bodies and generate other negative environmental impacts or render unfeasible alternative uses considered priorities. Article 64 Order of preference of uses 1 - In the event of a conflict between different uses of the public water domain, the preference criteria established in the river basin management plan shall be followed, with priority in any case being given to water abstraction for public supply over the other planned uses, and under equal conditions, the use that ensures the most economically balanced, rational, and sustainable use shall be preferred, without prejudice to the protection of water resources. 2 - In considering the conflict situation referred to in paragraph 1, not only new applications for titles of use shall be considered, but also existing titles of use that may be revoked. 3 - In the event of a declaration of a scarcity situation, the order of priority referred to in the preceding paragraphs may be altered by the river basin district administration, after consulting the river basin district council. 4 - The uses referred to in Article 61 are considered as principal uses of the public water domain, and all others are considered as complementary. Article 65 Request for prior information Any interested party may address a request for prior information to the National Water Authority regarding the possibility of using water resources for the intended purpose, but the information provided shall only constitute legally protected rights or interests in the sphere of the applicant if this is recognized in the complementary diploma provided for in Article 56. Article 69 Termination of titles of use 1 - The title of use is extinguished upon the expiry of the term fixed therein and under the other conditions provided for in the regulations to be approved under the terms of Article 56. 2 - Upon expiry of the term fixed in the title: a) In the case of a concession, the works executed and the installations built strictly within the scope of the concession for the use of water resources shall revert free of charge to the State; Article 90 Inspection and supervision 1 - Verification of compliance with the norms provided for in this law may take the form of: a) Supervision, to be carried out systematically by the licensing authorities, in compliance with their legal obligation of vigilance over users of water resources, whether or not they hold titles of use, and on an ad hoc basis in response to complaints and reports received regarding their area of jurisdiction; b) Inspection, to be carried out by entities with competence for this purpose on a case-by-case and random basis, or in execution of a previously approved inspection plan, or in the context of determining the scope of and responsibilities for pollution accidents. 2 - Supervision is the responsibility of the National Water Authority in the area of use and of the other entities legally granted competence for the licensing of the use of water resources in that area, and they are also responsible for initiating, investigating, and sanctioning administrative offense proceedings for infractions committed in their area of jurisdiction. 3 - Police or administrative authorities with jurisdiction in the area shall cooperate in the supervision action, and must prevent infractions of the provisions of this law and report any transgressions of which they have knowledge. 4 - Inspection is the responsibility of the Inspectorate-General for the Environment and Spatial Planning. - 5 The supervising entities referred to in paragraph 2 must maintain a public register of the complaints and reports received and the action taken on them. Article 92 Inspection and supervision plans 1 - Within the scope of applying the precautionary and prevention principle, the National Water Authority, together with the competent licensing, inspection, and supervision entities, must promote the preparation of inspection and supervision plans, which must state the spatial, temporal, and material scope, the programmes and procedures adopted, and the mode of coordination of the competent entities in matters of supervision and inspection. 2 The inspection and supervision plans are public and must be disseminated in the components that do not compromise their effectiveness. Article 93 Access to facilities, documentation, and information 1 - In the exercise of their functions, duly identified entities with inspection and supervision powers must be granted free entry to the facilities where activities subject to supervision or inspection measures are carried out. 2 - Those responsible for the facilities subject to inspection or supervision measures are obliged to grant entry and allow the stay of the entities referred to in the preceding paragraph and to provide them with the necessary assistance, namely by presenting requested documentation, books, or records, opening containers, and guaranteeing accessibility to equipment. 3 In the context of inspection or supervision action, the respective personnel may collect information on the inspected activities, proceed to examinations of any traces of infractions, as well as take samples for laboratory examination.
