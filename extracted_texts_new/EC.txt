THE PLENARY

WHEREAS:

That, Articles 12, 313, and 318 of the Constitution of the Republic establish the principle that water is a national strategic heritage, for public use, and is an inalienable, imprescriptible, and unseizable domain of the State, constituting a vital element for nature and for the existence of human beings, reserving for the State the right to administer, regulate, control, and manage strategic sectors, in accordance with the principles of environmental sustainability, precaution, prevention, and efficiency;

That, Article 318 of the Constitution prohibits any form of privatization of water and determines that the management of water shall be exclusively public or community-based and that the sanitation service, the supply of drinking water, and irrigation shall be provided solely by state or community legal entities; it further prescribes that the State, through the Single Water Authority, shall be directly responsible for the planning and management of water resources intended for human consumption and irrigation that guarantees food sovereignty, ecological flow, and productive activities, in this order of precedence, and that state authorization shall be required for the utilization of water for productive purposes by the public, private, and popular and solidarity economy sectors, in accordance with the Law;

That, Article 314 of the Constitution of the Republic assigns to the State the responsibility for the provision of public drinking water and irrigation services, for which it shall provide that their tariffs be equitable and shall establish their control and regulation. The same provision determines that the State shall strengthen the management and operation of community initiatives concerning water management and the provision of public services by encouraging alliances between the public and community sectors for the provision of services;

That, Article 411 provides that the State shall guarantee the conservation, recovery, and integrated management of water resources, hydrographic basins, and ecological flows associated with the hydrological cycle, and that it shall regulate all activities that may affect the quality and quantity of water, and the balance of ecosystems, especially at the sources and in recharge zones.

The sustainability of ecosystems and human consumption shall be priorities in the use and utilization of water;

That, the First Transitional Provision of the Constitution of the Republic provides that the Law regulating water resources, uses, and utilization of water shall include current and future use and utilization permits, their terms, conditions, and mechanisms for review and audit to ensure the formalization and equitable distribution of this heritage;

That, Articles 66 and 276 recognize and guarantee to individuals and communities the right to equitable, permanent, and quality access to water, air, and soil, and to a dignified life that ensures health, food and nutrition, drinking water, housing, environmental sanitation, education, work, employment, rest and leisure, physical culture, clothing, social security, and other necessary social services;

That, Article 281 establishes that food sovereignty constitutes a strategic objective and an obligation of the State to ensure that individuals, communities, peoples, and nationalities achieve self-sufficiency in healthy and culturally appropriate food on a permanent basis.

ORGANIC LAW ON WATER RESOURCES, USES AND UTILIZATION OF WATER

TITLE I
PRELIMINARY PROVISIONS

CHAPTER I
PRINCIPLES

Art. 1.- Legal nature. Water resources are part of the natural heritage of the State and shall be under its exclusive authority, which shall be exercised concurrently between the Central Government and the Decentralized Autonomous Governments, in accordance with the Law.

Water is a national strategic heritage for public use, an inalienable, imprescriptible, unseizable, and essential domain for life, a vital element of nature, and fundamental to guaranteeing food sovereignty.

Art. 2.- Scope of application. This Organic Law shall apply throughout the national territory, and all persons, whether nationals or foreigners, within it shall be subject to its provisions.

Art. 3.- Object of the Law. The object of this Law is to guarantee the human right to water, as well as to regulate and control the authorization, management, preservation, conservation, and restoration of water resources, use and utilization of water, and its integrated management and recovery in its different phases, forms, and physical states, in order to guarantee sumak kawsay or good living and the rights of nature established in the Constitution.

Art. 4.- Principles of the Law. This Law is based on the following principles:

a) The integration of all waters, whether surface, groundwater, or atmospheric, into the hydrological cycle with ecosystems;
b) Water, as a natural resource, must be conserved and protected through sustainable and lasting management that guarantees its permanence and quality;
c) Water, as a public domain good, is inalienable, imprescriptible, and unseizable;
d) Water is a national and strategic heritage at the service of the needs of citizens and an essential element for food sovereignty; consequently, any type of private property over water is prohibited;
e) Access to water is a human right;
f) The State guarantees equitable access to water;

g) The State guarantees the integrated, integral, and participatory management of water; and,
h) The management of water is public or community-based.

Art. 5.- Strategic sector. Water constitutes a national heritage, a strategic sector of decision and exclusive control of the State through the Single Water Authority. Its management shall be oriented toward the full exercise of rights and the public interest, in attention to its decisive social, community, cultural, political, environmental, and economic influence.

Art. 6.- Prohibition of privatization. All forms of privatization of water are prohibited, due to its importance for life, the economy, and the environment; for the same reason, it cannot be the subject of any commercial agreement with any government, multilateral entity, or national or foreign private company.

Its management shall be exclusively public or community-based. No form of individual or collective appropriation or possession of water, whatever its state, shall be recognized.

Consequently, the following are prohibited:

a) Any delegation to the private sector of water management or any of the competencies constitutionally or legally assigned to the State through the Single Water Authority or to the Decentralized Autonomous Governments;
b) The indirect management, delegation, or outsourcing of the provision of public services related to the integral water cycle by private initiative;
c) Any commercial agreement that imposes an economic regime based on profit for water management;
d) Any form of commercialization of environmental services related to water for profit;
e) Any form of convention or cooperation agreement that includes clauses that undermine the conservation, sustainable management of water, biodiversity, human health, the human right to water, food sovereignty, human rights, and the rights of nature; and,
f) The granting of perpetual or indefinite-term authorizations for the use or utilization of water.

Art. 7.- Activities in the strategic water sector. The provision of the public water service is exclusively public or community-based. Exceptionally, private initiative and the popular and solidarity economy may participate in the following cases:

a) Declaration of emergency adopted by the competent authority, in accordance with the legal framework; or,
b) Development of subprocesses of the public service administration when the competent authority does not have the technical or financial conditions to do so. The maximum term shall be ten years, subject to a prior audit.

Art. 8.- Integrated management of water resources. The Single Water Authority is responsible for the integrated and integral management of water resources with an ecosystem-based approach and by hydrographic basin or basin systems, which shall be coordinated with the different levels of government according to their areas of competence.

A hydrographic basin is understood as the territorial unit delimited by the watershed line of its waters that drain superficially towards a common channel, including in this space populations, infrastructure, conservation areas, protection areas, and productive zones.

When the boundaries of groundwater do not coincide with the surface water divide, said delimitation shall include the projection of the groundwater recharge waters that flow towards the superficially delimited basin.

The Single Water Authority shall approve the specific delimitation of the hydrographic basins and their

TITLE II
WATER RESOURCES

CHAPTER I
DEFINITION, INFRASTRUCTURE AND CLASSIFICATION OF WATER RESOURCES

Art. 10.- Public water domain. The public water domain is constituted by the following natural elements:

a) Rivers, lakes, lagoons, wetlands, snowcaps, glaciers, and natural waterfalls;
b) Groundwater;
c) Aquifers for the purposes of protection and disposal of water resources;
d) Water sources, understood as the sources of rivers and their tributaries, a spring or natural source where groundwater emerges to the surface or is collected at the beginning of its runoff;
e) The natural beds or channels of a continuous or discontinuous current, which are the lands covered by water during maximum ordinary floods;
f) The beds and subsoils of rivers, lakes, lagoons, and surface reservoirs in natural channels;
g) The banks, which are the natural strips of the channels located above the low water level;
h) The geomorphological conformation of the hydrographic basins and their mouths;
i) Coastal marine wetlands and coastal waters; and,
j) Waters originating from the desalination of seawater.

Publicly owned hydraulic works or infrastructure and their hydraulic protection zones are considered an integral part of the public water domain.

Art. 11.- Hydraulic infrastructure. Hydraulic works or infrastructure are considered to be those intended for the capture, extraction, storage, regulation, conduction, control, and utilization of water, as well as for the sanitation, purification, treatment, and reuse of utilized waters, and those whose purpose is the artificial recharge of aquifers, actions on channels, correction of the flow regime, protection against floods or high waters, such as dams, reservoirs, canals, conduits, population supply depots, sewerage, collectors of pluvial and residual waters, sanitation, purification, and treatment facilities, gauging stations, piezometers, quality control networks, as well as all necessary works and equipment for the protection of the public water domain.

Hydraulic works or infrastructure may be of public, private, or community ownership, depending on who has built and financed them, although their use is of public interest and they are governed by this Law.

In the event of a state of exception or declaration of emergency, in which the State requires water to guarantee its provision to the affected population, the administration, maintenance, and use of all hydraulic infrastructure may be carried out by the State, regardless of its ownership.

Art. 12.- Protection, recovery, and conservation of sources. The State, community systems, drinking water boards, and irrigation boards, consumers, and users are co-responsible for the

Art. 13.- Forms of conservation and protection of water sources. The forms of conservation and protection of water sources are: public use easements, water protection zones, and restriction zones.

The lands bordering public watercourses are subject throughout their longitudinal extension to a public use easement zone, which shall be regulated in accordance with the Regulations and the Law.

For the protection of the waters that circulate through the channels and associated ecosystems, a water protection zone is established. Any utilization intended to be developed at a distance from the channel, which shall be defined by regulation, must be subject to authorization by the Single Water Authority, without prejudice to other authorizations that may be required.

The same public use easements and water protection zones shall exist in surface reservoirs.

In aquifers, restriction zones shall be delimited in which activities that may be carried out in them shall be conditioned in the form and with the effects established in the Regulations to this Law.

Art. 14.- Change of land use. The State shall regulate activities that may affect the quantity and quality of water and the balance of ecosystems in the water protection areas that supply water systems for human consumption and irrigation; based on environmental impact studies that ensure minimal impact and the restoration of the mentioned ecosystems.

Section One
National Strategic System and Single Water Authority

Art. 15.- National strategic water system. The national strategic water system is created, which constitutes the set of processes, entities, and instruments that allow the interaction of the different social and institutional actors to organize and coordinate the integrated and integral management of water resources.

The national strategic water system shall be composed of:

The Single Water Authority, which directs it;

The Intercultural and Plurinational Water Council;

The institutions of the Executive Function that fulfill competencies related to the integrated management of water resources;

The Water Regulation and Control Agency, attached to the Single Water Authority;

The Decentralized Autonomous Governments; and,

The Basin Councils.

Art. 16.- Objectives of the national strategic water system. The objectives of the national strategic water system are:

To articulate the actors that form part of the national strategic water system for the integrated and integral management of water resources; and,

To generate mechanisms and instances to coordinate the planning and application of public policy on water resources with the social actors linked to water and the different levels of government, to guarantee good living.

Art. 17.- The Single Water Authority. It is the entity that directs the national strategic water system, a public law legal person. Its head shall be appointed by the President of the Republic and shall have the rank of Minister of State.

It is responsible for the governance, planning, and management of water resources. Its management shall be deconcentrated throughout the territory.

Art. 18.- Competencies and attributions of the Single Water Authority. The competencies are:

a) To direct the National Strategic Water System;
b) To exercise governance and execute public policies related to the integrated and integral management of water resources; and to monitor their compliance;
c) To coordinate with the national environmental authority and the national health authority the formulation of policies on water quality and control of water contamination;
d) To prepare the National Water Resources Plan and the integrated and integral water resources management plans by hydrographic basin; and to approve national water planning;
e) To establish and delimit the water protection zones and areas;
f) To define the administrative delimitation of hydrographic units;
g) To grant authorizations for all uses and utilizations of water;
h) To grant authorizations for the change of use or utilization of water and the renewals of authorization where applicable;
i) To grant legal personality to the drinking water administration boards and to the Irrigation and Drainage Boards;
j) To maintain and update the public water registry;
k) To declare of public interest the information on the availability of surface, groundwater, and atmospheric waters;
l) To establish mechanisms of coordination and complementarity with the Decentralized Autonomous Governments regarding the provision of public services for irrigation and drainage, drinking water,

Art. 21.- Water Regulation and Control Agency. The Water Regulation and Control Agency (ARCA) is a public law body of a technical-administrative nature, attached to the Single Water Authority, with legal personality, administrative and financial autonomy, with its own assets and national jurisdiction.

The Water Regulation and Control Agency shall exercise regulation and control over the integrated and integral management of water resources, the quantity and quality of water in its sources and recharge zones, the quality of public services related to the water sector, and in all uses, utilizations, and destinations of water.

The regulation and control management of the Agency shall be periodically evaluated by the Single Water Authority.

Art. 22.- Integration of the Regulation and Control Agency. The Regulation and Control Agency shall have a board of directors integrated as follows:

The representative of the Single Water Authority or their delegate, who shall preside;

The representative of the entity responsible for coordinating the strategic sectors; or their delegate; and,

The representative of the national entity responsible for planning and development or their delegate.

The board of directors shall appoint an executive director and, by resolution, shall establish the administrative and financial structure of the Regulation and Control Agency.

The executive director shall comply with the resolutions of the board, shall exercise the legal representation of the Agency, and shall have the powers and attributions assigned to them by the governing body.

Section Two
Water Planning

Art. 28.- Planning of Water Resources. The execution of water planning corresponds to the Single Water Authority, based on the National Water Resources Plan and the Integrated Water Resources Management Plans by hydrographic basin.

The State and the Decentralized Autonomous Governments shall be subject to water planning with respect to the exercise of their competencies. Likewise, the integrated water resources management plans by basin shall be binding on the entities dedicated to the provision of community services related to water.

Users shall adapt their actions related to the use and protection of water to what is established in the water planning.

Existing authorizations for the use and utilization of water must be compatible with what is established in the integrated water resources management plans by basin; otherwise, they must be revised in harmony with the National Water Resources Plan, in accordance with the provisions of the Regulations to this Law.

Art. 29.- Content of the water plans. The water plans shall contain:

The National Water Resources Plan shall contain:

a) National-level water balances;
b) The hydraulic works that must be built to satisfy water needs;
c) The factors for the conservation and protection of water and the ecosystems in which it is found; and,
d) The forecast and conditions for carrying out water transfers between different areas of hydrographic planning by basin.

Art. 31.- Transfers. The construction of transfers between hydrographic basins may be carried out provided that it is considered in the water planning and does not threaten the water supply for human consumption and irrigation. To authorize transfers, the Single Water Authority shall require the technical, economic, and environmental justifications of the project and shall subject it to the respective environmental evaluation and licensing process, and its implementation shall be coordinated with the Decentralized Autonomous Governments involved in the transfer area.

Section Three
Management and Administration of Water Resources

Art. 32.- Public or community management of water. The management of water is exclusively public or community-based.

The public management of water comprises, in accordance with the provisions of this Law, the governance, formulation, and execution of policies, planning, integrated management in hydrographic basins, organization and regulation of the institutional water regime and control, knowledge, and sanction of infractions, as well as the administration, operation, construction, and maintenance of the hydraulic infrastructure under the charge of the State.

Community management shall be carried out by the communes, communities, peoples, nationalities, and boards of user service organizations, drinking water boards, and irrigation boards. It comprises, in accordance with this Law, participation in the protection of water and in the administration, operation, and maintenance of infrastructure from which the members of a water system benefit and which is not under the administration of the State.

Art. 33.- Scope and modalities of the management of water resources. The public management of water resources shall include planning, formulation of national policies, integrated management in hydrographic basins, the granting, monitoring, and control of authorizations for use and authorizations for productive utilization of water, the determination of ecological flows, the preservation and conservation of sources and water recharge zones, the regulation and technical control of management, cooperation with environmental authorities in the prevention and control of water contamination and in the disposition of discharges, the observance of user rights, the organization, governance, and regulation of the institutional water regime, and the control, knowledge, and sanction of infractions.

Art. 34.- Integrated and integral management of water resources. The Single Water Authority is responsible for the integrated and integral management of water resources with an ecosystem-based approach and by basin or hydrographic basin systems, which will be coordinated with the different levels of government according to their areas of competence.

A hydrographic basin is understood as the territorial unit delimited by the divide of its waters that drain superficially towards a common channel. It includes in this space, populations, infrastructure, conservation areas, protection areas, and productive zones.

When the limits of the groundwater do not coincide with the surface water divide, said delimitation will include the projection of the groundwater recharge waters that flow towards the superficially delimited basin.

The Single Water Authority will approve the specific delimitation of the hydrographic basins and their possible grouping for planning and management purposes, as well as the attribution of the groundwater to the corresponding basin.

The integrated and integral management of water resources will be a transversal axis of the national decentralized system of participatory planning for development.

Art. 35.- Principles of the management of water resources. The management of water resources throughout the national territory will be carried out in accordance with the following principles:

a) The hydrographic basin will constitute the unit of planning and integrated management of water resources;
b) Planning for the management of water resources must be considered in the territorial ordering plans of the territories included within the hydrographic basin, environmental management, and collective knowledge and ancestral wisdom;
c) The management of water and the provision of the public service of sanitation, drinking water, irrigation, and drainage are exclusively public or community-based;
d) The provision of drinking water, irrigation, and drainage services must be governed by the principles of obligation, generality, uniformity, efficiency, responsibility, universality, accessibility, regularity, continuity, and quality; and,
e) Social participation will be carried out in the spaces established in this Law and the other legal bodies issued for that purpose.

Art. 36.- State duties in integrated management. The State and its institutions within the scope of their competencies are responsible for the integrated management of water resources by hydrographic basin. Consequently, they are obliged to:

a) Promote and guarantee the human right to water;
b) Regulate the uses, the utilization of water, and the actions to preserve it in quantity and quality through sustainable management based on technical norms and quality parameters;
c) Conserve and sustainably manage the coastal marine, high Andean, and Amazonian ecosystems, especially páramos, wetlands, and all ecosystems that store water;
d) Promote and strengthen the participation in water management of user organizations, consumers of public and community water systems, through the hydrographic basin councils and the Intercultural and Plurinational Water Council; and,
e) Recover and promote ancestral knowledge, research, and scientific knowledge of the hydrological cycle.

TITLE III
RIGHTS, GUARANTEES, AND OBLIGATIONS

CHAPTER I
THE HUMAN RIGHT TO WATER

Art. 57.- Definition. The human right to water is the right of all persons to have clean, sufficient, safe, acceptable, accessible, and affordable water for personal and domestic use in terms of quantity, quality, continuity, and coverage.

Part of this right is access to environmental sanitation that ensures human dignity, health, prevents contamination, and guarantees the quality of water reserves for human consumption.

The human right to water is fundamental and inalienable. No person may be deprived, excluded, or dispossessed of this right.

The exercise of the human right to water shall be sustainable, so that it may be exercised by future generations. The Single Water Authority shall define quality water reserves for the human consumption of present and future generations and shall be responsible for the execution of policies related to the effectiveness of the human right to water.

Art. 58.- Enforceability of the human right to water. Persons, communities, peoples, nationalities, collectives, and communes may demand from the corresponding authorities the fulfillment and observance of the human right to water, which they shall attend to in a priority and progressive manner. The authorities who fail to comply with the exercise of this right shall be subject to sanction in accordance with the law.

Art. 59.- Vital quantity and minimum tariff. The Single Water Authority shall establish, in accordance with national and international norms and directives, the vital quantity of water per person to satisfy their basic and domestic needs, the access to which constitutes the essential content of the human right to water.

The vital quantity of raw water intended for processing for human consumption is free of charge in guarantee of the human right to water. When the established minimum vital quantity is exceeded, the corresponding tariff will be applied.

The vital quantity of processed water per person shall have a tariff that guarantees the sustainability of the service provision.

Art. 60.- Free access and use of water. The human right to water implies free access to and use of surface or groundwater for human consumption, provided that it is not diverted from its course, no discharges are made, no alteration in its quality or significant decrease in its quantity occurs, and it does not affect the rights of third parties, in accordance with the limits and parameters established by the National Environmental Authority and the Single Water Authority. The Single Water Authority shall maintain a registry of the use of groundwater for human consumption.

Section One
Ecological Flow and Water Protection Areas

Art. 76.- Ecological flow. For the purposes of this Law, ecological flow is the quantity of water, expressed in terms of magnitude, duration, season, and frequency of the specific flow, and the quality of water expressed in terms of range, frequency, and duration of the concentration of parameters required to maintain an adequate level of health in the ecosystem.

The Single Water Authority, in coordination with the National Environmental Authority, shall establish by regulation the criteria, parameters, and methodologies for the determination of the ecological flow in accordance with the conditions and characteristics of the water bodies, which will be considered within the national water planning.

Any resolution of the Single Water Authority granting authorization for the productive use or utilization of water must establish and consider the ecological flow that was determined for it, in accordance with the criteria of the national water planning.

Art. 77.- Limitations and responsibilities. The ecological flow of the permanent water courses in any hydrographic basin is intangible.

It is the responsibility of the Single Water Authority, of the institutions, and of all persons, whether they are water users or not, to respect the required quantity and quality that protects aquatic biodiversity and the adjacent ecosystems.

All productive activities shall respect the ecological flow.

The defined ecological flow is not subject to authorization for its productive use or utilization, except for those uses that do not result in an affectation of the quality or quantity of the ecological flow.

The administrative authority that contravenes this provision shall be responsible for the environmental damages it generates and for the payment of compensation for damages and losses caused to affected third parties or to the natural heritage of the State; it shall also be sanctioned in accordance with the Law, without prejudice to the nullity of the granted authorization.

Only in the case of a declaration of a state of exception may the use of the ecological flow for human consumption be authorized, until emergency measures are adopted to guarantee the supply again.

Art. 78.- Water protection areas. Water protection areas are territories where water sources exist that are declared of public interest for their maintenance, conservation, and protection, which supply human consumption or guarantee food sovereignty; they shall form part of the National System of Protected Areas.

The Single Water Authority, upon a technical report issued by the National Environmental Authority and in coordination with the Decentralized Autonomous Governments within the scope of their competencies, shall establish and delimit the water protection areas that are necessary for the maintenance and conservation of the public water domain.

The use of the water protection areas shall be regulated by the State to guarantee their proper management. The protection regime established for the water protection areas shall respect the spiritual uses of peoples and nationalities. The procedure to establish these water protection areas shall be determined in the Regulations of this Law, provided they are not wetlands, forests, and protective vegetation.

Section Two
Objectives of Prevention and Control of Water Contamination

Art. 79.- Objectives of water prevention and conservation. The Single Water Authority, the National Environmental Authority, and the Decentralized Autonomous Governments will work in coordination to fulfill the following objectives:

a) To guarantee the human right to water for good living or sumak kawsay, the rights recognized to nature, and the preservation of all forms of life, in a healthy, ecologically balanced, and pollution-free environment;
b) To preserve the quantity of water and improve its quality;
c) To control and prevent the accumulation in soil and subsoil of toxic substances, waste, discharges, and other elements capable of contaminating surface or groundwater;
d) To control activities that may cause the degradation of water and of the aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems related to it, and when they are degraded, to order their restoration;
e) To prohibit, prevent, control, and sanction the contamination of waters through discharges or deposits of solid, liquid, and gaseous waste; organic, inorganic compounds, or any other toxic substance that alters the quality of the water or affects human health, fauna, flora, and the balance of life;
f) To guarantee the integral conservation and care of the delimited water sources and the balance of the hydrological cycle; and,
g) To prevent the degradation of ecosystems related to the hydrological cycle.

Art. 80.- Discharges: prohibitions and control. Discharges are considered to be the release of residual waters made directly or indirectly into the public water domain. The direct or indirect discharge of untreated residual waters, wastewater, and leachates capable of contaminating the waters of the public water domain is prohibited.

The National Environmental Authority will exercise control over discharges in coordination with the Single Water Authority and the Decentralized Autonomous Governments accredited in the single environmental management system.

It is the responsibility of the municipal autonomous governments to treat wastewater and solid waste to prevent water contamination in accordance with the law.

Art. 81.- Administrative authorization of discharges. The authorization to make discharges will be included in the environmental permits issued for this purpose. The parameters of the quality of the water to be discharged and the procedure for the granting, suspension, and revision of the authorization will be regulated by the National Environmental Authority or accredited, in coordination with the Single Water Authority.

The Decentralized Autonomous Governments within the scope of their competence and within their jurisdiction will issue the administrative discharge authorization provided for in this Law, subject to the public policies issued by the National Environmental Authority.

Art. 82.- Citizen participation and oversight. Individuals, peoples, nationalities, and social collectives may carry out processes of oversight, observatories, and other mechanisms of social control over the quality of water and the plans and programs for the prevention and control of contamination, in accordance with the Law.

CHAPTER VII
OBLIGATIONS OF THE STATE REGARDING THE HUMAN RIGHT TO WATER

Section One
Obligations and Progressivity

Art. 83.- Policies in relation to water. It is the obligation of the State to formulate and generate public policies aimed at:

a) Strengthening the sustainable management of water sources and ecosystems related to the water cycle;
b) Improving the infrastructure, the quality of water, and the coverage of water systems for human consumption and irrigation;
c) Establishing policies and measures that limit the advance of the agricultural frontier into water protection areas;
d) Strengthening the participation of communes, communities, peoples, and nationalities in water management;
e) Adopting and promoting measures with respect to adaptation and mitigation of climate change to protect the population at risk;
f) Fostering and incentivizing the efficient use and utilization of water, through the application of appropriate technologies in irrigation systems; and,
g) Promoting public-community alliances for the improvement of services and the optimization of water systems.

Art. 84.- Obligations of co-responsibility. The State at its different levels of government is co-responsible with users, consumers, communes, communities, peoples, and nationalities for the fulfillment of the following obligations:

a) To reduce unsustainable extraction, diversion, or damming of flows;
b) To prevent, reduce, and reverse water contamination;
c) To monitor and protect the declared reserves of optimal quality water;
d) To contribute to the analysis and study of the quality and availability of water;
e) To identify and promote technologies to improve efficiency in the use of water;
f) To reduce the waste of water during its capture, conduction, and distribution;
g) To adopt measures for the restoration of degraded ecosystems;
h) To support projects for the capture, storage, management, and rational, efficient, and sustainable use of water resources; and,
i) To develop and promote training, scientific, and technological research in the water sector.

Art. 85.- Progressivity and universality. The State and its institutions may not adopt policies or measures of a regressive nature that entail a significant restriction or worsening of the forms and conditions of access to water, or that signify an arbitrary limitation on the exercise of the human right to water.

By reason of its universal character, the State shall promote joint regional strategies for the conservation of water, within the framework of the international agreements to which the State is a party.

Section Two
Water Uses

Art. 86.- Water and its priority. In accordance with the constitutional provision, the order of priority among the different destinations or functions of water is:

a) Human consumption;
b) Irrigation that guarantees food sovereignty;
c) Ecological flow; and,
d) Productive activities.

Water for irrigation that guarantees food sovereignty includes the watering of animals, aquaculture, and other activities of domestic agricultural food production, in accordance with the Regulations of this Law.

Art. 87.- Types and terms of authorizations. The granting, suspension, or cancellation of authorizations is the competence of the Single Water Authority. Authorizations, according to the nature of their destination, are classified as:

Authorizations for water use. This is the administrative act issued by the Single Water Authority by which it favorably attends to a request presented by natural or legal persons for the use of a water flow destined for human consumption or irrigation that guarantees food sovereignty, including also the watering of animals and aquaculture production activities in the form and conditions provided for in this Law.

Authorizations for the productive utilization of water. This is the administrative act issued by the Single Water Authority by which it favorably attends to a request presented by natural or legal persons for the productive utilization of a water flow destined for any of the economic utilizations in the form and conditions provided for in this Law.

Authorizations by their duration are classified as:

a) Authorizations for human consumption: the term shall be twenty years, renewable for successive equal periods. These authorizations may be modified in relation to demographic and flow variations;
b) Authorization for irrigation, aquaculture, and watering of animals to guarantee food sovereignty: these shall be granted for a term not exceeding ten years, renewable for an equal period;
c) Authorizations of a determined term for productive activities not considered in food sovereignty: these shall be granted for a term of up to ten years, renewable for equal or longer periods depending on the investment time of the productive activity, provided that it is included in the National Development Plan. The Single Water Authority may, in accordance with water planning and national interest, modify with justification the terms determined in this article; and,
d) Occasional authorizations granted for a term not exceeding two years, non-renewable, for surplus or remaining resources.

The aforementioned authorizations shall be regulated in the Regulations to this Law.

Art. 88.- Use. Use of water is understood as its utilization in basic activities essential for life, such as human consumption, irrigation, aquaculture, and the watering of animals to guarantee food sovereignty in the terms established in this Law.

Art. 89.- Authorization of use. The use of water in accordance with the definition in the preceding article shall have the respective authorization granted in conformity with this Law, its Regulations, and water planning.

The authorization for the use of water for human consumption and irrigation for food sovereignty, watering of animals, and aquaculture confers upon the user, exclusively, the capacity for the capture, treatment, conduction, and utilization of the flow to which the authorization refers.

Art. 90.- Conditions for the granting of authorizations for water use. Prior to the granting of authorizations for water use, the Single Water Authority shall verify compliance with the following conditions:

a) That the order of priority established in the Constitution and this Law is respected;
b) That the availability of water in sufficient quality and quantity has been certified. With respect to water quality, the Single Water Authority will progressively implement the certification processes;
c) That the studies and projects of hydraulic infrastructure necessary for its utilization have been previously approved by the Single Water Authority;
d) That the beneficiary takes responsibility for the prevention and mitigation of environmental damages caused, and is obliged to contribute to the proper management of the authorized water; and,
e) That the utilization of the water be immediate or within a determined period for the destination for which it was authorized, in accordance with the respective technical report.

Art. 91.- Recreational and sporting use. Recreational events and aquatic competitions that involve a non-consumptive use of water will not require the prior authorization of the Single Water Authority.

Art. 92.- Cultural and sacred practices. The Single Water Authority shall guarantee the integrity and permanence of the places where communes, communities, peoples, and nationalities traditionally practice rites, cultural values, and sacred uses of water.

The Single Water Authority, jointly with the communes, communities, peoples, and nationalities, shall carry out and maintain a duly updated participatory and integral National Inventory of the sacred places and rituals of water.

The administration and conservation of sacred places in relation to water shall be carried out by the entities or organizations of peoples and nationalities in whose lands or territories they are located, with the support of national programs and projects of public bodies and the Decentralized Autonomous Governments, in accordance with the Constitution and their own rights.

Section Three
Conditions for Authorization for Utilization

Art. 93.- Definition. The productive utilization of water constitutes activities such as irrigation for the popular and solidarity economy, agro-industry, agricultural production or aquaculture production for export, or other productive activities such as tourism, generation of hydroelectricity, industrial production; mining exploitation and refining of minerals; hydrocarbons, bottling and commercialization of mineral, medicinal, treated, enriched waters, or those with certified purification and quality processes; and other productive activities that involve the utilization of water.

For the productive utilization of water, the administrative authorization granted by the Single Water Authority shall be required, upon request in accordance with water planning, the requirements, and conditions established by this Law.

The utilization of water for productive activities includes its use in activities not considered for food sovereignty, according to the definition of this Law, when it concerns agricultural or aquaculture production.

In the other productive activities that utilize water, the destination of the production to the domestic or external market is irrelevant.

The authorization for the utilization of water in productive activities confers upon its holder, exclusively, the capacity for the capture, treatment, conduction, and utilization of the flow to which the authorization refers. The holder must install, at their own expense, water flow measurement devices in the terms defined by the Single Water Authority.

Art. 94.- Order of priority for productive activities. Among the activities susceptible to water utilization, the following order of priority shall apply:

a) Irrigation for agricultural, aquaculture, and agro-industrial production for export;
b) Touristic activities;
c) Generation of hydroelectricity and hydrothermal energy;
d) Projects in strategic and industrial sectors;
e) Balneotherapy, bottling of mineral, medicinal, treated, or enriched waters; and,
f) Other productive activities.

The order of priority for productive activities may be modified by the Single Water Authority, in attention to the characteristics of the basin, within the framework of the objectives and guidelines of the national water planning and the National Development Plan.

Art. 95.- Conditions for the authorization of productive utilization of water. The authorization for the productive utilization of water shall be subordinate to compliance with the following conditions:

a) Respect for the precedence of uses and the priority of productive utilizations of water, under the conditions of publicity and competition determined in this Law;
b) Verification of the certain existence of water, in sufficient quality and quantity, based on the certification of availability. Regarding the quality of the water, the Single Water Authority will progressively implement the certification processes;
c) Studies and projects of hydraulic infrastructure necessary for the utilization of the water, which are previously approved by the Single Water Authority;
d) That the user be responsible for the prevention and mitigation of environmental damages that they cause and be obliged to contribute to the proper management of the authorized water; and,
e) That the utilization of the water be immediate, or within a determined period for the destination for which it was authorized.

The Single Water Authority shall develop these conditions in the Regulations of this Law.

Art. 96.- Prohibition of transfer. The authorization for the use and utilization of water is non-transferable, with the exception of succession by reason of death, provided that the destination for which the respective authorization was granted is maintained.

In the case of transfer of land ownership or change of ownership of the productive initiative, provided that the destination for which the authorization for water use was granted is maintained, the new owner, if applicable, shall update the authorization, complying with the requirements set forth in the Regulations. Once the authorization is granted, it shall be registered in the public water registry.

Art. 97.- Water deficit. In the event of a decrease in flows due to temporary or permanent scarcity, the water shall be delivered to the users of the current authorizations in proportion to the available volume and respecting the order of precedence indicated in this Law, by means of notification from the Single Water Authority.

CHAPTER VIII
EASEMENTS

Art. 98.- Types of easement. In the matter of water, there are two types of easements:

a) Natural.- Those which without human intervention cause a property to benefit from the water that crosses or is on another property; and,
b) Compulsory.- Every property is subject to an easement of aqueduct and its related components, such as capture, construction of damming works, extraction, conduction, drainage, passage, and surveillance, which includes their respective technical studies, channeling, and defense of the margins and banks in favor of another property that lacks the necessary water, as ordered by the respective authorities.

The Single Water Authority shall authorize the occupation of lands for the execution of the works referred to in this article, as well as the modifications of any easement in accordance with what is established in this Law.

Furthermore, it shall authorize the occupation of lands for the execution of the works and shall include the determination of special protection strips for public or community aqueducts referred to in this article.

The holder of the easement of aqueduct and its related components shall indemnify the holder of the property based on the damages caused by the establishment of the easement.

The holder of the servient property does not acquire any right over the works carried out within their property. They may use them only for domestic use and for watering animals, provided they do not destroy them, cause contamination, or affect the rights of third parties. Non-compliance with this norm will be subject to the sanctions regulated by the Law.

In case of subdivision of the servient property, the necessary easements for the use of the water shall be maintained.

The easements established in favor of State institutions, in addition to being compulsory, shall be preferential.

Art. 99.- Right of the owner of the servient property. The holder of the property has the right for the Single Water Authority to order the remediation of leaks, spills, or any other damage attributable to defects in construction, conservation, operation, and preservation.

Art. 100.- Prohibited activities. The Single Water Authority and the Decentralized Autonomous Governments, within the scope of their competencies, shall not authorize agricultural or construction activities and, in general, new works in the lateral spaces of the aqueduct or conduits of drinking water systems.

The owners of the servient properties are prohibited from grazing animals next to the open ditch or aqueducts that cross their lands, or from discharging waste or contaminated water in the water protection zones.

Forestry activities are prohibited in the servient area or in the water protection zones when the aqueducts are piped or enclosed.

Art. 101.- Use of the waters that flow through the servient property. The holder of the servient property does not acquire any right or authorization over the waters that flow through the servient property but may use them only for domestic purposes, without diverting them, contaminating them, or affecting the rights of third parties. Non-compliance with this norm shall be sanctioned in accordance with the Law.

Art. 102.- Extinction of easements. Easements are extinguished in the following cases:

a) Failure to execute the works ordered by the competent authority within the established period;
b) Unjustified lack of use for more than one year;
c) Fulfillment of the object for which the easement was authorized;
d) Different utilization from the authorized use; and,
e) Upon expiration of the term in the case of a temporary easement.

Art. 103.- Effects of extinction. Once the easement is declared extinguished, the assets that were affected by it revert to the exclusive property and use of the servient property.

Art. 104.- Indemnities. Indemnities for damages and losses derived from the constitution of an easement shall be processed before the civil judge in accordance with the norms established in the Law.

The value fixed as indemnity shall be delivered to the owner or legitimate possessor of the property for the relevant legal effects.

TITLE IV
UTILIZATION OF WATER

CHAPTER I
TYPES OF PRODUCTIVE UTILIZATION

Section One
Bottled Water

Art. 105.- On the utilization of water for bottling. The bottling of water for human consumption is a productive utilization consisting of the processing, potabilization treatment, or purification of waters captured from natural surface or groundwater sources, carried out through certified technical procedures.

The authorization of use for the utilization of water for bottling, captured directly from the natural surface or groundwater source, shall have a differentiated tariff that will be determined by the Single Water Authority, according to the volume of water captured. This authorization, granted by the Single Water Authority, shall be a mandatory requirement to carry out the activities of bottling, production, and commercialization of the product.

This utilization may be carried out by natural persons, legal entities, public, private, community, and mixed entities, as well as by organizations of the popular and solidarity economy, by themselves or in alliance with the Decentralized Autonomous Governments or the community water management systems.

The applications presented by community entities or those of the popular and solidarity economy, holders of collective rights, for the utilization of water in their territories or community lands, shall have preferential right in the granting of new authorizations.

The bottling of any type of water from public or community supply systems is prohibited. Exceptionally, it may only be carried out with the prior authorization of the Single Water Authority, the corresponding National Health Authority, and the Municipal Decentralized Autonomous Government, provided it is guaranteed that it has been subjected to the process of treatment, purification, or enrichment according to technical norms. The entity that administers the supply system shall establish a differentiated tariff according to the volume of processed water.

Section Two
Energy and Industrial Utilization of Water

Art. 106.- Principles and priorities for hydroelectric productive utilization. Within the framework of respecting the order of priority regulated in this Law, the Single Water Authority shall grant authorizations for the productive utilization of water for the generation of electricity, preferentially for those projects of national priority that are contemplated in the master plan for electrification, incorporating the principles of environmental sustainability, precaution, prevention, and efficiency.

Art. 107.- Industrial utilization. For any industrial activity in which water from water sources is used, the authorization for productive utilization must be requested from the Single Water Authority.

Industries that capture water from drinking water supply networks for productive utilization shall obtain from the decentralized autonomous government the authorization for the connection, which must be registered with the Single Water Authority.

Persons engaged in the extraction of salt from seawater for commercial purposes must obtain from the Single Water Authority the respective authorization for productive utilization of the water and pay the corresponding tariff.

The waters intended for industrial utilization, once used, shall be discharged by the user, after their treatment, complying with the technical parameters dictated by the National Environmental Authority.

Section Three
Aquaculture

Art. 108.- Utilization of water in aquaculture. Persons engaged in any fish farming or aquaculture activity, which is not considered included in food sovereignty in the terms regulated in this Law, must obtain from the corresponding public authority the necessary permits for the exercise of their activity, which, prior to granting them, must request from the Single Water Authority the reports regarding the productive utilization of the water, which will cause the payment of the tariffs established in this Law, when it is consumptive.

Art. 109.- Prohibition. Authorizations for the productive utilization of water in mangroves will not be granted. Only those who comply with the regularization process established in the Regulations may obtain and renew said authorization for aquaculture activities.

Section Four
Utilization of Water in Mining

Art. 110.- Authorization of utilization. Mining activities must have the authorization for the productive utilization of the waters they use, which will be granted by the Single Water Authority, in accordance with the procedures and requirements established in this Law and its Regulations, for which the order of priority established by the Constitution will be strictly respected, that is, human consumption, irrigation that guarantees food sovereignty, ecological flow, and productive activities. For this purpose, it will coordinate with the National Environmental Authority.

All activity that may affect the quality and quantity of water, and the balance of ecosystems, especially in the sources and recharge zones of water, will be regulated.

The sustainability of ecosystems and human consumption will be priorities in the use and utilization of water.

Authorization for the use of water for human consumption in camps must also be obtained.

Art. 111.- Protection in water sources. The Single Water Authority and the National Environmental Authority will issue the necessary regulations to guarantee the conservation and balance of ecosystems, especially of the sources and recharge zones of water.

The National Environmental Authority will coordinate with the Single Water Authority the monitoring of the environmental management system provided for in the respective environmental license issued by it.

Art. 112.- Return of waters. The water destined for mining activities will be returned to the original channel from which it was taken or to the channel that is more adequate, with the obligation of the user to treat it before its discharge and discharge, in accordance with what is established by the environmental permit and the Law, which will guarantee safe conditions that do not affect the freshwater aquifers in the subsoil, water sources for human consumption, irrigation, or watering places.

Section Five
Utilization of Water in Hydrocarbon Activities

Art. 113.- Authorization. The productive utilization of water in hydrocarbon activities in the national territory will require the authorization of the Single Water Authority, respecting the constitutional order of priority, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and its Regulations.

Authorization for the use of water for human consumption in camps must also be obtained.

Art. 114.- Return of waters. For the disposal of liquid waste by means of injection, the respective environmental permit must be obtained beforehand, which will guarantee safe conditions that do not affect the freshwater aquifers in the subsoil, water sources for human consumption, irrigation, or watering places.

Section Six
Touristic and Thermal Utilization

Art. 115.- Touristic utilization of water. The water used in permanent recreational tourist activities must have the authorization for productive utilization granted by the Single Water Authority, in accordance with the requirements, conditions, and procedures established in this Law and its Regulations. For this purpose, the Single Water Authority will coordinate with the National Tourism Authority.

Art. 116.- Thermal waters. Thermal waters may be productively utilized by natural persons, legal entities, public, private, mixed, or of the popular and solidarity economy; communes, communities, peoples, and nationalities.

The applications presented by community entities or those of the popular and solidarity economy, holders of collective rights, for the utilization of water in their territories or community lands, will have a preferential right in the granting of new authorizations.

The National Environmental Authority will exercise control of discharges in coordination with the Single Water Authority and the Decentralized Autonomous Governments accredited in the single environmental management system.

CHAPTER II
USE AND UTILIZATION OF GROUNDWATER AND AQUIFERS

Art. 117.- Use and utilization. For the exploration and surfacing of groundwater, the respective license granted by the Single Water Authority must be obtained. In case of finding them, authorization for their use or productive utilization will be required, subject to the following requirements:

a) That its surfacing does not harm the conditions of the aquifer, the quality of the water, or the surface area included in the radius of influence of the well or gallery; and,
b) That it does not cause interference with other wells, galleries, or water sources, and in general, with other pre-existing surfacings.

For this purpose, the Single Water Authority will require from the applicant for its use or utilization, the presentation of the pertinent studies that justify the fulfillment of the indicated conditions, the details and parameters of which will be established in the Regulations of this Law.

Art. 118.- Co-responsibility in the conservation of groundwater. Community systems, drinking water boards, irrigation boards, and water users are co-responsible with the State for the protection, conservation, and management of groundwater.

Art. 119.- Exploration and surfacing licenses. Licenses to carry out exploration and surfacing works of groundwater may be granted on third-party lands provided that the destination is for use to meet human consumption and irrigation needs for food sovereignty. The owners will have priority to obtain authorization for the use or utilization of the surplus.

Authorizations for the use or utilization of surfaced groundwater will be granted based on the quality of the aquifer's water and its replacement speed, in accordance with the provisions of the Regulations of this Law.

Art. 120.- Inspection of exploitations. The Single Water Authority will inspect the groundwater exploitations to verify compliance with the guidelines and conditions established in the corresponding authorization.

At any time, this authority will order, ex officio or at the request of a party, the modifications of the methods, systems, and installations for surfacing or sustainable utilization of groundwater that do not conform to the parameters established by regulation.

Art. 121.- Obligation to inform. Natural or legal persons who, during their productive activities, drill the soil and surface groundwater, will be obliged to notify the Single Water Authority immediately and to provide the location, studies, and technical data they obtain about them and to apply the precautionary and preventive measures dictated by said authority.

Art. 122.- Other forms of utilization. The authorization for productive utilization for other destinations will be granted by the Single Water Authority, based on the technical studies established in the Law and other applicable regulations.

CHAPTER III
PROCEDURAL RULES FOR WATER USE AND CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Section One
Administrative Procedure to Regulate the Use or Utilization of Water

Art. 123.- Determination of jurisdiction. The Single Water Authority exercises national jurisdiction in matters of water resources, and by delegation, the administrative authority in the respective jurisdiction.

The Single Water Authority at its deconcentrated levels will exercise the administrative competence to hear, process, and resolve, in the first instance, the petitions for the granting of authorizations for the use or utilization of water, as well as to order their registration, mediation, and resolution of conflicts, without prejudice to collective rights.

Art. 124.- Norms of administrative procedure. The administrative procedure contemplated in this Chapter shall be subject to the norms established in this Law and in the Statute of the Administrative Legal Regime of the Executive Function.

Art. 125.- Of the initial petition. Applications for authorizations for the use or utilization of water or the constitution of easements shall be made before the Single Water Authority, which shall inform the basin council.

The Single Water Authority shall qualify and accept the processing of the application based on the water balance of the basin, within the legal terms; it shall issue the respective resolution that grants or denies the requested authorization, which shall be inscribed in the public water registry and published on the official website of the Single Water Authority. Notifications, opposition, evidence, expert opinions, and required technical reports shall be regulated in the Regulations of this Law.

The Single Water Authority shall issue its duly reasoned pronouncement within a maximum period of three months from the date of receipt of the application for use, and an additional three months in case of objections or opposition, and three months from the delivery of the projects. In the case of applications for utilization, non-compliance shall be sanctioned in accordance with the Law.

Art. 126.- On the principles of publicity and competition. For the procedure of granting authorizations for the use and productive utilization of water, the principles of publicity and competition shall be applied in accordance with the following actions:

a) When a water authorization is requested, it must be made public and disseminated so that users and interested parties in the utilization of the waters to which the request refers may present their opposition, petitions, adhesions, or alternative projects;
b) When several applications have been presented within the granted period, the Single Water Authority shall decide among them by applying the order of priority established in this Law and taking into account, as a preliminary point, the non-existence of a water deficit. When the applications refer to the same level in the order of priority, the decision shall be made based on the best social, economic, or environmental utility of each application, the decision being expressly motivated;
c) When only one application has been presented, the decision shall be made based on the existence, or not, of a water deficit, in the place where the capture and utilization are to take place; and,
d) When a water deficit exists, the authorization in favor of an applicant for a utilization that is lower in the established order of priority may be cancelled or modified if the applicant so requests and the Single Water Authority considers it in accordance with this Law, its Regulations, and the water planning. The costs of the indemnity to the person whose authorization is cancelled or modified shall be borne by the beneficiary of the administrative act.

The matters regulated in this article shall be developed in the Regulations to this Law.

Art. 127.- Renewal and modification. The renewal and modification of authorizations for productive utilizations of water shall be carried out in the following terms:

Authorizations for the productive utilization of water may be renewed upon their expiration, provided that the requirements established in the Regulations, the obligations established by this Law, and the conditions provided in the respective authorization have been met.

When a user requires to increase or decrease the authorized flow for the same use and utilization, the modification of the authorization proceeds, provided that there is water availability and the priority established in the Constitution is not altered.

Art. 128.- Grounds for reversion, suspension, or ex officio modification of an authorization. The Single Water Authority shall revert, suspend, or modify ex officio the authorization for the use and utilization of water when it verifies that the holder has incurred in one of the following grounds:

a) Suspension of the authorization:
1.- Non-compliance with the period provided for in the Law or in the approved technical study and project, for the initiation of the use or utilization of water or the construction of the hydraulic infrastructure; and,
2.- For suspension of the environmental license. The suspension shall be maintained for the period set by the authority to remedy the non-compliance.

b) Reversion of the authorization:
1.- For non-compliance with the conditions established in the authorization;
2.- For total or partial lack of utilization of the flows granted in the authorization; and,
3.- For revocation of the environmental license.

In cases where the proven decrease in flows is manifest and permanent, the Single Water Authority shall proceed, if applicable, to modify the authorization.

The administrative procedure establishes the summons to a preliminary hearing of the holder of the authorization, in accordance with the provisions of the Regulations of this Law.

Art. 129.- Hoarding of water. It is the disposition or retention, by any means, of a flow or flows of water for productive use and utilization in quantities greater than necessary, which prejudices third parties.

The Single Water Authority, based on a technical study that guarantees efficiency in use and management, shall determine in each case the existence or non-existence of hoarding.

In the case of hoarding of water for use and utilization, the Single Water Authority, ex officio or at the request of a party, shall resolve the cancellation of the authorizations in a specific jurisdiction. Subsequently, it shall proceed to reassign the previously authorized water, in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Art. 130.- Redistribution and reassignment of water. The authority shall proceed to the reassignment of the reverted flows, in attention to the guarantee of the human right to water, irrigation for food sovereignty, and to effectuate socially equitable access to the use and productive utilization of water. The reassignment shall be dictated by administrative act, based on technical, efficiency, social, economic, and environmental criteria and the respective basin council.

The Single Water Authority, ex officio or at the request of a party, shall also proceed to the reassignment of water obtained without authorization or in case of non-compliance with the conditions of the authorization.

Authorizations for irrigation water that guarantee food sovereignty, granted to community systems, holders of collective rights, may only be temporarily suspended until the cause that originated the suspension is remedied.

The Single Water Authority shall proceed to the reassignment of water with the application of the principles of competition and publicity, starting from the order of priority and affirmative actions of collective rights in favor of their holders, in accordance with the Constitution and the Law.

Art. 131.- Control of authorizations. The authorizations for the use and utilization of water, granted by the Single Water Authority, shall be controlled by the Regulation and Control Agency.

Art. 132.- Construction of infrastructure for productive utilization of water. The holder of an authorization for the use and utilization of water for productive activities shall be obliged to construct the works for capture, conduction, utilization, measurement, and control so that only the authorized water flow flows, without being able to be modified or destroyed when the authorization period concludes. The hydraulic works that comply with the technical specifications and designs shall be approved by the Single Water Authority within a period of sixty days.

The holder of an authorization for the use and utilization of water who does not use the authorized flow must notify the Single Water Authority to proceed with its cancellation; otherwise, they shall be sanctioned in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Section Two
Conflict Resolution

Art. 133.- Conflict resolution. Conflicts and controversies between holders of authorizations may be processed and resolved in a specialized administrative dependency for alternative conflict resolution.

Art. 134.- Mediation and arbitration. The holders of authorizations for the use or utilization of water, in accordance with the provisions of the law that regulates the systems of arbitration and mediation, without prejudice to opting for the administrative procedure, may submit their controversies to mediation or arbitration processes in legally established centers in the jurisdiction where the water object of the conflict is located.

Direct agreements and arbitral awards that resolve the controversies must be notified by the corresponding courts to the Single Water Authority for their inscription in the public water registry.

In case the disagreements persist after having exhausted the administrative procedures and alternative conflict resolution mechanisms, the parties in conflict shall submit to the jurisdictional route.

CHAPTER IV
ECONOMIC REGIME

Section One
Tariffs

Art. 135.- General criteria for water tariffs. A tariff is understood as the retribution that a user must pay for the provision of services and authorization for the uses and utilization of water.

For the purposes of protection, conservation of the basins, and financing of the costs of the related services, the corresponding tariffs shall be established, according to the principles of this Law, the criteria, and technical parameters indicated in the Regulations.

The tariffs for the authorization of the use and utilization of water shall be regulated and set by the Single Water Authority.

The tariffs for the provision of drinking water, sanitation, irrigation, and drainage services shall be set by the public or community providers respectively, based on the regulations issued by the Single Water Authority through the Regulation and Control Agency.

Art. 136.- General principles for the setting of water tariffs. In the establishment of tariffs for the authorization of the use and utilization of water, as well as for the services of drinking water, sanitation, and the services of irrigation and drainage, the principles of solidarity, equity, sustainability, and periodicity must be considered.

Art. 137.- Tariff component for water conservation. The Single Water Authority, as part of the tariffs for the authorization of use and utilization and for the water service, will contemplate a component for the conservation of the public water domain with priority in sources and water recharge zones.

The Decentralized Autonomous Governments, within the scope of their competencies, shall establish components in the tariffs of the public domiciliary services linked to water to finance the conservation of the public water domain with priority in sources and water recharge zones.

Art. 138.- Tariff for the authorization of use and utilization of raw water. The tariffs for the authorization of use and utilization of raw water shall be based on the following criteria:

a) Application to all uses and utilizations of water;
b) Differentiation according to the volume and type of use or utilization of water, considering the criteria that will be established for them in the Regulations;
c) Periodic review; and,
d) Contribution in the operation and maintenance of multipurpose works.

The tariffs shall be approved by the Single Water Authority based on the technical studies determined for this purpose, in application of what is prescribed in this Law and in the Regulations.

Art. 139.- Tariff for basic public services. Basic public services shall be understood as those of drinking water supply, sanitation, irrigation, and drainage.

The competence to set the tariffs corresponds to the public providers of said services or to the community entities that legitimately provide them, based on the regulations of the Single Water Authority.

The establishment of the tariffs shall attend to the following criteria:

a) Proportional inclusion of what the holder of the service must pay to the Single Water Authority for the supply of raw water; and,
b) Proportional inclusion of the cost of capture, management, pumping, conduction, operation, treatment, administration, depreciation of assets, amortization, distribution, environmental sanitation, and new investments for the supply of water.

In any case, the tariffs for the services shall be differentiated and shall consider the socioeconomic situation of persons with lower incomes and the disability condition of the consumers.

Section Two
Tariffs for Use

Art. 140.- Tariff for the supply of raw water for human and domestic consumption. The delivery of the minimum vital quantity of raw water established by the Single Water Authority for the provision of drinking water services shall not be subject to any tariff.

When the volume delivered to the service providers exceeds the determined minimum vital quantity, the corresponding tariff shall be applied, in accordance with the provisions of this Law and its Regulations.

Art. 141.- Tariff for the authorization of water use for irrigation that guarantees food sovereignty. The criteria for setting the volumetric water tariff for irrigation that guarantees food sovereignty are the following:

a) Volume used;
b) Quantity of cultivated land and type of soil; and,
c) Contribution to the conservation of the water resource.

Community systems that are holders of collective rights and community service providers that receive flows of less than five liters per second and are linked to production for food sovereignty are exempt from the payment of this tariff.

Section Three
Tariffs for Productive Utilization

Art. 142.- Tariffs for the productive utilization of water. The tariffs for productive utilization shall consider the following criteria:

a) Volume used;
b) Efficiency of utilization;
c) Contribution to the conservation of the water resource; and,
d) Generation of employment.

Art. 143.- Tariff for the utilization of water in electricity generation. The productive utilization for the generation of electricity and the utilization of hydrothermal energy shall have a tariff that will be established by the Single Water Authority, based on the regulations issued by the Water Regulation and Control Agency.

Art. 144.- Tariff for productive utilization for the popular and solidarity economy. The Single Water Authority, based on the regulations issued by the Regulation and Control Agency, shall set the tariff for the popular and solidarity economy in attention to the capacity and productivity of the authorized parties.

Art. 145.- Tariff for the authorization of discharges. The authorization of discharges will generate the annual payment of a tariff, which will be set based on technical criteria established by the National Environmental Authority, which will be in charge of its control and collection.

Art. 146.- Hydraulic infrastructure for the utilization of water. The investments required for the construction of hydraulic or civil infrastructure for a specific productive utilization of water shall be made by the interested party directly.

In attention to the national interest, the State may also participate as an investor, in accordance with the priorities established in the National Development Plan.

Art. 147.- Coactive jurisdiction. The Single Water Authority, the National Environmental Authority, and the public service providers shall exercise coactive jurisdiction for the collection of tariffs and other pending concepts and obligations, established in this Law and in its Regulations.

TITLE V
INFRACTIONS, SANCTIONS, AND RESPONSIBILITIES

CHAPTER I
INFRACTIONS

Art. 148.- Procedure. The processing of the administrative procedure for the knowledge and sanction of the administrative infractions established in this Law is governed by the norms of this Chapter.

Art. 149.- Sanctioning competence. The knowledge and sanction of the infractions of the provisions of this Law or its Regulations, provided that the act does not constitute a crime or contravention, are the competence of the Single Water Authority and the Regulation and Control Agency, in the manner established in this Law and in its Regulations. In those infractions that, in accordance with this Law, must be determined by the National Environmental Authority or by the National Health Authority, their final resolution will be required, in the common administrative procedure, before dictating the sanction by the Single Water Authority or the Regulation and Control Agency, as appropriate.

Art. 150.- Classification of infractions. The administrative infractions contemplated in this Law are classified as minor, serious, and very serious.

Art. 151.- Administrative infractions in matters of water resources. The administrative infractions in matters of water resources are the following:

a) Minor infractions:

Causing the flooding of third-party lands and public roads, when the responsibility lies with the user; and,

Placing obstacles at the bottom of the channels or other devices to raise the water level.

b) Serious infractions:

Modifying without authorization the surroundings of the water sources from which human consumption or irrigation is supplied;

When persons who do not belong to the community impede the application of customary law in matters of access and distribution of water for human consumption or irrigation in the territories of the communes, peoples, and nationalities; and,

Not paying annually the volumetric tariff established by the authority for the use and utilization of water.

c) Very serious infractions:

Carrying out works of capture, conduction, distribution, without having the respective authorization;

Altering or modifying the public water domain, without having the corresponding authorization;

Modifying the soil and soil conditions in the water protection zones and areas, without having the corresponding authorization;

Accessing and capturing individually or collectively, without legal authorization, water for any use or utilization;

Breaching technical norms that contravene the authorized use and utilization of water resources;

Modifying the banks and beds of the courses and bodies of water, without having the authorization of the competent authority;

Obstructing the natural flow of the waters or modifying their course, without having the authorization of the Single Water Authority;

Breaching the technical norms adopted by the Single Water Authority to guarantee water security;

Discharging contaminated water without treatment or contaminating substances into the public water domain;

Accumulating solid waste, debris, heavy metals, or substances that may contaminate the public water domain, the soil, or the environment, without observing technical prescriptions;

Obstructing water conduction lines intended for irrigation and flood control; breaking, altering, or destroying aqueducts and sewerage;

Selling or transferring the ownership of the authorizations for the use and utilization of water; and,

Fraudulently using the organizational forms proper to the community water management systems to conceal their privatization.

Art. 152.- Recidivism and aggravating factors. Recidivism is the reiteration in the commission of an infraction of the same class, by the same person within a period of one year counted from the commission of the first. Recidivism shall be considered an aggravating factor by the Single Water Authority at the time of imposing the sanction.

Art. 153.- Sanctioning procedure for administrative infractions. The initiation of the sanctioning procedure for the administrative infractions referred to in this Title proceeds by complaint of any person in the exercise of their rights or ex officio by decision of the Single Water Authority.

Art. 154.- On due process. In all cases, due process shall be complied with, in a way that guarantees the exercise of the legitimate defense through the execution of administrative acts that ensure the notification and knowledge of the complaint; submission of evidence, investigation, expert opinions, legal and technical valuation, and a motivated resolution, among others, in accordance with the Law and the Regulations.

Art. 155.- Preventive measures. The Single Water Authority may adopt preventive measures in relation to the event that gave rise to the administrative sanctioning procedure, which shall be determined in the Regulations to this Law.

Art. 156.- Unattended complaints. In the case of complaints for infractions of this Law that are not attended to by the Single Water Authority within the period provided for in the Regulations, the responsible official shall be sanctioned in accordance with the Law.

Art. 157.- Resolution. The resolution of the administrative file shall be dictated by the authority in charge of it and shall be duly motivated.

This resolution may be challenged in the administrative sphere, before the Single Water Authority, through the resources established in the Statute of the Administrative Legal Regime of the Executive Function.

The Single Water Authority shall order the inscription of the sanctioning resolution in the public water registry, and its compliance shall be mandatory.

Art. 158.- Termination of the procedure. The resolution, the withdrawal, and the declaration of abandonment shall put an end to the administrative sanctioning procedure.

Art. 159.- Legal responsibility. In addition to the administrative sanction, the Single Water Authority has the obligation to file the corresponding civil action to obtain payment for damages and losses from the responsible party; likewise, if applicable, it shall file the complaint with the Prosecutor's Office, so that the corresponding actions may be initiated.

CHAPTER II
SANCTIONS

Art. 160.- Sanctions. The infractions determined in this Law shall be sanctioned with:

a) A fine;
b) Suspension of the authorization for the use and productive utilization of water; and,
c) Cancellation of the authorization for the use and productive utilization of water.

In the case of concurrence of infractions, the sanction corresponding to the most serious of those committed shall be applied.

In the case of infractions whose knowledge also corresponds to the National Environmental Authority, the sanctioning procedure shall be coordinated.

The authority may impose, as a precautionary measure, the suspension of the authorization for the use or productive utilization of water during the corresponding administrative process.

Art. 161.- Remediation. The sanctioning resolution shall also order the remediation that is appropriate; in case of non-compliance, the Single Water Authority shall assume the remediation and proceed to seek recovery from the infringer, for the total value assumed with a surcharge of up to 20%, without prejudice to the actions for damages and losses that may be appropriate.

Art. 162.- Fines. In the corresponding sanctioning resolution, the Single Water Authority shall apply a fine in accordance with the following scale:

a) In the case of minor infractions, a fine of between one to ten unified basic salaries of the general worker shall be applied;
b) In the case of serious infractions, a fine of between eleven to fifty unified basic salaries of the general worker shall be applied; and,
c) In the case of very serious infractions, a fine of between fifty-one to one hundred and fifty unified basic salaries of the general worker shall be applied.

Art. 163.- Responsibility in cases of inactivity or expiration. The inactivity or expiration of an authorization for the productive use and utilization of water does not exempt the holder from the responsibilities that may derive from the affectation of the public water domain.

GENERAL PROVISIONS

FIRST.- It is the responsibility of the provincial decentralized autonomous governments to plan, construct, operate, and maintain the irrigation and drainage systems in application of national policies and planning guidelines, and technical parameters defined by the Single Water Authority.

The public irrigation service also includes agricultural drainage activities.

SECOND.- The Single Water Authority shall issue the regulations that establish the technical parameters for the evaluation and determination of the infractions, sanctions, and fines prescribed in this Law.

THIRD.- The State, at all its levels of government, shall assign in its annual budget the necessary financial resources for the recovery and restoration of the hydrographic basins and infrastructure that guarantee the preservation and conservation of the quality and water supply, especially for the community systems and irrigation boards, within the public-community alliance established in the Constitution.

TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS

FIRST.- For the fulfillment of what is prescribed in the twenty-seventh transitional provision of the Constitution and subject to what is established in this Law, the Single Water Authority, within a period of up to three hundred and sixty days from the promulgation of this Law in the Official Registry, shall proceed to review the concessions of rights of use and utilization of water in the basins, granted under the previous Law, in order to identify cases of hoarding, concentration, or accumulation of water concessions for irrigation, and shall issue the resolution of affectation of the indicated concessions and shall order its marginal notation in the corresponding inscription in the public water registry.

The Single Water Authority, within a period of up to one hundred and eighty days, counted from the finalization of the period provided for in the preceding paragraph, shall proceed to initiate and resolve the corresponding individualized files for the cancellation, modification, or expiration of the authorizations or concessions previously declared affected by the review, following for this purpose the procedure and the periods established in this Law and in its Regulations.

SECOND.- The concessions and authorizations of the right of use and utilization of water, granted before the entry into force of this Law and inscribed in the public water registry, shall be replaced in accordance with the provisions of this provision by authorizations for the use and utilization of water, within one year, extendable for one more year from the publication of this Law in the Official Registry.

For the substitution, the cancellation of the owed tariffs is required. For this purpose, the Single Water Authority shall issue the certification of the debt for liquidation.

Once the indicated period has concluded, the rights of water concession shall expire.

The substitution of concessions for authorizations of use and utilization of water does not exclude or limit the power of the Single Water Authority for their review.

THIRD.- The informal uses or utilizations of water that occurred before the entry into force of this Law may be regularized exceptionally, within a maximum period of one year, extendable for one more year, from the promulgation of this Law in the Official Registry, in accordance with the regulations issued for this purpose by the Single Water Authority.

For this, the informal user must present their regularization request within a period of ninety days from the promulgation of this Law in the Official Registry.

The request must include the certification that accredits the cancellation of the values set by the Single Water Authority, corresponding to said informal uses and utilizations of water.

The regularization will be obtained through a motivated resolution issued by the Single Water Authority, which will have the prior report ensuring that said authorization of use or utilization does not affect other uses of water that legally have higher priority or generate, due to its relation with other requested or granted authorizations, a situation of hoarding.

In case of rejection of the regularization request, the Single Water Authority will proceed to initiate the corresponding administrative sanctioning file.

FOURTH.- Within a period of up to two years from the entry into force of this Law, the Single Water Authority, in coordination with the Decentralized Autonomous Governments, users, and user organizations, will carry out the national inventory of surface waters, and within a period of up to five years, the groundwater by hydrographic basins, with progress reports of 20% annually, which will include the situation of the sources and the cadastre of users.

FIFTH.- The competent Decentralized Autonomous Governments, in matters of water and sanitation provision, will implement adequate systems for the supply of drinking water, so that, within the period provided for in the National Development Plan for Good Living and in the strategy for the eradication of poverty and inequality, the total access of the population to drinking water is fully guaranteed. Similarly, they will proceed in accordance with the goals, objectives, and periods provided for in the national development plan and the national water resources plan for the planning, implementation, and construction of sewerage systems and the infrastructure for the treatment of residual waters and urban waste, so that the sanitation needs of the population are covered and all wastewater is treated.

The Decentralized Autonomous Governments will establish, in coordination with the Single Water Authority, a program of works and the respective financing.

SIXTH.- The pending applications for the granting of authorizations for the use or utilization of water will continue to be processed in accordance with the procedural rules in force at the time of the submission of the petition. In all other matters, that is, regarding the required information, the conditions, and obligations that the holders of an authorization must assume, the provisions of this Law shall apply.

Within a period of one hundred and eighty days counted from the date of entry into force of this Law, the Single Water Authority will resolve the administrative processes that correspond to its jurisdiction and that have not been advanced for more than eighteen months, which will be counted from the last diligence that has been practiced or from the last request made by any of the parties.

The Single Water Authority will declare the abandonment of the administrative processes that exceed said period and will resolve their archiving.

SEVENTH.- All concessions of the right of water utilization, destined for the use and utilization of water, granted for an indefinite term or for the useful life of the company, will be cancelled by the Single Water Authority. Without prejudice to the presentation of a new authorization request subject to the requirements established in this Law, which will be presented within a period not exceeding thirty days, to be counted from the date of cancellation.

During the processing period of the new request, the users of the authorization may continue to use the authorized flow, on a temporary basis, until the Single Water Authority resolves within a period not exceeding one hundred and eighty days.

EIGHTH.- Within a period of one hundred and eighty days from the entry into force of this Law, the Single Water Authority, in the absence of legal title to the land or an agreement with the owners by the users of a concession for the utilization of thermal waters on community lands, will proceed, ex officio or at the request of a party, to cancel said concession.

NINTH.- In guarantee of the order of priority provided for in the Constitution, the Single Water Authority, within a period of two years from the entry into force of the Law and without prejudice to its subsequent extension or modification, in coordination with the National Environmental Authority, will proceed to delimit the water protection zones and areas and restriction zones, from which the public or community water systems for human consumption or irrigation are supplied, which guarantee food sovereignty.

In this delimitation, the criteria established in the Law and in its respective Regulations will be attended to.

TENTH.- Within a period of two years counted from the entry into force of this Law, the Single Water Authority must identify and delimit by means of a motivated resolution, the lands where natural sources, recharge zones, and water protection areas affected by use or utilization are located, in the terms of this Law, to guarantee the integrity of the public water domain, the human right to water, and food sovereignty.

ELEVENTH.- The Single Water Authority, within a period of up to two years counted from the publication of this Law, will carry out an inventory of the drinking water and sewerage administration boards and irrigation boards to evaluate their technical and financial functioning and the fulfillment of the services provided to their members. This information will serve for the strengthening of the drinking water and irrigation boards and the services they provide, through public-community alliances.
