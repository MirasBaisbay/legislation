**Article 1** - The provisions published hereafter relating to the use of waters belonging to the public domain are consolidated into a single text entitled "The Water Code."

**Article 2** - As of the date of entry into force of the Water Code, all prior provisions contrary to the said Code are repealed, in particular:

However, the decrees and orders issued in application of the two aforementioned texts shall remain provisionally in force until the decrees and orders provided for in the Water Code are published.

### The Water Code

#### **TITLE ONE**
#### **The Public Water Domain**

**Article 1** - Belonging to the public water domain are:
*   Watercourses of all types and the land included within their free banks.
*   Waters contained within wadis.
*   Springs of all types.
*   Groundwater aquifers of all types.
*   Lakes and salt flats (sabkhas).
*   Canals, wells, and basins used by the public, as well as navigation, irrigation, or drainage canals constructed by or on behalf of the State for the public interest, along with the land within their free banks and their appurtenances.

**Article 2** - Notwithstanding the foregoing, legally acquired private rights related to the use of wadis, springs, and water sources are recognized and preserved, as they shall be determined by a Water Rights Settlement Committee in accordance with the conditions stipulated in Title Three of this Code.

**Article 3** - The public water domain is inalienable and imprescriptible.

**Article 4** - The public water domain shall be managed by the Minister of Agriculture, unless otherwise provided by decree.
The Minister of Agriculture shall be assisted by a National Water Committee and a Public Water Domain Committee, whose composition and operating conditions shall be determined by decree.
Acts of management of the public water domain that cause damage to the property of others shall only give rise to financial indemnity. The indemnities payable in such cases shall be determined in the same manner as for expropriation for public utility.

**Article 5** - The boundaries of wadis are determined by the highest water level flowing within their banks before overflow. These boundaries, as well as the boundaries of lakes and salt flats, shall be determined by decree following an administrative inquiry and without prejudice to any potential rights of third parties.

**Article 6** - The ownership of alluvium, accretions, sediment deposits, islands, and islets that form naturally in watercourses and wadis shall be subject to the provisions of Articles 28, 29, 30, 31, and 32 of the Code of Real Rights.

**Article 7** - Should a wadi change its course, whether for natural or other reasons, the new course of the wadi, including its free banks, shall be incorporated into the public water domain.
If the water does not completely abandon its old course, and if the new course was formed for natural reasons, the owners of the land through which the new course passes may not claim any indemnity.

#### **TITLE TWO**
#### **Conservation and Regulation of Waters Belonging to the Public Water Domain**

**Article 8** - Agents of the Ministry of Agriculture, duly authorized by decree, are charged with the conservation and regulation of the public water domain. They shall take all necessary measures to ensure the free flow of water or to carry out any necessary monitoring operations, as the case may be.
In all cases, the rights of third parties shall remain preserved.

**Article 9** - Exploratory drillings and wells not exceeding a depth of fifty meters, and which are not located within a restricted or protection zone as defined in Articles 12 and 15 of this Code, may be undertaken without prior authorization, provided that the owner or operator notifies the administration.

**Article 10** - Without prejudice to the provisions of Article 9 of this law, the following actions are prohibited unless authorized by the Minister of Agriculture:
1.  Obstructing the flow of waters belonging to the public water domain.
2.  Encroaching in any manner, particularly by means of construction, upon the free banks of temporary or permanent watercourses, lakes, salt flats, and springs, as well as within the right-of-way of aqueducts, water conduits, and navigation, irrigation, or drainage canals that have been declared to be of public utility. However, pre-existing structures may be maintained or repaired, provided that their external footprint is not increased and that the construction materials used are the same as those originally employed.
3.  Making any deposit, carrying out any work, or undertaking any planting or cultivation on the free banks and in the beds of temporary or permanent wadis, lakes, and salt flats, as well as within the right-of-way of water canals and conduits that have been declared to be of public utility.
4.  Disposing in temporary or permanent watercourses of substances harmful to public health or objects of any kind likely to obstruct the said course or cause sediment accumulation therein.
5.  Uprooting grasses, trees, shrubs, or removing soil and stones from the free banks or beds of temporary or permanent wadis.
6.  Creating excavations of any kind if they are located at a distance from the boundary of the free banks of temporary or permanent watercourses, pipes, aqueducts, and canals that is less than the depth of the said excavations, provided that this distance is not less than three meters.
7.  Dredging, deepening, widening, straightening, or leveling temporary or permanent watercourses.
8.  In general, undertaking any action likely to affect the regime of surface waters belonging to the public water domain.
9.  Carrying out works related to the exploration for and abstraction of artesian or non-artesian groundwater.
10. Digging wells or carrying out non-artesian drillings outside the public interest on private property if such works constitute clandestine operations to draw water from a spring.

**Article 11** - The execution of works mentioned in Article 10 of this Code without authorization shall be punishable by a fine equal to one-tenth of the estimated value of the works performed.
Works initiated in this manner may be suspended temporarily or permanently by the Minister of Agriculture, without prejudice to any precautionary measures that the administration may order if the conservation or quality of the water is threatened.
The execution of works found to be in violation of the requirements of the authorization order shall be punishable by a fine that may reach one-tenth of the value of the works performed.

**Article 12** - Restricted zones may be established by decree, after consultation with the Public Water Domain Committee, in areas where the conservation or quality of water is endangered by the extent to which existing water resources are being exploited.

**Article 13** - Within any restricted zone:
a) It is prohibited to construct any new well or drilling or to carry out any work to modify existing wells or drillings intended to increase the quantity of water abstracted therefrom.
b) The following are subject to prior authorization from the Minister of Agriculture:
Works to replace or rehabilitate wells or drillings, provided they are not intended to increase the quantity of water exploited from the said wells or drillings.
c) The following are subject to authorization and directives from the Minister of Agriculture:
The exploitation of existing groundwater. These directives may require limiting the maximum quantity of water to be exploited from wells or drillings and may include the decommissioning of a number of wells or drillings or any other measure capable of avoiding harmful interactions and ensuring the conservation of existing water resources.

**Article 14** - The execution of the works referred to in paragraphs (a) and (b) of Article 13 of this Code may be halted by order of the Minister of Agriculture, without prejudice to any precautionary measures that the administration may order. These precautionary measures may include the partial or total demolition of the works, as well as the restoration of the site to its original state.
Rehabilitation works carried out in violation of the provisions of the authorization order shall be punishable by a fine that may reach one-tenth of the estimated value of the works performed.

**Article 15** - Protection zones may be established by decree, issued after consultation with the Public Water Domain Committee, for aquifers where it is feared that the rate and volume of exploitation of existing water resources may be detrimental to the conservation of water in terms of quantity and quality.
Within these zones, works for the exploration and exploitation of groundwater aquifers are subject to authorization from the Minister of Agriculture, with the exception of works for the repair or operation of existing installations.

**Article 16** - Water management and use zones may be established by decree, issued after consultation with the National Water Committee, in areas where resources appear or are feared to be insufficient relative to current or planned priority needs.
Within the zones mentioned in the preceding paragraph, water resource allocation plans for the area concerned shall be determined by order of the Minister of Agriculture, following an administrative inquiry among the natural or legal persons who may be affected and after obtaining the opinion of the National Water Committee, according to the type and location of the needs to be met.
The decree referred to in the first paragraph of this article may, where applicable, provide for water transfer programs and works programs designed to enable the application or realization of the water allocation plan and may declare all or part of the transfer or works programs so defined to be of public utility.

**Article 17** - Within restricted zones and protection zones, the administration reserves the right to make all observations and take measures designed to monitor the evolution of water resources in wadis, wells, and existing drillings.
The owners or operators of these wells, drillings, or wadis must allow authorized agents of the administration access thereto to obtain all information regarding the quantities of water withdrawn and the manner in which such withdrawal occurs.

**Article 18** - Within a water management zone, every owner or operator of installations for diversion, abstraction, or drawing from wells must submit a declaration identifying their installations.
However, certain categories of installations that have a negligible impact on the water regime may be exempted from the aforementioned declaration. This shall be done by the decree establishing the water management zone, as provided for in Article 16 of this Code.

**Article 19** - The National Water Committee shall provide its opinion on general matters relating to water management and planning, on water management and allocation projects of a national character, and on major regional development projects. It may also be consulted on any matter relating to the conservation and protection of water.
It may conduct all general studies necessary for planning water consumption for domestic, industrial, or agricultural purposes, as well as for the prevention of the harmful effects of water.

**Article 20** - The Public Water Domain Committee is responsible for providing its technical opinion on any matter related to the public water domain. Its composition and operating conditions shall be determined by decree.

#### **TITLE THREE**
#### **Water Use Rights**

**Article 21** - Water ownership rights, particularly in the southern oases, existing at the date of promulgation of this Code and determined by the Water Rights Settlement Committee under the conditions specified below, shall be converted into water usufruct rights, the volume of which shall be equal to the volume of the ownership rights.

**Article 22** - The water usufruct right entitles its holder to dispose, under all circumstances, of a specific annual volume of the total available water resources, subject to the provisions set forth below.

**Article 23** - The water usufruct right remains attached to a specific parcel of land, within the framework of use focused on the maximum valuation of the cubic meter of water. The holder of the water usufruct right may not, except in cases of imperative necessity and with the approval of the National Water Committee, use the water to which they are entitled for the benefit of another parcel of land.
In the event of a transfer of the land, the water usufruct right is obligatorily transferred to the new owner, who must declare the said transfer within six months from the date of the land transfer.
Any transfer of a water usufruct right that takes place separately from the land for which the right was granted shall be considered null and void. In the event of a subdivision of the land, the distribution of water among the resulting parcels shall become the subject of new water usufruct rights that replace the original usufruct right.

**Article 24** - Water usufruct rights may be revised taking into account the total available water resources, based on actual needs and the objective of maximizing the value of each cubic meter of water.

**Article 25** - Usufruct rights may, in particular, be modified due to a comprehensive program for the revitalization of irrigation in the concerned area, within the framework of maximizing the value of each cubic meter of water, with priority given to meeting drinking water needs.
The aforementioned revitalization program, initiated by the administration or the beneficiaries, shall be subject to an administrative inquiry for a period of thirty days after the observations or objections have been examined by the relevant Water Interest Group and then by the National Water Committee.
The program, revised as necessary, becomes binding on all parties upon approval by the Minister of Agriculture. Disputes concerning the modifications made to the said usufruct rights shall be brought before the competent courts, which may only award indemnities to the aggrieved parties.

**Article 26** - Within the framework of the program provided for in Article 25 of this Code, if meeting water needs requires the construction of water infrastructure, the resulting expenses shall be borne by the State. The related operating expenses shall be divided proportionally to the volume of water actually distributed.
However, the State shall bear the operating expenses for the quantities corresponding to the water rights established as of the date of promulgation of this Code, until the complete cessation of artesian flow and the drying up of the springs that were the source of the said rights.

**Article 27** - If the construction and use of the water infrastructure provided for in the preceding article allow for an increase in the available water quantities, the related operating expenses shall be divided per cubic meter of additional water obtained, after deducting the expenses borne by the State pursuant to Article 26 of this Code.

**Article 28** - Owners and beneficiaries who claim to have acquired water rights must, within one year from the date of promulgation of this Code, submit a request to the Minister of Agriculture to establish their rights, accompanied by all useful supporting documents; otherwise, their right shall be forfeited. The Minister of Agriculture shall rule on these rights, unless an action is brought before the courts.
The establishment of these acquired rights to waters belonging to the public water domain is subject to the conditions set out in the following articles.

**Article 29** - The Minister of Agriculture shall determine the established and recognized water rights after obtaining the opinion of the Settlement Committee. The organization and functioning of the said Settlement Committee shall be determined by decree.
If rights that were the subject of a declaration within the specified time limits are not recognized, persons who consider themselves aggrieved by the decision of the Minister of Agriculture may bring their case before the courts within one year from the date of notification of the decision. Such persons may only claim an indemnity.

**Article 30** - Acquired usufruct rights over springs originating on private land may be established by the Minister of Agriculture, upon submission of a request by the beneficiary of the said rights, provided that the usufruct rights do not conflict with the public interest or harm the water supply of any population center, and without prejudice to all rights of third parties.

**Article 31** - If the waters of a spring, upon leaving the property where they originate, form a watercourse having the character of running water, the owner of the land may not divert the said waters from their natural course to the detriment of downstream users.

**Article 32** - Private water installations intended to supply private rural holdings are not considered to be of public interest. However, the construction, operation, maintenance, and renewal of said installations shall be regulated by decree.

**Article 33** - Every landowner has the right to use and dispose of the rainwater that falls on their land.
For this purpose, they may benefit from a right of passage through lower-lying lands by the most appropriate and least damaging route. If the use of this water or the direction given to it increases the natural servitude of water flow, an indemnity must be paid to the owner of the lower-lying land.
Disputes arising from the establishment and exercise of the above servitudes and from the payment of indemnities due to the owners of lower-lying lands, where applicable, shall be brought before the civil courts.

**Article 34** - The owner of a lower-lying property is obliged to receive on their land the waters that flow naturally from the higher land, especially rainwater, snowmelt, or un-channelled springs.
No neighbor may obstruct this natural flow to the detriment of another neighbor.

**Article 35** - The owner of a lower-lying property must accept water resulting from the drainage of a higher-lying property if it flows onto their land naturally.
In the event of damage, they may demand that the owner of the higher-lying property, at the latter's expense, construct a channel across the lower-lying land.

**Article 36** - Any natural or legal person who, for the benefit of their holdings, wishes to use water for which they have obtained a usufruct right may obtain passage for this water in underground conduits through intermediate lands, under the most appropriate conditions and those least damaging to the current and future use of the lands, provided that a fair and prior indemnity is paid to the owners of the intermediate lands.
Disputes that may arise from the establishment of servitudes or that result from determining the route, size, and shape of the water conduit, and the indemnities to be paid, whether to the owner of the crossed land or to the owner of the land that receives the water, fall under the jurisdiction of the courts. Such disputes concerning indemnities shall suspend the works.
The same servitudes may be claimed, under the same conditions, for water used for irrigation and for drainage and sanitation canals.
Houses, courtyards, enclosed gardens, parks, and pens adjoining dwellings are exempt from these rights.

**Article 37** - Used water from dwellings and holdings supplied with water may be directed via an underground conduit towards treatment or disposal facilities under the same conditions and reservations as for water conveyance.

**Article 38** - Any landowner wishing to drain their land by means of water discharge or by any other drying method may, under the same conditions and reservations as stipulated in Article 37 of this Code, direct groundwater or surface water across the lands separating this land from a watercourse or from any other means of flow.
Houses, courtyards, enclosed gardens, parks, and pens adjoining dwellings are exempt from this servitude.

**Article 39** - Owners of land adjacent to or crossed by water are entitled to use the works carried out in accordance with the preceding article for the flow of water from their lands.
In this case, they shall bear:
1.  A proportional share of the value of the works from which they benefit.
2.  The expenses resulting from the modifications that this diversion may necessitate.
3.  For the future, a contributory share in the maintenance of the works that have become common in nature.

#### **TITLE FOUR**
#### **Servitudes**

**Article 40** - The banks of wadis, lakes, and salt flats designated by decree are subject to a servitude known as the free bank servitude, within a width of three meters from the bank. This right is intended solely to permit the free passage of administration personnel and their equipment and does not confer any right to indemnity.
No new construction, fencing, or planting shall be carried out within the areas subject to the servitude without prior authorization from the Minister of Agriculture.

**Article 41** - If the free bank servitude proves insufficient for the establishment of a path along the watercourse, the administration may, in the absence of express consent from the adjacent landowners, acquire the necessary land through expropriation for public utility.

**Article 42** - The administration may demand the uprooting of trees located within the boundaries of the areas subject to the free bank servitude. It may carry out the uprooting by force if this request is not executed within a period of three months.

**Article 43** - It is prohibited for any owner to erect any construction within the limits of the free banks. However, structures existing prior to the date of the decree provided for in Article 5 of this Code concerning the boundaries of the free banks may be maintained and repaired, provided that their volume is not increased and that the materials used are the same as those previously employed.

**Article 44** - The right-of-way for the operation and maintenance of conveyance aqueducts and conduits shall be determined by the Minister of Agriculture.
This zone, the boundaries of which shall be clearly marked on site, may be subject to expropriation for public utility or to temporary occupation.
In the latter case, the concerned landowners are required to allow the installation of conveyance aqueducts, pipes, canals, or drains on their lands in return for global compensation for the damage caused to them, if these works cannot be executed otherwise and without excessive expense.

**Article 45** - Except with authorization from the Ministry of Agriculture, it is prohibited to carry out any planting within the right-of-way of canals and conveyance aqueducts or to undertake any cultivation in the same area if it concerns unfenced land.

**Article 46** - The administration may order the demolition of constructions and installations, as well as the removal of plantings, existing at the time of promulgation of this Code and prohibited by Articles 44 and 45, in return for an indemnity calculated in accordance with the legislation concerning expropriation for public utility.

**Article 47** - A landowner or user is subject to servitudes related to the installation of markers by the State, signaling devices, and works for measurement and land surveying related to water.

**Article 48** - The landowner, user, or their representative must be notified in writing of the execution of works on the lands subject to servitudes.
A site inspection report must be drawn up if such an inspection is necessary to assess the value of the damages resulting from the execution of the works.
Damages resulting from the works shall be determined by the competent court in the absence of an amicable agreement.

**Article 49** - The servitude obliges the owners and their successors to refrain from any act likely to compromise the proper functioning, maintenance, and preservation of the installations.

**Article 50** - Landowners adjacent to irrigation or drainage canals declared to be of public utility by the administration are obliged to allow the free passage and use of mechanical equipment intended for maintenance work on their lands, within a width of four meters from the bank of the drainage or irrigation canal. They must also allow the deposit of cleaning materials in certain places.
The area subject to the servitude may, in said places, reach double the width between the free banks of the irrigation or drainage canal. In the absence of an amicable sale, the expropriation of lands subject to the servitude of deposit becomes mandatory.
New constructions, the erection of permanent fences within the areas subject to the servitude of passage or deposit, as well as plantings, are subject to prior authorization from the Minister of Agriculture.

**Article 51** - Any owner of land subject to a servitude of deposit may at any time demand that the beneficiary of the said right purchase the land.
If this request is not met within one year, the owner may bring a legal action before the courts to obtain a judgment ordering the transfer of ownership and determining the amount of the indemnity.
The indemnity shall be calculated in the same manner as for expropriation for public utility.

#### **TITLE FIVE**
#### **Licenses or Concessions Related to Waters of the Public Water Domain**

##### **1 - General Provisions**

**Article 52** - The following are subject to a simple license regime:
1.  The installation of non-permanent structures intended for the use of waters from the public water domain.
2.  The construction, reconstruction, or repair of installations located between the free banks of wadis, lakes, salt flats, canals, and navigation, irrigation, and drainage canals.
3.  Deposits, plantings, and cultivation on the free banks and in the beds of wadis, lakes, and salt flats.
4.  Works for the exploration for and abstraction of groundwater, with the exception of the use of said water.
5.  Works for the abstraction and use of waters from natural springs located on private property that are not intended for exploitation for purposes of public interest.
6.  Dredging, deepening, straightening, or leveling of temporary or permanent wadis.
7.  The installation of works of all kinds in the public water domain and installations leading to or from dams and free banks.

**Article 53** - The following are subject to a concession regime:
1.  Water intake works of a permanent nature in the bed of wadis.
2.  The use of artesian or non-artesian groundwater.
3.  The use of mineral and thermal springs; however, the concession for the use of these springs must be approved by decree.
4.  The construction of permanent dams, as well as the use of impounded or diverted waters.
5.  The draining and use of lakes and salt flats.

**Article 54** - Operations not provided for in Articles 52 and 53 that may affect waters of the public water domain shall be classified by the Minister of Agriculture under either the simple license regime or the concession regime.

**Article 55** - Requests for concessions may be refused if they are contrary to the public interest or to the legally established rights of third parties.

**Article 56** - A concession may be declared by decree to be of public utility if the interest of the planned development so requires.

**Article 57** - The administration reserves the right to limit the quantity of water authorized for use or subject to a concession to the volume actually necessary for the execution of the planned exploitation program.

**Article 58** - Concessions are granted within the limits of reasonable water availability, estimated on the basis of the statements, measurements, observations, statistics, and calculations available to the administration.
No indemnity may be claimed from the State if the actually available volume does not reach the volume that is the subject of the concession, which constitutes the maximum limit that should not be exceeded.

**Article 59** - The administration may require a concession holder to accept a temporary limitation of the rights related to their concession for the execution of works of public interest on public waters, after introducing modifications to minimize the harm caused to the concerned party.

**Article 60** - Concessions not declared to be of public utility are renewable for the benefit of their holders, without prejudice to the possibility of introducing modifications to the conditions of the original concession as required by the public or economic interest.
At the end of the concession period, the lands, buildings, and installations that constitute the real property appurtenances of concessions declared to be of public utility shall revert to the State free of charge, in good condition, and free of all encumbrances.

**Article 61** - The conditions for granting simple licenses and concessions shall be determined by decree.

**Article 62** - If the public interest requires the removal or modification of installations legally established by virtue of a license or concession, the license holder or concession holder is entitled to an indemnity corresponding to the stripped value of the damage they have suffered, unless otherwise stipulated in the license or concession document.

**Article 63** - Licenses and concessions not declared to be of public utility shall give rise to the payment of a fee to the State, calculated on the basis of the number of cubic meters of water that may be withdrawn, and determined by an adjustable scale adopted by a joint order of the Ministers of Finance and Agriculture.
If the owner of the land does not exploit it themselves, the fees shall be collected from the operator. In the event of the said operator's inability to pay, the fees shall be collected from the owner.

**Article 64** - The fees referred to in Article 63 of this Code are separate from the fees payable, where applicable, for the temporary occupation of the public domain for the establishment of water intake or use installations.

**Article 65** - A full exemption from the fees provided for in Articles 63 and 64 of this Code may be granted if the water license or concession is intended for the provision of a public service.

**Article 66** - No full or partial transfer of a concession, nor any change of concession holder, may occur without prior approval from the Minister of Agriculture.
In the event of the death of the concession holder, their heirs must request the registration of the concession in their names within a period of six months; otherwise, their right shall be forfeited upon the expiration of the said period.

**Article 67** - Without prejudice to the special conditions included in the concession order, the forfeiture of the concession may be declared for the following reasons:
*   Use of waters other than those authorized or outside the designated area of use.
*   Non-compliance with the legislation and regulations concerning water.
*   Non-payment of annual fees after a formal notice has been sent to the concession holder.
*   Transfer made without prior authorization from the administration or without a request for its registration within six months following the death of the holder, except in the case of an express decision by the Minister of Agriculture regarding the time limits.
*   Non-use of the water within one year from the date the concession was granted.
*   Non-use of the water subject to the concession for two consecutive years.

**Article 68** - The administration may order the demolition of all works carried out without a concession or in violation of the regulations specific to water, at the expense of the offenders, and may order, where applicable, the restoration of the site to its original state.
Furthermore, the administration may demand the modification of works carried out in violation of the conditions of the concession.

**Article 69** - In the event of forfeiture of the concession, the administration may order the restoration of the site to its previous state and, if necessary, have this operation carried out by force at the expense of the concession holder who has been stripped of their said right.

**Article 70** - In the event of a fire or other public disaster, the use of public waters subject to a license or concession is permitted without authorization.

##### **2 - Special Provisions for Surface Waters**

**Article 71** - For the public interest, water intakes and other installations established in the public water domain may be modified or removed, even if authorized.
However, removal and modification can only be ordered in accordance with the procedures and guarantees established for the granting of these licenses.
The license holder is solely entitled to receive compensatory indemnity.

**Article 72** - Licenses or concessions granted for the establishment of installations on wadis may be withdrawn, annulled, or modified in the following cases:
1.  For the benefit of public health protection, especially if the annulment or modification is necessary to supply populated areas with potable water.
2.  To prevent or stop floods.
The said modification or cancellation shall give rise to the right to an indemnity for the license or concession holder, according to the direct damage caused, where applicable.

**Article 73** - The Minister of Agriculture may order the mandatory closure of water intakes from which a quantity of water exceeding that stipulated in the licenses and concessions is taken, without prejudice to other legal measures, and the closure of unauthorized or unlawful water intakes.

**Article 74** - If the quantities used by a license or concession holder, after two or more years from the date the license or concession for water intake was granted, are less than the quantities they were authorized to establish, the corresponding license or concession may be adjusted according to that reduction, without the holder having any right to object or to an indemnity.

##### **3 - Special Provisions Relating to Groundwater**

**Article 75** - The conditions for the exploration and exploitation of groundwater shall be determined by decree.

##### **4 - Servitudes Specific to Concessions**

**Article 76** - To execute works related to a set of projects declared to be of public utility or of common private interest, the concession holder may benefit, under the conditions set out in the following articles, from the servitudes listed below:
1.  Servitude related to the occupation of private properties necessary for the establishment of abstraction and water intake installations, and conduits for conveyance, discharge, or drainage of water.
2.  Servitude related to the abutment of dams built on wadis.
3.  Servitude related to the submersion of banks by the rising water level and servitude related to the flooding of lands in the event of the creation of water storage dams.
4.  Servitude related to the flow of usable water and its effluents.
5.  And, in general, any servitude recognized for the State and legally provided for in the concession document.
Buildings, courtyards, and enclosed gardens adjacent to dwellings are exempt from these servitudes.

**Article 77** - The execution of works of public utility by the State confers the benefit of the servitudes provided for in the preceding article without the need for a concession document.

**Article 78** - In the absence of an amicable agreement with the landowners, the concession holder may be authorized by an order from the Minister of Agriculture to benefit from the servitudes specified in Article 76 of this Code, after the said landowners have been heard.
The landowners shall be notified of the authorization order by an extrajudicial act by the concession holder. In all cases, the landowner is entitled to a prior indemnity, the calculation of which, in the absence of an amicable agreement, shall be determined according to the following provisions:
*   If the works performed are of a temporary nature, the indemnity shall be paid as an indivisible annual amount, which must not exceed double the rental value of the occupied lands at the time of their occupation.
*   If the occupation lasts for more than three years, or if the occupied lands are no longer suitable, after the completion of the works, for the purpose for which they were previously intended, the owner may demand that the concession holder purchase the land.
*   Parcels that are severely damaged, spoiled over a large part of their area, or of which only a very small area remains unoccupied, preventing their use, must be purchased in their entirety if the owner so demands.
The purchase price shall be determined by agreement between the parties. In the absence of an amicable agreement, the said price shall be determined by the court, which, in its assessment of the price, must take into account the increases in value from which the remaining parts of the occupied property directly and specifically benefit or have benefited.
The judgments rendered in this matter are always enforceable with the deposit of a provisional sum, regardless of appeal. The occupation may take place as soon as the amount of the determined indemnity has been deposited.
However, the concession holder may, through summary proceedings, request immediate occupation of the lands indicated in the authorization order in return for depositing a provisional sum on account of the amount of the disputed indemnity.

**Article 79** - If public utility is declared, the legislation specific to the execution of public works shall apply to the installations authorized by the Minister of Agriculture.

**Article 80** - The costs of all works necessary to protect their installations from water shall be borne by the concession holder. They are, moreover, responsible for the damages that these works may cause to third parties.

**Article 81** - If mine exploration or exploitation works, or the exploitation of a surface quarry, are likely to cause harm to the conservation of water and the use of springs and aquifers that supply populations, the administration shall take various measures to safeguard the water intake operations declared to be of public utility and intended for supplying communities, and to preserve the effect of the general measures ordered within water management zones.

**Article 82** - The holder of a mining exploration license and the holder of a mining exploitation concession are subject, with respect to the conservation and use of water discovered during the execution of works, to the preceding conditions relating to the conservation and use of water in the public domain, unless exceptions are made by decree.

**Article 83** - If a landowner brings water to the surface on their land by means of drillings or works carried out underground, which are the subject of a license, the owners of lower-lying lands are obliged to allow the passage of the water along the most reasonable and least damaging route. These owners are entitled to an indemnity in the event of damage to them resulting from the flow of said water.

**Article 84** - Any landowner who wishes to use for the irrigation of their land surface water for which a concession has been granted may be authorized to establish on the property of the opposite neighbor the necessary installations for their water intake, provided they pay the latter a fair and prior indemnity.

**Article 85** - The neighbor from whom it is requested to accept the installation provided for in the preceding article may request to use the installations jointly by contributing half of the establishment and construction costs. In this case, no indemnity is due, and any indemnity that may have been previously paid must be returned.

#### **TITLE SIX**
#### **Beneficial Effects of Water**

##### **1 - Water Conservation:**

**Article 86** - The planning of the use of the country's water resources, while ensuring the conservation of these resources in quantity and quality, must be based on the principle of the maximum valuation of the cubic meter of water at the national level, taking into account the minimum quality requirements obtained under acceptable economic conditions.
However, it is particularly noted that the State reserves, at the appropriate time, the quantities of water necessary to ensure a satisfactory supply of water to the population in quantity and quality.

**Article 87** - In application of the principle mentioned in the preceding article, works for the transfer of water from one water basin to another, other than those provided for in Article 88 of this Code, must be preceded by a specific economic study demonstrating the best valuation of the quantities of water intended for transfer.
The said economic study is subject to an administrative inquiry and submitted to the National Water Committee, in whose deliberations the regional authorities and representatives of water users in the concerned regions shall participate.

**Article 88** - Transfers from one supply basin to another of water quantities necessary to meet the drinking water needs of the population of the latter basin are authorized without a justifying economic study, provided that the drinking water needs of the original basin are preserved beforehand and that all other measures relating to all other operations for fetching drinking water have been taken under acceptable cost price conditions or at a price lower than the cost price or at a price lower than the cost of the intended transfer. Also authorized are transfers from this basin to another of water quantities suitable for the maintenance of agricultural and industrial properties existing on the date the transfer was considered by the administration, without the said economic study, and after reserving all these drinking water needs and the quantities of water that may be necessary to protect the existing agricultural or industrial heritage in the water basin.

**Article 89** - The conditions under which measures to be taken for the construction and maintenance of public and private networks and installations may be imposed in order to avoid wasteful water consumption shall be determined by decree.

**Article 90** - If current water uses lead to waste, the administration may recommend the adoption of appropriate measures to avoid this waste or recall compliance with the regulatory provisions issued in this regard.
In the absence of acceptable reservations presented by the user, and if the written instructions issued by the Ministry of Agriculture are not implemented, the works may be carried out by force by the administration after a prior formal notice.

**Article 91** - The administration is specifically empowered to suspend the supply of water intended for irrigation:
1.  For the execution of irrigation works.
2.  To avoid waste in case of poor maintenance of installations.
3.  In case of legally established water waste.
4.  If notifications or demands regarding the maintenance and repair of installations have been unsuccessful.

**Article 92** - Quantities of water intended for domestic purposes and consumed in excess of what is determined according to water consumption standards, as defined by an order issued by the Minister of Agriculture after consulting the concerned ministers, shall be subject to a progressive tariff exceeding the normal tariff.

**Article 93** - Industries exploiting waters of the public water domain at a rate of 300 cubic meters per day or more, which by their nature could be used for human or agricultural supply, must prove the absence of other water resources that meet, under acceptable economic conditions, the minimum quality requirements of the said industry, both in quantity and quality.

**Article 94** - Industrial water users must demonstrate in their application for the establishment of installations that the planned measures are those that allow for the maximum conservation of the quantity of water used, the best protection of its quality, and the maximum limitation of pollution resulting from the used water.

**Article 95** - Industries using water must, to meet their needs, recycle the used water whenever it appears that this recycling can be achieved from both a technical and economic standpoint, without prejudice to the provisions of Articles 129 and 130 of this Code.

**Article 96** - The administration may grant financial and technical assistance for works aimed at reducing water loss, provided that it is proven that these works are economically viable and can be technically achieved.

##### **B - Special Provisions for Water Intended for Consumption:**
###### **Definition of Water for Consumption and Potable Water**

**Article 97** - Water intended for consumption is raw or treated water intended for drinking, domestic purposes, and the manufacture of soft drinks, mineral waters, ice, and any foodstuff.
Water intended for consumption must not contain harmful quantities, chemical substances, or germs detrimental to health. Furthermore, it must be free from signs of pollution and have acceptable characteristics in terms of its organoleptic composition.

**Article 98** - For water to be considered potable and distributable to the human community, it must, in addition to the characteristics specified in Article 97 of this Code, meet the conditions and standards set by decree.

**Article 99** - Communities or public or private institutions that may have their own water supply method must systematically verify the quality of the distributed water and also regularly conduct bacteriological analysis of the distributed water in accordance with the standards set by decree.
Potential water disinfection methods or recourse to treating said water with simple or complex chemical additives must be authorized in advance by the Ministry of Public Health after consulting the Higher Council for Public Hygiene.
Potential additives must not, under any circumstances, result in a change in the organoleptic characteristics of the water.

**Article 100** - If the water intended for distribution does not comply with the established standards, its use may be authorized with certain reservations determined by the Ministry of Public Health, which shall take appropriate preventive measures.
Water quality is monitored through periodic analyses conducted in laboratories approved by the Ministry of Public Health.

##### **C - Special Measures for Water Intended for Agricultural Purposes:**

**Article 101** - The development of agricultural or agriculturally-oriented lands located within public irrigation perimeters is mandatory under the conditions set by the legislation governing public irrigation perimeters.

**Article 102** - The development of agricultural or agriculturally-oriented lands located within common or private irrigation perimeters of interest must result in a more efficient valuation of the cubic meter of water used.

**Article 103** - Water for agricultural purposes must be used under conditions that allow the characteristics of the land to be maintained and the water table level in the area to remain compatible with the exploitation of lands in the area without irrigation.

**Article 104** - The choice of discharge areas and points for drainage water from irrigated areas must be made in a way that avoids spoiling adjacent lands due to their salinity.
In the event of a technical impossibility preventing this, an indemnity shall be paid to the owners whose lands have been spoiled, according to the damages they have suffered.

**Article 105** - Water used for irrigation purposes must maintain properties that prevent it from becoming a source of disease transmission or harm to neighbors.

**Article 106** - The reuse of wastewater for agricultural purposes is only authorized after it has been appropriately treated in a purification plant and by an order issued by the Minister of Agriculture with the approval of the Minister of Public Health.
In all cases, the reuse of used water, even if treated, for the irrigation of vegetables consumed raw is prohibited.

**Article 106 bis** (Added by Law No. 94 of 1988, dated August 2, 1988) - The general methods and conditions for the supply of irrigation water and the setting of its tariffs by the offices for the development of irrigation perimeters within public irrigation perimeters, as well as within irrigation perimeters equipped by the State, shall be determined by a set of specifications approved by decree.

#### **TITLE SEVEN**
#### **Harmful Effects of Water**

##### **Section 1 - Combating Water Pollution**

**Article 107** - The provisions of this section aim to combat water pollution in order to satisfy or reconcile the following requirements:
*   Supply of potable water.
*   Public health.
*   Agriculture, industry, and all other human activities of interest.
*   The biological life of the receiving environment, especially fish, as well as recreational activities related to water sports and the protection of natural sites.
*   The conservation and flow of water.
This section applies to spills, flows, discharges, and direct or indirect deposits of substances of all kinds, and more generally, to any act likely to cause or increase the degradation of waters by altering their physical, chemical, biological, or bacteriological properties, whether concerning surface water, groundwater, or marine waters within the limits of territorial waters.

**Article 108** - It is prohibited to pour or immerse in sea waters any substances of any kind, especially domestic or industrial waste, that are likely to harm public health, as well as marine fauna and flora, and to hinder the economic and touristic development of coastal regions.

**Article 109** - It is prohibited to allow the flow, pouring, or discharge of wastewater into the public water domain, whether subject to a concession or not, as well as waste or substances likely to be detrimental to public hygiene or the proper use of these waters for all potential purposes.

**Article 110** - Any surface deposit likely to contaminate groundwater by infiltration or surface water by runoff is prohibited.

**Article 111** - It is prohibited to throw dead animals into wadis and ponds and to bury them near wells, public fountains, and basins.

**Article 112** - It is prohibited to soak any textile plant in public basins and reservoirs. The soaking of fiber plants in running waters is subject to prior authorization, and prohibition shall only be decided after consulting the Higher Council for Public Health Preservation.

**Article 113** - Any discharge of used water or waste likely to harm public health into natural percolating wells, wells, drillings, and percolating tunnels, whether their use has been repurposed or not, is prohibited. Only the discharge of wastewater or used water into percolating wells preceded by a protective pit is authorized.
A declaration must be submitted regarding repurposed wells, drillings, or abstraction tunnels, and they are subject to the control of the administration, which may order their temporary suspension or permanent closure, without prejudice to the rights of third parties.
These installations must comply with the conditions set by an order of the Minister of Agriculture after consulting the concerned ministers.

**Article 114** - The discharge of wastewater into wadis whose water is used for the supply of potable water or to meet the needs of the food industry is only authorized if these waters have undergone prior physical, chemical, and biological treatment and, if necessary, have been disinfected.

**Article 115** - The discharge of aqueous or non-aqueous waste likely to harm public health into dry wadis is prohibited.

**Article 116** - Treated water may be discharged into wadis if it does not carry mixed, suspended, or dissolved substances that, after discharge into the wadis, could result in the formation of deposits on the banks or in the wadi bed, or give rise to fermentation accompanied by foul gases causing injury or poisoning to people, animals, or plants, or promote the development of pathogenic germs or harmful insects, or directly or indirectly hinder the normal use of waters downstream of the discharge points.

**Article 117** - It is prohibited to damage, through negligence or carelessness, any installations intended to receive or convey water suitable for consumption, and to allow the introduction of substances consisting of waste or any other materials likely to be detrimental to the safety of springs, public fountains, wells, irrigation channels, canals, and basins of water suitable for consumption.

**Article 118** - The installations for abstraction, treatment, conveyance, and distribution of potable water, including pumps, reservoirs, and canals, must be constructed and maintained in a condition that ensures the protection of the water from any influence detrimental to its quality or safety.

**Article 119** - No person suffering from diseases that can be transmitted by water may be assigned to a service for the management, maintenance, or operation of potable water supply systems, especially at abstraction and treatment facilities and distribution reservoirs.
The list of these diseases shall be determined by an order from the Minister of Public Health.
Any person assigned to one of the posts concerned in the paragraph above must undergo a medical examination in accordance with the instructions to be set by an order from the Minister of Public Health.

**Article 120** - Public supply springs for potable water must be protected from any accidental or intentional cause likely to compromise the water quality prescribed by the decree provided for in Article 98 of this Code.

**Article 121** - A protection zone shall be established around every drilling, spring, well, or any other facility intended for supplying cities and villages with potable water.
An order from the Minister of Agriculture shall, in each case, define the boundaries of the protection zone, which shall include:
1.  An immediate protection zone, the lands of which are to be acquired in full ownership and fenced by the institution responsible for the installation and distribution of water for potable supply.
2.  A close protection zone, within which deposits or activities likely to lead, either directly or indirectly, to the pollution of the water resource are prohibited, the list of which shall be determined by an order of the Minister of Agriculture and the Minister of Public Health.
3.  Where applicable, a remote protection zone, within which the deposits or activities mentioned in the preceding paragraphs of this article may be regulated.
The immediate protection zones for potable water installation areas may be subject to expropriation for public utility.
If the prohibitions set out in the second and third paragraphs of this article effectively result in the non-use of actually developed land parcels, the owner has the right to demand expropriation.

**Article 122** - A protection zone shall be established around every treatment plant, pumping station, or reservoir for water intended for consumption, the boundaries of which shall be determined by an order from the Minister of Agriculture. The said protection zone, which shall be fenced by the concerned institution, may be subject to expropriation for public utility.

**Article 123** - With regard to dams impounding water intended for potable water supply, the following shall be established:
1.  An immediate protection zone consisting of the lands adjacent to the dam impounding water at its highest level, over a width of ten meters, which must be purchased in full ownership by the institution responsible for the operation of the dam.
2.  A servitude zone with a width of 50 meters beyond the adjacent rectangular plot, within which all works and activities likely to lead, directly or indirectly, to the pollution of the impounded water are prohibited.

**Article 124** - Notwithstanding the provisions of Articles 107 to 123 of this Code, a decree shall be issued, after consultation with the Higher Council for Public Health Preservation and the National Water Committee, determining the conditions under which discharges, flows, jets, and direct or indirect deposits of water or other materials, and more generally, any act likely to alter the quality of surface or groundwater, may be regulated or prohibited.
The said decree shall specify, on the one hand, the technical characteristics and the physical, chemical, biological, and bacteriological standards that must be met by wadis, canals, lakes, dams, ponds, or any impounded water in general, particularly with regard to water intakes that supply the population. On the other hand, it shall specify the deadline by which the quality of each water-receiving environment must be improved in a way that meets the interests defined in Article 107 of this Code, in a manner that reconciles them.
It shall also clarify the conditions under which:
1.  Discharges, flows, jets, and direct or indirect deposits of water may be regulated or prohibited, taking into account the preceding provisions, and more generally, any act likely to alter the quality of surface water or groundwater.
2.  The chemical, biological, and bacteriological characteristics of the resulting waters and discharges may be monitored, especially the conditions under which samples are taken and their analyses are conducted.
This decree shall specify, as needed, for each watercourse, canal, lake, dam, groundwater body, and other impounded waters, the special conditions under which the preceding provisions apply, as well as the deadlines by which the said provisions must be respected with regard to existing installations.
In all cases, the rights of third parties with respect to the perpetrators of pollution shall remain preserved.

**Article 125** - A statistical inventory establishing the degree of pollution shall be drawn up within a period of three years after the inclusion of this Code for surface waters, wadis, canals, lakes, and salt flats. The condition of each shall be determined according to physical, chemical, biological, and bacteriological standards. This statistical inventory shall be reviewed periodically and promptly whenever an exceptional or sudden change occurs in the condition of the said waters.

**Article 126** - The removal of pollution is the responsibility of the operators, institutions, and public bodies who are responsible for the discharge of their waste into the waters.

**Article 127** - Notwithstanding the obligations arising from the legislation in force, the owners of discharge facilities existing before the issuance of the decree provided for in Article 124 of this Code must take all measures to comply, within the deadlines provided for in the said decree, with the conditions imposed on their effluents so that the receiving environment achieves the characteristics it must have at the end of the said deadline.
The administration, considering the danger that may thereby befall public security and the protection of public health, may take any measure capable of rapid execution in order to avert the possible danger.

**Article 128** - For all new installations established after the issuance of the decree mentioned in Article 124 of this Code, the methods of treating wastewater, the devices for evacuation and discharge of treated water, as well as the technical plan of the purification installations, must be subject to approval by the Minister of Agriculture.
The said installations must be executed in accordance with the approved plans.

**Article 129** - Financial assistance granted by the State for the development of industries shall be accompanied by conditions requiring, in particular, the establishment of appropriate purification methods.

**Article 130** - Financial aid, supplemented where applicable by technical assistance for the operation of wastewater purification means, may be granted by the State for the treatment of wastewater after use.
The conditions for monitoring the efficiency of the purification of discharged water shall be determined by agreements concluded between the State and the builders of the installations who are likely to benefit from State aid for the construction of purification plants.

**Article 131** - Institutions that build or have built for them installations intended for the purification of industrial water may, upon completion of these constructions, apply a special charge of 50% of the cost price.
The remaining value of the properties can be amortized over the normal period of use.

**Article 132** - The discharge of water from ditches into watercourses, the sea, and lakes is only authorized after consulting the services responsible for the preservation of the public water domain or the public maritime domain and of the adjacent installations, regarding the measures intended to be taken for the treatment or distribution of the water.
A joint order of the Minister of Agriculture and the Minister of Public Health shall determine the biological and chemical characteristics that the means of a used water treatment plant must meet, except in the case of special provisions.
Urban sanitation projects shall provide for networks for the rapid and stagnation-free evacuation, far from dwellings, of all human or animal waste that is likely to cause infections or foul odors.

**Article 133** - Furthermore, it is required that the implementation of the said sanitation projects does not result in the contamination of the discharged materials into groundwater of any kind, as well as watercourses, lakes, and the seashore, in a manner that is dangerous to the inhabitants of the residential community or to other potential users, as well as to the agents responsible for the maintenance and operation of the installations.

**Article 134** - Any discharge of non-domestic wastewater into public ditches must be authorized in advance by the Minister of Agriculture, after consulting the community that owns the installations through which the used water passes before reaching the natural environment. This discharge, after authorization, may be subject to preliminary treatment, either individually or, where applicable, collectively.
In this case, if a delay is observed in the realization of the preliminary treatment installations, the administration reserves the right to carry out the said installations at the expense of the concerned parties after sending them a formal notice. They are also obliged to contribute to the maintenance and operating expenses of the installations.

**Article 135** - If the public interest so requires, the connection of private branch sewers to sanitation networks or treatment plants may be ordered or accepted if they do not meet the characteristics of the receiving watercourse. This shall be done under the conditions determined by the Minister of Agriculture. This connection may be conditional on the contribution of the concerned private enterprise to the additional construction costs and, where applicable, to the operating costs resulting from the supply of used water.
In the event of non-execution of the works imposed on the enterprise within the specified deadline for achieving the said connection to the public installations, the necessary works shall be carried out by force and at the expense of the concerned party, after their evaluation.

**Article 136** - Any application for the opening of an establishment with characteristics of danger, harm to health, or nuisance must specify the method intended to be followed for the evacuation, use, and treatment of wastewater and its conditions.
The measures intended to be taken in this manner must effectively prevent the harm that the said establishment could cause to the health or comfort of neighbors, or to public health or agriculture.

**Article 137** - Any public sanitation service, regardless of its operating method, shall result in the collection of sanitation fees, which shall be determined by decree.
A public service is considered to be any service responsible for the collection, transport, or, where applicable, purification of used water.

**Article 138** - The revenue from sanitation fees is allocated to financing the costs borne by the sanitation service.

**Article 139** - If the court renders a judgment punishing a violation of the provisions of this section or the texts adopted for its application, it shall set the deadline by which the works must be executed, the developments carried out, or all other necessary obligations fulfilled.
In the event of non-execution of the works, developments, or obligations within the specified deadline, the offender shall be subject to a fine of 100 D to 1000 D, without prejudice, where applicable, to the application of all other legislative or regulatory provisions in force.
The court may, furthermore, after hearing a representative of the administration, and until the works or developments are completed or the imposed obligations are fulfilled, impose a coercive fine, the amount of which shall not exceed, for each day of delay, 1/4000th of the estimated cost of the works or developments to be executed, or prohibit the use of the installations causing the pollution.
Any person who operates installations in violation of the prohibition ordered under the preceding paragraph of this article shall be punished by imprisonment from 6 months to two years and a fine of 500 to 5000 D, or by one of these two penalties only.
The court may also authorize the administration, at its request, to carry out by force the works or developments necessary to end the violation.

##### **Section Two - Flood Control**

**Article 140** - The initiative for the study and implementation of public works intended for water protection is entrusted to the State, which shall carry out in this field the general flood control programs designed to stop extreme floods within limits that allow for the minimization of the impact of the said floods.

**Article 141** - Governorate and municipal councils may be authorized to execute all flood protection works under the supervision of the Minister of Agriculture, with or without subsidies from the State, either individually or after forming associations in each governorate or inter-governorate associations called flood control associations.
The method of formation and functioning of the organizations mentioned in the preceding paragraph shall be determined by decree.

**Article 142** - With regard to the construction of dikes for the protection of private properties from watercourses, the necessity of this construction is only established by the administration, and the costs of protection are only charged to the protected properties in proportion to their interest in the said works.
The State may grant a subsidy for the execution of these works according to their interest, and especially according to the economic viability of the development and the inclusion of the planned development in a broader or regional framework. The amount of the subsidy shall be determined by an order from the Minister of Agriculture.

**Article 143** - The administration reserves the right, when necessary, to build or modify dikes erected against floods, to occupy the banks that are privately owned, to remove them, and to acquire the lands necessary to reinforce the dikes against floods.

**Article 144** - Dikes, embankments, deposits of obstructive materials, buildings, or other installations erected before the date of this Code, which are found to obstruct the flow of water or harmfully reduce the flood plain, may be modified or removed, with payment of an indemnity to compensate for the damage, where applicable.
The same applies to legally established installations if the modifications made to them and their removal become necessary for the reasons mentioned.

**Article 145** - For all watercourses, no new planting or any deposit may be made without authorization from the administration on the lands located between the watercourses and the dikes built on the bank directly adjacent to the watercourses.

**Article 146** - Any old planting, deposit, or construction of dikes or parallel dikes on the lands located between watercourses or built on the bank adjacent to the watercourse, and dikes that are likely to obstruct the flow of water or harmfully reduce the flood plain, may be removed within one year by a decision of the administration.

**Article 147** - It is prohibited to erect, without authorization, in the parts of the territory liable to be submerged by water, dikes and parallel dikes to watercourses, and to carry out other developments that are likely to obstruct the flow of floodwaters, except in cases where the aim is to protect dwellings and adjacent enclosed gardens.

**Article 148** - Any person who damages dikes intended for flood protection shall be punished by a fine of 100 D to 1000 D and imprisonment for a term of one to six months, or by one of these two penalties only.

**Article 149** - The movement and passage of animals on the dikes located on the banks of watercourses are prohibited in all seasons.

**Article 150** - By decree issued on the proposal of the Minister of Agriculture, areas known as sanitation zones may be defined in rainfed agricultural regions where agricultural properties are threatened by floodwaters and the rise of the groundwater table or excess surface water.

**Article 151** - If it is established that the execution of rural sanitation works within the sanitation zone mentioned in the preceding article is economically justified, the administration may grant the association of concerned owners and users the necessary assistance and financial facilities for the execution of the said works.

**Article 152** - The association of concerned owners and users is required to maintain the installations so that they can perform their function.
In the event of non-execution of the said maintenance works, the administration reserves the right to carry them out by force at the expense of the association of concerned owners and users, after a prior formal notice has been sent to them.

#### **TITLE EIGHT**
#### **User Associations**

**Article 153** - An advisory body shall be formed in each governorate under the chairmanship of the Governor, composed of representatives from the administration and water users, called a Water Interest Group, entrusted with the task of:
1.  Submitting proposals concerning the uses of public interest of the public water domain located in the concerned area.
2.  Providing an opinion on water management and distribution projects related to the governorate.
3.  Supervising the associations of owners and users concerned with the exploitation of water in the governorate.
The organization and method of operation of the Water Interest Group shall be determined by decree. (This paragraph was amended by Law No. 35 of 1987, dated July 6, 1987).

**Article 154** (Amended by Law No. 35 of 1987, dated July 6, 1987) - The associations of owners and users mentioned in the preceding Article 153 shall be called Joint Interest Associations. They are responsible for one or a combination of the following activities:
1.  Exploiting waters belonging to the public water domain within their areas of operation.
2.  Executing, maintaining, and using works concerning waters of the public water domain that they have the right to use.
3.  Irrigating or draining lands by management or by any other drying method.
4.  Operating a potable water network.
Joint Interest Associations enjoy civil personality. They can be formed either at the request of the users or at the initiative of the administration in the case of exploiting an irrigation perimeter, a potable water network, or sanitation, drainage, or drying zones established or planned to be established by the State or by any other public or semi-public institution.
The methods of formation, organization, and management of Joint Interest Associations shall be determined by decree.

**Article 155** (Amended by Law No. 35 of 1987, dated July 6, 1987) - The statutes of the Joint Interest Associations must conform to the model statute that will be approved by decree.
Irrigation syndicates, syndicated water interest associations, and private owners' associations are given a period of one year from the date of publication of the model statute for Joint Interest Associations to adopt this model statute.
After the expiration of the said period, and in case of non-compliance with this procedure, these associations shall be considered automatically dissolved.

#### **TITLE NINE**
#### **Jurisdiction and Penalties**

**Article 156** - Violations of the provisions of this Code and the decrees issued for its implementation shall be reported by:
*   All officers of the police and the National Guard.
*   Agents and employees of the Ministries of Agriculture and Public Health who are legally sworn in.
Without prejudice to the right granted to all officials and agents provided for in this article to draw up reports for acts of damage that may be committed in their presence, damages caused to the public domain of the State, to public health, and to the health of the population, as provided for in the preceding articles of this Code, shall be reported by engineers of the Ministry of Agriculture and doctors and engineers of the Ministry of Public Health who are legally qualified for this work.

**Article 157** - Reports of violations and offenses shall be transmitted by the agents mentioned in the preceding article to the concerned ministries, which shall, within a maximum of one month from their drafting, forward the said reports to the competent court.
Reports drawn up in application of this Code and the decrees issued for its application shall have the status of proof unless proven otherwise.
However, in cases of urgency, the reports shall be forwarded without delay to the Ministers of Agriculture and Public Health, who shall, each in their respective domains, order by decision the immediate demolition of the installations at the expense of the offender, if the installations erected on the public domain of the State without authorization threaten the safety of transportation routes, are likely to cause damage to private property, or if their continuation disturbs public tranquility or threatens the health of the population or public health.

**Article 158** - All violations of the provisions of this Code or the decrees and orders issued for its application shall be punished by a fine of 50 D to 1000 D and by imprisonment for a term of 6 days to 6 months, or by one of these two penalties only.
These penalties apply to any person who obstructs the execution of works authorized in accordance with the provisions of this Code or ordered by the Minister of Agriculture in the public water domain.

**Article 159** - Any person who has been previously convicted for one of the two offenses provided for in this Code or the decrees and orders issued for its application and commits the same offense again within twelve months from the date the conviction became final shall be punished by the maximum penalties of imprisonment and fine, or by the maximum of one of these two penalties only, with the possibility of doubling these penalties, unless it is proven that they acted in good faith.

**Article 160** - If a violation of the provisions of this Code and the decrees and orders issued for its implementation causes damage to the public domain of the State, the offender shall be sentenced to pay the repair costs determined by the Minister of Agriculture, in addition to the penalties provided for in this law.
The court may order, at the expense of the offender, the removal of the illegal works or installations.
