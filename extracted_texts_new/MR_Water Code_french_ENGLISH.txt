**TITLE I: GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**CHAPTER I: PURPOSE AND DEFINITIONS**

**Article 1:** This Law establishes the legal framework for water.

It aims for the rational and sustainable use and management of water resources in order to guarantee equal access to water for all, to meet water needs for all uses, and to preserve the resources for future generations, while respecting the balance of ecosystems.

**Article 2:** Water is part of the common heritage of the Nation. Its protection, conservation, and management are of general interest.

Every natural or legal person has the right to use water, subject to the provisions of this Law and the regulations enacted for its application.

**CHAPTER II: PUBLIC WATER DOMAIN**

**Article 3:** The Public Water Domain shall include:
*   all surface and ground watercourses;
*   lakes, ponds, and springs of all kinds;
*   groundwater aquifers;
*   lagoon waters and saltwater bodies located within the national territory;
*   hydraulic works of all kinds carried out for the purpose of general interest by the State, Territorial Collectivities, or public entities.

**Article 4:** The Public Water Domain is inalienable and imprescriptible.

**Article 5:** Rights of ownership, usufruct, or use legally acquired over lands do not grant their holders any right of ownership over the waters of the Public Water Domain. They shall not impede the application of the provisions of this Law and the regulations enacted for its enforcement.

**Article 6:** Public utility easements may be established pursuant to this Law.

They shall not give rise to any compensation if they do not affect the prior condition of the premises and do not render the exercise of rights of ownership and use impossible.

**Article 7:** The administration of water shall be ensured by the State, subject to the powers devolved to the Territorial Collectivities.

**TITLE I - GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**Section 1 - Purpose**

**Article 1:** The purpose of this code is to define the legal framework for continental, surface, and groundwater, excluding seawater, and in particular the rules relating to the planning, use, and preservation of water, and those relating to the organization and operation of the public water service.

**Section 2 - General Principles**

**Article 2:** Water is part of the Nation's heritage. The use of water constitutes a right recognized for all, within the framework of the laws and regulations in force.

The protection and development of water resources, while respecting natural balances, are in the public interest and constitute a national imperative involving the State, local authorities, private legal entities, and the entire population.

The policy of the State aims to guarantee the population's access to drinking water. It favors partnership between the State, local authorities, and private operators as a framework for financing and managing drinking water production and distribution infrastructure.

**Article 3:** Water management shall be comprehensive, sustainable, and balanced. It aims to ensure:
1) the quantitative protection of existing resources and the search for new resources;
2) protection against all forms of pollution;
3) the fight against waste and over-exploitation;
4) the equitable distribution of this resource, so as to satisfy or reconcile, for different uses, the requirements of:
- the supply of drinking water and, more generally, health and sanitation;
- livestock farming, agriculture, fish farming, forestry, industry and mining, energy production, navigation, tourism, inland fishing, as well as all other legally practiced human activities.

Water resource management is subject to compliance with the following principles:
1) the precautionary principle, aimed at preventing serious and irreversible risks to water resources through the adoption of effective measures;
2) the principle of pollution prevention, with priority given to the source;
3) the user-pays principle, according to which the water user bears a significant portion of the costs resulting from measures for pollution prevention and reduction and for the restoration of the resource in terms of quality and quantity;
4) the principle of involving users in the administrative management of water;
5) the principle that users of public drinking water distribution and sanitation services shall cover a significant portion of the costs generated by these services, according to the users' ability to pay.

Any abusive or unregulated consumption of surface water or groundwater for any purpose whatsoever is prohibited.

**Article 4:** Waters falling under the authority of the Senegal River Basin Development Organization shall be managed in accordance with the principles and standards provided for in the Senegal River Water Charter as 291. Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 ratified by the Islamic Republic of Mauritania.

**Section 3 - Use Priorities**

**Article 5:** Water resources shall be allocated with priority to supplying water to the population.

Once the population's water supply has been met and the security of their supply is not jeopardized, priority shall be given, in order and according to local priorities, to meeting the needs of livestock farming, agriculture, forestry, fish farming, inland fishing, reforestation projects, and, finally, industrial, mining, and agro-industrial complexes.

Other needs shall be met according to their economic interest and local priorities.

**TITLE II - PUBLIC WATER DOMAIN**

**Section 1 - Public Domain Status of Surface or Groundwater**

**Article 6:** Surface and atmospheric water resources, wherever they are located within the national territory, are collective property and, as such, form an integral part of the inalienable and imprescriptible public domain of the State.

**Section 2 - Composition**

**Sub-section 1 - Natural Public Water Domain of the State**

**Article 7:** Subject to the duly established rights of third parties, the following form part of the natural public water domain of the State:
1) the beds of watercourses, whether permanent or not, navigable or not, floatable or not, as well as the beds of lakes or ponds within the limits determined in Article 10 below;
2) aquifers.

**Article 8:** The boundaries of the natural public water domain, for 1096 watercourses, lakes, or ponds, shall be the line reached by the highest waters before overflowing. They shall be determined, in consultation with the minister concerned, by order of the minister in charge of water, on his own initiative, where applicable, or at the request of landowners with property abutting the water, following a public inquiry and subject to the rights of third parties.

**Article 9:** Actions for the recognition of acquired rights on land included by demarcation within the natural public water domain shall be brought, under penalty of foreclosure, within a period of one year from the date of publication of the demarcation order.

**Article 10:** In the event of a change in the boundaries of the public water domain due to a natural cause, the abutting landowners may submit a request for a new demarcation to the minister in charge of water, who shall rule within a period of one year. After this period, the abutting landowners shall be entitled to refer the matter to the competent court.

**Sub-section 2 - Artificial Public Water Domain of the State**

**Article 11:** The following developments and facilities, as well as their appurtenant structures, within the limits of the land occupied, shall form part of the artificial public water domain of the State when they have been constructed by the State and for its account:
1) works constructed to facilitate the impoundment or storage of water;
2) navigation canals and their appurtenances;
3) river ports and their appurtenances;
4) hydraulic works intended for abstraction for drinking water supply and for the transport of wastewater, as well as their appurtenances;

6) immediate protection perimeters for water abstraction points intended for human consumption mentioned in Article 40 below;
7) works for the development of hydraulic power;
8) works for flood defense and their appurtenances or for the control of such waters.

**Sub-section 3 - Artificial Public Water Domain of Local Authorities**

**Article 12:** The developments and facilities intended for drinking water supply shall form part of the artificial public water domain of local authorities, within the limits of the actual occupation of the lands concerned, when they have been acquired or constructed by the local authorities, either directly or through a delegated contracting authority, or when they have been transferred to them by the State.

The minister in charge of water shall represent the Islamic Republic of Mauritania before intergovernmental organizations of an international and regional nature specializing in water-related issues, and shall promote international and regional cooperation.

**TITLE IV - WATER USE REGIMES**

**SUB-TITLE I - PROCEDURES FOR QUANTITATIVE AND QUALITATIVE PROTECTION**

**Section 1 - General Principle**

**Article 18:** The use of water abstracted for domestic or similar purposes from available, perennial, or seasonal sources shall be exempt from any formality. A decree shall define the criteria for the domestic use of water.

Operations, installations, works, various activities, discharges, abstractions, and withdrawals carried out for non-domestic purposes on surface water or groundwater, resulting in a modification of the level or flow of these waters or an impairment of their quality, shall be subject, under the conditions provided for by decree, to declaration, authorization, or concession, according to thresholds that take into account the seriousness of their effects on water resources and aquatic ecosystems, 294 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 the dangers to public health and safety, as well as the public interest presented by these operations.

**Section 2 - Regimes for the Use of Water for Non-Domestic Purposes**

**Sub-section 1 - Declaration Regime**

**Article 19:** The operations listed in paragraph 2 of Article 18 above, which are likely to present only low dangers to public health and safety and have limited impacts on water flow, on the resource from both a quantitative and qualitative point of view, as well as on the diversity of the aquatic environment, shall be subject to the declaration regime.

**Sub-section 2 - Authorization Regime**

**Article 20:** The operations listed in paragraph 2 of Article 18 above, which are likely to present dangers to public health and safety, to impede the free flow of water, to reduce the water resource, or to impair water quality or the diversity of the aquatic environment, shall be subject to the authorization regime.

**Sub-section 3 - Concession Regime**

**Article 21:** The following shall be subject to the concession regime:
- the operations listed in paragraph 2 of Article 18 above of such importance that they are likely to present serious dangers to public health and safety, to very significantly impede the free flow of water, to reduce the water resource, to notably increase the risk of flooding, or to seriously impair the quality or diversity of the aquatic environment;
- water use operations that are in the public interest or have benefited from a declaration of public utility.

**Sub-section 1 - Provisions Relating to the Declaration**

**Article 22:** The minister in charge of water shall process the declaration file and shall issue an acknowledgment thereof if he deems that it is complete and that the impacts of the operation on the resource are limited.

The acknowledgment of declaration shall be accompanied by any specific requirements designed to limit its impacts.

**Sub-section 2 - Provisions Relating to the Authorization**

**Article 23:** The minister in charge of water shall process the application file for authorization, which shall include, in particular, a detailed technical study and an impact assessment, and shall grant or refuse the authorization by order based on the results of a prior public inquiry.

**Sub-section 3 - Provisions Relating to the Concession**

**Article 24:** The minister in charge of water shall process the application file for a concession jointly with the minister under whose authority the concession holder's activity falls. The file shall include, in particular, a detailed preliminary design study and an impact assessment.

The concession shall be granted or refused by a joint order of the ministers mentioned in the first paragraph of this article, based on the results of a prior public inquiry.

**Sub-section 4 - Provisions Common to Authorization and Concession**

**Article 25:** Authorizations or concessions shall be granted on a precarious and revocable basis, except in cases where the operation subject to the concession is in the public interest or benefits from a declaration of public utility.

In other cases, the modification or revocation without compensation of the authorization or concession instrument shall be declared by right, at any time by the minister in charge of water, particularly when this modification or revocation is 295 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 1096 made necessary for the water supply of the population, either to prevent or stop a harmful disturbance caused by the waters, or due to non-observance of the clauses it contains.

**Article 26:** Any partial or total transfer of an authorization or concession may only be made after prior approval from the minister in charge of water.

**Article 27:** In the event of competing applications for authorization or concession, the minister in charge of water shall rule in consideration of the use priorities defined in Article 5 above. When no application has priority over another, the minister in charge of water shall decide whether preference should be given to the earliest application.

**Article 28:** The costs of the on-site investigation of applications for authorization or concession, whether they are granted or refused, shall be borne by the applicant. The same shall apply to the costs of works inspection.

**Article 29:** Authorizations and concessions shall be granted subject to the rights of third parties.

**Article 30:** Authorization and concession shall give rise to the collection of a fee fixed by decree issued on the joint report of the minister in charge of finance and the minister in charge of water.

The fee shall be calculated based on the quantity of water abstracted or the quantity of pollution discharged into the public water domain, based on assessment or metering devices installed at the expense of the beneficiary of the authorization or concession. Existing installations shall have a period of two years from the publication of this code in the Official Journal to comply.

**Article 31:** The implementing procedures for Articles 18 to 30 above shall be fixed by decree, particularly with regard to:
- the thresholds for submission to the declaration, authorization, and concession procedures;
- the definition of domestic use of water;
- the procedures for declaration, authorization, and concession;
- the conditions under which checks for compliance with requirements are carried out;
- the conditions for determining the basis and rate of the fee due for abstraction or pollution.

**SUB-TITLE II - PROTECTION STANDARDS**

**Article 32:** To address a threat or the consequences of accidents, drought, accidental pollution, floods, or a risk of shortage, the minister in charge of water may, by right and without compensation, order measures for the limitation or temporary or permanent suspension of water uses.

In the event that the water resource is threatened in terms of quantity or quality, strategic protection zones may be established on surface water or groundwater, at the initiative of the minister in charge of water.

**Article 33:** The implementing procedures for Article 32 above shall be fixed by decree, particularly with regard to:
- the conditions for implementing measures for the limitation or temporary or permanent suspension of water uses;
- the conditions for establishing strategic protection zones for the water resource.

**SUB-TITLE III - QUALITY OF WATER PROVIDED FOR HUMAN CONSUMPTION**

**Article 34:** Anyone who provides water to the public for human consumption, whether for a fee or free of charge, in any form 296 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 whatsoever, including mineral or natural water and edible ice, is required to ensure that this water complies with the potability standards defined by the legislation and regulations in force.

In the case of public distribution of drinking water, the water distribution service is required to ensure that the distributed water complies with the standards mentioned in the preceding paragraph.

**Article 35:** The water distribution service is required to have the distributed water analyzed periodically and as many times as it deems necessary in cases of an epidemic or strong suspicion of an epidemic, under the supervision of agents of the Ministry of Public Health who, for this purpose, shall have free access to all installations and documentation. The costs of analysis shall be borne by the distribution service or the delegatee.

For the periodic monitoring of the quality of water provided for human consumption, a laboratory accredited by the minister in charge of public health must be used.

**Article 36:** The water distribution service is presumed liable for damages resulting from a lack of conformity of the water with the potability standards mentioned in Article 34 above, with the burden on said service to prove the existence of a cause exonerating it from liability.

**TITLE V - SANITATION OF CONNECTED DOMESTIC AND INDUSTRIAL WASTEWATER**

**Article 37:** Urban areas with a master urban plan must be equipped with a collective sanitation system to ensure the rapid and complete evacuation of connected domestic and industrial wastewater, as well as its treatment, under conditions that comply with the requirements of public health and environmental protection.

Within a period, fixed by joint order of the ministers in charge of water, local authorities, and public health, from 1096 the commissioning of the collective sanitation network, the connection to the sewer of any dwelling or establishment discharging wastewater shall be mandatory. However, the connection of wastewater other than domestic wastewater is subject to the prior approval of the network manager.

**Article 38:** In cases where the establishment of a collective sanitation network is not mandatory, the evacuation of domestic wastewater shall be carried out by means of individual sanitation installations maintained in good working order.

An order from the minister in charge of water shall set the technical standards for individual sanitation installations.

**TITLE VI - EASEMENTS**

**SUB-TITLE I - LEGAL EASEMENTS**

**Section 1 - Protection Perimeters for Water Abstraction Points Intended for Human Consumption**

**Article 39:** The purpose of protection perimeters for water abstraction points is to ensure the qualitative and quantitative protection of water intended for human consumption, whether it comes from groundwater or surface water.

**Article 40:** By order of the minister in charge of water, the following shall be established around any water abstraction point intended for human consumption:
1) an immediate protection perimeter, the land for which shall be acquired in full ownership and fenced by the institution responsible for the abstraction or distribution of drinking water, following an expropriation procedure for public utility purposes;
2) a close protection perimeter, within which the following are prohibited:
- the installation, without a prior impact assessment, of additional abstraction works;
297 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005
- the deposit of garbage, filth, debris, animal carcasses, and all products and materials likely to alter the quality of the water;
- the installation of pipelines, tanks, or depots for hydrocarbons, chemical products, or toxic substances;
- the spreading of manure, the watering and grazing of animals;
- the operation of open-pit quarries;
- the establishment of any construction whatsoever.

**Article 41:** The compensation that may be due to the owners and occupants of the land to be acquired either to constitute the immediate protection perimeter or for the implementation of easements in the close protection perimeter shall be determined according to the rules applicable to expropriation for public utility purposes.

If, as a result of the application of the prohibitions listed in 2) of Article 40 above, an owner loses the enjoyment of developed land, he shall be entitled to demand just compensation.

**Section 2 - Easements for the Protection of the Public Water Domain**

**Article 42:** Lands abutting a watercourse or a lake shall be encumbered, from the boundaries of the natural public water domain determined in accordance with Article 8 above, with a public utility easement six meters wide, known as an easement for towpaths and free access, for the benefit of agents of the ministry in charge of water, within which space no construction or fence may be erected. The minister in charge of water may require the demolition of any construction or the removal of any fence.

The same lands shall also be encumbered with an easement allowing agents of the ministry in charge of water to install thereon the measuring instruments necessary for the 1096 collection of water data as provided for in Article 14 above.

**Article 43:** Lands supporting one or more elements of the artificial public water domain of the State or of local authorities shall be encumbered with an access easement to these elements, for the benefit of agents of the ministry in charge of water.

**Section 3 - Easements for Pipes and Channels**

**Article 44:** Intervening lands shall be encumbered with an easement for pipes and channels allowing any operator of the water resource, acting for a public utility purpose, to run drinking water or wastewater pipes, water supply canals, or irrigation or drainage canals through them.

The establishment of this easement shall give rise to just compensation and shall not apply to dwellings, courtyards, and appurtenant gardens.

**SUB-TITLE II - PRIVATE EASEMENTS**

**Article 45:** Lower lands are obliged, towards those which are higher, to receive the waters that flow naturally from them without human contribution. The owner of the lower land may not erect any obstacle that prevents this flow. The owner of the higher land may do nothing that aggravates the easement of the lower land.

**TITLE VII - PUBLIC CONTRACTING AUTHORITY**

**Article 46:** The minister in charge of water shall be the contracting authority for operations relating to the development of water resources and the implementation of national public investment programs in the water sector.

**Article 47:** Municipalities shall exercise the public contracting authority with regard to the developments, installations, and equipment falling within their competence in the 298 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 water domain which they hold under the provisions of Article 2 of Ordinance No. 87-289 of 20 October 1987, when these developments, installations, or equipment have been acquired or constructed by the municipalities, either directly or through a delegated contracting authority, or when they have been transferred to them by the State.

**TITLE VIII - PUBLIC WATER SERVICE**

**SUB-TITLE I - DEFINITION**

**Article 48:** The public water service covers all activities aimed at supplying drinking water to the population, including the exploitation of mineral water and the sanitation of domestic wastewater.

The public water service may be subject to a delegation to a public or private entity.

**SUB-TITLE II - DELEGATION OF THE PUBLIC WATER SERVICE**

**Section 1 - Scope of Application**

**Article 49:** Beyond a threshold fixed by decree, issued on the proposal of the minister in charge of water, the public distribution of drinking water shall be subject to a service delegation, under the conditions provided for by this code.

In areas not subject to the delegation regime, the conditions for drinking water distribution shall be fixed by a joint order of the minister in charge of water and the minister in charge of local authorities.

**Section 2 - Modalities of Delegation**

**Sub-section 1 - Granting, Transfer, Revocation**

**Article 50:** Delegations of public drinking water service shall be granted by the 1096 contracting authority, upon proposal of the authority in charge of regulation.

These delegations shall be granted, for a determined period, to public or private legal entities and, in certain cases, to natural persons carrying out an activity in the public interest. The duration of the delegation must be sufficient to allow the delegatee to make a return on its investments.

By effect of the delegation, the delegatee shall be subject by right to all the standards applicable to public drinking water distribution services, and in particular to the rules provided for in Articles 34 to 36 above.

**Article 51:** Delegations shall be granted on the basis of a public call for applications accompanied by terms of reference.

The procedure for awarding delegations shall be implemented, by means of calls for tenders, by the authority in charge of regulation, in strict compliance with the principles of fairness, transparency, and non-discrimination.

**Article 52:** Delegations shall give rise to the payment of a fee under the conditions provided for in the terms of reference.

The procedures for allocating the proceeds of this fee shall be defined by decree issued on the joint report of the minister in charge of finance and the minister in charge of water.

**Article 53:** Modifications to the delegations or to the operators' terms of reference may be made, after approval by the contracting authority, upon the opinion of the authority in charge of regulation. The reasons for these modifications must be objective and non-discriminatory.

The opinion provided for in the paragraph above shall be reasoned. It shall be published in the official bulletin of the authority in charge of regulation.

The authority in charge of regulation shall indicate the period, which may not be less than 30 days, during which the holder of the delegation may express his opinion on the proposed modification and request a hearing. The authority in charge of regulation must 299 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 hear each operator who has so requested.

**Article 54:** Any modification to the delegation or the terms of reference that affects the obligations of its holder must take into account the economic and financial balance of the delegation.

Decisions concerning the modification of delegations are subject to the appeals provided for by the laws and regulations in force.

**Article 55:** Delegations issued pursuant to this code shall be personal. They may not be transferred to a third party without the agreement of the contracting authority and upon the proposal of the authority in charge of regulation.

The authorization or refusal of the transfer shall be notified in writing within a maximum period of two (2) months from the date of referral to the authority in charge of regulation. The refusal must be reasoned.

**Article 56:** Any transfer implies the continued compliance with all obligations related to the delegation.

In the event of a transfer of a delegation, the parties are required to inform the authority in charge of regulation at least fifteen days before the conclusion of the transfer agreement and to complete the formalities provided for this purpose.

Non-compliance with these procedures shall be sanctioned in accordance with the provisions of this code.

**Article 57:** Delegations of public drinking water service shall be withdrawn by the contracting authority, upon a report from the authority in charge of regulation, when the holder has seriously and/or repeatedly violated the legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations incumbent upon him.

The decision to withdraw a delegation shall be reasoned. It shall be taken for objective and non-discriminatory reasons.

**Article 58:** The withdrawal shall be pronounced after the interested party has received notification of the grievances and has been given the opportunity to consult the file 1096 and to present written and verbal observations.

The interested party may exercise all avenues of appeal provided for by the laws and regulations in force.

**Sub-section 2 - Sanctions**

**Article 59:** The authority in charge of regulation shall sanction, either on its own initiative or at the request of the contracting authority, a professional organization, a users' association, or a natural or legal person with an interest in acting, the breaches it observes on the part of operators benefiting from a delegation of public service.

**Article 60:** When a request for a sanction is submitted to it, the authority in charge of regulation shall issue a formal notice to the perpetrator of the breach to comply with the rules applicable to its activity within a specified period. The authority in charge of regulation shall make this formal notice public by any appropriate means.

**Article 61:** The authority in charge of regulation shall inform the operator liable to be sanctioned of the grievances raised against it. It shall grant the operator a period to allow it to consult the file concerning it and to present its written and verbal observations.

**Article 62:** When the perpetrator of the breach does not comply within the period set by the authority in charge of regulation, the latter shall impose upon it, without prejudice to any criminal penalties, one or the other of the following sanctions:
- total or partial suspension of the delegated activity;
- a pecuniary penalty proportional to the seriousness of the breach observed.

**Article 63:** The pecuniary penalties imposed on an operator in the 300 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 sector may not exceed, per breach observed, 2% of the turnover, excluding taxes, of the last completed financial year of the perpetrator of the breach. In the absence of an activity allowing this amount to be determined, the sanction may not exceed 20 million Ouguiya. Any repeated offense will result in the doubling of the pecuniary penalties.

**Article 64:** The decision by which the authority in charge of regulation inflicts a sanction on the operator shall be reasoned. It is subject to an appeal before the Administrative Chamber of the Supreme Court, within a period of one month from the notification made by the authority in charge of regulation. The sanction shall be published in the official bulletin of the authority in charge of regulation.

**Sub-section 3 - Tariffs**

**Article 65:** Water tariffs shall be approved by the minister in charge of water, upon the opinion of the authority in charge of regulation.

**Article 66:** The principles of water pricing shall be set by the authority in charge of regulation. These principles must take into account the requirements of the economic and financial balance of the delegatees.

Delegatees have the obligation to maintain cost accounting records. The tariff conditions shall remain in force for a determined period which will be defined in advance in the terms of reference of the holder of the delegation.

**Section 2 - Penal Sanctions**

**Article 70:** Any person who has carried out, operated, or participated in the carrying out of an operation mentioned in paragraph 2 of Article 18 above, without having satisfied the procedural formalities mentioned in Articles 19 to 21 above, shall be liable to:
- A fine of 50,000 to 100,000 Ouguiyas, in the case of an acknowledgment of declaration;
- Imprisonment of one to ten days and a fine of 100,000 to 300,000 Ouguiyas, or only one of these two penalties, in the case of an authorization;
- Imprisonment of ten to thirty days and a fine of 200,000 to 500,000 Ouguiyas, or only one of these two penalties, in the case of a concession.

The judge may also order the suspension of the installation's operation, or even its removal at the offender's expense.

**Article 71:** Any person who has thrown, discharged, or allowed to flow into surface or groundwater, directly or indirectly, one or more substances whose action or reactions have caused harmful effects on health and the environment, shall be liable to imprisonment of ten to thirty days and a fine of 100,000 to 300,000 Ouguiyas, or only one of these two penalties.

**Article 72:** Any person who does not comply with the requirements issued by the minister in charge of water under the procedures provided for in Articles 19 to 21 above, shall be liable to:
- A fine of 30,000 to 50,000 Ouguiyas, in the case of requirements attached to the acknowledgment of declaration;
- A fine of 50,000 to 150,000 Ouguiyas, in the case of an authorization;
1096 - A fine of 100,000 to 250,000 Ouguiyas, in the case of a concession.

The same penalty is applicable in case of absence or non-conformity of the assessment or metering devices provided for in Article 30 above.

**Article 73:** Any person who does not respect the prohibitions, requirements, and easements established within a protection perimeter for a water abstraction point intended for human consumption, as mentioned in Article 39 above, shall be liable to imprisonment of one to ten days and a fine of 50,000 to 500,000 Ouguiyas, or only one of these two penalties.

**Article 74:** Any person who does not respect the measures for the limitation or temporary or permanent suspension of uses mentioned in Article 31 above shall be liable to imprisonment of one to ten days and a fine of 100,000 to 300,000 Ouguiyas, or only one of these two penalties.

**Article 75:** Failure to connect to the sewer system within the period prescribed in Article 37 above is punishable by a fine of 50,000 to 200,000 Ouguiyas.

The same penalty is applicable in case of non-conformity or lack of maintenance of the individual sanitation installations provided for in Article 38 above.

**Article 76:** Any contracting authority or delegatee of the public water service who provides water for human consumption that does not conform to the prescribed potability standards or who fails to carry out the required quality controls, shall be liable to imprisonment of ten to 30 days and a fine of 300,000 to one million Ouguiyas, or only one of these two penalties.

**Article 77:** Any person who consumes water obtained directly or indirectly through illegal or fraudulent connections shall be liable to the penalties of imprisonment and fine, or only one of these penalties, 302 Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania on 15 June 2005 provided for theft by the penal legislation in force.

**Article 78:** Any person who is found to have abusively used water leading to waste or over-exploitation, whether intentionally or by negligence and for any purpose whatsoever, shall be liable to a fine of 3,000 to 30,000 Ouguiyas.

The same penalty is applicable to any person who has failed to bring to the attention of the administration in charge of water any fact likely to harm the water resource.

**TITLE X - TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS**

**Article 79:** Declarations filed, authorizations or concessions granted under Ordinance No. 85-144 of 4 July 1985 establishing the Water Code shall be deemed to be a declaration, authorization, or concession under this code.

When operations legally carried out without the application of the aforementioned Ordinance of 4 July 1985 become subject to declaration, authorization, or concession under this code, these operations may continue without this declaration, authorization, or concession on the condition that the operator, owner, or person in charge informs the minister in charge of water within a period of two years from the publication of this code in the Official Journal of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania.

Failure to comply with the information obligation within the prescribed period shall render any acknowledgments of declaration obtained, and any authorizations and concessions granted, null and void.

**Article 80:** The National Water Company (SNDE), as created by Decree No. 88-2001/PM of 29 July 2001 splitting the National Water and Electricity Company into two national companies, shall be deemed to benefit from a delegation within the meaning of this code. 1096 A decree will specify the duration of this delegation and the conditions of its exercise.

**Article 81:** The National Drinking Water and Sanitation Agency (ANEPA), recognized as being of public utility by Decree No. 2002-19 of 31 March 2002, shall be deemed to benefit from delegations, within the meaning of this code, in the urban or rural centers managed under its supervision and which, on the date of publication of the decree provided for in Article 49 above, reach the threshold provided for in that same article.

The delegation provided for in the paragraph above shall be granted for a period of three years and exercised under the conditions defined in terms of reference approved by the authority in charge of regulation.

During the period provided for in the paragraph above, the authority in charge of regulation shall implement the procedure provided for in Articles 50 et seq. above in order to select service delegatees.

In the event that the procedure for selecting a delegatee is unsuccessful, the minister in charge of water may, by order, extend the duration of the delegation granted to ANEPA, upon request of the authority in charge of regulation.

**TITLE XI - FINAL PROVISIONS**

**Article 82:** The provisions of this code shall be specified, as needed, by regulatory means.

**Article 83:** All prior provisions contrary to the provisions of this law, and in particular those of Ordinance No. 85-144 of 4 July 1985 establishing the Water Code, are hereby repealed.

**Article 84:** This law shall be published under the emergency procedure and executed as the law of the State.
