### **Part I**
### **General Provisions**

**Article 1 (Revised). Purpose**
This Law defines the principles, regulations, and measures concerning the management, administration, protection, development, and use of water and water resources; the prevention of damage from water; and the restoration of affected areas, in order to ensure the sustainable quality and quantity of water and water resources. It aims to meet the needs of the people's livelihoods, agricultural and industrial production, and services; ensure the protection of the social and natural environment; promote development in line with green, sustainable principles and national security; integrate with the region and the international community; and contribute to the protection and development of the national economy and society.

**Article 2 (Revised). Water and Water Resources**
Water is a type of natural resource that exists as a liquid, which is the primary and most fundamental resource among water resources.
Water resources are natural resources comprising water, water sources, and living or non-living things within water sources, such as plants, aquatic animals, rocks, ore-bearing rocks, sand, silt, and minerals.

**Article 3 (New). Definitions**
The terms used in this Law shall have the following meanings:
1.  **Water source** means a place where water collects, resides, is stored, or flows, either permanently or temporarily; water sources may be natural or man-made and exist on the surface, underground, and in the atmosphere;
2.  **Surface water source** means water on the land surface, such as rivers, streams, creeks, channels, ponds, canals, lakes, wetlands, swamps, oxbow lakes, waterfalls, reservoirs, sinkholes, and cave dripwater;
3.  **Groundwater source** means water under the ground in layers, caverns, or channels, or mixed with soil, such as from dug wells, springs, mineral water, artesian wells, and underground streams;
4.  **Atmospheric water source** means rainwater, hail, fog, and dew;
5.  **Reservoir** means a system for impounding water by excavating a pond or damming a creek or stream to ensure sufficient water is stored to meet the needs of livelihoods, agricultural production, the stability of the natural and social environment, and other purposes;
6.  **Dam, weir** means a structure built to obstruct a watercourse and impound water for hydropower generation, irrigation, or other purposes;
7.  **Wastewater** means water containing contaminants exceeding standard values or water that has been used, which causes a change in its quality, such as from household, agricultural, or industrial use and other activities;
8.  **Integrated Water Resource Management** means a process that promotes the coordinated management and development of water and water resources, land, forests, and other resources in a harmonious, balanced, and closely integrated manner to ensure that the management and development of such resources yield maximum economic, social, and environmental benefits;
9.  **Erosion prevention and control** means the prevention and inhibition of erosion in river basins, reservoirs, and along water banks;
10. **Conservation Pool** means an area of water, such as a deep pool within a water source or along a river, that has been designated as a permanently prohibited zone to serve as a safe habitat and breeding ground for aquatic animal resources;
11. **Household** means a house with an individual or group of individuals who live together and regularly conduct daily activities jointly; one household may consist of one or more families with one or more family books;
12. **Ecosystem** means the interrelationship, both positive and negative, between living and non-living things that exist in nature;
13. **Wastewater treatment pond** means a pond that eliminates or destroys contaminants in wastewater completely or to the minimum level according to prescribed standards, without causing pollution to the environment.

**Article 4. Ownership of Water and Water Resources**
Water and water resources within the territory of the Lao PDR are the property of the national community, for which the State acts as the representative in managing and allocating them for use by various sectors in a comprehensive and reasonable manner.
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations shall have the right to possess or use water and water resources for any activity only after receiving permission from the relevant state agency, with the exception of small-scale use.

**Article 5 (Revised). State Policy on Water and Water Resources**
The State shall encourage and promote the use of water and water resources that is clean, safe, economical, and efficient to achieve maximum benefits, and to ensure the protection of water, water resources, and ecosystems in line with green and sustainable principles, by building awareness and educating everyone to see the benefits and importance of water and water resources.
The State shall encourage and promote human resource development, infrastructure construction, scientific and technological research that is environmentally friendly, and the development of local wisdom in the effective management, administration, protection, use, development, and restoration of water and water resources.
The State shall promote investment by domestic and foreign individuals, legal entities, or organizations in the protection, development, and restoration of water and water resources according to existing potential, in order to supply water to citizens, including in remote areas, and to use water and water resources for national socio-economic development.

**Article 6 (Revised). Principles of Water and Water Resources Management**
The management of water and water resources shall be implemented in accordance with the following principles:
1.  Ensuring the management, use, conservation, and protection of water, water resources, and the environment in line with green and sustainable principles;
2.  Conforming with the National Socio-Economic Development Plan, the strategic plan for the management, administration, and use of water and water resources, the water and water resources management plan, the Strategic Environmental Assessment, integrated resource management, and national defense and public security work;
3.  Prioritizing the prevention of harm to water and water resources and emphasizing the restoration of water and water resources;
4.  Ensuring the participation of individuals, legal entities, and relevant organizations in the planning, development, use, and protection of water and water resources for sustainability;
5.  Bearing responsibility for damage to water and water resources that one has caused.

**Article 7 (Revised). Obligation to Manage, Protect, and Use Water and Water Resources**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations have an obligation to manage, protect, develop, use, and restore water and water resources to ensure the sustainability of the quantity and quality of water and water resources.

**Article 8 (New). Scope of Application of the Law**
This Law applies to domestic and foreign individuals, legal entities, and organizations who live and operate within the Lao PDR.

**Article 9 (New). International Cooperation**
The State shall promote relations and cooperation with foreign countries, the region, and the international community in the management, protection, development, use, and restoration of water and water resources through the exchange of lessons learned, information, science, and technology, and by implementing treaties and international agreements to which the Lao PDR is a party.

### **Part II**
### **Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources**

**Article 10 (New). Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources**
The Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources is the fundamental policy concerning the work of managing, using, protecting, and developing water resources.
The Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources comprises plans, programs, projects, and activities related to water resources.

**Article 11 (New). National-Level Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources**
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall create and review the national-level Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources to align with the National Socio-Economic Development Plan for each period, in coordination and consultation with relevant sectors at the central and local levels, for submission to the Government for consideration, and then for submission to the National Assembly for consideration and adoption.

**Article 12 (New). Local-Level Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources**
The Provincial/Capital Department of Natural Resources and Environment shall create and review the local-level Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources to align with the Socio-Economic Development Plan of the province or capital and the national-level Strategic Plan for the Management, Administration, and Use of Water and Water Resources for each period, in coordination and consultation with relevant sectors at the local level, for submission to the Provincial Governor or Capital Mayor for consideration, and then for submission to the Provincial People's Council for consideration and adoption.

### **Part III**
### **Survey, Inventory, River Basins, and Reservoirs**

#### **Chapter 1**
#### **Survey and Inventory of Water and Water Resources**

**Article 13 (Revised). Survey and Inventory**
The survey of water and water resources is the collection of data on water and water resources through measurement, exploratory drilling, and analysis of the physical and chemical characteristics of water and water resources, for the purpose of allocation and use in planning the management of water and water resources.
The inventory of water and water resources is the compilation, categorization, and coding of data on water and water resources, which is then entered into the database of the natural resources and environment sector to serve as a tool for monitoring, inspecting, assessing conditions, analyzing issues, supporting decision-making, and managing, planning, and developing water and water resources.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment is responsible for the work of surveying water and water resources, particularly international water sources, trans-provincial water sources, watershed headwaters, water-related land areas, and river basins nationwide; compiling an inventory of water and water resources that have been surveyed and obtained from other sectors; and registering data on water use by various sectors, in coordination with relevant sectors and local administrative authorities.
For surveys related to the use of water and water resources by each sector, the relevant sector shall be responsible for surveying and creating an inventory of use, and then providing the data to the natural resources and environment sector for compilation into the national water resources database.

**Article 14 (New). Information System for Water and Water Resources**
The information system for water and water resources is a fundamental and necessary tool for the management of water and water resources to ensure quality, convenience, timeliness, speed, and accuracy in the use of information for effective planning of water and water resources management.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment is responsible for collecting, compiling, and creating the information system, and for using and providing information on national water and water resources, ensuring it is linked to the information system of the National Statistics Center, in coordination with other sectors and relevant local administrative authorities.
Local-level natural resources and environment sectors are responsible for creating an information database within their scope of responsibility, ensuring it is linked to the national information system for water and water resources, and coordinating with relevant local-level sectors.
Other relevant sectors, such as agriculture and forestry, energy and mines, public works and transport, industry and commerce, public health, information, culture and tourism, and education and sports, shall provide data on water use in their respective fields to the natural resources and environment sector, as well as update, manage, and use the information database on the use of water and water resources of their own sector, ensuring it is linked to the national information system for water and water resources, and coordinating with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities.
The management of the national information system for water and water resources is defined in separate regulations.

**Article 15 (New). Access to and Use of Information on Water and Water Resources**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations may access and use information on water and water resources in the national information system for water and water resources. For information related to research and surveys of water and water resources used for business purposes, fees and service charges must be paid in accordance with regulations.

#### **Chapter 2**
#### **River Basins**

**Article 16 (Revised). River Basin**
A river basin is the area of land, water, and forest from the headwaters to the river mouth, defined by watersheds, where rainfall collects and flows into a water source system, such as the Mekong, Ou, Ngum, Theun-Kading, Sebangfai, and Sekong rivers.
River basins within the territory of the Lao PDR are of three sizes, as follows:
1.  **Large river basin** means the Mekong river basin or other river basins that are tributaries of the Mekong or other rivers, with an area greater than four thousand square kilometers;
2.  **Medium river basin** means a river basin that is a tributary of the Mekong or other rivers, with an area from one thousand to four thousand square kilometers;
3.  **Small river basin** means a river basin that is a tributary of the Mekong or other rivers, with an area of less than one thousand square kilometers.

**Article 17. Allocation of River Basins**
The allocation of river basins, to determine the division, management, and use of water and water resources in accordance with objectives and with effectiveness, shall be based on surveys and collected data.

**Article 18 (New). Management of River Basins**
The management of river basins shall be carried out in accordance with integrated water resource management, based on the conditions of each river basin, and with management decentralized as follows:
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment is responsible for managing large river basins and river basins that flow through two countries or two or more provinces.
The Provincial Department of Natural Resources and Environment is responsible for managing medium river basins located within its province or capital.
The District Office of Natural Resources and Environment is responsible for managing small river basins located within its district, municipality, or city.
All levels of local administrative authorities have the duty to protect all sizes of river basins located within their territory.
The detailed decentralization of river basin management is defined in an inventory created by the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment.

**Article 19 (New). River Basin Management Plan**
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall take the lead in coordinating with relevant sectors and local administrative authorities to create a river basin management plan according to its scope of responsibility every five years, and then submit it to the Government for consideration and adoption.
The Provincial/Capital Department of Natural Resources and Environment shall take the lead in coordinating with relevant departments and local administrative authorities at the district level to create a river basin management plan according to its scope of responsibility every five years, and then submit it to the Provincial Governor or Capital Mayor for consideration and adoption.
The District/Municipality/City Office of Natural Resources and Environment shall take the lead in coordinating with relevant offices and village administrative authorities to create a river basin management plan according to its scope of responsibility every five years, and then submit it to the District Chief, Municipality Head, or City Mayor for consideration and adoption.
The river basin management plan must be consistent with the vision, the national-level strategic plan for the management, administration, and use of water and water resources, and the National or local Socio-Economic Development Plan, based on the conditions, special characteristics, and topography of each river basin in each locality.

**Article 20 (New). Coordination in the Use of Water and Water Resources Related to River Basins**
The natural resources and environment sector shall coordinate with relevant sectors and local administrative authorities to study and define coordination mechanisms and measures for the management and use of water, water storage, water release, problem-solving, and provision of information concerning that river basin, in accordance with the river basin management plan, the Strategic Environmental Assessment, and regulations on Social and Natural Environmental Impact Assessment.

**Article 21 (New). Determination of Minimum Flow**
The determination of minimum flow is the determination of the volume of water flow that must be maintained in a watercourse or river basin in areas where there is development and water use, in order to ensure basic needs for the people's livelihoods and the sustainability of the ecosystem in the river basin.
The natural resources and environment sector shall determine the minimum flow for each period as required, in coordination with relevant sectors and local administrative authorities.

#### **Chapter 3**
#### **Reservoirs**

**Article 22 (New). Types of Reservoirs**
There are two types of reservoirs, as follows:
1.  Natural reservoirs;
2.  Man-made reservoirs.

A natural reservoir is a pond, lake, wetland, oxbow lake, marsh, swamp, or other area formed by nature, where the volume of water differs between the dry and rainy seasons.
A man-made reservoir is an area created by excavating a pond, damming a watercourse, or other methods to impound water for a specific purpose, such as for electricity, mining, industry, agriculture, irrigation, fisheries, household use, tourism, or ecosystems.

**Article 23 (New). Management of Reservoirs**
The natural resources and environment sector, in collaboration with relevant sectors and local administrative authorities, shall manage natural reservoirs by surveying, creating an inventory, and establishing measures for the management of such reservoirs to ensure their abundance.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall issue general regulations on the management of natural and man-made reservoirs, in coordination with relevant ministries, ministry-equivalent state organizations, and local administrative authorities.
For reservoirs created by any sector, such as the energy and mines sector or the agriculture and forestry sector, that sector shall be responsible for its management, for issuing specific regulations, and for creating a coordination mechanism, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other relevant sectors, and local administrative authorities, and in accordance with the river basin management plan and relevant laws.

**Article 24 (New). Obligations of Project Developers or Operators**
A project developer is a person who has received permission to study, survey, design, construct, and operate a project.
An operator is a person who has received permission to conduct any activity using water for business purposes, such as aquaculture, tourism, or a water supply plant.
A project developer must create a management plan for the reservoir within their development scope by establishing safety measures for the reservoir, preventing erosion, floods, and droughts, creating a plan for planting and restoring watershed protection forests, maintaining minimum flow, managing water quality, protecting aquatic biodiversity, addressing emergencies, restoring damage that occurs, and other matters, in accordance with the river basin management plan, regulations on Social and Natural Environmental Impact Assessment, and other relevant regulations. The project developer must report and provide information on reservoir management to the relevant sector and the natural resources and environment sector.
An operator must protect water quality and the abundance of the watercourse, not discharge waste into the river, restore any damage that occurs, participate in protecting aquatic biodiversity, prevent bank erosion, preserve the beauty of the natural scenery, and strictly comply with relevant laws and regulations.

**Article 25 (New). Emergency Response Measures in Case of Problems from a Reservoir**
In the event of an emergency in a reservoir, the project developer or operator must immediately report to the natural resources and environment sector, other relevant sectors, and local administrative authorities to implement timely response measures, such as providing warnings and assisting and relocating people.
The Provincial Governor, Capital Mayor, District Chief, Municipality Head, or City Mayor shall be the one to declare an emergency within their locality, defining response measures to assist and relocate people from the emergency area, after which a restoration plan shall be developed to return the situation to its original state.
The Prime Minister shall be the one to declare an emergency and define response and restoration measures on a nationwide scale in the event of an emergency with severe impacts.
The project owner has the obligation and responsibility to implement emergency response measures if the emergency was caused by their own actions.

### **Part IV**
### **Protection of Water and Water Resources**

**Article 26 (Revised). Protection of Water and Water Resources**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations have an obligation to protect water and water resources to ensure their abundance, not to cause damage to water and water resources, and to protect, restore, and rehabilitate forest and land resources in river basins in strict accordance with the management plan for the allocation of water sources, forests, and land, and according to the season, especially in headwater areas or around water sources.

**Article 27 (New). Protected Zones for Water and Water Resources**
A protected zone for water and water resources is an area reserved for protecting the quality and quantity of water for consumption and use, preventing floods and droughts, conserving aquatic flora and fauna, ecosystems, and areas of natural, historical, cultural, tourism, educational, research, and scientific experimental value.
Protected zones for water and water resources are as follows:
1.  Zones reserved for consumption and use;
2.  Headwater areas;
3.  Zones at risk of floods and droughts;
4.  Water-related land areas;
5.  Zones with risks to the quality and quantity of groundwater;
6.  Zones of natural, historical, and cultural value;
7.  Aquatic biodiversity conservation areas and riparian zones;
8.  Areas with risks to ecosystems in communities or towns.

**Article 28 (New). Designation of Protected Zones for Water and Water Resources**
The Government shall designate national-level protected zones for water and water resources upon the proposal of the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment and based on the approval of the National Assembly Standing Committee.
The Provincial Governor or Capital Mayor shall designate provincial-level protected zones for water and water resources upon the proposal of the Provincial/Capital Department of Natural Resources and Environment and based on the approval of the Provincial People's Council Standing Committee.
The District Chief, Municipality Head, or City Mayor shall designate district-level protected zones for water and water resources upon the proposal of the District/Municipality/City Office of Natural Resources and Environment and based on the approval of the Provincial Governor or Capital Mayor.
The Village Chief shall define the boundaries of village-level conservation pools upon the proposal of the village's economic-finance unit, in accordance with the specific characteristics and location of the conservation pool within the village, based on the decision of a village meeting, and with the approval of the District Chief, Municipality Head, or City Mayor.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall establish general regulations on the designation of protected zones for water and water resources, in coordination with other relevant ministries, organizations, and local administrative authorities.

**Article 29 (New). Management of Protected Zones for Water and Water Resources**
The natural resources and environment sector shall manage protected zones for water and water resources in coordination with relevant sectors and local administrative authorities by establishing regulations or installing markers.
Within protected zones for water and water resources, it is not permitted to build or construct obstructions that alter the flow of water; cut trees in headwater areas or around water sources; engage in agricultural or industrial production or animal husbandry; fill, modify, or change the land area of rivers, ponds, lakes, or wetlands; drill, dredge, or alter the land surface in a way that would cause erosion; extract rock, minerals, soil, or sand; or dispose of garbage, waste, wastewater, poisons, chemical substances, explosives, or bury corpses or animal carcasses and other things that would cause damage to the protected zone for water and water resources.
As for traditional family-based cultivation and animal husbandry for subsistence by people in protected riparian zones, this use is permitted but must ensure that it does not negatively impact water quality or cause bank erosion.

**Article 30 (Revised). Water Quality Standards and Wastewater Discharge Standards**
Water quality standards are the measurements, units, and standard values of water quality based on the physical, chemical, and biological characteristics of water, to serve as a benchmark for comparing the quality of water in rivers, streams, creeks, channels, ponds, lakes, groundwater, and other natural water bodies.
Wastewater discharge standards are the measurements of water quality based on the physical, chemical, and biological characteristics of water, to determine whether water to be discharged from various activities complies with wastewater discharge standards, such as discharge from mining, factories, hotels, hospitals, markets, households, agriculture, and other activities.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment, in coordination with other relevant ministries, organizations, and local administrative authorities, shall define water quality standards and wastewater discharge standards.

**Article 31 (New). Permission for Wastewater Discharge**
The discharge of wastewater into a water source by an individual, legal entity, or organization must be treated to meet wastewater discharge standards and requires permission from the natural resources and environment sector. Fees and service charges for wastewater discharge must be paid in accordance with regulations.
Wastewater discharge is classified into three scales: small, medium, and large, based on the volume of wastewater and the concentration of chemical substances mixed in the wastewater. For wastewater discharge from households that does not have a severe impact, a permit to discharge wastewater is not required.
A wastewater discharge permit is valid for two years and may be renewed, based on the conditions and wastewater discharge standards.
A wastewater discharge permit may be suspended or modified in the following cases:
-   Occurrence of drought;
-   The ecosystem is destroyed;
-   Wastewater is discharged in excess of the permitted standard values.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall establish regulations concerning the management of wastewater discharge, in coordination with relevant ministries and local administrative authorities.

**Article 32 (New). Canal Systems, Drainage Pipes, and Wastewater Treatment Ponds**
In the urban planning of towns, systems of canals, drainage pipes, and wastewater treatment ponds must be constructed to ensure the protection of water quality, the health of people, animals, and plants, and the quality of the environment within urban areas, as well as to protect and reserve water-related land areas, such as ponds and lakes, which serve as natural wastewater treatment systems and as water retention areas to effectively prevent and mitigate floods and droughts.

**Article 33 (New). Water Pollution**
Water pollution is pollution caused by the contamination of water by toxic substances exceeding national environmental standards, occurring naturally or from human actions, and affecting rivers, streams, creeks, channels, ponds, lakes, groundwater, the health of people, animals, plants, other living organisms, and ecosystems.
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations that have caused water pollution must be responsible for the resulting damage and must restore the affected water source to its original or better condition.
Furthermore, the party causing the damage will also be subject to legal proceedings.

**Article 34 (New). Water-Related Land Areas**
Water-related land areas are lands under water or within the vicinity of a water source, such as wetlands, ponds, lakes, flooded grasslands, land at springs, riverbank land, islands, newly formed land, abandoned or oxbow riverbeds, and land created by the modification or diversion of a watercourse, or where water is retained or flows, whether naturally occurring or man-made, and whether permanent or temporary.
In the planning of national and local socio-economic development, including land use planning, importance must be given to water-related land areas in order to preserve ecosystems, natural wastewater treatment, and flood prevention.
The natural resources and environment sector shall survey and allocate water-related land areas and create plans for the protection, development, and restoration of such land, in coordination with relevant sectors, local administrative authorities, and with the participation of the people in those areas.

**Article 35 (New). Use, Protection, and Development of Water-Related Land Areas**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations may develop and use these water-related land areas, but must obtain permission from the natural resources and environment sector and must ensure that they do not cause damage to the quantity and quality of water, do not alter the course and flow of water without permission, avoid causing erosion and floods, and ensure the sustainability of the ecosystem, in accordance with the law.
The protection and development of water-related land areas must be consistent with the National and local Socio-Economic Development Plan, the national master plan for land use allocation, and regulations on the management of water-related land areas, without adversely affecting the ecosystem and the social and natural environment.
Individuals, legal entities, and organizations are not permitted to fill in natural water-related land areas designated as protected zones, such as ponds, canals, lakes, and streams.

### **Part V**
### **Use and Services Related to Water and Water Resources**

#### **Chapter 1**
#### **Use of Water and Water Resources**

**Article 36 (Revised). Purposes of Water Source Use**
The purposes of water source use are as follows:
1.  For the consumption and use of citizens;
2.  For irrigation, agricultural and forestry production, animal husbandry, and fisheries;
3.  For electricity generation and mining;
4.  For industrial production;
5.  For communication and transport;
6.  For medical and sanitation purposes;
7.  For recreation, sports, and tourism;
8.  For use for other purposes.
Any given water source may be used for one or more purposes, on a case-by-case basis and as appropriate.
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations wishing to use a water source must survey and identify the water source to be used, and then submit a proposal to the natural resources and environment sector for permission to use the water, in accordance with regulations.

**Article 37 (New). Use of Water Sources for Consumption**
A water source for consumption is a water source allocated specifically to serve only the consumption needs of citizens. Individuals, legal entities, or organizations are strictly prohibited from using it for any other purpose.
In the area surrounding a water source for consumption, it is not permitted to construct hospitals, markets, factories, restaurants, animal farms, hotels, or other activities that would have a negative impact on water quality.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall designate water sources and the extent of the surrounding area for consumption.

**Article 38 (Revised). Right to Use Water**
Individuals, legal entities, and organizations have the right to use water for any purpose, but must do so economically, reasonably, effectively, and with the least possible impact on the social and natural environment.
Water use is classified into three scales: small, medium, and large.
Medium- and large-scale water use requires permission from the natural resources and environment sector, and fees for water use, duties, and service charges must be paid in accordance with regulations.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall establish regulations for the use of water at each scale, in coordination with relevant ministries and local administrative authorities.

**Article 39 (Revised). Small-Scale Water Use**
Small-scale water use is the use of water as follows:
1.  Use for consumption and daily life for subsistence;
2.  Use in cultivation and animal husbandry by the people for subsistence;
3.  Use for irrigation of one thousand five hundred cubic meters per hectare per day.
Small-scale water use does not require permission and is exempt from duties and service charges for use.

**Article 40 (Revised). Medium-Scale Water Use**
Medium-scale water use is the use of water for the following purposes:
1.  Electricity generation with a storage volume of more than one hundred thousand to two hundred million cubic meters, and a reservoir area of one hundred to one thousand five hundred hectares;
2.  Use for irrigation of one thousand five hundred to one hundred fifty thousand cubic meters per activity per day;
3.  Use in industry of fifteen to one thousand cubic meters per day;
4.  Use in mineral production of fifteen to one thousand cubic meters per day.

**Article 41 (Revised). Large-Scale Water Use**
Large-scale use is the use of water for the following purposes:
1.  Electricity generation with a storage volume of more than two hundred million cubic meters or a reservoir area of more than one thousand five hundred hectares;
2.  Use for irrigation of more than one hundred fifty thousand cubic meters per activity per day;
3.  Use in industry of more than one thousand cubic meters per day;
4.  Use in mineral production of more than one thousand cubic meters per day.

**Article 42 (New). Water Use Permit**
A water use permit is an official document that grants the right to use water to the recipient.
The issuance of a use permit shall comply with regulations on Social and Natural Environmental Impact Assessment and other relevant regulations.

**Article 43 (New). Suspension, Modification, and Revocation of a Water Use Permit**
A water use permit may be suspended or modified in any of the following cases:
1.  There is a drought or an emergency;
2.  To maintain the minimum flow;
3.  Upon the request of the project developer who holds the water use permit;
4.  There is a public complaint;
5.  There is a violation of water use regulations that does not cause a severe impact.
A water use permit shall be revoked in any of the following cases:
1.  The water use permit is transferred to another party without permission;
2.  There is a final and conclusive court judgment;
3.  There is a need to use it for the public interest, for which appropriate compensation must be provided;
4.  There is a severe impact on the social and natural environment;
5.  There is a violation of water use regulations that has a severe impact or occurs after a warning has been issued.

**Article 44 (New). Extraction of Ore-Bearing Rock, Sand, and Silt from a Water Source**
Individuals, legal entities, and organizations intending to extract ore-bearing rock, sand, and silt from a water source must obtain permission from the relevant sector, based on coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, with the exception of use by people for their households.
The extraction of rock, ore, sand, and silt from a water source must not alter the flow or course of the water, must not cause bank erosion, must not affect the aquatic ecosystem or water quality, and must comply with technical standards, laws, and regulations.

#### **Chapter 2**
#### **Groundwater Use**

**Article 45 (New). Groundwater**
Groundwater is natural water that is under the ground, such as from artesian wells, springs, and underground streams.

**Article 46 (New). Purposes of Groundwater Use**
The purposes of groundwater use are as follows:
1.  For the consumption and use of citizens;
2.  For irrigation, animal husbandry, and agricultural and forestry production;
3.  For mining;
4.  For industrial production;
5.  For medical and sanitation purposes;
6.  For use for other purposes as permitted.
Groundwater may be used for one or more purposes, on a case-by-case basis and as appropriate.

**Article 47 (New). Right to Use Groundwater**
Individuals, legal entities, and organizations have the right to use groundwater for any purpose, but must do so economically, reasonably, effectively, and with the least possible impact on the social and natural environment.
Groundwater use is classified into three scales: small, medium, and large.
Small-scale groundwater use with a volume of twenty cubic meters or less does not require a use permit and is exempt from resource use fees, duties, and service charges, but the village administrative authority must be notified.
Medium-scale groundwater use with a volume of more than twenty to fifty cubic meters requires permission from the District/Municipality/City Office of Natural Resources and Environment, and fees for water resource use, duties, and service charges must be paid in accordance with regulations.
Large-scale groundwater use with a volume of more than fifty cubic meters per day requires permission from the Department of Natural Resources and Environment, and fees for water resource use, duties, and service charges must be paid in accordance with regulations.
The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment shall establish regulations concerning the management of groundwater, in coordination with relevant ministries, ministry-equivalent state organizations, and local administrative authorities.

#### **Chapter 3**
#### **Services Related to Water and Water Resources**

**Article 48 (New). Services Related to Water and Water Resources**
Services related to water and water resources are as follows:
1.  Consultancy;
2.  Groundwater drilling or boring;
3.  Water quality analysis;
4.  Other services.

**Article 49 (New). Consultancy Services**
Consultancy services are services related to the study, survey, design, analysis, assessment, and preparation of reports on the condition of water and water resources for investment projects or development activities, as well as the creation of management and monitoring plans for water and water resources.
A consultancy service provider must meet the following conditions:
1.  Have at least three specialists in fields related to water and water resources with a Master's degree or higher and at least three years of experience;
2.  Have at least three technicians with a Bachelor's degree and at least two years of experience;
3.  Have an office and sufficient specialized equipment.

**Article 50 (New). Groundwater Drilling or Boring Services**
Groundwater drilling or boring services are services related to the study, survey, drilling, or boring of soil layers, and assessment of the quantity and quality of groundwater.
A provider of medium- and large-scale groundwater drilling or boring services must meet the following conditions:
1.  Have at least one geologist with an intermediate-level vocational certificate or higher and at least two years of experience;
2.  Have at least one technician with experience in drilling or boring and at least two years of experience;
3.  Have an office, machinery that meets standards, and a complete set of drilling or boring equipment.
For small-scale groundwater drilling or boring services, there must be a technician who has undergone training.
In the case that ancient artifacts, fossils, valuable minerals, or objects of cultural, economic, or geological educational value are discovered during drilling or boring, the groundwater drilling or boring service provider must immediately report to the relevant sector and local administrative authority.

**Article 51 (New). Water Quality Analysis Services**
Water quality analysis services are services related to the analysis and testing of water quality.
A water quality analysis service provider must meet the following conditions:
1.  Have a laboratory supervisor with a Master's degree or higher in chemistry, environmental science, general science, or another related field, and at least five years of analysis experience;
2.  Have at least three laboratory analysis staff with a Bachelor's degree or higher in chemistry, environmental science, general science, or another related field, and at least three years of analysis experience;
3.  Have a laboratory with a complete set of equipment and an analysis system that meets international standards.
Individuals, legal entities, and organizations that will provide water quality analysis services, including the establishment of such an analysis laboratory, must obtain permission from the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment.

**Article 52 (New). Operation of Services Related to Water and Water Resources**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations intending to operate services related to water and water resources shall submit an application with supporting documents to the relevant sector as stipulated in the Law on Investment Promotion and must obtain technical permission from the natural resources and environment sector.

### **Part VI**
### **Prevention and Mitigation of Damage from Water**

**Article 53 (Revised). Damage from Water**
Damage from water is damage caused by water due to natural disasters or human actions, such as floods, flash floods, high water levels, overflows, salinization, water pollution, turbidity, low water levels, drought, and erosion.
The natural resources and environment sector, in coordination with relevant sectors and local administrative authorities, shall create plans and define measures to address damage from water.

**Article 54 (Revised). Prevention and Mitigation of Floods and Droughts**
The natural resources and environment sector, in coordination with relevant parties, shall disseminate meteorological and hydrological information to local administrative authorities; build the capacity of technical staff in flood and drought risk management; and monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of the implementation of regulations related to natural disaster risks, including providing warnings and assistance to the public to ensure timely preparedness to respond to such disasters.
Local administrative authorities shall take the lead in directing, preparing plans, and using effective measures, and shall comply with the overall plan and instructions issued by higher levels concerning the prevention and mitigation of floods and droughts. In the event of a sudden flood, the local administrative authority in that area must take the lead in providing a timely response.
Local administrative authorities have the duty to prepare a budget plan and mobilize materials, equipment, labor, and contributions from individuals, legal entities, or organizations for the prevention and mitigation of floods and droughts. When the work is completed, the materials and equipment must be returned to their owners, and compensation for damage to such materials and equipment shall be provided as appropriate.
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations have an obligation to cooperate with local administrative authorities in the prevention and mitigation of floods and droughts.

### **Part VII**
### **Restoration of Water and Water Resources**

**Article 55 (New). Damaged or Degraded Water and Water Resources**
Damaged or degraded water and water resources refer to the negative physical, biological, and chemical changes in the quantity and quality of water and water resources, such as the drying up or lowering of water levels in watercourses and groundwater, and contamination by toxic substances from the discharge of wastewater from communities, service establishments, processing industries, mines, agriculture, and other activities.

**Article 56 (New). Restoration of Water and Water Resources**
The restoration of water and water resources is the process of returning the quantity and quality of damaged or degraded water and water resources to a good or normal state through the use of various methods, such as reforestation, conservation of headwater forests, economical water use, promotion of the use of bio-fertilizers, limitation of garbage disposal, and wastewater treatment.
The treatment of wastewater in various water sources must be done through chemical, biological, physical, physico-chemical, or other processes.
The methods and procedures for each type of wastewater treatment are defined in separate regulations.

**Article 57 (New). Responsibility for the Restoration of Water and Water Resources**
Individuals, legal entities, and organizations that operate investment projects or various activities that cause damage to water and water resources must be responsible for rectifying and restoring what was damaged or degraded, with monitoring by local administrative authorities and relevant sectors.
In the case of a natural disaster that has caused damage to water and water resources, local administrative authorities and relevant sectors must coordinate to establish measures to restore the affected area. Individuals, legal entities, or organizations must contribute capital, intellect, labor, and materials.

### **Part VIII**
### **Servitudes**

**Article 58 (Revised). Servitude**
A servitude is a factual condition that must be accepted according to nature or law.

**Article 59 (Revised). Natural Servitude**
The user of an upstream plot of land does not have the right to obstruct the normal flow of water in a way that would cause damage to the user of water on a downstream plot of land. In the case of an obstruction that causes damage to the water user on the downstream plot, the user of the upstream plot must be responsible for compensating for the damage.
The user of a downstream plot of land must allow water that flows naturally from an upstream plot to enter or pass through their land. In the case that the user of the downstream plot obstructs the water, causing damage to the user of the upstream plot, that user of the downstream plot must be responsible for compensating for the damage that occurred and must rectify the situation to allow the water to flow naturally.

**Article 60 (Revised). Legal Servitude**
A person who has built or who has received permission to conduct any activity has the right to a right-of-way for all types of water, including wastewater, by using pipes or digging channels through the land of an individual, legal entity, or organization, but must take appropriate measures and not cause damage to the user of the land through which the water flows.
In the case that the person who has built or received permission has caused damage to the user of the land providing the right-of-way, that person and the person who benefits from such a situation must provide compensation for the damage as appropriate.
If the user of the land providing the right-of-way also benefits from it, no compensation for damage shall be calculated, unless the damage is substantial, in which case compensation must be calculated appropriately.

**Article 61 (Revised). Rights of the User of Land or a Person Adjacent to Land that is a Right-of-Way for Water**
The user of land or a person adjacent to land that is a right-of-way for water has the right to use the structures that serve as the water's channel, such as water pipes or channels, as stipulated in Article 60 of this Law, but must contribute to various expenses, such as construction costs, usage fees, and maintenance costs, in proportion to their use.

**Article 62 (Revised). Rights of Sectors and Local Administrative Authorities**
Sectors and local administrative authorities have the right to install or construct necessary items on the land of an individual, legal entity, or organization for the distribution or storage of equipment and tools; for the inspection or improvement of water quality; as temporary shelter for those affected by floods; including the right to run power lines and water pipes through land and along drainage channels, but must provide compensation for any damage that occurs as appropriate.

### **Part IX**
### **Water and Water Resources Fund**

**Article 63 (New). Water and Water Resources Fund**
The Water and Water Resources Fund is the same fund as the Environmental Protection Fund as stipulated in the Law on Environmental Protection.

**Article 64 (New). Sources of the Water and Water Resources Fund**
In addition to the funding sources stipulated in the Law on Environmental Protection, the Water and Water Resources Fund also has other funding sources, as follows:
1.  Fees for the restoration of water and water resources from investment projects and various activities;
2.  Service charges for the use of water and water resources and the discharge of wastewater;
3.  Other sources.

**Article 65 (New). Purposes of Fund Use**
The Water and Water Resources Fund shall be used for the following purposes:
1.  Creating and improving policies, strategies, plans, and legislation concerning the management of water and water resources;
2.  Scientific and technological research to protect water and water resources; creating models for the management, administration, development, and restoration of water and water resources for sustainability;
3.  Preventing, controlling, or eliminating water pollution and various disasters related to water and water resources; establishing training centers, information centers, and water quality analysis laboratories, including equipment to serve such work;
4.  Upgrading the technical knowledge and capacity of personnel; education, creation, and raising of awareness on water and water resources; and promoting activities for the work of protecting water and water resources, such as National Water Day and World Water Day;
5.  Managing and resolving priority issues of water and water resources at various levels;
6.  Encouraging, promoting, and developing local wisdom in the management, protection, use, development, and restoration of water and water resources to a good condition.

**Article 66 (New). Management and Use of the Water and Water Resources Fund**
The management and use of the Water and Water Resources Fund shall be implemented in accordance with the following principles:
1.  Centralized and unified, in accordance with the state budget system and consistent with the Law on the State Budget;
2.  Correctly targeted, economical, and effective;
3.  All revenues and expenditures must be specified in the annual state budget plan, pass through the National Treasury's accounting system, and be recorded according to the principles and basic regulations of state accounting;
4.  Flexible, agile, fair, and transparent, ensuring expenditures are in accordance with the annual plan;
5.  Regular inspection and auditing in accordance with the law.

### **Part X**
### **Prohibitions**

**Article 67 (Revised). General Prohibitions**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations are prohibited from engaging in the following behaviors:
1.  Illegally possessing or using water-related land areas;
2.  Extracting ore-bearing rock, sand, and silt from a water source without permission;
3.  Diverting, separating, or altering the flow of water;
4.  Cutting trees in a headwater protection area or constructing in a protected zone for water and water resources;
5.  Constructing anything upstream of a water source for use and consumption that would impact water quality, such as hospitals, markets, factories, or landfills;
6.  Digging, drilling, filling, dredging, or modifying the land surface in a way that would cause erosion in a river basin; or discarding or pouring objects into a water source that would cause the water source to become shallow, dry up, putrefy, or become toxic;
7.  Discharging wastewater or toxic chemical substances; using explosives; or disposing of waste and garbage along the banks of rivers, streams, canals, channels, creeks, ponds, or lakes, and on water-related land areas;
8.  Engaging in other behaviors that violate laws and regulations.

**Article 68 (New). Prohibitions for Officials or Authorities**
Officials or authorities are prohibited from engaging in the following behaviors:
1.  Abusing power, position, or duty related to water and water resources for personal gain;
2.  Demanding, soliciting, or accepting bribes from users of water and water resources or service providers related to water and water resources;
3.  Performing duties unfairly, with bias, or incorrectly towards individuals, legal entities, or organizations operating in work related to water and water resources;
4.  Being negligent or indifferent in the performance of duties; or obstructing or delaying the processing of various documents related to water and water resources;
5.  Falsifying documents or using forged documents; disclosing state secrets or official secrets; or destroying documents related to water and water resources;
6.  Illegally issuing permits for the right to use water-related land areas, headwater land, or headwater forests;
7.  Engaging in other behaviors that violate the law.

**Article 69 (New). Prohibitions for Users of Water for Business Purposes and Service Providers Related to Water and Water Resources**
Users of water for business purposes and service providers related to water and water resources are prohibited from engaging in the following behaviors:
1.  Using water and water resources for business purposes or providing services related to water and water resources without permission;
2.  Operating projects, activities, or providing services related to water and water resources that are not consistent with the permission received;
3.  Operating a business that creates a negative impact on water and water resources;
4.  Threatening, obstructing, or failing to cooperate with the performance of duties by officials responsible for water and water resources;
5.  Giving bribes or promising to give bribes to officials or authorities; providing false information; or falsifying documents or seals;
6.  Evading the performance of obligations under laws and regulations;
7.  Encroaching upon or destroying protected zones for water and water resources;
8.  Discharging wastewater exceeding national environmental standards or using toxic chemical substances that affect water sources;
9.  Assigning, lending, or selling permits for the use of water and water resources and permits for wastewater discharge to other persons without permission;
10. Moving, altering, or destroying markers of protected zones for water and water resources;
11. Drilling wells or artesian wells that affect the rights of other persons;
12. Engaging in other behaviors that violate the law.

### **Part XI**
### **Dispute Resolution**

**Article 70 (New). Forms of Dispute Resolution**
Dispute resolution may be conducted in any of the following forms:
1.  Mediation or compromise;
2.  Administrative resolution;
3.  Resolution by the economic dispute resolution authority;
4.  Filing a lawsuit in the People's Court;
5.  Resolution of an international nature.

**Article 71 (New). Mediation or Compromise**
In the case of a dispute that affects the use, management, or operation of activities and businesses related to water and water resources, which is not serious and involves a small value, the conflicting parties may consult, mediate, or compromise with each other.

**Article 72 (Revised). Administrative Resolution**
In the case of a dispute that affects the use, management, or operation of activities and businesses related to water and water resources, which is serious and involves a small value, the conflicting parties have the right to propose that the management authority for water and water resources consider and resolve it. If a party is not satisfied with the resolution, they have the right to propose that the next higher-level authority resolve it.
Disputes arising from servitudes shall be resolved by the local administrative authority where the dispute occurred.

**Article 73 (New). Resolution by the Economic Dispute Resolution Authority**
In the case of an economic dispute arising from the operation of activities related to water and water resources, the conflicting parties have the right to request that the economic dispute resolution authority consider and resolve it in accordance with the law.

**Article 74 (New). Filing a Lawsuit in the People's Court**
In the case of a dispute arising from the operation of activities related to the use of water and water resources, the conflicting parties have the right to file a lawsuit in the People's Court for consideration and resolution in accordance with the law.

**Article 75 (Revised). Resolution of Disputes of an International Nature**
Disputes that arise between the Lao PDR and other countries concerning the extraction, use, management, protection, and prevention of damage from water and water resources shall be resolved in accordance with the relevant laws of the Lao PDR and the treaties and international agreements to which the Lao PDR is a party.

### **Part XII**
### **Management and Inspection**

#### **Chapter 1**
#### **Management of Water and Water Resources Work**

**Article 76 (New). Management Authorities for Water and Water Resources Work**
The Government manages the work of water and water resources in a centralized and unified manner throughout the country, assigning the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment to be directly responsible and to take the lead in coordinating with the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry, the Ministry of Energy and Mines, the Ministry of Public Works and Transport, the Ministry of Industry and Commerce, the Ministry of Public Health, the Ministry of Information, Culture and Tourism, the Ministry of Education and Sports, local administrative authorities, and other relevant sectors to implement this work.
The management authorities for water and water resources work consist of:
1.  The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment;
2.  The Provincial/Capital Department of Natural Resources and Environment;
3.  The District/Municipality/City Office of Natural Resources and Environment;
4.  The Village Economic-Finance Unit.

**Article 77 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment**
In the management of water and water resources work, the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment has the following rights and duties:
1.  To research and create national policies, strategic plans, laws, and regulations on the management, administration, and use of water and water resources;
2.  To concretize policies, strategic plans, laws, and regulations on the management of water and water resources into detailed plans, programs, and projects, and then organize their implementation;
3.  To publicize, disseminate, and educate on policies, strategic plans, laws, and regulations concerning the management of water and water resources;
4.  To create management plans for water and water resources and river basins, in coordination with relevant ministries and local administrative authorities, to form a unified system throughout the country;
5.  To research and designate national-level protected zones for water and water resources for submission to the Government for consideration, and then for submission to the National Assembly Standing Committee for consideration and adoption;
6.  To survey, register, create inventories, define river basin types, and allocate river basins;
7.  To create a national information database system for water and water resources;
8.  To determine the minimum flow of surface water and define the scales of groundwater use;
9.  To coordinate with relevant ministries and local administrative authorities to define water quality standards and wastewater discharge standards, and to conduct Strategic Environmental Assessments for sectors to align with actual conditions;
10. To create plans and define measures for the prevention and mitigation of damage from water, and for the restoration of water and water resources, in coordination with relevant ministries and local administrative authorities;
11. To manage, monitor, and inspect investment projects and various activities that use water and water resources to ensure compliance with relevant laws and regulations;
12. To issue, suspend, modify, or revoke permits for the large-scale use of water and water resources and for wastewater discharge, in coordination with relevant ministries and local administrative authorities;
13. To issue permits for providing services related to water and water resources;
14. To receive proposals and reports on water and water resource issues from the public and relevant parties, and to resolve disputes within its scope of responsibility;
15. To engage in relations and cooperation with foreign countries and international organizations on water and water resources work;
16. To summarize and regularly report on the implementation of water and water resources management work to the Government;
17. To exercise other rights and perform other duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 78 (New). Rights and Duties of the Provincial/Capital Department of Natural Resources and Environment**
In the management of water and water resources work, the Provincial/Capital Department of Natural Resources and Environment has the following rights and duties within its scope of responsibility:
1.  To concretize, publicize, and organize the implementation of policies, strategic plans, laws, and regulations on water and water resources;
2.  To guide, monitor, inspect, and evaluate the implementation of water resources work by the District/Municipality/City Office of Natural Resources and Environment;
3.  To research and designate protected zones for water and water resources for submission to the Provincial Governor or Capital Mayor for consideration, and for submission to the Provincial People's Council Standing Committee for consideration and adoption;
4.  To coordinate with relevant departments and district/municipality/city administrative authorities to conduct surveys, registrations, and inventories of water and water resources;
5.  To create, manage, and use an information database on water and water resources;
6.  To create management plans for water and water resources and river basin management plans;
7.  To monitor, inspect, and enforce water quality standards and wastewater discharge standards as defined;
8.  To manage, monitor, and inspect investment projects and various activities that use water and water resources as assigned by the Ministry;
9.  To issue, suspend, modify, or revoke permits for the medium-scale use of water and water resources and for wastewater discharge, in coordination with relevant departments and local administrative authorities;
10. To issue permits for the large-scale use of groundwater;
11. To issue permits for groundwater drilling services;
12. To receive proposals and reports on water and water resource issues from the public and relevant parties, and to resolve disputes;
13. To engage in relations and cooperation with foreign countries on water and water resources work as assigned;
14. To summarize and regularly report on the results of the implementation of water and water resources work to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment and the Provincial/Capital administrative authority;
15. To exercise other rights and perform other duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 79 (New). Rights and Duties of the District/Municipality/City Office of Natural Resources and Environment**
In the management of water and water resources work, the District/Municipality/City Office of Natural Resources and Environment has the following rights and duties within its scope of responsibility:
1.  To publicize and organize the implementation of policies, strategies, laws, and regulations on water and water resources;
2.  To guide, monitor, inspect, and evaluate the implementation of water and water resources work by the village economic-finance unit;
3.  To research and designate protected zones for water and water resources for submission to the District Chief, Municipality Head, or City Mayor for consideration and approval;
4.  To coordinate with relevant offices and village administrative authorities to conduct surveys, registrations, and inventories of water and water resources;
5.  To create, manage, and use an information database on water and water resources;
6.  To create river basin management plans;
7.  To monitor, inspect, and enforce water quality standards and wastewater discharge standards as defined;
8.  To manage, monitor, and inspect investment projects and various activities that use water and water resources as assigned by the Department;
9.  To issue permits for the medium-scale use of groundwater;
10. To issue permits for providing groundwater drilling services;
11. To receive proposals and reports on water and water resource issues from the public and relevant parties, and to resolve disputes;
12. To summarize and regularly report on the results of the implementation of water and water resources work to the Provincial Department of Natural Resources and Environment and the Provincial/Capital administrative authority;
13. To exercise other rights and perform other duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 80 (New). Rights and Duties of the Village Economic-Finance Unit**
In the management of water and water resources work, the Village Economic-Finance Unit has the following rights and duties within its scope of responsibility:
1.  To implement laws, regulations, plans, programs, and projects related to water and water resources work;
2.  To disseminate, educate, build awareness, lead, mobilize, encourage, and promote the people to take ownership and participate in the management, protection, resolution of issues, and restoration of water and water resources within the village;
3.  To research and designate the village's conservation pool and submit it to a village meeting for consensus, and then submit it to the District Chief, Municipality Head, or City Mayor for consideration and approval;
4.  To encourage the people to build drainage channels, maintain cleanliness, and treat household wastewater to ensure the quality of water in water sources and prevent flooding and standing water;
5.  To participate in discussions and exchanges of views on water and water resources work and various activities to resolve water and water resource issues;
6.  To participate in surveying and collecting data on water and water resources, including groundwater drilling;
7.  To receive proposals and resolve issues related to water and water resources from the public;
8.  To summarize and regularly report on the implementation of water and water resources work to the District Office of Natural Resources and Environment and the district-level administrative authority;
9.  To exercise other rights and perform other duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 81 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry**
The Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry has the right and duty to manage the use of water and water resources for irrigation, fisheries, and agricultural and forestry production, and to manage watershed forests, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities; to take the lead and be responsible for collecting data and creating plans for the effective management and use of water and water resources within its sector, including providing data on the use of water and water resources in the fields for which it is responsible to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment; and has other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 82 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Energy and Mines**
The Ministry of Energy and Mines has the right and duty to manage the use of water and water resources for hydropower electricity generation and in the mineral extraction process, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities; to take the lead and be responsible for collecting data and creating plans for the effective management and use of water and water resources within its sector, including providing data on the use of water and water resources in the fields for which it is responsible to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment; and has other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 83 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Public Works and Transport**
The Ministry of Public Works and Transport has the right and duty to manage the use of water and water resources for water supply production, navigation, and water transport, riverbank erosion protection, and the installation of drainage pipes and wastewater treatment systems in urban areas, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities; to take the lead and be responsible for collecting data and creating plans for the effective management and use of water and water resources within its sector, including providing data on the use of water and water resources in the fields for which it is responsible to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment; and has other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 84 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Industry and Commerce**
The Ministry of Industry and Commerce has the right and duty to manage the use of water and water resources for processing industrial production, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities; to take the lead and be responsible for collecting data and creating plans for the effective management and use of water and water resources within its sector, including providing data on the use of water and water resources in the fields for which it is responsible to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment; and has other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 85 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Public Health**
The Ministry of Public Health has the right and duty to manage the use of water and water resources for therapy and health, treatment, inspection of the quality of drinking water sources, and the supply of clean water to people in rural areas; and to monitor the quality of drinking and domestic water, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities; to take the lead and be responsible for collecting data and creating plans for the effective management and use of water and water resources within its sector, including providing data on the use of water and water resources in the fields for which it is responsible to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment; and has other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 86 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Information, Culture and Tourism**
The Ministry of Information, Culture and Tourism has the right and duty to manage the use of water and water resources for nature-based tourism, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities; to take the lead and be responsible for collecting data and creating plans for the effective management and use of water within its sector, including providing data on the use of water and water resources in the fields for which it is responsible to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment; and has other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 87 (New). Rights and Duties of the Ministry of Education and Sports**
The Ministry of Education and Sports has the right and duty to manage the use of water and water resources for sports; teaching and learning, curriculum development, research, and scientific experimentation, in coordination with the natural resources and environment sector, other sectors, and relevant local administrative authorities; to take the lead and be responsible for collecting data and creating plans for the effective management and use of water within its sector, including providing data on the use of water and water resources in the fields for which it is responsible to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment; and has other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 88 (New). Rights and Duties of Local Administrative Authorities**
Local administrative authorities have the right and duty to adopt river basin management plans, designate protected zones for water and water resources in their locality, declare emergencies, and take the lead in the timely prevention, mitigation, and resolution of damage from water that occurs in their locality; and have other rights and duties as stipulated by law.

**Article 89 (New). Rights and Duties of Other Sectors and Parties**
Other sectors and parties have the right and duty to coordinate and cooperate with the natural resources and environment sector and the sectors stipulated in Article 76 of this Law in the management, monitoring, inspection, and implementation of water and water resources work in accordance with their roles.

#### **Chapter 2**
#### **Inspection of Water and Water Resources Work**

**Article 90 (New). Inspection Bodies for Water and Water Resources Work**
The inspection bodies for water and water resources work consist of:
1.  Internal inspection bodies, which are the same as the management authorities for water and water resources work as stipulated in Article 76 of this Law;
2.  External inspection bodies, which include the National Assembly, Provincial People's Councils, the Government Inspection Authority, the State Audit Organization, mass organizations, social organizations, and the general public.

**Article 91 (New). Rights and Duties of Inspection Bodies for Water and Water Resources Work**
Inspection bodies for water and water resources work have the following rights and duties:
1.  To create plans and programs for the inspection of water and water resources work;
2.  To inspect the implementation of policies, strategies, laws, regulations, plans, programs, and projects related to water and water resources;
3.  To propose that the relevant authorities issue an order to suspend or cancel investment projects and various activities when it is found that they have an impact on water and water resources;
4.  To coordinate with other relevant organizations in the implementation of their inspection work.

**Article 92 (New). Content of Inspection**
The inspection of water and water resources work has the following content:
1.  The implementation of policies, laws, and regulations related to water and water resources work;
2.  The organization and activities of the bodies implementing water and water resources work;
3.  The responsibility, conduct, and working methods of officials concerning the management of water and water resources.

**Article 93 (New). Forms of Inspection**
The inspection of water and water resources work has three forms, as follows:
1.  Regular inspection;
2.  Inspection with prior notice;
3.  Surprise inspection.
**Regular inspection** is inspection conducted according to a plan, with a periodic and definite schedule.
**Inspection with prior notice** is an off-plan inspection conducted when deemed necessary, with the entity to be inspected being notified at least twenty-four hours in advance.
**Surprise inspection** is an inspection conducted when deemed necessary and urgent, without prior notice to the entity being inspected.
Inspection shall involve both the examination of documents and the inspection of actual implementation and shall be conducted in strict accordance with the law.

### **Part XIII**
### **Policies for Persons with Outstanding Achievements and Measures against Violators**

**Article 94 (Revised). Policies for Persons with Outstanding Achievements**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations with outstanding achievements in the implementation of this Law, such as in the effective management, protection, use, development, and extraction of water and water resources, shall receive commendations and other policies in accordance with regulations.

**Article 95 (Revised). Measures against Violators**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations that violate this Law shall be subject to education, warning, disciplinary action, fines, civil liability for damages, or criminal penalties, depending on the lightness or severity of the case.

**Article 96 (New). Educational Measures**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations that violate this Law for the first time, in a manner that is not dangerous or serious, is unintentional, and causes damage of a small value, shall be educated and warned.

**Article 97 (New). Disciplinary Measures**
Officials who violate this Law, such as the prohibitions, in an act that constitutes a serious offense but not a criminal offense, causes damage of a small value, and fail to sincerely report their actions, shall be subject to disciplinary action in accordance with the Law on State Officials and Civil Servants.

**Article 98 (New). Fining Measures**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations that violate this Law, such as the prohibitions and conditions stipulated in the permit for the use of water and water resources, for wastewater discharge, and others, which do not constitute a criminal offense, shall be fined in accordance with regulations.

**Article 99 (New). Civil Measures**
Individuals, legal entities, or organizations that violate this Law and cause damage to the property of the State or others must be responsible for compensating for the damage they have caused.

**Article 100 (New). Criminal Measures**
Individuals who violate this Law in an act that constitutes a criminal offense under this or other laws that define criminal penalties shall be subject to criminal punishment, depending on the lightness or severity of the case.

### **Part XIV**
### **Final Provisions**

**Article 101 (New). National Water Day**
National Water Day is October 11th. Each year, movements shall be organized and celebration ceremonies shall be held in an appropriate form to promote, build awareness, and raise consciousness in the management and use of water and water resources throughout society.

**Article 102. Implementation**
The Government of the Lao People's Democratic Republic is the implementer of this Law.

**Article 103 (Revised). Entry into Force**
This Law shall enter into force from the date the President of the Lao People's Democratic Republic issues the Edict promulgating it and fifteen days after its publication in the Official Gazette.
This Law replaces the Law on Water and Water Resources No. 02-96/NA, dated 11 October 1996.
All regulations and provisions that conflict with this Law are hereby repealed.
