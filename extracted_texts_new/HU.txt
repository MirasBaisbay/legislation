**Act LVII of 1995**
**on Water Management¹**

**In force: 28. 06. 2023. – 31. 12. 2023.**
To define the fundamental rights and obligations related to the use of waters, the preservation of opportunities for their use, and the prevention of their harmful effects — with due regard to environmental and nature protection requirements — Parliament enacts the following Act:

**Chapter I**
**General Provisions**

**Section 1** (1)² The scope of this Act shall extend to:
a) groundwater and surface waters (hereinafter: waters), the natural water-bearing formations of groundwater, and the beds and banks of surface waters;
b) facilities that influence or may alter the flow and current conditions, quantity, quality, bed, or bank of waters, or the water-bearing formations of groundwater;
c) activities that influence or may alter the flow and current conditions, quantity, quality, bed, or bank of waters, or the water-bearing formations of groundwater;
d) the use of waters, the preservation of their usability, and the management of water resources;
e) the measurement, data collection, processing, provision, and use necessary for understanding waters and assessing their condition (hereinafter: hydrographic activity), as well as the evaluation of the state of waters, and research;
f) protection and defense against water damage,
and furthermore, to natural and legal persons, and their unincorporated organizations, engaged in the activities specified in points c)-f).

(2) The scope of this Act shall extend to the performance of water management tasks arising from international cooperation, unless an international treaty provides otherwise.

(3) A separate act shall provide for the requirements and tasks — not regulated in this Act — related to the protection of waters.

(4) A list of terms requiring separate definition is contained in Annex 1.

*Application of Act XXXIII of 1992 on the Legal Status of Public Employees and Act I of 2012 on the Labour Code*

**Section 13** (1) The provisions of Act XXXIII of 1992 on the Legal Status of Public Employees (hereinafter: Kjt.) shall apply to the legal relationship of public employees employed by water management authorities (hereinafter: public employees), with the derogations contained in this Act.

(2) In the application of this Act, Section 20/B(5), Section 22(15), Section 22/B, Section 23, Section 39(1), (3), and (4), Sections 56 and 57, Sections 60–67, Sections 69–72, Sections 74–75, Section 77(1) and (2), and Section 77/A of the Kjt. shall not apply to public employees employed by water management authorities.

(3) The following provisions of the Kjt. shall be applied with the following derogations:
a) Section 20/B(1)–(4), with the understanding that "senior manager" and "manager" shall mean a managerial position under this Act;
b) Sections 22–22/A, with the understanding that a "public employee classified in pay grades 'E'–'H'" shall mean a public employee holding a position requiring higher education;
c)⁸¹ Section 22(16), with the understanding that "unsuitable" shall mean unacceptable, and "qualification" shall mean performance evaluation;
d) Section 23/B(8), with the understanding that "public employee with a managerial assignment" shall mean a managerial position under this Act;
e) Section 41(2), with the understanding that "senior manager" and "manager" shall mean a managerial position under this Act;
f) in Section 82, "senior manager" shall mean a managerial position under this Act.

(4) In the application of this Act, Section 53(1) and (2) of Act I of 2012 on the Labour Code shall not apply to public employees employed by water management authorities.

(5)⁸² The water management authority may also provide for the performance of tasks related to the operation of the monitoring service during protection and defense against the harmful effects of water (water damage control) within the framework of an employment relationship or an assignment contract.

*Norms of Professional Ethics, Ethical Procedure*

**Section 13/A** (1) The fundamental principles of professional ethics applicable to the public employee are, in particular, professionalism, reliability, a strong sense of vocation and solidarity, unconditional and selfless helpfulness, commitment and loyalty, dignity and integrity, impartiality, and a willingness to cooperate.

(2) In addition to the principles of professional ethics under subsection (1), further ethical principles for managers are, in particular, setting an example, enforcing professional criteria, and the obligation of accountability.

(3) A person who violates the provisions of subsections (1) and (2) commits an ethical misconduct.

(4) In the event of a violation of the principles of professional ethics defined in subsections (1) and (2), the Water Management Ethics Committee shall conduct an ethical procedure.

(5) Based on the results of the ethical procedure, the Water Management Ethics Committee may issue a warning or a reprimand, of which it shall notify the employer employing the public employee.

**Section 13/B** (1) The head of the national water management authority shall determine the members of the Water Management Ethics Committee by selecting from the nominees under subsection (2), and shall also maintain a list of the members. The list shall contain at least ten and at most thirty persons.

(2) The heads of the water management authorities shall make proposals for the members of the Water Management Ethics Committee. The members shall be drawn from the public employee staff of the water management authorities. The selection shall take place after a personal hearing, and proposals may only be made for public employees who, by their exemplary conduct and work, are worthy of participating in the work of the Water Management Ethics Committee. The consent of the public employee is required for the nomination.

(3) The Water Management Ethics Committee shall conduct the ethical procedure in a panel of three members.

(4) A person may not be a member of the panel of the Water Management Ethics Committee if they
a) exercise employer's rights over the public employee under the ethical procedure,
b) are personally biased against the person concerned.

(5) The head of the national water management authority shall ensure the operational conditions of the Water Management Ethics Committee.

(6) The detailed rules of professional ethics, the detailed rules of operation of the Water Management Ethics Committee, and the system of the ethical procedure shall be established by the Rules of Procedure of the Water Management Ethics Committee.

*Classification, Advancement, and Salary System of the Public Employee*

**Section 13/C** (1) The public employee must be provided with a foreseeable and planned career advancement opportunity within the water management career path, through advancement in pay grades within the job category, as well as advancement to other positions within the job category, and to positions belonging to a higher job category.

(2) The job categories and the positions belonging to each job category shall be established by the Government in a decree.

(3) The public employee and the manager shall be placed in the appropriate pay grade of the salary scale established in the government decree under subsection (2), based on the time spent in the employment relationship, their educational qualifications, and their position.

**Section 13/D** The general conditions for advancement are:
a) fulfillment of the training and further training obligations prescribed in law,
b) fulfillment of the conditions for the next pay grade as defined in law,
c) practical experience, skills, and competencies required for the performance of the position,
d) a performance evaluation and qualification at the specified level.

**Section 13/E** (1) The public employee is entitled — based on their legal relationship — to the salary determined according to this Act, which shall be rounded to the nearest hundred forints.

(2) The amount of the public employee's guaranteed salary must reach at least the amount of the minimum wage and the guaranteed wage minimum established by the Government in a decree.

(3) The salary of the public employee must be determined in such a way that its amount reaches the lower limit of the advancement grade corresponding to their classification, but does not exceed its upper limit. The public employee is entitled to the salary corresponding to the lower limit of the advancement grade of their classification by subjective right. The advancement grades of the public employees employed in the water management sector and the lower and upper limits of the salaries belonging to them shall be determined by the Government in a decree.

(4) The person exercising the employer's rights shall review the public employee's salary by January 31 of each year.

(5) The person exercising the employer's rights may, during the review under subsection (4), increase the salary up to the upper limit of the salary belonging to the advancement grade of the public employee's classification for the current year, or decrease it to the lower limit. The person exercising the employer's rights, when reviewing the salary, shall take into account in their decision:
a) the time spent in the public employment relationship,
b) the experience gained in water management,
c) significant experience gained with another employer, relevant to the tasks performed in their position,
d) the qualifications, other skills, and language proficiency acquired by the public employee,
e) the responsibility associated with the performance of the position,
f) the level of the performance evaluation and qualification, and
g) the physical, psychological, and work environment-related burdens associated with the performance of the position.

(6) If the person exercising the employer's rights does not carry out the review under subsection (4), or does not modify the amount of the public employee's salary during it, the public employee shall be entitled to the salary applicable on December 31 of the preceding year.

**Section 13/F** (1) The person exercising the employer's rights — not including substitution — may establish an extraordinary, task-related objective for the public employee based on the agreement of the person exercising the employer's rights and the public employee, the completion of which significantly and durably exceeds the general workload arising from the performance of the position (hereinafter: objective task).

(2) The person exercising the employer's rights shall, for the successful completion of the objective task — in addition to the salary stated in the public employee's letter of appointment and subsequent employer measures — determine in writing, at the time of establishing the objective task, a target bonus within the appropriation for personal benefits.

(3) The public employee is entitled to the target bonus if the objective task has been successfully completed and this is certified by the person exercising the employer's rights.

(4) The person exercising the employer's rights, in addition to the provisions of subsection (1), may establish an objective task for a public employee employed by another employer, with the agreement of the head of the employer. The target bonus shall be determined by the person exercising the employer's rights who sets the objective task.

(5) The target bonus shall not be included in the amount of the salary serving as the basis for the jubilee bonus to which the public employee is entitled.

*Rest Period*

**Section 13/G** (1) The public employee is entitled to twenty-five working days of basic leave per year.

(2) The public employee is entitled to supplementary leave of:
a) one day from their twenty-fifth year of age,
b) two days from their twenty-eighth year of age,
c) three days from their thirty-first year of age,
d) four days from their thirty-third year of age,
e) five days from their thirty-fifth year of age,
f) six days from their thirty-seventh year of age,
g) seven days from their thirty-ninth year of age,
h) eight days from their forty-first year of age,
i) nine days from their forty-third year of age,
j) ten days from their forty-fifth year of age.

(3) The manager is entitled to ten working days of supplementary leave per year instead of the supplementary leave specified in subsection (2).

*Transfer for Official Interest*

**Section 13/H** (1) A public employee may be temporarily employed in a different position, at a different workplace, or with a different employer through a transfer for official interest (hereinafter: transfer). It shall not be considered a modification of the appointment if the public employee, based on the written instruction of the head of the employer or the person exercising the employer's rights — for reasons of work organization necessary for the effective operation of the employer — temporarily performs tasks belonging to another position corresponding to their educational qualifications, professional qualifications, or skills instead of their original position, or performs their tasks at another workplace or with another employer.

(2) The public employee must be informed in writing about the ordering of the transfer and its expected duration at least three working days in advance.

(3) A transfer may last for a maximum of six months without interruption. The duration of the transfer may not exceed six months per calendar year.

(4) A transfer during defense against the harmful effects of water shall be applicable regardless of the provisions of subsections (1)–(3).

*Termination of the Public Employment Relationship*

**Section 13/I** (1) The public employment relationship may be terminated with immediate effect if the public employee engages in conduct — whether in connection with their official work (performance of duties) or the breach of an obligation arising from their public employment relationship, or outside the workplace — that is capable of seriously undermining the prestige of the position they hold or the good reputation of the employer or good public administration, or which causes the public employee to lose the trust of their superior, and as a result, the employer cannot be expected to maintain the employment relationship.

(2) In the case of termination of the employment relationship under subsection (1):
a) before its communication, the public employee must be given the opportunity to become acquainted with the reasons for the termination of the employment relationship and to defend themselves against them, unless, based on all the circumstances of the case, this cannot be expected from the employer,
b) the rules regarding the justification of dismissal under Section 30(2) of the Kjt. must be applied.

(3) The right to terminate the employment relationship under subsection (1) may be exercised:
a) within fifteen days from becoming aware of the reason for it, but no later than one year from the occurrence of the reason,
b) in the case of a criminal offense, until the statute of limitations for punishability expires.

(4) If a public employee's employment relationship is terminated according to subsection (1), they may not establish a public employment relationship for three years from the termination of the employment relationship.

(5)⁸³ In connection with the establishment and termination of a public employment or employment relationship, the employer may, under the conditions specified in law, use and provide to its employees the services provided by the Public Service Human Resources Framework System of the central human resources body under the Act CXXV of 2018 on Government Administration.

**Section 13/J** If a public employee has received severance pay upon the termination of their employment relationship pursuant to Section 37 of the Kjt., they may establish another public employment relationship within eight months from the termination of the employment relationship only if they undertake to repay to their former employer the portion of the severance pay amount proportional to the time remaining of the eight-month period until the establishment of the new employment relationship.

*Training and Further Training of the Public Employee*

**Section 13/K** (1) The public employee is entitled to advancement and is obliged to participate in the training and further training prescribed centrally or by the employer — including leadership training — (hereinafter collectively: further training).

(2) In the case of a public employee holding a position requiring higher education, the National University of Public Service shall provide the further training.

(3) In the case of a public employee holding a position requiring basic or intermediate education, the further training system shall ensure the renewal of knowledge, which the water management authority designated by the Government in a decree — with the involvement of the National University of Public Service and professional and scientific water management organizations — shall provide.

(4) The National University of Public Service and the water management authority designated by the Government in a decree (hereinafter collectively: further training organizer), during the operation of the further training system, shall:
a) perform tasks related to the development of further training programmes,
b) perform tasks related to ensuring the quality requirements of further training programmes,
c) provide for the professional, educational-methodological, and adult education further training of the collaborating instructors and examiners during the further training courses in a planned and regular manner,
d) operate the system for the qualification of further training programmes.

(5) The further training organizer shall register and manage the following data in connection with the implementation of the further training:
a) the natural personal identification data of the public employee,
b) data regarding the employer of the public employee,
c) data necessary for the completion of the further training.

(6) For the performance of the tasks specified in subsection (4) — contrary to Section 42 of the Kjt. — another legal relationship may be established for instructor, curriculum development, or educational organization tasks.

(7) The employer is obliged to reimburse the public employee's salary for the working time lost due to the prescribed further training and the related reporting or examination obligations.

(8) The public employee is obliged to reimburse the costs of the further training to the employer if they fail to attend the further training for a reason attributable to them, or do not fulfill the prescribed requirements, or if their legal relationship has been terminated with immediate effect according to this Act.

(9) A government decree shall contain the detailed rules for the planning and financing of the further training of public employees, and for the system of requirements for the training courses.

*Performance Evaluation and Qualification*

**Section 13/L** (1) The person exercising the employer's rights, acting within their discretionary power, shall evaluate in writing once a year the work performance of the public employee and their compliance with the requirements set for them (hereinafter: performance evaluation). The level of qualification shall be determined based on the level of the performance evaluation.

(2) The performance evaluation consists of mandatory and recommended elements.

(3) The individual performance evaluation concluding the reference year, supplemented by a textual justification containing conclusions on the evaluated person's skills and personal qualities related to their work performance, their suitability for performing their duties, their further developability, and their advancement, shall constitute the qualification for the evaluated reference year. The evaluation level of the qualification shall be the same as the level of the performance evaluation.

(4) The public employee may initiate a legal dispute for the annulment of an erroneous or untrue factual finding or a finding that violates their personal rights in the performance evaluation.

(5) The evaluating manager, in the qualification, may:
a) initiate the procedure for the inclusion of the evaluated person in the leadership and succession database or for their removal from it, or
b) initiate the modification of the public employment relationship.

(6) The mandatory and recommended elements of the public employee's performance evaluation and qualification, the levels and procedural rules of the performance evaluation and qualification, and the content of the evaluation form shall be contained in a government decree.

*Managers*

**Section 13/M** (1) A public employee may be appointed to a managerial position specified in a government decree. Only a public employee with a higher education degree may be a manager. A public employee may not be appointed to a managerial position during their probationary period.

(2) An appointment to a managerial position shall be for an indefinite period.

(3) A managerial appointment may also be made for a definite period for the purpose of substitution. A definite-term managerial appointment made for the purpose of substitution may be extended.

(4) If, as a result of the modification of the appointment, the manager:
a) holds a lower managerial position, their salary shall be determined according to their new managerial position,
b) does not hold a managerial position, their salary shall be determined according to their new position, by applying the appropriate salary scale specified in the government decree, based on their pay grade corresponding to the time spent in the public employment relationship.

(5) The employer may modify the appointment for the reason specified in subsection (4), point a) or b), without the consent of the public employee, if the new position corresponds to the public employee's educational qualifications, professional qualifications, skills, and professional experience.

(6) Upon their written request submitted within four working days from the communication of the modification of the appointment for the reason specified in subsection (4), point a) or b), the public employee must be dismissed — effective from the date of the declaration — if:
a) the amount of their salary according to their new position does not reach 80% of their former salary, or
b) they have been transferred from a managerial position to a non-managerial position.

(7) In the case of the dismissal of the public employee under subsection (6), the salary established in their former position held before the modification of the appointment shall be taken as the basis for determining the salary for the period of dismissal, the severance pay, and the amount of other payments.

(8) A government decree may establish other conditions for the advancement of managers.

*Allowance*

**Section 13/N** Public employees holding a defense organizational position involved in defense as specified in the government decree on the rules of defense against the harmful effects of water at water management authorities are entitled to an availability allowance. The amount of the availability allowance is twenty thousand forints per month.

**Chapter V**
**Management of Water Resources**

**Section 14** (1) To preserve the opportunities for the use of waters, efforts shall be made through:
a) the regular monitoring of the usability conditions of natural waters,
b) the prevention of water pollution,
c) the establishment and operation of water facilities serving the protection or regulation of waters,
d) the prevention, reduction, or elimination of water quality damage that hinders water use,
e) the maintenance of the beds of waters and water facilities for water protection purposes,
f)⁸⁴ the monitoring of waters and their characteristics, and the evaluation of their condition,
g)⁸⁵ the analysis of the effects of human interventions on the state of surface and groundwaters,
h)⁸⁶ the economic analysis of water uses,
i)⁸⁷ the establishment of a legal and economic regulatory system that encourages the rational use of water resources.

(2) Waters serving drinking water supply, mineral water, and medicinal water use, or designated for such purposes, must be kept under enhanced protection and security (water base protection) through the designation and maintenance of protection zones and protection areas for water abstraction, as specified in a separate legal regulation.

(3) For the protection of a long-term drinking water base or a water resource already reserved under a preliminary water rights permit, the water authority may, based on this Act, order restrictions on ownership and use as per a separate legal regulation.

(4) A person who has acquired the right to use a water resource is obliged to maintain the water resource drawn for use in proportion to the extent of the use, and to provide for the collection, discharge, treatment, and disposal of wastewater in accordance with environmental regulations.

(5) A person who discharges a harmful substance into a public sewage and treatment works in excess of the specified limit must pay a sewer fine.

(6)⁸⁸ The Government shall determine in a decree the types of harmful substances mentioned in subsection (5), the rules related to the imposition of the sewer fine, and the amount of the sewer fine, and the minister responsible for water management shall determine the discharge threshold values.

(7)⁸⁹ The manager of the bed of natural waters that are in the exclusive ownership of the state is obliged to provide for the water quality protection reed management of the reeds found in the bed.

**Section 15** (1)⁹⁰ Groundwater — taking into account the provisions of this Act — may only be abstracted to an extent that the balance between water abstraction and water replenishment is maintained without qualitative degradation, and the requirements for achieving the objectives for the good status of waters, as set out in a separate legal regulation, are met.

(2) Water demands shall be met primarily from water resources not yet allocated for water use, also taking into account the protection of the quantity and quality of the available water resource.

(3)⁹¹ In the use of mineral, medicinal, and thermal waters, priority shall be given to therapeutic and spa-related uses.

(4) The order of priority for satisfying water demands, also taking into account the provisions of subsections (1)–(3), shall be for:

c)⁹³ animal watering, fish farming,
d) nature conservation,

f)⁹⁵ economic,
g)⁹⁶ other (e.g., sports, recreational, holiday, bathing, tourism)
water uses.

(5) If the restriction of water use becomes necessary, the order of restriction is the reverse of the priority order for satisfaction specified in subsection (4).

When setting prices, the social, environmental, and economic effects of the recovery must be taken into account.

*Water Resource Contribution*

**Section 15/A**¹⁰⁰ (1)¹⁰¹ The water user is obliged to pay a water resource contribution for the quantity of water allocated in a water rights construction or operation permit, or authorized in a mining supervisory authority contract for the extraction and use of geothermal energy through thermal water production (hereinafter: mining supervisory contract), or used without a permit, and for the water quantity actually used by the industrial consumer.

(2) According to subsection (1):
a)¹⁰² a water user is a person who practices water use or reserves a water resource in a water rights permit based on a water rights permit, mining supervisory contract, or the provisions of Section 28/A(1a),
b) an industrial water consumer is a person who uses a water quantity greater than 10,000 m³ per year per settlement from a public utility providing drinking water for their own economic water use.

(3)¹⁰³ A water user holding a water rights permit or a mining supervisory contract may calculate, declare, and pay the water resource contribution electronically.

(4)¹⁰⁴ For the purpose of verifying the existence and determining the amount of the obligation to pay the water resource contribution, the competent water authority is entitled to access all data of water users related to any water use from the authentic public e-water utility register.

(5)¹⁰⁵ The water management authorities are entitled to access the data declared by water users on the actual water use, which serves as the basis for the water resource contribution, for the purpose of performing their water resource management tasks.

**Section 15/B**¹⁰⁶ (1)¹⁰⁷ The water user shall calculate the water resource contribution based on:
a) a basic contribution of 4.50 forints/m³,
b)¹⁰⁸ if the water quantity specified in the water rights permit or mining supervisory contract is exceeded by more than 10% — also considering each separately specified period — a basic contribution of 9.00 forints/m³ for the entire excess quantity,
c)¹⁰⁹ if the activity is carried out without a mining supervisory contract, or if the activity subject to a water rights permit is carried out without a permit (hereinafter: water use without a permit), by taking into account a basic contribution of 28.90 forints/m³, the amount of the payment shall be calculated based on the actual quantity of water used, by applying multipliers depending on the method of determining the quantity of water used, the nature of the water use and the water resource, and the water resource management situation of the given area, as prescribed in a separate legal regulation.

(2) The determination of the water quantity in the case of water use without a permit shall be carried out as specified in a separate legal regulation.

(3) The water user is obliged to calculate the water resource contribution for 80% of the water quantity specified in the water rights permit, by taking into account the basic contribution specified in subsection (1), point a) — as specified in this section — if the quantity of water they have used does not reach 80% of the quantity specified in the water rights permit.

(4)¹¹⁰ The industrial consumer shall calculate the water resource contribution based on a basic contribution of 14.10 forints/m³ for the actually used water quantity.

**Section 15/C**¹¹¹ (1) The water user is not required to pay a water resource contribution:
a) for the quantity of water re-injected into the water-bearing layer specified in the water rights permit in the case of groundwater abstraction, which does not endanger the groundwaters,
b) for the quantity of water used for firefighting, civil defense preparedness purposes, and for the duration of life-saving in the event of a life-threatening water inrush in a mine,
c) for a water quantity not exceeding 500 m³ per year per water rights permit,
d) for the abstraction of a water quantity identical to the quantity supplied for groundwater recharge, if it is abstracted from the aquifer recharged by groundwater recharge,
e) for the quantity of water specified in the water rights permit, used for filling canals, fishponds, and reservoirs designated for flood and inland water reception for defense purposes, and conveyed through the main works,
f)¹¹² for the portion of the water quantity specified in the water rights permit or mining supervisory contract that cannot be used because the water resource is not available for natural reasons,
g)¹¹³ if the water authority has restricted the water abstraction,
h) after the repeated use or transfer of used water, except for water abstraction from dual-function canals,
i) for the quantity of water used for ecological purposes from surface water,
j) if the amount of the water resource contribution for the reference year does not exceed 1,000 forints,
k) in the case of water abstraction for remediation purposes, if it was done based on an official order or permit,
l)¹¹⁴ in the case of water use for irrigation, for a quantity not exceeding 400,000 m³ per year per water rights permit or 4,000 m³ per year per hectare of irrigated area per water user; in the case of water use for fish farming and rice production, for a quantity not exceeding 400,000 m³ per year for users of groundwater, and for users of surface water, for a quantity not exceeding 25,000 m³ per year per hectare,
m)¹¹⁵ for the quantity of water used for irrigation, fish farming, and rice production during periods of prolonged water scarcity as per subsection (9),
n)¹¹⁶ for the utilized water resource originating from the retention and storage of surplus winter waters — surface waters, floods, and inland waters — from the water quantity specified in the water rights permit issued by the water authority, provided that the water management authority certifies that the surplus surface water is available at the point of abstraction.

(1a)¹¹⁷ The state may deviate from the provision regarding the obligation to pay the water resource contribution in subsection (1), point l) in favor of the water user for reasons based on social, environmental, and economic impacts, as well as geographical and climatic characteristics.

(2)¹¹⁸ In the cases specified in subsection (1), points f), g), l)–n), the exemption from the obligation to pay the water resource contribution does not affect the obligation of declaration and data provision regarding water use under Section 15/E.

(3) The water user must request the determination of the exemption specified in subsection (1), points b) and f) from the competent water authority within sixty days from the occurrence of the reason. After sixty days, the exemption cannot be claimed.

(4) The industrial consumer is not required to pay a water resource contribution if public health regulations specify a drinking water quality for more than 50% of the used water quantity.

(5)¹¹⁹ The industrial consumer is obliged to report their payment obligation to the competent water authority on the form prepared for this purpose within 15 days of its occurrence (registration).

(6)¹²⁰ The obligation to pay the water resource contribution arises for the water user upon the finalization of the water rights permit, the entry into force of the mining supervisory contract, and for the industrial consumer — taking into account the provisions of Section 15/B(4) — upon the commencement of the use of a water quantity exceeding 10,000 m³.

(6a)¹²¹ For the water user, the obligation to pay the water resource contribution arises in the case specified in Section 28/A(1a) upon the communication of the approval of the notification by the irrigation management authority under Act CXIII of 2019 on Irrigated Farming.

(7) A separate legal regulation shall determine to which organization of the state budget the water resource contribution shall be credited, as well as its amount.

(8)¹²² The water user may reduce the water resource contribution payable by them for the use of thermal water extracted exclusively for energy utilization purposes — up to the amount of the water resource contribution — by the amount of the certified costs incurred in connection with the construction of a well ensuring the re-injection of the thermal water or the modernization of the equipment, which have been accepted by the body performing water authority tasks.

(9)¹²³ The minister responsible for water management shall announce the expected start and end of periods of prolonged water scarcity in a notice in the Official Gazette, taking into account hydrometeorological forecasts.

**Section 15/D**¹²⁴ (1)¹²⁵ The water user shall pay the water resource contribution calculated for the first three quarters of the calendar year and the industrial water consumer shall pay the water resource contribution, with the exceptions specified in subsections (2) and (3), to the account designated by the competent water authority by the 15th day of the month following the reference quarter.

(2)¹²⁶ By the 15th day of the first month following the reference year, the water user is obliged to pay the water resource contribution,
a) if the average daily water quantity specified in the water rights permit (based on 365 days) does not exceed 25 m³/day (small consumer),
b) if they used water only during a specific period of the year (seasonal water user).

(3)¹²⁷ The water resource contribution for water use without a permit must be calculated and paid by the 15th day of the month following the communication of the final official decision.

(4)¹²⁸ By the 15th day of the first month following the reference year, a water user subject to quarterly payment obligation is obliged to pay the difference between the water resource contribution payable for the total annual water use and the water resource contribution paid for the first three quarters' water use as specified in subsection (1).

(5)¹²⁹ The amount paid by the person obliged to pay the contribution — if it is less than the total debt — shall be credited in order (starting with the oldest debt for each type) to the tax penalty, the default penalty, then the late payment surcharge, and finally to the water resource contribution debt.

(6) The amount of money spent on the purchase or expropriation as specified in the government decree on the protection of water bases, long-term water bases, and water facilities serving drinking water supply may be claimed back from the paid water resource contribution.

**Section 15/E**¹³⁰ (1)¹³¹ For the first three quarters of the calendar year, the water user, and quarterly, the industrial consumer, is obliged to declare to the competent water authority on the data sheet provided for this purpose, by the 15th day of the month following the reference quarter, the actual water use, as well as the basic data and calculation of the payment obligation.

(1a)¹³² The water user is obliged to declare the annual data specified in subsection (1) — also in a consolidated manner — to the competent water authority by the 15th day of the month following the reference year.

(1b)¹³³ Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (1), a water user falling within the scope of the annual payment obligation specified in Section 15/D(2) is obliged to declare the data specified in subsection (1) on the data sheet provided for this purpose by the 15th day of the month following the reference year.

(1c)¹³⁴ The public utility provider is obliged to declare the quantity of water allocated and actually provided to the industrial consumer on the data sheet provided for this purpose by the 15th day of the month following the reference year.

(2)¹³⁵ The water authority shall check the documents of the water resource contribution payment. If the person liable to pay fails to comply with their registration, declaration, record-keeping, or water resource contribution and surcharge payment obligations, the Act on the Rules of Taxation shall apply, with the derogation that where the act mentions the tax authority, it shall mean the water authority. **Chapter VI**
**Protection and Defense Against the Harmful Effects of Water**

**Section 16** (1) The performance of the tasks necessary for the protection against the harmful effects of water — the construction, development, maintenance, and operation of protective works, as well as the defense activities — is the obligation of the state, the local governments, and those interested in the prevention or elimination of the damage.

(2)¹⁴¹ The water management authority designated by the Government in a decree shall prepare hazard and risk maps, as well as risk management plans, for areas affected by the risk of excess water.

(3)¹⁴² The tasks of the water management authorities are the regulation of rivers for water damage control purposes, the construction, maintenance, and development of water damage control facilities serving more than two municipalities — the main flood defense lines, water damage control reservoirs, main inland water defense works (hereinafter: protective works), and the provision of defense thereon, as well as the performance of agricultural water service and water damage control tasks.

(4)¹⁴³ The tasks of the water management authority related to water damage control are:
a) the direction or performance of the technical and administrative tasks of water damage control;
b) the construction, development, or coordination of the construction and development of protective works;
c) the regional planning, organization, and professional direction of defense activities;
d) the professional direction of the water damage control activities of local governments;
e)¹⁴⁴ providing data to local governments for the preparation and review of water damage control and development plans related to the protection against the harmful effects of water, and, in the case of open flood plain settlements along rivers, the preparation and review of water damage control plans;
f) the professional direction of the water damage control activities of water associations;
g) providing information related to the protection against the harmful effects of water.

(5)¹⁴⁵ The tasks of the local governments are:
a) the establishment of protective works serving a maximum of two municipalities, the maintenance and development of protective works owned by the local government, and the provision of defense thereon;
b) the prevention of damage caused by floods of streams and canals, as well as by precipitation and other waters in the inner area of the municipality — through the construction of protective works in outer and inner areas — the maintenance and development of the protective works, and the provision of defense thereon;
c) the performance of tasks specified in a separate legal regulation related to the protection against the harmful effects of water.

(6)¹⁴⁶ The performance of activities necessary for the protection against the harmful effects of water that do not fall within the scope of state or local government tasks is the responsibility of the interested owners or users of the property under another legal title.

**Section 17** (1)¹⁴⁷ The national direction of flood and inland water defense is the responsibility of:
a) the minister responsible for the direction of water management authorities, until the establishment of the extraordinary defense preparedness,
b) the minister responsible for the direction of water management authorities, during the period of extraordinary defense preparedness, if a state of emergency is not declared,
c) the designated member of the Government, in the case of coordinated defense activities.

(2)¹⁴⁸

(3)¹⁴⁹ The technical tasks of flood and inland water defense on works maintained by municipalities defending with their own organization shall be performed within the administrative boundaries of the municipality — under the professional direction of the water management authority — by the mayor (in Budapest, the Lord Mayor) through the mayor's (Lord Mayor's) office.

(4)¹⁵⁰ The public administration tasks and competence of flood and inland water defense, as well as local water damage control, shall be performed — as specified in separate legal regulations — by the chairperson of the county, capital defense committee, or the mayor, and in the capital, the Lord Mayor.

(5)¹⁵¹ The detailed tasks and methods of defense against the harmful effects of water, and the competence of the minister responsible for the direction of water management authorities, shall be established by the Government in a decree.

(6)¹⁵² The Lord Mayor, the chairperson of the county, capital defense committee, or the mayor shall order the evacuation, rescue, and resettlement related to flood and inland water defense, as well as local water damage control, within the scope of public administration tasks specified in a separate legal regulation, and shall cooperate in the execution of other related tasks.

(7) The mayor (Lord Mayor), within their public administration tasks and competence related to flood and inland water defense:
a) shall cooperate in the execution of the tasks of the regional flood and inland water defense committee as specified in law;
b)¹⁵³ shall provide for the inventory, registration, and, if necessary, mobilization of public forces — including those assigned to the civil protection organization and public employees —, as well as the materials, tools, and equipment necessary for defense, and the general supply of public forces — including those assigned to the civil protection organization and public employees;
c)¹⁵⁴ shall plan the evacuation, rescue, and resettlement, and in the case of their ordering, shall provide for their execution;
d) shall take other necessary measures for the security of life and property, as well as for rescue;
e)¹⁵⁵ shall provide for the health care of those participating in the defense, and shall take measures related to the prevention and control of epidemics during evacuation, rescue, and resettlement, with the cooperation of the public health administration body;
f) shall take the necessary measures for the restoration related to the damage caused by flood and inland water, and in connection with the defense.

(8) The mayor (Lord Mayor), within their public administration tasks and competence related to public utility water supply — in accordance with the plan approved by the representative body — shall order the restriction of water consumption.

(9)¹⁵⁶ Along rivers and on the backfilled sections of watercourses and canals that flow into them, flood protection works, as well as structures, pipelines, or other facilities crossing the high-water bed of rivers or flood protection works, or located in the high-water bed, must be designed, dimensioned, and constructed taking into account the design flood levels determined by the minister responsible for water management in a decree.

**Section 18**¹⁵⁷ (1)¹⁵⁸ A person who, by their activity or omission, endangers or damages the waters, is liable under Act LIII of 1995 on the General Rules of Environmental Protection, and is obliged to take the measures determined by the water authority and the local water management authority acting within their official competence.

(2) The rules for the prevention and elimination of environmental damage occurring in water quality shall be established by the Government in a decree.¹⁵⁹

**Section 19** (1)¹⁶⁰ For the application of Section 18(1), the person exercising the right of fishery shall be considered the person causing the damage if the fish kill and the resulting water quality damage occurred due to the violation of the regulations on fishery and not due to water pollution or water quality degradation.

(2)¹⁶¹ If the person causing the damage is unknown, the costs of damage control shall be borne by the owner of the polluted water or water facility.

**Chapter VII**
**Provisions Concerning Properties Related to Waters and Water Facilities**

**Section 20** (1)¹⁶² The owner (user) of a property is obliged to tolerate that, based on a decision of the water authority, a public-purpose water facility be placed and operated on their property, and that the necessary water works be carried out, provided that this does not preclude the intended use of the property (water pipeline easement).

(1a)¹⁶³ In the case of water facilities that are relocated or constructed in connection with the construction of a national public road and its structures and accessories, or a national railway line and its accessories, and which do not fall under the scope of subsection (3a), the authority — without a separate request from the constructor under Act I of 1988 on Road Transport or Act CLXXXIII of 2005 on Rail Transport — shall, within the framework of the construction permit procedure, decide on the water pipeline easement with respect to all affected properties.

(2)¹⁶⁴ The water authority shall, upon request, permit connection to an existing water facility operating under a water rights permit, if the water facility is suitable for both its original purpose and the purpose of the connection, or can be made suitable for it (water use easement).

(3) The owner (user) of a bank property is obliged to tolerate that the owner of the bed or their agents, to the extent necessary for the performance of their professional tasks, taking into account the interests related to the property:
a) access the bank of the water through the property;
b) transport materials extracted from the bed for the execution of their water management tasks, as well as materials necessary for the performance of their water management tasks, through the property, or place them, as well as the equipment necessary for carrying out the works, and temporary facilities on the property;
c) place and maintain signs and facilities necessary for hydrographic observation, the marking of the navigation channel, and the performance of other professional tasks on the property.

(3a)¹⁶⁵ The owner (user) of a property is obliged to tolerate that a public-purpose water facility serving the dewatering of a national public road and its accessories, or a national railway line and its accessories, be placed and operated on their property, and that the necessary water works be carried out, provided that this does not preclude the intended use of the property.

(4)¹⁶⁶ The owner (user) of the property is entitled to compensation corresponding to the extent of the restriction under subsections (1), (1a), (3), and (3a). If, as a result of the restriction, the use of the property, the exercise of a right related to the property, or a profession becomes impossible or significantly more costly, the owner may request the expropriation of the property.

(5)¹⁶⁷ The operator of a bed-use water facility located in the bed of waters belonging to the national assets shall be granted a right of bed use by operation of this Act from the date of the finalization of the water rights operation permit, which shall be registered as specified in a separate legal regulation.

**Section 21**¹⁶⁸ (1) A reservoir for water damage control purposes shall include a storm water reservoir, a flood peak reduction reservoir, an emergency reservoir, a flood relief reservoir, and an inland water reservoir.

(2) The owner or user of a property is obliged to tolerate its designation by the water authority as a reservoir for water damage control purposes.

(3) The designation of a storm water reservoir, a flood peak reduction reservoir, an emergency reservoir, and an inland water reservoir is in the public interest.

(4)¹⁶⁹ The water authority competent according to the location of the property shall decide on the designation of the property, or a part thereof defined by area, as a reservoir for water damage control purposes — with the exception of a flood relief reservoir. The water authority shall, with the final decision, request the real estate supervisory authority competent according to the location of the property to record the legal character of a reservoir for water damage control purposes. If the legal character of a reservoir for water damage control purposes does not affect the entire property, a survey sketch certified by the real estate supervisory authority, depicting the affected part of the property, must be attached to the decision.

(5)¹⁷⁰ The water authority shall decide on the termination of the classification as a reservoir for water damage control purposes if its conditions no longer exist. The water authority shall send the final decision to the real estate supervisory authority for the cancellation of the legal character of a reservoir for water damage control purposes from the real estate register.

**Section 21/A**¹⁷¹ (1) If the placement of a water facility connected to a storm water reservoir, a flood peak reduction reservoir, an emergency reservoir, or an inland water reservoir, or a use restriction under Section 21/E, terminates or significantly hinders the intended use of the property, the owner may request the purchase of the property from the investor, or initiate the expropriation of the property with the investor.

(2) In the case of the necessity of frequent use of a storm water reservoir, a flood peak reduction reservoir, an emergency reservoir, or an inland water reservoir, the investor may initiate the purchase or expropriation of the reservoir area to avoid disproportionately high compensation costs.

**Section 21/B**¹⁷² (1) The owner registered in the real estate register of a property belonging to the area of a storm water reservoir, a flood peak reduction reservoir, an emergency reservoir, or an inland water reservoir is entitled to a one-time compensation for the availability and use, as well as for the depreciation related to the use restriction under Section 21/E, in proportion to their ownership share. If the property was originally under state or local government ownership, or comes into state or local government ownership pursuant to Section 21/A(1) or (2), no one-time compensation shall be paid.

(2) In the case of the use of a reservoir for water damage control purposes for flood regulation, the user of the property is entitled to compensation for the actual damage caused, unless the reservoir area was purchased or expropriated pursuant to Section 21/A and a lease or usufructuary lease agreement was subsequently concluded.

(3) The rules of the Civil Code shall apply to compensation related to the use of properties in the area of a reservoir for water damage control purposes for flood regulation, in the absence of a contrary provision in this Act.

(4) The owner of the property must inform the lessee or usufructuary lessee of the use restrictions under Section 21/E when concluding the lease or usufructuary lease agreement.

(5)¹⁷³ If the usufructuary lessee has received compensation under subsection (2), they are not simultaneously entitled to claim the usufructuary lease payment reduction under the Act on certain provisions related to Act CXXII of 2013 on the turnover of agricultural and forestry lands and transitional rules.

**Section 21/C**¹⁷⁴ (1) The water authority shall decide in its decision on the payment of the one-time compensation under Section 21/B(1), and the investor shall provide for the payment of the one-time compensation.

(2)¹⁷⁵ The basis for the one-time compensation is the gold crown value (hereinafter: GC) of the agricultural and forestry land (hereinafter: land) as per the real estate register. The amount of the one-time compensation at the time this provision comes into force is 8,000 forints per GC. The amount of the one-time compensation shall increase annually on January 1st by the amount of the consumer price index for the reference year, as published by the Central Statistical Office.

**Section 21/D**¹⁷⁶ The water authority shall hold a public hearing during the designation of a reservoir for water damage control purposes only to clarify the facts of the case.

**Section 21/E**¹⁷⁷ (1)¹⁷⁸ On land located in the area of a storm water reservoir, a flood peak reduction reservoir, an emergency reservoir, or an inland water reservoir:
a) for chemical soil improvement, irrigation, or amelioration investments,
b) for the establishment of nurseries, seed production, or the production of propagation material,
c) for the planting of plantations and perennial crops with poor water tolerance,
d) for breaking up grasslands,
state aid may not be claimed, and in such cases, no compensation shall be paid for damage caused by the use of the reservoir for flood relief purposes.

(2) In the area of a storm water reservoir, a flood peak reduction reservoir, an emergency reservoir, or an inland water reservoir, it is not permitted to:
a) carry out construction activities subject to notification or permit,
b) carry out landscaping that results in a reduction of the reservoir's capacity or alters the flow conditions of the waters, without the consent of the operator.

(3) No compensation shall be paid for the portion of the damage that has occurred due to the violation of the provisions specified in subsection (2).

(4)¹⁷⁹ The cultivation method of land located in the area of a reservoir for water damage control purposes may only be changed with the prior consent of the reservoir operator.

**Section 22** (1) The owner (user) of a property:
a) may only use and cultivate the property in such a way that it does not obstruct the natural flow of waters; does not endanger the condition, operation, and maintenance of the bed, the bank, and the facilities along the bank and other public-purpose water facilities, and does not damage the quality of the water;
b) may use the bed and harvest its products only under the conditions specified in law; in the case of an activity subject to a water rights permit, in accordance with the water rights permit.

(2) The owner of a bank property is entitled to protect their bank property against erosion and to restore the eroded part based on a water rights permit.

**Section 22/A**¹⁸⁰ (1) The water management authority shall examine whether a watercourse or canal section obstructs the conveyance of irrigation water or drainage, for the purpose of agricultural water service, and the prevention of drought and water damage.

(2) If the water management authority determines, based on the examination under subsection (1), that the watercourse or canal section obstructing the conveyance of irrigation water or drainage
a) is not state-owned,
b) cannot be replaced by drainage on another route,
c) its use is of public interest for agricultural water service or for the prevention of drought and water damage, and
d) state water management development is necessary for its intended use,
it shall initiate the acquisition of the ownership of the area for the benefit of the state.

(3) The watercourse or canal section acquired by the state pursuant to subsection (2) shall be considered a public-purpose water facility.

(4) In the absence of an acquisition of ownership pursuant to subsection (2), the water management authority shall initiate its designation as an operator and maintainer with the water authority with respect to the non-state-owned watercourse or canal section obstructing the conveyance of irrigation water or drainage. The water authority shall designate the water management authority as operator and maintainer if
a) it determines that the use of the non-state-owned watercourse or canal is of public interest for agricultural water service or for the prevention of drought and water damage, and
b) the neglected state of the watercourse or canal obstructs the drainage or conveyance of irrigation water necessary for the agricultural water service or for the prevention of drought and water damage.

(5) The designating decision of the water authority under subsection (4) is valid for two years.

(6) The designating decision under subsection (4) shall provide a period of at most three months from its communication for the water management authority to take over the watercourse or canal for operation and maintenance.

**Section 22/B**¹⁸¹ If the owner of the water facility obstructing the conveyance of irrigation water concludes an operation agreement with the water management directorate for the operation and maintenance of the watercourse or canal instead of the official designation under Section 22/A, the water management directorate shall withdraw its request for designation, and the water authority shall terminate the ongoing procedure, and, if the designation has already occurred, the water authority shall withdraw the decision. The water management authority shall inform the water authority of the conclusion of the contract within fifteen days.

**Section 22/C**¹⁸² (1) The water management authority shall regularly examine the existence of the public interest specified in Section 22/A(3)(a).

(2) As a result of the examination under subsection (1), the water management authority may initiate
a) the review of the designating decision or the extension of its temporal validity,
b) the review of the operation agreement.

(3) The owner of the watercourse or canal serving irrigation may initiate the review of the operation agreement or the designating decision if, in their opinion, the public interest that formed the basis for the conclusion of the contract or the issuance of the official decision no longer exists.

**Section 23** (1) Following the entry into force of this Act, the bank of natural waters shall be maintained for public purposes by the state owner or the local government owner, therefore, undeveloped real estate on the bank of natural waters cannot be acquired from the state or the local government.

(2) The Government is authorized to grant an exemption from the restriction specified in subsection (1) by decree in particularly justified cases with respect to a specific property or a specific bank section.

(3) The local government may, in a municipal decree, grant an exemption from the restriction specified in subsection (1) with respect to undeveloped properties located on the bank of natural waters in its ownership as non-transferable core assets, taking into account the interests of the municipality.

**Section 24**¹⁸³ (1) The primary purpose of the high-water bed is the conveyance of floodwaters and ice leaving the bed.

(2) The high-water bed of a river, as well as the area on the landside of the levee where seepage or leakage may occur, may only be managed, used, and utilized in accordance with flood protection regulations.

(3) A structure may be placed in the high-water bed with the consent of the bed manager of the relevant river section.

(4)¹⁸⁴ The owner or user of a property located in the high-water bed may conduct agricultural activities, forestry, or other activities in the high-water bed exclusively at their own risk, without obstructing the conveyance of floods, and while observing environmental, nature protection, and cultural heritage protection regulations. In the high-water bed, according to the Act on the Protection of Arable Land, the change of use of the land for other purposes, as well as the change of the cultivation method of the land, may only be carried out with the prior consent of the bed manager of the relevant river section.

(5) The bank strip of rivers, streams, small watercourses, inland water and irrigation canals, lakes, reservoirs, and oxbow lakes, as well as the protective strip of flood protection facilities, must be used in such a way that the owner (user) of the bed or facility can use it unhindered for the purpose of maintenance works, measurements, and for defense purposes.

(6) In the bank strip and protective strip, as well as in areas regularly under water, a structure may be placed with the consent of the owner or manager of the bed. In the absence thereof, no compensation is due for the damage caused to the person who placed it — unless the law provides otherwise.

(7)¹⁸⁵ A management plan must be prepared for the high-water beds of rivers, which is binding on those carrying out activities in the high-water bed. The management plan shall be established by the minister responsible for the direction of water management authorities in a decree. The management plan must be harmonized with other plans affecting or having an effect on the high-water beds of rivers, in particular with the nature conservation management plans prepared for protected natural areas under the Act on Nature Conservation, with the nature conservation management and maintenance plans prepared for Natura 2000 sites under the government decree on sites of European Community importance, and with the nature conservation objectives defined in the ministerial decree on land parcels affected by sites of European Community importance, as well as with the regional forest plans prepared for forests under the Act on Forests, the Protection of Forests, and Forest Management.

**Section 25** (1) No compensation is due to the owner of a bank property for the use of the area temporarily occupied for works serving the protection of the banks; however, damage caused to perennial vegetation and standing crops, as well as damage caused to buildings, must be compensated.

(2) The area necessary for public-interest riverbed regulation must be purchased from the property owner, or — if a sales contract is not concluded — it must be expropriated.

(3)¹⁸⁶ The owner (user) of a property may not carry out activities that endanger the water base, as specified in a separate legal regulation or in the official decision establishing the protection area, on the protection area or protective strip designated for the protection of water bases and water abstraction works, and, subject to the provisions on compensation, is obliged to tolerate the exercise of public-interest rights of use related thereto, and the specified scope of restriction on the use of the property.

**Section 26** If the quantity of waters or the capacity of water facilities necessitates the gradual release of excess waters (emergency situation), the owner (user) of the affected property is obliged to tolerate that the waters be temporarily held on the property, directed there, temporarily stored there, or conveyed through the property for the purpose of reducing and preventing greater damage.

**Section 27**¹⁸⁷ The water pipeline and water use easement established pursuant to Section 20(1) and (2) must be registered in the real estate register, and furthermore, the fact of being located in a reservoir for water damage control purposes under Section 21, in a protection area under Section 25(3), in a high-water bed, or on a bank strip, as a legal character, must be registered in the real estate register based on the final decision of the water authority concerning the relevant property.

**Chapter VIII**¹⁸⁸
**WATER MANAGEMENT OFFICIAL JURISDICTION**

**Section 28** (1) Official jurisdiction related to water management may be water management official jurisdiction or local water management official jurisdiction.

(2) In water management official jurisdiction — within the framework of a water management official procedure — the water authority, and in local water management official jurisdiction — within the framework of a local water management official procedure — the body specified in the government decree on the exercise of water management official jurisdiction shall act.

(3) In a local water management official procedure, the provisions regarding the water management official procedure shall apply, with the exception of:
a) Section 28/A(2),
b) Section 28/D(1)(b),
c) Section 28/D(4) and (5),
d) Section 28/E,
e)¹⁸⁹ Section 31(1),
f) Section 31/A.

**Section 28/A** (1)¹⁹⁰ With the exception of activities subject to notification under law — and with the exception of the extraction and use of geothermal energy through thermal water production —, a water rights permit is required for:
a) carrying out water works, and for the construction and alteration of water facilities (water rights construction permit),
b) the commissioning and operation of water facilities, and for water use (water rights operation permit), and
c) the decommissioning of water facilities (decommissioning permit).

(1a)¹⁹¹ A well for agricultural irrigation purposes may be established after prior notification and approval, if the following conditions are met jointly:
a) the well is established without using the internal, external, and hydrogeological protection zones, protection areas, as well as karst or confined aquifer resources of water bases, long-term water bases, and water facilities serving drinking water supply, as designated, under designation, or preliminarily demarcated according to the government decree, and on an area not affected by soil or groundwater contamination listed in the official register,
b) the well's sole depth does not exceed 50 meters and it does not reach the first aquitard layer,
c) the irrigation system connected to the well exclusively serves the irrigation of areas under the cultivation of the establisher or operator,
d) the establisher has previously notified the well to the official register according to the government decree on the exercise of water management official jurisdiction, and following the approval of the notification, has equipped it during construction with a digital well water meter ensuring the measurement of the water quantity.

(1b)¹⁹² Following the prior notification to the official register, the irrigation management authority shall, within the period specified in the government decree on the exercise of water management official jurisdiction, with regard to the state of the water base and the effect of the planned well on the water base, for the protection of groundwaters — based on the information in the prior notification — approve or prohibit the construction, or determine that the construction may only be started with a final water rights construction permit.

(2) A preliminary water rights permit may be requested for the technical design of a water use, water work, and water facility subject to a water rights permitting obligation.

(3) The water authority shall grant a permit for the use of wells drilled for hydrocarbon exploration purposes but unsuitable for that purpose, for the purpose of thermal water production.

**Section 28/B** In a water management official procedure,
a) the request,
b) the remedy of deficiencies, and
c) a declaration made by the client
may only be submitted in writing.

**Section 28/C**¹⁹³

**Section 28/D** (1) The following must be notified in writing of the initiation of a procedure initiated upon request:
a) the known client,
b) the water management authority concerned with the area of operation, in the case of waters, a bed, or a water facility under Section 3(2).

(2) Written notification may only be omitted if:
a) the authority makes a decision on the merits within eight days of the initiation of the procedure, or dismisses the request, or terminates the procedure, or
b) a legal regulation excludes it for reasons of national defense, national security, or public security.

(3) The written notification must include:
a) the name of the requesting client,
b) the subject and file number of the case, the date of initiation of the procedure, and the applicable procedural deadline for the specific case type, the periods not included in the procedural deadline, the name and official contact details of the case officer, and
c) information on the possibility of inspecting the documents and making a declaration.

(4) If at least fifty clients are known in a water management official procedure, the known clients must be notified of the initiation of the procedure by public notice instead of written notification. The notification may be made public.

(5) A notification communicated by public notice shall include:
a) the subject and a brief description of the case, and
b) information on where and when the parties concerned may inspect the case documents.

**Section 29** (1) The water authority may only grant a water rights permit if the prescribed conditions are met and the water facility, water work, or water use:
a) does not endanger the interests related to the protection of the water resource, and
b) complies with the water management, technical, and safety regulations issued for the implementation, alteration, and decommissioning of water works and water facilities, as well as for their operation and the practice of water uses, and with other regulations related to water management, water quality, and the protection of groundwater and surface waters.

(2)¹⁹⁴ In a mining supervisory contract for the extraction of thermal water exclusively for energy utilization purposes — in accordance with the conditions prescribed in legal regulations — provision must be made for the method of disposal of the extracted water.

(3)¹⁹⁵ A new water rights permit may only be granted or a mining supervisory contract may only be concluded if the quantity of water specified in the permit can be ensured for the permit holders.

(4) If the completion of the water work, or the construction or alteration of the water facility, was carried out without a final official permit or contrary to the final official permit, the issuance of the operation permit to the constructor may be refused. If the water authority, after examining the water work or water facility — also taking into account all the circumstances of the case — subsequently grants a permit for continued existence to the constructor, it must simultaneously impose the payment of a water management fine. The fine may amount to 80% of the value of the structure constructed without a permit, and up to 1,000,000 forints for water works or water use without a permit. The amount of the fine imposed on a natural person may not exceed 300,000 forints.

(5)¹⁹⁶ The water management fine, with the exception of the provisions in subsections (6) and (9), must be imposed on the constructor of the water work or water facility.

(6) The water management fine must be imposed on the establisher of the water work or water facility if:
a) the constructor proves beyond doubt that they are not responsible for the illegal construction, or
b) the identity of the constructor is unknown.

(7)¹⁹⁷ The establisher or operator who has established or operated a groundwater abstraction facility without a permit or contrary to a permit is exempt from paying the water management fine if they apply for the water rights permit for continued existence procedure by December 31, 2023, or if the water rights permit for continued existence procedure does not need to be conducted according to the provisions of this Act.

(8) A water management fine may not be imposed:
a) for necessary works to immediately avert accidental pollution affecting waters, and
b) in the case of water facilities established or water works carried out without a water rights permit, which are connected to the technical tasks of extraordinary defense under the government decree on the rules of defense against the harmful effects of water, if they would otherwise have been permittable.

(9)¹⁹⁸ If the construction or alteration of the water facility was carried out without notification or contrary to its approval as specified in Section 28/A, the payment of a water management fine must be imposed on the constructor. The fine may amount to 80% of the value of the structure constructed without a permit. The amount of the fine imposed on a natural person may not exceed 300,000 forints.

**Section 29/A** An appeal may be lodged against a decision made in a first-instance water management official procedure and a first-instance local water management official procedure.

**Section 30** (1) The water authority, in the event of the occurrence of conditions or events specified in separate legal regulations, ex officio or upon request:
a) may modify, suspend, or revoke the water rights permit (including the preliminary permit), or
b) may order the decommissioning of the water facility.

(2) If the modification, suspension, or revocation of the water rights permit or the decommissioning of the water facility was caused by an activity or omission, the person whose activity or omission necessitated the official measure is obliged to compensate the permit holder or the constructor (operator) for the resulting damages.

(3) No compensation is due for damages arising from measures taken ex officio under subsection (1), if the measures were justified by:
a) the public interest, in particular the interest of water management, public health, environmental, and nature protection,
b) a change in the quantity and quality of waters due to natural or other unavoidable reasons,
c) the failure of the permit holder to fulfill an obligation specified in a legal regulation or a breach of a legal obligation.

**Section 30/A** The costs of sampling, laboratory, or other instrumental examinations incurred during a public administration official procedure, as well as other procedural costs for personal and material expenses incurred in clarifying the facts of the case during the procedure, shall be considered other procedural costs.

**Section 31** (1) An administrative service fee must be paid for certain public administration procedures conducted by the water authority (including expert authority procedures, administrative services, and notifications).

(2)¹⁹⁹ The following are exempt from the obligation to pay duties and fees:
a) the permitting procedure for irrigation equipment or irrigation plants, if it takes place within the framework of the permitting of the water use;
b)²⁰⁰ the permitting procedure for facilities providing groundwater abstraction.

**Section 31/A** No administrative service fee needs to be paid for the activities of the water management authorities specified in the government decree on the exercise of water management official jurisdiction.

**Section 32** If the quantitative protection of waters, the prevention of the harmful effects of water, the prevention of damage, or the lawfully practiced water use otherwise requires it, the water authority shall oblige the water user, or the person who has unlawfully interfered with the water conditions, to terminate the harmful or potentially harmful state, to carry out the necessary water works, or to practice them in a specified manner.

**Section 32/A** (1)²⁰¹ A person who, in addition to the provisions of Section 29(4), violates a water management regulation contained in a legal regulation, an official decision, or a directly applicable Community legal act, is obliged to pay a water management fine of up to 1,000,000 forints, commensurate with the gravity of the unlawful conduct. The amount of the fine imposed on a natural person may not exceed 300,000 forints.

(1a)²⁰² With the exception of a breach of the obligation of water management data provision or record-keeping, a warning shall not be applied as an administrative sanction for the violations specified in subsection (1).

(2) The collected water management fine is a revenue of the central budget.

**Section 33** (1) The water authority shall keep a water register of the water works, water facilities, and water uses it has permitted, and furthermore, the water management authority shall keep a register of water management objects and water resources.

(2) The authority acting in local water management official jurisdiction shall keep a register of water facilities.

**Section 33/A** The water authority is entitled to order a prohibition on subdivision or construction in the high-water bed for the purpose of preventing natural and environmental hazards (i.e., preserving the water conveyance capacity of the high-water bed, ensuring the conveyance of floods).

**Chapter VIII/A**²⁰³
**DATA MANAGEMENT**

**Section 33/B**²⁰⁴ (1) Following the final closure of the official permitting procedures, the water authority shall manage the following data for the purpose of maintaining the water register, monitoring the payment of imposed fines, enforcing the final decision, inspection, review, legal remedies related to the final decision, case tracking, and the review of the decision:
a) the natural personal identification data of the client,
b) the client's address, notification address, electronic mail address, telephone numbers,
c) the company registration number, registration number, unique state budget identifier, date of establishment and termination, scopes of activity, registered office address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of a non-natural person client,
d) the natural personal identification data, address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of the natural person representative or authorized representative of a non-natural person client,
e) the natural personal identification data, address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of the natural person authorized representative of a natural person client,
f) the natural personal identification data of the owner of the property affected by the water work, water facility, and water use,
g) the basic technical data related to the water work, water facilities, and water uses, as recorded in the e-water register, and
h) the rights and legally significant facts related to the exercise of the activity specified in the water rights permit.

(2) The water authority with national competence is entitled to access and manage the following data for the purpose of performing tasks related to the water resource contribution:
a) the natural personal identification data of the client,
b) the client's address, notification address, electronic mail address, telephone numbers,
c) the client's tax identification number or tax number, community tax number,
d) the client's account number, IBAN number,
e) the company registration number, registration number, unique state budget identifier, date of establishment and termination, scopes of activity, registered office address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of a non-natural person client,
f) the natural personal identification data, address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of the natural person representative or authorized representative of a non-natural person client,
g) the natural personal identification data, address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of the natural person authorized representative of a natural person client,
h) the quantitative and basic technical data related to the water uses.

(3) The personal data under subsection (1), points a)–f), and subsection (2), points a)–g) — in the absence of a contrary provision in law — may not be transferred to another body or person and may be managed until the end of the sixth year following the expiry of the validity of the water rights permits.

(4) The data under subsection (1), points g) and h), and subsection (2), point h), which do not qualify as personal data, may be managed for an unlimited period and shall not be erased.

**Section 33/C**²⁰⁵ (1) The water management authority is entitled to access and manage the following data for water management data collection, the management of state property, and the identification of water management objects:
a) the natural personal identification data of the client,
b) the client's address, notification address, electronic mail address, telephone numbers,
c) the company registration number, registration number, unique state budget identifier, date of establishment and termination, scopes of activity, registered office address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of a non-natural person client,
d) the natural personal identification data, address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of the natural person representative or authorized representative of a non-natural person client,
e) the natural personal identification data, address, electronic mail address, and telephone number of the natural person authorized representative of a natural person client,
f) the natural personal identification data of the owner of the property affected by the water work, water facility, and water use,
g) the basic technical data related to the water work, water facilities, and water uses, as recorded in the permit holder's documentation and in the e-water register.

(2) The personal data under subsection (1), points a)–f) — in the absence of a contrary provision in law — may not be transferred to another body or person and may be managed until the end of the sixth year following the expiry of the validity of the water rights permits.

(3) The data under subsection (1), point g), which does not qualify as personal data, may be managed for an unlimited period and shall not be erased.

(4) The surveying and mapping state administration body shall, through the identification service provided by the Government, transfer the land registry data content supplemented with ownership data (including personal data) as an electronic document or in an electronically processable form for the design works necessary for submitting a water rights permit request, to the designer who has the right to practice water engineering design and is commissioned by the owner or asset manager involved in the water rights permit request. The designer is entitled to manage the land registry's personal data content transferred to them until the date the official decision on the water rights permit becomes final. The designer may not transfer the personal data content of the land registry transferred to them to another body or person — in the absence of a contrary provision in law.

**Section 33/D**²⁰⁶ For the operation of the data collection interface specified in Section 33/J, and for the purpose of acquiring the information necessary for the official procedure, and ensuring efficient and fast administration for the applicant, the operator of the electronic system operated by the body designated by the Government (hereinafter: VIZEK system) is entitled to transmit the data indicated by the applicant to the connected bodies through the data collection interface, and to transmit the information returned by the connected bodies to the applicant's storage space within the data collection interface.

**Section 33/E**²⁰⁷ (1)²⁰⁸ For the management of the data specified in Section 33/B(1), points a)–h), and the basic technical data related to the water work, water facilities, and water uses recorded in the permit holder's documentation, of a client participating in a water rights permitting procedure or already holding a water rights permit, registered in the VIZEK system — related to the concerned water use and water facility — the following are entitled:
a) the minister responsible for agricultural policy, for making agricultural economic analyses and agricultural professional policy decisions,
b) the institute under the direction of the minister responsible for agricultural policy, engaged in agricultural research, for the performance of its irrigation development, agricultural economic analysis, and statistical tasks,
c) the irrigation management authority, for the performance of tasks related to irrigation development,
d) the National Chamber of Agriculture, for the performance of its tasks related to the agricultural structure register.

(2) The Hungarian State Treasury is entitled to manage and process the data of an agricultural producer holding a water rights permit, which also includes personal data, in a manner suitable for individual identification, for the purpose of fulfilling its obligations specified in law related to the control of aid.

(3) For the purpose of keeping the Hungarian implementation of the Common Agricultural Policy and the Integrated Administration and Control System's Land Parcel Identification System up to date, the surveying and mapping state administration body, as well as the agricultural and rural development support body, may access the spatial data files of water bodies and canal networks managed by the water management authorities, together with their relevant attribute data, free of charge, without cost, and without compensation.

(4) The data specified in subsection (1) — with the exception specified in subsection (5) — may be managed for three years, and the data specified in subsection (2) may be managed until the end of the auditability of the aids.

(5) The data specified in subsection (1) related to water use and water facilities, which does not qualify as personal data, may be managed for an unlimited period and shall not be erased. The minister responsible for the operation of the Uniform Agricultural Client Register shall provide access to this data.

**Section 33/F** The data managed in the register of the VIZEK system shall be transferred to the Central Statistical Office — in accordance with Section 28 of Act CLV of 2016 on Official Statistics — for statistical purposes, to the extent necessary for that purpose, in a manner suitable for individual identification, free of charge, based on prior certification of the statistical purpose, and may be used for statistical purposes by the Central Statistical Office. The scope of the transferred data and the detailed rules of the data transfer shall be specified in a cooperation agreement.

**Section 34**²¹⁰ (1) Public water management tasks may also be performed through water management associations established under the conditions specified in this Act. **Section 35**²¹¹ (1) An association shall carry out its activities serving public tasks within its area of interest.

(2)²¹² The members of a public utility association are natural and legal persons, and unincorporated organizations, who own or use real estate under another legal title in the area of interest.

(3) The members of a public utility association are obliged to pay a public utility association interest contribution (hereinafter: contribution) to the public utility association. The contribution is a public debt enforceable as taxes.

(4) A member of a public utility association may request a review of the contribution imposed on them from the court within 30 days of the communication of the general assembly's decision.

(4) The management of the public utility association shall be carried out by the management board.
(5) The supervisory body of the public utility association is the supervisory board.

(7)²¹⁶ The chairperson and members of the management board and the supervisory board — with the exception of the constituent general assembly — shall be elected by the general assembly. The chairperson of the management board is the chairperson of the public utility association.

Chapter IX/A²⁶⁰
PUBLIC SERVICE FOR THE COLLECTION OF NON-PUBLIC UTILITY COLLECTED DOMESTIC WASTEWATER
Section 44/B The owner, asset manager, or user under another legal title of a property (in the application of this chapter, hereinafter collectively: property owner) is obliged to collect the domestic wastewater generated on their property, which is not discharged into a public sewage network or into a locally approved treatment facility, in the manner specified in the government decree on the general rules for activities and facilities serving the use, protection, and prevention of harmful effects of waters, and to hand it over to the public service provider authorized to collect it.
Section 44/C (1) The local government or their association shall organize and maintain, as a mandatory public service, a public service for the collection of domestic wastewater not discharged into a public sewage network or into a receiving body after treatment as authorized by the government decree on the exercise of water management official jurisdiction (hereinafter: public service). The water authority — after seeking the opinion of the clerk of the local government of the place of transfer — shall designate the wastewater treatment plant, the element of the wastewater collection works designated for this purpose, the pond, or other wastewater treatment works (hereinafter collectively: place of transfer) obliged to receive the non-public utility collected domestic wastewater.
(2) The representative body of the local government shall determine in a municipal decree:
a) the content of the public service and the boundaries of the area served by the public service;
b) the name of the public service provider and the boundaries of its operational area, within which the public service provider is obliged to regularly provide the public service, and the place of transfer for the purpose of harmless disposal;
c) the order, manner, and duration of the provision of the public service, the related rights and obligations of the public service provider and the property owner, and certain content elements of the contract for the public service;
d) the obligation to use the public service, and the manner and conditions of using the public service;
e) the fee payment obligation encumbering the property owner, the maximum amount of the applicable fee, the order of payment, possible reductions, and cases of free service;
f) the specific rules for holiday properties, temporarily used properties, and unused properties;
g) the provisions regarding the management of personal data related to the public service (natural personal identification data and address).
Section 44/D (1) A fee proportional to the quantity of non-public utility collected domestic wastewater shall be paid for the use of the public service.
(2) When determining the fee for the public service, the following shall be taken into account:
a) the quantity of domestic wastewater,
b) the continuous operational costs necessary for the effective operation of the public service provider, including collection costs,
c) the costs necessary for the developable and sustainable maintenance of the public service,
d) the investment costs incurred prior to the commencement of the public service, necessary for the provision of the public service.
(3) The fee determined based on subsections (1) and (2) shall be reduced by the amount of budgetary support provided for the provision of the public service.
(4)²⁶¹ The public service fee may be single-factor or two-factor. The public service fee shall be determined for a fee payment period of at least one year, in unit rates calculated without value-added tax. The unit rate of the emptying fee for non-public utility collected domestic wastewater shall — separately — include the costs of collection, and in the case of disposal at a non-wastewater treatment plant, the costs of post-treatment and monitoring.
(5) A mandatory two-factor fee determination shall be applied in the case of municipalities participating in the National Municipal Wastewater Collection and Treatment Program.
(6)²⁶² The two-factor fee consists of a basic fee and an emptying fee. The basic fee covers the operational costs incurred irrespective of the quantity of non-public utility collected domestic wastewater, the costs of billing and fee collection, environmental protection expenses and expenditures, amortization, and the necessary renewal funds. The emptying fee covers the costs dependent on the quantity of non-public utility collected domestic wastewater, as well as the costs dependent on the land use unit.
(7) Prior to the adoption of the municipal decree determining the public service fee, the public service provider shall prepare a fee calculation in accordance with subsections (1)–(6), based on a cost analysis. The fee calculation shall be submitted by the clerk, and in Budapest, the chief clerk, based on the proposal of the public service provider.
(8)²⁶³ If the representative body of the local government determines the public service fee payable by the property owner at a lower rate than the fee calculated based on the provisions of subsections (1)-(6) by applying a fee reduction, or in the case of granting an exemption, establishes the service as free of charge, it shall reimburse the difference in the form of a fee compensation. For the payment of the fee compensation, the representative body and the provider of the public service shall conclude a mandate contract, which shall contain the elements set out in Article 4 of Commission Decision 2012/21/EU of 20 December 2011 on the application of Article 106(2) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union to State aid in the form of public service compensation granted to certain undertakings entrusted with the operation of services of general economic interest, and shall otherwise comply with all other requirements set out in that Decision.
Section 44/E (1) In the event of failure to meet the payment deadline for the public service fee payable by the property owner, a fee arrear shall arise, which, together with the default interest established in connection with it and other collection costs, shall be considered a public debt enforceable as taxes.
(2)²⁶⁴ Within 30 days of the fee arrear arising, the public service provider or other entitled party of the claim (hereinafter: entitled party of the claim) shall notify the property owner of the failure to meet the payment obligation and call for its fulfillment, while providing information on the legal consequences.
(3)²⁶⁵ In the event of an unsuccessful notice, after 90 days have elapsed from the arising of the fee arrear, the entitled party of the claim shall initiate the collection of the fee arrear, default interest, and other costs as taxes.
(4) The clerk of the local government shall, within 8 days of receiving the initiation, take measures for the collection of the fee arrear, default interest, and incurred costs in accordance with the separate legal regulation. The collected fee arrear, default interest, as well as the costs incurred and collected by the public service provider in connection with this, shall be transferred by the clerk of the local government to the entitled party of the claim within 8 days.
(5) If the fee-defaulting user of the public service is not the owner of the property and the collection procedure conducted against them is unsuccessful, the fee arrear, default interest, and incurred costs shall be collected from the owner or asset manager of the property. In the case of a property in joint ownership, the liability of the owners is joint and several.
(6) In the case of an uncollectible fee arrear, the clerk of the local government shall — within 8 days of the collection being unsuccessful — issue a certificate of this fact and its reasons to the entitled party of the claim.
Section 44/F (1) The public service provider — in the manner prescribed in the municipal decree — is obliged to continuously provide for the collection of non-public utility collected domestic wastewater, while observing environmental, water, and public health regulations.
(2) The collector is obliged to notify the water authority of its collection activity. The water authority shall keep a register of those carrying out collection activities, based on the notification.
(3) A person who can:
a) ensure the personal and material conditions of the public service, which guarantee the lasting, regular, and environmentally sound provision of the public service,
b) has notified the collection activity to be performed to the competent water authority of the place of collection, and the water authority has registered the activity,
c) has concluded a public service contract with the local government based on a public procurement procedure or — if not required under the Public Procurement Act — by designation,
may perform the public service.
(4) The public service must be performed in a manner that excludes danger and pollution to the environment.
(5) The person performing the public service is responsible for the safe delivery of the non-public utility collected domestic wastewater to its destination.
(6) In the event of pollution resulting from the performance of the public service, the person performing the collection is obliged to provide for the cleanup of the waste, the decontamination of the area, the prevention of damage, and the restoration of the original environmental state.
Section 44/G (1) The representative body of the local government shall conclude a written contract for the provision of the public service.
(2) The contract for the public service may be concluded for a maximum period of 10 years. Such a contract must specify the facilities that will receive the domestic wastewater.
(3) The public service contract may only be terminated by the local government if the public service provider:
a)²⁶⁶ during the provision of the public service, seriously violates the legal regulations concerning environmental protection and water management, or the provisions of an official decision applicable to it, and this fact has been established by a final court judgment or a final official decision;
b) seriously breaches its obligation stipulated in the contract, for a reason attributable to it.
(4) Following the commencement of performance, the public service provider may terminate the public service contract if:
a) the local government, despite the public service provider's notice, seriously breaches its obligation stipulated in the public service contract, and thereby causes damage to the public service provider or hinders the performance of the public service, or
b) a legal regulation that enters into force after the conclusion of the public service contract changes the content elements of the public service contract in a way that significantly infringes on the essential and legitimate interests of the public service provider related to the contractual performance of the public service.
(5) The notice period shall be at least two months and at most six months.
(6) In the event of the termination of the public service contract, the local government must take measures to ensure that the public service is provided after the expiry of the notice period.
Section 44/H (1) The public service provider is obliged to prepare a detailed annual cost accounting of its public service activity and to submit it to the local government by March 31 of the year following the reference year.
(2) The costs, accounting, and fees of other services not falling within the scope of the public service must be strictly separated, and these costs may not be financed from the public service fee.
Section 44/I (1) The local government is entitled to task-related support in the amount and manner provided for in the budget act for the organization and operation of the public service.
(2) The local government is only entitled to the task-related support under subsection (1) if it fully complies with its obligations related to the harmless disposal of non-public utility collected domestic wastewater.
(3) If the local government fails to comply with its obligation to provide the public service, the body responsible for the legal supervision of local governments shall initiate that the body providing the task-related budgetary support withhold or freeze it until the public service is organized, and shall at the same time proceed under the Act on Local Governments of Hungary to ensure the provision of the public service.
Section 44/J (1) If the property owner or the public service provider carries out their activity related to non-public utility collected domestic wastewater in a manner deviating from the legal regulation or the provisions of the notification, they are obliged to pay a public service fine.
(2)²⁶⁷ The public service fine shall be imposed by the water authority upon the initiative of the clerk.
(3) A fine may not be imposed more than five years after the commission of the act specified in subsection (1), unless the act consists of maintaining an unlawful state. In this case, the statute of limitations does not begin as long as the unlawful state persists. The water authority may impose the fine within one year of becoming aware of the act.

Definitions
For the purposes of this Act:
flood control: a preventive activity against the harmful effects of waters and floods leaving the riverbed, which protects the inundated area (floodplain) from regular flooding by establishing flood protection works (levees, walls, flood peak reduction reservoirs, flood relief channels) (protected floodplain);
mineral water: water originating from a natural underground reservoir or aquifer, whose mineral content typically differs from that of regular drinking water, and whose composition meets the limit values (e.g., biological, chemical) specified in the relevant legislation;
canal: a water facility suitable for performing one or more water management tasks simultaneously (water conveyance, water replenishment, inland water drainage, agricultural and other water services);
bank erosion: damage to the bank caused by the wave action of water (river, stream, canal, reservoir, lake);
5.³⁷⁸ primary flood protection water facility: a main defense structure declared along watercourses, or a regional flood protection facility (e.g., levee, wall, high bank, flood reservoir, flood relief channel) serving the flood protection of three or more municipalities, as well as a levee serving the flood control of a settlement or part of a settlement located in the open floodplain of a river;
6.³⁷⁹ interested party: a natural person, legal person, or unincorporated organization who/which owns or uses real estate in the area of interest;
7.³⁸⁰ area of interest: in the case of a public utility association, the areas where the services of the water facilities they implement (water supply, sewerage, inner-area precipitation or groundwater drainage) can be used;
medicinal water: mineral water that has proven therapeutic effects and its use for therapeutic purposes is authorized according to separate legislation;
drinking water: water suitable for regular human consumption that meets the physical, chemical, bacteriological, toxicological, and radiological limit values;
extent of public interest: the provision of public tasks at a standard determined with due regard to the personal and material conditions specified in separate legislation;
public task: a task of the state, the local government, and the association specified in this Act, as well as a task arising from the ownership or use of waters and water facilities in their possession or use;
12.³⁸¹ bed: the natural depression or constructed landform containing a watercourse or standing water, which is regularly covered by water up to a defined bank line;
a)³⁸² high-water bed: the area containing a watercourse or standing water, which is regularly covered by water during the passage of a flood, and which is demarcated by the design flood level or the highest flood level that has occurred, whichever is higher;
b) management of the high-water bed: the activity carried out for the use and utilization of the area, its surveying and registration, protection, safeguarding, and maintenance;
13.³⁸³ agricultural water management: water use and water damage control activities serving the tasks and interests of agriculture;
14.³⁸⁴ regional public water utility: interconnected public water utilities — which cannot be economically operated in technical isolation — that form a unified system and serve a large number of settlements (county) in a contiguous geographical area (part of the country, region), typically far from a water base, for water abstraction, treatment, and distribution, to which the public utility drinking water supply and sewerage of consumers also belong;
15.³⁸⁵ related to wastewater collection:
a) agglomeration: an area where the population and/or economic activity is sufficiently concentrated for municipal wastewater to be collected and directed to a wastewater treatment plant and a final discharge point;
b) population equivalent: the organic, biodegradable load having a five-day biochemical oxygen demand (BOD5) of 60 g of oxygen per day;
16.³⁸⁶ thermal water: all water of underground origin (from a water-bearing layer) whose outlet temperature (measured at the surface) is 30 °C or higher;
17.³⁸⁷ protective work: a water facility necessary for defense against the harmful effects of water;
18.³⁸⁸ protection zone: the underground space surrounding an operating or planned water abstraction works, which must be kept in greater security than its surroundings for the protection of the water abstraction in terms of quantity and quality;
19.³⁸⁹ protection area (including protective strip): the area surrounding an operating or planned water abstraction works, which must be kept in greater security than its surroundings for the protection of the water abstraction in terms of quantity and quality;
20.³⁹⁰ water base: the area or underground space used or designated for water abstraction works, and the water resource that can be extracted from there for human consumption or use, together with the existing or planned water supply facilities;
21.³⁹¹ watercourse: any natural or artificial landform in which water flows permanently or intermittently;
22.³⁹² water management: the use of waters, the preservation of opportunities for their use, and the protection and defense against the harmful effects of water (water damage control);
23.³⁹³ water use: an activity whose consequence is the influence on the flow and current conditions, quantity, or quality of water, as well as its bed or bank, for the purpose of utilizing the water;
24.³⁹⁴ water user: a natural person, legal person, or unincorporated organization who/which uses water for the provision of services or for their own purposes;
25.³⁹⁵ water work: an activity whose purpose is to influence the flow and current conditions, quantity, or quality of water, or its bed or bank, for the purpose of preventing the harmful effects of water, utilizing water, monitoring its quality and quantity, conducting mineral and geological research, or extracting mineral resources;
26.³⁹⁶ water facility: a structure (public utility), work of art, equipment, or installation whose purpose is to influence the state of the flow and current conditions, quantity, or quality of water, or its bed or bank, for the purpose of preventing the harmful effects of water, utilizing water—including the service provided through public utilities—, monitoring its quality and quantity, or for the purpose of conducting mineral and geological research or extracting mineral resources;
a)³⁹⁷ public-purpose water facility: a water facility that serves the water management tasks of the state or the local government as defined by law, in particular the service provided by public utilities, the protection against the harmful effects of water, the exploration, protection, use, replenishment, and monitoring of the state of water resources, and the management of water resources; public-purpose water facilities also include water facilities serving the construction, development, and maintenance of national public roads and the national railway network;
b)³⁹⁸ private-purpose water facility: a structure that, according to its purpose, serves industrial, household, or agricultural water supply, as well as water damage control, water purification, or hydropower utilization, and is not a public utility collecting, treating, using, or disposing of wastewater that is not considered a public-purpose water facility, and which, according to the Act on public utility water services, is not considered a public water utility;
27.³⁹⁹ water register: an authentic public register of the rights and obligations related to water works, water facilities, and water uses;
28.⁴⁰⁰ water damage: damage arising from the excess or scarcity of water;
29.⁴⁰¹ water resource management: the totality of activities whose purpose is to satisfy the demands for water use in such a way that no irreversible change occurs in the state of the waters and the possibility of accessing the water resource does not decrease;
30.⁴⁰² protection and defense against the harmful effects of water (water damage control): an organized activity against excessively much or excessively little water, including the construction, reconstruction, development, operation, and maintenance of protective works serving the prevention of damage, as well as the restoration following the defense;
31.⁴⁰³ water services: public tasks of the state and the local government, especially for satisfying water demands, for the discharge of wastewater, or for the harmless disposal of used waters, and for the protection of water resources, in particular:
a) the extraction, damming, storage, treatment, and distribution of surface or groundwater,
b) the collection and treatment of wastewater, which is then discharged into surface waters;
32.⁴⁰⁴ inhabited area: the inner area of the administrative territory of a municipality, and, regardless of zoning classification, any outer settlement part where, considering the registered land parcels (properties), at least 50% of the population registered with a permanent place of residence lives, and where the land use and the development of the infrastructure network comply with the settlement development plan that is in harmony with the approved national and regional spatial development plans affecting the municipality;
33.⁴⁰⁵ drinking water quality improvement programme (action plan): the totality of tasks aimed at achieving the goal of satisfying the quality requirements specified in the legislation concerning water intended for human consumption (drinking water).
34.⁴⁰⁶ reservoir for water damage control purposes:
a) flood reservoir:
aa) storm water reservoir: a reservoir built on or along a watercourse, serving exclusively to mitigate the peak discharges and water levels of flood waves, which holds water intermittently,
ab) flood peak reduction reservoir: a reservoir built on or along a watercourse that holds water permanently or intermittently, serving to mitigate the peak discharges and water levels of flood waves, where at least 50% of the maximum storage volume is kept free for flood retention,
ac) emergency reservoir: an area designated along watercourses for the partial retention of flood waves, serving for temporary water retention, and equipped with inlet and outlet points,
ad) flood relief reservoir: an area along watercourses that can be used for the partial retention of flood waves and the prevention of greater damage by means of a levee breach—in the event of an extraordinary state of defense or a state of emergency,
b) inland water reservoir: an area surrounded by natural boundaries or levees, serving for the collection of inland water.
35.⁴⁰⁷ well for agricultural irrigation purposes: a groundwater abstraction facility that exclusively provides agricultural water service to an area under agricultural cultivation;
36.⁴⁰⁸ (Repealed)
37.⁴⁰⁹ non-public utility collected domestic wastewater: domestic wastewater that, in the absence of a connection to a public sewer or the possibility of on-site treatment and discharge into a receiving body, is transported by vehicle from its place of generation or temporary storage for the purpose of harmless disposal;
38.⁴¹⁰ collection: the reception of non-public utility collected domestic wastewater from the property's owner, asset manager, possessor, or user, its transport, and its transfer for the purpose of harmless disposal.
39.⁴¹¹ right of bed use: the right of use belonging to the operator of a bed-use water facility located in the bed of waters belonging to the national assets, to the extent of the area occupied by the water facility;
40.⁴¹² bed-use water facility: a port mooring structure, pier—with the exception of a floating pier—, slipway, bank protection works, breakwater, or energy dissipator located in the bed of waters belonging to the national assets.
41.⁴¹³ agricultural water service: an activity aimed at the irrigation of areas under agricultural and forestry cultivation, the water supply of fishponds and other agricultural water uses, and the satisfaction of other water demands connected to the agricultural water service system.
42.⁴¹⁴ business organization: a business organization as defined by the act on civil procedure;
43.⁴¹⁵ flood conveyance channel: that part of the high-water bed which participates in the conveyance of floodwaters and ice, which may be a primary, secondary, or temporary conveyance channel.
44.⁴¹⁶ ecological water use: the use of the quantity of water necessary for the survival and preservation of natural systems.
