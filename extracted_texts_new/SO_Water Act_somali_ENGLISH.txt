**CHAPTER 1: THE NATIONAL WATER ACT**

**Article 1**
**Definitions**

**Water Abstraction Fee:** The fee a person pays to the competent authority when they wish to abstract water from natural reserve sources.

**Water Abstraction Permit:** An authorization granted by the designated authority to a person holding a water right to abstract water in accordance with the conditions specified in Article 15.4.

**The Act:** means the National Water Act.

**Aquifer:** Is the underground water reservoir.

**Pond:** A natural depression that collects or stores runoff water from the ground surface, including water captured during rainfall. A pond may be public or privately owned.

**Cistern:** An underground water catchment, mostly constructed underground, used to store or collect runoff water from rainfall. A cistern may be owned by the public or a private individual.

**Well:** Refers to a privately owned hand-dug well.

**Coastal Marine Waters:** Means the waters of the sea outside the coast, excluding fresh inland waters and saline groundwater reservoirs.

**The Laws:** Refers to the national laws.

**The Committee:** Refers to the National Water Policy Committee.

**Competent Authority:** Is the Ministry or any government branch holding legal authority based on this Water Act. Competent authorities include the Ministry responsible for water affairs, the Ministry responsible for Agriculture, the Ministry responsible for livestock, the Ministry responsible for Health, and the Ministry responsible for Rural Development and Environment, or any other Authority, Agency, or Ministry assigned responsibility in accordance with the provisions of this Water Act.

**Authorized Officer:** Is a senior inspector, investigator, or other government employee responsible for inspecting, enforcing, or implementing a matter, deriving authority from their agency in a specific government position or by an administrative order with instructions.

**District:** Refers to an administrative area, as defined in the Regions and Districts Act, Law No. 23/2002.

**Domestic Water Supply:** Covers the water needs to meet the general water requirements of the people, including irrigation for household gardens and for domestic livestock.

**Official Gazette:** Refers to the official media of the government of Somaliland.

**Groundwater:** Refers to a natural water body or flow beneath the ground, excluding man-made reservoirs or similar structures such as Cisterns; it includes aquifers located in coastal areas.

**Holder of the Right of Use:** Means a government entity to which the Ministry responsible for water affairs or any other party delegated by the Ministry has assigned the right to use government water assets or systems.

**Discharge:** Means any liquid poured into surface or ground water, directly or indirectly, intentionally or unintentionally, whether it causes pollution or not, through a pipe, channel, drain, or other man-made means.

**Lease Fee:** Means the fee paid by a service provider holding a contract from the district or other authorities (as defined in Article 28.4) to the holder of the right of use of the water system.

**Local Authority:** Is the government structure as defined in the Regions and Districts Act, Law No. 23/2002.

**Mayor:** Is the Chairman of the Local Council as defined in Law No. 23/2002.

**Standards:** Standards consist of any agreed-upon specifications for systems or services to be suitable for public use or consumption, including water quality standards.

**Person:** Means a legal water abstractor, which can be a private individual, company, cooperative, self-organized association, or a private group interested in the development or management of water as an independent organization, local property owners such as a village, town, ministry, or agency, or a part of the government at the national, regional, or district level, or a foreign group or an international or local organization.

**Pollution or Contamination:** Means any direct or indirect change to the physical, chemical, or biological properties of any part of the environment, or the discharge or emission of any waste or other substances that may have an adverse effect on water services or other natural resources, or that may create a hazardous condition for the environment and the health and life of people, animals, and plants. Furthermore, pollution is any condition that damages, limits, or restricts the quality of water as described in the Act and other water regulations.

**Polluter:** Is any person who may cause pollution to water.

**Region:** Is a local administrative area as defined in Law No. 23/2002.

**Riparian Owner:** Is the ownership of one or both banks of a river or valley.

**Specifications:** means any technical requirement for the construction of a government service, and includes the specifications for constructing a borehole.

**Surface Water:** Means water that flows or rests continuously or intermittently on the ground surface remaining from rainfall. It includes streams and ponds.

**Special Payment:** Means a monetary penalty determined by the water authority to be paid after it is established that the level or limit specified in the Water Abstraction Right has been violated.

**Water Committee:** Means an independent committee selected by the Ministry to oversee a work contract. It consists of local representatives from various water stakeholders.

**Water Tariff:** Means the price or amount of money a water user pays to the water provider.

**Water Management Unit:** Is a group of district employees assigned by the district to operate and manage the water service on behalf of the District.

**Water Provider:** Is the person who distributes water or water services to generate income.

**Water Resources:** Consist of surface and groundwater, including natural and man-made lakes, valleys, rainwater channels, desalinated water, treated or reused water, drains or waterfalls, and coastal marine waters.

**Water Right:** Means the right of a person who holds a legal document to abstract water from a water source for use, in strict compliance with water control and other regulations; water rights must be registered as specified in Article 12.

**Water Supply:** Means the provision of a water service intended for people's needs, not including its use for other purposes such as irrigation of farms, livestock, etc.

**Water Works:** Or water resource development works include channels and aqueducts, dams such as ponds and cisterns, water sources like wells and boreholes, pumps and their installations, water pipes, water diversions such as weirs and sluices, water treatment and sedimentation tanks, any structure built to store, convey, divert, control, or abstract water, intended to protect water sources from erosion and pollutants, and anything used for water of any kind or any water work protected by water works. They are divided into groundwater development and surface water development.

**Article 2**
**Title of the Act**
This Act shall be cited as the National Water Act of the Republic of Somaliland.

**Article 3**
**Scope of Application of this Act**
This Act shall apply to the management of the national waters of the Republic of Somaliland.

**Article 4**
**Objectives of the National Water Act**

1. The objectives of the Act are:
    a. The Water Act is to improve access to water resources through a sustainable and equitable management system based on the different types of service, water resources, and environmental protection.
    b. To recognize, legitimize, strengthen, and inform national institutions, the socio-economic system of water in Somaliland regarding water management and adjudication matters.
    c. To recognize, legitimize, strengthen, and present to the legislative bodies, the customary laws of Somaliland and the structures of traditional leaders of Somaliland regarding water management, the tax system, and adjudication matters.
    d. To govern the management of water resources and the distribution of water services by implementing and enforcing this Water Act and its by-laws.
    e. To enable the development of a national water strategy and its translation into national plans, to be evaluated and incorporated into the overall national plan and the utilization of grants and international cooperation.
    f. To enable comprehensive planning and evaluation shared among water resource programs and projects, the implementation and construction of which shall be left to the Ministry of Minerals and Water and the relevant Agencies.
    g. To establish cooperation between government Ministries and independent organizations working on the development, management, and use of water resources.
    h. To define the duties and rights of individuals regarding the social and economic aspects of using water in a proper manner aligned with the public interest.
    i. To encourage private ownership, particularly the system of joint ownership between the government and the public [Public and Private Partnership â€“ PPP].

**CHAPTER 2: GENERAL PRINCIPLES**

**Article 5**
**General Principles of the Water Act**

1. The conservation, development, and improvement of water quality and the protection of water resources, as well as the proper use of those resources.
2. The use of water resources is prohibited without a permit, unless there are circumstances specified and permitted by this Act.
3. To establish a water pricing (tariff) system, as water is a limited commodity with both social and economic importance.
4. To strengthen water supply services, especially the reuse of previously used water, by encouraging the creation of suitable facilities.

**Article 6**
**Role and Responsibilities of the Ministry**

1. The responsibilities of the Ministry of Minerals and Water include:
    a. Drafting the necessary by-laws and administrative procedures, and resolving administrative implementation issues to fulfill the substance of the objectives contained in this Act, as indicated in Article 4.
    b. Supervising the enforcement of regulations and managing matters related to the prevention and control of water pollution.
    c. Establishing and implementing technical and non-technical systems for water standards to protect the quality of water use, utilizing appropriate knowledge, equipment, and technology.
    d. Implementing and developing standards for drinking water quality, including bottled water, as well as the reuse of mixed water (water from farms, sewers, industrial wastewater, etc.), and how to protect and utilize aquifers, surface water, coasts, seas, and other water resources.
    e. Creating and maintaining a complete register of all rights and obligations related to water, including information on water, land, and the specific socio-economic system of water use.

**CHAPTER 4**
**Water Rights and Abstraction Permits**

**Article 9**
**Ownership/Co-ownership**

1. Ownership of natural water resources is vested in the Government of Somaliland, which acts as a trustee for the citizens of Somaliland.
2. The use of water resources shall be managed by the appropriate laws related to the administrative systems of the Government of Somaliland.

**Article 10**
**Private Ownership**

1. Natural water resources such as aquifers, rivers, lakes, watercourses, etc., cannot be privately owned.
2. Water resources are national property and cannot be claimed through ownership of land adjacent to or containing water such as a Lake, Well, Sea, Valley, etc.
3. The use of water resources, once abstracted from their natural source, may become private property in accordance with water laws.
4. The owner may sell the abstracted water resources in accordance with water laws; a seller of drinking water must comply with the conditions stipulated in Article 45.1.

**Article 11**
**The Right to Use Water Resources**

1. The use of water resources is the right to use a specific quantity of water abstracted from a water body in a specified location. The specific characteristics of the right to use water include the permit holder, location, purpose of use, quantity, and details of the equipment.
2. The permit to use water resources may be held by a government agency, a private individual, or a community, such as a water users' association.
3. The Water Act recognizes all customary practices of water resource use that do not conflict with the law.
4. If there is a customary practice for the use of water resources, it must be registered as stipulated in Article 12 of this Act.
5. Any water abstraction using mechanical equipment must be registered upon application to the Ministry as stipulated in Article 12 and shall be registered if it meets the requirements.
6. Anyone previously registered for the use of water resources must apply for a water abstraction permit in accordance with Article 15 if their operation requires it.

**Article 12**
**Registration of Water Rights**

1. This Act stipulates the registration of all water rights, including customary water rights. The competent authority must maintain and correct the register of water rights. The register shall serve as the basis for resolving disputes concerning the rights to and use of water resources.
2. The Ministry shall appoint a water officer for each district, who shall be responsible for creating, maintaining, safeguarding, and making available the district's register of water rights. The district must regularly submit an updated copy of the register to the Ministry responsible for water affairs.
3. The following services shall be managed free of charge: the registration system, the initial registration certificate, and inspection of the register when required.
4. Notwithstanding the provisions of sub-article 3 of this article, any person who needs to extract information from the register, for any purpose, must pay a reasonable fee at the office of the district water officer, the amount of which will be specified in the by-laws of this Act.
5. The Ministry responsible for water affairs shall publish by-laws concerning the procedure for registration and maintenance of the register. The by-laws shall detail the right of every person to inspect the register and the procedure required to correct any entry.
6. Every entry in the register must provide the specific details of the water right, including the date of the claim, its registration, rejection, and any possible corrections concerning that right.
7. Any person applying for the registration of a water right, hereinafter referred to as the applicant, must follow the procedure mentioned in sub-article 5 of this article.
8. Notwithstanding sub-article 7 of this article, the following procedure must be followed:
    a. The applicant must write an application to the officer responsible for the district register, completing the application form provided by the officer.
    b. The application must be published in the official gazette for a period of not less than one month and not more than three months.
    c. Within the aforementioned period, any person opposing the application must register their objection, in writing, at the district office.
    d. The officer must acknowledge the objection, consult with the applicant and affected parties, and then make a decision within the period specified in Article 12, sub-article 8, paragraph c.
    e. If no objection is registered within that period, the Water Right shall be granted and registered.
    f. The officer responsible for the register must issue a registration certificate detailing the specific characteristics of the applicant's Right.
    g. The registration of a water right cannot change or alter the nature and purpose of its use.

**Article 13**
**Transfer, Revocation, and Modification of Water Rights**

1. Any change to the specific characteristics of a water right requires it to be reflected in the register, following the procedure for correcting information in the register established by the Competent Authority. The holder of the water right is required to follow the necessary steps to amend the information stored in the register.
2. Heirs of a water right may inherit it in accordance with the relevant inheritance laws. The new heirs are required to submit their specific details to the district office to correct the register.
3. A registered water right may be transferred to another (third) party in accordance with the procedure set out in this Act.
4. A water right may be acquired whenever it becomes necessary to use the water for a public water system or to use it for the development or expansion of private water works, in order to install a system that serves the public interest.
5. In the event of the acquisition of a private water right, the owner of the water right must be given equivalent compensation, as determined by a competent Court.

**Article 14**
**Settlement of Disputes related to Water Rights**

1. If a dispute arises concerning a water right, community-based methods for resolving such matters shall be strengthened by applying customary law.
2. If it is not possible to resolve the dispute as per sub-article 1 of this article, it shall be referred to the competent Court.

**Article 15**
**Water Abstraction Permit**

1. All abstraction and use of water from natural sources must be authorized by the competent authority that issues the abstraction permit.
2. No permit shall be issued if it would cause harm to the natural environment, such as:
    a. Over-exploitation of groundwater or surface water reserves.
    b. Saline water intrusion into the reserve.
    c. Unauthorized diversion of natural water flow.
    d. If the available water could lead to an over-concentration of people and livestock in one area, causing excessive erosion or endangering the conservation of rangelands and pastoral areas.
    e. If such activities could lead to the total depletion of the water reserve, thereby depriving users from another area of their rights.

National Water Act Law No. 49/2011 2011

3. The Ministry responsible for water affairs shall define the abstraction threshold, which, if exceeded, requires a specific water abstraction permit. This permissible threshold will be defined in the by-laws.
4. A water abstraction permit is required when:
    a. The abstraction volume of the water works exceeds the permissible threshold.
    b. The abstracted water is intended for a public water supply system, regardless of the abstraction volume.
    c. The water source is located in a designated area restricted by this Act.
5. The competent authority for issuing an abstraction permit is the Ministry of Minerals and Water, which is responsible for water affairs, unless the Ministry delegates this power to a district office for specific reasons, in accordance with the water by-laws.
6. When registering a water right operating with machinery, the district water officer shall decide whether the water right requires an abstraction permit, referring to sub-article 4 of this article. If so, they shall provide an application form and assist the applicant with the application procedure.
7. The applicant shall submit their application to the competent authority designated by the Ministry responsible for water affairs for a decision.
8. The water abstraction permit must be recorded in the general register kept at the Ministry responsible for water affairs.
9. Any change to the information on the abstraction permit must be registered in accordance with the water by-laws.
10. The competent authority shall develop a plan for the design and operation of boreholes, clustered wells, and large ponds. This plan must specify details including: location, number of wells, maximum depth, and maximum yield.
11. The abstraction volume stated in the permit will determine the tax to be levied, if any.

**Article 16**
**Restrictions, Temporary Suspension, Acquisition, and Revocation.**

1. It is illegal to abstract a quantity of water exceeding that specified in the by-laws without a permit, or to exceed the quantity stated in the permit. Such abstraction may result in the competent authority confiscating the equipment or taking other measures that may lead to the cessation of such abstraction, regardless of ownership.
2. An abstraction permit may be restricted or temporarily suspended in the public interest for the following reasons:
    a. A severe water shortage occurs in the water reserve from which abstraction is permitted.
    b. When it is confirmed that the applicant has used more than the permitted water quantity for a period of two years.
    c. Damage to the quality or pollution of the permitted water reserve that exceeds the permissible level.
    d. Abstraction that may cause damage to the water reserve, such as from polluted water or seawater intrusion.
    e. A threat to the environment that could cause erosion, and an over-concentration of people and livestock in one area.
3. There shall be equivalent compensation for such a restriction or suspension if the permit holder is not at fault, the amount of which will be determined by by-laws issued by the competent authority.
4. If the permit holder fails to use the permit for five consecutive years, the Ministry responsible for water affairs may revoke the permit without paying any compensation.
5. In the case of the situation described in sub-article 2, paragraph (b) and sub-article 4 of this article, the Ministry has the authority to grant the water right to new applicants in accordance with the by-laws.

**CHAPTER 5**
**IMPLEMENTATION & OWNERSHIP**

**Article 17**
**Public Ownership and Use of Water**

1. The ownership of natural water resources cannot be transferred.
2. All water assets financed with government property are national property for which the government is responsible.

**Article 18**
**Delegation of Public Interest Service**

1. The Central Government may delegate the right to use water resources to a Local Government after an agreement is reached by both parties.
2. The agreement must fully transfer to the concerned local government the provision of water services and the right to use the assets.
3. Notwithstanding the provisions of sub-articles 1 and 2 of this article, the Local Government has no authority to sell, transfer to another party, or enter into a joint management agreement with a third party regarding the Central Government assets entrusted to it.

**Article 19**
**Resumption of Right of Use by the Central Government**

1. If Local Governments fail to ensure the proper provision of water services, thereby crippling the delivery of water services, the central government has the authority to resume the right of use of the national property.
2. When the Central Government resumes the right of use of the property, the central government must continue the responsibility of fulfilling the water services for the concerned area.

**Article 20**
**The Right to Use Water Assets**

1. The right to use water assets for the public interest entails the responsibility to ensure the continuous management of services related to:
    a. Properly organizing the efficient operation and maintenance of the water use assets.
    b. Organizing investment to renew the water resource use assets, planned according to the expected lifespan of the various components.
    c. Planning for expansion based on need.

**Article 21**
**Investment by Non-Governmental Organizations in Public Assets**

1. Investment by non-governmental organizations in water assets intended for public service must receive approval as provided in Article 15.
2. Assets developed under sub-article 1 of this article must, upon completion, be added to the national property and the property register, based on the standards of Article 15.
3. In accordance with sub-article 2 of this article, the investing organization must transfer the water assets to the lowest level of administration capable of fulfilling the right to use water rights as provided in sub-article 1 of this article, following the procedures prepared by the ministry responsible for water affairs.
4. When the water service is located entirely within the boundaries of a single district, the lowest appropriate level for the right of use is the district.
5. When the water service is located within the boundaries of different districts, the most appropriate level for the right of use is the region.

**Article 22**
**District Investment in Government Assets**

1. Investment by districts in productive assets intended for public water supply must be permitted as provided in Article 15.
2. Water assets in which a district has invested national property belong to that district upon completion, and the transfer procedures do not apply, and there is no compensation.

**Article 23**
**Central Government Investment in National Assets**

1. Central government investment must be aligned with the district's needs plan and development priorities at the local level.
2. Assets developed under sub-article 1 of this article must be added to the national property register and handed over to the lowest administrative level capable of operating the right of use and the responsibility defined in Article 20.
3. The most appropriate and lowest level for the right to use water system assets is the district.

**Article 24**
**Private Sector Investment in Former Government Assets**

1. When private individuals invest in a government-owned service with the purpose of expanding or renewing that service, the investment agreement made must specify how those assets will be incorporated into public property, including:
    a. The agreed-upon date.
    b. The regulations to be used for valuing the recovery of the private investment.
    c. The methods for compensating for losses incurred by the private ownership.

2. Investment by private individuals in public water services can be classified into the following levels:

**CHAPTER 6**
**URBAN WATER SUPPLY**

**Article 26**
**Definition of Urban Areas**

1. Urban areas are the Grade 'A' districts as per the Regions and Districts Act (Law No. 23/2002). At the time this Act was passed, the Grade 'A' districts were Hargeisa, Gebilay, Burco, Erigavo, Las Anod, Berbera, and Borama.

**Article 27**
**Districts and Urban Water Supply Committees**

1. The responsibility for organizing and supervising the proper distribution of water services in specified areas shall be delegated by the ministry to the competent authority of that area.
2. The local authority empowered for urban water distribution is the district.
3. For the purpose of overseeing the water service and the work contract, the independent water committee must include members representing the various consumers, who are appointed by the Ministry responsible for water affairs.

**Article 28**
**Operation of Urban Water Supply**

1. To fulfill the responsibility defined in Article 27, sub-article 2, the district must delegate the operation and management of the water service to a suitable entity, hereinafter referred to as the water provider.
2. The water provider can be:
    a. An independent public Water Agency at the local level (with separate accounts).
    b. An independent public Water Agency at the national level (with separate accounts).
    c. An independent private water provider (an individual, small company, or large private or joint-stock company such as a PPP).
3. The legal agreement entered into by the district and the water provider shall be called an 'Operational Agreement', and it shall define the responsibilities assigned to the water provider. It must be approved and co-signed by the ministry, the district mayor, and the authorized representative of the water provider.

Page 21 of 41

National Water Act Law No. 49/2011 2011

4. The Operational Agreement shall specify:
    a. The area where the water provider is required to deliver its service.
    b. The different levels of service the water provider commits to providing to its customers.
    c. The technical standards that the water provider must follow when carrying out works (maintenance, rehabilitation, and development) on the system, including the abstraction base and household connections.
    d. The tariff the water provider is permitted to charge for its service and the regulations for setting this price during the contract period.
    e. The lease fee that the water provider must pay to the empowered authority as compensation for the use of national assets.
    f. The fee required to be paid by the water provider to the Ministry responsible for water affairs, which are the fees for water abstraction and the use of water resources.
    g. An inventory of the national property assets transferred to the water provider, with details of their value on the day the agreement is signed and the method for calculating the depreciation of the equipment during its use.
    h. The requirements for promoting accountability and transparency, which is that the water provider must submit operational and financial reports to the Ministry, the District, and the Water Committee to monitor the proper execution of the work.
    i. The performance standards that the water provider is required to meet when providing the service.
    j. Details of the works and investments required to be carried out by the water provider.
    k. Details of the work and investments required to be carried out by the holder of the right of use.
    l. The duration of the agreement and the conditions for renewal.
    m. The conditions under which the agreement can be terminated by one of the three parties.
    n. A copy of the Operational Agreement must be given to the competent authority for registration, and likewise, a copy must be given to the water committee where one exists.
    o. If a disagreement regarding the interpretation of the agreement's articles arises between the district and the water provider, the independent water committee shall mediate between the district and the water provider.

**Article 29**
**Financial Management for Asset Renewal**

1. Every water provider must create a budget from its revenue intended for the renewal and expansion of the water system.
2. The amount of that budget, expressed as a percentage (%) of revenue, must be specified in the Operational Agreement.
3. The use of that budget will be based on an investment program, agreed upon annually by the parties to the agreement.

**Rights to Agricultural Water**

**CHAPTER 10**
**DRINKING WATER QUALITY STANDARDS**

**Article 44**
**Role in Water Quality**

1. The Ministry of Health is the competent authority for defining the required sanitation standards around drinking water sources and for personal hygiene.
2. The Ministry responsible for water affairs must define and issue standards for drinking water quality, the system for its monitoring, methods for its testing, and the registration of certified laboratories.
3. Districts must implement water quality protection programs in accordance with the provisions of the operational agreement as specified in Article 28, sub-article 3 of this Act.

**Article 45**
**District Responsibilities Regarding Water Quality**

1. The district is responsible for implementing compliance with the requirements for drinking water quality.
2. If the quality of the distributed water violates the established national standard, the district must order the water provider to stop the service until the approved national water quality standard is met.
3. Notwithstanding sub-article 2 of this article, the district, in consultation with the competent Ministry of Health, may, in very difficult circumstances, allow the water provider to continue distributing water of sub-standard quality if the following conditions exist:
    a. No alternative water source of better quality is available.
    b. The permission is for a limited period.
    c. Consumers are aware of the health risk, and precautions are being taken to prevent it.
    d. When the permitted time expires, the district must check the water quality and grant permission if the quality meets the required standard.

**Article 46**
**Water Provider's Responsibility Regarding Water Quality**

1. The responsibility of the water provider is to ensure that the quality of drinking water and water used for preparing food and beverages complies with the water quality standards defined in Article 44, sub-article 1.
2. The service provided by the water supplier to the public must have a quality that complies with the national standards for drinking water quality.
3. The water provider must pay all costs for water quality testing.
4. Notwithstanding sub-article 1 of this article, in the event of the situation mentioned in Article 45, sub-article 3, the water provider must resolve the quality problems within a reasonable period of time, which will be set by the Ministry of Health.

**CHAPTER 11**
**GROUNDWATER PROTECTION**

**Article 47**
**Priority and Explanation**

1. The use of groundwater must be permitted in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter, and other relevant regulations and by-laws.
2. Groundwater means the abstraction of water, construction works, operation, and installation of equipment related to water abstraction, groundwater recharge, yield, and any action that affects the flow and quality of underground water.
3. Groundwater works or equipment refers to installations used for groundwater abstraction, such as shallow wells, boreholes, channels, interconnected streams, any excavation intended for abstraction equipment, their installation, and any related construction.

**Article 48**
**Competent Authority for Water Protection**

1. The Ministry of Minerals and Water is the body responsible for water; however, this does not prevent other ministries and agencies from having a role in the management of groundwater, if delegated by the ministry in accordance with the law and water by-laws.
2. The Ministry responsible for water affairs is the competent authority for the collection, investigation, inventory, tracing of hydrogeological data, and the survey and proper execution of groundwater works.

**Article 49**
**Permits and Information**

1. Water abstraction permits must specify the maximum annual abstraction volume, with reports submitted to the ministry.
2. Any person using groundwater is legally required to provide new and old technical information to the designated authority.

**Article 50**
**Hydrogeological Survey**

1. The Ministry shall issue permits for hydrogeological surveys or works for the exploitation of groundwater resources, and it must ensure that the provisions of this Act concerning the conservation of water resources, their quality, and the continuous provision of information and data are upheld.
2. Any person who encounters groundwater during a survey or while carrying out surface works must not harm the water resource.
3. Any person carrying out survey or drilling activities for groundwater, oil, minerals, or other work underground for any purpose must promptly inform the designated authority upon encountering groundwater and provide other information as the work proceeds.

**Article 51**
**Yield and Discharge into Groundwater**

**Article 53**
**By-laws concerning Drilling**

**Article 55**
**Priority and Definition**

**Article 56**

1. The Ministry of Minerals and Water is the competent authority for the provisions of this chapter concerning natural surface water, including water for agricultural irrigation.

**Article 57**
**Normal Use of Surface Water and its Limitation**

1. No permit is required for the use of natural surface water sources, except for abstraction by machinery and use for the public interest.
2. Livestock have the right to drink surface water, which obliges harmless passage through the land of others without any fee or obstruction.

**Article 60**
**Rights of Downstream Users**

1. When granting an abstraction permit to private individuals developing water assets, the rights of downstream users must be protected.
2. A permit shall not be granted to private individuals developing water works if its issuance harms the rights of downstream water users.
3. All users of surface water have equal rights, whether upstream or downstream. Upstream users cannot block the natural flow of water to downstream areas, the runoff from rain, and anything in excess of the amount permitted in their abstraction permit.
4. Notwithstanding sub-article 3 of this article, upstream users cannot be held responsible for problems arising from sedimentation and anything that impedes the natural flow of water in which they were not involved.

**Article 61**
**Discharge of Waste**

1. The discharge of wastewater and other liquids into surface water, directly or indirectly, requires a permit. The Ministry must establish a by-law concerning the maximum permissible and prohibited levels of substances whose discharge would not harm surface water.
2. No discharge is permitted if it causes physical, chemical, biological, olfactory, radiological, or bacterial changes to the surface water, which could cause harm to people, animals, and the environment.
3. A specific by-law must be established for the discharge of waste into flowing water and into reservoirs that are constantly refilled.
4. Any agreement between private individuals regarding discharge into flowing water shall be illegal if it contravenes the articles of this Act.

**Article 62**
**Deposition of Solids**

1. It is not permitted to place on or near the dry banks of surface water solid materials that could cause pollution, change the water level, obstruct the flow and distribution of water, or pose any other danger to the water and its users.
2. No construction unrelated to water works may be built or erected within dry watercourses and public access ways, which could obstruct the natural flow of water, without a permit from the ministry.

**Article 63**
**Permit for Extraction from Watercourse Beds**

**CHAPTER 14**
**DELINEATION OF INSTITUTIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES**

**Article 72**
**The Ministry and Agencies**

1. The Ministry responsible for water affairs is known as the Ministry of Minerals and Water.

**Relationship between Water and Land**

**CHAPTER 16**
**MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS**

**Article 80**
**Liability for Damage**

1. The competent authority is responsible for lost or damaged property and personal injury caused by those involved in the administration of the work. This relieves those individuals of liability for lawsuits and compensation for injured persons and damaged property that should have been brought against them, if it resulted from an administrative act, except for negligence such as failure to follow safety regulations.
2. The concerned person is responsible for personal injury and property damage caused by a mistake they themselves made.
3. A person who obstructs the provisions of this Act and its administrative system shall be liable for a payment based on the damage their action caused and the consequences of misusing the Water Act.
4. The owner or their agent may place liability on a third party for any loss or damage directly or indirectly caused by them while carrying out water resource development works or legal requirements.

**Article 81**
**Penalties**

1. A person who obstructs any part of the provisions of the articles of this Water Act and the orders or instructions issued in the by-laws of this Water Act, or obstructs an officer or a person representing them from carrying out an operation, after being warned by the designated officer, shall be liable for an initial fine and another added for each day the contravention continues.
2. A person who, after being penalized for a violation as per sub-article 1 of this article, commits the same act again, shall be liable for a fine double the previous one.
3. A person who directly contravenes the provisions of this Water Act, depending on the severity of the violation and its repetition, shall be sentenced to imprisonment for 12 months and a fine as per sub-article 2 of this article.
4. The competent authority shall issue a by-law or legally published notice concerning the fines and imprisonment appropriate for obstructing the articles of the Water Act.
