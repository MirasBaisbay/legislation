Official Gazette – Issue 41 (bis) on October 16, 2021

Law No. 147 of 2021
Issuing the Water Resources and Irrigation Law

In the name of the People,
The President of the Republic,

The House of Representatives has decreed the following law, and we have promulgated it:

(Article One)
The provisions of the accompanying law concerning water resources and irrigation shall be enacted.

(Article Two)
Law No. 12 of 1984 issuing the Irrigation and Drainage Law shall be repealed, as shall any provision that contravenes the provisions of this Law.

(Article Three)
The Prime Minister shall, upon the proposal of the Minister of Water Resources and Irrigation and the Minister of Agriculture and Land Reclamation, issue the Executive Regulations for the accompanying law within six months from the date of its enactment. Until these regulations are issued, the existing regulations and decisions shall continue to be in force on the date this law and the accompanying law come into effect, in a manner that does not conflict with their provisions.

(Article Four)
This Law shall be published in the Official Gazette and shall come into effect on the day following the date of its publication.
This Law shall be stamped with the seal of the State and shall be enforced as one of its laws.

Issued at the Presidency of the Republic on 10 Rabi' al-Awwal, 1443 H
(Corresponding to October 16, 2021 AD)

Abdel Fattah El-Sisi

Water Resources and Irrigation Law

Part One
Water Resources, Public Property, and Related Private Works

(Chapter One)
Definitions

Article (1):
For the application of the provisions of this Law, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings indicated next to each:

The Ministry: The Ministry concerned with water resources and irrigation.

The Minister: The Minister concerned with water resources and irrigation.

Competent General Administration: The general administration concerned with water resources and their facilities within the Ministry's sectors, authorities, and bodies.

Competent Director-General: The Director-General of the Competent General Administration within the Ministry's sectors, authorities, and bodies.

Competent Engineer: The engineer granted the capacity of a judicial police officer concerning the application of the provisions of this Law, each within their scope of jurisdiction.

Administration Officials: Mayors and Sheikhs.

Water Users' Association: An entity that includes all water users and beneficiaries of public or private irrigation and drainage networks and systems, groundwater wells, dams, and reservoirs in old or new lands at the level of specific command areas.

Water Councils: Entities composed of representatives of water users from the concerned bodies and water users' associations at the level of district engineering offices and higher organizational levels, to undertake coordination and consultation with the Ministry and concerned bodies in the management of available water resources.

Public Canal and Drain: Any watercourse intended for irrigation or drainage established, managed, or maintained by the Ministry before or after the effective date of this Law.

Groundwater: Water stored in the subsoil between cracks, pores, sands, and rocks.

Watercourses: Rayahs (main canals), public canals, public drains, and flood spillways and their channels, which are supervised by the Ministry.

Water Resources: The resources that the Ministry is responsible for managing and developing, including Nile water, rainwater, floodwater, groundwater, desalinated saltwater, cloud seeding, and reused water from agricultural, industrial, or sanitary drainage after treatment.

Well: Any hole or structure that penetrates the earth's layers, through which groundwater is extracted and utilized on the surface, or water is drained from the surface. The structures built over the well, as well as the equipment and machinery used for this purpose, are considered part of the well.

Contour Line: An imaginary (virtual) line on contour maps that connects all points having the same elevation relative to a reference point (sea level).

River Setback Limit:

Represents the end of the river setback area, extending up to 80 meters outside the two embankment lines on both sides of the river, and also up to 80 meters from the embankment line on islands, in the section from behind the Aswan Dam to the estuary, except for areas with approved regulation lines, in which case the established public corniche boundary is considered the river setback limit.

The setback area for Lake Nasser (Lake Nasser Basin) is defined as up to a distance of three kilometers outside the 182.00 contour line along the sides of the lake, its islands, and khors (inlets).

The setback area for the Toshka Depressions (Toshka Depressions Basin) is defined as a distance of 200 meters outside the 170.00 contour line.

The setback of the Toshka Spillway Canal (Toshka Spillway Canal Basin) is defined as a distance of 200 meters outside the 182.00 contour line.

The setback of the reach between the High Dam and the Aswan Dam is defined as a distance of 200 meters outside the 123.00 contour line.

The setback area for the main barrages on the Nile River and its two branches is defined as up to a distance of 150 meters outside the embankment line, with a length of 500 meters upstream of the barrage and 500 meters downstream of the barrage.

Nile River Course: Everything that falls within the two setback limits of the Nile River, extending from the international borders with Sudan to the estuaries of the Damietta and Rosetta branches into the Mediterranean Sea, including Lake Nasser and its khors, the Toshka Spillway Canal, and the Toshka Depressions.

Embankment Line: The line that defines the area necessary to accommodate the maximum discharges of the Nile River, as determined by the Ministry, and is located outside the waterway section.

Course Boundary: The line resulting from the intersection of the water surface corresponding to the maximum discharges of the Nile River—as determined by the Ministry—with the side slope of the cross-section.

Cross-Section: A section perpendicular to the Nile River, its branches, and watercourses.

Waterway Section: The area where the Nile water flows at maximum discharges—as determined by the Ministry—and is located between the two nearest course boundaries at the cross-section, and in the case of Lake Nasser and the Toshka Spillway up to the 182.00 contour line, and in the case of the Toshka Depressions up to the 170.00 contour line.

Prohibited Zone: The area where it is prohibited to carry out any works or installations, etc., except for public utility works after the Ministry's approval. It is defined by the area located outside the two embankment lines up to a distance of 30 meters at the cross-section of the Nile River course and its permanent islands, and in the case of Lake Nasser up to a distance of two kilometers from the 182.00 contour line. In the case of the Toshka Depressions, up to a distance of 100 meters from the 170.00 contour line, and in the case of the Toshka Spillway, up to a distance of 100 meters from the 182.00 contour line. In the case of the reach between the High Dam and the Aswan Dam, up to a distance of 100 meters outside the 123.00 contour line, and in the case of the main barrages on the Nile River and its two branches, up to a distance of 100 meters outside the embankment line and with a length of 500 meters upstream of the barrage and 500 meters downstream of the barrage.

Restricted Zone: The area where it is not permitted to carry out any works or activities except with the approval of the Ministry. These are the following areas:

The area located outside the embankment line in the distance between 30 meters and 80 meters at the cross-section of the Nile River course and its permanent islands.

Lake Nasser, from a distance of two kilometers from the 182.00 contour line from both banks to a distance of three kilometers from the 182.00 contour line from both banks.

Toshka Depressions, from a distance of 100 meters to a distance of 200 meters from the 170.00 contour line from both banks.

Toshka Spillway, from a distance of 100 meters to a distance of 200 meters from the 182.00 contour line from both banks.

The reach between the High Dam and the Aswan Dam, from a distance of 100 meters to a distance of 200 meters outside the 123.00 contour line from both banks.

The main barrages on the Nile River and its two branches, in the distance between 100 meters and 150 meters at the cross-section outside the embankment line, and with a length of 500 meters upstream of the barrage and 500 meters downstream of the barrage.

For watercourses, a distance of 20 meters outside the public easements as a maximum limit.

The prohibited zone for sea coasts as stated in Articles (87, 88) of this Law.

Nile Silt Islands: All lands located within the two setback limits of the river and surrounded by water from all sides. These islands are considered:

Permanent: If the elevations of their lands are higher than the corresponding elevations for the maximum discharges determined by the Ministry.

Temporary: If submerged by water at these discharges, in which case they are considered part of the waterway section.

Nile Alluvium Lands: Lands and islands that the river shifts from their location or uncovers, whether located inside or outside the river setback limits.

Nile Bridges: The existing bridges of the Nile River and its branches, which are public property related to water resources and irrigation.

Shoreline: The maximum extent to which sea water reaches on land during the highest tide, as determined by the Ministry.

Prohibition Line for Sea Coasts: The boundary of the zone where it is prohibited to establish any facility on the sea coasts, in light of what is determined by the Ministry and the Ministry of Environment, with the prohibition line being final after approval by the competent higher committee.

Final Prohibition Zone for Sea Coasts (Shore Sanctity): The area enclosed between the shoreline and the final prohibition line inland along the Egyptian sea coasts.

Flood Spillway: Any artificial watercourse that receives rainwater and floodwater from a collection basin for its discharge. The Ministry is responsible for its demarcation, management, and maintenance, including its structures.

Natural Valley: A natural depression on the earth's surface extending between plains, plateaus, and mountains, formed by running water from rain and floods. The valley begins in a high area and ends in a low area, sloping towards water bodies or natural or artificial depressions. Its jurisdiction is subject to the competent governorate or other authorities of jurisdiction.

Protection Structures: All structures supervised by the Ministry for the purpose of protection from flood risks, or for collecting and storing rainwater, or directing or discharging it.

Prohibited Zone for Protection Structures: The area where it is not permitted to establish any activities within the scope of protection structures, defined by the boundaries and distances approved by the Ministry.

(Chapter Two)
Public Property Related to Water Resources and Irrigation

Article (2):
Public property related to water resources and irrigation includes the following:

The course of the Nile River and its bridges, including the lands and structures located between these bridges, unless they are privately owned by the state or others.

Watercourses and their bridges, including the lands and structures located between these bridges, unless they are privately owned by the state or others.

Flood spillways and protection structures supervised by the Ministry.

Structures specific to balancing irrigation and drainage water or protecting lands, cities, or villages from the encroachment of water or from erosion, as well as other industrial structures owned by the state related to irrigation and drainage and established within public property.

The basin of Lake Nasser, the basin of the Toshka Depressions, the Toshka Spillway Canal, and any other basins determined by the Ministry.

Lands determined by the Ministry around its groundwater wells for their protection and to ensure their proper use, and lands on which the Ministry carries out artificial recharge of groundwater.

Lands whose ownership is expropriated for public benefit for purposes of developing, managing, and using water resources, or those lands owned by the state and allocated for these purposes.

Nile alluvium lands located inside and outside the river setback limits, excluding lands privately owned by the state or others.

Article (3):
Without prejudice to any prior dispositions or established legal positions, jurisdiction over all lands and real estate from the state's public property related to water resources and irrigation, as referred to in Article (2) of this Law, shall be transferred to the Ministry.
By a decision of the Minister, the lands and real estate from the public property related to water resources and irrigation whose purpose of allocation for public benefit has ended shall be determined. The decision shall specify either the allocation of all or part of these lands to the State Property Administration to achieve a public interest, or the Ministry's retention of all or part of these lands to serve one of the Ministry's current or future activities.

The Ministry shall undertake the management, utilization, and disposal of the lands whose purpose of allocation for public benefit has ended, and shall exercise the rights of the owner in all matters related to them, in accordance with the provisions of the Law on Regulating Contracts Concluded by Public Bodies, issued by Law No. 182 of 2018, and the Law on Regulating the Partnership with the Private Sector in Infrastructure Projects, Public Services, and Utilities, issued by Law No. 67 of 2010. All of this shall be done after obtaining the opinion of the Ministry of Defense and Military Production.

Article (4):
The following lands shall be encumbered by the restrictions mentioned in the second paragraph of this article, for the service of public purposes related to water resources, even if supervision over them has been entrusted to one of the entities referred to in Article (5) of this Law:

First: Lands located between the river setback limits or within the expropriation limits for watercourses, whether privately owned by the state or others from public or private legal persons or individuals.

Second: Lands located outside the easements of watercourses for a distance of twenty meters as a maximum limit.

Third: Lands alongside the lines and networks of covered drains (main and field), developed irrigation networks, and covered irrigation pipe networks for a distance not exceeding five meters from each side, measured from the axis of these lines and networks.

Fourth: Lands of the restricted zone of the Nile River course according to the definition mentioned in Article (1) of this Law and for the distances specified for paragraphs (1) to (6) of this zone.

Fifth: Lands located outside the protection zone for groundwater wells mentioned in clause (6) of Article (2) of this Law for a distance not exceeding five meters from each side.

Sixth: Lands located outside the prohibited zone for structures for protection against flood risks for a distance of twenty meters as a maximum limit from each side.

Seventh: The final prohibition zone for sea coasts according to the provisions of Articles (87, 88) of this Law.

The restrictions are as follows:

The establishment of any works that the Ministry deems necessary for the protection of bridges or public structures, and their maintenance and restoration. The Ministry shall have the right to take the necessary earth from these lands in case of necessity, provided that their owners are fairly compensated.

The disposal of the output of clearing watercourses on the lands referred to in case of necessity, with fair compensation to their owners.

It is prohibited, without a license from the Ministry, to establish any structures or excavations for the lands referred to.

The competent engineer of the Ministry may enter the lands referred to for inspection of works carried out therein. If it is found that works have been carried out or initiated in violation of the preceding provisions, he shall have the right to order the violator to remove them immediately; otherwise, the work shall be stopped and removed administratively at the violator's expense.

Article (5):
The Ministry, to the exclusion of others, is responsible, within the framework of the provisions of this Law, for supervising all works or other activities undertaken by the concerned authorities on public property related to water resources and irrigation. However, the Ministry may entrust the supervision of any part of this property to one of the ministries, public authorities, local administration units, public bodies, or water users' associations.
It is prohibited for these authorities to make any change or modification or to authorize any works on this property except after obtaining the approval of the Ministry. In case of violation of the conditions set by the Ministry, it is permissible to cancel the arrangement with these authorities.

Article (6):
The state shall not be held responsible for damages that occur to lands or structures located within the public property related to water resources and irrigation if the damage arises from a change in water levels for emergency reasons or as required by irrigation and drainage balancing works, or due to a rise in sea level or storms, provided that the Ministry has taken the necessary preventive measures and procedures, all in accordance with the rules of liability stipulated in the Civil Code.

Article (7):
It is not permissible to cultivate state-owned lands located within public property related to water resources and irrigation or to use them for any purpose except with a license from the competent general administration, and in accordance with the conditions specified by the Executive Regulations of this Law.

Article (8):
It is not permissible to dispose of trees and palm trees that have been or will be planted on public property related to water resources and irrigation by cutting or uprooting them except with a license from the competent general administration. This administration may establish a system for planting trees and palm trees on this property and determine the reasons and methods for their removal in accordance with the regulations set by the Executive Regulations.

(Chapter Three)
Private Works within Public Property Related to Water Resources and Irrigation

Article (9):
It is not permissible to carry out any private work within the boundaries of public property related to water resources and irrigation, nor to make any modification or restoration therein, except with a license from the Ministry for a period not exceeding ten years, renewable upon payment of a fee not exceeding five hundred thousand pounds. Half of the fee is due upon renewal of the license. The Executive Regulations shall specify the conditions, regulations, licensing procedures, and categories of this fee.

In the event that any structures or works are established on public property related to water resources and irrigation without a license, they shall be removed by administrative means.

Article (10):
The Ministry may stipulate in the license for any of the works referred to in Article (9) of this Law that it shall be considered public property related to water resources and irrigation upon the expiration of the license period and without compensation.

In the case of the removal of the work or a change in the purpose of the license before the end of its term without agreement, the licensee shall be compensated for the work's expenses in proportion to the remaining period, unless the Ministry has made an arrangement that obviates the need for the licensed work.

If the license is not renewed and the Ministry has not decided to annex the works that were its subject to the public property related to water resources and irrigation, the licensee must remove them and restore the public property to its original state by the date specified by the Ministry; otherwise, the Ministry shall do so at the licensee's expense.

Article (11):
If the purpose of the licensed work is to irrigate or drain land, the Ministry may require that the owners, possessors, or tenants of other lands be permitted to benefit from that work after they have paid a portion of its construction costs, to be determined by the competent Director-General based on the proportion of their land holdings. The license must specify the area of land benefiting from the licensed work, and the benefit to the lands shall continue even if their ownership, possession, or tenancy changes.

Article (12):
The licensee is obligated to the following:

Maintain the work subject to the license and keep it in good condition according to the terms of the license.

Carry out any restoration or modification that the Ministry deems necessary for the public interest, at the time it specifies and according to the specifications it approves; otherwise, the Ministry may perform it at the licensee's expense.

Allow the Ministry to conduct inspections at any time.

Not to make any modifications to the work without prior approval from the Ministry.
If there are multiple licensees, they shall be jointly liable for the obligations in the contractual liability.

Article (13):
The Minister, or their delegate, may issue a decision to revoke the license if a violation of its conditions occurs and the licensee fails to prevent or remove it by the date set by the Ministry in a registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt or by notification through the administration officials. The decision shall include the removal of the work at the expense of the violating licensee.

Article (14):
Private bridges and water structures established over watercourses with a prior license from the Ministry shall, upon their construction and without compensation, become part of the public property related to water resources and irrigation, provided that all of this does not impede river navigation.

Part Two
Rights of Usufruct for Private Sources and Methods of Irrigation and Drainage

Article (15):
The owners, possessors, and tenants of lands that benefit from a single private water channel (masqah) or any private irrigation and drainage networks and facilities owned by them may take water from it or drain into it in proportion to the ownership or possession of each of them of these lands. The competent engineer shall prepare rotation schedules for the lands subject to this system, which the owners, possessors, and tenants of these lands must adhere to. The administration officials shall be responsible for implementing these schedules under his supervision, and the competent Director-General shall decide on any grievance submitted in this regard with a reasoned decision.

Article (16):
The owners, possessors, or tenants of lands benefiting from private water channels (masqis), private drains, private groundwater wells, or private irrigation and drainage facilities must clear them, remove plants and weeds that impede the flow of water, maintain them, and preserve their banks in good condition.

Article (17):
The competent Director-General, based on a report from the competent engineer or upon examining a complaint from concerned parties regarding a violation of Article (16) of this Law, may notify the competent authorities, administration officials, and water users' associations to assign the owners, possessors, or tenants to clear the water channel, drain, or well, or to remove what obstructs the flow of water, and to maintain or repair its banks or reconstruct them within a period not exceeding thirty days. Otherwise, the competent agencies of the Ministry of Agriculture and Land Reclamation shall carry out the work and collect the actual costs and administrative expenses by administrative means from the owners, possessors, or tenants, as the case may be, each in proportion to the area they possess.

Article (18):
If the lands located on both sides of a private water channel or private drain are in the possession of multiple persons, the axis of the channel or drain shall be considered a dividing line between what they own concerning clearing and maintenance works, unless there is evidence to the contrary.

Article (19):
Lands through which a private water channel or private drain passes are considered encumbered by an easement right for the benefit of the other lands that benefit from that channel or drain, unless there is evidence to the contrary.

Article (20):
If the owner, possessor, or tenant of land submits a complaint to the competent general administration due to being unlawfully prevented or hindered from using a private water channel, private drain, any developed irrigation method or system, or from entering any of the lands necessary to clear and maintain that channel, developed irrigation method, or drain, or to repair any of them, and this was their sole means of irrigation or drainage, the competent Director-General may, if it is determined from inspection or by any other means that the complainant's land was benefiting from the claimed right in the year preceding the submission of the complaint, issue a decision allowing them to use the claimed right along with other beneficiaries using their rights. The decision shall include rules organizing the use of these rights. The said decision shall be issued within a period not exceeding thirty days from the date the complaint is received by the Director-General.

Article (21):
If a landowner is unable to irrigate his land or drain its water sufficiently except by establishing or using a private water channel or a private drain on another's land and is unable to reach an agreement with its owner, he must submit his complaint to the competent Director-General for investigation. The competent general administration shall request all maps and documents required for examining the request within a period not exceeding two weeks from the date the complaint is received by the competent Director-General. The competent engineer shall conduct the investigation at the site of the water channel or drain after notifying, by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt, every concerned party, the president of the competent agricultural cooperative society, and the head of the water users' association of the location and time, which they shall set before moving to the said location by at least fourteen days. The result of this investigation shall be presented to the competent Director-General to issue a reasoned decision to either grant or deny the request, within two months from the date of completion of those maps and documents. The decision shall be announced to the concerned parties by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt. The preceding provisions shall apply in the case of a request to install a lifting machine at the water intake or its outfall on another's land, as well as when creating the necessary channel for it to irrigate or drain land separated from the intake or outfall.

Article (22):
If, due to public utility works, the irrigation or drainage route of a land is changed or cut off, the competent Director-General must issue a decision to establish another route for irrigation or drainage in accordance with the procedures stipulated in Article (21) of this Law. The decision shall be implemented before the irrigation and drainage route is cut off and at the expense of the party that caused the change.

Article (23):
Decisions issued in application of the provisions of Articles (20, 21, 22) of this Law shall be enforced by administrative means after paying compensation to all persons who have suffered damage therefrom. If the decision permits the use of an existing private water channel or drain, the compensation must include a portion of the construction costs at the time the use is decided, calculated based on the area of the land that benefits from either. The maintenance expenses of the channel or drain shall be in proportion to the area of the lands that benefit from either. If the concerned party refuses to accept the estimated compensation or if it is impossible to pay it to him, it shall be deposited in the treasury of the competent general administration for the account of the concerned parties, who shall be notified of this by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt. The deposit shall be considered as payment of the compensation.

Article (24):
The competent general administration may, upon the issuance of a decision in favor of more than one person in application of the provisions of Articles (20, 21, 22) of this Law, authorize one or more of them to implement the decision on behalf of the others. The person who implemented the decision shall have the right of recourse against the others for their respective shares of the costs, in proportion to the area of their land.

Article (25):
If it becomes apparent to the competent Director-General that a private water channel, private drain, private groundwater well, or any other irrigation source has become useless due to the existence of another route for irrigation or drainage, he may decide to block or cancel it. In the event of proven harm from a private water channel, private drain, or private groundwater well, he may take the necessary measures to prevent the harm. The owners of the channel or well are obliged to implement the decision by the date he sets; otherwise, the competent general administration may carry out the work at their expense.

Part Three
Water Distribution

(Chapter One)
Management, Regulation, and Distribution of Water

Article (26):
The Ministry shall be responsible for the management and regulation of water distribution from all its sources to the intakes and private openings for all uses, and it may modify their system of use in a manner consistent with the nature of their purpose. This must be announced through the competent general administrations, each within its area of jurisdiction.
The Ministry shall determine the dates of the period of lowest water needs and publish them in the Official Egyptian Gazette, and each competent general administration shall announce, within its jurisdiction, the rotation schedules and the period of lowest needs by administrative means, in accordance with what is specified by the Executive Regulations.

Article (27):
The competent Director-General may order at any time, even during operational periods, to prevent the taking of water from a public canal or more, or from a groundwater well for irrigation, except for privately owned wells not burdened by easements for others, in order to ensure fair water distribution, or to prevent giving agricultural lands more water than their needs, or to prevent its waste, or for any emergency circumstance required by the public interest.
The competent general administration may take the necessary measures to prevent any violation of the decisions issued in implementation of the provisions of the preceding paragraph. In particular, it may, by administrative means, prevent the flow of water in any of the water channels or their branches, or lift it by appropriate means.

Article (28):
By a decision of the Minister, in agreement with the Minister concerned with agriculture and land reclamation, the areas and regions allocated for rice cultivation annually, as well as the types of crops with high water needs, and their cultivation areas and regions annually shall be determined. This decision shall be published in the Official Egyptian Gazette. The cultivation of rice and crops with high water needs is prohibited in areas and regions other than those specified in the aforementioned decision.
The agencies of the Ministry of Agriculture shall, under the full follow-up, supervision, and oversight of the competent governor, take the necessary measures to remove rice nurseries and cultivations and crops with high water needs grown in violation outside the specified areas and regions annually, in cooperation with the agencies of the Ministry of Water Resources and Irrigation and the local administration agencies and administration officials, while observing the urgent deadlines for this removal, and notifying the Ministry with a list containing the names of the violators and the area of each violation, to take the necessary legal measures according to this Law. This shall not preclude the imposition of the penalty stipulated in the penalties section of this Law.

(Chapter Two)
Water Intakes and Drain Outfalls

Article (29):
No water intakes may be established, for whatever purpose, on the Nile or watercourses except with a license from the Ministry and in accordance with the conditions it specifies. The execution of all these works shall be under the supervision of the competent general administration and at the expense of the licensee.

Article (30):
If it becomes apparent to the competent general administration that the discharge of private water intakes intended for irrigating the designated command areas is more or less than the need of the land allocated for them, the administration may modify them to achieve the intended purpose at the expense of the state. The modification shall be approved by the competent Director-General to be implemented at suitable times for agriculture. If the owner, possessor, or tenant requests other modifications from the competent general administration, the administration may, after studying and approving them, carry them out at his own expense.

Article (31):
If it becomes apparent to the competent general administration that a groundwater well, an intake, or a private water outfall located on the Nile or a watercourse is causing harm to others due to a defect in its construction, neglect of its maintenance, or for other reasons, it shall notify the owners, possessors, tenants, and related water users' associations of the necessary works to avert the harm within the period it specifies. If they refrain from carrying out the required works within that period, the competent general administration may perform them at their own expense.
If it becomes apparent to the competent general administration that an intake or private water outfall located on the bank of the Nile or the banks of a watercourse poses a danger to the bank that requires its removal or blocking, it may assign the owner, possessor, or tenant to remove or block it at an appropriate time, according to the circumstances, which shall be announced to them. Otherwise, the competent general administration shall carry it out at the expense of the owner or the concerned party after arranging another means of irrigation or drainage for his land at the expense of the state, before cutting off the irrigation or drainage route, and after notifying the owners, possessors, tenants, and water users' associations.

Article (32):
The competent general administration may, if it finds that there is more than one irrigation route for an area of land, order the cancellation of what it deems surplus to the needs of the said area or its share of water. The cancellation shall be at the expense of the state after notifying the concerned parties.

Article (33):
If the Ministry, at its own expense, takes the necessary measures to deliver water from the Nile, a public canal, or a groundwater well to land that is irrigated from a private water intake located on the banks of the Nile or the banks of a public canal, the competent general administration must order the cancellation or removal of the private intake at the expense of the state, after notifying the concerned parties or the related water users' associations.

Article (34):
Without prejudice to the provisions of Law No. 48 of 1982 concerning the protection of the Nile River and watercourses from pollution, the provisions of this chapter shall apply to openings created in the banks of the Nile or the banks of watercourses for the discharge of agricultural drainage water into the Nile or into a watercourse.
It is not permissible, without a license from the Ministry, to create openings in the banks of the Nile or the banks of watercourses for the discharge of agricultural drainage water into the Nile or into a watercourse, in accordance with the conditions and regulations specified by the Executive Regulations.

(Chapter Three)
Water Lifting Machines

Article (35):
It is not permissible, without a license from the Ministry, to install or operate any fixed or mobile lifting machine, operated by mechanical or other means, to lift or drain water on the course of the Nile River, watercourses, public irrigation and drainage networks, or reservoirs, for purposes of irrigation, drainage, drinking, industry, or otherwise, as well as lifting water from Lake Nasser. The license shall be issued for a period of five years, renewable, in return for payment of a fee not exceeding two hundred and fifty pounds per year. The Executive Regulations shall specify the categories of this fee, and the procedures, data, and conditions necessary for it. Pumps of less than one hundred horsepower that irrigate an area not exceeding ten feddans shall be exempt from this fee.

Article (36):
If the lifting machine or its accessories are to be installed on land not owned by the license applicant, he must obtain written permission from the landowner. If its installation is on private water channels, private drains, wells, or reservoirs of joint use, the license shall be issued by the competent general administration on the condition that the licensee does not prejudice the rights of the other beneficiaries. The competent general administration shall have the right, during the license period, to stop the lifting machine for a specific period for the benefit of the other beneficiaries, without the licensee having the right to claim compensation.

Article (37):
A new license must be obtained if the replacement of a lifting machine results in a change in the water discharge or the location. In the case of transfer of ownership or replacement of the lifting machine without a change in the water discharge, it is sufficient to endorse this on the license. The old owner remains responsible, along with the new owner, for implementing the provisions of this Law until the endorsement is made on the license.

Article (38):
The Ministry may decide to move any licensed lifting machine, change the location of a licensed groundwater well, or move the works established for any of them to another location to prevent danger to bridges or other irrigation and drainage facilities, or for the construction of new works or the modification of existing works of public benefit, all at the expense of the state.

Article (39):
If the licensing of a lifting machine requires the performance of additional works necessary for taking or draining water, they shall be carried out at the expense of the license applicant.

Article (40):
The licensee for an irrigation or drainage machine is obliged to enable the users of all lands within the area specified in the license to irrigate or drain them from the machine subject to the license.

Article (41):
The license to install a lifting machine does not entail any right for the passage of water on another's land, and the licensee alone shall be responsible for any act or work that causes harm to others.
If the Nile shifts from its course and leaves behind an island or alluvium opposite land on which a licensed lifting machine is installed, the licensee has the right to dig a water channel on the new land to deliver water to that machine at his own expense and after obtaining the approval of the authority with jurisdiction, without paying any compensation.

Article (24):
A license to install a lifting machine in accordance with the provisions of this Law does not exempt from the obligation to obtain any other license required by other laws.

Article (43):
By a decision of the Prime Minister, after the approval of the Council of Ministers, the fee for lifting water for irrigating lands and draining water from them by means of the state's pumps and machines shall be determined, unless the benefit of irrigation and drainage without charge was taken into account in the assessment of the land tax. The proceeds of these sums shall accrue to the "Fund for Restoring Things to Their Original State" stipulated in Article (114) of this Law.

Article (44):
A person licensed to use water for non-agricultural purposes or to discharge it shall be obligated to pay a fee for the utilization of watercourses for each cubic meter of such water, in accordance with the conditions and regulations specified by the Executive Regulations.

Likewise, a person licensed to use water for non-agricultural purposes or to discharge it, where such water is lifted by government pumps, shall be obligated to pay a fee for the lifting of water, in accordance with the conditions and regulations specified by the Executive Regulations.

By a decision of the Prime Minister, after the approval of the Council of Ministers, the value of the fee for the utilization of watercourses or the value of the fee for lifting water for the two preceding cases shall be determined. The proceeds of these amounts shall accrue to the "Fund for Restoring Things to Their Original State" stipulated in Article (114) of this Law.

Article (45):
Users of groundwater wells and lifting machines shall not refrain from irrigating or draining the benefiting lands mentioned in the license, nor may they cease the utilization of such wells or machines for the aforementioned purpose, except for serious reasons approved by the competent Director-General.

Article (46):
In the event of a violation of the provisions of Articles (40, 45), the competent Director-General may temporarily entrust the management of the well or the lifting machine to a person appointed for this purpose, at the expense of the licensee.

Article (47):
The competent Director-General, in case of necessity, may stop any lifting machine operated in violation of the provisions of this Law or prevent water from reaching it, without waiting for the outcome of the legal proceedings regarding the violation.

The Minister, or their delegate, may issue a reasoned decision to revoke the license if any violation of its conditions occurs.

Part Four
Water Users' Associations and the Development and Improvement of Irrigation and Drainage Systems

(Chapter One)
Water Users' Associations

Article (48):
A decision shall be issued by the Ministry approving the establishment of water users' associations on lands designated for irrigation within sixty days of submitting the establishment request. These associations are considered private legal entities that do not aim to make a profit.

The aim of these associations is to activate the participation of water users with the Ministry in the management, operation, and maintenance of irrigation and drainage systems at different levels to achieve optimal use, and to protect and preserve water resources.

Each governorate shall have a secretary-general, and each district (markaz) shall have a secretary. They shall have a general assembly comprising all water users or beneficiaries of irrigation and drainage network systems at the national level, and its meetings shall be held at the Ministry's premises in the governorates.

These associations shall have a federation at the national level, formed by election from the general assembly, consisting of a chairman of the board, and a suitable number of members, not less than six and not more than ten, for a term of four years. The election of the federation shall take place for the first time from the general assembly of the established water users' associations before the approval of this Law. The general assembly shall establish a statute and programs for the work of these associations.

The Ministry shall be the competent authority for supervising these associations, organizing the method of participation of the beneficiaries, and approving their statutes and special programs.

Article (49):
The funds of water users' associations are considered public funds, and their resources consist of what the state allocates to them in the Ministry's budget, grants, donations, soft loans, and the subscriptions paid by the beneficiaries in return for services. These resources are directed to achieving their objectives and financing their activities. The associations are prohibited from obtaining any external grants, donations, or contributions except after the approval of the competent authorities.

The Executive Regulations shall regulate the procedures for establishing water users' associations, the formation of their boards of directors, their competencies, their course of work, their relationship with third parties, methods of financing, and obtaining the necessary resources to achieve their objectives. The chairman of the board of directors shall represent the association before administrative and judicial bodies, and appeals against the decisions of the board of directors shall be made before the competent court.

Article (50):
Without prejudice to the provisions of the laws on public utility concessions, the Ministry may entrust to a private or joint sector entity or to water users' associations the management, operation, and maintenance of a part or parts of the irrigation and drainage networks and facilities. It shall have the right to coordinate with the head of the federation of water users' associations in accordance with the organization, regulations, and conditions specified by the Executive Regulations.

Article (51):
By a decision of the Prime Minister, upon the proposal of the Minister, councils shall be established at the national, regional, and local levels, in which ministries, water users' associations, and concerned agencies are represented for coordination and consultation regarding the management and use of water resources among the concerned bodies. The establishment decision shall specify the competencies of these councils, their organizational structure, and their method of operation.

(Chapter Two)
Development and Improvement of Irrigation and Drainage Systems

Article (52):
The Permanent Undersecretary of the Ministry shall issue a decision regarding the command areas for which it is decided to convert their private water channels from their current state to developed private water channels, based on technical, field, and social studies conducted by specialists at the Ministry in coordination with the competent agencies of the Ministry of Agriculture. The decision shall be binding on all parties involved in the development process, including the beneficiaries. The actual costs shall be collected in accordance with the provisions of Article (57) of this Law.

Article (53):
The Fund for the Development and Maintenance of Water Channels Projects shall replace the currently existing fund, established by Law No. 213 of 1994 amending some provisions of the Irrigation and Drainage Law issued by Law No. 12 of 1984. All its rights and assets shall devolve to it, and it shall bear all its obligations.

The Fund is responsible for providing the necessary financing to implement irrigation system development projects and for working to raise awareness, conduct studies, and scientific research in the field of water uses, and to assist water users' associations in achieving their purposes.

The resources of the Fund shall consist of the amounts allocated to it in the state's general budget, the proceeds of loans, donations, and installments paid by the beneficiaries of the development projects, and the return on the investment of the Fund's money. Its money is considered public money.

The Minister shall issue a decision on the rules organizing the work of the Fund, the formation of its board of directors, its statute, and its financial regulations.

Article (54):
It is prohibited to operate any pumps on the developed water channels or to make any modification to the components of the developed irrigation system except after the approval of the competent general administration.

The Executive Regulations of this Law shall regulate the method of implementation, management, and use by farmers of the developed irrigation system in the old agricultural lands that are irrigated by surface irrigation systems through canals and water channels.

The owners of old lands and the beneficiaries of the developed irrigation system approved by the Ministry are obliged to comply with it.

Article (55):
The Ministry shall set the necessary plans for the improvement and development of irrigation and drainage systems in agricultural lands. The Minister may take measures to expropriate the lands necessary for the construction of open and covered irrigation and drainage networks, in accordance with the provisions of Law No. 10 of 1990 concerning the expropriation of real estate for public benefit.

Article (56):
The Ministry shall undertake the construction, replacement, and renewal of the covered or open field drainage network, provided that all lands within the scope of a drainage unit are connected to a series of main and branch public drains. The costs of constructing, replacing, and renewing the covered drainage network or the open field drains and their accessories shall be distributed among all lands located in the drainage unit in proportion to the command area.

The competent general administration shall be responsible for the periodic maintenance of the covered drains only, and the possessors or farmers of the land shall bear the costs other than that.

Article (57):
The Ministry shall prepare a statement of the actual costs for the construction, replacement, and renewal of covered drain networks, open field drains, or developed water channels, which are carried out based on the Ministry's plan or the request of the beneficiaries, including the compensations it has borne. To these amounts shall be added (10%) for administrative expenses. What pertains to one feddan of the lands within the drainage or irrigation unit shall be determined. The landowner shall bear the cost of constructing or replacing the covered field drain network, open field drains, and developed water channels.

The owner shall pay the amounts referred to in the preceding paragraph either in a single payment or in annual installments, such that all costs are paid within a period not exceeding twenty years, with collection beginning from the first year following implementation. The Ministry shall send to the competent authorities a statement of the basins included in the drainage or irrigation unit and the value of the amounts to be collected per feddan.

A decision shall be issued by the Minister of Finance to collect these amounts on the dates set for the collection of the land tax, and they shall have the lien established for this tax.

A statement of each owner's share of the expenses shall be displayed at the headquarters of the agricultural cooperative society and the notice board of the district (markaz) or police station in whose jurisdiction the land is located, for a period of at least one month. This display shall be preceded by an announcement of its date and place in the Official Egyptian Gazette.

Concerned parties shall have the right, within the thirty days following the end of the display period, to file a grievance against the value of the expenses; otherwise, its assessment shall be final. The grievance shall be submitted to the director of the competent Survey Directorate, and a committee chaired by the competent Director-General or his deputy and comprising a representative of the Ministry of Agriculture, the agricultural cooperative society, a technical employee from the Survey Directorate, and an engineer from the competent general administration shall decide on it. Its decision shall be subject to appeal before the competent administrative court, and the appeal shall not result in a stay of execution of the decision.

Article (58):
The Ministry shall, within one year from the date of construction, replacement, and renewal of the covered or open drainage network, the public drainage network, or the developed irrigation systems, notify the Real Estate Tax Authority of the lands on which the network was established to reassess the tax thereon.

Article (59):
It is prohibited to interfere with the structures, equipment, lines, and networks of covered drains, inspection chambers, washing columns, outfalls, as well as developed irrigation networks and covered irrigation pipe lines and networks, whether by damaging their parts, misappropriating them, filling them, disposing of waste in them, or discharging irrigation or sanitary wastewater into them, or connecting any networks for sanitary or industrial drainage to them (covered drain networks), or establishing any structures or fish farms on them. Without prejudice to the provisions stipulated in the penalties section, the competent engineer must document any violations of the provisions of this article and may assign the violator to restore the thing to its original state within a maximum period of four days; otherwise, the competent general administration shall carry out the implementation at his expense.

Part Five
Irrigation and Drainage of New Lands

Article (60):
For the purposes of this Law, new lands are considered to be any lands not previously licensed for irrigation according to its provisions, whether these lands are inside the Valley and Delta or in any other lands within the Arab Republic of Egypt and for which water resources are available in the state's plan.

The Ministry shall determine the irrigation source for the new land and its drainage outfall. The license shall be issued by the competent general administration after payment of a fee not exceeding two hundred thousand pounds. The Executive Regulations shall specify the categories of this fee. The licensee is obliged to follow the licensed method of irrigation and drainage. In case of violation, the competent general administration may, after warning the violator and giving him the necessary time to remove the violation, implement the licensed method of irrigation and drainage at the violator's expense, and its value shall be collected by administrative means.

The licensee is obliged to implement and follow the conditions of the license and to obtain water according to the time schedules and quantities determined by the competent general administration.

The Executive Regulations shall specify the conditions and regulations for licensing the irrigation and drainage of new lands and the actual costs for delivering and distributing water to them and draining it.

Article (61):
It is not permissible to allocate any lands for new horizontal agricultural expansion before obtaining the approval of the Ministry to ensure the availability of irrigation water as well as a drainage outfall determined by the Ministry.

Article (62):
Authorities and individuals planning to establish development projects or new urban communities or to expand existing ones must submit their plans and studies in this regard to the Ministry in advance for review and approval in light of the national plan for water resources.

These authorities or individuals are obliged to attach to their request to the Ministry the studies and time programs for implementing the water treatment system and ensuring its reuse. The Ministry may request any additional information or a restudy to fulfill all conditions.

These authorities or individuals are obliged to implement the structures and water supply networks according to the planning, openings, and diameters approved by the Ministry, with the obligation to install measuring devices and monitor discharges at the intakes or on the water source, and to continuously maintain and repair any malfunctions promptly. They must also assist and enable the competent agencies of the Ministry to conduct inspections, tests, and examinations of these structures, networks, and measuring devices, and to review any related data.

If the matter requires the use of any of the Ministry's watercourses, facilities, and irrigation and drainage networks for the transport and distribution of water for these projects or urban communities or for the discharge of final wastewater or other new constructions, these authorities shall bear the costs of rehabilitation or construction, as well as the costs of operation and maintenance for the watercourses, facilities, and irrigation and drainage networks.

These authorities shall bear any costs for the required studies or the review of the studies submitted to the Ministry. The Executive Regulations shall regulate the conditions and procedures.

Article (63):
For matters not specifically addressed in this chapter, the provisions concerning the irrigation and drainage of new lands or the establishment of new development projects and urban communities, and all other provisions stipulated in this Law or in other laws, shall apply in a manner that does not conflict with its provisions.

Part Six
Protection of the Nile River Course and its Banks

Article (64):
By a decision of the Prime Minister, upon the proposal of the Minister, two committees shall be formed, comprising representatives of the concerned authorities, who shall be of at least senior rank. One shall be a permanent coordinating committee for licensing activities in Lake Nasser, its shores, and its appurtenances, and the other shall be a higher committee to review applications submitted to obtain licenses for works or occupancies on the course of the Nile River, its two branches, and their banks behind the High Dam.

The decision forming them shall specify their competencies and rules of procedure. Their recommendations shall be endorsed by the Minister, and these recommendations, upon endorsement, shall be binding on all other administrative authorities.

Article (65):
Without prejudice to the provisions of this Law and all other laws, and the competencies of the authorities and bodies established by other laws, the commission of any of the following acts is prohibited:

Establishing any buildings or structures or carrying out any works in the prohibited zone and on the banks of the Nile River and its two branches. This excludes public utility works, after obtaining the approval of the Ministry of Defense and Military Production and the Higher Committee for Licensing, and the endorsement of the Minister, while adhering to the Ministry's requirements, within a maximum period of sixty days from the date of completion of the documents.

Establishing any jetties attached to tourist vessel moorings (fixed or mobile) in the course of the Nile River and its two branches. It is permissible, after submission to the Higher Committee for Licensing and the approval of the Ministry, to construct a walkway to connect the shore to the floating vessel according to the models prepared by the Ministry. It is also prohibited to construct any new floating structures except with prior approval from the Ministry. In the case of licensing, the construction and maintenance of the floating structures shall be carried out in specialized workshops accredited by the General Authority for River Transport. The Ministry of Defense and Military Production is exempt from this approval.

The mooring of any houseboats or dahabiyas of various types on the banks of the Nile and its two branches, or any public watercourse, without a license from the Ministry.

Establishing any structures within the sanctuary of drinking water intakes from the Nile River and its two branches, which is defined by a distance of 500 meters upstream and 200 meters downstream, without a license from the Ministry.

Carrying out any works on the course of the Nile River and its two branches, or any protections, revetments, or facings for slopes and berms, except based on specialized technical studies and under the supervision of the Ministry and in accordance with its requirements.

Establishing any tourist facilities, clubs, or other establishments in the area of the Nile River course and its two branches except with a license from the Ministry and in accordance with its requirements.

The replacement, renewal, modification, or addition to buildings and structures, other than public utility works that previously obtained a license in the prohibited zone, with their removal after the expiration of the license, as well as their removal in case of their lack of technical or structural soundness, along with the removal of buildings and structures that were not licensed. The Ministry's responsibility is negated in the event of flooding of these buildings and structures.

Article (66):
The owner of a vessel or its cargo may not claim compensation from the government for any delay caused by the closing of a lock, the opening of a navigational bridge on the course of the Nile River, its two branches, or watercourses, or due to a change in water levels or the implementation of balancing measures necessary for water distribution in any of the said watercourses, in cases of necessity, emergency circumstances, and force majeure.

Article (67):
If a vessel has collided, sunk, or stopped sailing due to a lack of water, whether in the Nile, a canal, or a drain, its owner or captain must immediately report it to the nearest police station, which shall draft a report documenting the vessel's and its cargo's condition. This report shall be sent to the competent general administration, which shall notify the vessel's owner, cargo's owner, or captain to salvage the vessel or remove its wreckage within a period not exceeding three days; otherwise, the administration shall do so. It may, however, if the competent general administration deems that the public interest requires the immediate salvage of the vessel or removal of its wreckage, do so without being bound by the preceding procedures.

The state may not be claimed for compensation for damages that may affect the vessel or its cargo during its removal by the competent general administration. In all cases, the owner of the vessel and the owner of the cargo shall be jointly responsible for paying the expenses of removal or salvage to the competent general administration. The administration shall have the right to detain the vessel and its cargo as a guarantee for the collection of these expenses within the period it specifies; otherwise, it may sell the vessel, its cargo, or both by public auction.

Part Seven
Groundwater

Article (68):
A higher committee shall be formed to review applications submitted for obtaining licenses or regularizing the drilling of wells, comprising representatives of the relevant authorities, who shall be of at least senior rank. A decision shall be issued by the Prime Minister to form the committee, its rules of procedure, and to define its competencies. Its recommendations shall be submitted to the Minister for endorsement. The Minister's endorsement of the committee's work shall be binding on other authorities.

The Ministry shall conduct the necessary technical studies to discover groundwater sources at the national level, inventory these sources, and establish policies for the development and utilization of groundwater, and set regulations for its use, protection, and monitoring of its quantity and quality.

Article (69):
A license for drilling a groundwater well, specifying its characteristics, and replacing it with another shall be issued by a decision of the competent general administration based on a recommendation from the Higher Committee for Licensing, provided that the license period is five years, renewable. The license shall be issued in return for a fee not exceeding one thousand pounds for each year for new license applications or upon renewal of existing licenses.

The Executive Regulations shall regulate the procedures, conditions for licensing, and categories of this fee. Wells licensed in the old lands in the Valley and Delta and used for supplementary irrigation shall be exempt from these fees.

Article (70):
It is prohibited to drill any wells for groundwater within the territory of the Republic except with a license from the Ministry and in accordance with the conditions it specifies.

It is prohibited for contractors, companies, and individuals engaged in drilling groundwater wells to contract for the drilling of any wells unless they have obtained a license for it from the Ministry and in a manner that does not conflict with Law No. 143 of 1981 concerning desert lands.

It is prohibited to dispose of petroleum products or waste resulting from drilling petroleum wells into any geological layer, except in the layer from which it was produced. The Executive Regulations shall specify the necessary conditions, specifications, and procedures.

Article (71):
The competent engineer, or the person assigned to the work, has the right to enter all well sites for inspection to ensure the well's use complies with the conditions included in the license and to take the necessary measures to remove any violation and collect the costs of removal by administrative means.

Article (72):
The Ministry may collect a fee for each cubic meter of water extracted from wells if the purpose of the license for the wells is for non-agricultural uses. The Executive Regulations shall regulate the procedures and conditions for licensing the drilling of these wells and shall determine the fee due or the exemption from it by a decision of the Prime Minister, after the approval of the Council of Ministers. The proceeds of these amounts shall accrue to the State's Public Treasury.

Article (73):
The license for a well is revoked if it is not drilled within one year from the date of the license or if it is not used within two years of its construction. The license may also be revoked if the well is used for purposes other than those for which it was licensed. The Ministry may backfill the well at the licensee's expense.

In the event of damage to or contamination of the well, the Ministry may, after examining the well owner's request and verifying their non-liability for such damage or contamination, approve the licensing of a replacement well with the technical specifications it approves. The owner of the damaged or contaminated well is obliged to backfill it within the period specified by the competent general administration; otherwise, the administration shall do so at his expense.

Article (74):
The Ministry, after endorsing the recommendation of the Higher Committee, has the right to accept or reject any request to modify the licensed purpose of the well's utilization or to change the rates of discharge according to the circumstances and capabilities of the aquifer in the area specified by the Ministry.

Article (75):
If the ownership of the land where the well is located is transferred to a new owner, the competent general administration must be notified of the sale. It is sufficient to endorse this on the license; otherwise, the old owner shall be responsible, along with the new owner, for implementing the provisions of this Law.

Article (76):
Authorities engaged in prospecting for petroleum, minerals, or mineral or historical resources, or similar activities, are obliged to inform the Ministry of the presence of groundwater immediately upon its discovery and to provide the Ministry with all data, information, and maps available to them in this regard. This obligation applies to these authorities whether they are conducting the prospecting themselves or have assigned the prospecting work to another party.

All government agencies, public authorities, public and private sector companies, and others are also obliged to provide the Ministry with any available data on the quantities of groundwater consumed in the various uses that fall within their competence, as well as their expected future needs for groundwater. The same obligation applies to natural and legal persons using groundwater in their various activities. The Ministry may take appropriate measures to verify this, and these authorities are obliged to cooperate and facilitate the Ministry's task.

Article (77):
The Ministry has the right to dispose of and utilize the wells it implements, in accordance with the provisions of the Law on Regulating Contracts Concluded by Public Bodies, issued by Law No. 182 of 2018, and the Law on Regulating the Partnership with the Private Sector in Infrastructure Projects, Public Services, and Utilities, issued by Law No. 67 of 2010.

Article (78):
Investors, companies, and owners of development projects based on the utilization of groundwater are obliged to do the following:

Construct monitoring wells at their own expense for agricultural development projects when the area reaches one thousand feddans, in accordance with the specifications and requirements set by the Executive Regulations.

Construct a monitoring well for the injection zone at their own expense for non-agricultural development projects in coastal areas and those based on the desalination of saline and brackish water, where the resulting wastewater is disposed of in injection wells, in accordance with the specifications and requirements set by the Executive Regulations.

In case of failure to comply with these obligations, the Ministry shall construct these wells at their expense, and the value of the actual costs and administrative expenses incurred for restoring the thing to its original state shall be collected by administrative seizure.

Article (79):
Well owners are obliged to install a system to control the actual rates of use according to the regulations and criteria set by the Ministry, except for wells used for supplementary irrigation.

Article (80):
It is not permissible to drill injection wells used for the disposal of wastewater resulting from the desalination of saline and brackish water except with a license from the Ministry. The Executive Regulations shall specify the necessary conditions, regulations, and procedures.

Article (81):
The Ministry has the right to close any well based on public interest considerations or if technical reports from monitoring and inspection prove contamination of the well water or deterioration of its quality, in accordance with the conditions and rules set by the Executive Regulations.

Part Eight
Protection of Water Resources, Facilities, and Coasts

(Chapter One)
Protection against the Dangers of Rising Water Levels

Article (82):
A state of danger shall be declared by a decision of the Minister if water levels rise to an unusual height, or upon the occurrence of unusual floods, or the surge of groundwater under high pressure, or the collapse of water structures or bridges, which requires the implementation of urgent protective works to ward off water hazards and protect dams, reservoirs, bridges, and structures.

The Minister, or their delegate, upon the declaration of a state of danger, shall take immediate measures to assign contracting companies from the public sector, public business sector, or private sector, or individual contractors, to carry out monitoring, observation, restoration, and strengthening of the banks of the Nile, canals, drains, and flood spillways belonging to the Ministry, and the sea coasts, and to dam and repair any breaches therein, and any other works for the protection of lands and structures.

The Minister, or their delegate, may in this situation temporarily seize any necessary equipment, machinery, or materials, and any adjacent lands outside the river setback limit and outside the public easements of watercourses, and may take from them the necessary earth for the protection of the banks or for storing materials and equipment necessary for protection works.

Governors, security directors, and administration officials in the governorates shall take the necessary measures to facilitate and assist the competent general administrations until the end of the state of danger.

The Minister shall, by his decision, determine the appropriate wages and compensations or the value of restoring the thing to its original state, as the case may be, for all that is required for the protection works and to avert the state of danger.

Article (83):
In the event of a potential danger from flooding, the competent Director-General may request the assistance of the administration officials and the competent civil protection agencies of the Ministry of Interior to take immediate measures and actions to avert the danger, without the need for a decision from the Minister declaring a state of danger.

(Chapter Two)
Protection of Water and Removal of Obstructions to Irrigation and Drainage

Article (84):
It is prohibited to carry out any of the following acts:

Wasting or squandering water resources by exceeding the prescribed or licensed quantities, or by discharging it without necessity into a private or public drain or into a covered drainage network, or by using it on lands not designated for irrigation or not licensed for irrigation, or by using it for purposes not licensed, or by using an unlicensed irrigation method.

Obstructing the flow of water in the Nile River and its two branches, watercourses, a covered drainage network, a flood spillway, or performing any work that would disrupt the hydraulic balance.

Opening, closing, or causing any damage to any lock, regulator, or any of the irrigation and drainage facilities and equipment belonging to the Ministry, or other works designed to balance the flow of water, and those constructed in watercourses or crossing the banks of the Nile or the banks of a watercourse.

Placing stakes to tie nets in the Nile River and its two branches, the banks of watercourses, or in their beds, or on the banks of the basin of a regulator, lock, or bridge.

Establishing fish farms and cages for aquaculture in the course of the Nile and its two branches up to five hundred meters behind the Edfina barrage and the Damietta dam and lock, as well as in the main canals (rayahs), public canals, and Lake Nasser.

(a) Backfilling by dumping silt, earth, or the like in the course of the Nile River and its two branches, its inlets, or flood spillways.
(b) Backfilling by dumping silt, earth, or the like in watercourses, on the banks of any of them, or in a covered drainage network.

Using fresh water to feed fish farms without the approval of the Ministry.

Cutting the banks of the Nile or watercourses.

Excavating and taking earth, stones, or other materials and supplies from the banks of the Nile and its sides and berms, or from the banks of watercourses or flood spillways, or from irrigation and drainage structures and equipment, or any other work within the public property related to water resources.

Establishing basins for private water lifting machines and their associated pipes on the banks of the Nile, its two branches, and watercourses.

Any violation of the provisions of this article shall be removed by administrative means at the violator's expense.

Article (85):
Without prejudice to the provisions of Law No. 48 of 1982 concerning the protection of the Nile River and watercourses from pollution, it is not permissible, without a license from the Ministry, to carry out any of the following acts:

Discharging into a public canal, a covered drainage network, or a flood spillway.

Irrigating with agricultural drainage water if another source of irrigation is not available.

The passage of any heavy-load moving machinery on the bridges or structures of irrigation, drainage, and flood protection facilities belonging to the Ministry if it is likely to cause damage to the bridges or structures of irrigation, drainage, and flood protection.

It is not permissible, without a license from the Ministry and the approval of the Ministry of Health and Population, to irrigate agricultural crops with treated sanitary or industrial wastewater.

(Chapter Three)
Administration and Protection of Sea Coasts

Article (86):
A higher committee shall be formed to review applications submitted for obtaining new licenses for works or occupancies within the prohibited zone of the sea coasts, comprising representatives of the relevant authorities, who shall be of at least senior rank. A decision shall be issued by the Prime Minister to form the committee, its rules of procedure, and to define its competencies.
The committee shall submit its recommendations to the Minister for endorsement. The Minister's endorsement of the committee's work shall be binding on other administrative authorities and shall annul any committees formed in the governorates or elsewhere for this purpose. The Executive Regulations shall regulate the procedures and conditions to be followed.

Article (87):
It is prohibited to establish any structures or works on the state's sea coasts for a distance of two hundred meters from the shoreline into the land, except with the approval of the Ministry.

Article (88):
The Ministry, in coordination with the Ministry of Environment, shall determine the final prohibition line based on studies in this regard, which it is forbidden to cross by establishing any structures or works. The provisions of Article (87) of this Law shall continue to apply in the areas where the prohibition line has not been determined by the Ministry and the Ministry of Environment and all concerned authorities have not been notified to comply with it.

Article (89):
Without prejudice to the provisions of the Environment Law issued by Law No. 4 of 1994, if it becomes apparent to the competent engineer that there are works violating the preceding articles of this Law on the state's sea coasts, he has the right to take the necessary legal measures to stop the work and for its immediate administrative removal and the restoration of the thing to its original state at the violator's expense, and to collect the costs of removal by administrative means.

Article (90):
It is not permissible to establish private structures or structures for public benefit within the prohibited zone referred to in Articles (87, 88) of this Law except in cases of necessity that require it and on the condition of obtaining prior approval from the Ministry and the Ministry of Environment after the endorsement of the Higher Committee by the Minister. The approval must include the determination of the necessary protection works for it, based on specialized studies in this regard from a consulting firm approved by the Ministry, without prejudice to the provisions of the Environment Law issued by Law No. 4 of 1994.

The protection works shall be executed under the supervision of the Ministry, and the concerned party shall bear the costs of the studies, supervision, and execution of these works. The Executive Regulations shall regulate the procedures and conditions to be followed in this regard.

Article (91):
It is prohibited to carry out any work that affects the nature of the coast or alters its course, entering into the sea or receding from it, except after the approval of the Ministry and based on a recommendation from the competent Higher Committee. The Executive Regulations shall regulate the procedures and conditions to be followed in this regard.

Article (92):
It is not permissible to carry out any of the following acts except with a license from the Ministry based on a recommendation from the Higher Committee for Coasts:

Transporting any sand from the sand dunes or others present in the prohibited zone of the sea coasts. The licensee is obliged to replace the exploited quantities with other similar quantities in volume.

Changing the topography of the land by excavation or backfilling within the prohibited zone of the sea coasts.

The Executive Regulations shall specify the necessary conditions and regulations in this regard.

(Chapter Four)
Protection from the Dangers of Rain and Floods

Article (93):
It is prohibited to carry out any work that would affect flood spillways and protection structures, except in cases of necessity assessed by the Ministry, and after obtaining a license from it in accordance with the regulations and conditions specified by the Executive Regulations.

Article (94):
Companies, individuals, authorities, and owners of tourist establishments or any other establishments or activities are obliged to execute the special protection works necessary to protect them from the dangers of rain and floods at their own expense, after obtaining the necessary licenses from the Ministry in accordance with the regulations and conditions specified by the Executive Regulations.

Article (95):
It is not permissible for any individual or entity, whether governmental or non-governmental, or any legal or natural person, to establish any structures or works to impound the running water of rain and floods in natural valleys, which would divert or alter their natural course, except with a license from the Ministry in accordance with the regulations and conditions specified by the Executive Regulations.

Article (96):
Each governorate is obliged, in coordination with other authorities of jurisdiction, to establish the necessary plans and policies to protect lives, infrastructure, and public and private establishments, existing or planned to be established within the governorate, from the dangers of rain and floods, while submitting the technical studies and protection plans and mechanisms for their utilization for approval by the Ministry.

Article (97):
The competent Director-General shall issue a decision to remove any structure or stop any activity or any procedure carried out without a license that would affect flood spillways or protection structures and the discharge of rain and floods, all in accordance with the requirements of the public interest.

Part Nine
Penalties

Article (98):
Without prejudice to the Ministry's right to restore the thing to its original state at the violator's expense or to any more severe penalty stipulated in any other law, a violation of any provision of this Law shall be punished with the penalties indicated in the following articles.

Article (99):
Anyone who violates the provision of Article (8) of this Law shall be punished with a fine of not less than one thousand pounds and not more than five thousand pounds for each tree or palm tree.

Article (100):
Anyone who violates any provision of the articles: clause (3) of the second paragraph of Article (4), the second paragraph of Article (5), clause (10) of Article (84), and Articles (15, 16, 27, 37) shall be punished with a fine of not less than two thousand pounds and not more than ten thousand pounds. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (101):
Anyone who violates any provision of Articles (12, 40, 45) and the third paragraph of Article (60) shall be punished with a fine of not less than five thousand pounds and not more than twenty thousand pounds. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (102):
Anyone who violates any provision of Articles (31, 47, 54, 79, 85) shall be punished with a fine of not less than ten thousand pounds and not more than thirty thousand pounds. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (103):
Anyone who violates any provision of Article (35), the second paragraph of Article (60), Article (80), and clauses (1, 6/b, 7) of Article (84) shall be punished with a fine of not less than ten thousand pounds and not more than fifty thousand pounds. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (104):
Anyone who violates the provision of Article (28) shall be punished with imprisonment for a period not exceeding six months and a fine of not less than three thousand pounds and not more than ten thousand pounds per feddan or fraction of a feddan, or with one of these two penalties. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (105):
Anyone who violates any provision of Articles (9, 29), and clauses (2, 4, 5, 8, 9) of Article (84), and Article (95) shall be punished with imprisonment for a period not exceeding one year and a fine of not less than ten thousand pounds and not more than fifty thousand pounds, or with one of these two penalties. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (106):
Anyone who violates any provision of clauses (1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7) of Article (65), the third paragraph of Article (70), and clauses (3, 6/a) of Article (84), and Articles (87, 88, 91, 92, 93) shall be punished with imprisonment for a period of not less than six months and a fine of not less than one hundred thousand pounds and not more than five hundred thousand pounds, or with one of these two penalties. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (107):
Anyone who violates any provision of: the first, second, and third paragraphs of Article (62), the first and second paragraphs of Article (70), and Articles (76, 78, 90, 94) shall be punished with a fine of not less than twenty thousand pounds and not more than two hundred thousand pounds. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism, along with the seizure of the machines and equipment used. In case of a conviction, the competent court may order their confiscation.

Article (108):
Anyone who violates the provision of Article (7) shall be punished with a fine of not less than five thousand pounds and not more than twenty thousand pounds per feddan or fraction of a feddan. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (109):
Anyone who violates any provision of the second paragraph of Article (34) and Article (59) shall be punished with imprisonment for a period not exceeding six months and a fine of not less than ten thousand pounds and not more than one hundred thousand pounds, or with one of these two penalties. The penalty shall be doubled in case of recidivism.

Article (110):
In all the preceding cases, anyone who resumes any of the works that were previously stopped by administrative means shall be punished with the same penalty specified for each violation of the provisions of the preceding articles.
The contractor or any person who undertakes the execution for the benefit of the concerned party shall also be punished with the same penalty if the works were established or are being established without a license from the Ministry or in violation thereof, provided they were aware of the violation or the stop-work order. The Ministry may seize the machines, tools, supplies, and equipment used, and the court shall order their confiscation in case of a conviction.

Article (111):
Without prejudice to the penalties prescribed by this Law, the violator is obliged to pay compensation for the quantities of water wasted or used in excess of the authorized quantities, in accordance with the rules specified by the Executive Regulations. This compensation may be collected by administrative means.

Part Ten
General and Transitional Provisions

(Chapter One)
General Provisions

Article (112):
Any concerned party may file a grievance with the Permanent Undersecretary of the Ministry against the decisions issued by the competent Director-General, except for decisions issued in accordance with the provisions of Articles (15, 20) of this Law. The grievance must be submitted within fifteen days from the date the concerned party is notified of the decision. The grievance shall be decided upon within thirty days from the date of its submission. If it is not decided upon within this period, the grievance shall be considered rejected.

Article (113):
Without prejudice to the provisions of Law No. 10 of 1990 concerning the expropriation of real estate for public benefit, a committee shall be formed in the jurisdiction of each governorate, chaired by a judge from the Court of First Instance, chosen in accordance with the provisions of the Judicial Authority Law issued by Law No. 46 of 1972, and with the membership of the Director-General of the competent general administration, the director of the competent Survey Directorate, the director of the competent Agriculture Directorate, and a representative of the governorate nominated by the competent authority. It shall be competent to decide on grievances regarding the value of compensations stipulated in this Law. Its meeting shall not be valid unless attended by its chairman and at least two of its members. The committee shall issue its decision within one month of the documents being deposited with the committee's secretariat. The decision shall be issued by a majority of votes, and in case of a tie, the side of the chairman shall prevail. Appealing the decision shall result in a stay of its execution until the appeal is decided. The members of the committee shall be named by a decision of the Minister.

Article (114):
The "Fund for Restoring Things to Their Original State" shall replace the currently existing fund established by the Irrigation and Drainage Law issued by Law No. 12 of 1984. All its rights and assets shall devolve to it, and it shall bear all its obligations. The proceeds of fees, fines, and amounts awarded by judgment in accordance with the provisions of this Law shall accrue to the Fund. The capital of the Fund shall be ten million pounds. In the event that the collected revenues exceed the estimate, the expenditure shall be by license from the Minister of Finance, with the surplus proceeds being carried over. It may not be used except with the approval of the Minister of Finance or their delegate. Its funds are considered public funds.

The Fund is responsible for contributing to the financing of the expenses of maintaining or rehabilitating watercourses and irrigation and drainage facilities, as well as the costs of removing violations and restoring the thing to its original state in the event that the violator fails to do so in accordance with the law, as well as the expenses of scientific research and studies related to water resources and irrigation.
The Prime Minister shall, upon the proposal of the Minister, issue a decision on the rules organizing the Fund, the formation of its board of directors, its term, its statute, and its financial regulations.

Article (115):
Without prejudice to the provisions of the Law on Regulating the Use of Non-Cash Payment Methods issued by Law No. 18 of 2019, the Ministry shall undertake the collection of the fees prescribed in this Law and their deposit into the account of the "Fund for Restoring Things to Their Original State" stipulated in Article (114) of this Law. The Executive Regulations shall specify the regulations for their collection.

Article (116):
The competent engineers or other designated employees, each within their scope, shall be granted the capacity of judicial police officers with respect to the crimes stipulated in this Law and which fall within their areas of jurisdiction, by a decision of the Minister of Justice based on a proposal from the Minister.

Article (117):
Administration officials and the local administration, under the follow-up and supervision of the competent governor, are obliged to assist the competent general administrations of the Ministry in preserving the Nile River, watercourses, flood spillways, their banks, facilities, structures, and equipment related to irrigation and drainage from any encroachments, and to notify the competent general administration of any encroachments or violations that threaten the efficiency of these channels, structures, and equipment in their early stages, and to participate in the implementation of administrative decisions in application of the provisions of this Law.

Article (118):
All authorities with jurisdiction over flood spillways not belonging to the Ministry are obliged to continuously clear and maintain these spillways and their facilities, strengthen their banks, and establish plans for the transfer of any residential communities or other structures or the removal of any encroachments located within these spillways.
In the event that any entity wishes to implement any development projects within the scope of all flood spillways, it must obtain prior approval from the Ministry.

Article (119):
The competent engineer or the person assigned to the work, upon the occurrence of an encroachment on public property related to water resources and irrigation, shall assign the encroacher or the beneficiary of this encroachment, orally, telegraphically, or by any other means, to restore the thing to its original state immediately and to document these procedures in the violation report. If the thing is not restored to its original state, the administration officials shall be notified to stop the violation and guard it until the removal is executed. The competent Director-General shall issue a decision for the administrative removal of the encroachment and shall notify the violator of the value of the usufruct fee determined by the Ministry and the costs of restoring the thing to its original state. The violator is obliged to pay this amount within one month from the date of his notification; otherwise, the competent general administration shall collect it by administrative seizure.

Article (120):
The Minister shall issue a decision on the percentage of contribution to the costs of construction, operation, and maintenance for the beneficiaries of multi-purpose water and structural projects established on the Nile River, its two branches, watercourses, coastal protection projects, and protection from the dangers of rain, floods, and groundwater projects, in coordination with the benefiting authorities, after the endorsement of the recommendations of the higher committees, each in its own field.

Article (121):
The Ministry may license the use of agricultural drainage water and brackish groundwater to feed fish farms that have obtained the approval of the Ministry of Agriculture and Land Reclamation, with the requirement of obtaining prior approval from the Ministries of Health and Environment on the suitability of the water. The license shall be for a period of five years, renewable, in return for a fee not exceeding ten thousand pounds. Half of the fee is due upon renewal of the license. The Executive Regulations shall specify the conditions and regulations for granting the license and the categories of this fee. Licenses shall be revoked in the event that the unsuitability of the water used is proven due to a change in its quality or for any other reason and according to periodic analyses accredited by the Ministry of Health. A fee shall be collected for what the Ministry incurs in expenses for the works of rehabilitation, operation, and maintenance of public or private drainage networks or wells that serve these farms, in accordance with the criteria and regulations issued by a decision of the Minister.

Article (122):
The Minister may, based on public interest considerations and the extent of availability of water quantities and sources as well as a drainage outfall, approve the study of regularizing the status of existing illegal water intakes, in a manner that does not conflict with the national plan for water resources. Concerned parties may submit to the Ministry requests for the study of regularization within a period not exceeding six months from the date of publication of the Executive Regulations, provided that the request is accompanied by the approval of the authority of jurisdiction or the competent authority that permitted the activity practiced by the applicant, and that the request is accompanied by a declaration of agreement to pay any compensation for the quantities of water wasted and previously exploited, and any other costs for modifying or rehabilitating irrigation and drainage networks and facilities, as well as the costs of constructing any groundwater wells or works for the treatment and reuse of agricultural drainage water, and the costs of constructing any new structures if the matter so requires, and the costs of developing irrigation systems and methods in a command area equal to the violating command area or a command area equivalent to the water discharges taken in violation for other non-agricultural activities, all to contribute to providing water sources to cover all or part of the requirements of these illegal intakes.
After a preliminary study by the competent agencies of the Ministry, the concerned parties shall be notified of the intakes or some of them whose situations can be regularized, and the estimated costs required, whether for the necessary detailed studies or for the execution of the required works, as well as the value of compensations and the like, and a deadline for payment shall be set. The proceeds of these amounts shall accrue to the fund designated for them, as determined by the Minister, in accordance with what is specified by the Executive Regulations.

Article (123):
The regularization shall be in accordance with the provisions of this Law and with the conditions and procedures specified by the Ministry, and a decision thereon shall be issued by the Minister after the approval of the authority of jurisdiction. The regularization shall be in return for a fee not exceeding one hundred pounds per feddan or fraction thereof for which irrigation from this intake is approved to be regularized.
All illegal water intakes and outfalls whose situations have not been regularized, or for which the concerned parties have not submitted requests for the study of their regularization within the period specified in the first paragraph of this article, or for which they have not paid the required costs and fees by the specified deadlines, shall be removed administratively.

Article (124):
The Minister shall issue a decision determining the fee for supervising the works executed under a license from the Ministry's agencies in accordance with the provisions of this Law, and the rules, regulations, and procedures for its collection, as specified by the Executive Regulations.

Article (125):
All amounts due to the state by virtue of the provisions of this Law shall have a lien on the debtor's assets in accordance with the provisions of Article (1139) of the Civil Code, coming in order after judicial expenses and alimony debt, and shall be collected by all legal means.

(Chapter Two)
Transitional Provisions

Article (125):
Reconciliation may be made for violations committed before the issuance of this Law for structures and works that were established without a license and are located in the following places:

Within the restricted zone of the Nile River course, except for those established on lands related to water resources and irrigation.

Outside the two setback limits of the river and established on lands privately owned by the state or by other public or private legal persons or individuals.

The following is required for this reconciliation:
Submitting the application within one year from the date of issuance of this Law.
Paying any dues to the competent authorities of jurisdiction.
Approval of the competent Higher Committee on the reconciliation request. The Council of Ministers shall, by its decision, specify the other conditions, regulations, and rules necessary in this regard.

Article (126):
Based on public interest considerations, reconciliation or regularization of situations may be made for a fee of usufruct for violations existing before 1/9/2016 for structures and works that occurred within the final prohibition zone of the sea coasts and are established on lands privately owned by the state or by other public or private legal persons or individuals. The following is required for this:

Payment of any dues to the competent authorities of jurisdiction.

Submitting the request for reconciliation or regularization within one year from the date of issuance of this Law.

Approval of the competent Higher Committee.

The Council of Ministers shall determine the fee of usufruct and the rules, regulations, and conditions necessary in this regard.
