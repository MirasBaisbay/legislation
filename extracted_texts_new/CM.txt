LAW NO. 98-005 of 14 April 1998
ON THE WATER REGIME

The National Assembly has deliberated and adopted,
at its plenary session of 31 March 1998,
bill No. 634/PJL/AN, the content of which is as follows:

TITLE I
GENERAL PROVISIONS

ARTICLE 1.- This Law establishes, in compliance with the principles of environmental management and public health protection, the general legal framework for the water regime.

ARTICLE 2.- (1) Water is a common heritage of the Nation, which the State shall protect and manage and to which it shall facilitate access for all.

(2) However, the State may transfer all or part of its prerogatives to decentralized local authorities.

(3) The management of water may, furthermore, be subject to a concession or a lease, in accordance with the modalities established by a decree implementing this Law.

ARTICLE 3.- For the purposes of this Law and its implementing texts, the following definitions shall apply:
(1) surface water: runoff water, watercourses, and stagnant water;
(2) groundwater: infiltration water and aquifers;
(3) spring water: water offered for sale in commerce on the basis of its therapeutic qualities;
(4) mineral water: groundwater containing dissolved mineral substances with a therapeutic effect.

ARTICLE 4.- (1) The discharge, flow, jetting, infiltration, burial, spreading, and direct or indirect deposit into water of any solid, liquid, or gaseous matter, and in particular industrial, agricultural, and atomic waste, likely to:

impair the quality of surface water, groundwater, or sea water within the territorial limits;

harm public health as well as aquatic or marine fauna and flora;

jeopardize the economic and tourism development of the regions;
are prohibited.

(2) However, the Minister in charge of Water may, after an inquiry and the opinion of the other concerned Administrations, authorize and regulate the discharges referred to above in cases where they guarantee safety and the absence of nuisances, taking into account the characteristics of the effluent and the receiving environment.

(3) The authorization granted may be modified or withdrawn at the request of the holder or interested third parties, at the initiative of the Administration, or automatically, in the case provided for by the authorization act.

ARTICLE 5.- (1) An implementing decree of this Law shall, upon a joint report of the competent Administrations, establish the list of noxious or dangerous substances whose rejection, discharge, deposit, immersion, or direct or indirect introduction into water is either prohibited or subject to the prior authorization of the said Administrations.

(2) The discharge of wastewater into the public sanitation network must not harm either the conservation of the works or the management of the water.
(3) Discharge facilities established prior to the date of promulgation of this Law must comply with the regulations within a period to be set by an implementing decree of the said Law.

(4) Facilities established after the date of promulgation of this Law must, from the time they become operational, comply with the discharge standards set by the regulations in force.

ARTICLE 6.- (1) Any natural or legal person owning facilities likely to cause water pollution must take all necessary measures to limit or eliminate their effects.

(2) Any person who produces or holds waste must ensure its elimination or recycling, or have it eliminated or recycled in facilities approved by the Administration in charge of classified establishments, after the mandatory opinion of the Administration in charge of the environment.

Furthermore, subject to the rules related to confidentiality, such a person is required to inform the public about the effects of the production, possession, elimination, or recycling of waste on water, the environment, and public health, as well as on the measures intended to prevent or compensate for their prejudicial effects.

(3) Furthermore, the cleaning and maintenance of motor vehicles, internal combustion engines, and other similar machinery in the vicinity of water are prohibited.

ARTICLE 7.- (1) In order to protect the quality of water intended for consumption, a protection perimeter is established around water abstraction, treatment, and storage points.

(2) The lands included within the protection perimeters are declared to be of public utility.

ARTICLE 8.- (1) A sanitation tax is collected by the State from natural or legal persons who own facilities connected to public or private networks for the collection and treatment of wastewater.

(2) The rate and collection modalities of the tax provided for in paragraph (1) are established by the finance law.

ARTICLE 12.- The quality control of consumer water is ensured at all times by sworn and commissioned personnel from the Administrations in charge of water and public health, respectively.

CHAPTER II
EXPLOITATION OF SPRING AND MINERAL WATERS

ARTICLE 13.- The exploitation of spring and mineral waters is governed by a specific law.

TITLE IV
LIABILITY AND SANCTIONS

CHAPTER I
LIABILITY

ARTICLE 14.- Without prejudice to the applicable penalties under criminal liability and notwithstanding the verifications carried out by the Administrations in charge of control, any person who has caused personal or material damage resulting from the poor quality of the water they distribute shall be civilly liable, without it being necessary to prove fault.

CHAPTER II
CRIMINAL SANCTIONS

ARTICLE 15.- (1) Any person who:

carries out a withdrawal of surface or groundwater in violation of the provisions of this Law and/or its implementing texts;

carries out a withdrawal of surface or groundwater that does not conform to the criteria, standards, and measures set forth in the impact assessment;

prevents the performance of controls, surveillance, and analyses provided for by this Law and/or by its implementing texts;

operates an abstraction, treatment, and water storage facility in violation of the provisions of this Law and/or its implementing texts;

offers drinking water to the public without complying with the quality standards in force;

violates a protection perimeter around water abstraction, treatment, and storage points;
shall be punished by imprisonment from two (2) to five (5) years and a fine from five million (5,000,000) to ten million (10,000,000) FCFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

(2) In the event of a repeat offense, the offender shall be liable to double the maximum penalties provided for in paragraph (1) above.

ARTICLE 16.- (1) Any person who pollutes and impairs the quality of water shall be punished by imprisonment from five (5) to fifteen (15) years and a fine from ten million (10,000,000) to twenty million (20,000,000) FCFA.

(2) In the event of a repeat offense, the offender shall be liable to double the maximum penalties provided for in paragraph (1) above.

ARTICLE 17.- The sanctions provided for by this Law are supplemented by those contained in the Penal Code as well as in the legislation relating to environmental protection.

ARTICLE 18.- The provisions of Articles 54 and 90 of the Penal Code relating to suspended sentences and mitigating circumstances are not applicable to the sanctions provided for by this Law.

CHAPTER III
INVESTIGATION OF OFFENSES

ARTICLE 19.- (1) Without prejudice to the prerogatives recognized to the public prosecutor's office and to judicial police officers with general jurisdiction, the sworn agents of the Administration in charge of water or other concerned Administrations, particularly those of health and the environment, are responsible for the investigation, establishment, and prosecution of offenses against the provisions of this Law and its implementing texts.

(2) The agents mentioned in paragraph (1) above shall take an oath before the competent court, at the request of the interested Administration, in accordance with the modalities established by an implementing decree of this Law.

(3) In the exercise of their functions, sworn agents are required to carry their professional card.

ARTICLE 20.- (1) Any offense found shall be the subject of a regular official report.

(2) The investigation and establishment of offenses are carried out by two (2) agents who co-sign the official report. This official report shall be considered authentic until proven false.

ARTICLE 21.- (1) Any official report establishing an offense must be transmitted immediately to the Administration in charge of water, which shall notify the offender. The latter has a period of twenty (20) days from this notification to contest the official report. After this period, any contestation becomes inadmissible.

(2) In the event of a contestation within the time limits provided for in paragraph (1) of this article, the claim is examined by the Administration in charge of water.

If the contestation is founded, the official report is dismissed without further action.

In the contrary case, and in the absence of a definitive settlement or arbitration, the Administration in charge of water shall initiate legal proceedings in accordance with the legislation in force.

CHAPTER IV
SETTLEMENT AND ARBITRATION

ARTICLE 22.- (1) The Administration in charge of water has full powers to enter into a settlement. To do so, it must be duly seized by the author of the offense.

(2) The amount of the settlement is fixed in concert with the Administration in charge of finance. This amount may not be less than the minimum corresponding criminal fine.

(3) The settlement procedure must be prior to any eventual judicial procedure, under penalty of nullity.

(4) The proceeds of the settlement are fully paid into the Fund provided for by this Law.

ARTICLE 23.- The parties to a dispute relating to water management may settle it by mutual agreement through arbitration.

ARTICLE 24.- (1) Traditional authorities have the competence to settle disputes related to the use of water resources on the basis of local customs and practices, without prejudice to the right of the parties to the dispute to seize the competent courts.

(2) An official report of the settlement of the dispute shall be drawn up. A copy of this official report, duly signed by the administrative authority of the territorial jurisdiction of the village community where the dispute takes place, shall be made.

TITLE V
MISCELLANEOUS AND FINAL PROVISIONS

ARTICLE 25.- (1) In order to guarantee the financing of sustainable development projects in the field of water and sanitation, the finance law shall annually determine the specific resources to supply a special allocation account created for this purpose by presidential decree, in accordance with the provisions of Articles 39 and 41 of Ordinance No. 62/OF/4 of 7 February 1962 relating to the financial regime of the State.

This decree shall determine, in particular, the management modalities of the aforementioned account.

(2) The special allocation account provided for in paragraph (1) above may also receive, where applicable:

contributions from international donors;

any other voluntary contributions;

donations and legacies.

(3) The specific resources provided for in paragraphs (1) and (2) above may not be allocated to other purposes.

ARTICLE 26.- (1) Without prejudice to the provisions of the legislation relating to environmental management, a National Water Committee is hereby established by this Law.

(2) The attributions, organization, and functioning of the National Water Committee are established by an implementing decree of this Law.

ARTICLE 27.- The specifications relating to the construction, operation, and maintenance of public or private water supply and sanitation networks and installations are established by an implementing decree of this Law.

ARTICLE 28.- The use of water as a means of transport is governed by the Merchant Shipping Code.

ARTICLE 29.- Implementing decrees of this Law shall specify, as needed, its modalities.

ARTICLE 30.- All prior provisions of Law No. 84/013 of 5 December 1984 on the water regime are hereby repealed.

ARTICLE 31.- This Law shall be registered, published following the emergency procedure, and then inserted in the Official Journal in French and English./-
