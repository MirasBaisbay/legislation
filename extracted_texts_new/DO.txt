Law No. 5852 of March 29, 1962, on the Domain of Terrestrial Waters and Distribution of Public Waters O.G. No. 8666.

The Council of State
In the name of the Republic

Number: 5852

CHAPTER I
ON THE DOMAIN OF RAINWATER.

Art. 1.- For the purposes of this law, rainwater is considered to be that which proceeds immediately from rainfall.

Art. 2.- Rainwater that falls on a property belongs to the owner of said property while it flows through it. Consequently, the owner may build, within their property, ponds, marshes, cisterns, or reservoirs to store it for that purpose, or use any other adequate means, provided that it does not cause harm to the public or to third parties.

Art. 3.- Rainwater that flows through ravines or dry riverbeds, whose channels are also in the public domain, is public property.

Art. 4.- The Secretariat of State for Agriculture, through the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, may grant authorization to those who request it to build cisterns or reservoirs on public lands to collect rainwater. For such purposes, the interested party must submit a plan of the works to be executed.

Art. 5.- The water collected in this manner shall be used by the executor of the works in the form and under the conditions determined by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources. In any case, said Directorate may use any surplus that may result after the constructor's use for the benefit of interested third parties.

Art. 6.- The owners of properties adjoining public roads may utilize the verges of said roads.

Art. 7.- The owners of properties bordering public watercourses of non-continuous flow, such as dry riverbeds, ravines, and the like, which are in the public domain, may use for irrigation the rainwater that flows through them and may construct, for that purpose, earth and loose stone embankments or mobile or permanent dams, provided that this does not prejudice the pre-existing rights of downstream properties that had previously built dams for the use of said waters. For such works, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources shall be duly informed in advance to determine the priority of use.

Paragraph: The General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may, in any case, construct dams within public domain lands to collect rainwater that flows through dry riverbeds, ravines, or discontinuous streams for the purpose of distribution among the needy property owners of the region.

Art. 8.- When these embankments or dams may cause floods or any other harm to the public, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, either ex officio or at the request of a party, upon verifying the danger, shall order the builder to modify them as necessary to dispel any fear, or if necessary, to destroy them. If they threaten to cause harm to private individuals, the latter may file a timely claim with the local authority, and if the harm occurs, their rights before the Courts of Justice shall be expedited.

Art. 9.- The provisions in the preceding articles regarding rainwater are applicable to the water from intermittent springs and streams that only flow in times of abundant rainfall.

Art. 10.- When the construction of permanent dams is intended for the purpose of utilizing rainwater or water from springs and streams for irrigation.

CHAPTER II
DOMAIN OF LIVE, SPRING, AND RUNNING WATERS.

Art. 11.- The waters of rivers, waters that rise continuously or discontinuously on public domain lands; continuous waters from springs and streams that run through their natural courses, constitute part of the public domain... and consequently, are considered available for distribution for agricultural or industrial purposes on any land on whose slope it is possible to practice the conveyance of water, in the form and under the conditions established in this Law.

Art. 12.- In the properties of private individuals, the waters that rise continuously or discontinuously therein belong to the owner for their use or enjoyment while they flow through the properties.

When the unused waters leave the property, they become public waters for the purposes of this Law. However, if after leaving the river where they originate, they naturally enter to flow through another private property, the owner of said property may use them, fulfilling the requirements demanded by the Law, as may the immediately downstream owner if there is one, and so on, with prior authorization from the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Art. 13.- Any eventual use of water from springs and streams in natural channels may be freely maintained by the owners of downstream properties, provided they obtain the corresponding prior authorization from the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

1.- The properties through which the waters flow before their incorporation into the river, maintaining the order of their proximity to the source of the currents and respecting their right to eventual use along the entire length of each property.

2.- The properties fronting or adjoining the channel, in order of proximity to it and always preferring the upstream ones.

However, it is understood that in these downstream and lateral properties, whoever has anticipated the use cannot be deprived of it by another, even if the latter is situated further upstream in the course of the water. And no eventual use may interrupt or attack previously acquired rights to the same waters in a downstream region.

Art. 15.- Waters not used by the owner of the property where they originate, as well as any surplus from their uses, shall exit the property at the same point of the accustomed natural channel, and may not be diverted in any way from the course through which they originally flowed. The same is understood for immediately downstream properties in relation to the next, with this order always being observed.

Art. 16.- If the owner of a property where a natural spring has emerged does not use more than half, a third, or another fractional amount of its waters, the remaining surplus shall be used by the owners of inland properties in the manner determined in Article 7 of this Law.

However, it is understood that in these downstream or lateral properties, the one who anticipates or had anticipated cannot be deprived of it by another, even if the latter were situated further upstream in the course of the water.

Art. 17.- The domain of mineral waters that rise or flow in lands of public or private property constitute part of the public domain and consequently are considered available for concession in accordance with the conditions determined by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources. When granting a concession for these sources, the owner of the land where said source rises or flows will have priority under equal conditions.

For the purposes of this law, mineral waters are understood to be those that contain dissolved substances useful for industry in general, whatever their nature may be.

Art. 18.- Mineral-medicinal waters are considered public domain, and the State may grant concessions for their exploitation or direct exportation, regulating the same in both cases. When said sources rise on private lands, the State shall give priority to the owner of the same for their exploitation, in the event that it does not directly undertake the exploitation of said springs.

CHAPTER III
ON THE DOMAIN OF STILL OR STAGNANT WATERS.

Art. 19.- Lakes and lagoons formed by nature that occupy public lands or whose size constitutes sources of use for communities that can be utilized in irrigation systems, reservoir for aqueducts, or any other work of public utility are in the public domain.

Art. 20.- Lagoons and ponds formed on lands owned by private individuals, and which do not fall under the designation established in the preceding article, are the property of said individuals.

CHAPTER IV
ON THE DOMAIN OF SUBTERRANEAN WATERS.

Art. 21.- Subterranean waters obtained by means of ordinary wells, that is, wells for domestic purposes and for watering livestock, belong to the owner of a property in full ownership.

Art. 22.- Every property owner may freely open ordinary wells to carry water within their properties, even if this results in the diminishment of their neighbors' waters. However, the distance of meters between wells within towns and 15 meters in the countryside must be maintained between the new excavation and the permanent wells, ponds, springs, and aqueducts of the neighbors.

Art. 23.- The owner of any land may explore for and fully appropriate, by means of artesian wells, deep wells, and through tunnels and galleries, the waters that exist beneath the surface of their property, provided that they do not divert or separate public or private waters from their natural course.

Art. 24.- When, as a consequence of the works of an artesian well, deep well, tunnel, or gallery, public or private waters destined for a public service or a pre-existing private use, with legitimately acquired rights, are diverted or diminished, the officials of the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, as soon as it comes to their knowledge by any means, may suspend the works in progress.

Art. 25.- The works referred to in the preceding article for water exploration may not be executed at a distance of less than 40 meters from others' buildings, a railway, or a road, nor less than 100 meters from another water exploration site, spring, river, canal, aqueduct, or public watering place, without the corresponding license from the owners.

Art. 26.- Concessions of public domain lands for the exploration of subterranean waters by means of galleries, tunnels, artesian wells, or deep wells shall be granted by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, with everything related to the domain, limitations of ownership, and use of the explored waters being subject to what this law prescribes with respect to these private matters.

Concessions for these subterranean explorations may only be granted on public domain lands whose surface or soil has not been granted for a different purpose, unless both are compatible.

No concession of this kind may prejudice pre-existing uses, whether of public interest or private, with legitimately acquired rights.

CHAPTER V
ON DEFENSE WORKS FOR PUBLIC WATERS.

Art. 27.- The owners of properties bordering public watercourses have the liberty to place defenses against the waters on their respective banks by means of plantations, dikes, revetments, walls, or any other work, provided they deem them convenient, giving timely notice thereof to the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources. Said Directorate may, however, after due process, order the suspension of such works and even the restoration of things to their former state, when due to such circumstances they threaten to cause prejudice to navigation or the floating of logs in rivers, divert the currents from their natural course, or produce floods.

Paragraph: When the defense works consist of simple walls erected within the scope of private property and longitudinally to the course of the river, no third party may allege prejudice as a consequence of said work, and therefore the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may not order the destruction of said works.

Art. 28.- When plantations and any defense work that is intended are to invade the channel, they may not be executed without prior authorization from the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Art. 29.- In channels where it is convenient to execute inexpensive dense works, the Director General of Hydraulic Resources shall grant a general authorization so that the owners of the bordering properties, each on the part of the channel bordering their respective bank, may construct them, but subject to the conditions set in the concession, aimed at preventing some owners from causing prejudice to others.

Art. 30.- When the projected works are of some consideration, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, at the request of those who promote them and after studying them, may oblige all the property owners who are to benefit from them to bear their cost, provided that the majority of them, computed by the share of property each one represents, gives their conformity and the common utility that the works are to produce appears fulfilled and factually justified. In such a case, each one shall contribute to the payment according to the benefits they receive.

Art. 31.- Whenever, in order to prevent or contain imminent floods, it is necessary in an emergency to carry out provisional works or to destroy existing ones on any class of properties, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may order it.

Art. 32.- Works of general, provisional, or local interest necessary to defend populations and to keep navigable and floatable rivers channeled and clear shall be decided upon or paid for by the State.

The examination and study of post-projects related to this class of works corresponds to the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, which, through the corresponding Secretary of State, shall submit to the Executive Power the necessity of executing them.

Art. 33.- The lands and works that are necessary for the construction, expansion, or improvement of works for the use of public waters are declared of public utility, without prejudice to any indemnifications that may be appropriate, in accordance with the law.

TITLE II
ON SPECIAL USES OF PUBLIC WATERS

CHAPTER I
ON THE USE OF PUBLIC WATERS FOR FISH HATCHERIES AND FARMS.

Art. 34.- The General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, through the corresponding Secretariat of State, may grant concessions for the use of waters to form lakes, pools, or ponds intended for fish hatcheries or farms, provided that no harm is caused to public health or to any other downstream uses with previously acquired rights.

Art. 35.- For the industry referred to in the preceding article, the petitioner shall present the complete project of the works and the title proving ownership of the land where they are to be built or having obtained the consent of the owner.

Art. 36.- The concessionaires of public waters for irrigation or industrial establishments may, upon prior request for that purpose, form pools or ponds for fish hatcheries in their canals or on adjacent lands they have acquired.

Art. 37.- Authorizations for fish hatcheries shall be granted in perpetuity. However, it is understood that if the beneficiary does not make use of their permit within the period agreed upon by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, or if they abandon the care and attention of the hatchery for more than 2 years, they shall forfeit their concession by full right.

CHAPTER II
ON THE USE OF PUBLIC WATERS FOR INDUSTRIAL PURPOSES.

Art. 38.- The waters of rivers may be used for the production of mechanical energy, directly or for the production of light and electrical power, and for industrial uses, provided that it does not cause the deviation of the waters from their natural course. In such cases, their establishment shall not hinder the free flow of the waters nor prejudice the bordering properties, irrigated lands, or established industries, including fishing.

Art. 39.- Any natural person............ studies for the use of public waters destined for industrial enterprises of public or private interest must first direct a request for authorization to the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Paragraph: To the aforementioned request, the interested party or parties shall attach an explanatory report of the project, indicating the water source to be used, its available flow, and the purposes of the projected work.

Art. 40.- The right to use the waters of rivers for the purposes of Article 38 may be granted to private individuals or companies, Dominican or foreign, who request it from the Executive Power through the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, with the request containing an attachment for its study by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, as follows:

a) The project of the works, composed of the plans, descriptive memory, budget of expenses, and technical and economic conditions in general;

b) The declaration to adhere to the stipulations of this Law.

Art. 41.- In both navigable and non-navigable rivers, the Executive Power shall grant the necessary authorization for the establishment of mills or other industrial plants in buildings located near houses, to which the necessary water is conducted through canals and which is then returned to the river's current. In no case shall this authorization be granted if it harms the navigation of the rivers and existing industrial establishments.

To obtain the authorization referred to in this article, it is an indispensable requirement that the person requesting it be the owner of the land where they intend to build the structure or be authorized for it by the owner.

Art. 42.- When an industrial establishment releases into the waters substances and properties harmful to health, vegetation, or the procreation and life of fish, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources shall have a technical inspection conducted, and if the harm is found to be true, it shall order the industrial work to be suspended until its owners adopt the appropriate remedy. The rights and expenses of the inspection shall be paid by the person who filed the complaint, if it is found to be unfounded, and otherwise by the owner of the establishment.

When the owner or owners, within a period of six months, have not adopted the appropriate remedy, it shall be understood that they renounce continuing the exploitation of their industry.

Art. 43.- Concessions for the use of public waters for industrial purposes shall be granted in perpetuity and on the condition that if at any time the waters acquire properties harmful to health, vegetation, or fish, the concession shall be forfeited by right without any right to compensation.

Art. 45.- Concessions for industrial purposes shall be granted by means of contracts celebrated by the Executive Power, requested through the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, which shall be in charge of examining the plans and projects provided for in the indicated law. In said contracts, post-payments shall be stipulated that the concessionaire must make to the Treasury for the use of the waters, according to the importance of the concessions and taking into account the information contained in the preceding article.

Art. 46.- Small-scale coffee growers who use water for washing the coffee from their production are exempted from the prescriptions of this article, when the quantity, solely and exclusively necessary, is for a depulping machine moved by muscular force or hydraulic power. They shall only pay a basic tax for each contract and one peso annually for the effective use of the waters.

Art. 47.- Private individuals who wish to use public waters must first provide themselves with a title.... And fulfill, at the time of effective use, the other requirements established in this law.

Art. 48.- Every application for water titles, whether for agricultural or industrial uses, must be addressed to the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, accompanied by a plan of the land. In said plan, the parcels that constitute the land shall be indicated, if it is registered; otherwise, all the boundaries of the land shall be indicated, in order to determine the extent and the settlement.

Paragraph: The right of priority is established in the applications for water titles. This priority shall not be effective, however, when the requested waters may be of interest to the Government for use in national irrigation or other purposes of public interest.

Art. 49.- Applications for water titles must be made on the forms prepared for that purpose by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources and must be signed by the owner of the land.

Paragraph: The tenants of lands may also apply for water titles, when they are authorized by the owner of the land, with the understanding that if the water titles are granted, they will constitute a right inherent to the land and not to the tenant.

Art. 50.- When the quantity requested for a water title exceeds fifty liters per second, the application must be accompanied by a general plan and details of the work by grid, on a scale of 1 to 2000, design of sections, corresponding calculations, and descriptive memories.

The General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may grant water titles for quantities less than those requested, when the flow of the source so requires, in order to make its distribution equitable.

Likewise, said Directorate may grant water titles to be used solely and exclusively in the semi-arid regions dedicated to pastures and other crops; for which purpose the Directorate shall arrange for the study of the rivers from the point of view of the best regime and the gauging of their currents, in order to determine the amount of water available in the different seasons of the year.

Art. 52.- The water rights obtained through the concession of a title shall form an inseparable part of the corresponding property. These rights shall consist of the faculty to use waters for the purposes indicated in the titles, but with the obligation, on the part of the holder, to request annual permits for the effective use of the waters, fulfilling the requirements of the Law.

Paragraph: However, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may authorize, after a technical report proving its justification, the transfer of water titles from one land to another, in which case the title shall form an inseparable part of the new land.

Art. 53.- In all titles for the use of public waters, their nature, the quantity of liters per second of the granted water, and if it were for irrigation, the extent in hectares of the land to be irrigated, shall be specified.

Art. 54.- The quantity of one liter per second per hectare is designated as the unit of water for distribution purposes.

Paragraph: The General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources shall determine for each case the respective irrigation allocation, taking into account the physical constitution of the land and the class of crops.

Art. 55.- The waters granted for one use may not be dedicated to another diverse use without the formation of a file, as if it were a new concession; it being provided, however, that the waters granted for irrigation may also be applied in the industrial preparation of the products of the irrigated property itself, provided that the number of liters granted is not increased, and that the waters granted to move machinery and which must therefore be returned to the channel, may also be used for any other industrial purposes that do not utilize them in a way that prevents their return to the channel.

Art. 56.- The General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources and consequently the corresponding Secretariat of State shall not be responsible for any lack or decrease that may result in the flow expressed in the title, whether it proceeds from an error or any other cause.

Art. 57.- Whenever in the concessions, the water titles, and in the enjoyment of determined quantities of water for a fixed period of time, nothing else is expressed, continuous use is understood to be at all times; if it is by day, the natural day shall be understood as 24 hours from midnight; if it is during the day or the night, it shall be understood as between sunrise and sunset; and if it is by week, it shall be counted from 12 o'clock on Sunday night; if it is for festive days or with their exclusion, it shall be understood to be those of precept on which one cannot work, with only those days that were such at the time of the concession or contract being considered festive. The application of these provisions and the details regarding the manner and time of water enjoyment are the responsibility of the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, which shall put them into practice through its officials.

Art. 58.- Concessions for the use of waters shall lapse for failure to comply with the conditions and terms in accordance with which they were granted.

CHAPTER II
ON THE APPLICATION FOR PERMITS FOR THE USE OF WATERS.

Art. 59.- For the construction of canals, ponds for fish hatcheries and farms, industrial facilities, as well as to effectively use the waters, the holders of water titles must request and obtain permits from the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Paragraph: Applications for the use of waters may also be made by the tenants or users of the lands in any form, provided that the lands are covered by water titles.

Art. 60.- From November 1 to 30 of each year, all holders of water titles or the tenants or users who have obtained permits for the effective use of the waters must renew their permits. Both the applications for use and for renewal shall be addressed to the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, which shall prepare said Directorate for this purpose.

Paragraph: Excluded from the annual renewal for colonization of the waters are industrial exploitations that will be governed by the special contracts signed with the Executive Power, as well as persons or companies that use said waters in ponds for fish hatcheries, which will be subject to an annual fee determined in the concession, and those that use rainwater and non-continuous waters, which due to their scarcity only permit the irrigation of pastures.

Art. 62.- Ranchers, breeders, and cattlemen in general who have animals on lands where there are State canals or aqueducts may use their waters for the needs of their livestock, free from the payment of any fee, tax, or contribution.

Art. 63.- The use shall be equitable among the interests, and for this purpose, the Executive Power shall have the capacity to regulate it by means of regulations or administrative inspections directed at the authorities of the State canals and aqueducts.

Art. 64.- Every permit for the use of water entails the obligation on the part of the user to whom it is granted to make use of the quota that the permit determines. In all cases that are not due to fortuitous causes or force majeure in which the user does not make use, for a period of two years, of the total or partial amount stated in their permit, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may grant it to whoever requests it. No exemptions from payment will be granted for lack of use of the waters.

Art. 65.- When the flow of the source so requires, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may regulate the use of the waters in turns among the persons who hold the corresponding water titles.

Paragraph: The provision of the preceding article regarding the organization of turns shall not be taken into account for the purposes of payment of the fee for the use of public waters.

Art. 66.- In the general interest of the best use of the waters, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources shall arrange for the recognition of existing rivers and irrigation canals, with the aim of ensuring that no irrigator wastes the water of their allocation that could serve another in need of it, and to prevent torrential waters from precipitating unproductively and even harmfully into the sea, when other regions desire and request them for irrigation and seasonal use, without prejudice to acquired rights.

CHAPTER III
ON IRRIGATION DISTRICTS

Art. 67.- An Irrigation District constitutes the area of land that includes the distribution of water by means of canals constructed at the expense of the State or constructed on account of landowners interested in the irrigation works and whose limits shall be fixed by decree of the Executive Power.

Art. 68.- The Public Administration shall gradually organize Irrigation Districts, as the needs of each region of the country demand, upon prior recommendation made to the Executive Power by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, through the corresponding Secretariat of State.

Art. 69.- The establishment of a land area as an Irrigation District entails by full right the official direction and irrigation in said area.

Art. 70.- In cases where the State constructs irrigation works at its own cost, these shall be paid for by the owners of the benefited lands in proportion to the amount of water that said owners are able to use at the time of the construction of the work or subsequently.

Paragraph I: (Modified by Law No. 436, of October 10, 1964, published in O.G. No. 8897, of October 10, 1964). The owners of lands who use or are able to use the waters of a canal constructed by the State shall pay the quotas of their lands solely and exclusively in kind, in the following form:

a) 25% of their irrigable lands, when their crops are for human sustenance;

b) 35% of their irrigable lands when these are cultivated with pastures, that is, for animal feed;

c) 50% of their irrigable lands when these are cultivated with pastures, that is, for animal feed;

d) When the extent of a parcel, upon calculating its quota share, results in an area of less than 60 tareas, the owner shall be exempt from the payment of their quota share, provided it is proven that they only own that property, with the object of avoiding smallholdings (minifundio), which is contrary to the principles of the Agrarian Reform.

In all these cases, the lands delivered as a quota share shall be those within reach of the canal in question at the time of construction.

Paragraph II: (Modified by Law No. 436, of October 10, 1964, published, as soon as the canal is finished, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, through the corresponding Secretariat of State, shall communicate this to the Director General of the Dominican Agrarian Institute with an indication of the cost and all pertinent data. The Director General of the Dominican Agrarian Institute shall notify each owner, through an Inspector in its service or the rural authorities of the place, in writing, of the amount of land that they will be obliged to deliver to the Agrarian Institute, to be destined for the plans of the Agrarian Reform. Each owner shall have a period of fifteen days to make the arguments they deem appropriate in writing, to the Director General of the Dominican Agrarian Institute.

Paragraph III: (Modified by Law No. 436, of October 10, 1964, published in O.G. No. 8897, of October 10, 1964). The delivery of the quota share in kind must be made by the Director General of the Dominican Agrarian Institute, within 30 days, from the expiration of the period established in the previous paragraph, and the selection shall be made by common agreement between the parties. In the event that the owner refuses to deliver the corresponding quota share, the Director General of the Agrarian Institute shall demand the delivery of said quota share in a compulsory manner according to the Law of the matter.

The executive power shall have the faculty to grant deadlines for the delivery of the owed quota shares of land and in special circumstances that it will sovereignly weigh, may waive them totally or partially, whatever the quota share of suitable lands may be.

Paragraph IV: (Modified by Law No. 436, of October 10, 1964, published in O.G. No. 8897, of October 10, 1964): The delivery of the quota share shall be made before the justice of the peace or before a Notary Public; the latter may be from the municipality where the parcel is located, or from the National District, which is where the Dominican Agrarian Institute has its domicile and main office that instruments it, by the Director General of the Agrarian Institute or an official in his service that he delegates, by two witnesses, and in it, mention shall be made of the notification and the selection that was made by common agreement between the parties.

Paragraph V: (Modified by Law No. 436 of October 10, 1964, published in O.G. No. 8897, of October 10, 1964): In the event that the owner does not appear before the Justice of the Peace or chosen Notary within the indicated period, it shall be deemed that he makes the cession of lands in the situation indicated by the Director of the Institute or his delegate, and a record of this legal cession shall be drawn up, in which the notification and the allegations referred to in the previous paragraphs shall be mentioned, and must be signed in this case by the Justice of the Peace or Notary who acts, the Director General of the Dominican Agrarian Institute, or his delegate, and four witnesses.

Paragraph VI: (Modified by Law No. 436, of October 10, 1964, published in O.G. No. 8897, of October 10, 1964): The copy of the act, authenticated by the Justice of the Peace and his Secretary, or by a Notary Public, shall constitute in all cases a title of ownership for the Dominican Agrarian Institute over the extent of land to which it refers for all legal purposes, including surveys, registrations or consolidations by the Land Tribunal, when so requested by the Director General of the Dominican Agrarian Institute. In the event that the delivered lands are not surveyed, the survey shall be immediately requested from the Land Tribunal, and priority must be given to it so that the Registrars of Titles can issue the Certificates of Title as soon as possible.

Paragraph VII: It is understood that these payments are independent of those that the owners or tenants must make both to obtain the water titles and to use the waters, through the corresponding annual permits.

Paragraph VIII: The canals thus constructed and paid for shall belong as undivided property to the landowners who have contributed to their construction; but, in their condition as works of public utility, they shall be under the control of the State in accordance with this law and may not be alienated.

Art. 71.- In cases where the State constructs irrigation canals, it may suppress the private canals that are in the irrigable zone or take advantage of their courses for the execution of the construction plan when technical reasons so advise.

Paragraph I: The persons whose canals have been suppressed in accordance with this article shall be served, exempt from the construction fee, with the amount of water considered necessary, in the judgment of the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, for the equitable irrigation of the region in question; said persons, however, are obliged to contribute their share of maintenance and to pay the annual permit for the use of the waters.

Paragraph II: The person who is the owner of a water title and who, despite this circumstance, benefits or has the possibility of benefiting from the waters of a canal constructed by the state, must make the proportional payment of the quota share that corresponds to them in accordance with the provisions of this law regarding the area in excess irrigable by the new canal, consequently excluding the area irrigated by their old canal.

However, when it is determined that a private canal exists and that its owner does not use the waters of the State canal in any way, they shall not be obliged to pay the quota share for the construction of the state canal.

Paragraph III: (Modified by Law No. 736, of October 10, 1964, published in O.G. No. 8897, of October 10, 1964): In the report that the Director General of Hydraulic Resources sends to the Director General of the Dominican Agrarian Institute through the corresponding Secretariat of State, for the purposes of Article 70 of this law, everything that is relevant for the exemption of the construction quota provided in the preceding paragraphs shall be indicated, or that is relevant according to any other provision of this Law.

Art. 72.- The service of cleaning and maintenance of the canals constructed by the Government for irrigation purposes shall be the responsibility of those who use its waters by virtue of the rights they acquire in accordance with this Law, and said service shall be rendered in conformity with the regulations that for each District, the corresponding Secretariat of State dictates through the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Paragraph: The proportion of these services, which will consist solely of the contribution of laborers, shall be in accordance with the amount of water used by each irrigator or association of irrigators.

Art. 73.- The Office of each irrigation district shall notify each irrigator, 30 days in advance, of the proportional share of contribution that will correspond to them in the cleaning period of the main canals, in accordance with the distribution approved by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Paragraph: The irrigator who does not contribute their proportional share within the time fixed by the local Office shall lose their rights to use the waters in the next harvest season, and their portion of the water quota may be prorated among all the other irrigators who proportionally cover the other irrigators who proportionally cover this part.

CHAPTER IV
SPECIAL PROVISIONS FOR THE DISTRICTS.

Art. 74.- Free-range animal breeding is prohibited in the entire land area that comprises the Decree that creates an Irrigation District.

Art. 75.- It is prohibited to cross the drainage and irrigation canals of the general system of each District with animals, carts, or other vehicles, except by the passages expressly constructed for such purposes.

Art. 76.- In all main and secondary canals, constructed by the State or at its expense, there shall be an easement strip for said canals, the width of which shall be fixed by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources; but it shall not exceed eight meters on both sides of the canal.

Paragraph: It is prohibited on this strip of land to build fences, dwellings, warehouses, crops, industries, stables, latrines, and in general everything that could contaminate the waters to the detriment of public health and obstruct transit.

Art. 77.- It is prohibited to bathe, wash clothes, or wash animals, whether public or private.

Art. 78.- The conveyance of water from the place of delivery to the land to be irrigated shall be under the care of the interested party, who shall be responsible for any damages or prejudices of any kind that may be caused, whether due to lack of capacity of said canals, negligence, or clumsiness in the handling of the water.

Art. 79.- Every irrigator shall be obliged to maintain their internal canals, as well as their drainage systems, clean of weeds and mud within the deadlines set by the Local Administration of the District.

Art. 80.- Before starting the annual irrigation service, the Local Manager of the District or the Water Inspector shall inspect the internal canals and drainage system of each parcel, and those that are not in the conditions established by the previous article shall not be supplied with water until they meet the legal requirements.

Art. 81.- The irrigators are obliged to construct in their intake points, gates according to the model and specifications set by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, in order to control the water flow that has been indicated.

Paragraph: The management of these gates shall be exclusively the responsibility of the Water Inspector or his Representative, and it shall be considered a violation of this law for any other person to manage or alter the disposition of said gates.

Art. 82.- The owners of canals are obliged to construct solid bridges at the discretion of the District Manager, when these canals intercept roads, and to construct drainage canals that establish the necessary drainage to avoid unnecessary stagnation of water.

Art. 83.- When in the construction of a canal, its owner has to intercept lands of other owners, he must, at the discretion of the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, place firm palisades or fences on both sides of the canal, erect walls to prevent floods, and build bridges that allow internal communication of the intercepted properties, as well as any other kind of... necessary works when they cross other canals or established drains.

Paragraph I: The owners of parcels intercepted by canals, in these cases, have the option to use the water in an equivalent portion to wet an equal amount of land as that used in the canal in compensation for the lack of land ceded for the construction of the canal, without prejudice to the payment of the corresponding annual fee for the use of the water.

Paragraph II: (Modified by Law No. 436, of October 10, 1964, published in O.G. No. 8897, of October 10, 1964): When a parcel owner who has complied with the payment stipulated in Article 70, regarding the corresponding quota share to the Dominican Agrarian Institute, for the construction of a canal, for technical reasons has to cross lands of private individuals to make use of the waters, they shall be obliged to give their consent for the passage of the lateral through their respective properties, with the Director General of Hydraulic Resources or the representative he authorizes being in charge of determining the site where the lateral must pass.

Art. 84.- The construction of irrigation ditches at a distance of less than two meters from a boundary separating a parcel from any road or highway is prohibited.

Art. 85.- Any person who by any means increases the flow of the distribution canals shall be guilty of water theft and punished with a penalty of correctional imprisonment of six days to two years or a fine of twenty-five to two hundred pesos, or both penalties at once.

Art. 86.- It is forbidden to erect a "Palenque" or other fences crossing the irrigation or drainage canals that obstruct the free flow of water; such shall be permitted only in cases authorized by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, and must be constructed in such cases at a height of not less than thirty meters above the ...... With the material used, so that they cannot construct obstacles to the canal.

Art. 87.- Any person who erects dikes in the main canal and its laterals or destroys falls, or alters the organization of the water service regime, shall be punished with a penalty and correctional imprisonment of six to two years and a fine of twenty-five to two hundred pesos, or with both penalties at once.

Paragraph: If the person in view of such facts is an irrigator and a repeat offender, the water service may also be temporarily suspended.

CHAPTER V
ON IRRIGATORS' ASSOCIATIONS.

Art. 88.- In the collective uses of public waters for irrigation, a community or society of irrigators subject to the regime of its ordinances or regulations shall necessarily be formed. The community or society of irrigators shall be necessary:

When the number of irrigators reaches ten and the number of irrigable hectares is not less than 200;

When, in the judgment of the Secretariat of State for Agriculture, the local agricultural interests so require.

Outside of these cases, the formation of the community or society of irrigators shall be at the will of the majority of the irrigators of a locality.

Art. 89.- The community or society of irrigators shall have as its purpose the construction of common irrigation canals for the best distribution and use of the waters, from the public source to determined points where the members of the said associations can dispose of the waters, each one on their respective lands.

Paragraph I: These societies shall be governed by a regulation accepted and signed by all the members, in which the individual obligations of each member and their respective rights over the amount of water that belongs to them and the form of its distribution shall be expressed in a clear and precise manner.

Paragraph II: A copy of this regulation must be attached to the construction application required by Article 59 of this law.

Art. 90.- The Irrigators' societies shall pay the fees in the following manner:

The water titles, individually for each member;

The construction application, jointly by all the members;

The annual renewal application, individually by each member of the Society, in accordance with the amount that belongs to them according to the fixed regulation.

Art. 91.- Each member of the Society shall have their individual canal derived from the main canal of the Society or from a derivation of said canal and shall construct a gate at their intake according to the uniform design and specifications adopted by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Art. 92.- Every community of irrigators shall have a Board of Directors for it and in charge of the execution of the ordinances and the agreements of the same Society or Community of Irrigators.

Art. 93.- The number of individuals on the Board of Directors and their election by the Community or society of Irrigators shall be determined in the ordinances of the same. In said ordinances, the conditions of the electors and those eligible shall be fixed, and the time and form of the election shall be established, as well as the duration of the positions, which shall always be gratuitous and may only be renewed in case of re-election.

Art. 94.- In the Boards of Directors of the societies of Irrigators or Communities, whether agricultural or industrial, directly interested in the good administration of their waters, all shall have in the Board of Directors their corresponding representation, proportional to the right that respectively assists them in the use and enjoyment of the same waters.

Art. 95.- The powers of the Board of Directors of the Society of Irrigators or Communities of Irrigators shall be:

To watch over the interests of the community, promote its development, and defend its rights;

To dictate the convenient provisions for the best distribution of the waters, respecting the acquired rights and local customs;

To propose to the Boards the Ordinances and the Regulation or any alteration that it considers useful to introduce in the existing ones;

To establish the rigorous turns of water, reconciling the interests of the diverse crops among the irrigators and taking care that in the years of scarcity it is distributed in the convenient manner for their own interests.

To settle all the internal questions that arise among the members of the Society of Irrigators, by reason of the execution of their regulations.

Art. 96.- In all cases in which the Board of Directors of a Society of Irrigators cannot settle the internal questions that arise among its members, by reason of the execution of their regulations, they shall be settled by the Water Tribunal of their respective jurisdictions, to which both any member of the society and the Jurisdictional Water Inspector or whoever acts in his place may appeal.

Paragraph: The Water Tribunals empowered with any question relating to Societies of Irrigators or Communities shall resolve them in accordance with the internal regulations of the same and in the absence of provisions adequate to the case in said regulations, in accordance with the provisions of this law.

CHAPTER VI
ON THE DRAINING OF LAGOONS AND SWAMPY LANDS AND THE DRAINAGE OF LANDS.

Art. 97.- The drainage of all swampy lands, lagoons, and lands under irrigation is declared of public utility.

Art. 98.- For such purposes, when a proprietor of parcels whose lands are flooded or who has obtained permission for the use of water for irrigation or who must drain, after a study verified by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, shall have the right to cross lands of private individuals to let out the necessary water that makes the lands in question suitable for cultivation. The drainage canals shall pass through the site determined for that purpose by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

Art. 99.- The owners of lower-lying properties may oppose receiving the surplus waters from industrial establishments that carry or have dissolved in them harmful substances introduced by the owners of these establishments.

Art. 100.- In all cases where the drainage waters cross lower-lying properties, the owners of these may eventually take advantage of said waters if they are useful to them, fulfilling the requirements determined in this law. Likewise, the owners may use the drains to drain their property or part of it, according to the capacity of said drains.

Art. 101.- The construction of ditches for drainage at a distance of less than two meters from a boundary that separates a parcel from any road or highway is prohibited. The water level of those ditches or drains shall at no time be less than 50 centimeters below the... of the highway or road.

Art. 102.- The owners of lagoons or swampy or waterlogged lands who wish to drain or sanitize them may extract from public lands, with the corresponding authorization from the competent authority, the earth and stone they consider indispensable for the embankment and other works.

Art. 103.- When the lagoons or swampy lands to be drained or sanitized belong to several owners, and partial drainage is not possible, and several of them wish for it to be done in common, the Secretariat of State for Agriculture, through the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, when the interest of the agricultural exploitation of the region so requires, may oblige all the owners to collectively pay for the works destined for that purpose, provided that the majority is in agreement, with such being understood as those who represent the largest area of sanitizable lands. If any of the owners of said lands were to resist the payment and prefer to cede their part of sanitizable property to the other co-owners, it shall be determined according to the value of the lands without taking into account the sanitation works.

Art. 104.- When a lagoon or swampy or waterlogged terrain is declared unhealthy by the corresponding authority, its drainage or sanitation is mandatory. If it were of private property, the resolution shall be made known to the owners so that they may arrange for the drainage or sanitation in the plan indicated to them.

Art. 105.- If the majority of the owners refuse to execute the drainage or sanitation, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, with prior authorization granted by the Executive Power, may proceed to carry out the necessary works. The sanitized land shall become the property of the Dominican State, which shall pay only to the former owners the sum corresponding to the capitalization.

Art. 106.- When the sanitation and drainage works are of such magnitude that they constitute works of public utility by favoring the growth and economic development of a region or the health of a region, said works shall be studied and executed by the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources on behalf of the Dominican State.

CHAPTER VII
ON THE WATER POLICE.

Art. 107.- The Managers of Irrigation Districts, the Inspectors, and Distributors of Waters are designated as Water Police Officers, in addition to the officials of the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources.

It is the responsibility of said officials to:

a) Exercise the functions of the Public Ministry in the Water Tribunal of the Municipality where the infraction has occurred;

b) Enforce the rulings and provisions of the corresponding Water Tribunal.

c) Submit to the Water Tribunal of their Jurisdiction the irregularities that affect the good irrigation service of the Districts or Municipalities under their charge;

d) Periodically inspect the canals and drains to demand that they be maintained within the prescriptions of the law.

e) Inspect the intake points and gates to control the flow that has been assigned to each one;

f) Attend to the gauging of rivers, streams, and canals within their jurisdiction;

g) Attend to the observations of Waters in all the Irrigation Districts and in those Municipalities where the extent of the crops under irrigation requires such functioning.

CHAPTER VIII
ON THE WATER TRIBUNALS.

Art. 109.- The justices of the peace are invested as Water Tribunals to hear and rule on other matters related to the execution of this Law of Waters.

Art. 110.- The Public Ministry before the Courts of Peace constituted as Water Tribunals shall be exercised by the Water Inspector of the corresponding Municipality.

Paragraph: In cases where, for any circumstance, the corresponding water inspector cannot exercise the functions of the Public Ministry before the court of peace constituted as a Water Tribunal, said functions shall be exercised by the Prosecutor of said Court of Peace.

Art. 111.- Once the Court of Peace is empowered, the Justice of the Peace, within 24 hours from the receipt of the act of submission made by the Water Inspector, shall summon the offender by means of a summons and through the corresponding rural authority within a period of one more day plus the legal time for distance.

Art. 112.- The rulings of the Courts of Peace in their functions as Water Tribunals shall be appealable before the Court of First Instance of the corresponding Judicial District, which shall hear the matter in a summary form.

Art. 113.- All rulings rendered by the Water Tribunals or by the Courts of First Instance on appeal must be notified by the Secretaries of said Tribunals to the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources, through the corresponding Irrigation District, within a period not exceeding eight days.

Art. 114.- The records of contraventions to the prescriptions of this law drawn up by the Water Police Agents shall be held as true until proven false.

CHAPTER IX
GENERAL PROVISIONS.

Art. 115.- In every river, stream, or other source of water where there are intake points for canals belonging to one or several persons, and when those canals can be unified, the General Directorate of Hydraulic Resources may order the unification as canals of the State.

Art. 116.- The construction works of canals that are not executed within the time indicated by the construction permit may be continued, provided that an extension has been requested and previously obtained from the Director General of Hydraulic Resources. Once this extension has expired, without the works having been completed, said permit shall be declared without effect, requiring a new application in accordance with Article 59 to continue the work.

Art. 117.- The waters granted for its use, or for the irrigation of a specific parcel, may not be applied to other uses or another parcel, without obtaining the corresponding permit.

Art. 118.- The waters obtained from public sources may not be sold for any purpose, except as an accessory to the sale of the lands to which the water rights correspond, subject to the provisions of Article 52 of this Law.

Art. 119.- All surplus irrigation waters proceeding from seepage, as well as drainage, may be necessary and be under the Public Administration when it deems it necessary, and be served to those who request such waters, observing the prescriptions established in this Law.

Paragraph: In the cases of this article, the Public Administration may not guarantee the permanence or the flow of these residual waters.

Art. 120.- The rights over waters for irrigation or industrial purposes shall not affect the rights of persons who live on the banks of rivers and springs or those who, even living at a distance, use their waters for domestic purposes or for the use of their animals.

Art. 121.- Municipal works for the purpose of using waters for the direct consumption of people or for the production of power shall also have priority over applications from private individuals or corporations with analogous purposes.

Art. 122.- No person may dirty or contaminate the waters of rivers, streams, canals, or other sources of public waters, intended for any authorized use under this Law.

Art. 123.- Articles 78, 79, 80, 82, 83, 84, 85, and 86 of this Law shall be applied to private irrigation, without prejudice to the other obligations established in this law for all who use public waters for irrigation purposes.

Art. 124.- The lands which, as of the date of this law, are held by water titles, shall not require new titles.

Art. 125.- The exemptions from water rights, the payments stipulated for the use of the same, and all other rights, obligations, and limitations relative to the use of public waters that were the subject of contracts celebrated with private individuals by the Executive Power, and approved by the National Congress, shall be governed by said contracts and the provisions of this law shall not be applicable to them.

Paragraph: In no case may the contracts signed by the Executive Power with private individuals prejudice or extinguish pre-existing rights.

Art. 126.- The infractions of this law, for which a sanction is not specially provided, shall be punished separately or jointly with the following penalties:

a) A fine of six to five hundred pesos;
b) Correctional imprisonment of six days to six months.

Art. 127.- This Law repeals Laws 4916 of November 22, 1909, 127 of November 14, 1942, and their modifications, as well as any other legal provision that is contrary to it.
