**ACT**
of 20 July 2017
**Water Law**¹⁾

**TITLE I**
**General Principles**

**Chapter 1**
**General provisions**

Art. 1. The Act shall regulate water management in accordance with the principle of sustainable development, in particular the formation and protection of water resources, the use of waters, and the management of water resources.
Art. 2. The Act shall regulate matters of ownership of waters and land covered by waters, as well as the principles of managing these components as property of the State Treasury.
Art. 3. The provisions of the Act shall apply to inland waters and marine internal waters.
Art. 4. The provisions of the Act shall apply to the territorial sea waters with regard to planning in water management, protection against pollution from land-based sources, and flood protection, and in the remaining scope – in cases specified in the Act.
Art. 5. The provisions of the Act shall apply to the exclusive economic zone of the Republic of Poland in cases specified in the Act.
Art. 6. The provisions of the Act shall not apply to marine internal waters and territorial sea waters to the extent that the use of these waters and the land covered by them is regulated by the provisions of the Act of 21 March 1991 on the maritime areas of the Republic of Poland and the maritime administration (Journal of Laws of 2023, item 960).

Art. 7. 1. To the extent regulated by the provisions of the Act of 9 June 2011 – Geological and Mining Law (Journal of Laws of 2023, item 633), the provisions of this Act shall not apply to:
1) the prospecting and exploration of groundwater;
2) brines, medicinal waters, and thermal waters;

Art. 8. The provisions of the Act shall not apply to water services for the storage, treatment, or distribution of surface and groundwaters and the collection of wastewater, which are covered by the provisions of the Act of 7 June 2001 on collective water supply and collective wastewater disposal (Journal of Laws of 2023, item 537).
Art. 9. 1. Water management shall be conducted while observing the principle of rational and comprehensive treatment of surface and groundwater resources, taking into account their quantity and quality.
2. Water management shall take into account the principle of common interests and shall require cooperation between public administration, water users, and representatives of local communities to the extent that allows for the achievement of maximum social benefits.
3. Water management shall be based on the principle of cost recovery for water services, which includes environmental and resource costs, as well as economic analysis.
4. Water management shall be conducted in accordance with the public interest, preventing any avoidable deterioration of the ecological functions of waters and the deterioration of the state of terrestrial ecosystems dependent on water.
Art. 10. The management of water resources shall serve to meet the needs of the population and the economy, as well as to protect waters and the environment associated with these resources, particularly with regard to:
1) ensuring an appropriate quantity and quality of water for the population;
2) protection against flood and drought;
3) protection of water resources against pollution and improper or excessive exploitation;
4) maintaining or improving the status of aquatic and water-dependent ecosystems;
5) providing water for agricultural and industrial needs;
6) creating conditions for the use of waters for energy, transport, and fisheries;
7) satisfying needs related to tourism, sport, and recreation.
Art. 11. The instruments of water resources management shall include:
1) water permits;
2) planning in water management;
3) fees for water services and other charges;
4) control of water management;
5) the water management information system.
Art. 12. The management of water resources shall be implemented taking into account the division of the state into river basin districts, water regions, and catchment areas.

**Chapter 3**
**Waters and bodies of water**

Art. 18. Waters are divided into surface waters and groundwaters.
Art. 19. Waters, excluding marine internal waters and territorial sea waters, are inland waters.
Art. 20. Surface waters are the waters of the territorial sea, marine internal waters, and inland surface waters.
Journal of Laws -15- Item 1478
Art. 21. Inland surface waters are divided into inland flowing waters and inland standing waters.
Art. 22. Inland flowing waters are waters in:
1) natural watercourses and the springs from which these watercourses originate;
2) lakes and other natural water bodies with a continuous or periodic natural inflow or outflow of surface waters;
3) artificial reservoirs located on flowing waters;
4) canals.
Art. 23. 1. Inland standing waters are inland waters in lakes and other natural water bodies not directly connected, in a natural way, to surface inland flowing waters.
2. The provisions on inland standing waters shall apply accordingly to waters in land depressions created as a result of human activity, which are not ponds.
Art. 24. For the purposes of water management, including planning in water management, waters are divided into bodies of water, taking into account:
1) bodies of surface water, including bodies of:
a) transitional or coastal waters,
b) artificial or heavily modified waters;
2) bodies of groundwater, with the designation of groundwater in balance areas, which are hydrogeological units designated to determine renewable and available groundwater resources, together with an assessment of the degree of development of these waters, in accordance with the provisions issued on the basis of Art. 97(1)(3) of the Act of 9 June 2011 – Geological and Mining Law.
Art. 25. Transitional waters are surface waters at the mouth of rivers or in the vicinity of river mouths which, due to their proximity to saline waters, are partly saline but which remain substantially influenced by freshwater flows, and the marine internal waters of the Bay of Gdańsk.
Art. 26. 1. Coastal waters shall comprise the area of surface waters from the shoreline, the outer boundary of which is determined by a distance of one nautical mile seaward from the baseline referred to in Art. 5(2) of the Act of 21 March 1991 on the maritime areas of the Republic of Poland and the maritime administration, excluding the marine internal waters of the Bay of Gdańsk and the adjacent territorial sea waters.
2. If the extent of transitional waters is greater than the belt of coastal waters, the outer boundary of this extent shall constitute the outer boundary of the coastal waters.
Art. 27. The provisions of Art. 22, items 2 and 3 shall not apply for the purposes of examining and assessing the status of surface waters, conducted as part of water monitoring.
Art. 28. The Council of Ministers shall, by way of a regulation, determine the boundaries between inland surface waters and marine internal waters and territorial sea waters, guided by the economic importance and the manner of use of these waters.

**Chapter 4**
**Water monitoring**

Art. 349. 1. The purpose of water monitoring is to obtain information:
1) on the status of surface waters and groundwaters and on the status of waters in protected areas for the purposes of planning in water management and assessing the achievement of the environmental objectives specified in Art. 56, Art. 57, Art. 59, and Art. 61;
2) on the environmental status of marine waters for the purposes of planning in water management and assessing the achievement of environmental objectives for marine waters, as well as for the current assessment of the environmental status of marine waters.
2. The examination and assessment of the status of surface waters, the status of groundwaters, the environmental status of marine waters, and the status of waters in protected areas shall be carried out as part of the state environmental monitoring system.
