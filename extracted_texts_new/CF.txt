TITLE I: GENERAL PROVISIONS

CHAPTER 1: DEFINITIONS

Art. 1: For the purposes of this Law, the following definitions shall apply:

Water administration: The Ministry in charge of water.

Regulatory agency: The body responsible for the proper execution of the public water service.

Hydraulic development: The organization of water resources in a given area according to the socio-economic activities of the population.

Potable water supply: The set of processes intended to provide potable water, from mobilization, transport, and treatment to distribution.

Sanitation: The action of collecting, evacuating, discharging, or destroying liquid or solid waste, rainwater, and any other substances harmful to health and the environment.

Users' association: A group of natural or legal persons organized for the use of the water service and the development of water resources.

Self-producer: A natural or legal person with means of production not for the purpose of public service but for its own needs.

Authority: Any holder of public power at the national, regional, or local level.

Dam: A structure intended to retain water, made of a solid mass consisting of a simple embankment, a rockfill mass, or masonry.

Hydraulic basin: The whole of a region where all surface runoff passes through a common point called an outlet.

Hydrogeological basin: An aquifer domain in which groundwater flows towards the same outlet or group of outlets.

Watershed: A surface whose topography determines the flow of surface water towards a point of convergence.

Water abstraction: A device for withdrawing or diverting water from a spring, a lake, a watercourse, or from groundwater.

Delegation: An action by which the State entrusts, by contract, to any public or private natural or legal person the management of all or part of the public water service.

Public domain: All land properties belonging to the State.

Water: A transparent, odorless, colorless, and tasteless liquid in its pure state.

Shared water: Water that separates or crosses two or more territories.

Potable water: Water intended for consumption whose physicochemical and bacteriological characteristics are not harmful to the health of consumers.

Surface water: All water that flows or stagnates on the earth's surface.

Groundwater: All water contained in reservoir rocks in the subsoil.

Sacred water: Water considered or used, with or without its contents, by a community and which calls for absolute respect, worthy of adoration and veneration.

Operator: A natural or legal person under public or private law responsible for management.

Classified establishments: Establishments presenting dangers or inconveniences to the safety, health, or convenience of the environment.

Environmental impact assessment: A set of procedures used to evaluate the effects of an activity on the environment before its realization or execution and to propose any measures or actions to eliminate, reduce, or mitigate the harmful effects likely to be generated by this activity.

Water source: Point of emergence of groundwater.

CHAPTER 2: OBJECTIVES, FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES, AND SCOPE OF APPLICATION

Section 1: Objectives and fundamental principles

Art. 2: This Law aims to:

plan in a coherent manner the use of water resources at both the watershed and national levels;

mobilize and manage water resources in order to guarantee the conditions for sustainable development through rational use while preserving the interests of present and future generations;

protect water from all forms of pollution and preserve aquatic ecosystems;

develop water resources as economic resources according to the order of priority for the various uses of water, as defined in Article 44 of this Law;

develop and protect hydraulic developments and works;

establish an institutional framework that defines the role of stakeholders and the financial mechanisms;

Special Edition Water Code and Decrees on the Organization and Functioning of Regulatory Agencies

improve public finances by alleviating the burden of the water sector through an equitable sharing of costs among the partners concerned: public authorities, private sectors, communities, civil society, and users.

Art. 3: The Central African Republic adheres to the principles of integrated water resources management, which are:

precaution and prevention: preliminary measures must aim to avoid or reduce the risks of nuisance and waste of water resources;

participation: decision-making must involve decision-makers and a maximum number of actors;

planning and cooperation: public authorities, international institutions, non-governmental organizations, and individuals shall cooperate to protect water resources at all levels;

the polluter-pays principle: the activities of natural or legal persons likely to cause direct or indirect damage to aquatic environments are subject to taxes and/or fees;

the abstractor-pays principle: all water users are subject to taxes and/or fees.

Section 2: Scope of application

Art. 4: This Law applies to:

the legal regime for water, hydraulic developments, and works;

the protection regime for water, hydraulic developments, and works;

the management regime for water, hydraulic developments, and works;

the policing of water, to infractions, and to sanctions.

Art. 5: The following are subject to the provisions of this Law:

public or private natural and legal persons carrying out an activity related to water resources;

classified establishments in accordance with the legislation in force;

unclassified establishments, works, and activities carried out for domestic or other purposes, by any natural or legal person.

TITLE II: LEGAL REGIME FOR WATER, DEVELOPMENTS, AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

CHAPTER 1: COMMON PROVISIONS

Section 1: The public water domain

Art. 6: Water is part of the public hydraulic domain. Its mobilization, management, development, and protection must be carried out in respect of natural balances and the general interest.

It may only be subject to appropriation under the conditions determined by the Laws and regulations in force.

Art. 7: For the purposes of this Law, the public hydraulic domain consists of:

Water resources, including:

springs and watercourses within the limits determined by the high-water mark before overflow and, concerning navigable or floatable watercourses, a 25-meter wide zone from these limits on each bank of the islands;

lakes and ponds within the limits determined by the level of the highest waters before overflow, as well as a 25-meter wide zone from these limits on each bank of the islands;

groundwater aquifers.

Hydraulic developments and works installed on the public domain, including:

navigation canals and their towpaths, irrigation canals and

drainage works carried out for a public utility purpose, as well as their appurtenances;

conduits and sewers, river ports, lighting and beaconing works, as well as their appurtenances;

Hydraulic dams, water abstraction works, and any other work declared of public utility for the use of hydraulic forces.

Art. 8: The following are not part of the public hydraulic domain:

rainwater that has fallen on private property;

swimming pools, ponds, artificial water reservoirs built by private persons on private property;

ponds and marshes created by rainwater or the overflowing of watercourses onto private property;

springs emerging on private property.

Art. 9: In the event of established public necessity, the local authority may make the water resources mentioned in Article 8 available to the public, by orders setting the terms of this provision.

Art. 10: The public hydraulic domain includes the public hydraulic domain of the State and that of the territorial authorities.

The definition and nomenclature of the waters, developments, and hydraulic works of the public hydraulic domain of the State and that of the territorial authorities are subject to the provisions of the Domain and Land Code.

Art. 11: In case of water accumulation or construction of hydraulic developments and works on private property, the operator is required to declare its capacity, nature, and purpose to the water administration. The conditions for the artificial accumulation of water and the private realization of hydraulic developments and works, as well as their categorization, are defined by Decree issued in the Council of Ministers.

Section 2: Use of water, developments, and hydraulic works

Art. 12: Withdrawals from the waters of the public hydraulic domain are subject, as the case may be, to authorization or prior declaration.

The conditions for declaration and authorization provided for in this article are defined by regulatory acts.

Art. 13: Authorization is granted, subject to the rights of third parties, for a fixed term after a preliminary investigation.

Art. 14: The authorization may be withdrawn or modified by the water Authority with compensation:

in the interest of public health or the necessity of supplying potable water;

to prevent or stop floods or in case of a threat to public safety;

to preserve the aquatic environment from threats or when the environment is subject to critical hydraulic conditions incompatible with its preservation;

it may be withdrawn or modified without compensation, after formal notice has been sent in writing to the interested party;

when the object of the authorization has not begun to be executed within a period of one year;

when the developments or works are abandoned or are no longer subject to regular maintenance;

in case of non-observance of the conditions prescribed in the authorization.

Art. 15: Decisions to refuse, withdraw, or modify an authorization must be justified by the water administration.

The conditions for the application of this article are defined by regulation.

Art. 16: Any operation or installation intended for the use of water resources for the general interest encumbers intermediate land with a right-of-way servitude,

CHAPTER 2: LEGAL REGIME FOR WATER

Art. 17: Water resources include:

atmospheric water;

surface water;

groundwater.

Section 1: Atmospheric water

Art. 18: Atmospheric water belongs to the person who receives it on their private property.

The artificial accumulation of water falling on private property entitles the owner of the property to use and dispose of it, provided that:

this water remains on this property;

its use complies with the prescriptions enacted by the Laws and regulations in force.

Art. 19: The accumulation of rainwater must not infringe upon the rights of third parties or constitute risks to public health. Works intended to collect rainwater must be designed in such a way that their conservation does not entail harmful consequences for the surrounding properties.

Art. 20: Every property owner must, under penalty of sanctions, construct works to facilitate the drainage of rainwater from their property to the public drainage canals.

The sanctions referred to in this article are defined by regulation.

Section 2: Surface water and groundwater

Art. 21: Surface water and groundwater remain, subject to the recognition of acquired rights by the competent authorities, the heritage of the Central African State.

No one has the right, without prior authorization, to prevent the free flow of surface water or groundwater.

Art. 22: All mobilization and exploitation of surface and groundwater are subject to the authorization and declaration regime in accordance with the provisions of Articles 12 and 13 of this Law.

Art. 23: The owners of properties on which a source is located may use it to satisfy their personal needs in compliance with the provisions of this Law.

Any exploitation for commercial purposes must be subject to an authorization issued by the water administration in collaboration with the other stakeholders concerned.

CHAPTER 3: REGIME FOR DEVELOPMENTS AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

Art. 24: The location, realization, and exploitation of hydraulic developments and works are subject, as the case may be, to authorization or prior declaration, in accordance with the provisions of Articles 12 and 13 of this Law.

Art. 25: The administrative declaration must mention the characteristics of the hydraulic infrastructure to be realized.

Art. 26: The realization of hydraulic developments and works, according to their categorization, may be subject to a prior environmental impact assessment, carried out in collaboration with the competent ministerial departments whose technical opinions are necessary and mandatory.

Art. 27: The following are subject to prior authorization: installations, developments, works, and activities likely to:

hinder navigation;

present dangers to public health and safety;

harm the free flow of water;

alter the quality;

reduce the quantity of water resources;

TITLE III: MANAGEMENT OF WATER RESOURCES, DEVELOPMENTS, AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

CHAPTER 1: THE INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK

Art. 29: The State is the institutional guarantor of the integrated management of water resources.

As such, it defines:

the legislative framework (Water Code...);

the regulatory framework and ensures its application (water policy, Master Plans, National Action Plan for Integrated Water Resources Management "IWRM"...).

Art. 30: The water administration provides technical oversight for the sector.

As such, it is responsible for:

implementing the sector's policy and strategies in collaboration with the other actors involved;

developing regulations and application texts;

mobilizing financing for development in collaboration with other institutions;

granting all prior authorizations for the exercise of activities covered by this Code.

Art. 31: The integrated management of water resources is carried out according to the principles that distinguish the manager from the user.

The manager's mission is to update and implement the national water policy and to ensure the good practice of integrated water resources management.

Users have the rights and obligations recognized by the competent authorities in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

Art. 32: The institutional framework is organized according to the principle of decentralization, which takes into account the:

national level;

watershed level;

local level.

Art. 33: A National Council for Water and Sanitation, abbreviated CONEA, is hereby created, responsible for ensuring the oversight of water resources management structures.

CONEA is a joint body composed of representatives of the State, elected officials, communities, Non-Governmental Organizations, Water Users' Associations, and specialized institutions.

With a view to integrating effective participation of actors at the grassroots level, the Council may be decentralized at the watershed level.

The organization and functioning of CONEA are defined by a Decree issued in the Council of Ministers.

Art. 34: A National Agency for Water and Sanitation, abbreviated ANEA, is hereby created.

The organization and functioning of ANEA are established by a Decree issued in the Council of Ministers.

Section 1: Role of the manager

Art. 38: The water administration ensures the integrated management of water resources while preserving their quality and quantity.

It guarantees:

the supply of potable water;

the satisfaction of other needs.

It ensures the promotion of the realization and protection of hydraulic developments and works, and the prevention of and fight against nuisances of hydraulic origin.

It exercises, through its competent services, the policing of water.

In this capacity, it receives declarations and requests for prior authorization relating to the use of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works.

Art. 39: If there is a risk of pollution or destruction of the natural environment, or a threat to public health and the potable water supply, the water administration shall take and enforce the necessary measures required by the circumstances.

Section 2: Rights and obligations of users

Art. 40: The Law recognizes every citizen's right to have access to water corresponding to their needs and to the elementary requirements of their life and dignity.

The Minister in charge of water proposes and implements, in conjunction with the competent public authorities, the measures necessary for the exercise of this right.

Art. 41: Any operator or user of water resources whose rights have been duly recognized by the competent authorities is subject, as the case may be, to the polluter-pays and abstractor-pays principles.

Art. 42: Any person who has knowledge of an incident or an accident presenting a danger to public safety, the quality, circulation, or conservation of water resources, has the obligation to inform the competent authority thereof without delay.

The circumstances of the incident or accident, as well as the measures to be taken to remedy it, are brought to the attention of the population by the authorities through all appropriate means.

Art. 43: The occupants of a watershed or water users may form a water users' association to:

develop and operate watersheds;

enhance water resources;

ensure the protection of water resources, works, and hydraulic developments;

defend the rights recognized to users and operators of water resources.

Art. 44: The supply of potable water to the population remains the priority in the allocation of water resources.

establish, after approval from the conceding authority, all works useful for the operation of the conceded domain;

Art. 51: Without prejudice to specific clauses, the forfeiture of the delegatee may be declared in the following cases:

use of water other than that authorized;

non-compliance with obligations relating to the protection of water quality and the preservation of the environment;

non-payment or non-remittance of fees.

In case of forfeiture of the delegatee, the water administration, in collaboration with the competent ministries, may order the restoration of the site to its original state and have the work carried out ex officio at the expense of the delegatee.

Art. 52: In the case where the operator, the producer, or the self-producer supplies a water distribution network, the technical and tariff conditions are defined by a contract approved by the Regulatory Agency for the Water and Sanitation Sector.

However, the operation, production, and self-production installations must be realized in accordance with the standards of the public water service.

withdrawals made by third parties for commercial or industrial purposes;

CHAPTER 1: COMMON PROVISIONS

Art. 65: Any activity likely to pollute water, alter its quality, and degrade hydraulic developments and works is subject to regulatory measures by the water administration.

Art. 66: Protection standards are established concerning:

the quality and quantity of water;

the design, implementation, and protection of hydraulic developments and works.

Art. 67: The protection perimeters for water resources, developments, and hydraulic works include immediate, close, and remote protection perimeters.

The limits of these perimeters are determined by Decree and may be modified if circumstances so require.

Art. 68: No unauthorized activity may be carried out within the protection perimeters.

The discharge of wastewater and other waste likely to harm the quality of the water, degrade the developments and hydraulic works is prohibited within the protection perimeters.

Art. 69: The authors of an incident or accident, the operators or owners who have become aware of a danger or damage to the aquatic environment, are obliged to take or have taken at their own expense the necessary measures to stop the danger.

Without prejudice to the compensation of victims for other damages suffered, the persons who have intervened materially or financially to stop the danger are entitled to reimbursement by the authors, co-authors, or accomplices of the incident or accident, for the costs incurred by them.

CHAPTER 2: PROTECTION OF WATER RESOURCES

Art. 70: Water resources are subject to quantitative and qualitative protection.

Section 1: Quantitative protection

Art. 71: Any withdrawal or diversion of surface water that may temporarily or permanently alter its course, harm its free flow, or reduce its bed may not be carried out without prior authorization from the water administration after advice from the National Council for Water and Sanitation.

Art. 72: Apart from non-equipped traditional wells, any withdrawal of groundwater is subject to prior authorization from the water administration.

Art. 73: The modalities, conditions of withdrawal, and quantity of water are established by regulation.

Section 2: Qualitative protection

Art. 74: Potable water as defined in Article 1 of this law is that which comes from a public water treatment and distribution station or from modern water points. Any water of whatever origin is considered a priori non-potable and may not be used for activities related to human consumption.

Art. 75: The standards for water potability are established by a joint Order of the Ministers in charge of Water and Public Health in accordance with the recommendations of the World Health Organization.

Art. 76: Anyone who offers water to the public for human consumption, for a fee or free of charge, in whatever form, is required to ensure that this water is potable, under penalty of law.

The use of non-potable water is prohibited for the preparation and preservation of all foodstuffs intended for human consumption.

Art. 77: In the case of public distribution of potable water, the distributing service or the delegatee must verify at all times that the standards of the physicochemical and bacteriological parameters that determine the potability of the water are respected.

Distribution networks must be compulsorily and systematically disinfected before being put into service, in accordance with the conditions established by a joint Order of the Ministers in charge of Water and Public Health, under penalty of sanctions.

Additional disinfection measures must be taken after development and repair work or during operation to guarantee the quality of the water.

The following are responsible for verifying water quality:

the Ministries in charge of Water and Public Health;

other specialized structures.

Art. 78: In the absence of a public potable water distribution system, the use of public or private sources and wells for human consumption is only authorized if all precautions are taken to protect them from contamination due to the proximity of latrines, septic tanks, manure deposits, garbage, filth, and other sources of pollution.

Water must be drawn using an appropriate device to avoid its pollution.

Art. 79: Prohibited are the flows, discharges, direct or indirect releases or deposits of dangerous substances likely to alter the quality of surface and groundwater or to destroy the aquatic environment.

Art. 80: It is forbidden to deposit or bury animal carcasses, butchery debris, manure, fecal matter, and all residues in surface waters or in the bed of a watercourse.

Section 3: Sacred waters

Art. 81: The sacred character of certain waters is recognized. The recognition of the sacred character of waters is made by regulation by the water administration in concert with the other competent public authorities.

The protection of sacred waters is the responsibility of the leaders of the concerned community under the control of the State, which may, if the general interest justifies it, take special measures.

CHAPTER 3: PROTECTION OF DEVELOPMENTS AND HYDRAULIC WORKS

Art. 82: Access to classified hydraulic developments and works is strictly forbidden to any person foreign to the service.

Art. 83: The protection perimeters of hydraulic developments and works are marked by live hedges or by signs bearing the words "Access forbidden".

It is forbidden to carry out activities within the protection perimeters that are likely to damage the works intended for the exploitation of water resources.

Art. 84: It is forbidden, except in cases of force majeure, to:

degrade or have degraded, destroy or remove hydraulic developments and works;

damage the temporary works carried out for the construction or maintenance of the works referred to above.

Art. 85: Classified or unclassified establishments, works, and developments that are sources of pollution must be subject to exceptional protection measures to avoid any risk of contact with living beings, food, or drinking water.

Art. 86: Hydraulic developments and works of national interest are subject to special measures.

The water administration, in agreement with the Ministries in charge of Defense and Security, shall ensure the protection of these hydraulic developments and works by the Defense and Security Forces.

TITLE V: POLICING OF WATER RESOURCES

CHAPTER 1: INFRACTIONS, INVESTIGATION, AND PROSECUTION

Section 1: Infractions

Art. 87: The following are considered infractions within the meaning of this code:

the exercise of activities in the water sector without official authorization;

non-compliance with the verification of standards for hydraulic infrastructure and the quality of drinking water;

O.J.C.A.R. / OCTOBER 2008
13
Special Edition Water Code and Decrees on the Organization and Functioning of Regulatory Agencies

obstruction of the exercise of control missions;

fraudulent importation and/or exportation of water;

false declarations;

any act likely to pollute water;

any act of wasting water resources;

any violation of the provisions relating to the qualitative and quantitative protection of water resources;

non-compliance with the conditions and objects of the authorization for the use of water and the realization of hydraulic developments and works.

Section 2: Investigation and prosecution

Art. 88: Responsible for investigating infractions of the provisions of this Law, gathering evidence, and seeking out the authors are: officers and agents of the judicial police, sworn civil servants and agents duly appointed for this purpose by the Ministries in charge of Water, Public Health, and the Environment.

A Decree issued in the Council of Ministers shall establish the modalities for the application of this article.

Art. 89: In the exercise of their functions, sworn agents and civil servants may request the assistance of the public force.

They have access to the installations and places where the operations giving rise to the infractions are carried out.

They shall draw up reports of their findings, a copy of which must be sent to the Public Prosecutor's Office.

Art. 90: Without prejudice to the prerogatives of the Public Prosecutor, actions and prosecutions are exercised by the Minister in charge of water before the competent jurisdictions.

Art. 91: The agents and civil servants referred to in Article 89 may have access to private residences in the presence of the owner or witnesses upon requisition by the competent judicial authorities.

In cases of flagrante delicto and under the conditions provided for by law, the agents and civil servants referred to in Article 89 may order the cessation of work and confiscate objects and products whose use constitutes an infraction.

CHAPTER 2: ADMINISTRATIVE AND PENAL SANCTIONS

Section 1: Administrative sanctions

Art. 92: In case of emergency, the water administration, as soon as it becomes aware of an infraction, shall order the necessary measures to stop the damage.

Outside of this hypothesis, it must, under penalty of abuse of power, await the decision of the judicial authorities.

Art. 93: When an infraction is noted, the Minister in charge of water may propose a settlement, the amount of which may not be less than the minimum fine provided for the infraction.

In the absence of a settlement, the procedure shall be pursued before the competent courts.

Art. 94: Any violation of the provisions of Articles 72, 74, 82, and 83 of this Law shall be punished by a fine of 300,000 to 3,000,000 FCFA.

Section 2: Penal Sanctions

Art. 95: Any industrialist who has not observed the regulations on effluents shall be punished by imprisonment of one (1) year to five (5) years and a fine of 1,000,000 to 20,000,000 FCFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

Art. 96: Anyone who has diverted from its purpose, wasted, or fraudulently used water resources shall be punished by imprisonment of one (1) month to three (3) months and a fine of 20,000 to 200,000 CFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

Art. 97: Anyone who, by acts of violence, has opposed the execution of works authorized by the Government shall be punished by imprisonment of three (3) months to one (1) year and a fine of 102,000 to 1,000,000 FCFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

Art. 98: Anyone who has fraudulently modified, altered, or degraded water distribution installations shall be punished by imprisonment of three (3) months to three (3) years and a fine of 102,000 to 1,000,000 FCFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

Art. 99: Anyone who has installed a well for human consumption in the vicinity of latrines, septic tanks, manure deposits, garbage, filth, and other sources of pollution shall be punished by imprisonment of one (1) month to six (6) months and a fine of 10,000 to 100,000 FCFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

Art. 100: Anyone who has violated the provisions of Article 77 of this code shall be punished by imprisonment of two (2) months to two (2) years and a fine of 200,000 to 1,000,000 FCFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

Art. 101: Anyone who has imported, produced, exported, or marketed mineral or table waters not in conformity with the standards in force shall be punished by imprisonment of two (2) months to one (1) year and a fine of 1,000,000 to 10,000,000 FCFA, or by only one of these two penalties.

The Judge shall order the confiscation and destruction of these products.

In case of recidivism, the penalty is doubled.

Art. 102: Infractions and sanctions not provided for in this Code are subject to a referral to the provisions of the Penal Code.

TITLE VI: TRANSITIONAL AND FINAL PROVISIONS

CHAPTER 1: TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS

Art. 103: Until the creation of the new water resources management structures instituted by this Law, the management of water resources, developments, and hydraulic works is ensured by the General Directorate of Hydraulics.

Art. 104: Pending the development of national standards for water potability, the applicable standards are those of the World Health Organization.

Art. 105: Installations, works, developments, and activities presenting the characteristics described in Article 26 and existing before the entry into force of this Law must be modified or destroyed by the owners or public authorities.

Art. 106: The owners of works and installations of any nature subject to authorization under this Law are required to act diligently within a period of two (2) years from the date of entry into force of this Law, by declaring the existence of these works.

Art. 107: Rights of ownership, usufruct, or use legally acquired over the public hydraulic domain prior to the entry into force of this Law are maintained.

The holders of the rights mentioned above are required to declare to the water administration the existence of such prior rights within a period of three (3) years from the date of entry into force of this Law.

The owners whose rights have been duly recognized may only be dispossessed by way of expropriation for reasons of public utility.

Art. 108: Owners having a source of water on their property and who have exploited this source for lucrative purposes prior to the entry into force of this Law are required to declare them to the authorities in charge of water within a period of two (2) years from the date of entry into force of this Law.

Art. 109: Owners of existing discharge installations prior to the publication of this Law must take all necessary steps to satisfy, within a period of one (1) year, the conditions required for the protection of waters, developments, and hydraulic works.
