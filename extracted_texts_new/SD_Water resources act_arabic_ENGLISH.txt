***In the name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful***

**The Water Resources Act of 1995 (22/7/1995)**

### Chapter One: Preliminary Provisions

**1. Title of the Act**
This Act shall be cited as "The Water Resources Act of 1995."

**2. Repeal and Saving**
The Nile Pumps Control Act of 1939 is hereby repealed; provided that all regulations issued thereunder shall remain in force as if they had been issued under the provisions of this Act, until they are repealed or amended in accordance therewith.

**3. Interpretation**
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:
"**Non-Nile Rivers**" means rivers whose waters do not connect to the Nile.
"**Embankments**" means those natural, or which are constructed by man on both sides of the course of the Nile, non-Nile rivers, or other watercourses, between which water flows, or those that are constructed in any specific cases to protect lands and properties adjacent to the said watercourses.
"**Other Watercourses**" means the courses of any Nile tributaries, wadis, khors, springs, lakes, or swamps from which water flows or in which it is confined, whether permanently or seasonally, and includes artificial watercourses.
"**The Council**" means the National Water Resources Council established under the provisions of Article 4(1).
"**The Licensee**" means the person to whom the Ministry grants the necessary license to draw water, or the person licensed to share with the license holder in the use of the drawn water.
"**Groundwater**" means water present in the various layers of the earth, whether near the surface of the earth or deep.
"**Water Resources**" means transboundary and non-transboundary surface and groundwater resources.
"**Surface Water**" means water present on the surface of the earth, whether it be the waters of the Nile, non-Nile rivers, or other watercourses.
"**Transboundary Water**" means surface or groundwater that crosses the international borders of Sudan or the borders of any two adjacent states.
"**The Nile**" means the Main Nile, formed by its two tributaries, the Blue Nile and the White Nile, and the Atbara River, and the tributaries of those rivers.
"**The Ministry**" means the Ministry of Irrigation and Water Resources.
"**The Minister**" means the Minister of Irrigation and Water Resources.

### Chapter Two: The National Water Resources Council

**4. Establishment, Domicile, and Supervision of the Council**
(1) A council to be called "The National Water Resources Council" shall be established, and it shall have a corporate personality, perpetual succession, a common seal, and the right to sue and be sued in its own name.
(2) The headquarters of the Council shall be in Khartoum State.
(3) The Council shall be under the supervision of the President of the Republic.

**5. Composition of the Council**
The Council shall be formed by a decision of the President of the Republic, under the chairmanship of the Minister, and with the membership of a number of members who possess suitable qualifications and sufficient experience and knowledge in water resources affairs, and in which all bodies relevant to such affairs shall be represented.

**6. Competencies and Powers of the Council**
(1) Notwithstanding the provisions of any other law, the Council shall have, in particular, the following competencies and powers:
    (a) Formulating the general policy for water resources, including their inventory, assessment, and the rationalization of their management and use; averting the effects of natural disasters resulting from drought and floods; protecting these resources from pollution and degradation; and developing them in an integrated and balanced manner with other natural resources, in a way that ensures their maximum utilization and achieves the desired coordination and cooperation with other competent authorities.
    (b) Developing a long-term national program for the optimal and balanced use of water resources, while determining priorities.
    (c) Submitting the necessary decisions and recommendations concerning:
        (i) Water resources shared with other countries.
        (ii) The bases for cooperation with international organizations for the purpose of attracting national and international funding sources to implement programs for the development, protection, and conservation of water resources.
    (d) Periodically reviewing legislation related to water resources to ensure it is keeping pace with the development and protection of water resources, and submitting recommendations to the competent authorities in this regard.
    (e) Encouraging scientific research in the field of water resources.
    (f) Forming specialized committees to assist it in performing its duties.

(2) Taking into account the total volume of water available from Sudan's share of international waters under agreements with other countries, as well as the volume of water available from various national water resources, the Council shall have competence over the following matters:
    (a) General supervision of the drawing of water from the Nile, non-Nile rivers, other watercourses, and groundwater for the purposes of irrigation, drinking, industry, hydropower generation, sanitation, and otherwise; and the distribution and use of such water, including determining the area of land to be cultivated and the installations to be erected for the use of water for various purposes; and protecting watercourses and water from pollution by means of sanitary, industrial, and agricultural chemical drainage, and treating drainage water, in coordination with the Higher Council for Environment and Natural Resources and the competent authorities.
    (b) Establishing the bases for the following:
        (i) Granting, renewing, and revoking licenses for drawing water from the Nile, non-Nile rivers, other watercourses, or groundwater, and licenses for sharing in the use of water drawn by the public or cooperative sector.
        (ii) Allocating any specified quantities of surface or groundwater, constituting a percentage or part of the total national surface or groundwater quantity, to any of the states, whether generally or for any specific purpose or purposes, and drawing the water so allocated for any specific purpose or purposes in the use of the drawn water.
        (iii) Distributing available water equitably for use for various purposes in a manner consistent with its optimal use.
        (iv) Regulating the use of water for various purposes in a manner that ensures it is not polluted, in coordination with the competent authorities.
        (v) Conducting the necessary inspections to ensure effective supervision over the use of surface or groundwater licensed to be drawn for any purpose.
        (vi) Establishing dams, hydropower generation plants, and works for protection, sanitary and industrial drainage, irrigation water, drinking water, and others, in coordination with the competent authorities.
        (vii) Regulating the drilling of deep and shallow wells, and conducting the necessary studies to determine groundwater quantities, their movement, and the method of their recharge.

**7. Meetings of the Council**
(1) The Council shall meet at least twice a year to conduct its ordinary business.
(2) The Chairman or one-third of the members may call the Council for an extraordinary meeting on urgent matters.
(3) In the absence of the Chairman, the members shall elect one from among them to assume the chairmanship.
(4) The legal quorum for the Council's meetings shall be constituted by the attendance of half of the members.
(5) The Council's decisions and recommendations shall be passed by a majority vote in accordance with the regulations it issues.
(6) The Council may invite any person to attend any session without having the right to vote.

**8. General Secretariat of the Council**
(1) The Council of Ministers shall, by its decision, appoint a full-time Secretary-General to undertake the duties of the General Secretariat of the Council, which consist of administrative, clerical, and financial work, recording the minutes of meetings, preparing the necessary information and data on water resources and analyzing them, preparing the Council's agenda, recording the proceedings of meetings, preserving documents, and any other competencies assigned to him by the Chairman of the Council.
(2) The decision of the Council of Ministers shall determine the remuneration and allowances of the Secretary-General based on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance and National Economy, the Minister of Labor, Public Service and Human Resource Development, and the Higher Council for Wages.(2)

### Chapter Three: Finance, Accounts, and Audit

**10. Financial Resources of the Council**
The financial resources of the Council shall consist of the following:(1)
    (a) Appropriations allocated to it by the National Government.(3)
    (b) Donations, grants, and exemptions.
    (c) Any other resources approved by the President of the Republic.

**Budget of the Council**(4)
(1) The Council shall have an independent budget prepared in accordance with sound accounting principles.
(2) The Council shall approve the budget proposals prepared by its General Secretariat.

**11. Deposit of Funds**
The Council shall deposit its funds in current accounts or deposit accounts at the Bank of Sudan or any bank approved by the Central Bank of Sudan, provided that disbursements from such accounts shall be made in the manner specified by the regulations.(5)

**12. Accounts and Audit**
(1) The Council shall keep regular and accurate accounts of its revenues and expenditures in accordance with sound accounting principles.
(2) The National Audit Chamber, or whomever it delegates, shall audit the accounts of the Council after the end of each fiscal year.(6)

### Chapter Four: Provisions Related to Water Resources

**13. Right to Use Water**
(1) The State is the owner of the right to the flow, regulation, control, and use of water, and all matters related to water resources.
(2) The public shall have the right to use water for various purposes in accordance with the provisions of this Act and any other relevant law.

### Chapter Five: Special Provisions on Licensing

**14. Requirement to Obtain a License**
(1) No person, other than an officer designated by the Ministry for that purpose, shall:
    (a) use a pump or other means to draw water from the Nile, non-Nile rivers, other watercourses, or groundwater for irrigation, drinking, sanitary or industrial drainage, or otherwise, unless he obtains the necessary license from the Ministry.
    (b) enter into any agreement for the use by any person of any of the waters he is licensed to draw for any purpose, unless he obtains prior approval for that from the Ministry.
(2) Subject to the provisions of sub-article (1), the Ministry may issue either of the following two types of licenses for drawing and using water:
    (a) a license to draw water.
    (b) subject to the provisions of paragraph (b) of sub-article (1), a license to share in the use of the water licensed to be drawn.
(3) The regulations shall specify the procedures to be followed in granting the two licenses provided for in sub-article (2), and the conditions and restrictions under which they are issued, signed, revoked, and renewed.
(4) For the purposes of sub-articles (1) and (2), the Ministry must verify the ownership or lease by the license applicant of the land for which water is intended to be drawn for irrigation, as well as the intended type of use for the water licensed to be drawn.

**15. Non-liability of the Ministry for Acts of the Licensee**
The Ministry shall not be liable for any act carried out by the licensee under the license granted to him.

**16. Validity of Previous Licenses**
(1) Every person holding a license to draw water or to share in the use of drawn water, which is valid and in force on the date this Act comes into effect, shall have the right to continue to operate under that license until its term expires.
(2) In the case of licenses that have not expired when this Act comes into effect, the Ministry may, by means of a written notice of six months, call upon the licensees to submit applications for licenses in accordance with the provisions of this Act.
(3) Subject to the provisions of Article 13, the Ministry shall issue new licenses in accordance with the provisions of this Act to every licensee whose license term has expired after this Act came into effect, as well as to everyone who submits an application in accordance with the provisions of sub-article (2). If the licensee does not submit any application within the period specified in sub-article (2), the license he holds shall be considered null and void.

**17. Validity of Previous Agreements and Concessions**
No provision in this Act shall have any effect on any of the terms of any agreement or concession, other than a license, which is in force between the Government of Sudan and any person, granting that person the right to draw water or to enter into any other agreement with others to share in the use of the drawn water for irrigation, drinking, or any other purposes.

**18. Competencies and Powers of the Ministry**
(1) The Ministry shall have the following competencies and powers:
    (a) Issuing licenses for drawing water from the Nile, non-Nile rivers, other watercourses, or groundwater, and licenses for sharing in the use of the drawn water.
    (b) Monitoring the implementation of licensed installations for drawing water from the Nile, non-Nile rivers, or other watercourses.
    (c) Monitoring the flow of the Nile, non-Nile rivers, and other watercourses, as well as monitoring the quantities and movement of groundwater, provided that no other body may carry out this work except after obtaining the approval of the Ministry or pursuant to the provisions of international agreements.
    (d) Determining the areas for groundwater extraction for the purposes of irrigation, drinking, and any other purposes.
    (e) Subject to the provisions of Article 6(2)(b)(v), carrying out the necessary inspections to ensure the use of the drawn water for its specified purposes.
    (f) Ensuring compliance with the crop rotation cultivated on the lands irrigated by the water for which the license was granted.
    (g) Carrying out, at the expense of the licensee, any obligation imposed upon him by the granting of the license which he refuses to carry out.
    (h) Imposing licensing fees and any other services it provides in accordance with the provisions of this Act, and determining the body to which such fees shall accrue, with the approval of the Minister of Finance and National Economy.(7)
(2) The Ministry may delegate any of its powers contained in sub-article (1) to any body within any of the states.
(3) Without prejudice to any of the following acts:
    (a) Issuing licenses for vessels for inland river navigation, in accordance with the provisions of Article 9 of the Inland River Navigation Regulation Act of 1993; provided that if any accident occurs to any vessel, whether by collision, sinking, or cessation of navigation due to lack of water, its owner or captain must, after completing the procedures for reporting that accident, notify the Ministry to ensure the removal of that vessel or its wreckage, and in case of his refusal, the Ministry may do so at his expense.
    (b) Issuing licenses for the establishment of moorings for vessels on the banks of the Nile, non-Nile rivers, or any other watercourse, in accordance with the provisions of Article 10(c) of the Inland River Navigation Regulation Act of 1993.
    (c) Issuing licenses for brick kilns on the banks of the Nile, non-Nile rivers, or other watercourses, in accordance with the provisions of Article 7(2) of the Mines and Quarries Act of 1972.*

**19. Prohibition of Certain Acts**
No person shall carry out any of the following acts:
    (a) Erecting dams on the Nile, non-Nile rivers, other watercourses, or over groundwater, except with the approval of the Minister.
    (b) Cultivating state-owned land located within the embankments, except with permission from the Ministry issued according to the conditions it specifies.
    (c) Erecting any installations between the embankments except with permission from the Ministry issued in accordance with the conditions it specifies.
    (d) Subject to the provisions of any other law, cutting trees or palms that were or are planted on land adjacent to the embankments owned by individuals.

**20. Powers of the Minister**
(1) In the event of an unusually high rise in the water levels of the Nile, non-Nile rivers, or other watercourses, the Minister may exercise any of the following powers:
    (a) Declare the existence of a state of public danger threatening citizens residing on both sides of the Nile or those rivers or watercourses.
    (b) Take urgent measures to protect the embankments and existing water installations from danger, in consultation with the Minister of Interior, the Minister of Finance and National Economy, and the governor of the concerned state or governors of the concerned states.(8)
(2) In the event of a scarcity of water in the Nile, non-Nile rivers, other watercourses, or groundwater, or if it is insufficient to meet the requirements of the licenses, the Minister, or whomever he delegates, shall have the authority to distribute the water among the license holders in the manner he deems equitable, provided that the license holders are notified of this distribution in writing, and the Minister's decision in this regard shall be final.

### Chapter Six: Final Provisions

**21. Offenses and Penalties**
Anyone who contravenes any of the provisions of this Act or the regulations issued thereunder shall be deemed to have committed an offense, and shall be punished with imprisonment for a term not exceeding six months or a fine, or with both penalties, without prejudice to any more severe penalty to which he may be liable under the provisions of any other law.

**22. Stoppage or Removal of Work**
The competent court must, upon a request from the Minister in the case of transboundary water, or the governor of the concerned state in the case of non-transboundary water, order at any stage of the proceedings the stoppage of any work in contravention of the provisions of Article 19, or its removal.

**23. Power to Issue Regulations**
The Minister may, in consultation with the Council, issue the regulations necessary for the implementation of the provisions of this Act.
