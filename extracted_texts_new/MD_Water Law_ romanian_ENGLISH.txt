Republic of Moldova PARLIAMENT LAW No. LP272/2011 of 23.12.2011 on Water Published: 26.04.2012 in the OFFICIAL MONITOR No. 81 art. 264 Date of entry into force AMENDED by LP64 of 23.04.20, MO115-117/15.05.20 art.203; in force 14.06.20 The Parliament adopts this organic law. This law, partially harmonized with the Council Directives: No. 91/271/EEC of 21 May 1991 concerning urban waste-water treatment and No. 91/676/EEC of 12 December 1991 concerning the protection of waters against pollution caused by nitrates from agricultural sources, with the Directives of the European Parliament and of the Council: No. 2000/60/EC of 23 October 2000 establishing a framework for Community action in the field of water policy; No. 2006/7/EC of 15 February 2006 concerning the management of bathing water quality; No. 2007/60/EC of 23 October 2007 on the assessment and management of flood risks; No. 2008/105/EC of 16 December 2008 on environmental quality standards in the field of water policy, creates the necessary legal framework for the management, protection, and use of water. Chapter I GENERAL PROVISIONS Article 1. Purpose of the law The purpose of this law is to: a) create a regulatory framework for the monitoring, assessment, management, protection, and efficient use of surface waters and groundwater based on public participation in assessment, planning, and decision-making; b) establish water use rights and promote investments in the water sector; c) establish mechanisms for the protection of the status of waters, the prevention of any further degradation of waters, the protection and restoration of the aquatic environment, and the gradual and systematic convergence of their protection and management with European requirements; d) prevent further deterioration and protect and enhance the status of aquatic ecosystems and, with regard to their water needs, of terrestrial ecosystems and wetlands directly depending on the aquatic ecosystems; e) ensure a sufficient supply of good-quality surface water and groundwater as necessary for sustainable, balanced, and equitable water use; f) establish a legal basis for international cooperation in the field of joint management and protection of water resources. Article 2. Main definitions For the purposes of this law, the following main terms shall have the following meanings: *aquifer* – an underground layer or layers of rock or other geological strata of sufficient porosity and permeability to allow either a significant flow of groundwater or the abstraction of significant quantities of groundwater; *agglomerations* – areas where the population and/or economic activities are sufficiently concentrated for urban and rural wastewater to be collected and conducted to a treatment plant or to a final discharge point; *surface waters* - inland waters, except groundwater; standing water and flowing water on the surface of the ground; *groundwater* - all water which is below the surface of the ground in the saturation zone and in direct contact with the ground or subsoil; *wastewater* - water originating from domestic, social, and economic activities, containing pollutants or residues that alter its initial physical, chemical, and bacteriological characteristics; *environmental authorization for special water use* - a document issued by the institution subordinated to the central public administration authority for the environment, the holder of which has the right to special water use under certain conditions, in accordance with the provisions of this law; *river basin* - the area of land from which all surface run-off flows through a sequence of streams, rivers and, possibly, lakes into the sea at a single river mouth, estuary or delta, delimited by the watershed line; *water basin* - a temporary artificial water body, filled by pumping or by runoff, intended for irrigation and/or other purposes, except for fish reproduction and farming; *water balance* - a set of calculations for water resource management, carried out during the preparation of schemes and projects, which determines the ratio between available water resources (the volume of surface and groundwater available for guaranteed multi-annual use) and the water consumption calculated at the forecasted level of economic development; *State Water Cadastre* - a state information system in which data on water resources, water resource management, hydrotechnical constructions, protected areas, the water balance, and the land of the water fund are collected, processed, and stored; *environmental quality standard for water* - the concentration of a particular pollutant or group of pollutants in water, sediment or biota which should not be exceeded in order to protect human health and the environment; *river basin district committee* - the coordinating and advisory body of a river basin district; *hydrotechnical constructions* - constructions intended for the use of water resources for various purposes, their protection, as well as the prevention of the destructive effects of water, including those containing pollutants; *artificial water body* - a surface water body created by human activity; *surface water body* - a discrete and significant element of surface water such as a lake, a reservoir, a pond, a stream, river or canal, a part of a stream, river or canal, or transitional waters; *body of groundwater* - a distinct volume of groundwater within an aquifer or aquifers; *watercourse* - a body of water, a component of the hydrographic network, which flows naturally through linear negative landforms. A watercourse can be permanent, intermittent, or ephemeral; *ephemeral watercourse* - a watercourse in which water flow is recorded only during periods of atmospheric precipitation and/or snowmelt, as well as for a short period after the respective phenomenon ends; *intermittent watercourse* - a watercourse in which no water flow is recorded for more than 2 months within a year; *permanent watercourse* - a watercourse in which water flow is recorded for more than 10 months within a year; *ecological flow* - the minimum flow in a section of a watercourse necessary to ensure the living conditions of existing aquatic ecosystems; *river basin district* – the area of land in the Republic of Moldova, made up of one or more neighbouring river basins together with their associated groundwater, identified as the main unit for management of river basins; *fish pond (heleșteu)* - an artificial water body, formed by diking an area adjacent to a watercourse and intended for the reproduction and farming of fish; *pond (iaz)* - an artificial water body formed by damming water or by diverting a watercourse, intended for fish farming, irrigation, etc., with a water volume at the normal retention level of up to one million m³; *floods* – the temporary covering by water, from the extraordinary overflow of high flood waters or from abundant precipitation, of a portion of land not normally covered by water; *lake* - a body of standing surface water that has no connection to the global ocean; *reservoir* - an artificial water body constituting a water reserve, with possible use for various purposes, with a water volume, at the normal retention level, of over one million m³; *river basin management* - a system for managing the water resources within a river basin; *water pollution* - the direct or indirect introduction, as a result of human activity, of substances or heat into the air, water or land which may be harmful to human health or the quality of aquatic ecosystems or terrestrial ecosystems directly depending on aquatic ecosystems, which results in damage to material property, or which impairs or interferes with amenities and other legitimate uses of the environment; *pollutant* - any substance liable to cause pollution; *stream (pârâu)* – a permanent or intermittent watercourse, fed by atmospheric precipitation and groundwater, having a length of less than 10 km and a catchment area of less than 50 km²; *water resources* - surface waters, groundwater, and atmospheric precipitation fallen on the territory of the Republic of Moldova; *flood risk* - the combination of the probability of a flood event and of the potential adverse consequences for human health, the environment, cultural heritage and economic activity associated with a flood event; *hydrographic network* - the totality of watercourses (permanent, intermittent, and ephemeral) and lakes (natural and anthropic) within a given territory; *river* - a permanent or intermittent watercourse, fed by atmospheric precipitation and groundwater, which flows through the channel it has formed, having a length of more than 10 km and a catchment area of more than 50 km²; *drought* - a temporary deficiency of surface and groundwater as a result of climate changes; *sub-basin* - the area of land within a river basin district from which all surface run-off flows through a network of streams, rivers and lakes to a particular point in a watercourse within that river basin district; *hazardous substances* - substances or groups of substances that are toxic, persistent and liable to bio-accumulate, and other substances or groups of substances which give rise to an equivalent level of concern; *land of the water fund* - land under water, the beds of watercourses, the basins of lakes, ponds, reservoirs, marshes, land on which hydrotechnical constructions and other water service structures are located, land allocated for the deviation strips (on the banks) of rivers, water basins, main canals and collectors, as well as land used for the construction and operation of facilities that ensure the satisfaction of needs for drinking water, technical water, curative water, and other public interest needs; *emission limit values* – the mass, expressed in terms of certain specific parameters, concentration and/or level of an emission, which may not be exceeded during one or more periods of time; *protection zone* - a water protection zone for rivers and water basins, as defined in Law No. 440/1995 on protection zones and strips for rivers and water basins; *protected area* - an area designated for the protection of surface waters and groundwater or for the conservation of habitats and species directly depending on water; *sensitive area* - a water body, identified as a sensitive area, which includes: a) natural freshwater lakes, other freshwater bodies and estuaries which are found to be eutrophic or which may become eutrophic in the near future if protective action is not taken; b) surface freshwaters intended for the abstraction of drinking water which could contain a nitrate concentration greater than that provided for by the environmental quality standards for surface waters, established by the Government; *wetlands* - areas of marsh, fen, peatland or water, whether natural or artificial, permanent or temporary, with water that is static or flowing, fresh, brackish or salt, the depth of which does not exceed 6 m; *vulnerable zones* - areas of land which drain into surface waters and/or groundwater and which cause their pollution by nitrates from agricultural sources. Article 3. Scope of the law (1) This law regulates: a) the management and protection of surface waters and groundwater, including measures for flood prevention and control, erosion control, and measures to counteract drought and desertification; b) activities that have an impact on surface waters and groundwater, including the abstraction and use of water, the discharge of wastewater and pollutants, and other activities that could harm water quality. (2) The following activities are also regulated by special laws: a) the use of natural mineral and medicinal mineral waters, including thermal waters; b) the safety of dams and other hydrotechnical structures; c) the establishment of water users' associations for irrigation, as well as the transfer and use of irrigation infrastructure by such associations; d) fishing and aquaculture, with the exception of pollutant discharges from aquaculture facilities and the introduction of hazardous substances into water regulated by this law; e) ensuring the supply of water, the discharge of wastewater, and water treatment services for the population, commerce, and industry. Article 4. Water ownership regime (1) Water is a renewable, vulnerable, and limited natural resource, an indispensable element for life and society, a determining factor in maintaining ecological balance, a raw material for productive activities, a source of energy, and a means of transport. (2) Water is not a commercial product like any other, but a natural heritage which must be protected, treated, and defended as such. (3) Water is part of the public domain of the state. (4) Any natural or legal person has the right to use water under the conditions of this law. (5) The land under a pond may be part of either the public or private domain. Any natural or legal person may build a pond on land they own, without damming watercourses and only under the conditions of the legislation in force. (6) The land of the water fund is an indivisible asset. (7) A reservoir, pond, and fish pond are considered indivisible water bodies consisting of the following assets: the land on which the water body is located, the riparian water protection strip, and the hydrotechnical constructions. Article 5. Management of water resources (1) The efficient management of the water resources of the Republic of Moldova is carried out based on the Nistru river basin located on the territory of the Republic of Moldova and the Danube-Prut and Black Sea river basin located on the territory of the Republic of Moldova, called river basin districts. (2) The river basin district is the main unit for the management of river basins and their associated groundwater. (3) The river basin districts in the Republic of Moldova are: a) the Nistru river basin district; b) the Danube-Prut and Black Sea river basin district. (4) Sub-basins may be formed within the river basin districts. (5) The boundaries of the river basin districts, the boundaries of the sub-basins, and the special maps in which they are determined shall be approved by the Government. Article 6. Principles of water resource management The management of water resources is fundamentally based on: a) the principle of participation of water users, central and local public authorities, civil society, and other interested parties in the planning and decision-making processes regarding the use and protection of water resources; b) the "polluter pays" principle, according to which the costs of preventing pollution or decontaminating water resources are borne by the polluter; c) the precautionary principle, which implies that where there are risks of serious or irreversible damage to water resources, the lack of full scientific certainty shall not be used as a reason for postponing measures; d) the principle of sustainable water use, in the sense that the needs of present and future generations must be taken into account in the use and protection of waters; e) the principle of the economic value of water, in the sense that the economic value of water resources and their management must be recognized through the introduction of mechanisms for recovering the costs of water resource management. Chapter II STATE REGULATION AND MANAGEMENT OF WATER USE AND PROTECTION Article 7. The Government (1) In the field of water use and protection, the Government shall: a) coordinate the activity of all public authorities with responsibilities in the water sector; b) approve the regulatory framework for the implementation of this law; c) undertake other actions provided for by law. (2) The Government shall coordinate the activities to be undertaken under this law and may delegate certain powers, established in this law, to the central public administration authorities and other authorities with responsibilities in the water sector. Article 8. The central public administration authority for the environment The central public administration authority for the environment is responsible for implementing the state policy in the field of water resource management and shall: a) develop policies, normative acts, and plans to be approved by the Government, as well as the necessary amendments to bring the legislation into conformity with European requirements; b) carry out the monitoring of the status and use of water resources and maintain the State Water Cadastre; c) establish the subordinated institution whose responsibilities include issuing environmental authorizations for special water use and maintaining the Register of environmental authorizations for special water use (hereinafter referred to as the competent institution); d) ensure, jointly with the Ministry of Health, Labour and Social Protection, compliance with the legislation on water quality and protection; e) encourage and promote the participation of civil society and all interested parties in the debate on certain water-related projects of national interest; f) ensure international cooperation and coordinate the implementation of activities resulting from international treaties; g) coordinate external donations and manage investments in the water sector; h) undertake other measures provided for by law. Article 9. The administrative authority for water management (1) The administrative authority for water management shall undertake measures to implement legislation in the field of water protection and management and shall: a) participate in the development and implementation of policies, programs, plans, and measures in water management; b) manage the hydrotechnical constructions that are in the public domain; c) maintain the surface water bodies, protection zones, and water protection strips, according to Art. 50 para. (3); d) exercise other responsibilities established by legislation, as well as those delegated by the central public administration authority for the environment. (2) The manner of organization and operation of the administrative authority for water management shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government. Article 10. The river basin district committee (1) For each river basin district, the central public administration authority for the environment shall form a committee, composed of representatives of central and local public administration authorities, sub-basin committees, water users' associations, civil society, and the scientific community. (2) The river basin district committee has the following basic responsibilities: a) to consult the central public administration authority for the environment on the development, modification, and completion of the River Basin District Management Plan, including determining water use priorities within the river basin district, developing measures and determining their implementation deadlines and planned results, and to develop proposals and recommend funding priorities; b) to participate in monitoring the implementation of the River Basin District Management Plan; c) to coordinate the process of creating and operating sub-basin committees, to approve sub-basin management plans, and to monitor their implementation; d) to consult the central public administration authority for the environment and the administrative authority for water management on the development of flood risk and drought risk management plans; e) to facilitate the exchange of information in the field between public authorities, water users, and civil society; f) to participate in transboundary cooperation on the management of shared river basins. (3) The method of establishment and operation of the river basin district committee, its structure, and responsibilities shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government. (4) To exercise its responsibilities, the river basin district committee shall have a permanent technical secretariat, the structure and responsibilities of which shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government. Article 11. Local public administration authorities The local public administration authorities have the following responsibilities: a) to maintain and manage the surface water bodies, protection zones, and water protection strips under their management, according to Art. 50 para. (4); b) to delegate their representatives to the river basin district committee; c) to inform civil society and interested parties about certain aspects of the water sector, including restrictions and prohibitions on water use; d) to create, in collaboration with public associations, sub-basin committees for small rivers; e) to carry out other tasks provided for by law. Article 12. Collaboration and public access to information on water (1) The central public administration authority for the environment, directly or through its subordinated institutions with water management responsibilities, shall support the participation of public and private institutions, non-governmental organizations, the media, water users' associations, citizens, and other interested parties in carrying out measures for the rational use and protection of water, by encouraging: a) active collaboration in the examination of various issues subject to public debate; b) the initiation of public education campaigns to foster a caring attitude towards water; c) the organization of public control over the implementation of water protection measures; d) the reporting of cases of violation of legislation in the field, as well as cases whose effects are likely to cause pollution, waste, or depletion of waters; e) the submission of proposals for optimizing the use and protection of waters; f) involvement in works to optimize the use and protection of waters, as well as in the prevention and liquidation of the destructive effects of water; g) the undertaking of other actions intended to ensure the rational use and protection of waters. (2) Any natural or legal person has the right to consult registers, plans, lists, and any other public information in accordance with the legislation on access to information. Chapter III DATA MANAGEMENT. POLICIES AND PLANNING Article 13. Monitoring of water resources (1) The systematic monitoring and recording of the status of surface waters, groundwater, and protected areas shall be carried out by the central public administration authority for the environment, through its subordinated institutions, in the manner established by a regulation approved by the Government. At drinking water intakes, irrigation water intakes, and in recreational areas, monitoring shall be carried out jointly with the Ministry of Health, Labour and Social Protection. (2) The regulation mentioned in para. (1) shall specify the parameters to be monitored, the procedures and technical measures necessary, including but not limited to, for sampling and compliance with analysis requirements for each parameter, for quality control, for laboratory practices, and for data recording. (3) Owners of riparian lands are obliged to allow authorized representatives of the central public administration authority for the environment, without charge, to undertake the following actions on their land: a) the passage or movement of personnel with official duties in water management for the purpose of their fulfillment; b) the transportation and temporary storage of materials and equipment, their movement and that of personnel, experimental pumping, in the case of hydrogeological drillings that are part of the national monitoring network; c) the placement, in the channel and on the banks, of markers, benchmarks, measuring and control devices, or other apparatus or installations necessary for the study of waters, as well as access for the maintenance of installations intended for these activities; d) access for carrying out scientific research work on the ground. (4) The damage caused by the actions provided for in para. (3) letters a) and b) shall be compensated from state budget allocations or from the account of the authorized persons who caused the damage, and that caused by the actions in letters c) and d) shall be compensated in another manner provided for by law. Article 14. The State Water Cadastre (1) The State Water Cadastre is maintained by the central public administration authority for the environment through the administrative authority for water management. The State Water Cadastre collects, processes, and stores data on monitored water resources, the hydrographic network, water bodies, hydrotechnical constructions, protected areas, protection zones and riparian water protection strips, as well as data on water abstractions and discharges, the water balance, river basin management, the land of the water fund, and other relevant information. (2) The structure and content of the State Water Cadastre shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government. Article 15. The Register of environmental authorizations for special water use (1) The Register of environmental authorizations for special water use is maintained by the competent institution. The Register contains data on the holders of authorizations, the type of authorizations, the date of issuance, the term of validity, the location of the authorized activity, and other entries regarding the issuance, modification, extension, suspension, termination, or withdrawal of authorizations. The documents on the basis of which the entries were made in the Register of environmental authorizations for special water use shall be kept on file. (2) The structure and content of the register mentioned in para. (1) shall be established in a regulation approved by the Government. Article 16. Transparency of information from the State Water Cadastre and the Register of environmental authorizations for special water use (1) Information from the State Water Cadastre and the Register of environmental authorizations for special water use is public. (2) Any natural or legal person has the right to study and receive data from the State Water Cadastre and the Register of environmental authorizations for special water use in accordance with the legislation on access to information. Article 17. National policy documents on water resources (1) National policy documents on water resources shall be approved in accordance with the legislation. These documents must contain provisions regarding: a) the quantity and quality of existing water resources and water bodies, and their use by all sectors of the economy; b) the volume of unused flow of water resources, taking into account environmental needs and international treaties on water use; c) short-, medium-, and long-term water requirements for each main sector of the economy; d) investment priorities, taking into account the need to ensure efficient water supply and sanitation services for the urban and rural population, as well as compliance with applicable European requirements and environmental objectives for water; e) special measures for the prevention and control of surface water and groundwater pollution; f) priority measures to promote adaptation to the effects of climate change, including the identification and combating of water scarcity, taking into account the long-term imbalance between water demand and supply, as well as the increased risk of drought and floods; g) programs of measures for meeting the environmental quality standards for water and/or for achieving the environmental objectives for water, in line with those of the European Union; h) specific deadlines for the achievement of certain objectives, measures, actions, requirements, etc. (2) The central public administration authority for the environment shall carry out the strategic environmental assessment procedure and promote the active participation of civil society in the development of national policy documents on water resources by publishing documents and informing all interested parties, providing everyone with the opportunity to comment and make recommendations on the content of these documents. (3) The central public administration authority for the environment shall ensure the publicity of national policy documents on water resources through the national media. Article 18. National policy documents on wastewater (1) National policy documents on wastewater shall be approved in accordance with the legislation and shall contain provisions regarding: a) the design, financing, construction, commissioning, and operation of wastewater collection systems and treatment plants in urban localities, as well as the timeframe within which the Republic of Moldova must comply with the requirements applicable in the European Union in this field; b) the identification and implementation of measures that would provide cost-effective wastewater treatment in rural localities, so as to reduce and minimize water pollution, and that would protect the drinking water supply, as well as the design, financing, construction, commissioning, and operation of local wastewater collection systems, treatment plants, and the implementation of appropriate alternative processes, technologies, and methods. (2) The central public administration authority for the environment or, as the case may be, its territorial subdivision shall carry out the strategic environmental assessment procedure and promote the active participation of civil society in the development of national policy documents on wastewater. Article 19. The river basin district management plan (1) The river basin district shall be managed on the basis of a plan developed by the central public administration authority for the environment, in consultation with the river basin district committee, and approved by the Government. (2) The river basin district management plan provides for measures to implement national policy documents on water resources and includes: a) an assessment of the quality and quantity of water resources within the river basin district; b) an assessment of the risk of water scarcity, drought, floods, pollution, and dam failure within the river basin district, and an assessment of the costs of preventing, reducing, or mitigating such risks; c) the identification of areas within the river basin district where there is a risk of pollution from diffuse sources; d) an analysis of existing protected areas and protection zones established in accordance with the legislation, and the identification of the need to establish new areas or zones or to modify existing ones; e) priorities for special water use, measures to address the risks and problems identified in letters b) and c), and existing and future standards; f) other relevant aspects. (3) The procedure for developing the plan mentioned in para. (1) and (2), the strategic environmental assessment procedure, as well as the procedure for its review, shall be established by the central public administration authority for the environment and approved by the Government. (4) The river basin district management plan shall be reviewed every 6 years. Article 19¹. Protected areas (1) Protected areas shall be identified and mapped by the administrative authority for water management and, respectively, by the competent authority for public health surveillance, in the manner established by a regulation approved by the Government. (2) At the level of each river basin district, the registration and records of protected areas shall be carried out through the Register of Protected Areas, a component of the State Water Cadastre, which includes the following types of protected areas: a) areas intended for the abstraction of drinking water from surface and groundwater, which have an average flow of more than 10 m³ per day or which serve more than 50 people, as well as from water bodies that can be used in this way in the future; b) areas intended for the protection of aquatic species of economic importance - stagnant water bodies or watercourses, habitats of indigenous species, which maintain biodiversity and whose existence is important for water resource management; c) water bodies intended for recreation, including those identified as bathing waters; d) nutrient-sensitive areas, including vulnerable zones and sensitive areas, especially those in agglomerations without wastewater treatment plants, those where discharges of insufficiently or improperly treated wastewater occur, and those where there are no systems for the biological treatment of wastewater, identified based on a methodology approved by the Government; e) areas designated for the protection of habitats or species, where the maintenance or improvement of the status of water is an important factor for their protection, including areas important for the Emerald network and wetlands of international importance. (3) The Register of Protected Areas is established and maintained by the central public administration authority for the environment, through the administrative authority for water management, in accordance with Law No. 71/2007 on registers. (4) Information on protected areas shall be included in the River Basin District Management Plan, at each revision, and shall be supplemented with maps indicating the location of each protected area, as well as the provisions of national legislation on the basis of which the respective areas were identified. (5) The method of collecting, processing, storing, and using the data from the Register of Protected Areas, as well as the responsibility of the public authorities with responsibilities in the field for providing the information and data necessary for establishing and maintaining the register, shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government. (6) The environmental objectives for protected areas are established in accordance with the provisions of Art. 38 para. (1) and (6) and involve the implementation of measures with a direct or indirect impact on the status of protected areas, such as measures to control water abstractions, measures to promote efficient and sustainable water use, and measures to control diffuse pollution. (7) The measures provided for in para. (6) shall be included in the River Basin District Management Plan, developed in accordance with Art. 19. Article 19². Hydrotechnical constructions (1) Depending on their purpose, hydrotechnical constructions are classified as: a) dams; b) flood protection dikes; c) water pumping stations; d) irrigation systems: water basins and pressure pipelines; e) hydroelectric power plant buildings; f) artesian wells; g) storage facilities for liquid waste from industry and agriculture. (2) The registration and records of hydrotechnical constructions shall be carried out through the Register of Hydrotechnical Constructions, a component of the State Water Cadastre. (3) The Register of Hydrotechnical Constructions is established and maintained by the central public administration authority for the environment, through the administrative authority for water management, in accordance with Law No. 71/2007 on registers. (4) The structure, content, method of collecting, processing, storing, and using the data from the Register of Hydrotechnical Constructions, as well as the responsibility of the public authorities with responsibilities in the field for providing the information and data necessary for establishing and maintaining the respective register, shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government. Article 20. Informing and consulting civil society (1) The central public administration authority for the environment, directly or through its subordinated institutions with water management responsibilities, shall encourage the active participation of all interested parties in the development, review, and updating of the river basin district management plan by publishing and submitting for public debate: a) the timetable and work program for the development of the plan, including a statement of the consultation measures to be taken; b) an interim overview of the significant water management issues identified in the river basin district; c) draft copies of the river basin district management plan or other relevant information. (2) The information mentioned in para. (1), the reference documents, and the information used for the development of the river basin district management plan shall be made available to applicants free of charge, upon request. (3) The deadline for consultation and for submitting written comments and objections on the documents under debate must be at least 6 months. Chapter IV WATER USE AND AUTHORIZATION OF WATER USE Article 21. Water use (1) The following activities are considered water use: a) the abstraction of water from a surface water body or a body of groundwater, its transport, and subsequent use; b) the impoundment or storage of water behind a dam or other hydrotechnical constructions and installations; c) the collection, treatment, and discharge of wastewater; d) the diversion, restriction, or modification of water flow in a water body; e) the modification of the channel, banks, course, or characteristics of a water body; f) the construction of permanent structures on the land of the water fund. (2) Water users are required to: a) use water rationally and economically; b) take measures to protect waters against pollution; c) respect the rights of other water users; d) keep records of the water used and report it, according to a regulation approved by the Government. Article 22. General water use (1) The use of water for the following purposes is considered general use and does not require an environmental authorization for special use: a) human consumption and other domestic needs; b) watering animals without the use of permanent structures; c) irrigating home gardens; d) bathing and recreation; e) abstracting and using water for firefighting or in any other emergency situation. (2) Irrigation carried out by a person legally supplied with water through a centralized irrigation system managed by a water users' association for irrigation or by another person holding an environmental authorization for special water use does not require such an authorization. (3) The use of water for bathing and recreation may be limited or prohibited: a) by the central public administration authority for the environment or other authorized public administration bodies in case of imminent danger to human life and health or in a protection zone established in accordance with the legislation; b) by the holder of an environmental authorization for special water use in accordance with the conditions of the authorization. (4) Upon notification from the authorized public administration authority, the central public administration authority for the environment may temporarily limit or prohibit the general use of water if there is an imminent danger to human life and health or a danger to the status of the waters and the ecological balance. (5) Atmospheric precipitation falling on private land may be used by the owner in accordance with the provisions of the Civil Code. (6) The holder of an environmental authorization for special water use is obliged to ensure access to the general use of water indicated in para. (1). (7) The right to general water use does not imply the right of passage through private land except under the conditions provided for by the Civil Code. Article 23. Special water use (1) Water use that does not fall under the scope of Art. 22 is considered special use and may only be carried out on the basis of an environmental authorization for special water use. (2) The following activities are also considered special water use: a) the abstraction of water from surface and groundwater sources for water supply intended for human consumption; b) the abstraction and use of water from surface and groundwater sources for technical and industrial purposes, including in food processing and agro-industry; c) the abstraction and use of water from various sources for irrigation; d) the use of water in aquaculture and fish farming; e) the discharge of wastewater; f) the use of water for generating hydroelectric power; g) the operation of pontoons, wharves, and other hydraulic structures on the land of the water fund; h) the commercial development and operation of beaches and recreational areas. Article 24. Priorities for water use (1) Satisfying the population's need for drinking water and water for domestic needs, and ensuring ecological flows, have priority over the use of water for other purposes. (2) If the river basin district management plan does not specify a different order of priorities for the use and allocation of water, including groundwater, the special use of water shall conform to the following priorities: a) irrigation and animal watering; b) industrial activities, including mining and agro-industry activities; c) fishing and fish farming; d) hydropower generation; e) sports and recreation; f) other fields. Article 25. Application for the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The application for the issuance of an environmental authorization for special water use shall be submitted to the competent institution. Term of the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The environmental authorization for special water use shall be issued for a term of 12 years, except in cases where: a) the authorization is requested for a shorter term; b) the authorization is requested for a long term under para. (2) A long-term environmental authorization for special water use shall be issued for 25 years if the applicant undertakes to make a long-term investment for the purpose of constructing, improving, or rehabilitating: a) a dam for hydropower or other purposes; b) a hydrotechnical construction or facility for the treatment and supply of drinking water. Article 27. Issuance of the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The environmental authorization for special water use, including for the long term, shall be issued free of charge by the competent institution. (2) When examining the application for the issuance of an environmental authorization for special water use, the following shall be taken into account: a) the relevant river basin district management plan; b) the obligations of the Republic of Moldova assumed under international treaties; c) the applicable water use priorities within the relevant river basin district; d) the content of environmental expert reviews or environmental impact assessments required by law; e) the use of the best available techniques; f) written objections, as well as the conclusions of public hearings regarding the application. (3) An environmental authorization for special water use shall not be issued if it would negatively affect: [Art.27 para.(3), letter a) repealed by LP64 of 23.04.20, MO115-117/15.05.20 art.203; in force 14.06.20] b) the environmental quality standards for water mentioned in Art. 37 or the environmental objectives for water mentioned in Art. 38; c) the minimum requirements for ecological flow. (4) The environmental authorization for special water use shall be entered in the Register of environmental authorizations for special water use, in accordance with the provisions of Art. 15. (5) The decision to issue or refuse to issue an environmental authorization for special water use may be challenged in an administrative court, without the need for a preliminary procedure. Article 28. Content of the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The environmental authorization for special water use must specify: a) the purpose for which the water will be used; b) the volume of water that its holder is entitled to abstract, impound, divert, and use, by reference either to a fixed volume or to a share of the flow; c) the volume of the return flow, if applicable, and the place where the return flow will be discharged. (2) The environmental authorization for special water use obliges the holder to comply with the following general conditions: a) the rational use of water for the specified purpose; b) the prevention of water pollution; c) the installation of equipment to measure the volume of water used; d) the provision of information on the volume of water used; e) compliance with discharge standards; f) ensuring sanitary protection zones for the water source; g) compliance with hygienic requirements for water quality. (3) The environmental authorization for special water use obliges the holder to comply with certain special conditions regarding: a) the manner in which the water will be used; b) seasonal or temporal variations in water use; c) taking measures to reduce the effects of pollution and undertaking other environmental protection measures; d) the efficient management and protection of waters. Article 29. Holder of the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The environmental authorization for special water use is a personal document, which cannot be assigned except with the prior consent of the competent institution, with the assignee complying with the provisions of Art. 27 para. (1). Prior consent is not required in case of: a) succession; b) alienation of the land or hydrotechnical construction to which the environmental authorization for special water use refers. (2) An environmental authorization for special water use, assigned according to para. (1), remains valid provided that, within two months from the date of assignment, the assignee registers the assignment with the competent institution. Article 30. Modification and suspension of the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The environmental authorization for special water use may be modified by the competent institution: a) at the request of its holder, including in the case of a substantial change in operation that may have significant effects on the waters; b) temporarily, if it is necessary to apply requirements related to the ecological flow of the water source as a result of a decision declaring a state of drought in accordance with Art. 48. (2) The environmental authorization for special water use may be suspended by the competent institution: a) at the request of the holder; b) in case of failure to comply within a maximum of 30 days with a prescription issued as a result of non-compliance with the authorization conditions; c) if the discharge conditions have not been reviewed in accordance with Art. 42 para. (3) and (4); d) if it is necessary to apply requirements related to the ecological flow of the water source as a result of a decision declaring a state of drought in accordance with Art. 48; e) if it is necessary to protect public health or in other exceptional cases. (3) The competent institution, within 3 days from the date of suspension provided for in para. (2) letters b)-e), shall initiate legal proceedings. The decision of the competent institution regarding the suspension shall apply until a final court judgment is rendered. Article 31. Termination and withdrawal of the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The environmental authorization for special water use shall terminate: a) at the request of the holder; b) upon expiry of its term of validity; c) if the water to which it refers has not been used for 3 consecutive years; d) in case of withdrawal. (2) The competent institution has the right to withdraw an environmental authorization for special water use if it is necessary to reallocate water for another purpose in accordance with public utility and/or the relevant river basin district management plan. (3) In the case of withdrawal provided for in para. (2), the holder of the authorization is entitled to obtain, free of charge, a new authorization for the use of an equivalent volume of water from an alternative source or compensation calculated on the same legal basis as expropriation for public interest. (4) The competent institution shall initiate legal proceedings for the withdrawal of the environmental authorization for special water use if: a) it is found that the holder of the authorization does not comply with the authorization conditions after a previous suspension period of the authorization of a maximum of 30 days; b) the authorization has been assigned without compliance with Art. 29. Article 32. Extension of the environmental authorization for special water use (1) The holder of an environmental authorization for special water use may request the competent institution to extend the authorization in accordance with the procedure for issuing such an authorization. The application for the extension of the environmental authorization for special water use must be submitted at least 3 months before its expiry. (2) The competent institution shall extend the environmental authorization for special water use, except in cases where: a) the order of priorities of the applicable river basin district management plan provides for all or part of the water to be reallocated for another purpose; b) there are other reasons of public interest for rejecting the extension application. (3) The restrictions specified in para. (2) do not apply to the abstraction of water for supply intended for human consumption. Article 33. Land and hydrotechnical constructions for special water use (1) If the land of a water body, the hydrotechnical constructions, and the land on which they are located are necessary for exercising the right of special water use, but are not owned by the applicant for the environmental authorization for special water use, the applicant must obtain the right to use the land and hydrotechnical constructions. (2) The right of ownership and/or the right of use of hydrotechnical constructions and land intended for special water use may be acquired and/or assigned in accordance with the provisions of the Civil Code and the Law on the administration and privatization of public property. (3) The central public administration authority for the environment, directly or through its subordinated institutions, shall initiate legal proceedings for the demolition of unauthorized constructions and the dismantling of unauthorized hydrotechnical installations located on the land of the water fund or on waters. Chapter V WATER PROTECTION Article 34. Prohibition of water pollution (1) The discharge or introduction of pollutants into a surface water body, a body of groundwater, on the lands of the water fund, or on lands from which runoff into a surface water body or a body of groundwater is possible is prohibited, except when carried out in accordance with the wastewater discharge conditions provided for in the environmental authorization for special water use. (2) Specific substances that are introduced into surface waters for fishing or aquaculture purposes, but which do not have a negative impact on the quality of the receiving waters and for which it is not necessary to obtain wastewater discharge conditions, shall be established in a regulation approved by the Government. (3) Sanitary protection zones for water intakes from surface waters and groundwater shall be established according to a regulation approved by the Government. Article 35. Priority hazardous substances The list of priority hazardous substances which, due to their hazardous nature or the increased risk they pose to the environment, cannot be discharged into surface water bodies, groundwater bodies, or on the lands of the water fund, shall be approved by the Government. Article 36. Requirements for the discharge of hazardous substances (1) The requirements for the discharge of hazardous substances, other than the priority hazardous substances provided for in Art. 35, shall be established in a regulation approved by the Government, which shall provide for: a) emission limit values; b) emission controls and technical requirements for the treatment of liquid waste and effluents before their discharge, including the requirement to use the best available techniques; c) monitoring and reporting requirements. (2) The content of the best available techniques shall be approved by the central public administration authority for the environment. Article 37. Environmental quality standards for water (1) Environmental quality standards for water shall provide for, but not be limited to, values for temperature, acidity/alkalinity, dissolved oxygen, and chemical and microbiological parameters. The requirements shall be established in a regulation approved by the Government. The regulation must also contain provisions regarding: a) requirements for monitoring water quality, including methods of measurement, sampling, frequency of analysis and procedures, and data management requirements; b) public disclosure of information on compliance with environmental quality standards for water; c) the classification of waters by reference to the environmental quality standards for water established in accordance with para. (3); d) the prohibition of water use for certain purposes in bodies where the water does not meet the established requirements; e) the imposition of remedial measures to be taken to ensure conformity with environmental quality standards for water, including the development of management and remediation plans through participatory processes involving the public and/or users of the respective waters. (2) Environmental quality standards for water are established in accordance with para. (1) for all water resources, as well as for: a) the use of certain water bodies; b) activities that are authorized or take place in the water bodies referred to in letter a); c) water bodies identified in accordance with para. (3), which require protection in order to support certain activities. (3) The central public administration authority for the environment shall identify the waters or water bodies to which environmental quality standards for water are applicable or which need protection to support certain activities in these areas, in accordance with a procedure established by the Government. (4) The identification carried out in accordance with para. (3) shall be taken into account when reviewing the river basin district management plan. Article 38. Environmental objectives for water (1) Environmental objectives for water with reference to the chemical and/or ecological status and/or the quantitative status of surface waters, groundwater, and protected areas shall be established by the Government. (2) For the purposes of this law: a) the status of surface water means the status of a surface water body determined by the poorer of its ecological and chemical status; b) the status of groundwater means the status of a body of groundwater determined by the poorer of its quantitative and chemical status; c) the chemical status of surface water means the status generated by the concentration of pollutants in surface waters; d) the chemical status of groundwater means the chemical status of a body of groundwater; e) the ecological status means the quality of the structure and functioning of aquatic ecosystems associated with surface waters; f) the quantitative status is the degree to which a body of groundwater is affected by direct and indirect abstractions. (3) The general criteria regarding the status of water for surface water bodies, including rivers, lakes, artificial or heavily modified water bodies, for bodies of groundwater, as well as the necessary definitions, shall be established by the Government. (4) Ensuring the conformity of the status of water for water bodies in each river basin district with the objectives indicated in para. (1) shall be carried out based on measures developed by the central public administration authority for the environment, in consultation with the river basin district committee. The measures must be included in the river basin district management plan. (5) In case of exceptional circumstances of natural cause or force majeure, or if, compared to the anticipated benefit, this would only be possible at a disproportionate cost in relation to technical feasibility, the river basin district committee may request the Government for a derogation from compliance with the environmental objectives for water. (6) The minimum content of the measures established in accordance with para. (4) and the criteria for the derogation granted in accordance with para. (5) shall be established by the Government. Article 39. Requirements for wastewater treatment in urban localities The requirements for the operation of wastewater collection systems in urban localities and for the operation of treatment plants shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government, which must contain provisions regarding: a) the method and degree of treatment to be provided depending on the number of inhabitants/size of the locality served or to be served by a collection system and a treatment plant and/or the quality of the receiving waters into which the treated wastewater is discharged; b) the identification and classification of such receiving waters, designated as sensitive areas; c) the obligation to discharge all industrial wastewater into a collection system in urban localities, which must take place on the basis of an agreement, except in cases justified from a technical, economic, and environmental point of view; d) the conditions regarding the management of sludge resulting from the treatment process; e) the obligation to monitor liquid waste discharges and their effects, in addition to reporting requirements; f) other relevant aspects. Article 40. Requirements for wastewater treatment in rural localities The requirements for the collection, storage, treatment, and discharge of domestic wastewater in rural localities, including the requirements for the operation of local collection systems, alternative treatment plants and processes, and appropriate technologies and processes, shall be established in a regulation approved by the Government. Article 41. Regulation of wastewater discharge (1) If the use of water involves the discharge of wastewater or another process of water pollution, the application for the issuance of an environmental authorization for special water use shall be accompanied by the environmental agreement to the documentation on environmental impact assessment or, as the case may be, the opinion of the state ecological expertise on the project documentation for the discharge process. (1¹) The manner of applying for, granting, suspending, and withdrawing permissive acts for entrepreneurs regarding the water use regime is established by Law No. 160/2011 on the regulation by authorization of entrepreneurial activity, in the part not regulated by this law. (2) When examining the application mentioned in para. (1), the competent institution shall: a) apply the relevant discharge requirements adopted under Art. 36, except where the achievement of the environmental quality standards for water mentioned in Art. 37 and the environmental objectives for water mentioned in Art. 38 imposes more severe conditions and, therefore, stricter emission controls; b) take into account the impact of the requested discharge on other water users; c) consult the authorized bodies in the case of wastewater discharge in the protected areas indicated in Art. 19¹ para. (2). Article 42. Conditions of the environmental authorization for special water use involving wastewater discharge (1) The environmental authorization for special water use involving the discharge of wastewater, in addition to the provisions of Art. 28, must contain: a) emission limit values for each substance whose discharge is authorized; b) a description of the location of the discharge structures and a description of their characteristics; c) the obligation for the authorization holder to monitor, as well as the frequency with which monitoring information is to be provided to the competent institution; d) a description of the treatment or pre-treatment processes to which waste or pollutants must be subjected before discharge; e) a description of the construction, operation, and maintenance of any structure that is necessary for the treatment or pre-treatment of waste and pollutants or that controls the manner or place of their discharge; f) the aspects necessary to minimize or prevent the pollution of water bodies, to achieve environmental quality standards for water and environmental objectives for water, including seasonal or other variations regarding the quantity or concentration of pollutants that may be discharged. (2) The environmental authorization for special water use involving the discharge of wastewater may specify the deadline for the progressive reduction of the quantity and/or concentration of pollutants that may be discharged. (3) The wastewater discharge conditions provided for in the environmental authorization for special water use shall be reviewed every 3 years. (4) The holder of the authorization shall submit an application for review to the competent institution at least 3 months before the expiry of the 3-year term. The application shall be accompanied by a description of the discharge process and a copy of the monitoring reports provided in accordance with para. (1) letter c). (5) Based on the application mentioned in para. (4), the competent institution shall review the wastewater discharge conditions provided for in the authorization, with the aim of improving water quality, and shall modify, when necessary, these conditions to reduce the quantity, type, and/or concentration of discharged pollutants: a) as a result of the introduction of stricter emission limit values into legislation; b) to ensure compliance with environmental quality standards for water and/or with environmental objectives for water; c) by virtue of technological changes regarding the best available techniques. Article 43. Prevention of pollution from agricultural activities (1) The identification of waters which are or could be affected by pollution from agricultural activities and the designation of the areas of land which drain into those waters as vulnerable zones shall be carried out by the central public administration authority for the environment, in consultation with each river basin district committee, based on a regulation approved by the Government. (2) The regulation mentioned in para. (1) must also contain provisions regarding: a) the rules for the use of plant protection products and fertilizers within vulnerable zones; b) the development of a code or codes of good agricultural practice to be implemented by farmers on a voluntary basis, as well as programs to promote the implementation of the codes, including training and information for farmers; c) general action programs or programs applied in certain areas, which may contain mandatory rules on the storage and use of certain plant protection products and fertilizers; d) monitoring the effect of the activities undertaken under this article, as well as the concentration of nitrates in freshwaters. Article 44. Discharge of drainage water from land (1) It is not necessary to obtain wastewater discharge conditions for a drainage system from land used for agricultural, forestry, or horticultural purposes, with the exception of the provisions of para. (2) letter b). (2) If the discharge of drainage water from land could cause serious pollution of waters or in order to comply with the environmental quality standards for water mentioned in Art. 37 or the environmental objectives for water mentioned in Art. 38, by a regulation approved by the Government: a) technical requirements for the treatment of drainage water shall be established, including for the creation and use of special ponds, constructed wetlands, and filters; and/or b) it shall be required that such discharges take place only under discharge conditions. Chapter VI USE AND PROTECTION OF GROUNDWATER Article 45. Use of groundwater (1) The use of groundwater with potable properties for purposes other than drinking and domestic water supply is prohibited. (2) In areas where there are no necessary surface water sources, but there are sufficient reserves of groundwater, including with potable properties, its use for purposes other than drinking and domestic water supply is permitted based on regulations approved by the Government. (3) The use of groundwater shall be carried out only on the basis of an environmental authorization for special water use. (4) It is not necessary to obtain an environmental authorization for special water use if the use of phreatic water falls under the general use of water according to Art. 22. Article 46. Quality of groundwater (1) The quality requirements for groundwater shall be established by a regulation approved by the Government, which must contain provisions regarding: a) the management objectives for groundwater or bodies of groundwater which may provide for, but not be limited to, the quantitative and chemical status, the concentration of pollutants as a result of human activities, and ensuring a balance between abstraction and recharge; b) quality requirements for groundwater; c) permission for recharge and the establishment of specific conditions for the exploration and abstraction of groundwater; d) sanitary zones for wells. (2) The measures to ensure compliance with the objectives provided for in para. (1) shall be developed in consultation with the river basin district committee. Chapter VII DROUGHT AND FLOOD MANAGEMENT Article 47. Drought management plans Drought management planning is regulated by a regulation approved by the Government, which must contain provisions regarding: a) the development of individual drought management plans for each river basin district or sub-basin; b) the specification of indicators that allow for the identification of drought circumstances; c) the establishment of specific monitoring systems for identifying drought circumstances; d) the identification of management objectives and measures to mitigate the effects of drought, including measures to rationalize water demand, establish priorities for water use, support basic human needs, including for food, and protect aquatic ecosystems; e) measures for public participation in the development and implementation of drought management plans, including information measures; f) the creation of water reserves in case of drought; g) other necessary measures. Article 48. Declaration of a state of drought (1) At the proposal of the central public administration authority for the environment, the Government shall adopt a decision declaring a state of drought. The decision will indicate whether its provisions apply to the entire country, a river basin district, or sub-basins, and will also contain other relevant information about the specific drought situation. (2) The decision declaring a state of drought may temporarily provide for: a) restrictions on some or all activities involving the use of water; b) restrictions or prohibitions on the use of water by those holding an environmental authorization for special water use; c) restrictions or prohibitions on the general use of water; d) new priorities for water use in the area subject to drought and water scarcity; e) the use of water reserves. Article 49. Flood risk management (1) Flood risk management is regulated by a regulation approved by the Government, which must contain provisions regarding: a) the scope of flood risk management plans; b) the phased integration of flood risk management plans into river basin district management plans; c) the establishment of emergency procedures and mechanisms in case of floods to coordinate the provision of assistance to the affected population; d) the coordination of the development and adoption of flood risk management plans with similar planning exercises undertaken in neighboring countries, on the basis of bilateral or multilateral treaties; e) other relevant aspects. (2) The central public administration authority for the environment shall carry out an assessment of the areas within the river basin district that are subject to flood risk, shall develop hazard maps of the geographical areas that could be flooded and flood risk maps in relation to those areas, and, in consultation with each river basin district committee, shall develop flood risk management plans specifying the objectives of flood risk management, as well as measures for achieving these objectives, including measures to reduce flood risk and to protect human health and life, economic activities, ecosystems, and cultural heritage. (3) Landowners are obliged to allow authorized representatives of the central public administration authority for the environment, without charge, passage, as well as the temporary transport and storage of materials and equipment for operational interventions for flood defense. (4) The central public administration authority for the environment shall initiate negotiations with riparian countries in developing joint flood prevention and management plans. Chapter VIII MAINTENANCE OF WATER BODIES, PROTECTION ZONES, AND STRIPS Article 50. Maintenance of water bodies, protection zones, and strips (1) The administrative authority for water management and the local public administration authorities shall ensure the maintenance of surface water bodies, protection zones, and strips as a mandatory public service. (2) The maintenance of surface water bodies, protection zones, and strips, which is based on respecting the environmental objectives for water mentioned in Art. 38, includes: a) strengthening river banks and the beds of water bodies; b) ensuring the discharge capacity of river channels and removing excess alluvium; c) removing excessive vegetation from the minor channel; d) maintaining navigable waters in a navigable state; e) removing objects and waste from the water surface and from the lands of the water fund; f) annual inventory and assessment of the condition of the hydrotechnical constructions of ponds and anthropic lakes in the country; g) other necessary works. (3) The administrative authority for water management is responsible for the maintenance of the surface water bodies, protection zones, and strips, as well as the hydrotechnical constructions established by the Government. (4) Local public administration authorities and other private owners are responsible for the maintenance of the surface water bodies, protection zones, and strips, as well as the hydrotechnical constructions under their management. (5) Owners of riparian lands are obliged, after being notified, to allow the passage of representatives responsible for maintenance, as well as the temporary transport and storage of materials and equipment necessary for fulfilling maintenance duties. Article 51. Legal regime of protection zones and strips The method of creation, the regime of use, and the protection activity of water protection zones and strips are regulated by a special law. Article 52. Water protection zones (1) Water protection zones are established, where this is necessary in the public interest, for the purpose of: a) protecting water bodies against harmful effects to ensure the current or future public water supply; b) protecting water intended for abstraction for drinking water supply; c) recharging groundwater; d) preventing harmful effects caused by rainwater runoff, erosion, [...] (2) In water protection zones, [...]. (3) If the measures taken in accordance with para. (2) are equivalent to an expropriation, compensation shall be paid according to the law. Article 53. Wetlands (1) The identification and delimitation of areas qualified as wetlands shall be carried out in accordance with the Law on the fund of state-protected natural areas. (2) The specific qualities and importance of wetlands must be taken into account in the implementation of this law and in the development of river basin district management plans. (3) The draining, use, and conversion of wetlands are not permitted. They must be used in a sustainable manner, so that their nature and essential characteristics are protected. Chapter IX FINANCIAL ASPECTS Article 54. The cost recovery principle (1) To achieve the objectives of this law, the principle of full recovery of costs related to water use shall be applied, including a full assessment of the cost of water as a component of the environment and a natural resource, based on an economic analysis of water use and respecting the "polluter pays" principle. (2) The economic analysis mentioned in para. (1) shall include the relevant calculations necessary to achieve the principle of cost recovery for services related to water use, taking into account long-term forecasts for water demand and supply in the river basin districts, and, where necessary, with an estimate of the volume, prices, and costs associated with water use services and an estimate of the relevant investments. (3) The system of the water fee is regulated by the Tax Code. Article 55. Fee and payment for water use and pollution (1) General use of water is free of charge. Access fees for bathing areas and spa resorts may only be established by the holder of an environmental authorization for special water use who has built constructions and/or facilities intended for recreational activities. (2) Fees for water use are established by the Tax Code. (3) Payments for water pollution are established by law. Chapter X COMPETENCE IN THE CONTROL OF WATER USE AND PROTECTION Article 56. Control of water use and protection (1) The Inspectorate for Environmental Protection coordinates and carries out inspection and control activities in the field of water use and protection. (2) For the purpose of fulfilling control duties, inspectors have the right: a) of access to waters, in areas along waters, as well as in any other place, unit, or installation, to make findings regarding compliance with regulations and the application of measures for water use and protection; b) to check flow measurement installations, to collect water samples, and to examine, under the conditions of the law, any data or documents necessary for control; c) to control works, constructions, installations, and uses related to water and to verify if they are carried out and operated in accordance with specific legal provisions and in compliance with the conditions of the authorizations issued in accordance with this law; d) to draw up the appropriate documents when finding facts that constitute contraventions in the water sector; e) to order the removal of waste, other substances, or goods stored or disposed of in contradiction with environmental legislation. f) to seal water abstraction facilities and restrict access to water sources if the water use is not authorized or if very serious violations of the legal regulations on water use and protection have been found during the state control, according to the procedure provided for in Law No. 131/2012 on state control over entrepreneurial activity; g) to issue decisions regarding the reparation of damages caused to water resources as a result of pollution and/or unauthorized/irrational use of water. Article 57. Pollution control (1) If certain pollutants or waste have been discharged or are likely to be discharged into a water body or onto land, thus creating a risk of pollution, the Inspectorate for Environmental Protection shall issue a prescription, requiring that, within a reasonable time, specific measures be taken to protect the water against pollution, to minimize, or to remedy the effects of pollution. (2) If the specific measures mentioned in para. (1) are not taken within the prescribed time limits, the Inspectorate for Environmental Protection shall carry them out, having the right to recover the costs incurred. (3) The prescription issued in accordance with para. (1) may be contested according to the procedure provided for in Law No. 131/2012 on state control over entrepreneurial activity. (4) The filing of the contestation mentioned in para. (3) does not suspend the execution of the prescription. (5) The decision of the central public administration authority for the environment may be challenged in an administrative court. Article 58. Liability for violation of water legislation The violation or non-execution of legislation in the water sector entails civil, contraventional, or criminal liability, under the conditions of the law. Chapter XI INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION Article 59. International water resource management (1) The Government, the central public administration authority for the environment, the administrative authority for water management, and the river basin district committees shall participate in the joint management of international water resources on the basis of bilateral and/or multilateral treaties to which the Republic of Moldova is a party. (2) The Government shall initiate processes for the development of bilateral and/or multilateral treaties with the riparian states of the Nistru and Prut rivers, based on the principles of integrated river basin management. (3) The Government shall promote the participation of the Republic of Moldova in international organizations for the management of international river basins that include parts of the territory of the Republic of Moldova. Article 60. Cooperation actions (1) To fulfill the obligations of the Republic of Moldova deriving from international treaties, the Government shall approve: a) mechanisms for the direct and timely exchange of water management and protection data and information between the central public administration authority for the environment, the administrative authority for water management, and the river basin district committees, on the one hand, and similar institutions in other countries, on the other hand; b) procedures for coordinating the plans to be developed in accordance with this law, with equivalent documents from other countries; c) the composition of delegations, which may include members of the river basin district committees, for meetings with representatives from other countries. (2) The Government shall approve the regulatory framework and/or submit legislative initiatives to fulfill the commitments assumed towards the European Union and international organizations. Chapter XII FINAL AND TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS Article 61 (1) Authorizations for special water use issued before the entry into force of this law are valid until their expiry. (2) The central public administration authority for the environment shall develop, submit for approval to the Government, and, from the date of entry into force of this law, implement a phased plan for the registration of special water use for the river basin districts. (3) Water users who do not hold an authorization for special water use are obliged to declare their special water use to the central public administration authority for the environment within 90 days of the entry into force of this law. (4) Water users who have declared special water use in accordance with para. (3) shall be granted a period of 6 months to obtain an environmental authorization for special water use under the conditions of this law. Article 62 (1) This law shall enter into force 18 months from the date of its publication. (2) The Government shall: a) within 6 months of the adoption of this law, submit to Parliament proposals for bringing the legislation in force into conformity with this law; b) within 18 months of the adoption of this law: review and bring its normative acts into conformity with its provisions; develop and approve the normative acts necessary for its implementation.
