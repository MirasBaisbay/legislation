WATER CODE OF THE REPUBLIC OF AZERBAIJAN

The internal waters of the Republic of Azerbaijan, and the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan, are the national wealth of the Azerbaijani people, are used and protected as the basis of life and activity for the population, and ensure the existence of flora and fauna.

This Code regulates the legal relations concerning the use and protection of water bodies in the Republic of Azerbaijan.

SECTION I
General Provisions

CHAPTER I
Basic Provisions

Article 1. Basic Concepts

The concepts used in this Code shall have the following meanings:
water body — a naturally formed landscape or geological structure with boundaries, dimensions, and special water regime characteristics where waters are collected on the surface or in the subsoil of the earth in accordance with the relief form;
water resources — the volume of surface and groundwater located in, used, or available for use in water bodies;
user of a water body — a physical or legal person who has been granted the right to use water bodies;
water consumer — a physical or legal person who receives water from a user of a water body to meet their own needs;
water management facility — systems and structures created in connection with the use, restoration, and protection of water resources;
pollution of a water body — the discharge or disposal of harmful substances that degrades the quality of waters and has a negative impact on the surface, bottom, and surroundings of a water body;
littering of a water body — the disposal of objects, solid particles of various technogenic origins, and domestic waste that complicates the use of water bodies;
harmful effect of waters — flooding, waterlogging, and other impacts of surface and groundwater on a certain territory or facility.

Article 2. Water Legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan

The water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan consists of this Code and other normative legal acts adopted in accordance with it.

If the rules established in the international treaties of the Republic of Azerbaijan concerning the use and protection of water bodies do not conform to the rules provided for in this Code, the rules of the international treaties to which the Republic of Azerbaijan is a party shall apply.

Water relations in the Alat free economic zone are regulated in accordance with the requirements of the Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan "On the Alat free economic zone".

Article 3. Regulation of Relations Concerning the Use and Protection of Water Bodies

Relations concerning the use and protection of water bodies and their water resources (water relations) are regulated by the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Relations concerning the use of water are regulated by this Code, as well as by the civil legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan, legislation on sanitary-epidemiological safety, and other legislative acts.

Relations concerning the supply of drinking water and the discharge of wastewater are regulated by this Code, as well as by the relevant legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Relations arising during the use and protection of water bodies with respect to land, forests, the subsoil, flora and fauna, atmospheric air, the exploration and protection of groundwater, as well as property and other administrative relations, are regulated by this Code, as well as by the relevant legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 4. Objects of Water Relations

The objects of water relations are the water fund of the Republic of Azerbaijan (hereinafter referred to as the water fund) and the lands of the water fund.

Article 5. Water Fund of the Republic of Azerbaijan

The internal waters of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan (water bodies) constitute the water fund of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

According to its location in nature, physical-geographical, administrative-territorial characteristics, and other features, the water fund of the Republic of Azerbaijan consists of surface, underground, border water bodies, and the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 6. Surface Water Bodies

Surface water bodies include surface watercourses, surface reservoirs, glaciers, and snow covers located on the earth's surface.
Surface water bodies consist of their surface water part, bed, bottom, and banks.
Surface water bodies have a multi-purpose significance and may be used for one or several purposes simultaneously.

Article 7. Groundwater Bodies

Groundwater bodies include waters collected in geological structures located in the subsoil of the earth.
The following are attributed to groundwater bodies:
aquifer — a layer of rock within a geological structure in the subsoil of the earth, in whose pores, cracks, and voids hydraulically connected waters are collected;
groundwater basin — a collection of aquifers and water-bearing fractures located within a certain geological structure in the subsoil of the earth;
groundwater deposit — a part of an aquifer with favorable conditions for the extraction of groundwater.

Article 8. Border Water Bodies

Surface and groundwater bodies that define an interstate border, cross a border, or over which the state border of the Republic of Azerbaijan passes are considered border water bodies.
The rules for the use and protection of border water bodies are determined by this Code, the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan on the state border, and the international treaties and agreements to which the Republic of Azerbaijan is a party.

Article 9. Section of the Caspian Sea (lake) Belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan

The rules for the use and protection of the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan are determined by this Code, other legislative acts of the Republic of Azerbaijan, and international legal acts to which the Republic of Azerbaijan is a party.

Article 10. Lands of the Water Fund

The following land areas are included in the lands of the water fund:
lands under the internal waters of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan (water bodies);
lands under water protection zones and coastal strips, islands, and wetlands;
lands under hydraulic structures, amelioration, and water management facilities.

Article 11. Subjects of Water Relations

The subjects of water relations are the Republic of Azerbaijan, the Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic, municipalities, users of water bodies, and water consumers.
The relevant executive authorities participate in water relations on behalf of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic within their powers.
Municipalities participate in water relations within the powers granted to them by legislative acts defining their status.
Users of water bodies and water consumers participate in water relations for the purpose of meeting their needs and conducting entrepreneurial activities in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

CHAPTER II
Forms of Ownership Over Water Bodies

Article 12. State Ownership Over Water Bodies

The following water bodies are maintained in state ownership:
the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan;
border water bodies;
surface and groundwater bodies of state and local importance;
specially protected water bodies.
Water bodies in state ownership may be granted for use, lease, and to municipal ownership in the manner prescribed by legislation.
State management in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies in state ownership is carried out by the relevant executive authorities within their powers.

Article 13. Municipal Ownership Over Water Bodies

Water bodies of local importance located on land areas in municipal ownership are granted to municipal ownership in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Water bodies in municipal ownership may be granted for general use, for use by physical and legal persons, for lease, and also to the ownership of citizens and legal persons of the Republic of Azerbaijan in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Municipalities carry out state management in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies in municipal ownership within the powers granted to them by legislation.

Article 14. Private Ownership Over Water Bodies

Small reservoirs located on land areas in private ownership, which have no hydraulic connection with other water bodies and no outflow, may be granted to private ownership in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Water bodies cannot be granted to the private ownership of persons who are not citizens of the Republic of Azerbaijan and legal persons of foreign states.

Article 15. Ownership Over Water Management Facilities

Forms of state, municipal, and private ownership are established over water management facilities.
Water management facilities maintained in state ownership, granted to municipal and private ownership, are determined by the relevant legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

SECTION II
Management in the Sphere of Use and Protection of Water Bodies

CHAPTER III
Organization of Management in the Sphere of Use and Protection of Water Bodies

Article 16. Basic Principles of Management in the Sphere of Use and Protection of Water Bodies

Management in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies is based on the following principles:
development of the economy and protection of the environment;
supply of the population with quality water;
reconciliation of the principle of territorial structure with the basin principle;
separation of the management functions for the protection of water bodies from the functions of use of water bodies and water management.

Article 17. Management System in the Sphere of Use and Protection of Water Bodies

The management system in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies includes the relevant executive authorities of the Republic of Azerbaijan, the relevant executive authority of the Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic, users of water bodies and municipalities authorized by them in the manner prescribed by legislation.

Article 18. Duties of the State in the Sphere of Use and Protection of Water Bodies

The duties of the state in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies are as follows:
to determine the state policy in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies;
to implement investment policy in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies;
to approve and implement state programs for the use, protection of water bodies, and prevention of the harmful effects of waters, and to organize control over their implementation;
to implement scientific-technical policy in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies;
to determine the rules for conducting state monitoring of water bodies, maintaining the state water cadastre, and implementing the registration of surface and groundwater;
to determine the rules for the use of water bodies and ensure their implementation;
to determine the rules for granting permission for the use of water bodies for special purposes;
to determine the rules for establishing water protection zones and coastal protection strips of water bodies, and to determine the regime of use of their territories;
to determine the principles of economic regulation of the use, restoration, and protection of water bodies, the fee for the use of water bodies and water, and their payment rules, and to determine concessions and exemptions for water use;
to determine the rules for organizing and implementing state control over the use and protection of water bodies;
to approve standards, norms, and rules in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies;
to ensure the state ecological expertise of construction and reconstruction projects of economic and other facilities that affect the state of water bodies;
to prohibit the commissioning of technical structures, equipment, enterprises, and other facilities that have a harmful effect on the state of waters, as well as to restrict, suspend their operation, and prohibit their activities;
to determine the regime of specially protected water bodies, including sources of drinking water supply, and their sanitary protection zones and districts;
to prepare and implement measures against floodwaters, as well as for the prevention of the harmful effects of waters and the elimination of their consequences;
to determine and organize the implementation of measures for the prevention of disasters and accidents, ecological crises, and the elimination of their consequences;
to determine the purposes of paid water use in water bodies;
to perform other duties determined by the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies.
Concessions and exemptions for water use apply only to water taken from natural water sources for the following purposes:
provision with unprocessed water by an authority (body) determined by the relevant executive authority;
drinking water supply by an authority (body) determined by the relevant executive authority;
use of water for the production of electrical energy by an authority (body) determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 19. Duties of the Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies

The duties of the Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies are as follows:
to prepare, approve, and implement programs for the use, restoration, and protection of water bodies, and to organize control over their implementation;
to exercise state control over the use and protection of water bodies;
to restrict, suspend, and prohibit the use of water bodies in the manner prescribed by legislation;
to prepare and implement measures against floodwaters, for the prevention of the harmful effects of waters and the elimination of their consequences, as well as for the restoration of water management facilities damaged or destroyed as a result of natural disasters and accidents;
to prohibit the commissioning of economic and other facilities that affect the state of waters, as well as to restrict, suspend their operation, and prohibit their activities;
to perform other duties determined by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies.

Article 20. Powers of municipalities in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies

The powers of municipalities in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies are as follows:
to own, use, and dispose of reservoirs in municipal ownership;
to control compliance with environmental protection measures during the implementation of measures for the use and protection of water bodies;
to determine, in agreement with the relevant executive authorities, places where water intake for drinking and domestic water supply, swimming, and animal watering is prohibited, and other conditions of general water use, as well as places for recreation and sports from water bodies;
to exercise other powers determined by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies.

CHAPTER IV
State Regulation in the Sphere of Use and Protection of Water Bodies

Article 21. Water management balances

Water management balances consist of comparative reporting materials on water resources and the demand for water in the relevant territory.
Water management balances are compiled for the purpose of assessing the availability of water resources in the water bodies of the relevant territories and the degree of their use, and are used for planning and decision-making on issues of use and protection of water bodies.
Water management balances for the republic, water basins, and administrative-territorial units are compiled by the relevant executive authorities and the bodies created by the relevant executive authority in the manner determined by the relevant executive authority.
The rules for the approval of the annual water management balance for the republic, compiled by the relevant executive authority, are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 22. Schemes for the complex use and protection of water resources

Schemes for the complex use and protection of water resources combine systematic research and project materials about the state of water resources and the prospects for the use and protection of water bodies.
Schemes for the complex use and protection of water resources are developed to determine water management measures and the country's prospective demand for water resources, to ensure the efficient use and protection of water bodies, and to prevent the harmful effects of waters and eliminate their consequences.
Schemes for the complex use and protection of water resources are developed by the relevant executive authorities.
The rules for the development, coordination, state expertise, approval, and implementation of schemes for the complex use and protection of water resources are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 23. State programs for the use, protection of water bodies, and prevention of the harmful effects of waters

State programs are developed for preparing and implementing measures for the use, protection of water bodies, and prevention of the harmful effects of waters, based on water management balances, schemes for the complex use and protection of water resources, and state water cadastre data.
State programs for the use and protection of water bodies are developed and implemented in the manner prescribed by legislation, taking into account the proposals of municipalities, users of water bodies, and public organizations.
A strategic environmental assessment (SEA) document is prepared for the drafts of these programs in accordance with the Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan "On Environmental Impact Assessment," and a state environmental expertise of that document is carried out.

Article 24. State monitoring of water bodies

State monitoring of water bodies consists of a system of regular observations of the hydrological and hydrogeological indicators of water bodies for the purpose of timely detection and assessment of processes occurring in their condition, forecasting the development of these processes, preventing their harmful effects, and ensuring the effectiveness of implemented water protection measures.
State monitoring of water bodies is a component of the state environmental monitoring system.
State monitoring of water bodies is carried out by the relevant executive authorities in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 25. State registration of waters

The state registration of waters consists of the regular determination of the quantity and quality of water resources available in the relevant territory in the prescribed manner.
The state registration of waters is carried out for the purpose of ensuring the current and prospective planning of the efficient use and protection of water bodies.
The state water registration data characterize the state of surface and groundwater bodies according to their quantitative and qualitative indicators, their study, and degree of use.
The state registration of waters in the Republic of Azerbaijan is carried out through a unified system by the relevant executive authority in the prescribed manner, based on the registration data of users of water bodies and state monitoring.

Article 26. State water cadastre

The state water cadastre is a collection of information about water bodies, water resources, the use of water bodies, and the users of water bodies. The state water cadastre is maintained through a unified system in the Republic of Azerbaijan and is based on the state water registration data.
The submission of information to be included in the state water cadastre by the users of water bodies to the relevant executive authorities is mandatory.
The data of the state water cadastre is the basis for decision-making by the relevant executive authorities.
The state water cadastre is maintained by the relevant executive authorities in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 27. State ecological expertise of construction and reconstruction projects of economic and other facilities affecting the state of water bodies

An environmental impact assessment (EIA) document for construction projects is prepared in the manner prescribed by the Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan "On Environmental Impact Assessment" to determine the compliance of construction and reconstruction projects of economic and other facilities that affect the state of water bodies with the relevant norms, technical conditions, and requirements, and a state environmental expertise of that document is carried out by the relevant executive authorities in the manner prescribed by the Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan "On Environmental Protection".

Article 28. State control over the use and protection of water bodies

State control over the use and protection of water bodies ensures compliance with:
rules for the use and protection of water bodies;
water use limits;
sanitary-hygienic, ecological, hydrogeological, hydrological, technological, and metrological standards, norms, and rules in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies;
the regime of use of the territory of water protection zones and other requirements of the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The rules for exercising state control over the use and protection of water bodies are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 29. Standardization in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies

Standardization in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies consists of the establishment of water use limits and the development and adoption of sanitary-hygienic, ecological, hydrogeological, hydrological, technological, and metrological standards, norms, and rules in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies.
The rules for standardization in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies are determined by the relevant executive authority.

SECTION III
Use and Protection of Water Bodies

CHAPTER V
Use of Water Bodies

Article 30. Users of water bodies

Enterprises, institutions, organizations, and other legal persons (hereinafter referred to as legal persons), regardless of their form of ownership, citizens of the Republic of Azerbaijan, foreigners, and stateless persons (hereinafter referred to as physical persons) may be users of water bodies in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Users of water bodies may provide water for the use of legal and physical persons (consumers) in the manner prescribed by legislation.
The rules and conditions for water use, and the mutual rights and obligations of the parties, are regulated on the basis of contracts.

Article 31. Right to use water bodies

Legal and physical persons who are not the owners of water bodies may have the following rights over them:
long-term use;
short-term use;
limited use (water servitudes).
Users of water bodies shall exercise the use of water bodies in the manner prescribed by this Code.
Users of water bodies may dispose of the right to use water bodies in the cases provided for by this Code.
The leasing of water bodies is carried out in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan and in conformity with this Code.
A change in the course of a river or other changes in the location of water bodies do not cause a change or termination of the right to use water bodies, unless other cases are provided for in this Code.

Article 32. Right to long-term and short-term use of water bodies

Water bodies in state ownership may be granted for the long-term or short-term use of physical and legal persons, depending on their purpose of use, resource potential, and ecological condition, in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The right to short-term use of water bodies is established for up to three years, and the right to long-term use for up to twenty-five years.
The term of the right to use water bodies may be extended in the manner prescribed by legislation.

Article 33. Right to limited use of water bodies (water servitude)

The rights of legal and physical persons who have been granted the right of use or ownership over water bodies may be limited in favor of other persons in the form of general and special water servitudes.
In the form of a general water servitude, every person may use a water body in general use and other water bodies, unless other cases are provided for by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Interested persons may use water bodies in the special use of other persons in the form of a special water servitude. A special water servitude may also be established on the basis of a court decision.
The general rules provided for in civil legislation regarding servitudes apply to the extent that they do not contradict the requirements of this Code.
General and special water servitudes may be established for the following purposes:
to take water without applying technical means and structures;
to water livestock and drive them across a water body;
to use water bodies as waterways for ferries, boats, and other small-sized floating craft.
Other water servitudes may also be established in the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Permission is not required for the exercise of water servitudes.

Article 34. Acquisition of the right to use water bodies

The right to use water bodies may be acquired in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan. The types of use of water bodies that require permission and the rules for granting permission are determined by the relevant executive authorities.
When a user of a water body who is a physical person dies, or a user of a water body who is a legal person is reorganized, the right to use the water body passes to their successors.
The right to use water bodies may pass from one person to another, provided that the purpose of use of the water body is not changed, in the manner prescribed by this Code.
Rights to water bodies in private ownership are acquired in the manner prescribed by civil legislation, land legislation, and this Code.

Article 35. Types of use of water bodies

Water bodies may be used for the following purposes:
drinking water and domestic supply;
medical, resort, and health-improvement;
recreation and sports;
industry, construction, and energy;
agriculture;
forestry;
hydropower;
transport;
fishery, including aquaculture;
hunting;
discharge of wastewater;
fire protection and other purposes.
A water body or a part thereof may be granted for use for one or several purposes at the same time.

Article 36. Use of water bodies for general purposes

Water bodies in general use are water bodies whose use is possible and open to everyone. Physical persons may use such water bodies free of charge for the purpose of meeting their needs (swimming, boating, sports, amateur fishing of fish and other aquatic bioresources, watering of livestock, taking water without using technical devices, etc.).
When using water bodies for general purposes, the requirements of the authorities that protect the environment and exercise state sanitary supervision, that protect fish and other aquatic bioresources, and that regulate vessel traffic, as well as the rules for protecting human life on water, and for the efficient use and protection of waters, must be observed.
For the purpose of protecting the life and health of the population and creating the necessary sanitary conditions, the relevant executive authorities and municipalities may, within their powers, determine places where swimming, boating, fishing of fish and other aquatic bioresources, taking water for drinking and domestic needs, watering of livestock, and other uses of water are prohibited, and may also establish other conditions for the use of water for general purposes in water bodies located in the territory of a city, district, settlement, or rural residential area.
The use of water for general purposes in water bodies that are in the private ownership of, or have been granted for lease or separate use to, physical and legal persons may be allowed with the consent of the owners and users of the water body under the conditions they have determined or by a decision of the relevant executive authorities.
Dry land strips are allocated along the banks of water bodies in general use. Every person has the right to move along this strip, to use it for fishing and other aquatic bioresources, and to ensure that boats can come ashore.

Article 37. Separate use of water bodies

Water bodies in separate use are water bodies used by a limited number of persons.
Water bodies in state ownership may be granted in whole or in part for separate use for defense, energy, transport, and other state or public needs, by a decision of the relevant executive authority in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The purpose and basic conditions for the use of water bodies are indicated in the decision to grant them for separate use.
When water bodies are granted for separate use, they are removed from the list of water bodies in general use.
In exceptional cases, water bodies granted for separate use may be granted or taken back for general purposes by a decision of the relevant executive authority.

Article 38. Use of water bodies for special purposes

The use of water bodies for special purposes is carried out on the basis of permission by applying technical structures and equipment for the purpose of meeting the needs of the population for drinking water, domestic use, medical treatment, health improvement, and other needs, as well as for agriculture and forestry, industry, energy, transport, fishery, and other state or public needs.
The types of use of water bodies for special purposes are determined by the relevant executive authorities.
A state fee is charged in the amount determined by the Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan "On State Fees" for the granting of permission for the use of water bodies for special purposes.

Article 39. Water use limits

Water use limits are the permissible volume of water resources to be taken from water bodies or of wastewater to be discharged into water bodies, established for each user and consumer of water bodies.
The rules for the preparation and implementation of water use limits are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 40. Rules for granting water bodies for use

Water bodies are granted for use on the basis of permission and a water use contract.
Surface water bodies are granted for use with the allocation of a water area, places for water intake, and discharge of wastewater, in the manner prescribed by the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The granting of groundwater for use is carried out in accordance with the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan on the subsoil.
The granting for use of the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan and of border water bodies is carried out in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the international treaties to which the Republic of Azerbaijan is a party.

Article 41. Anti-monopoly requirements for the use of water bodies

Activities of users of water bodies aimed at monopolization and unfair competition, which result or may result in the restriction of the rights and legal interests of other users of water bodies, as well as other physical and legal persons, are prohibited by law.
It is prohibited for the relevant executive authorities to adopt decisions and engage in activities that create discriminatory conditions for the activities of individual users of water bodies, leading to the monopolization of water use, and thereby violating the rights and legal interests of other users of water bodies.
The substantial violation of the rights and legal interests of other users of water bodies by creating a monopoly in the sphere of water use is grounds for the application of sanctions provided for in the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 42. Restriction, suspension, or prohibition of the use of water bodies

The use of water bodies or a part thereof may be restricted, suspended, or prohibited in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan for the purpose of ensuring the defense and security of the country, protecting the health of the population, the environment, and historical-cultural heritage, as well as the legal rights and interests of other persons.
The restriction, suspension, or prohibition of the use of water bodies is carried out on the basis of a submission by the relevant executive authorities in accordance with this Code and other legislative acts of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

CHAPTER VI
Rights and Obligations of Water Users

Article 43. Rights of Water Users

Water users have the following rights:
to reuse water;
to construct appropriate structures and facilities for the use of water bodies for special purposes;
to check the quantity and quality of the water provided and of the wastewater;
to demand compensation for water not received in the amount provided for in the contract, except in cases provided for by legislation;
to exercise other rights provided for by the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The violated rights of those using water bodies shall be restored in the manner prescribed by legislation.

Article 44. Termination of the right to use water

The right of legal and physical persons to use water, regardless of the form of ownership, is terminated in the following cases:
when there is no longer a need for water use or when use is renounced;
when the term of use of the water body has expired;
when legal persons are liquidated;
when water bodies are granted for use to others in contradiction with legislation;
when water bodies are taken back from separate use;
when the fee for water use and for the discharge of wastewater is not paid;
when the fee for water use is not paid, taking into account the requirements of the second part of Article 18 of this Code;
when the water body is not used for the intended purpose (except for the right to use for drinking water and domestic needs) and in other cases provided for by legislation.

Article 45. Obligations of Water Users

The obligations of water users are as follows:
to use water bodies efficiently, to ensure the economical use of water, and the improvement of water quality;
to comply with water use plans, limits, rules, norms, and regimes;
not to allow the discharge of wastewater into water bodies containing pollutants in amounts exceeding the norms;
not to allow the violation of rights granted to other users of water bodies on the basis of water servitudes, nor to cause harm to economic and natural objects (land, forests, fauna, mineral resources, etc.);
to use water bodies according to their designation;
to maintain water management structures and technical equipment in good condition, and to improve their operational quality;
to keep records of water use;
to make payments and pay fines for the right to use water and for the discharge of wastewater into water bodies and the environment in a timely manner, taking into account the requirements of the second part of Article 18 of this Code;
to perform other duties provided for by the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 46. Compensation for damage caused as a result of water management activities

Damage caused to physical and legal persons as a result of carrying out water management measures, termination of the right to use water bodies, or changes in the conditions of water use is compensated in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

CHAPTER VII
Use of water bodies for the population's drinking water, domestic, medical-resort, health-improvement, recreation, and sports purposes

Article 47. Water bodies provided for the population's drinking water and domestic needs

Water bodies whose water quality meets the established sanitary norms and state standards are provided for meeting the population's drinking water and domestic needs.
The population's drinking water supply is, as a rule, carried out by means of centralized water pipeline facilities.
In the absence of a water pipeline, the population's drinking water and domestic needs may be met by using water directly from rivers, water wells, karezes, springs, and other water sources only in accordance with the sanitary norms established by legislation.
The natural water sources to be used for meeting the population's drinking water and domestic needs for residential areas are determined by municipalities with the consent of the relevant executive authorities.

Article 48. Restriction on the use of drinking water for other purposes

As a rule, the use of drinking water for purposes not related to drinking water and domestic needs is not permitted.
In regions with insufficient surface water sources and abundant drinking groundwater resources, the use of these waters for other purposes may be permitted with the consent of the relevant executive authorities.

Article 49. Use of water bodies classified as medical facilities

Water bodies located in the territory of the Republic of Azerbaijan and possessing natural medicinal properties are classified as medical facilities by a decision of the relevant executive authority.
Water bodies classified as medical facilities are used primarily for medical and resort purposes.
In exceptional cases, the use of water bodies classified as medical facilities for other purposes may be permitted with the consent of the relevant executive authorities.
The discharge of wastewater into water bodies classified as medical facilities is not permitted.

Article 50. Use of water bodies for recreation and sports purposes

In compliance with the requirements of Article 36 of this Code, the use of water bodies in general use for recreation and sports is permitted without a license.
For the organization of mass recreation and to meet the needs of sports, water bodies may be granted in whole or in part for separate use.

CHAPTER VIII
Use of water bodies for agricultural needs

Article 51. Use of water bodies for agricultural needs

The use of water bodies for agricultural needs is carried out in compliance with water use limits, under the rules of general and special use.
Water use in agriculture is carried out on the basis of on-farm and general system plans.

Article 52. On-farm water use plans

On-farm water use plans are drafted by water users.
On-farm water use plans are approved in the manner prescribed by the relevant executive authority.
In on-farm water use plans, the water demand of all water consumers must be taken into account, regardless of their form and type of ownership.

Article 53. General system plans for water use

General system plans for water use are drafted for district and republic-level irrigation systems.
General system plans for water use are drafted by the users of water bodies on the basis of on-farm water use plans and water use contracts.
General system plans for water use are approved in the manner prescribed by the relevant executive authority.
Water use contracts are concluded between the users of water bodies and water consumers.
The contracts specify the amount and location of water provided, the term, the mutual obligations of the parties, and other information.

Article 54. Modification of the water use plan

The water use plan may be modified only with the consent of the authority that approved it in the following cases:
in cases of water scarcity;
when weather conditions, irrigation norms, or irrigable areas are changed;
when water consumers refuse the water allocated to them according to the plan;
in other cases determined by the relevant executive authority.
In the event of extraordinary incidents, the users of water bodies may modify the water use plan and temporarily suspend the supply of water. They must immediately inform the water consumers and the relevant executive authority that approved the water use plan about these measures. The unsupplied water may be compensated in subsequent periods, depending on the condition of the water sources.

Article 55. Use of reservoirs located on the territory of landowners and users for agricultural needs

Agricultural enterprises, family peasant farms, and other legal and physical persons have the right to use springs, karezes, and other natural water sources on the lands granted to their ownership and use in the manner prescribed by legislation, and to build reservoirs and water management structures on these sources.
The said legal and physical persons have the right to carry out large-scale amelioration works, or to reorganize and liquidate existing amelioration works, on the lands designated for agricultural use that have been granted to their ownership and use, in accordance with the rules determined by the relevant executive authority on the use and protection of waters.

Article 56. Use of wastewater for agricultural needs

The use of wastewater for agricultural needs may be carried out in agreement with the relevant executive authorities.

Article 57. Irrigation of forest belts and areas with forest saplings

The irrigation of forest belts and areas with forest saplings is carried out from water bodies in accordance with the rules for agricultural use.

Article 58. Determination of places for driving, watering livestock, and passage of vehicles

The driving of livestock across canals and other hydraulic structures, and the passage of tractors, agricultural machinery, automobiles, and other means of transport across the territory of water bodies not designated for this purpose, is prohibited.
Livestock may drink from rivers, canals, and other surface reservoirs only at specially constructed watering places or in areas designated for this purpose.
Places for the passage of means of transport and agricultural machinery, and for the driving and watering of livestock, are determined by the relevant executive authorities and are indicated by special signs.
Livestock driving routes must be supplied with water in accordance with the rules and conditions determined by the relevant executive authority.

CHAPTER IX
Use of water bodies for industrial and hydropower purposes

Article 59. Use of water bodies for industrial purposes

As a rule, technical waters are used for industrial purposes.
Those using water bodies for industrial purposes must comply with the established limits, technological norms, and rules for water use, and must also take measures to reduce water consumption and prevent the discharge of wastewater by improving production technology and water supply schemes.
Enterprises using drinking water for technical purposes must, as a rule, switch to recycling and circulating water supply systems.

Article 60. Restriction of the use of drinking water for industrial purposes

In cases of natural disaster, accident, and other emergency situations, or when an enterprise uses more than the allocated limit of drinking water, the relevant executive authorities may restrict or prohibit the intake of drinking water from water pipelines for industrial purposes in favor of meeting the drinking and domestic needs of the population, and may also temporarily reduce the intake of drinking water for industrial purposes from the water pipelines under the authority of individual enterprises, institutions, and organizations.

Article 61. Use of groundwater for technical supply and other production needs

Groundwater not classified as drinking or medicinal may be used for technical supply, industrial purposes, including the extraction of chemical elements contained therein, obtaining thermal energy, and other purposes in the manner prescribed by the relevant executive authority.

Article 62. Use of water bodies for the extraction of mineral resources

The extraction of mineral resources from water bodies, geological exploration, and drilling works, and the construction of underground structures are carried out in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan on the subsoil and this Code.

Article 63. Use of water bodies for hydropower needs

The use of water bodies for hydropower needs is carried out taking into account the interests of other sectors of the economy and in compliance with the conditions of complex water use, in accordance with the rules determined by the relevant executive authority.
Water bodies provided for hydropower needs may also be used for other purposes at the same time.
Hydropower enterprises must comply with the established rules for the use of water bodies, the filling and operation regime of reservoirs, ensure the unhindered and safe passage of ships and ferries, and ensure that fish are released into spawning grounds.

CHAPTER X
Use of water bodies for transport needs

Article 64. Waterways in general use

The rivers, lakes, reservoirs, canals of the Republic of Azerbaijan, the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan, and other surface water bodies are considered waterways in general use if their use as a waterway has not been fully or partially prohibited, or if these bodies have not been granted for separate use.
Legal and physical persons may use the waterways in general use for the navigation of their own water transport vessels in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 65. Rules for the use of water bodies for water and air transport needs

The classification of waterways in general use, and the rules for their use for the needs of water and air transport, are determined by the relevant executive authority in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

CHAPTER XI
Use of water bodies for fishery and hunting

Article 66. Use of water bodies for fishery

Fishery water bodies or a part thereof are used for the catch, collection of fish and other aquatic bioresources, and for aquaculture for fishery needs in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Legal and physical persons using fishery water bodies must take measures to protect these reservoirs from pollution and littering.
Users of water bodies, with the consent of the relevant executive authority, must take measures for fishery land improvement and fish breeding to improve the condition of reservoirs and restore fish and other aquatic bioresources, and must also maintain the places where fishing gear is installed in a proper sanitary condition.

Article 67. Use of reservoirs located on the territory of landowners and users for the catch of fish and other aquatic bioresources and for aquaculture

Legal and physical persons may use ponds, oxbow lakes, and pools located on lands in their ownership or use for the catch of fish and other aquatic bioresources and for aquaculture.
The use of the specified water bodies for the catch of fish and other aquatic bioresources and for aquaculture is carried out in accordance with the rules determined by the relevant executive authorities.

Article 68. Use of water bodies for sport and amateur fishing of fish and other aquatic bioresources

Except for specially protected natural areas and fish breeding farms, other fishery water bodies may be used for sport and amateur fishing of fish and other aquatic bioresources in accordance with this Code and other normative legal acts on the use of water bodies.
The relevant executive authorities must allocate fishery water bodies in state ownership or parts thereof for the specified purposes.
Sport and amateur fishing of fish and other aquatic bioresources in water bodies allocated for the organization of fishery farms may be carried out with the consent of the owners and users of these bodies.

Article 69. Restriction of the rights of users of water bodies in important fishery reservoirs

The rights of water users in important fishery water bodies or their individual parts may be restricted in favor of the fishery industry for the protection and reproduction of valuable species of fish and other aquatic bioresources. The list of such water bodies and the rules for restricting water use are determined by the relevant executive authorities.

Article 70. Operation of hydraulic structures in fishery water bodies

When operating hydraulic and other structures in fishery water bodies, measures must be taken to ensure the protection and restoration of fish and other aquatic bioresources.
When taking water from fishery water bodies, they must be equipped with fish protection devices.

Article 71. Protection of fishery water bodies from pollution, littering, and shallowing

The following measures are prohibited in fishery water bodies:
building dams and restoring destroyed dams in fish spawning grounds without the consent of the relevant executive authority;
creating blockages or damming up rivers, river branches, and canals, and releasing water from lakes and estuaries, except in cases where sanitary and hydraulic measures are carried out with the consent of the relevant authorities;
discharging waste from wood processing into reservoirs and piling it on the banks and in floodplains of these reservoirs;
piling soil excavated during dredging and bottom-cleaning works in fish spawning grounds and wintering pits;
other cases determined by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 72. Use of water bodies for hunting purposes

Water bodies inhabited by waterfowl and valuable fur-bearing animals classified as hunting objects are granted for hunting purposes in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 73. Rules for the use of water bodies for the needs of fishery and hunting farms

The rules for the use of water bodies for the needs of fishery and hunting farms are determined by the relevant executive authority of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

CHAPTER XII
Use of specially protected water bodies

Article 74. Specially protected water bodies

Specially protected water bodies are water bodies of special scientific, historical, cultural, aesthetic, and nature-protective significance.
These bodies are declared inviolable in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Specially protected water bodies are withdrawn from economic activity, in whole or in part, permanently or temporarily, on the basis of a submission by the relevant executive authorities, in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Specially protected water bodies are classified as republican, regional, and local specially protected water bodies.
A specially protected water body may also exist as a separate natural area or as part of a specially protected natural area determined by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The following categories of specially protected water bodies are determined:
water areas of the internal waters of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the section of the Caspian Sea (lake) belonging to the Republic of Azerbaijan;
wetland areas;
watercourses and reservoirs belonging to a rare natural landscape;
protection zones for the source and mouth of water bodies;
spawning and wintering grounds for valuable fish species;
water bodies that have an inseparable connection with forests, flora and fauna, and other specially protected natural resources.
The classification of specially protected water bodies into categories is carried out in the manner prescribed by the relevant executive authority.
Other categories of specially protected water bodies may also be provided for by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 75. Use of water from specially protected water bodies

The rules for using water from specially protected water bodies are determined by the relevant legislation.
The fishing and collection of fish and other aquatic bioresources from specially protected water bodies, and other activities that change the natural state of these water bodies, are prohibited.
The discharge of wastewater into specially protected water bodies and into the reservoirs that feed them is permitted only in the absence of other options, with the consent of the relevant executive authority, and in compliance with the requirements of Articles 77-79 of this Code.
Activities that may change the natural state of specially protected water bodies may be carried out only with the consent of the relevant executive authority.

Article 76. Revocation of the inviolability of specially protected water bodies

The inviolability of specially protected water bodies may be revoked only in the most necessary cases by a decision of the relevant executive authority.

CHAPTER XIII
Use of water bodies for the discharge of wastewater, fire protection, and other purposes

Article 77. Use of water bodies for the discharge of wastewater

The use of water bodies for the discharge of industrial, communal, domestic, drainage, and other types of wastewater is permitted with the consent of the relevant executive authorities in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The rules for using water bodies for the discharge of wastewater are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 78. Discharge of wastewater into water bodies

The discharge of wastewater into water bodies is permitted only in cases where it does not lead to an increase in the concentration of pollutants in these water bodies above the established norms, and when those using the water bodies treat the wastewater and bring it to the established norms.
The discharge of wastewater into reservoirs that are used as a source of drinking water or that feed groundwater suitable for drinking is strictly prohibited.

Article 79. Restriction, suspension, and prohibition of the discharge of wastewater into water bodies

When the requirements of Article 78 of this Code are violated, the discharge of wastewater into water bodies is restricted or prohibited by the relevant executive authorities.
In cases where the discharge of wastewater is dangerous to the health of the population, the activities of industrial installations and enterprises are suspended.

Article 80. Use of water bodies for fire protection needs

The taking of water from all water bodies in the required volume and free of charge for fire protection needs is permitted.
The use of water taken for fire protection and from the bodies where this water is stored for purposes other than its intended use is prohibited.
The rules for using water bodies for fire protection needs are determined by the relevant executive authority.

CHAPTER XIV
Protection of Water Bodies

Article 81. Protection of waters

All waters (water bodies) must be protected from pollution, littering, and depletion, which may cause harm to the health of the population, as well as lead to a decrease in fish and other aquatic bioresources, a deterioration of water supply conditions, and other unfavorable phenomena as a result of changes in their physical, chemical, and biological properties, a decrease in their natural self-purification ability, and a disruption of the hydrological and hydrogeological regime of the waters.
The rules for the protection of waters (water bodies), their protection from pollution, littering, and depletion, as well as the improvement of the quality and regime of waters, are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 82. Water protection zones

Water protection zones are established to maintain water bodies in a condition that meets ecological requirements, to prevent the pollution, littering, and depletion of surface and groundwater, and to preserve the habitat of animals and plants.
A water protection zone is an area adjacent to the water area of a water body, on which a special regime for the use of natural resources, protection, and other economic activities is established.
Coastal protection strips are established within the boundaries of water protection zones. In the coastal protection strips, plowing and cultivation of land, deforestation, uprooting of trees and shrubs, establishment of livestock farms and camps, cemeteries, and garbage dumps, as well as other types of activities, are prohibited, except in cases provided for by this Code.
In the coastal protection strips, the placement of water supply, recreation, sports, fishery, and hunting facilities, as well as ports and hydraulic structures, is permitted on the basis of a license.
The rules for determining the dimensions, boundaries, and use of water protection zones and their coastal protection strips are determined by the relevant executive authority.
The water protection zones of water bodies that are sources of drinking water and fish spawning grounds are declared specially protected areas in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 83. Granting of lands in water protection zones for use

Land areas located in water protection zones may be granted for the use of physical and legal persons in agreement with the relevant executive authorities, in the manner prescribed by the land legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan and this Code.
State control over compliance with the regime of use and protection of natural resources in water protection zones, and over other economic activities of physical and legal persons, is carried out by the relevant executive authorities within their powers.

Article 84. Protection of water bodies from pollution and littering

Physical and legal persons whose activities affect the quality of waters are prohibited from:
discharging industrial, domestic, and other wastes into water bodies;
polluting and littering waters as a result of the leakage of oils, wood, chemical, petroleum, and other industrial products due to technical and technological malfunctions;
polluting and littering water collection areas, reservoirs, the surface of snow and ice cover, and glaciers with industrial and domestic wastes and petroleum products;
burying industrial and domestic wastes in the sanitary protection zones of water sources and mineral springs, in areas where fractured rocks and water-bearing strata outcrop, and in the water catchment areas of groundwater bodies used for drinking water supply and economic-domestic needs;
polluting waters with radioactive and toxic chemical substances;
burying industrial and domestic wastes in areas where groundwater, economic, and drinking water reservoirs are located.
The discharge of wastewater into water bodies is permitted in compliance with the requirements provided for in Articles 77-79 of this Code.

Article 85. Establishment of sanitary protection zones

Sanitary protection zones are established in the relevant manner in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan for the purpose of protecting waters used for drinking and domestic water supply, medical, resort, and health-improvement needs of the population.

Article 86. Establishment of water-protective forest belts

Water-retaining and soil-protective forest belts are established around rivers, lakes, reservoirs, canals, collectors, groundwater, and other water bodies in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan to maintain a favorable water regime, prevent water and wind erosion of soils, silting of reservoirs, deterioration of animal habitats, reduce unnecessary surface runoff, and for other purposes.
Control over the protection of water-protective forest belts is exercised by the relevant executive authorities, and they have the right to suspend or prohibit activities that have a harmful effect on the condition of their water bodies.

Article 87. Protection of surface waters

Enterprises whose activities have a negative impact on surface water bodies must, together with the relevant executive authorities, take measures to preserve the quantity, quality, and purity of the water of surface water bodies.
The water protection zones of surface water bodies and the rules for their use are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 88. Protection of groundwaters

When drilling and other mining works related to the exploration, prospecting, and use of deposits of gas, oil, and other mineral resources are carried out, and groundwater horizons are discovered, the organizations conducting the mining works must immediately inform the relevant executive authorities and take measures in the prescribed manner for the protection of groundwaters.
Gushing wells, as well as exploration wells that are unsuitable for operation or whose use has been suspended, must be equipped with regulating devices, capped, or liquidated.
The construction of solid and liquid waste collectors and garbage dumps, and the placement of industrial, agricultural, and other facilities that may be a source of pollution of groundwater deposits, are not permitted in the zones where drinking groundwater deposits are located.
Control over the protection of groundwaters is exercised by the relevant executive authority in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 89. Permissible norms of harmful impact on water bodies

The permissible norms of harmful impact on water bodies are determined by the legislation on environmental protection and water.
The permissible norms of harmful impact on water bodies are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 90. Ecological water release

Water release from reservoirs (ecological water release) is carried out to maintain water bodies in a condition that meets the relevant ecological requirements, and the volume of water taken without restoration is determined.
The volume of ecological water release and water taken without restoration for each water body is determined by the relevant executive authorities in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The satisfaction of the water demand of water consumers at the expense of ecological water release is not permitted.

Article 91. Emergency ecological situation and ecological disaster zones in water bodies

The relevant executive authority may declare water bodies (or parts thereof) and their water catchment areas that may pose a threat to people, the animal and plant world, and the environment as a result of economic activity or natural processes as emergency ecological situation and ecological disaster zones.
The legal regime of emergency ecological situation or ecological disaster zones is determined by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 92. Prevention of the harmful effects of waters and elimination of their consequences

The relevant executive authority and the users of water bodies must take appropriate measures to prevent and eliminate the consequences of:
floods, waterlogging, and submersion;
the destruction of the banks of dams and other structures;
soil erosion, bogging, and salinization;
the formation of ravines, soil slides and subsidence, mudflows, and other harmful phenomena.
In the cases provided for by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan, special commissions are created by the relevant executive authorities to prevent the harmful effects of waters and eliminate their consequences. The decisions adopted by these commissions within their powers are binding on physical and legal persons.
The prevention and elimination of the consequences of an emergency situation caused by floods and strong mudflows in the territory of territorial units are organized by the relevant executive authorities and municipalities.
In cases of natural disasters and accidents in water bodies, the users of water bodies must participate in carrying out measures to prevent the harmful effects of waters and eliminate their consequences. These measures are carried out in agreement with the relevant executive authorities and municipalities.

Article 93. Basin agreements in the sphere of restoration and protection of water bodies

Basin agreements in the sphere of restoration and protection of water bodies may be concluded for the purpose of coordinating and consolidating activities aimed at the restoration and protection of water bodies.
Basin agreements are concluded between the relevant executive authority, municipalities, and water users.
A special water fund may be created to finance measures for the restoration and protection of water bodies in order to achieve the purpose and tasks of the basin agreement, in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
Basin agreements are prepared on the basis of proposals from the relevant executive authorities, based on water management balances, schemes for the complex use and protection of water resources, state programs for the use, restoration, and protection of water bodies, and other projects.

CHAPTER XV
Placement, design, construction, restoration, and operation of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters

Article 94. Conditions for the placement, design, construction, restoration, and commissioning of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters

When placing, designing, constructing, restoring, and commissioning new and reconstructed enterprises, structures, and other facilities, as well as when applying new technological processes that affect the state of waters, public opinion must be taken into account, and the efficient use of water must be ensured, conditional on meeting the drinking water and domestic needs of the population. At the same time, measures must be provided for the registration of water taken from and returned to water bodies, the protection of waters from pollution, littering, and depletion, the prevention of the harmful effects of waters, the restriction of land flooding to a minimum necessary level, the protection of lands from salinization, waterlogging, or drying up, as well as the preservation of favorable natural conditions and landscapes.
In fishery water bodies, when placing, designing, constructing, and commissioning new and reconstructed enterprises, structures, and other facilities, in addition to the measures mentioned above, measures must be implemented to ensure the protection and reproduction of fish and other aquatic bioresources.
The construction project of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters must be drafted taking into account the possibility of using them for recreation and sports.
An Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) document for the construction projects of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters is prepared in accordance with the Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan "On Environmental Impact Assessment," and a state environmental expertise of that document is carried out.
When existing enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters comply with the ecological requirements for environmental impact indicators, an environmental impact assessment (EIA) document is not required for the reconstruction of these facilities or the replacement of existing equipment and installations, but appropriate changes are made to the documents issued for these facilities by the relevant executive authority (body determined by the relevant executive authority) (permissible emission limit (PEL), permissible discharge limit (PDL), ecological passport), taking into account the requirements of normative legal acts and technical normative legal acts in the field of environmental protection.
When the operating conditions of existing enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters do not comply with the ecological requirements for environmental impact indicators, or when different technologies and technological methods are applied than those provided for in the initial project, a new environmental impact assessment (EIA) for these facilities must be carried out.
When the transboundary impact of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters is determined during the environmental impact assessment (EIA), the issues of transboundary impact assessment related to these facilities are resolved in accordance with the international treaties to which the Republic of Azerbaijan is a party.

Article 95. Prohibition of commissioning of enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters

The commissioning of newly built and reconstructed enterprises, structures, and other facilities affecting the state of waters is prohibited in the following cases:
if they are not provided with devices that prevent the pollution and littering of waters, or their harmful effects;
if measures provided for in the projects to prevent soil waterlogging, bogging, salinization, and erosion have not been implemented;
if water intake and other structures provided for in the projects are not ready;
if fish protection and fish passage structures provided for in the projects have not been built;
if water intake facilities intended for extracting groundwater with unapproved reserves have not been provided;
if water wells are not equipped with water-regulating devices and, in relevant cases, sanitary protection zones have not been established;
if reservoirs are filled with water before the bed preparation measures provided for in the projects are completed, and in other cases affecting the state of waters.

SECTION IV
Economic regulation of measures for the use and protection of water bodies, resolution of disputes, and liability for violation of water legislation

CHAPTER XVI
Economic regulation of measures for the use and protection of water bodies

Article 96. Principles of economic regulation of measures for the use and protection of water bodies

The economic regulation of measures for the use and protection of water bodies is based on the following principles:
paid use of water, taking into account the requirements of the second part of Article 18 of this Code;
payments related to the use of water bodies, taking into account the requirements of the second part of Article 18 of this Code;
financing of measures for the use and protection of water bodies.

Article 97. Paid water use

Paid water use is established in the Republic of Azerbaijan, taking into account the requirements of the second part of Article 18 of this Code.
The purpose of paid water use is to cover state expenditures directed at water supply, to ensure that water management enterprises, institutions, and organizations operate on the principle of self-financing, to create material interest in the efficient and economical use of water resources and the improvement of water protection, to conduct accurate water accounting, and to stimulate investment in the water management sector.
The rules for paid water use in the Republic of Azerbaijan are approved by the relevant executive authority.
The rules and conditions for paid water use in the water management facilities of municipalities are determined by this Code, other relevant legislative acts, and municipal acts.

Article 98. Determination of the fee for water use

The fee for water use is determined by the relevant executive authority.
The fee for water use is differentiated for various water management systems depending on its quality, designation for use (drinking water, technical water, irrigation water, etc.), the technical level of the water management facilities, the methods of water intake and transportation, and other factors affecting the volume of production costs.
When calculating the fee for the use of groundwater, the costs incurred for its exploration, prospecting, and the determination of its reserves are also taken into account in the manner prescribed by legislation.
The application of concessions for water use is determined by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
The fee for water use in water bodies in municipal ownership is determined by municipalities in the manner specified in the first part of this article.
The fee for water use from water bodies in private ownership is determined on the basis of a contract concluded between the owners and water consumers.

Article 99. Types and rules of payment related to the use of water bodies

The following types of payment are established for the use of water bodies:
for the intake of water and the discharge of wastewater into water bodies for special purposes;
for the use of water bodies for hydropower and transport needs;
for the extraction of mineral resources from water bodies and for conducting geological exploration and drilling works, as well as for the construction and operation of underwater structures;
for the use of water bodies for organized sports, recreation, and tourism purposes;
for use for other purposes provided for by legislation.
The types and rules of payment related to the use of water bodies are determined by the relevant executive authority.

Article 100. Financing of measures for the use and protection of water bodies

Measures for the use and protection of state-significant water bodies are financed from the state budget on the basis of relevant programs.
Measures for the use and protection of water bodies granted for separate use and ownership are financed by the owners and users of the water bodies.
Measures for the prevention of the harmful effects of waters and the elimination of their consequences are carried out at the expense of the users of water bodies, owners, local budgets, and the state budget in the manner prescribed by legislation.

CHAPTER XVII
Resolution of disputes in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies and liability for violation of water legislation

Article 101. Resolution of disputes in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies

Disputes between physical and legal persons in the sphere of use and protection of water bodies are resolved in court in accordance with the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 102. Invalidity of transactions

Transactions concluded in violation of the water legislation are invalid.

Article 103. Liability for violation of the water legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan

Officials, physical, and legal persons who violate the water legislation bear civil, material, administrative, and criminal liability in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan in the following cases:
in the cases specified in Article 102 of this Code;
when they unlawfully seize water bodies or use water without authorization;
when they unlawfully construct hydraulic and other structures in water bodies;
when they violate the conditions established by the license for the use of water bodies;
when they fail to comply with water use limits;
when they fail to provide water to water consumers within the specified periods and in the specified volume;
when they pollute, litter, and deplete water bodies;
when they operate enterprises, communal, and other facilities without structures and equipment that prevent the pollution, littering of water bodies, and their harmful effects;
when they use water resources wastefully;
when they pollute waters and cause soil erosion and other harmful phenomena by violating the protection regime of water bodies in water catchment areas;
when they carry out works related to hydraulic and communal water management without authorization;
when they damage water management structures and equipment and fail to comply with their operating rules;
when they violate the operating rules of water management structures and equipment;
when they use water for purposes other than its designated use;
when they take water from water pipelines, canals, and other water systems without authorization, and discharge drinking, domestic, technical, and wastewater to outside areas (fields, streets, communication and energy communication networks) without proper designation;
when they destroy collector and drainage systems, thereby worsening the amelioration condition of the lands;
when they fail to pay the fee for water use and fines for violating water use rules, as well as payments for wastewater discharged into water bodies and the environment, in a timely manner;
when they drill wells to extract water without the necessary permission or violate the rules and technology for drilling wells to extract water;
when they fail to comply with the rules of water protection zones and coastal protection strips;
when they fail to comply with the rules for conducting state monitoring of water bodies, state registration of waters, and maintenance of the state water cadastre;
when they violate the regime of specially protected water bodies;
when they fail to comply with the instructions of the authorities that control the use and protection of water bodies;
when they violate the requirements of the sanitary protection zones of drinking water sources, water treatment facilities, and water pipelines;
when the quality of drinking water does not meet sanitary requirements and state standards, and in other cases provided for by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Article 104. Return of unlawfully seized water bodies

The relevant executive authorities have the right to suspend these activities and the use of water from these sources when the rules established for the use and protection of waters are violated.
Unlawfully seized water bodies are returned to their proper ownership without compensation for the expenses incurred during the illegal use.

Article 105. Compensation for damage caused by violation of water legislation

Physical and legal persons must compensate for the damage they have caused by violating the water legislation in the manner prescribed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
